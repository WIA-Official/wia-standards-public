Chapter 08: Conformance, Farmer Sovereignty, Roadmap

8.1 Conformance test suite

A black-box conformance test suite is published at https://github.com/WIA-Official/wia-smart-farm-conformance and walks discovery, field publish/read, sensor stream subscribe/reconnect, irrigation/fertilisation/harvest event submit, scouting observation publish, federation handshake, farmer consent envelope enforcement, audience-control matrix, rate-limit headers and exhaustion behaviour. Hosts publishing bridge_profile=Full SHOULD additionally pass the suite extension tests for AgGateway ADAPT bridge and ISOBUS Task XML round-trip.

8.2 Reference container

The wia/smart-farm-host:1.0.0 container image implements every Phase 2 endpoint with mock data including a synthetic 32-hectare maize field with simulated weather, soil moisture, and harvest data.

8.3 Farmer sovereignty considerations

The standard takes farmer ownership of farm data as a Phase 3 primitive. Practical consequences: equipment vendors cannot share farmer data with seed companies, financial institutions, or insurance providers without the farmer\x27s explicit signature on a consent envelope. The default is no consent; the farmer must opt in. This contrasts with the dominant industry pattern where vendor terms-of-service grant broad data-use rights by default.

Within the 1.x line every Phase 2 endpoint MUST remain reachable. Breaking changes ride a major version bump with 12-month deprecation window per IETF RFC 8594 and RFC 9745.

8.6 Closing note

Precision agriculture has been "five years away from mainstream" for two decades; vendor lock-in has been the brake. WIA Smart Farm exists to remove the brake with an open, federation-aware, farmer-sovereign standard.

Farmer-First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through: does this make farm data more useful to the farmer who collected it, or just more accessible to the vendor who sold the equipment? When the two conflict, farmer sovereignty wins. That is why the standard mandates explicit farmer consent for cross-vendor data sharing, refuses to allow vendor terms-of-service to bypass the consent envelope, and requires implementations to surface vendor data flows so farmers can verify what their data is doing.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in smart-farm infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, sensor data volume during peak season: a 100-hectare farm with 20 soil sensors per hectare emitting at 5-minute cadence produces ~500 000 sensor readings per day. Hosts MUST provision for this peak load and SHOULD downsample to hourly aggregates after 30 days. Second, farmer consent revocation: when a farmer revokes consent, the data must stop flowing within minutes; the consent envelope MUST carry an explicit revocation timestamp and downstream consumers MUST honour it. Third, ISOBUS Task XML round-trip: variable-rate prescription maps emitted as ISOBUS Task XML must round-trip through WIA envelopes without losing the per-cell prescription rates; the harvest_record envelope embeds GeoJSON yield maps that are convertible back to ISOBUS Task XML for the next planting cycle.
