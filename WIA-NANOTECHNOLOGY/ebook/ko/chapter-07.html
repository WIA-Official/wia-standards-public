The fundamental principles underlying Environmental & Energy Applications are rooted in quantum mechanics, materials science, and engineering. At the nanoscale, materials exhibit properties that differ significantly from their bulk counterparts due to quantum confinement, increased surface-to-volume ratios, and novel physical phenomena.

7.2.1 Theoretical Foundation

The theoretical framework for understanding Environmental & Energy Applications involves multiple scientific disciplines. Quantum mechanics describes the behavior of electrons and phonons at the nanoscale, while classical mechanics and thermodynamics explain larger-scale phenomena. The interplay between these domains creates unique opportunities for innovation.

Key theoretical concepts include energy band structures, density of states, surface energy minimization, and kinetic vs thermodynamic control of synthesis. Understanding these principles enables rational design of nanomaterials with targeted properties.

  • Quantum Effects: Discrete energy levels, tunneling, wave-particle duality
  • Surface Phenomena: Surface reconstruction, adsorption, catalysis
  • Size-Dependent Properties: Optical, electronic, magnetic, mechanical
  • Thermodynamics: Phase equilibria, nucleation and growth, stability
  • Kinetics: Reaction rates, diffusion, mass transport
