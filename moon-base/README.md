> **홍익인간 (弘益人間) - Benefit All Humanity** - Comprehensive standard for establishing and operating permanent human bases on the lunar surface.

## 📋 Overview

The **WIA-SPACE-011 Moon Base Standard** provides complete specifications for creating sustainable, long-term lunar settlements supporting scientific research, resource extraction, and preparation for Mars missions. This includes:

- 🏔️ **Site Selection** - Polar ice regions, lava tubes, and crater rims
- 🏗️ **Construction** - 3D printing, modular assembly, inflatable habitats
- ⚡ **ISRU** - Water ice mining, oxygen production, resource processing
- 🌱 **Life Support** - Closed-loop systems, water recycling, food production
- 🛡️ **Radiation Protection** - Regolith shielding, storm shelters
- 🔌 **Energy** - Solar arrays, nuclear power, energy storage
- 🚀 **Operations** - Communications, transportation, daily management
- 💫 **Future Vision** - Lunar economy, space tourism, Mars gateway

## 🎯 Key Features

- **Comprehensive Coverage**: All aspects from site selection to long-term operations
- **Science-Based**: Built on Artemis Program plans and ISS experience
- **Practical**: Ready-to-implement specifications and guidelines
- **Scalable**: From 4-person outpost to 1000+ person lunar city
- **International**: Designed for global cooperation and shared benefit
- **Sustainable**: ISRU-focused for minimal Earth dependence

## 📊 Statistics & Facts

### Lunar Environment
- **Distance from Earth:** 384,400 km (3-day journey)
- **Gravity:** 1.62 m/s² (1/6 Earth)
- **Temperature:** -173°C to +127°C (300°C range)
- **Radiation:** 200-400 mSv/year (vs 2.4 on Earth)
- **Day/Night:** 14 Earth days each

### Resources
- **Water Ice:** 100M-1B tons estimated (polar regions)
- **Helium-3:** ~1 million tons in regolith
- **Oxygen:** 45% of regolith by mass
- **Metals:** Iron, aluminum, titanium, rare earths

### Timeline
- **2026:** Artemis III South Pole landing
- **2028:** Lunar Gateway operational
- **2030s:** Permanent base (4-6 crew)
- **2040s:** Expanded base (10-20 crew), commercial operations
- **2050s:** Lunar city (50-100+ crew)

## 🛠️ Technical Highlights

### Site Selection Criteria

**Primary Site: Shackleton Crater (South Pole)**
- Water ice: ✅ Abundant (millions of tons)
- Sunlight: ✅ 80-90% availability
- Temperature: ✅ Stable (-40°C to -20°C)
- Radiation: ⚠️ Requires shielding
- **Score:** 9.5/10

### Construction Methods

1. **3D Printing:**
   - Sintering regolith with microwave/laser
   - 20-50 MPa compressive strength
   - No Earth materials needed

2. **Modular Assembly:**
   - Pre-fabricated 4-8m diameter modules
   - CBM/APAS docking ports
   - Launched from Earth

3. **Inflatable Habitats:**
   - Compact launch, large volume
   - Multi-layer protection
   - BEAM-proven technology

### Life Support

- **Oxygen:** Water electrolysis, 0.84 kg/person/day
- **Water:** 95%+ recycling, ISRU extraction from ice
- **Food:** Phase 1 (Earth), Phase 2 (50% local), Phase 3 (90% local)
- **CO₂ Removal:** Sabatier reactor, closed-loop

### Energy

- **Solar:** 80-90% availability at poles
- **Nuclear:** Kilopower 1-10 kW reactors
- **Storage:** Lithium-ion batteries, regenerative fuel cells
- **Requirement:** 50-100 kW for small base
