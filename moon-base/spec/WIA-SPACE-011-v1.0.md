# WIA-SPACE-011: Moon Base Standard v1.0.0

**Status:** Draft
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Last Updated:** 2025-12-26
**Category:** Space Infrastructure
**Philosophy:** 弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity)

## Abstract

This standard defines comprehensive requirements and guidelines for establishing, constructing, and operating permanent human bases on the lunar surface. It covers site selection criteria, construction methodologies, life support systems, resource utilization (ISRU), radiation protection, communications, transportation, and long-term sustainability for lunar habitats including polar bases, equatorial outposts, and lava tube settlements.

## 1. Scope

### 1.1 Applicability

This standard applies to:
- Permanent and semi-permanent lunar surface bases
- Polar region installations (South Pole preferred)
- Equatorial temporary outposts
- Lava tube underground habitats
- Orbital lunar stations (Lunar Gateway)
- Commercial lunar facilities

### 1.2 Objectives

- Ensure crew safety and long-term health in lunar environment
- Enable self-sufficiency through ISRU
- Minimize dependence on Earth resupply
- Support scientific research and commercial activities
- Prepare for Mars exploration missions
- Facilitate international cooperation

## 3. Terms and Definitions

### 3.1 General Terms

**Lunar Base:** A permanent or semi-permanent sealed facility designed to support human life on the Moon's surface or in subsurface caverns.

**ISRU (In-Situ Resource Utilization):** The collection, processing, and utilization of materials found or manufactured on the Moon rather than imported from Earth.

**Regolith:** The layer of loose, fragmented material covering solid bedrock on the Moon, consisting of fine dust and rocky fragments.

**PSR (Permanently Shadowed Region):** Areas in lunar polar craters that never receive direct sunlight, containing water ice deposits.

**Peak of Eternal Light:** Elevated locations near lunar poles that receive near-continuous sunlight (80-90% annually).

### 3.2 Radiation Terms

**GCR (Galactic Cosmic Rays):** High-energy particles from outside the solar system; primary continuous radiation threat.

**SPE (Solar Particle Event):** Sudden bursts of energetic particles from solar flares; requires storm shelter protection.

**Radiation Dose:** Measured in Sieverts (Sv) or millisieverts (mSv); astronaut career limit: 1 Sv.

### 3.3 Construction Terms

**3D Printing (Additive Manufacturing):** Layer-by-layer construction using lunar regolith as feedstock.

**Sintering:** Heating regolith particles to fuse them together without fully melting.

**Inflatable Habitat:** Expandable pressurized structure deployed from compact folded state.

## 4. Site Selection Requirements

### 4.1 Priority Criteria

Lunar base sites SHALL be evaluated based on:

#### 4.1.1 Water Ice Accessibility
- **Priority:** Highest
- **Requirement:** Located within 10 km of confirmed PSR water ice deposits
- **Minimum:** Estimated 1 million tons accessible ice within transport range
- **Preferred:** Shackleton Crater rim (South Pole)

#### 4.1.2 Solar Power Availability
- **Priority:** Very High
- **Requirement:** Minimum 70% annual sunlight availability
- **Target:** 80-90% (Peaks of Eternal Light)
- **Alternative:** If <70%, nuclear power required

#### 4.1.3 Temperature Stability
- **Priority:** High
- **Requirement:** Daily temperature variation <150°C
- **Polar regions:** -50°C to -30°C (acceptable)
- **Equatorial:** -173°C to +127°C (requires extensive thermal management)

#### 4.1.4 Earth Communication
- **Priority:** Medium
- **Requirement:** Direct line-of-sight to Earth >50% of time
- **Alternative:** Lunar Gateway or relay satellites for far side

#### 4.1.5 Landing Zone
- **Priority:** High
- **Requirements:**
  - Slope <15° within 5 km radius
  - Boulder density <5% surface coverage
  - Clear approach corridor

### 4.2 Recommended Sites

**Primary:** Shackleton Crater rim (89.9°S)
- Score: 9.5/10
- Water: Excellent (millions of tons)
- Solar: 80-90% availability
- Temperature: Stable -40°C to -20°C

**Secondary:** Peary Crater region (88.6°N)
- Score: 9.0/10
- Water: Excellent
- Solar: 85% availability
- Less studied than South Pole

**Future:** Lava tubes (Marius Hills)
- Score: 8.5/10 (long-term)
- Radiation: Perfect shielding
- Temperature: Constant -20°C
- Solar: Requires nuclear power

## 5. Construction Requirements

### 5.1 Structural Standards

#### 5.1.1 Pressure Vessel Design

All habitable volumes SHALL:
- Maintain internal pressure: 55-101.3 kPa (0.54-1.0 atm)
- Use cylindrical or spherical geometry for stress distribution
- Withstand 2x safety factor on pressure differential
- Include automatic leak detection and isolation

#### 5.1.2 Materials

Habitat structures SHALL use:
- Aluminum alloys (2219, 6061) for primary structure
- Composite materials (carbon fiber) for secondary structure
- Inflatable fabrics (Kevlar, Vectran) for expandable modules
- Wall thickness: 5-10 mm minimum (pressure + micrometeorite)

### 5.2 Radiation Shielding

Bases SHALL provide radiation protection:
- **Surface modules:** 2-3 meters regolith covering (50-70% reduction)
- **Storm shelter:** 10 g/cm² equivalent shielding minimum
- **Underground:** Natural lava tube ceiling (100% GCR shielding)
- **Water tanks:** Dual-purpose as shielding walls

Annual dose limits:
- Routine operations: <200 mSv/year
- With storm shelter access: <500 mSv/year
- Career limit: <1000 mSv (1 Sv)

### 5.3 Construction Methods

#### 5.3.1 Regolith 3D Printing

- **Primary method:** Microwave or laser sintering
- **Layer thickness:** 5-10 mm
- **Feedstock:** In-situ regolith (no Earth import)
- **Applications:** Walls, landing pads, roads, shielding
- **Strength:** Minimum 20 MPa compressive strength

#### 5.3.2 Modular Assembly

- **Modules:** Pre-fabricated on Earth, 4-8 meters diameter
- **Mass:** 3-8 tons per module
- **Docking:** CBM or APAS compatible ports
- **Types:** Habitat, lab, ECLSS, airlock, storage, greenhouse

#### 5.3.3 Inflatable Structures

- **Deployment:** Compact launch, inflate on Moon
- **Volume:** 50-200 m³ per module
- **Layers:** Multi-layer (bladder, structure, micrometeorite shield)
- **Applications:** Large volume habitats, greenhouses

### 5.4 Thermal Control

Temperature management SHALL include:
- **Passive:** Multi-layer insulation (MLI), regolith covering
- **Active:** Fluid loops, radiators, heat pumps
- **Internal:** 18-27°C maintained
- **Equipment:** Within manufacturer specifications

## 6. Life Support Systems (ECLSS)

### 6.1 Atmosphere Control

#### 6.1.1 Composition
- Oxygen: 21% (similar to Earth)
- Nitrogen: 78%
- Total pressure: 101.3 kPa (1 atm)
- CO₂: <0.5% (5000 ppm maximum)
- Humidity: 40-60%

#### 6.1.2 Oxygen Generation
- **Method:** Water electrolysis (primary)
- **Capacity:** 0.84 kg/person/day minimum
- **Efficiency:** >90%
- **Backup:** High-pressure oxygen storage (7-day supply)

#### 6.1.3 CO₂ Removal
- **Method:** 4-bed molecular sieve or Sabatier reactor
- **Capacity:** 1 kg CO₂/person/day
- **Sabatier:** CO₂ + 4H₂ → CH₄ + 2H₂O (water recovery)

### 6.2 Water Management

#### 6.2.1 Water Budget
- Drinking: 2-3 liters/person/day
- Hygiene: 3-5 liters/person/day
- Food preparation: 1-2 liters/person/day
- EVA cooling: 2-3 liters/person/EVA
- Total: ~10 liters/person/day

#### 6.2.2 Water Recycling
- **Target:** 95% recovery rate minimum
- **Urine processing:** 85-90% recovery
- **Humidity condensate:** 100% recovery
- **Wastewater:** Multi-stage filtration, UV sterilization
- **Storage:** 30-day emergency supply

### 6.3 Food Production

#### 6.3.1 Initial Phase (2026-2030)
- 100% Earth supply
- Freeze-dried, shelf-stable
- 2-5 year shelf life
- 2 kg/person/day

#### 6.3.2 Transition Phase (2030-2040)
- 50% Earth, 50% lunar production
- Hydroponic greenhouses
- Fresh vegetables, limited grains
- 20-50 m² greenhouse/person

#### 6.3.3 Self-Sufficient (2040+)
- 90%+ local production
- Greenhouse area: 50-100 m²/person
- Crops: lettuce, tomatoes, potatoes, wheat, soybeans
- Protein: Cultured meat or aquaculture

## 7. ISRU Requirements

### 7.1 Water Extraction

#### 7.1.1 Ice Mining
- **Location:** PSR craters
- **Depth:** 0.5-2 meters
- **Method:** Excavation + heating (100-200°C)
- **Yield:** Target 10 tons/month by 2035

#### 7.1.2 Processing
- Sublimation/evaporation
- Condensation
- Filtration and purification
- Electrolysis (H₂ + O₂)

### 7.2 Oxygen Production

**From Water:**
- 2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂
- 1 kg water → 0.89 kg oxygen

**From Regolith:**
- Molten regolith electrolysis (1600°C)
- Hydrogen reduction (900-1000°C)
- Yield: ~40% oxygen by mass from regolith

### 7.3 Construction Materials

- **Regolith concrete:** Regolith + sulfur binder
- **Sintered blocks:** Microwave/laser sintering
- **Metals:** Iron, aluminum, titanium extraction
- **Glass:** Melted regolith (1400°C)

## 8. Energy Systems

### 8.1 Solar Power

- **Location:** Peaks of Eternal Light (polar)
- **Efficiency:** 20-30% (space-grade cells)
- **Area:** 1 m²/kW output
- **Dust mitigation:** Electrostatic cleaning, brushes

### 8.2 Nuclear Power

- **Type:** Kilopower fission reactors
- **Output:** 1-10 kW per unit
- **Fuel:** Uranium-235 or Plutonium-238
- **Lifespan:** 10-15 years
- **Shielding:** Required distance from habitats

### 8.3 Energy Storage

- **Batteries:** Lithium-ion, 100-500 kWh capacity
- **Fuel cells:** H₂/O₂ regenerative
- **Requirement:** 14-day night survival (if not polar)

## 9. Communications

### 9.1 Earth Communications

- **Delay:** 1.3 seconds one-way, 2.6 seconds round-trip
- **Bandwidth:** 10-100 Mbps
- **Frequency:** S-band, X-band, Ka-band
- **Antenna:** High-gain parabolic
- **Relay:** Lunar Gateway or dedicated satellites for far side

### 9.2 Local Communications

- **Wired:** Fiber optic backbone
- **Wireless:** Wi-Fi for indoor, UHF for EVA
- **Range:** 10-50 km for rover communications

## 10. Transportation

### 10.1 EVA Suits

- Pressure: 0.3-0.4 atm (pure oxygen)
- Duration: 6-8 hours
- Dust protection: Sealed joints, brushing system
- Temperature: -180°C to +120°C operational range

### 10.2 Rovers

**Small Rover (1-2 crew):**
- Speed: 10-20 km/h
- Range: 30-50 km round trip
- Unpressurized

**Large Rover (4-6 crew):**
- Speed: 20-30 km/h
- Range: 100-200 km
- Pressurized cabin
- Life support: Multi-day capability

## 11. Safety and Emergency

### 11.1 Redundancy

All critical systems SHALL have:
- 2x redundancy minimum (N+1)
- Automatic failover capability
- Independent power sources

### 11.2 Emergency Supplies

- Oxygen: 7-day supply in high-pressure tanks
- Water: 30-day supply
- Food: 90-day supply
- Medical: Comprehensive emergency kit

### 11.3 Storm Shelter

- Location: Central to habitat
- Shielding: 100 g/cm² minimum
- Capacity: All crew members
- Duration: 48-hour occupancy
- Supplies: Food, water, medical, communications

### 11.4 Evacuation

- Lander always maintained in ready state
- Evacuation window: <4 hours from decision
- Earth return capability: Always available

## 12. Operations

### 12.1 Crew

- **Initial:** 4-6 astronauts
- **Rotation:** 6-month tours (similar to ISS)
- **Training:** 2 years minimum
- **Health:** Continuous monitoring

### 12.2 Daily Schedule

- Sleep: 8 hours
- Work: 8 hours (research, construction, maintenance)
- Exercise: 2 hours (mandatory)
- Meals: 2 hours
- Personal time: 4 hours

### 12.3 Maintenance

- **Weekly:** Life support, power, communications checks
- **Monthly:** EVA equipment, rovers, structural inspection
- **Annually:** Complete system overhaul

## 13. International Cooperation

### 13.1 Artemis Accords Compliance

All operations SHALL comply with:
- Peaceful purposes
- Transparency
- Interoperability
- Emergency assistance
- Registration of space objects
- Release of scientific data
- Protecting heritage sites
- Space resource extraction
- Deconfliction of activities
- Orbital debris mitigation

### 13.2 Resource Sharing

- Water, oxygen, power shared in emergencies
- Scientific data openly accessible
- Communication infrastructure interoperable
- Standardized docking ports (CBM/APAS)

## 14. Environmental Protection

### 14.1 Planetary Protection

- Minimize surface disturbance
- No contamination of scientifically interesting areas
- Waste management and recycling
- Preservation of Apollo heritage sites (exclusion zones)

### 14.2 Sustainability

- 90%+ resource recycling target
- ISRU maximization to reduce Earth dependence
- Long-term infrastructure design (20+ year lifespan)
- Expandability for future growth

## 15. Future Expansion

### 15.1 Growth Pathway

**2026-2030:** 4-6 crew, basic ISRU, science focus
**2030-2040:** 10-20 crew, full ISRU, commercial operations begin
**2040-2050:** 50-100 crew, lunar economy, Mars gateway role
**2050+:** 100-1000 crew, lunar cities, terraforming research

### 15.2 Mars Preparation

Lunar bases SHALL serve as testbeds for Mars technologies:
- ISRU systems
- Closed-loop life support
- Radiation shielding
- Long-duration isolation
- 3D printing construction
- Autonomous operations

## Appendix A: Reference Missions

### A.1 Artemis Base Camp

- Location: Shackleton Crater rim
- Crew: 4 astronauts
- Duration: 1-2 months initial, 6 months later
- Facilities: 2 habitat modules, 1 rover, 1 ISRU pilot plant

### A.2 Commercial Lunar Station (2035)

- Operator: Private consortium
- Crew: 6-10 (mix of professionals and tourists)
- Purpose: Research, tourism, resource extraction
- Revenue: $500M-1B annually
