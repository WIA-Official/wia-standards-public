1. Gravity Environment

Effects of Low Gravity

The Moon's surface gravity is approximately 16.5% (1/6) of Earth's. This has significant impacts on base design and human physiology.

Impact on Construction and Structures

  • Reduced Load: Same mass structure weighs 6x less, enabling taller buildings
  • Material Strength: Lower structural stress allows lighter materials
  • Dust Settlement: Dust settles slowly, causing equipment contamination
  • Jump Height: People can jump 2-3m high, increasing fall risks

Effects on Human Body

Body System Effect Countermeasure
Muscular Muscle loss (1-5%/month) 2 hours daily resistance exercise
Skeletal Bone density loss (1-2%/month) High-intensity exercise, vitamin D, calcium
Cardiovascular Heart muscle weakening, BP changes Aerobic exercise, regular checkups
Vestibular Balance changes, motion sickness Adaptation training, medication

Thermal Management Challenges

  • Insulation: Habitat modules must protect from extreme temperatures
  • Thermal Expansion: Managing structural stress from expansion/contraction
  • Equipment Protection: Electronics must operate from -40°C to +85°C
  • Heat Dissipation: In vacuum, only radiative heat transfer possible

3. Radiation Environment

Types of Radiation

Without Earth's magnetic field and atmosphere, the Moon is directly exposed to space radiation.

Radiation Type Characteristics Dose (mSv/year) Risk Level
Galactic Cosmic Rays (GCR) High-energy protons, heavy ions 200-400 High (Continuous)
Solar Particle Events (SPE) Solar flare particles 100-1000 (per event) Very High (Intermittent)
Secondary Radiation Neutrons, gamma rays from surface 50-100 Medium
Comparison: Natural radiation at Earth's sea level is about 2.4 mSv/year. The lunar surface has 100-200 times higher radiation. The ICRP recommends a lifetime astronaut dose limit of 1 Sv (1000 mSv).

Radiation Shielding Strategies

  1. Regolith Shielding: 2-3m of lunar soil reduces radiation 50-70%
  2. Hydrogen-rich Materials: Water, polyethylene are effective shields
  3. Storm Shelter: High-shielding space for solar flare refuge
  4. Underground Base: Utilize lava tubes for natural shielding

5. Regolith (Lunar Soil)

Regolith Characteristics

Property Value Significance
Particle Size 40-800 μm Very fine powder
Density 1.5-1.8 g/cm³ Similar to Earth soil
Color Gray-black Varies with titanium content
Electrostatic Strong Sticks to equipment easily
Abrasiveness Very High Sharp particles cause wear

Regolith Problems and Solutions

  • Dust Contamination: Airlock brush systems, electrostatic removal
  • Health Risks: Sharp particles can cause silicosis if inhaled
  • Equipment Wear: Seals and mechanisms vulnerable to abrasion

Regolith Utilization

  • Construction Material: 3D printing feedstock, concrete manufacturing
  • Radiation Shielding: Cover habitats with 2-3m thickness
  • Oxygen Extraction: 45% of regolith is oxygen (from SiO₂, FeO)
  • Metal Extraction: Iron, aluminum, titanium

Conclusion

The Moon's environment is completely different from Earth. Low gravity, extreme temperatures, high radiation, vacuum, and troublesome regolith - all must be considered in base design. However, these challenges are surmountable. With appropriate technology and design, humans can live safely on the Moon.
