Emotion Detection and Analysis

Understanding the emotional states of our pets is crucial for effective translation. This chapter explores how the WIA-PET-010 system detects, analyzes, and interprets pet emotions through advanced machine learning techniques.

Dimensional vs. Categorical Emotion Models

We employ both approaches for comprehensive emotion understanding:

Dimensional Models

Three-dimensional space captures emotional nuance:

  • Valence: Pleasant (positive) to unpleasant (negative)
  • Arousal: Low energy (calm) to high energy (excited)
  • Dominance: Submissive to assertive/controlling

Categorical Models

Discrete emotion categories for clear interpretation:

  • Happiness and joy
  • Excitement and anticipation
  • Anxiety and stress
  • Fear and panic
  • Pain and discomfort
  • Hunger and thirst
  • Affection and bonding
  • Boredom and frustration
  • Curiosity and interest
  • Contentment and relaxation

Multimodal Emotion Indicators

Acoustic Emotion Signals

  • Pitch variations: Higher pitch often indicates arousal or distress
  • Harmonic structure: Irregularity suggests stress
  • Duration patterns: Sustained vocalizations indicate urgency
  • Amplitude dynamics: Loudness reflects intensity

Visual Emotion Signals

  • Facial muscle tension and relaxation
  • Eye shape and pupil dilation
  • Ear position and mobility
  • Tail position, movement speed, and pattern
  • Overall body posture and tension
  • Movement patterns (pacing, freezing, approach/avoidance)

Physiological Signals

When available from wearable sensors:

  • Heart rate and heart rate variability
  • Respiratory rate
  • Body temperature
  • Activity levels and sleep patterns

Emotion Recognition Architectures

Our deep learning models process multimodal inputs:

  1. Audio Emotion Network: CNN on spectrograms → LSTM for temporal patterns → Emotion classifier
  2. Visual Emotion Network: Pose estimation → Action recognition → Facial expression analysis → Emotion classifier
  3. Fusion Network: Attention-based fusion of audio and visual features → Final emotion prediction

Context-Aware Emotion Interpretation

The same physical signals can indicate different emotions depending on context:

  • Panting: Could indicate heat, excitement, or anxiety
  • Purring: Usually contentment, but sometimes pain or stress
  • Tail wagging: Can be friendly or agitated

Our system integrates environmental and situational context to disambiguate these signals.

Temporal Emotion Dynamics

Emotions change over time. We track:

  • Emotion transitions: How emotions shift in response to events
  • Baseline states: Individual pet's typical emotional patterns
  • Emotion regulation: How quickly pets return to baseline after arousal
  • Chronic emotional states: Ongoing anxiety, depression, or contentment

Clinical Applications

Pain Assessment

Objective pain detection through:

  • Vocalization patterns (whimpering, groaning)
  • Facial grimacing (pain scales like Feline Grimace Scale)
  • Guarding behaviors and reduced mobility
  • Changes from baseline behavior

Anxiety and Stress Detection

Early identification of anxiety enables intervention:

  • Separation anxiety when owners leave
  • Storm and noise phobias
  • Social anxiety with other animals
  • Chronic stress from environmental factors

Depression Screening

Detect mood disorders through:

  • Reduced activity and engagement
  • Changes in vocalization patterns
  • Altered sleep and eating patterns
  • Decreased interest in previously enjoyed activities

Emotion-Based Translation Enhancement

Emotional context enriches translations:

  • "I want to go outside" (neutral) vs. "I REALLY need to go outside NOW!" (urgent)
  • "I see a squirrel" (calm observation) vs. "SQUIRREL! Let me chase it!" (excited)
  • "I'm not feeling well" (mild discomfort) vs. "I'm in serious pain" (high distress)

The emotional layer transforms basic intent recognition into nuanced, empathetic communication that captures not just what pets are communicating, but how they feel about it.
