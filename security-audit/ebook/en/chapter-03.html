Chapter 3: Compliance Frameworks

Meeting Regulatory Requirements

3.1 Overview of Compliance Requirements

Security audit requirements vary by industry, geography, and data type. WIA-SEC-017 is designed to meet the requirements of all major compliance frameworks through comprehensive event logging, tamper-proof storage, and automated reporting.

3.2 SOC 2 Type II

SOC 2 is the de facto standard for cloud service providers. Type II reports verify that security controls operate effectively over an extended period (typically 6-12 months).

3.3 ISO/IEC 27001

ISO 27001 is the international standard for information security management systems (ISMS). Annex A provides specific logging requirements:

Annex A Controls

  • A.12.4.1: Event logs must record user activities, exceptions, faults, and information security events
  • A.12.4.2: Logging facilities and log information must be protected against tampering and unauthorized access
  • A.12.4.3: System administrator and operator activities must be logged and protected
  • A.12.4.4: Clocks of all relevant systems must be synchronized

WIA-SEC-017 ISO 27001 Compliance

The standard provides built-in ISO 27001 compliance through: cryptographic chain structure (A.12.4.2), administrator audit trails (A.12.4.3), and NTP time synchronization (A.12.4.4).

3.4 GDPR

The General Data Protection Regulation imposes strict requirements for organizations processing personal data of EU residents.

Key Articles

  • Article 30: Maintain records of processing activities
  • Article 33: Notify authorities within 72 hours of data breach
  • Article 35: Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments

72-Hour Breach Notification

GDPR requires breach notification within 72 hours of becoming aware. Your audit system must detect breaches in near-real-time to meet this requirement.

3.5 HIPAA

Healthcare organizations must implement audit controls under the HIPAA Security Rule:

  • §164.312(b): Implement mechanisms to record and examine system activity containing ePHI
  • §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(D): Regular review of audit logs
  • Retention: Minimum 6-year retention of audit logs

3.6 PCI-DSS

Requirement 10 of PCI-DSS focuses entirely on audit trails:

RequirementDescription
10.1Link all access to system components to individual users
10.2Implement automated audit trails for all system components
10.3Record specific data elements for each event
10.4Synchronize all critical system clocks
10.5Secure audit trails so they cannot be altered
10.6Review logs daily for all system components
10.7Retain audit history for at least one year
