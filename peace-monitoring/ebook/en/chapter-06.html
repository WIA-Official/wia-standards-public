6.4 Encryption & Security

All communications use end-to-end encryption with quantum-resistant algorithms planned for future phases.

Encryption Standards

  • Transport Layer: TLS 1.3 with mutual authentication
  • Message Encryption: AES-256-GCM
  • Key Exchange: X25519 (Diffie-Hellman)
  • Digital Signatures: Ed25519
  • Key Rotation: Every 30 days or after 1M messages
  • Perfect Forward Secrecy: Required for all sessions

6.5 Data Synchronization

Blockchain-based distributed ledger ensures all parties maintain consistent records.

Synchronization Features

  • Immutable Records: All critical events recorded on blockchain
  • Multi-Party Consensus: Major decisions require signatures from all parties
  • Conflict Resolution: Observer arbitration for disputed records
  • Historical Audit Trail: Complete traceability of all actions
  • Tamper Detection: Cryptographic verification prevents manipulation

6.7 De-escalation Protocol

Immediate actions to prevent minor incidents from escalating into major crises.

De-escalation Steps

  1. Activate Hotline: Immediate direct communication
  2. Cease-Fire Request: If armed exchange is occurring
  3. Establish Contact: All parties notified simultaneously
  4. Deploy Observers: Neutral observers to incident site
  5. Freeze Movements: All non-essential military activities halted
  6. Investigation: Joint team investigates root cause
  7. Resolution: Confidence-building measures, compensation if needed

6.8 Protocol Compliance Monitoring

Automated systems track compliance with all protocol requirements.

Compliance Metrics

  • Response Times: Percentage of messages responded to within required timeframes
  • Notification Compliance: Troop movements properly declared in advance
  • Inspection Cooperation: Verification requests processed according to timeline
  • Data Quality: Percentage of submissions meeting validation requirements
  • Security Incidents: Encryption violations, unauthorized access attempts
