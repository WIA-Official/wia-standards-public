Phantom Testing

Phantoms are test objects with known properties used to evaluate equipment performance. Common phantoms include the ACR CT phantom (water, air, acrylic inserts), ACR MRI phantom (multiple test structures), and various modality-specific designs. Consistent phantom positioning and acquisition protocols are essential for meaningful trending.

7.3 Radiation Dose Management

Radiation dose optimization follows the ALARA principle—As Low As Reasonably Achievable while maintaining diagnostic quality:

Dose Metrics

Metric Modality Description
CTDIvol CT Volume CT dose index (mGy)
DLP CT Dose-length product (mGy·cm)
SSDE CT Size-specific dose estimate
DAP/KAP Fluoroscopy Dose area product (Gy·cm²)
MGD Mammography Mean glandular dose (mGy)
Effective Dose All Whole-body risk estimate (mSv)

Diagnostic Reference Levels (DRLs)

DRLs are benchmark dose values representing typical doses for standard examinations. They are not limits but investigation levels—facilities should review protocols if doses consistently exceed DRLs. National DRLs (e.g., ACR DIR data) help identify optimization opportunities.

Dose Optimization Strategies

  • Protocol Optimization: Match technique to clinical indication
  • Automatic Exposure Control: Patient-size adaptation
  • Iterative Reconstruction: Maintain quality at lower dose
  • Pediatric Protocols: Age/size-specific parameters
  • Shielding: Protect sensitive organs when appropriate

7.4 Display Quality

Medical displays require calibration for accurate image interpretation:

Display Standards

Standard Application Key Requirements
DICOM GSDF Grayscale calibration Perceptually linear display
AAPM TG-18 QC test patterns Uniformity, resolution, noise
ACR Guidelines Mammography displays 5MP, calibrated luminance

Display QC Tests

  • Luminance Ratio: Maximum/minimum brightness (≥250:1)
  • Maximum Luminance: Primary: ≥350 cd/m², mammography: ≥450 cd/m²
  • Ambient Lighting: Reading room illumination (≤25-50 lux)
  • GSDF Conformance: JND visibility across gray scale
  • Uniformity: <30% variation across display

⚠️ Display Degradation

Display luminance decreases over time (typically 10-15% per year). Regular calibration and replacement of monitors approaching end-of-life is essential. Commercial display management software automates QC testing and tracking for enterprise deployments.

7.6 Incident Management

Systematic incident management improves safety and quality:

Incident Categories

  • Equipment Failures: Malfunction, unexpected shutdown
  • Examination Errors: Wrong patient, wrong study, wrong side
  • Dose Events: Exceeding thresholds, radiation injury
  • Near Misses: Errors caught before reaching patient
  • Safety Events: MRI projectile, contrast reactions

Root Cause Analysis

Significant incidents require root cause analysis (RCA) to identify contributing factors and prevent recurrence. RCA examines system factors—not individual blame—including communication, training, equipment design, and workflow vulnerabilities.

✓ QA Program Best Practices

Successful QA programs: designate a qualified medical physicist; establish written policies and procedures; maintain detailed records; trend data over time; address failures promptly; engage staff at all levels; pursue accreditation; and foster a culture of continuous improvement.

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Comprehensive QA programs encompass equipment QC, radiation dose management, display calibration, and clinical quality metrics
  • Equipment testing includes daily/weekly/monthly/annual tests with specific acceptance criteria for CT, MRI, and other modalities
  • Dose management follows ALARA principles using metrics (CTDI, DLP, SSDE) and Diagnostic Reference Levels as optimization benchmarks
  • Medical displays require DICOM GSDF calibration with luminance testing; monitors degrade 10-15% annually
  • Accreditation (ACR, Joint Commission, IAC) provides external validation through phantom testing and clinical image review
  • Incident management and continuous improvement use root cause analysis and quality metrics to drive systematic enhancement
