# WIA-ENE-003 Phase 3: Construction and Commissioning

## Overview
Phase 3 executes the detailed engineering plans, constructing and commissioning the carbon capture facility, transport infrastructure, and injection wells.

## 3.1 Construction Management

### 3.1.1 Project Organization
- **Owner's Team**
  - Project director
  - Engineering manager
  - Construction manager
  - HSE manager
  - Commissioning manager

- **EPC Contractor**
  - Project manager
  - Site managers (capture, transport, storage)
  - QA/QC manager
  - Subcontractor coordination

### 3.1.2 Construction Execution Plan
- **Schedule**
  - Gantt charts with critical path
  - Milestone dates
  - Resource loading
  - Weather contingencies

- **Logistics**
  - Material delivery and storage
  - Equipment laydown areas
  - Worker transportation and accommodation
  - Site access and traffic management

- **Quality Control**
  - Inspection and test plans
  - Material certifications
  - Welding procedures and qualifications
  - Non-destructive testing (NDT)

## 3.2 Capture Facility Construction

### 3.2.1 Site Preparation
- **Civil Works**
  - Grading and drainage
  - Access roads
  - Foundations and piling
  - Underground utilities (water, sewer, electrical duct banks)

- **Environmental Controls**
  - Erosion and sediment control
  - Stormwater management
  - Dust suppression
  - Noise barriers (if needed)

### 3.2.2 Equipment Installation
- **Heavy Lifts**
  - Columns and vessels (may require cranes up to 600-ton capacity)
  - Lift plans and rigging approvals
  - Safety exclusion zones

- **Mechanical Installation**
  - Equipment setting and alignment
  - Piping fabrication and installation
  - Insulation and heat tracing
  - Painting and coatings

- **Electrical and Instrumentation**
  - Cable pulling and termination
  - Motor and control wiring
  - Instrument tubing and calibration
  - Control system hardware installation

### 3.2.3 Quality Assurance
- **Inspections**
  - Foundation inspections
  - Weld inspections (radiography, ultrasonic testing)
  - Hydrostatic testing of vessels and piping
  - Instrument loop checks

- **Documentation**
  - As-built drawings
  - Welding records and certifications
  - Test reports
  - Equipment manuals and data books

## 3.3 Transport Infrastructure Construction

### 3.3.1 Pipeline Construction
- **Route Preparation**
  - Surveying and staking
  - Right-of-way clearing
  - Trench excavation

- **Pipe Installation**
  - Pipe stringing along route
  - Welding and NDT (100% radiography or ultrasonic of welds)
  - Coating and cathodic protection
  - Lowering-in and backfilling

- **Testing**
  - Hydrostatic testing: 1.25-1.5x maximum allowable operating pressure (MAOP) for 4-8 hours
  - Dewatering and drying
  - Pre-commissioning cleaning (pigging)

- **Reinstatement**
  - Subsoil and topsoil replacement
  - Revegetation
  - Permanent markers and signs

### 3.3.2 Pumping/Compressor Stations (if needed)
- Installation of booster compression
- Cooling systems
- Power supply and backup
- SCADA integration

## 3.4 Injection Well Construction

### 3.4.1 Drilling Operations
- **Well Planning**
  - Directional survey program
  - Casing and cement program
  - Mud program (drilling fluid properties)
  - Logging program

- **Drilling Execution**
  - Spud and drill surface hole
  - Set and cement surface casing
  - Drill intermediate and production holes
  - Run and cement casings to surface
  - Directional drilling if required

- **Logging and Testing**
  - Wireline logs (gamma ray, resistivity, porosity, sonic)
  - Formation integrity tests
  - Casing integrity logs (cement bond logs, ultrasonic imaging)

### 3.4.2 Completion
- **Tubing Installation**
  - Run injection tubing (CO₂-resistant alloy)
  - Install packer to isolate injection zone
  - Wellhead installation (rated for service conditions)

- **Injection Testing**
  - Injectivity test with brine or CO₂
  - Pressure fall-off analysis
  - Verification of connectivity to storage formation

## 3.5 Monitoring Infrastructure

### 3.5.1 Observation Wells
- **Purposes**
  - Pressure monitoring in injection zone
  - Groundwater monitoring in shallow aquifers
  - CO₂ saturation logging

- **Locations**
  - Within predicted plume area
  - Updip from injection (if structural trap)
  - Potential leakage pathway locations
  - Regulatory spacing requirements

### 3.5.2 Surface Monitoring
- **Installation**
  - Soil gas monitoring points
  - Groundwater wells
  - Eddy covariance towers for atmospheric flux
  - InSAR corner reflectors (if satellite monitoring)

- **Baseline Data Collection**
  - Pre-injection monitoring (typically 1 year)
  - Establish natural variability
  - Regulatory requirement

### 3.5.3 Data Management Systems
- **SCADA for Operations**
  - Real-time data from capture, transport, injection
  - Alarms and event logging
  - Remote monitoring capability

- **MRV Database**
  - Automated data ingestion from sensors
  - Manual data entry for lab results, surveys
  - QC checks and validation
  - Reporting dashboard per WIA-ENE-003 protocols

## 3.6 Commissioning

### 3.6.1 Pre-Commissioning
- **Mechanical Completion**
  - Punchlist items resolved
  - All systems inspected and accepted
  - Safety systems tested and operational

- **Utility Start-Up**
  - Electrical systems energized
  - Water systems operational
  - Compressed air available

- **Instrument Checkout**
  - Calibration verification
  - Control loops tested
  - Alarms and interlocks functional

### 3.6.2 Commissioning Plan
- **Sequence**
  1. Control systems and safety systems
  2. Utilities (cooling water, steam, electrical)
  3. CO₂ compression and conditioning (using N₂ or CO₂)
  4. Capture system (initially with N₂, then CO₂)
  5. Transport system (pressure testing, leak surveys)
  6. Injection system (brine injection first, then CO₂)

- **Performance Testing**
  - Each subsystem tested independently
  - Integrated system testing
  - Performance guarantee tests (capture efficiency, energy consumption)
  - Environmental compliance verification (emissions, noise)

### 3.6.3 Startup
- **Initial Operation**
  - Begin CO₂ capture at reduced rate (e.g., 50%)
  - Monitor all parameters closely
  - Tune control systems for optimal performance

- **Ramp-Up**
  - Gradually increase to design capacity over days-weeks
  - Identify and resolve operational issues
  - Optimize for energy efficiency

- **Demonstration Run**
  - Continuous operation at design capacity (typically 30-90 days)
  - Performance test to verify guarantees
  - Reliability demonstrated (e.g., 95% uptime)

### 3.6.4 Handover
- **Documentation Transfer**
  - As-built drawings
  - Operation and maintenance manuals
  - Spare parts lists and supplier contacts
  - Training materials

- **Training**
  - Operators trained on all systems
  - Simulators for upset conditions
  - Maintenance personnel trained on equipment
  - Management trained on MRV and reporting

- **Warranty and Support**
  - Warranty period (typically 12-24 months)
  - EPC contractor support during early operation
  - Vendor support agreements

## 3.7 Health, Safety, and Environment (HSE)

### 3.7.1 Construction Safety
- **Hazard Management**
  - Site inductions and toolbox talks
  - Permit-to-work system (hot work, confined space, work at height)
  - Personal protective equipment (PPE) requirements
  - Fall protection and scaffolding

- **Emergency Preparedness**
  - Emergency response plan
  - First aid and medical facilities
  - Fire equipment
  - Evacuation drills

- **Safety Performance**
  - Target: Zero lost-time incidents (LTIs)
  - Leading indicators: safety observations, near-misses
  - Lagging indicators: LTI frequency rate, total recordable incident rate (TRIR)

### 3.7.2 Environmental Compliance
- **Permit Adherence**
  - Air emissions within limits
  - Stormwater discharges compliant
  - Waste management according to permits
  - Spill prevention and response

- **Monitoring**
  - Air quality (if required)
  - Water quality (surface and groundwater)
  - Noise levels
  - Ecological (if sensitive receptors)

## 3.8 Phase 3 Deliverables

## 1. Overview

Phase 3 covers construction, equipment installation, system integration, and commissioning. Duration: 24-36 months. Budget: 70-80% of CAPEX.

## 2. Construction Execution

### 2.1 Site Preparation (Months 1-6)
- Site clearing and grading
- Foundations for major equipment
- Utility installation
- Access roads and laydown areas

### 2.2 Equipment Installation (Months 6-20)
- Major vessels (absorber, stripper)
- Heat exchangers
- CO₂ compressors
- Electrical and instrumentation

### 2.3 Pipeline Construction (Concurrent)
- Right-of-way preparation
- Pipeline welding and coating
- Hydrostatic testing
- Cathodic protection installation

## 3. Quality Assurance/Quality Control

### 3.1 Inspection and Testing
- Material certifications
- Weld inspection (100% for critical welds)
- Pressure testing (all vessels and piping)
- Electrical continuity and insulation testing
- Instrument calibration

### 3.2 Safety Management
- Daily safety meetings
- Incident reporting and investigation
- Safety training for all personnel
- Target: Zero LTIs (Lost Time Injuries)

## 4. Commissioning and Start-up

### 4.1 Pre-Commissioning (Months 20-24)
- System cleaning and flushing
- Leak testing
- Instrument loop checking
- Control system testing

### 4.2 Commissioning (Months 24-28)
- Subsystem functional tests
- Integrated system testing
- Solvent charging
- First CO₂ capture

### 4.3 Performance Testing (Months 28-30)
- Guarantee test runs
- Efficiency verification
- Environmental compliance testing
- Operator training

### 4.4 Reliability Run (Month 30-31)
- 30-day continuous operation
- Achieve 95% availability
- Document all issues and resolutions

## 5. System Integration

### 5.1 Integration with Host Facility
- Flue gas duct connections
- Steam supply integration
- Cooling water systems
- Electrical interconnection

### 5.2 Monitoring Systems
- SCADA implementation
- Data historian configuration
- Alarm and event management
- Compliance reporting systems

## 6. Training and Knowledge Transfer

### 6.1 Operations Training
- 6-month curriculum
- Classroom and hands-on training
- Simulator-based training
- Emergency response drills

### 6.2 Maintenance Training
- Equipment-specific training
- Preventive maintenance procedures
- Spare parts management
- Troubleshooting guides

## 7. Phase 3 Deliverables

- Fully constructed and tested facility
- Performance guarantees demonstrated
- Operating procedures documented
- Trained operations and maintenance staff
- Regulatory inspections passed
- Commercial operations date (COD) achieved
