# WIA-ENE-003 Phase 1: Planning and Site Assessment

## Overview
Phase 1 establishes the foundation for carbon capture system deployment through comprehensive planning, site assessment, and feasibility analysis.

## 1.1 Project Scoping

### 1.1.1 Emission Source Assessment
- **CO₂ Emissions Characterization**
  - Annual CO₂ emissions (tonnes/year)
  - Concentration in flue gas (%)
  - Pressure and temperature conditions
  - Impurities (SOx, NOx, particulates)
  - Temporal variation (continuous, seasonal, intermittent)

- **Capture Potential Analysis**
  - Target capture rate (%)
  - Minimum viable capture volume
  - Technical feasibility assessment
  - Co-benefits (air quality, process efficiency)

### 1.1.2 Stakeholder Identification
- **Internal Stakeholders**
  - Executive leadership
  - Operations teams
  - Environmental, health & safety (EHS)
  - Finance and procurement

- **External Stakeholders**
  - Regulatory agencies
  - Local communities
  - Environmental organizations
  - Technology providers
  - Financial institutions
  - Research institutions

## 1.2 Technology Selection

### 1.2.1 Capture Technology Evaluation
- **Post-Combustion Capture**
  - Amine absorption systems
  - Advanced solvents
  - Solid sorbents
  - Membrane systems
  - Suitability: CO₂ concentration 3-15%, retrofit applications

- **Pre-Combustion Capture**
  - Gasification with shift reaction
  - Physical absorption (Selexol, Rectisol)
  - Suitability: New builds, hydrogen production, high-pressure sources

- **Oxy-Fuel Combustion**
  - Air separation units
  - Flue gas recirculation
  - Suitability: New builds, high-purity CO₂ requirements

- **Direct Air Capture**
  - Solid sorbent systems
  - Liquid solvent systems
  - Suitability: Distributed emissions, carbon removal

### 1.2.2 Selection Criteria
- Technical compatibility with emission source
- Capture efficiency requirements (target: 90%+)
- Energy availability and cost
- Capital and operating costs
- Technology maturity and risk
- Footprint and site constraints
- Environmental impacts
- Regulatory compliance

## 1.3 Site Assessment

### 1.3.1 Capture Facility Siting
- **Space Requirements**
  - Capture equipment footprint
  - CO₂ compression and dehydration
  - Utilities and auxiliaries
  - Access and maintenance clearances
  - Future expansion capacity

- **Utilities Assessment**
  - Electrical power (MW)
  - Steam/heat (GJ/h)
  - Cooling water (m³/h)
  - Compressed air
  - Chemical storage

- **Infrastructure Evaluation**
  - Foundations and structural support
  - Piping and ductwork modifications
  - Electrical distribution
  - Control systems integration
  - Safety systems

### 1.3.2 Storage Site Identification
- **Geological Storage Options**
  - Deep saline aquifers
    - Capacity: 10,000+ Gt global
    - Depth: >800m (supercritical CO₂)
    - Porosity: >10%
    - Permeability: >10 mD
  
  - Depleted oil/gas fields
    - Proven containment
    - Existing infrastructure
    - EOR opportunities
  
  - Unmineable coal seams
    - ECBM potential
    - Limited capacity
  
  - Basalt formations
    - Rapid mineralization (months-years)
    - High reactivity

- **Site Selection Criteria**
  - Adequate capacity for project lifetime
  - Appropriate depth and pressure
  - Confirmed caprock integrity
  - Seismic stability
  - Distance from population centers
  - Environmental sensitivity
  - Regulatory jurisdiction
  - Access and infrastructure

### 1.3.3 Transport Corridor Assessment
- **Pipeline Route Planning**
  - Direct distance vs. practical route
  - Rights-of-way and easements
  - Terrain and environmental constraints
  - Road, rail, and utility crossings
  - Population density
  - Geotechnical conditions

- **Alternative Transport Modes**
  - Truck transport: <100 km, <50,000 t/year
  - Rail transport: 100-500 km, moderate volumes
  - Ship transport: >500 km, offshore, international

## 1.4 Regulatory and Permitting Framework

### 1.4.1 Regulatory Requirements
- **Capture Facility Permits**
  - Air quality permits (emissions, capture efficiency)
  - Industrial emissions regulations
  - Process safety management
  - Chemical handling and storage
  - Wastewater discharge

- **Transport Permits**
  - Pipeline construction and operation
  - Safety standards (materials, pressure, monitoring)
  - Emergency response plans
  - Environmental impact assessments

- **Storage Permits**
  - Underground injection control (UIC) Class VI (U.S.) or equivalent
  - Site characterization requirements
  - Monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) plans
  - Financial assurance for long-term stewardship
  - Post-injection site care and closure

### 1.4.2 Environmental Assessment
- **Baseline Studies**
  - Air quality monitoring
  - Groundwater characterization
  - Soil gas surveys
  - Ecological surveys
  - Cultural and archaeological resources

- **Impact Analysis**
  - Direct: facility footprint, emissions, water use
  - Indirect: induced economic activity, land use changes
  - Cumulative: interaction with other projects
  - Socioeconomic: employment, community impacts

- **Mitigation Measures**
  - Design modifications
  - Best management practices
  - Environmental monitoring
  - Adaptive management protocols

## 1.5 Economic Analysis

### 1.5.1 Cost Estimation
- **Capital Expenditures (CAPEX)**
  - Capture equipment: $300-800/tCO₂/year capacity
  - Compression and dehydration: $50-150/tCO₂/year
  - Transport infrastructure: $50-200/tCO₂/year
  - Storage wells and infrastructure: $30-100/tCO₂/year
  - Engineering, procurement, construction (EPC): 15-25% of equipment
  - Contingency: 15-30%

- **Operating Expenditures (OPEX)**
  - Energy costs: $20-100/tCO₂ (dominant cost)
  - Chemicals and materials: $5-20/tCO₂
  - Labor and overhead: $10-30/tCO₂
  - Maintenance: 2-4% of CAPEX annually
  - Monitoring and reporting: $2-10/tCO₂

### 1.5.2 Revenue and Financing
- **Revenue Streams**
  - Carbon credits (voluntary or compliance): $10-500/tCO₂
  - Tax credits (e.g., U.S. 45Q: $85-180/tCO₂)
  - Enhanced oil recovery: potential for positive cash flow
  - Product sales (CO₂-derived materials/fuels)

- **Financing Mechanisms**
  - Project finance (debt-to-equity 60:40 to 70:30)
  - Government grants and cost-share
  - Loan guarantees
  - Green bonds
  - Carbon removal purchase agreements

### 1.5.3 Financial Metrics
- **Key Performance Indicators**
  - Levelized cost of CO₂ captured ($/tCO₂)
  - Net present value (NPV)
  - Internal rate of return (IRR)
  - Payback period
  - Debt service coverage ratio

## 1.6 Risk Assessment

### 1.6.1 Technical Risks
- Technology performance below design specifications
- Integration challenges with existing processes
- Equipment reliability and availability
- Scaling from pilot to commercial
- **Mitigation**: Proven technologies, vendor guarantees, pilot testing

### 1.6.2 Economic Risks
- Carbon price volatility
- Energy cost increases
- Capital cost overruns
- Operating cost underestimation
- **Mitigation**: Long-term contracts, hedging, conservative estimates

### 1.6.3 Regulatory Risks
- Permitting delays
- Changing regulations
- Liability concerns
- **Mitigation**: Early engagement, flexible designs, insurance/indemnity

### 1.6.4 Social Risks
- Public opposition
- Community concerns
- Environmental justice issues
- **Mitigation**: Transparent communication, benefit-sharing, grievance mechanisms

## 1.7 Phase 1 Deliverables

### Required Documentation
1. **Feasibility Study Report**
   - Emission source characterization
   - Technology selection justification
   - Site assessment results
   - Preliminary engineering design
   - Cost estimates and financial analysis
   - Risk assessment and mitigation plans

2. **Environmental Assessment**
   - Baseline conditions
   - Impact predictions
   - Mitigation measures
   - Public consultation summary

3. **Regulatory Strategy**
   - Permit identification
   - Application timelines
   - Stakeholder engagement plan

4. **Business Case**
   - Financial projections
   - Sensitivity analysis
   - Financing strategy
   - Decision recommendation

### Decision Gate
**Go/No-Go Criteria:**
- Technical feasibility confirmed
- Site suitability validated
- Economic viability demonstrated (NPV > 0 under reasonable assumptions)
- Regulatory pathway clear
- Stakeholder support sufficient
- Risk profile acceptable

**Proceed to Phase 2:** Detailed engineering and permitting
