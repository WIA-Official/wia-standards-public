# WIA-ENE-003 Phase 2: Detailed Engineering and Permitting

## Overview
Phase 2 translates the feasibility study into detailed engineering designs and secures all necessary permits for construction and operation.

## 2.1 Front-End Engineering Design (FEED)

### 2.1.1 Capture System Design
- **Process Flow Diagrams (PFDs)**
  - Mass and energy balances
  - Operating conditions (T, P, flow rates)
  - Major equipment specifications
  - Utility requirements
  - Control philosophy

- **Piping and Instrumentation Diagrams (P&IDs)**
  - Detailed equipment connections
  - Instrumentation and control points
  - Safety systems and interlocks
  - Material specifications

- **Equipment Specifications**
  - Absorber/stripper columns (diameter, height, packing)
  - Heat exchangers (duty, area, materials)
  - Pumps and compressors (capacity, head, power)
  - Vessels and tanks (volume, pressure rating)
  - Instrumentation (type, range, accuracy)

### 2.1.2 CO₂ Compression and Conditioning
- **Multi-Stage Compression**
  - Inlet conditions: near-atmospheric, 95-99% CO₂
  - Target pressure: 100-150 bar (supercritical)
  - Interstage cooling and condensate removal
  - Power requirements: 100-150 kWh/tCO₂

- **Dehydration**
  - Target: <50 ppm H₂O (prevent corrosion, hydrates)
  - Technologies: glycol absorption, membrane drying, desiccants
  - Regeneration systems

- **Purification (if required)**
  - Remove H₂S, SO₂, NOx to meet storage/utilization specs
  - Activated carbon, caustic scrubbing, or selective membranes

### 2.1.3 Transport System Design
- **Pipeline Design**
  - Diameter: based on flow rate and allowable pressure drop
    - 10-50 cm for 0.5-5 Mt/year
  - Material: carbon steel with corrosion allowance or corrosion-resistant alloys
  - Pressure rating: 1.5x maximum operating pressure
  - Route optimization: minimize length, avoid sensitive areas
  - Valve stations: every 20-50 km for sectioning
  - Pig launchers/receivers for cleaning and inspection

- **Safety Systems**
  - Pressure relief devices
  - Leak detection (fiber optic, acoustic, visual)
  - Emergency shutdown valves
  - Cathodic protection (corrosion prevention)

### 2.1.4 Injection Well Design
- **Well Configuration**
  - Vertical or directional drilling
  - Depth to injection zone: 800-3000m
  - Multiple casing strings (surface, intermediate, production)
  - Cement to surface for containment integrity
  - Tubing for CO₂ injection with packer isolation

- **Injection Parameters**
  - Rate: 0.5-1.5 Mt/year per well
  - Bottomhole pressure: below fracture gradient (typically <90% fracture pressure)
  - Injection temperature: typically 20-50°C

- **Well Materials**
  - CO₂-resistant cement formulations
  - Corrosion-resistant alloys for tubing (13Cr, duplex stainless)
  - Wellhead rated for supercritical CO₂ service

## 2.2 Detailed Engineering

### 2.2.1 Civil and Structural
- **Foundations**
  - Equipment foundations sized for loads and seismic
  - Pile foundations if poor soil conditions
  - Concrete specifications (strength, durability)

- **Structures**
  - Buildings for electrical, control, maintenance
  - Equipment platforms and access
  - Pipe racks and cable trays

### 2.2.2 Electrical and Instrumentation
- **Power Distribution**
  - Transformers and switchgear
  - Motor control centers
  - Backup power (UPS, generators)
  - Grounding and lightning protection

- **Control Systems**
  - Distributed control system (DCS) or programmable logic controllers (PLCs)
  - Human-machine interface (HMI)
  - Supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) for remote monitoring
  - Safety instrumented systems (SIS) for critical shutdowns

- **Instrumentation**
  - Flow: Coriolis, ultrasonic, differential pressure
  - Temperature: thermocouples, RTDs
  - Pressure: gauge, differential, absolute
  - Composition: infrared, mass spectrometry, gas chromatography
  - Level: radar, ultrasonic, float

### 2.2.3 Safety and Environmental
- **Process Safety**
  - Hazard and operability (HAZOP) studies
  - Layers of protection analysis (LOPA)
  - Safety integrity level (SIL) determination
  - Emergency response planning

- **Fire Protection**
  - Detection systems (flame, smoke, heat)
  - Suppression systems (water deluge, foam, CO₂)
  - Fire walls and barriers
  - Emergency egress

- **Environmental Controls**
  - Emissions monitoring (CO₂ slip, criteria pollutants)
  - Wastewater treatment
  - Chemical containment and spill response
  - Noise control

## 2.3 Permitting and Approvals

### 2.3.1 Capture Facility Permits
- **Air Quality Permits**
  - Prevention of significant deterioration (PSD)
  - Title V operating permit
  - New source performance standards (NSPS)
  - CO₂ capture efficiency requirements

- **Industrial Permits**
  - Building permits
  - Electrical and mechanical permits
  - Chemical storage permits
  - Fire marshal approval

### 2.3.2 Transport Permits
- **Pipeline Permits**
  - Federal: if crossing federal lands, navigable waters
  - State: public utility commission or equivalent
  - Local: county/municipal for rights-of-way
  - Environmental: stream crossings, wetlands (U.S. Army Corps of Engineers Section 404, etc.)
  - Cultural resources: Section 106 (U.S.) or equivalent

### 2.3.3 Storage Permits
- **Underground Injection Control (UIC) Class VI**
  - Site characterization report
    - Geology and hydrogeology
    - Caprock integrity
    - Seismic analysis
    - Baseline water quality
  
  - Area of review (AoR) delineation
    - Computational modeling of CO₂ plume
    - Identification of potential leakage pathways
    - Corrective action for abandoned wells
  
  - Injection and monitoring plan
    - Injection pressures and rates
    - Monitoring well locations
    - MRV protocols per WIA-ENE-003
  
  - Financial responsibility
    - Plugging and abandonment
    - Post-injection site care (PISC) for 50 years
    - Emergency and remedial response

- **Offshore Permits (if applicable)**
  - Seabed lease
  - Marine environment impact assessment
  - Navigation and fisheries consultation

## 2.4 Procurement and Contracting

### 2.4.1 Contracting Strategy
- **Engineering, Procurement, Construction (EPC)**
  - Lump-sum turnkey: fixed price, single point responsibility
  - Reimbursable: cost-plus-fee, more flexibility
  - Hybrid: fixed price for engineered equipment, reimbursable for site work

- **Owner-Operated vs. Third-Party Operator**
  - Owner-operated: control, but requires expertise
  - Third-party: CCUS-as-a-service, reduced risk, but higher long-term cost

### 2.4.2 Equipment Procurement
- **Long-Lead Items** (order early to maintain schedule)
  - Compressors: 12-18 months
  - Large vessels and columns: 9-15 months
  - Control systems: 6-12 months

- **Vendor Selection Criteria**
  - Technical compliance
  - Performance guarantees
  - Price competitiveness
  - Delivery schedule
  - Service and support
  - References and track record
