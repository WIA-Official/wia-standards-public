# WIA-ENE-003: Carbon Capture & Storage Standard
## PHASE 2: Detailed Design and Implementation

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Published  
**Date:** 2025-12-25  
**Philosophy:** 弘益人間 (홍익인간) - Benefit All Humanity

---

## 1. Overview

Phase 2 focuses on detailed engineering design, procurement, and preparation for construction. Duration: 12-18 months. Budget: 10-15% of CAPEX.

## 2. Front-End Engineering Design (FEED)

### 2.1 Process Design
- Detailed P&IDs (Piping and Instrumentation Diagrams)
- Mass and energy balances (±5% accuracy)
- Equipment specifications and datasheets
- Process hazard analysis (HAZOP studies)

### 2.2 Mechanical and Structural
- 3D plant model and equipment layouts
- Structural calculations and foundations
- Piping design and stress analysis
- Equipment access and maintenance planning

### 2.3 Electrical and Instrumentation
- Single-line electrical diagrams
- Power distribution and load calculations
- Control system architecture
- Safety instrumented systems (SIS) design

## 3. Procurement Strategy

### 3.1 Long-Lead Equipment
**Order Timing (months before construction):**
- Absorber/Stripper Columns: 24 months
- CO₂ Compressors: 18-24 months
- Heat Exchangers: 12-18 months
- Main Transformers: 12-15 months
- Control Systems: 9-12 months

### 3.2 Contracting Approach
- EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction) vs. EPCM
- Competitive bidding (3-5 vendors)
- Vendor qualification and pre-qualification
- Total cost of ownership analysis

## 4. Regulatory Permitting

### 4.1 Permit Applications
- Submit all major permits
- Respond to regulatory comments
- Public consultation process
- Environmental impact statement

### 4.2 Storage Site Permitting (EPA Class VI)
- Submit UIC permit application
- Area of Review (AoR) delineation
- Monitoring and verification plan
- Financial assurance mechanisms
- Expected approval: 24-36 months

## 5. Detailed Cost Estimate

**Accuracy Target:** ±10%

**Cost Breakdown:**
- Equipment and materials: 50-60%
- Labor and construction: 25-30%
- Engineering and management: 10-15%
- Contingency: 10-15%

## 6. Project Financing

### 6.1 Financing Sources
- Equity (30-40%)
- Debt (50-60%)
- Government grants (0-20%)
- Strategic partners

### 6.2 Financial Close Requirements
- All permits in hand or line-of-sight
- EPC contract signed
- Offtake agreements secured
- Board approval

## 7. Phase 2 Deliverables

- Complete engineering package (100% design)
- All major permits submitted or approved
- EPC contract ready for execution
- Detailed cost estimate (±10%)
- Financial close achieved
- Construction execution plan

## 8. Go/No-Go Decision

**Proceed to Phase 3 if:**
- ✓ All critical permits approved or on track
- ✓ Financing secured
- ✓ EPC contract negotiated
- ✓ Cost estimate within budget (±10%)
- ✓ No major technical issues identified
- ✓ Project NPV positive with reasonable assumptions
