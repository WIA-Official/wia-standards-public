5.1 Operational Excellence

The WIA-ENE-003 standard emphasizes operational excellence through systematic approaches to process optimization, maintenance management, and continuous improvement. These best practices are derived from over 100 facility-years of operational experience across diverse industrial applications.

Daily Operations Management

✓ Best Practice: Shift Handover Protocol

Implementation: Structured 15-minute handover meetings between operating shifts

Key Elements:

  • Review of previous shift performance metrics (capture rate, energy consumption, uptime)
  • Status of ongoing maintenance activities and equipment limitations
  • Pending process changes or operational adjustments
  • Safety observations and near-miss incidents
  • Environmental monitoring results and any exceedances

Impact: 25% reduction in communication-related incidents, improved response time to process upsets

Solvent Circulation Rate Continuous ±5% of design Check pump performance, system pressure

5.2 Preventive Maintenance Programs

Maintenance Strategy Framework

A comprehensive maintenance program is essential for achieving >95% availability targets. The WIA-ENE-003 standard recommends a risk-based approach combining preventive, predictive, and reliability-centered maintenance.

MAINTENANCE STRATEGY BY EQUIPMENT CRITICALITY: CRITICAL EQUIPMENT (Failure stops production): ├─ CO₂ Compressors ├─ Main Solvent Circulation Pumps ├─ Reboiler Steam System └─ Emergency Shutdown Systems Strategy: ├─ Redundancy: 2×100% or 3×50% capacity ├─ Preventive Maintenance: Monthly inspections ├─ Predictive Maintenance: Continuous vibration monitoring, oil analysis ├─ Spare Parts: Critical spares on-site (impellers, seals, bearings) └─ Response Time: <4 hours to restore operation IMPORTANT EQUIPMENT (Failure causes performance degradation): ├─ Heat Exchangers ├─ Cooling Water Pumps ├─ Instrumentation & Control Systems └─ Water Treatment Systems Strategy: ├─ Redundancy: 2×60% capacity typical ├─ Preventive Maintenance: Quarterly inspections ├─ Predictive Maintenance: Performance trending ├─ Spare Parts: 30-day lead time acceptable └─ Response Time: <24 hours STANDARD EQUIPMENT (Failure has minimal impact): ├─ Secondary Pumps ├─ Utility Systems ├─ Lighting and HVAC └─ Non-critical Instrumentation Strategy: ├─ Minimal Redundancy ├─ Preventive Maintenance: Annual inspections ├─ Run-to-failure acceptable for some items └─ Response Time: <1 week

Solvent Management Best Practices

✓ Best Practice: Proactive Solvent Quality Management

Monitoring Schedule:

  • Daily: pH, color visual inspection, foam tendency test
  • Weekly: Amine concentration by titration, heat stable salts (HSS) screening
  • Monthly: Full composition analysis, degradation products quantification
  • Quarterly: Comprehensive laboratory analysis including corrosion inhibitor levels

Solvent Reclaiming: Implement on-site reclaiming when HSS >2% by weight (reduces makeup solvent costs by 60-70%)

Expected Consumption: 0.5-1.5 kg solvent makeup per tonne CO₂ captured (well-managed system)

Solvent Degradation Indicator Acceptable Range Action Level Corrective Action
Amine Concentration 28-32 wt% <27% or >33% Add fresh solvent or water adjustment
Heat Stable Salts (HSS) <1 wt% >2 wt% Initiate solvent reclaiming
Degradation Products <0.5 wt% >1 wt% Investigate SOx/NOx ingress, improve gas cleanup
Iron Content <100 ppm >200 ppm Check for corrosion, enhance inhibitor dosing
Foaming Tendency Foam height <20mm (standard test) >40mm Add antifoam agent, investigate contamination

5.3 Energy Efficiency Optimization

Heat Integration Strategies

Energy consumption for CO₂ capture typically represents 60-70% of total operating costs. Systematic heat integration can reduce specific energy consumption by 15-25%.

✓ Best Practice: Multi-Level Heat Recovery

Level 1: Rich/Lean Heat Exchange

  • Target 85-90% heat recovery efficiency
  • Use plate-and-frame or shell-and-tube exchangers
  • Regular cleaning to maintain performance (6-12 month intervals)
  • Energy savings: 0.6-0.8 GJ/tonne CO₂

Level 2: Flue Gas Heat Recovery

  • Cool flue gas from 120°C to 40°C, recover 80-100 MWth for 1 Mt/year facility
  • Use recovered heat for solvent regeneration or district heating
  • Energy savings: 0.3-0.5 GJ/tonne CO₂

Level 3: Compression Heat Recovery

  • Recover intercooler heat (40-60°C) for building heating or process uses
  • Energy savings: 0.1-0.2 GJ/tonne CO₂

Total Potential Savings: 1.0-1.5 GJ/tonne CO₂ (25-35% reduction in total energy)

Advanced Process Control

PROCESS CONTROL OPTIMIZATION STRATEGIES: 1. Model Predictive Control (MPC) for Absorber ├─ Inputs: Flue gas flow, CO₂ concentration, temperature ├─ Manipulated Variables: Solvent flow rate, pump speed ├─ Constraints: Minimum capture rate, maximum energy └─ Benefits: 3-5% energy savings, improved stability 2. Real-time Optimization (RTO) ├─ Economic objective function: Minimize $/tonne CO₂ ├─ Consider electricity prices, steam costs, carbon credits ├─ Adjust operating setpoints every 15-30 minutes └─ Benefits: 5-8% reduction in operating costs 3. Advanced Solvent Flow Control ├─ Dynamic adjustment based on flue gas composition ├─ Lean loading optimization (balance capture rate vs energy) ├─ Automatic compensation for fouling and degradation └─ Benefits: 2-4% energy savings, extended equipment life 4. Predictive Maintenance Scheduling ├─ Machine learning models predict equipment failures ├─ Schedule maintenance during low-demand periods ├─ Minimize unplanned downtime └─ Benefits: 2-3% improvement in availability IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP: Year 1: Basic PID control optimization Year 2: Implement MPC for critical loops Year 3: Deploy facility-wide RTO Year 4: Add AI/ML for predictive analytics

5.4 Safety and Emergency Response

Safety Culture Development

✓ Best Practice: Behavior-Based Safety Program

Key Components:

  • Safety Observations: Each employee conducts 2 safety observations per month
  • Near-Miss Reporting: Encourage reporting with non-punitive policy (target: 10+ reports per month per 100 employees)
  • Safety Meetings: 15-minute toolbox talks at start of each shift
  • Incident Investigation: Root cause analysis for all incidents using "5 Whys" methodology
  • Safety Metrics: Track Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR), Lost Time Injury Frequency (LTIF)

Target Performance: TRIR <0.5, LTIF <0.2 (world-class performance)

5.5 Environmental Stewardship

Emissions Minimization

While carbon capture dramatically reduces CO₂ emissions, attention must be paid to other environmental impacts:

✓ Best Practice: Comprehensive Emissions Management

Solvent Emissions Control:

  • Install water wash column above absorber to capture amine slip (<1 ppm in treated gas)
  • Monitor for degradation products (nitrosamines, nitramines) quarterly
  • Annual emissions: <10 kg amine per 1000 tonnes CO₂ captured

Ammonia Emissions (from Degradation):

  • Optimize solvent chemistry to minimize ammonia formation
  • Consider acid wash if NH₃ >10 ppm in vent gas
  • Target: <5 ppm NH₃ in treated flue gas

Wastewater Management:

  • Treat condensate and wash water before discharge (pH adjustment, organic removal)
  • Zero liquid discharge (ZLD) options for sensitive locations
  • Compliance with local discharge limits (typically COD <100 mg/L, pH 6-9)

5.7 Workforce Development and Training

Competency-Based Training Program

✓ Best Practice: Structured Operator Training

Training Curriculum (6-month program):

  • Month 1-2: Fundamentals of carbon capture technology, process chemistry, safety procedures
  • Month 3-4: Equipment operation, process control, troubleshooting basics
  • Month 5: Simulator training (normal operations, startups, shutdowns)
  • Month 6: Advanced topics (emergency response, optimization, environmental compliance)

Certification Requirements:

  • Written examination (80% pass score)
  • Practical skills assessment in plant
  • Simulator-based emergency scenario tests
  • Annual recertification and refresher training

Cross-training: All operators trained on multiple process areas to enable flexible staffing

5.8 Stakeholder Engagement

Community Relations Best Practices

Successful carbon capture projects maintain strong relationships with local communities and stakeholders, embodying the principle of 弘益人間 (홍익인간) - benefiting all people.

Stakeholder Engagement Framework

Pre-Construction Phase:

  • Public information sessions explaining technology and benefits
  • One-on-one meetings with neighboring property owners
  • Environmental impact assessment with public comment period
  • Establishment of community liaison committee

Construction Phase:

  • Monthly construction updates via newsletter and website
  • 24/7 hotline for noise/traffic complaints
  • Local hiring preferences and contractor requirements
  • Traffic management plan with community input

Operations Phase:

  • Annual facility tours for community members and students
  • Quarterly community liaison committee meetings
  • Real-time environmental monitoring data published online
  • Community investment program (local charities, education)
  • Emergency preparedness information and drills
