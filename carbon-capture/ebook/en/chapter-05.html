Chapter 5: Safety and Operational Security

Learning Objectives: Master critical safety protocols for carbon capture operations including pressure system management, chemical handling procedures, emergency response planning, and continuous monitoring requirements per WIA-CARBON_CAPTURE specifications.

Safety Overview

Carbon capture facilities involve handling of chemicals at elevated temperatures and pressures, compressed gases, and rotating equipment. A comprehensive safety management system is essential to protect personnel, the public, and the environment. This chapter outlines the safety requirements aligned with WIA-CARBON_CAPTURE standards and international best practices.

Carbon Dioxide Hazards

While CO2 is not flammable or toxic in the traditional sense, it presents significant asphyxiation hazards and can cause serious injury or death in confined spaces or areas of accumulation.

CO2 Concentration Effects Exposure Limit
0.04% (400 ppm) Normal atmospheric level Background
0.5% (5,000 ppm) Increased breathing rate 8-hour TWA limit
1.5% (15,000 ppm) Mild respiratory stimulation 15-min STEL
4% (40,000 ppm) Headache, dizziness, confusion IDLH level
10% (100,000 ppm) Loss of consciousness in minutes Life-threatening
20%+ (200,000 ppm) Rapid unconsciousness, death Fatal
Critical Safety Note: CO2 is heavier than air (density 1.98 kg/m³ vs 1.2 kg/m³ for air) and accumulates in low-lying areas, trenches, and confined spaces. Fixed and portable CO2 monitors are essential in all areas where CO2 may accumulate.

Pressure System Safety

Carbon capture facilities include equipment operating at elevated pressures, particularly in the CO2 compression and transport systems. Pressure safety is managed through proper design, inspection, and relief systems.

// Relief Valve Sizing
function sizeReliefValve(
    scenario: string,
    relievingRate: number,      // kg/hr
    setPresure: number,         // barg
    backPressure: number,       // barg
    temperature: number         // °C
): ReliefValveSpec {
    // API 520 sizing methodology
    const molecularWeight = 44.01;  // CO2
    const compressibility = 0.85;

    const requiredArea = relievingRate / (
        51.45 * setPresure * Math.sqrt(molecularWeight / (temperature + 273))
    );

    return {
        scenario,
        orificeSize: selectStandardOrifice(requiredArea),
        setPresure,
        capacity: relievingRate
    };
}

Summary

Safety is paramount in carbon capture operations. A comprehensive safety management system encompassing hazard identification, risk assessment, engineered safeguards, monitoring, emergency response, and management of change ensures that facilities operate safely while meeting environmental objectives. The WIA-CARBON_CAPTURE standard integrates these safety requirements into all aspects of carbon capture implementation.
