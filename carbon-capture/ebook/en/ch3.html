Technical Architecture
WIA-ENE-003 Carbon Capture & Storage Standard

3.1 System Architecture Overview

The WIA-ENE-003 standard defines a comprehensive technical architecture for carbon capture and storage systems. This architecture encompasses capture facilities, transport infrastructure, storage sites, and integrated monitoring systems, all connected through standardized interfaces and data protocols.

3.2 Capture Subsystem Architecture

Post-Combustion Capture System

The most widely deployed architecture, suitable for retrofitting existing facilities:

Component Function Key Parameters Performance Metrics
Direct Contact Cooler (DCC) Cools flue gas to optimal absorption temperature 40-60°C outlet, water spray ±2°C temperature control
Blower Overcomes pressure drop through absorber 10-20 kPa boost, variable speed Energy: 0.1-0.2 GJ/tonne CO₂
Absorber Column CO₂ absorption into solvent 20-40m height, structured packing 85-90% capture efficiency
Rich/Lean Heat Exchanger Heat recovery between streams 80-90% heat recovery efficiency Energy savings: 15-20%
Stripper Column CO₂ release from solvent 100-120°C, 1.8-2.0 bar 95-99% solvent regeneration
Reboiler Provides heat for regeneration Steam at 2.5-3.5 bar Energy: 2.5-4.0 GJ/tonne CO₂
Condenser Removes water from CO₂ stream Cooling to 35-45°C <500 ppm H₂O in CO₂

Process Flow Dynamics

Material Balance - 1 MtCO₂/year Facility: INPUTS: ├─ Flue Gas: 4,500,000 Nm³/hr (12% CO₂, 75% N₂, 6% H₂O, 7% O₂) ├─ Solvent: 30% MEA solution, 3,500 m³ inventory ├─ Steam: 450 tonnes/hr at 3 bar └─ Cooling Water: 25,000 m³/hr PROCESS: ├─ CO₂ in Flue Gas: 540 tonnes/hr ├─ CO₂ Captured: 470 tonnes/hr (87% capture rate) ├─ CO₂ Emissions: 70 tonnes/hr (stack) ├─ Solvent Circulation: 12,000 m³/hr └─ Solvent Makeup: 2 tonnes/day (degradation) OUTPUTS: ├─ Pure CO₂: 470 tonnes/hr (99.5% purity) ├─ Treated Flue Gas: 4,450,000 Nm³/hr ├─ Waste Heat: 380 MW thermal └─ Condensate: 185 m³/hr ENERGY CONSUMPTION: ├─ Reboiler: 1,600 MWth ├─ Blower: 12 MWe ├─ Pumps: 8 MWe ├─ Cooling: 4 MWe └─ Total: 3.7 GJ/tonne CO₂

3.3 Compression and Dehydration Architecture

After capture, CO₂ must be compressed to supercritical state for efficient transport and injection:

Stage Pressure In Pressure Out Temperature Purpose
Vacuum Compression 0.5 bar 2 bar 30°C Initial compression from stripper
Dehydration Unit 2 bar 2 bar 25°C Remove water to <50 ppm
Low Pressure Compression 2 bar 10 bar 40-50°C Multi-stage compression
Intermediate Cooling 10 bar 10 bar 35°C Reduce temperature before final stage
High Pressure Compression 10 bar 110 bar 60°C Achieve supercritical state
Final Cooling 110 bar 110 bar 31°C Dense phase for transport

Compressor Configuration

Typical Compression Train - 1 MtCO₂/year

Type: Centrifugal compressors (4-5 stages) with intercoolers

Power Requirement: 25-35 MWe total

Specific Energy: 0.4-0.6 GJ/tonne CO₂

Redundancy: 2×60% capacity for reliability

Control: Variable speed drives, anti-surge protection

3.9 Integration with External Systems

WIA-ENE-003 systems must integrate with various external platforms:

  • Grid Management Systems: Coordinate CCS operations with renewable energy availability
  • Carbon Trading Platforms: Automated carbon credit registration and trading
  • Regulatory Reporting Systems: Direct submission of compliance data to authorities
  • Energy Management Systems: Optimize energy consumption across industrial site
  • Supply Chain Systems: Track solvent, materials, and maintenance inventory
  • Weather Services: Anticipate operational impacts from weather conditions

3.10 Scalability and Modularity

The WIA-ENE-003 architecture supports scalable deployment from pilot projects to industrial-scale facilities:

Modular Deployment Approach

Pilot Scale: 10,000-100,000 tonnes CO₂/year - Single capture train, basic monitoring

Demonstration Scale: 100,000-500,000 tonnes/year - Multiple trains, comprehensive monitoring

Commercial Scale: 500,000-2,000,000 tonnes/year - Full redundancy, advanced analytics

Hub Scale: 2-10 million tonnes/year - Multiple facilities, shared infrastructure

Each scale can be implemented independently and expanded incrementally, following the principle of 弘益人間 (홍익인간) by making carbon capture accessible to organizations of all sizes.

Chapter Summary

This chapter presented the comprehensive technical architecture defined by WIA-ENE-003, covering capture subsystems, compression and dehydration, transport infrastructure, storage site design, monitoring systems, data management, safety systems, and external integrations. The architecture is designed to be scalable, modular, and interoperable, enabling deployment across diverse industrial contexts while maintaining consistent performance, safety, and environmental standards.
