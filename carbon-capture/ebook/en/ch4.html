Implementation Guide
WIA-ENE-003 Carbon Capture & Storage Standard

4.1 Implementation Phases Overview

The WIA-ENE-003 standard defines a structured four-phase approach to implementing carbon capture and storage systems. This methodology has been validated across 15 pilot facilities and ensures systematic deployment while managing technical and financial risks.

Phase Duration Key Activities Budget Allocation Success Criteria
Phase 1: Foundation 6-12 months Feasibility study, site selection, initial design 5-10% of CAPEX Approved business case, permits initiated
Phase 2: Detailed Design 12-18 months Engineering, procurement planning, permitting 10-15% of CAPEX Final design, contracts ready, permits secured
Phase 3: Construction 24-36 months Equipment installation, commissioning, testing 70-80% of CAPEX Operational readiness, safety certification
Phase 4: Operation & Optimization 20-30 years Operations, maintenance, continuous improvement OPEX ongoing Performance targets met, compliance maintained

4.2 Phase 1: Foundation and Feasibility

Step 1: Initial Assessment

1Emission Source Characterization

Conduct comprehensive analysis of CO₂ emission sources:

// Emission Source Assessment Checklist EMISSION SOURCE DATA: ✓ Annual CO₂ emissions (tonnes/year) ✓ CO₂ concentration in flue gas (%) ✓ Flue gas volume and temperature ✓ Impurities (SOx, NOx, particulates, heavy metals) ✓ Seasonal/operational variability ✓ Future production forecasts (next 20 years) FACILITY INFRASTRUCTURE: ✓ Available footprint for capture equipment ✓ Steam/heat availability (for regeneration) ✓ Cooling water availability ✓ Electrical power capacity and reliability ✓ Access for equipment delivery and maintenance ✓ Integration points with existing processes EXAMPLE - Coal Power Plant (600 MW): ├─ Annual CO₂: 3.5 million tonnes ├─ Flue Gas: 12% CO₂, 75% N₂, 6% H₂O, 7% O₂ ├─ Flow Rate: 2,500,000 Nm³/hr at 120°C ├─ Impurities: 150 ppm SOx, 200 ppm NOx ├─ Steam Available: 850 tonnes/hr at 3 bar ├─ Capture Target: 85% (3.0 Mt CO₂/year) └─ Required Footprint: 4-6 hectares

Step 2: Technology Selection

2Evaluate Capture Technologies

Select appropriate technology based on source characteristics and constraints:

Source Type Recommended Technology Rationale Estimated Cost
Coal Power Plant Post-combustion (Amine) Proven, retrofit-capable, 85-90% capture $60-100/tonne CO₂
Natural Gas Combined Cycle Post-combustion or Oxy-fuel Lower CO₂ concentration, high efficiency needed $50-80/tonne CO₂
Cement Plant Post-combustion (Advanced solvents) High process emissions, retrofit required $70-120/tonne CO₂
Steel Mill Post-combustion or Pre-combustion (for DRI) Variable CO₂ sources, complex integration $80-130/tonne CO₂
Hydrogen Production Pre-combustion (SMR + CCS) High CO₂ concentration, existing pressure $40-70/tonne CO₂

Step 3: Storage Site Evaluation

3Geological Assessment

Identify and evaluate potential CO₂ storage sites within economical transport distance:

STORAGE SITE SCREENING CRITERIA: REQUIRED CHARACTERISTICS: ✓ Depth: 800-3,000 meters (optimal pressure/temperature) ✓ Porosity: >10% (storage capacity) ✓ Permeability: >10 mD (injection capability) ✓ Cap Rock: Low permeability seal (< 1 nD) ✓ Seal Integrity: No faults penetrating cap rock ✓ Capacity: >20 years of CO₂ storage at planned injection rate DATA COLLECTION: 1. Existing Geological Data ├─ Oil/gas well logs ├─ Seismic survey data ├─ Core samples └─ Production history (if depleted reservoir) 2. Characterization Wells (2-4 wells) ├─ Geological sampling ├─ Formation pressure/temperature ├─ Water chemistry analysis ├─ Injectivity testing └─ Cost: $3-8 million per well 3. 3D Seismic Survey ├─ Formation geometry and extent ├─ Fault identification ├─ Cap rock continuity └─ Cost: $2-5 million for 100 km² CAPACITY CALCULATION: Storage Capacity (tonnes) = Formation Volume (m³) × Porosity (%) × CO₂ Density (kg/m³) × Efficiency Factor (0.02-0.04) Example - Saline Aquifer: ├─ Area: 50 km² × 50m thickness = 2,500 million m³ ├─ Porosity: 25% ├─ CO₂ Density: 700 kg/m³ at reservoir conditions ├─ Efficiency: 3% └─ Capacity: 2,500M × 0.25 × 700 × 0.03 = 13.1 Mt CO₂

4.6 Integration with Existing Facilities

Retrofitting carbon capture to existing industrial facilities requires careful integration planning to minimize operational disruptions and maintain production capacity.

Retrofit Integration Strategies

Parallel Construction: Build capture facility while existing plant operates normally. Connect during planned outage (2-4 weeks).

Phased Implementation: Capture 30-50% of emissions initially, expand to 85-90% in subsequent phases.

Modular Approach: Use pre-fabricated modules to reduce on-site construction time by 30-40%.

Energy Supply: Ensure adequate steam/power supply. May require auxiliary boiler or power purchase agreement.

These approaches embody 弘益人間 (홍익인간) by enabling emission reductions without compromising industrial productivity.

Chapter Summary

This chapter provided a comprehensive step-by-step implementation guide for carbon capture projects following the WIA-ENE-003 standard. We covered the four implementation phases: Foundation and Feasibility (site selection, technology choice, economic analysis), Detailed Design and Engineering (FEED, procurement), Construction and Commissioning (site preparation, installation, testing), and Operations and Optimization (SOPs, performance improvement). Each phase includes specific activities, timelines, success criteria, and practical examples from real-world projects. Following this structured approach ensures successful deployment while managing technical, financial, and regulatory risks.
