8.1 Technology Evolution Roadmap

The carbon capture and storage industry is rapidly evolving, driven by technological innovation, policy support, and urgent climate imperatives. This chapter outlines the expected trajectory of CCS technology development through 2050 and beyond.

Near-Term Developments (2025-2030)

2025-2027

Advanced Solvent Deployment:

  • Next-generation amine blends reducing energy consumption by 20-30%
  • Water-lean solvents (e.g., phase-change solvents) reaching commercial scale
  • Target: 2.5-3.0 GJ/tonne CO₂ (down from current 3.5-4.5)
  • 10+ commercial demonstrations worldwide

Modular Capture Systems:

  • Factory-built, containerized capture units (50-500 tonnes CO₂/day)
  • 50% reduction in construction time and 30% lower CAPEX
  • Enables smaller facilities and distributed capture networks
2027-2030

Direct Air Capture Scale-up:

  • DAC costs declining from $300-600/tonne to $150-250/tonne
  • First gigaton-scale DAC facilities (1+ Mt CO₂/year)
  • Integration with geothermal and renewable energy for low-carbon operation
  • Critical for achieving net-negative emissions

AI-Optimized Operations:

  • Machine learning models reducing operating costs by 10-15%
  • Autonomous control systems minimizing human intervention
  • Predictive maintenance extending equipment life by 20-30%
  • Digital twins enabling real-time optimization at facility and network scales

8.3 Policy and Regulatory Evolution

Expected Policy Developments

Carbon Pricing Trajectory

2025-2030:

  • Carbon border adjustment mechanisms (CBAM) expand beyond EU to US, Japan, Korea
  • Explicit carbon prices reach $50-100/tonne in developed economies
  • Aviation and maritime sectors included in emissions trading schemes
  • Enhanced tax credits: US 45Q increased to $100/tonne, similar programs in Asia-Pacific

2030-2040:

  • Global carbon price floor established at $75/tonne (IMF proposal)
  • CCS mandates for new industrial facilities >100,000 tonnes CO₂/year
  • Negative emissions certificates tradable separately from conventional carbon credits
  • Carbon pricing revenue recycled into CCS infrastructure development

2040-2050:

  • Carbon prices $150-250/tonne, making most CCS economically viable without subsidies
  • Net-zero requirements for all new industrial developments
  • Liability frameworks mature for long-term storage stewardship
  • International agreements on cross-border CO₂ transport and storage

Breakthrough Technologies Under Development

Quantum Computing for CCS:

  • Real-time reservoir optimization for injection strategies
  • System-wide network optimization across hundreds of facilities
  • Timeline: Practical applications by 2035-2040

Mineralization Acceleration:

  • Ex-situ mineralization producing valuable building materials

8.9 Societal Transformation Vision

Beyond Technology: Cultural and Societal Change

The successful deployment of carbon capture at the required scale demands more than technological innovation—it requires a fundamental shift in how society views industrial emissions and climate responsibility.

Vision for 2050: Carbon-Conscious Civilization

Universal Carbon Literacy:

  • Carbon footprint education integrated into primary and secondary curricula worldwide
  • Real-time carbon accounting visible for all products and services
  • Personal carbon budgets tracked via smartphone apps (voluntary initially, incentivized)
  • Result: Informed consumers driving low-carbon innovation

Carbon-Neutral as Default:

  • All new infrastructure carbon-neutral or carbon-negative by design
  • Carbon capture co-located with every major emission source
  • Cities achieving net-negative emissions through integrated urban CCS networks
  • Result: Emissions become choice, not default

Intergenerational Responsibility:

  • Legal frameworks recognizing climate obligations to future generations
  • Long-term storage stewardship viewed as civilizational duty
  • Carbon removal as reparations for historical emissions (climate justice)
  • Result: Ethical imperative driving action beyond economics

Conclusion: A Future Worth Building

The WIA-ENE-003 standard provides the technical foundation for deploying carbon capture and storage at the scale required to address the climate crisis. But standards, technologies, and infrastructure alone are insufficient. Success requires collective action grounded in shared values and common purpose.

The principle of 弘益인간 (홍익인간) - broadly benefiting humanity - must guide our path forward. Carbon capture is not merely a technological solution; it is a commitment to future generations, an act of global solidarity, and a demonstration that human ingenuity can solve the problems we create.

By 2050, carbon capture can transition from an emerging technology to a cornerstone of sustainable civilization. Industrial facilities will capture CO₂ as routinely as they manage wastewater. Cities will be carbon-negative, actively removing historical emissions. International cooperation on climate will be normalized, transcending borders and politics.

This future is achievable, but only with sustained commitment from all stakeholders. The roadmap is clear, the technology is ready, and the urgency is undeniable. The question is not whether carbon capture can contribute to climate solutions, but whether we will deploy it with the speed and scale required.

Let us build this future together. Let us benefit all humanity. 弘익인간.
