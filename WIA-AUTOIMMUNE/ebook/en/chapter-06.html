Chapter 6: Digital Health Integration

Digital health technologies offer unprecedented opportunities to improve autoimmune disease management through enhanced monitoring, better data sharing, and patient empowerment. This chapter establishes WIA-AUTOIMMUNE standards for digital health integration in autoimmune care.

6.1 Electronic Health Record Standards

EHR Requirements for Autoimmune Care
=====================================
Structured Data Elements:
├── Diagnosis codes (ICD-10, SNOMED-CT)
├── Disease activity scores (standardized)
├── Laboratory results (LOINC codes)
├── Medications (RxNorm)
├── Imaging results
└── Patient-reported outcomes

Interoperability:
├── FHIR R4 compliance
├── HL7 CDA support
├── API access for authorized apps
└── Consent management

Disease-Specific Templates:
├── RA: DAS28, joint counts, X-ray scoring
├── SLE: SLEDAI, organ involvement checklist
├── MS: EDSS, MRI tracking
└── IBD: Mayo score, endoscopy findings

6.3 Wearable Device Integration

Supported Wearable Data Types

  • Activity Tracking: Step count, mobility patterns, exercise
  • Sleep Monitoring: Duration, quality, disturbance patterns
  • Heart Rate: Resting HR, HR variability (inflammation proxy)
  • Skin Temperature: Potential flare detection
  • Joint Motion: Range of motion sensors for RA

6.4 Telemedicine Protocols

Telehealth Visit Standards
===========================
Appropriate Uses:
├── Follow-up stable patients
├── Medication management
├── Lab result review
├── Education and counseling
├── Triage and assessment
└── Care coordination

Technical Requirements:
├── HIPAA-compliant platform
├── Video and audio capability
├── Screen sharing for images
├── EHR integration
└── Prescription capability

Documentation:
├── Visit type clearly indicated
├── Patient location documented
├── Technical issues noted
├── Physical exam limitations stated
└── Follow-up plan including in-person needs

6.6 AI and Decision Support

Clinical Decision Support Standards
====================================
Permitted Applications:
├── Drug interaction checking
├── Dosing calculators
├── Disease activity scoring
├── Risk stratification
└── Guideline-based recommendations

Requirements:
├── Transparent algorithms
├── Clinical validation studies
├── Provider override capability
├── Regular algorithm updates
└── Bias testing and mitigation

Prohibited:
├── Autonomous treatment decisions
├── Diagnosis without provider review
└── Unvalidated predictive models

6.7 Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM)

RPM Program Standards
======================
Eligible Patients:
├── Stable disease requiring ongoing monitoring
├── High-risk patients (frequent flares)
├── Post-treatment initiation surveillance
├── Geographic barriers to in-person care
└── Patient preference with capability

Data Collection Requirements:
├── Daily: Symptoms, medication adherence
├── Weekly: Disease activity PROs
├── Continuous: Wearable data streams
├── Triggered: Flare questionnaires
└── Monthly: Comprehensive assessment

Clinical Integration:
├── Dashboard for care team review
├── Automated alert escalation
├── Protocol-driven responses
├── Documentation in EHR
└── Reimbursement compliance (CPT 99453-99458)

Digital Biomarker Implementation

Digital biomarkers represent a frontier in autoimmune disease monitoring, offering continuous, objective data collection in real-world settings. While promising, these measures require rigorous validation before clinical deployment. WIA-AUTOIMMUNE supports research in this area while maintaining standards for clinical use of only validated measures.

6.9 Patient Portal Standards

Patient Portal Requirements
============================

├── Lab result viewing (with context)

├── Appointment scheduling
├── Secure messaging
├── Educational resources
└── PRO completion

Autoimmune-Specific Features:
├── Disease activity tracking graphs
├── Flare reporting tools
├── Medication reminders
├── Side effect logging

Accessibility Requirements:

├── Mobile-responsive design
├── Multiple language support
├── Health literacy appropriate content
├── Screen reader compatibility
