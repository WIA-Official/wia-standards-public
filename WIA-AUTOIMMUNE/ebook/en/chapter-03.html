Chapter 3: Treatment Protocols

Treatment of autoimmune diseases aims to control inflammation, prevent organ damage, maintain quality of life, and achieve disease remission when possible. This chapter establishes WIA-AUTOIMMUNE standards for therapeutic approaches, from conventional DMARDs to advanced biologics.

3.1 Treatment Principles

Treatment Strategy Framework
============================
Goals of Therapy:
├── Control disease activity
├── Prevent structural damage
├── Preserve organ function
├── Minimize treatment toxicity
├── Maintain quality of life
└── Achieve remission or low disease activity

Treatment Approach:
├── Treat-to-Target strategy
├── Early aggressive intervention
├── Regular disease activity assessment
├── Therapy adjustment based on response
└── Shared decision-making with patient

3.4 Treatment Algorithms

RA Treatment Algorithm (Treat-to-Target)

Phase 1: Initial DMARD Therapy
├── Start MTX within 3 months of diagnosis
├── Bridging glucocorticoids if needed
├── Assess at 3 months
└── Target: Low disease activity (DAS28 ≤3.2)

Phase 2: Inadequate Response
├── Optimize MTX dose (up to 25mg/week)
├── Add/switch conventional DMARD
├── Reassess at 3-6 months
└── Consider poor prognostic factors

Phase 3: Biologic Addition
├── Add bDMARD (TNFi first line)
├── Continue background MTX
├── Assess response at 3 months
└── Switch mechanism if inadequate

Phase 4: Refractory Disease
├── Try different biologic class
├── Consider JAK inhibitor
├── Multidisciplinary consultation
└── Evaluate for alternative diagnosis

3.6 Glucocorticoid Management

Glucocorticoid Use Standards
=============================
Indications:
├── Acute flare management
├── Bridge to DMARD effect
├── Severe organ involvement
└── Refractory disease

Dosing Principles:
├── Use lowest effective dose
├── Shortest possible duration
├── Taper protocols (no abrupt stops)
└── Document cumulative exposure

Monitoring for Adverse Effects:
├── Bone density (DEXA baseline, periodic)
├── Glucose monitoring
├── Blood pressure
├── Ophthalmologic exams (cataracts, glaucoma)
├── Weight and cushingoid features
└── Infection surveillance

3.7 Response Assessment

  • ACR Response Criteria (RA): ACR20/50/70 improvement
  • DAS28: Disease Activity Score (remission <2.6)
  • SLEDAI: SLE Disease Activity Index
  • BILAG: British Isles Lupus Assessment Group
  • Patient-Reported Outcomes: HAQ, SF-36, FACIT-Fatigue

3.8 Combination Therapy Strategies

Combination DMARD Protocols
============================
Triple Therapy (csDMARD):
├── MTX + Sulfasalazine + Hydroxychloroquine
├── Efficacy comparable to TNFi in some studies
├── Cost-effective option
└── Step-up from MTX monotherapy failure

Biologic + csDMARD:
├── bDMARD + MTX (standard of care)
├── Improved efficacy vs monotherapy
├── Reduced immunogenicity
├── MTX dose can be lower (10-15mg/week)
└── Alternative csDMARDs if MTX contraindicated

Dual Biologic Therapy:
├── Generally NOT recommended
├── Increased infection risk
├── Limited efficacy benefit
├── Research settings only
└── Exception: Rituximab + Belimumab in refractory SLE

Biosimilar Transition Protocol

When transitioning patients from originator to biosimilar, ensure clear communication about the reason for change, expected outcomes, and reporting of any perceived differences. Nocebo effects can be minimized through proper patient education and shared decision-making.

3.10 Treatment Adherence Optimization

Adherence Enhancement Strategies
=================================
Assessment Methods:
├── Direct questioning (non-judgmental)
├── Prescription refill monitoring
├── Drug levels (where available)
├── Electronic monitoring devices
└── Patient-reported adherence scales

Intervention Strategies:
├── Simplify regimens (once daily, combination pills)
├── Address barriers (cost, side effects, beliefs)
├── Reminder systems (apps, alarms, pill organizers)
├── Motivational interviewing
├── Shared decision-making
└── Regular follow-up and support

High-Risk Populations:
├── Complex multi-drug regimens
├── Cognitive impairment
├── Depression/mental health issues
├── Limited health literacy
├── Financial constraints
└── Lack of social support
