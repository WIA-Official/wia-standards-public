Chapter 5: Patient Monitoring

Ongoing monitoring is essential for managing autoimmune diseases, detecting disease activity changes, identifying complications, and ensuring treatment safety. This chapter establishes WIA-AUTOIMMUNE standards for systematic patient monitoring across conditions.

5.1 Disease Activity Assessment

Validated Assessment Tools
===========================
Rheumatoid Arthritis:
├── DAS28 (Disease Activity Score)
├── CDAI (Clinical Disease Activity Index)
├── SDAI (Simplified Disease Activity Index)
└── Boolean remission criteria

Systemic Lupus:
├── SLEDAI-2K (SLE Disease Activity Index)
├── BILAG-2004 (British Isles Lupus Assessment)
├── PGA (Physician Global Assessment)
└── SLICC/ACR Damage Index

Multiple Sclerosis:
├── EDSS (Expanded Disability Status Scale)
├── MSFC (MS Functional Composite)
├── MRI lesion activity
└── Annualized relapse rate

5.3 Flare Recognition and Management

Flare Identification Criteria

  • Worsening of symptoms beyond normal variation
  • Objective signs of increased disease activity
  • Rising inflammatory markers
  • New organ involvement or damage
  • Patient-reported functional decline
Flare Management Protocol
==========================
Assessment:
├── Confirm flare vs. infection vs. other cause
├── Quantify severity (mild/moderate/severe)
├── Check medication adherence
└── Identify potential triggers

Response Options:
├── Mild: Optimize current therapy, NSAIDs
├── Moderate: Short-term glucocorticoids, dose adjustment
├── Severe: High-dose steroids, hospital admission
└── Refractory: Therapy change, specialist consultation

5.5 Pregnancy Planning and Monitoring

Pregnancy Considerations
=========================
Pre-conception:
├── Disease control (remission >6 months ideal)
├── Medication review (discontinue teratogens)
├── Folic acid supplementation
└── Rheumatology/high-risk OB consultation

Safe Medications:
├── Hydroxychloroquine (continue)
├── Sulfasalazine (continue with folate)
├── Azathioprine (acceptable if needed)
├── Low-dose prednisone (if necessary)
└── Certolizumab (minimal placental transfer)

Contraindicated:
├── Methotrexate (teratogenic)
├── Leflunomide (long washout needed)
├── Mycophenolate (high risk)
├── Cyclophosphamide
└── JAK inhibitors

5.6 Long-term Surveillance

  • Organ Damage Assessment: Annual review of cumulative damage using validated indices
  • Functional Status: HAQ-DI, SF-36, or disease-specific measures
  • Treatment Toxicity: Cumulative glucocorticoid exposure, drug-specific monitoring
  • Quality of Life: Patient-reported outcomes assessment

5.7 Imaging Monitoring Standards

Imaging Surveillance Protocols
===============================
Rheumatoid Arthritis:
├── Baseline: X-rays hands/feet, consider US/MRI
├── Progression monitoring: Annual X-rays
├── US for synovitis assessment (treat-to-target)
├── MRI for early detection, erosion monitoring
└── Chest X-ray baseline, CT if ILD suspected

SLE:
├── Echocardiogram if cardiac symptoms/risk
├── Chest imaging for pulmonary involvement
├── MRI brain for neuropsychiatric symptoms
├── Renal ultrasound for nephritis
└── DEXA for osteoporosis screening

Multiple Sclerosis:
├── Baseline MRI brain and spine
├── Annual MRI (or per protocol)
├── New lesions indicate disease activity
├── Brain volume measurement (atrophy)
└── OCT for retinal nerve fiber monitoring

Digital Self-Monitoring Tools

WIA-AUTOIMMUNE recommends validated digital tools for patient self-monitoring. These should integrate with EHR systems, provide automated alert thresholds, and support patient-provider communication. Regular self-monitoring empowers patients and enables early detection of disease flares, potentially preventing organ damage and reducing healthcare utilization.

5.9 Alert Thresholds and Escalation

Clinical Alert Framework
=========================
Red Flags (Immediate Action):
├── Fever with neutropenia on immunosuppression
├── Severe infection symptoms
├── New neurological deficits
├── Chest pain, dyspnea (cardiac/pulmonary)
├── Severe abdominal pain
└── Signs of anaphylaxis post-biologic

Amber Alerts (Urgent Review 24-48h):
├── Significant disease activity increase
├── Moderate infection symptoms
├── Lab abnormalities outside safe range
├── Medication side effects (non-severe)
└── Patient-reported significant worsening

Monitoring Response Protocol:
├── Red: Same-day assessment, ER if needed
├── Amber: Next available appointment
├── Routine: Scheduled follow-up
└── Document all alerts and responses
