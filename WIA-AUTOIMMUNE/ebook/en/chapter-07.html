Chapter 7: Research and Clinical Trials

Standardized data collection and research methodologies are essential for advancing autoimmune disease understanding and developing new therapies. This chapter establishes WIA-AUTOIMMUNE standards for research data, biobanking, and clinical trial conduct.

7.1 Data Standards for Research

Research Data Requirements
===========================
Core Data Elements (All Studies):
├── Demographics (age, sex, race/ethnicity)
├── Disease diagnosis and classification criteria
├── Disease duration and activity measures
├── Current and past medications
├── Comorbidities
└── Outcomes (disease-specific and PROs)

Extended Data Elements:
├── Biomarker panels
├── Genetic data (HLA, risk variants)
├── Imaging data
├── Microbiome data
└── Environmental exposures

Data Standards:
├── CDISC (Clinical Data Interchange Standards)
├── OMOP CDM (Observational Medical Outcomes)
├── FHIR for EHR integration
└── SNOMED-CT for clinical terms

7.3 Clinical Trial Endpoints

Recommended Primary Endpoints by Disease

Rheumatoid Arthritis:
├── ACR20/50/70 response
├── DAS28 remission/LDA
└── Radiographic progression (erosion score)

SLE:
├── SRI (SLE Responder Index)
├── BICLA (BILAG-based Composite Lupus Assessment)
└── Renal response (proteinuria reduction)

Multiple Sclerosis:
├── Annualized relapse rate
├── Confirmed disability worsening
├── MRI lesion activity (T2, Gd+)
└── Brain volume loss

7.4 Real-World Evidence Standards

RWE Study Requirements
=======================
Data Sources:
├── Electronic health records
├── Claims databases
├── Patient registries

└── Patient-reported outcomes

Quality Requirements:
├── Data provenance documentation
├── Missing data handling plan
├── Confounding adjustment methods
├── Sensitivity analyses
└── Transparent reporting (RECORD)

Acceptable Uses:
├── Comparative effectiveness
├── Long-term safety surveillance

7.5 Patient Involvement in Research

  • Research Prioritization: Patient advisory boards for study design
  • Consent Processes: Plain language, ongoing consent for longitudinal studies
  • Results Communication: Lay summaries, return of individual results when appropriate
  • Participatory Research: Patient co-investigators, citizen science

7.7 Biomarker Discovery Protocols

Biomarker Research Standards
=============================
Discovery Phase:
├── Multi-omics approach (genomics, proteomics, metabolomics)
├── Large, well-characterized cohorts
├── Standardized sample collection
├── Blinded analysis
└── Statistical rigor (multiple testing correction)

Validation Requirements:
├── Independent replication cohort
├── Different populations/ethnicities
├── Longitudinal validation
├── Clinical utility assessment
├── Analytical validation (assay performance)

Types of Biomarkers:
├── Diagnostic: Distinguish disease from non-disease
├── Prognostic: Predict disease course
├── Predictive: Predict treatment response
├── Pharmacodynamic: Monitor drug effect
└── Safety: Detect adverse events

Genetic Data Governance

Genomic data requires special consideration for consent (broad vs. specific), return of results (incidental findings), data security (de-identification challenges), and sharing (controlled access repositories). WIA-AUTOIMMUNE aligns with GA4GH (Global Alliance for Genomics and Health) standards for responsible genomic data sharing.

7.9 Multi-Center Collaboration Standards

Collaborative Research Framework
=================================
Governance Structure:
├── Steering committee with all site representation
├── Data access committee (independent)
├── Publication committee
├── Conflict of interest management
└── Clear IP and commercialization agreements

Data Harmonization:
├── Common data elements (CDEs)
├── Central data coordinating center
├── Regular data quality audits
├── Cross-site validation studies
└── Federated analysis options

Resource Sharing:
├── Biospecimen access protocols
├── Material transfer agreements
├── Authorship guidelines (ICMJE)
├── Data sharing timelines
└── Attribution requirements

Quality Assurance:
├── Central IRB where feasible
├── Site certification/training
├── Protocol compliance monitoring
├── Adverse event reporting
└── Regular investigator meetings
