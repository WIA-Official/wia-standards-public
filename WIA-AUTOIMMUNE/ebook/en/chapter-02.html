Chapter 2: Diagnostic Standards

Accurate diagnosis of autoimmune diseases is challenging due to overlapping symptoms, variable disease presentations, and the need for specialized laboratory testing. This chapter establishes WIA-AUTOIMMUNE standards for diagnostic criteria, laboratory testing, and clinical assessment protocols.

2.1 Diagnostic Approach Framework

Diagnostic Algorithm
=====================
Step 1: Clinical Suspicion
├── History of characteristic symptoms
├── Family history of autoimmunity
├── Physical examination findings
└── Pattern recognition (organ involvement)

Step 2: Initial Laboratory Screening
├── Complete blood count with differential
├── Comprehensive metabolic panel
├── Inflammatory markers (ESR, CRP)
├── Antinuclear antibody (ANA) screen
└── Organ-specific function tests

Step 3: Confirmatory Testing
├── Specific autoantibody panels
├── Imaging studies (if indicated)
├── Tissue biopsy (when necessary)
└── Specialist consultation

Step 4: Classification and Staging
├── Apply validated criteria
├── Assess disease activity
├── Document organ involvement
└── Identify comorbidities

2.3 Classification Criteria

WIA-AUTOIMMUNE mandates use of validated classification criteria developed by international societies:

SLE Classification (ACR/EULAR 2019)

Entry criterion: ANA ≥1:80. Additive scoring system across domains:

  • Constitutional (fever): 2 points
  • Hematologic (cytopenia, hemolysis): 1-4 points
  • Neuropsychiatric (delirium, psychosis, seizure): 2-5 points
  • Mucocutaneous (oral ulcers, alopecia, rash): 2-6 points
  • Serosal (effusion): 5-6 points
  • Musculoskeletal (joint involvement): 6 points
  • Renal (proteinuria, biopsy class): 4-10 points
  • Immunology (antibodies, complement): 2-10 points

Classification: ≥10 points = SLE

2.6 Differential Diagnosis

Common Diagnostic Challenges
=============================
Symptom Overlap:
├── Fatigue → Nearly all autoimmune conditions
├── Joint pain → RA, SLE, psoriatic arthritis
├── Rash → SLE, dermatomyositis, psoriasis
└── Fever → SLE, vasculitis, adult-onset Still's

Rule Out Mimics:
├── Infections (viral, bacterial, fungal)
├── Malignancy (lymphoma, leukemia)
├── Drug-induced syndromes
├── Primary immunodeficiency
└── Fibromyalgia/chronic fatigue

2.7 Documentation Requirements

  • Criteria met for classification
  • Disease activity assessment score

2.8 Disease Activity Assessment

Validated Activity Indices

Standardized disease activity assessment is crucial for monitoring treatment response and guiding therapeutic decisions:

  • SLEDAI-2K (SLE): 24 weighted clinical and laboratory parameters
  • DAS28 (Rheumatoid Arthritis): 28 joint assessment with ESR/CRP
  • BVAS (Vasculitis): Birmingham Vasculitis Activity Score
  • CDAI (Inflammatory Bowel Disease): Crohn's Disease Activity Index
  • PASI (Psoriasis): Psoriasis Area and Severity Index

2.11 Genetic Testing Guidelines

HLA Typing Indications

Genetic testing supports diagnosis and risk stratification in specific clinical scenarios:

  • HLA-B27: Ankylosing spondylitis, reactive arthritis (prevalence 90% in AS)
  • HLA-DR4: Rheumatoid arthritis susceptibility and severity marker
  • HLA-DQ2/DQ8: Celiac disease (99% carry one allele)
  • HLA-B51: Behçet's disease association

WIA-AUTOIMMUNE recommends genetic counseling for patients undergoing HLA testing.
