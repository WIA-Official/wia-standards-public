Chapter 5: Capacity Assessment for Consent

Evaluating Decision-Making Ability in BCI Contexts - WIA-BCI_CONSENT v1.0

5.1 The Concept of Consent Capacity

Consent capacity refers to the cognitive ability to provide legally and ethically valid informed consent. Unlike physical ability to sign a document, capacity involves complex cognitive functions including understanding information, appreciating its relevance to one's situation, reasoning about options, and expressing a choice. The WIA-BCI_CONSENT standard establishes rigorous capacity assessment requirements that acknowledge both the complexity of BCI technology and the diverse cognitive abilities of potential users.

BCI consent capacity presents unique challenges not encountered in other medical contexts. First, many potential BCI users have neurological conditions that may affect decision-making capacity - yet these same conditions may be the target of BCI treatment, creating a circular complexity. Second, BCI technology may itself affect the cognitive processes required for consent, raising questions about whether consent obtained before device activation remains valid after the device begins to influence brain function. Third, the complexity and novelty of BCI technology may exceed the cognitive demands of typical medical consent.

5.2 Capacity Assessment Methods

The WIA-BCI_CONSENT standard requires formal capacity assessment using validated instruments, conducted by qualified assessors. The assessment must be documented thoroughly and must be specific to the BCI consent decision at hand, not a general determination of overall capacity.

5.2.1 Standardized Assessment Instruments

Several validated instruments can be adapted for BCI consent capacity assessment. The standard provides guidance on their appropriate use and interpretation in the BCI context.

Instrument Components Assessed BCI Adaptation Typical Duration
MacCAT-CR Understanding, Appreciation, Reasoning, Choice BCI-specific vignettes 20-30 minutes
UBACC Brief understanding and appreciation BCI terminology added 10-15 minutes
Capacity to Consent Tool All four components BCI decision scenarios 30-45 minutes
SICIATRI Decision-making process Extended for BCI complexity 45-60 minutes
WIA-BCI-CAP BCI-specific capacity domains Purpose-built for BCI 60-90 minutes

5.2.2 The WIA-BCI-CAP Assessment Protocol

The WIA-BCI-CAP (WIA BCI Consent Assessment Protocol) is a purpose-built assessment tool developed specifically for BCI consent capacity evaluation. It incorporates traditional capacity assessment with BCI-specific domains. 

class WIABCICapacityAssessment:
    """
    WIA-BCI-CAP: Standardized capacity assessment for BCI consent
    """

    def __init__(self, assessor: QualifiedAssessor):
        self.assessor = assessor
        self.assessment_record = []
        self.scoring_rubric = self._load_scoring_rubric()

    def conduct_assessment(self, user: BCICandidate, bci_type: str) -> CapacityResult:
        """
        Full WIA-BCI-CAP assessment protocol
        """
        results = {}

        # Module 1: Understanding Assessment
        results["understanding"] = self._assess_understanding(user, bci_type)

        # Module 2: Appreciation Assessment
        results["appreciation"] = self._assess_appreciation(user, bci_type)

        # Module 3: Reasoning Assessment
        results["reasoning"] = self._assess_reasoning(user, bci_type)

        # Module 4: Expression Assessment
        results["expression"] = self._assess_expression(user)

        # Module 5: BCI-Specific Domains
        results["bci_specific"] = self._assess_bci_specific_capacity(user, bci_type)

        # Calculate overall capacity determination
        capacity_determination = self._determine_capacity(results, bci_type)

        return CapacityResult(
            user_id=user.id,
            assessment_date=datetime.utcnow(),
            assessor=self.assessor,
            domain_scores=results,
            overall_determination=capacity_determination,
            recommendations=self._generate_recommendations(results),
            reassessment_timeline=self._determine_reassessment_need(results)
        )

    def _assess_understanding(self, user: BCICandidate, bci_type: str) -> DomainScore:

            },
            {
                "id": "U2",
                "question": "What is the main purpose of this BCI for you?",
                "scoring": "0-2 scale based on personalization and accuracy",
                "threshold": 1.5
            },
            {
                "id": "U3",
                "question": "What are the main risks of this BCI?",
                "scoring": "0-2 scale based on risk identification",
                "threshold": 1.5
            },
            {
                "id": "U4",
                "question": "What happens to your brain signals/data?",
                "scoring": "0-2 scale based on data flow understanding",
                "threshold": 1.5
            },
            {
                "id": "U5",
                "question": "What alternatives do you have to this BCI?",
                "scoring": "0-2 scale based on alternative awareness",
                "threshold": 1.0
            }

            domain="understanding",
            item_scores=scores,
            total_score=sum(s["score"] for s in scores),
            max_score=10,
            threshold=7.5,
            passed=sum(s["score"] for s in scores) >= 7.5
        )

5.3 Capacity Thresholds and Proportionality

The WIA-BCI_CONSENT standard adopts a proportional approach to capacity thresholds: the required level of capacity increases with the gravity of the decision. Higher-risk BCI interventions require demonstration of higher capacity levels, while lower-risk applications may proceed with lower capacity thresholds.

Proportional Capacity Thresholds

BCI Risk Level Understanding Threshold Appreciation Threshold Reasoning Threshold Overall Required
Low (Consumer EEG) 70% 65% 60% 65%
Moderate (Medical monitoring) 75% 75% 70% 73%
High (Non-invasive stim) 80% 80% 75% 78%
Very High (Implanted device) 85% 85% 80% 83%
Maximum (Bidirectional implant) 90% 90% 85% 88%

5.4 Capacity Enhancement and Accommodation

When initial capacity assessment reveals deficits, the WIA-BCI_CONSENT standard requires attempts at capacity enhancement before concluding that an individual lacks capacity. Many apparent capacity deficits can be addressed through appropriate accommodations and support.

5.5 Surrogate Consent for Incapacitated Individuals

When an individual lacks capacity to provide consent despite enhancement efforts, the WIA-BCI_CONSENT standard provides for surrogate decision-making under strict conditions. Surrogate consent for BCI is more restrictive than for other medical interventions due to the unique implications for cognitive liberty and personal identity.

Surrogate Consent Requirements

  • Surrogate Hierarchy: Legally appointed guardian, healthcare proxy, spouse/partner, adult child, parent, adult sibling, other close relative or friend
  • Decision Standard: Substituted judgment (what the person would have wanted) takes priority; best interests used only when preferences unknown
  • Limitations on Surrogate Authority: Surrogates cannot consent to experimental BCI, enhancement-only BCI, or BCI with significant identity-altering potential
  • Ethics Committee Review: Required for all surrogate consent decisions involving implanted BCI
  • Regular Capacity Re-assessment: If capacity may be restored, regular re-assessment required with transition to direct consent
  • Assent Requirement: Even when surrogate provides consent, the individual's assent should be sought and resistance respected
class SurrogateConsentProtocol:
    """
    WIA-BCI_CONSENT protocol for surrogate decision-making
    """

    SURROGATE_LIMITATIONS = {
        "prohibited_decisions": [
            "experimental_bci_participation",
            "enhancement_only_bci",
            "significant_identity_alteration_risk",
            "non_therapeutic_neural_recording"
        ],
        "requires_ethics_review": [
            "any_implanted_bci",
            "therapeutic_neuromodulation",
            "extended_neural_monitoring"
        ],
        "requires_court_approval": [
            "permanent_implant_in_minor",
            "contested_surrogate_decision",
            "conflict_of_interest_present"
        ]
    }

    def validate_surrogate_consent(
        self,
        surrogate: SurrogateDecisionMaker,
        user: BCICandidate,
        bci_intervention: BCIIntervention
    ) -> SurrogateValidation:
        """
        Validate surrogate consent meets WIA-BCI_CONSENT requirements
        """
        validations = []

        # Verify surrogate authority
        authority_check = self._verify_surrogate_authority(surrogate, user)
        validations.append(authority_check)

        # Check if intervention is within surrogate authority
        scope_check = self._check_intervention_scope(
            bci_intervention,
            self.SURROGATE_LIMITATIONS
        )
        validations.append(scope_check)

        # Verify decision standard applied
        standard_check = self._verify_decision_standard(
            surrogate.decision_rationale,
            user.known_preferences
        )
        validations.append(standard_check)

        # Check ethics review if required
        if bci_intervention.type in self.SURROGATE_LIMITATIONS["requires_ethics_review"]:
            ethics_check = self._verify_ethics_review(bci_intervention)
            validations.append(ethics_check)

        # Verify assent process
        assent_check = self._verify_assent_attempt(user, bci_intervention)
        validations.append(assent_check)

        return SurrogateValidation(
            all_valid=all(v.passed for v in validations),
            validation_details=validations,
            recommendations=self._generate_recommendations(validations)
        )

Key Takeaways - Chapter 5

  • Four Components: Consent capacity requires understanding, appreciation, reasoning, and expression of choice - all must be assessed.
  • Decision-Specific: Capacity assessment must be specific to the BCI decision, not a general determination of overall capacity.
  • Proportional Thresholds: Required capacity levels increase with the risk and gravity of the BCI intervention.
  • Enhancement First: Before concluding incapacity, appropriate accommodations and supports must be attempted.
  • BCI-Specific Domains: Assessment must include BCI-specific capacity domains such as temporal reasoning and identity awareness.
  • Surrogate Limitations: Surrogate consent authority is more restricted for BCI than other medical decisions due to cognitive liberty implications.
