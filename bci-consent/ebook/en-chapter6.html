Chapter 6: Ongoing Consent Management

Dynamic Consent Systems for Long-Term BCI Use - WIA-BCI_CONSENT v1.0

6.1 The Continuous Nature of BCI Consent

Unlike a single surgical procedure where consent is obtained once, Brain-Computer Interface use represents an ongoing relationship between user, device, and service providers that may span decades. The WIA-BCI_CONSENT standard recognizes that consent for BCI technology must be managed as a continuous process, adapting to changing circumstances, technological updates, new scientific understanding, and evolving user preferences and values.

Traditional informed consent models treat consent as a discrete event - information is provided, understanding is verified, and a signature is obtained. This event-based model is fundamentally inadequate for BCI technology where the nature of the intervention may change over time through software updates, where new risks may be discovered through post-market surveillance, where the user's cognitive state and values may shift, and where the technology's capabilities may expand or contract. Dynamic consent management addresses these challenges through ongoing engagement and systematic renewal processes.

6.2 Consent Renewal Requirements

The WIA-BCI_CONSENT standard mandates periodic consent renewal at intervals determined by the risk level of the BCI system. Renewal is not merely a formality but a substantive re-engagement with the consent process that includes updated disclosure, reassessment of understanding, and reaffirmation of authorization.

BCI Category Standard Renewal Minimum Renewal Renewal Scope Format
Consumer EEG Annual Every 2 years Privacy and data use Electronic
Medical Monitoring Every 6 months Annual Full risk review In-person or video
Therapeutic Stimulation Every 3 months Every 6 months Comprehensive In-person required
Implanted Recording Monthly Quarterly Full reassessment In-person required
Bidirectional Implant Monthly Monthly Comprehensive + identity In-person required

6.2.1 Renewal Process Protocol

Each consent renewal follows a structured protocol designed to ensure meaningful re-engagement rather than perfunctory acknowledgment. 

class ConsentRenewalProcess:
    """
    WIA-BCI_CONSENT compliant renewal protocol
    """

    def conduct_renewal(self, user: BCIUser, consent_record: ConsentRecord) -> RenewalResult:
        """
        Execute comprehensive consent renewal process
        """
        renewal_steps = []

        # Step 1: Pre-Renewal Assessment
        pre_assessment = self._conduct_pre_renewal_assessment(user, consent_record)
        renewal_steps.append(("pre_assessment", pre_assessment))

        # Step 2: Updated Disclosure
        disclosure_updates = self._prepare_disclosure_updates(consent_record)
        disclosure_result = self._deliver_updated_disclosure(user, disclosure_updates)
        renewal_steps.append(("disclosure_update", disclosure_result))

        # Step 3: Experience Review
        experience_review = self._review_user_experience(user, consent_record)
        renewal_steps.append(("experience_review", experience_review))

        # Step 4: Comprehension Reassessment
        comprehension = self._reassess_comprehension(user, disclosure_updates)
        renewal_steps.append(("comprehension", comprehension))

        # Step 5: Preference Check
        preference_changes = self._check_preference_changes(user)
        renewal_steps.append(("preferences", preference_changes))

        # Step 6: Consent Modification Opportunity
        modifications = self._offer_consent_modifications(user, consent_record)
        renewal_steps.append(("modifications", modifications))

        # Step 7: Reauthorization
        reauthorization = self._obtain_reauthorization(user, modifications)
        renewal_steps.append(("reauthorization", reauthorization))

        return RenewalResult(
            user_id=user.id,
            renewal_date=datetime.utcnow(),
            steps_completed=renewal_steps,
            consent_continued=reauthorization.approved,
            modifications_made=modifications.changes,
            next_renewal_due=self._calculate_next_renewal(user)
        )

    def _prepare_disclosure_updates(self, consent_record: ConsentRecord) -> DisclosureUpdates:
        """
        Compile all updates requiring disclosure since last consent
        """
        since_date = consent_record.last_renewal_date

        updates = DisclosureUpdates(
            new_risks_identified=self._get_new_risks(since_date),
            software_updates_applied=self._get_software_changes(since_date),
            policy_changes=self._get_policy_changes(since_date),
            new_research_findings=self._get_relevant_research(since_date),
            regulatory_changes=self._get_regulatory_updates(since_date),
            data_practice_changes=self._get_data_changes(since_date)
        )

        return updates

    def _review_user_experience(self, user: BCIUser, consent_record: ConsentRecord) -> ExperienceReview:
        """
        Structured review of user's experience since last renewal
        """
        review_domains = {
            "adverse_events": self._query_adverse_events(user),
            "benefit_realization": self._assess_benefit_achievement(user),
            "side_effects": self._document_side_effects(user),
            "satisfaction": self._measure_satisfaction(user),
            "concerns": self._elicit_concerns(user),
            "questions": self._address_questions(user)
        }

        return ExperienceReview(
            domains=review_domains,
            overall_assessment=self._synthesize_experience(review_domains),
            recommendations=self._generate_experience_recommendations(review_domains)
        )

6.3 Trigger-Based Reconsent

Beyond scheduled renewals, certain events trigger immediate reconsent requirements. These triggers recognize that significant changes may occur between scheduled renewals that fundamentally alter the consent landscape and require immediate user re-engagement.

Reconsent Triggers

  • Safety Triggers: New safety signal identified, adverse event reported, device malfunction detected, recall or safety notice issued
  • Functionality Triggers: Major software update, new capability enabled, algorithm change affecting outputs, hardware modification
  • Data Triggers: New data use proposed, third-party sharing request, data breach affecting user, policy change
  • User Triggers: User requests review, significant health change, capacity concern raised, user complaint
  • Regulatory Triggers: New legal requirements, change in device classification, mandatory disclosure requirement
  • Research Triggers: New evidence of risk, new evidence of benefit, change in scientific consensus
class ReconssentTriggerMonitor:
    """
    Monitors for events requiring immediate reconsent
    """

    TRIGGER_DEFINITIONS = {
        "SAFETY_SIGNAL": {
            "description": "New safety concern identified",
            "severity": "HIGH",
            "response_timeline": "24 hours",
            "notification_method": "immediate_direct_contact",
            "operation_impact": "may_require_pause"
        },
        "MAJOR_UPDATE": {
            "description": "Significant software or algorithm change",
            "severity": "MEDIUM",
            "response_timeline": "7 days",
            "notification_method": "multi_channel",
            "operation_impact": "update_pending_consent"
        },
        "DATA_PRACTICE_CHANGE": {
            "description": "Change to data collection or use practices",
            "severity": "MEDIUM",
            "response_timeline": "14 days",
            "notification_method": "email_with_acknowledgment",
            "operation_impact": "new_practices_pending_consent"
        },
        "NEW_CAPABILITY": {
            "description": "Additional functionality available",
            "severity": "LOW",
            "response_timeline": "30 days",
            "notification_method": "standard_notification",
            "operation_impact": "capability_pending_consent"
        },
        "REGULATORY_CHANGE": {
            "description": "New legal or regulatory requirement",
            "severity": "VARIES",
            "response_timeline": "as_required",
            "notification_method": "formal_notice",
            "operation_impact": "compliance_dependent"
        }
    }

    def __init__(self, user: BCIUser, consent_record: ConsentRecord):
        self.user = user
        self.consent_record = consent_record
        self.active_monitors = []
        self._initialize_monitors()

    def check_triggers(self) -> List[TriggerEvent]:
        """
        Check all trigger conditions and return any activated triggers
        """
        activated_triggers = []

        for monitor in self.active_monitors:
            if trigger := monitor.check():
                activated_triggers.append(trigger)
                self._log_trigger_activation(trigger)

        return activated_triggers

    def handle_trigger(self, trigger: TriggerEvent) -> TriggerResponse:
        """
        Execute appropriate response to triggered event
        """
        trigger_def = self.TRIGGER_DEFINITIONS[trigger.type]

        # Determine if operation impact required
        if trigger_def["operation_impact"] == "may_require_pause":
            self._initiate_pause_assessment(trigger)

        # Send notification
        notification = self._send_trigger_notification(
            self.user,
            trigger,
            trigger_def["notification_method"]
        )

        # Schedule reconsent
        reconsent_deadline = datetime.utcnow() + self._parse_timeline(
            trigger_def["response_timeline"]
        )

        reconsent_request = self._create_reconsent_request(
            trigger=trigger,
            deadline=reconsent_deadline,
            scope=self._determine_reconsent_scope(trigger)
        )

        return TriggerResponse(
            trigger=trigger,
            notification_sent=notification,
            reconsent_required=reconsent_request,
            operation_impact=trigger_def["operation_impact"]
        )

6.4 Consent Dashboard and User Control

The WIA-BCI_CONSENT standard requires that BCI systems provide users with a consent dashboard - a user interface that provides visibility into current consent status, control over consent settings, and access to consent-related information and functions.

Required Dashboard Features

  • Consent Status Overview: Current consent state for all permissions, with expiration dates and renewal schedule
  • Permission Controls: Ability to modify individual permissions, enable/disable data sharing, adjust access levels
  • History View: Complete record of consent decisions, modifications, and renewals
  • Data Visibility: Information about what data has been collected and how it has been used
  • Notification Preferences: Control over how and when consent-related communications are received
  • Withdrawal Options: Clear, accessible pathway to withdraw consent partially or completely
  • Support Access: Direct connection to consent advocates and support resources
  • Documentation Access: Original consent documents and current policy documents
interface BCIConsentDashboard {
    // Core Status Functions
    getConsentStatus(): ConsentStatusSummary;
    getPermissionDetails(category: string): PermissionDetail[];
    getUpcomingRenewals(): RenewalSchedule[];

    // Control Functions
    modifyPermission(permission: string, newState: PermissionState): ModificationResult;
    requestTemporaryPause(duration: Duration, scope: string[]): PauseResult;
    initiateWithdrawal(scope: WithdrawalScope): WithdrawalProcess;

    // Information Functions
    getConsentHistory(): ConsentHistoryEntry[];
    getDataCollectionSummary(): DataSummary;
    getDataUsageReport(): UsageReport;
    getCurrentPolicies(): PolicyDocument[];

    // Communication Functions
    setNotificationPreferences(prefs: NotificationPreferences): void;
    contactConsentAdvocate(): ContactResult;
    submitConsentQuestion(question: string): QuestionTicket;

    // Document Functions
    downloadConsentRecord(): ConsentPackage;
    requestDataExport(): DataExportRequest;
}

class ConsentDashboardImplementation implements BCIConsentDashboard {
    private user: BCIUser;
    private consentRegistry: ConsentRegistry;
    private notificationService: NotificationService;

    constructor(user: BCIUser) {
        this.user = user;
        this.consentRegistry = ConsentRegistry.forUser(user);
        this.notificationService = new NotificationService(user);
    }

    getConsentStatus(): ConsentStatusSummary {
        const activeConsents = this.consentRegistry.getActiveConsents();
        const pendingRenewals = this.consentRegistry.getPendingRenewals();
        const recentChanges = this.consentRegistry.getRecentChanges(30); // Last 30 days

        return {
            overallStatus: this.calculateOverallStatus(activeConsents),
            categoryStatuses: this.getCategoryStatuses(activeConsents),
            nextRenewalDue: this.getNextRenewalDate(pendingRenewals),
            actionRequired: this.identifyRequiredActions(activeConsents, pendingRenewals),
            recentActivity: recentChanges,
            dataCollectionActive: this.isDataCollectionActive(),
            stimulationActive: this.isStimulationActive()
        };
    }

    modifyPermission(permission: string, newState: PermissionState): ModificationResult {
        // Verify user has authority to modify
        if (!this.canModify(permission)) {
            return { success: false, reason: "Permission modification not authorized" };
        }

        // Check if modification requires additional consent process
        if (this.requiresAdditionalConsent(permission, newState)) {
            return this.initiateModificationConsent(permission, newState);
        }

        // Apply modification
        const modification = this.consentRegistry.modifyPermission(
            this.user.id,
            permission,
            newState,
            { timestamp: new Date(), method: "dashboard" }
        );

        // Notify relevant systems
        this.notificationService.notifyPermissionChange(permission, newState);

        return {
            success: true,
            modification: modification,
            effectiveDate: modification.effectiveDate,
            reversible: true
        };
    }
}

6.5 Consent Documentation and Audit

Ongoing consent management requires comprehensive documentation and audit capabilities. The WIA-BCI_CONSENT standard mandates specific documentation practices to ensure accountability and enable verification of consent compliance throughout the BCI lifecycle.

Documentation Type Content Requirements Retention Period Audit Access
Initial Consent Record Full consent form, assessments, signatures Lifetime + 10 years User, Provider, Regulator
Renewal Records Each renewal process and outcome Lifetime + 10 years User, Provider, Regulator
Modification Log All consent changes with timestamps Lifetime + 10 years User, Provider, Regulator
Trigger Events Triggers detected and responses 10 years Provider, Regulator
User Communications All consent-related communications 10 years User, Provider
Audit Reports Periodic compliance assessments 10 years Provider, Regulator

Key Takeaways - Chapter 6

  • Continuous Process: BCI consent is managed as an ongoing relationship requiring regular renewal and responsive updates.
  • Scheduled Renewals: Consent must be renewed at intervals determined by BCI risk level, from annual to monthly.
  • Trigger-Based Reconsent: Certain events require immediate reconsent regardless of scheduled renewal dates.
  • User Dashboard: Users must have access to a dashboard providing visibility and control over their consent status.
  • Comprehensive Documentation: All consent activities must be documented with retention periods exceeding user lifetime.
  • Substantive Renewal: Renewal is a meaningful re-engagement process, not perfunctory acknowledgment.
