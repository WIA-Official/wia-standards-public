【 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY 】

The World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released the next-generation distributed system architecture standard 'WIA-COMP-009: Microservices Standard' under the MIT license for free public use. This standard provides a complete framework for building scalable, maintainable, and resilient distributed systems, including service decomposition, inter-service communication, service discovery, and circuit breakers. It dramatically reduces complexity in large-scale application development through team autonomy, independent deployment, and fault isolation.

The Monolithic Nightmare: 30-Minute Builds for One-Line Changes

[IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER_2: Problems with Monolithic Architecture]

Many enterprises in the Republic of Korea and worldwide are trapped in massive monolithic application swamps. Modifying one line of code requires a 30-minute build of the entire application, one team's deployment forces all other teams to wait, and one module's bug crashes the entire system.

Key Issues:

  • Deployment Bottleneck: Long deployment times (30 minutes to hours) for full app deployment
  • Team Conflicts: Merge hell from shared codebase
  • Technology Lock-in: Forever bound to once-chosen frameworks
  • Scalability Limits: Inefficient full app replication required
  • Failure Propagation: One module bug crashes entire system
  • Testing Complexity: Burden of testing entire application
  • New Developer Onboarding: Months to understand massive codebase
"Every development team has the right to deploy their services independently without approval from other teams. A payment service bug should not crash the entire shopping mall. Each team should choose their own technology stack, deploy dozens of times per day, and when failures occur, other services should continue operating normally. Microservices are the only way for large organizations to move quickly."
- Sangmin Park, CEO, SmileStory Inc. / WIA

WIA-COMP-009: True Distributed Systems

[IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER_3: Future of WIA Microservices Solution]

The WIA-COMP-009 standard provides a complete microservices framework realizing independent deployment, fault isolation, and team autonomy:

Core Features:

  • Service Decomposition: Domain separation based on Bounded Contexts
  • Inter-Service Communication: REST, gRPC, messaging (Kafka, RabbitMQ)
  • Service Discovery: Dynamic service registration and discovery
  • Circuit Breaker: Fault isolation and fallback patterns
  • API Gateway: Unified entry point and routing
  • Distributed Tracing: Request flow tracking and observability
  • Event-Driven: Asynchronous communication and loose coupling

About WIA

The World Certification Industry Association (WIA) is an international standards organization dedicated to researching, developing, and distributing next-generation technology standards. Based on the philosophy of 弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity), WIA releases all technology standards under the MIT license for free use by developers and enterprises worldwide.

WIA is developing over 600 standards, from Rust-based high-performance systems to the TypeScript/JavaScript ecosystem. All standards include production-ready SDKs, CLIs, and detailed documentation.

About the CEO

Sangmin Park is the founder of SmileStory Inc. and Chief Architect of WIA, leading the Republic of Korea's first one-person standards organization. As a distributed systems, microservices, and Rust expert, he personally designs over 600 WIA standards, believing "technology should be shared equally with everyone," and releases all standards for free.
