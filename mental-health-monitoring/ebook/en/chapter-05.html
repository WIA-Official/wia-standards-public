Chapter 5: Crisis Intervention & Suicide Prevention

"When the person in front of us is in danger, our interface must fail safe. Every toggle, every layout, every default — serves the one goal of getting them the right human, fast."

— WIA-MED-020, Safety-Critical Design Principles

5.1 Detecting elevated risk

Crisis detection in WIA-MED-020 combines three signal families:

  • Explicit screening. C-SSRS items, PHQ-9 item 9, and self-reported distress in EMA.
  • Behavioural change. Large deviations from personal baseline: sudden isolation, sleep collapse, late-night activity, typing-speed drop, erratic geolocation.
  • Content signals (opt-in). Text sentiment analysis of journal entries the user has marked as shareable, and the CBT app's in-session reports.

No single signal raises an alert. The risk stratifier combines signals into one of four levels — routine, elevated, acute, imminent — with explicit rules the user and their clinician can inspect. No black-box model is permitted at the stratifier layer.

5.3 Safety plan (Stanley–Brown)

Every user at elevated+ risk has a living Stanley–Brown safety plan stored locally and, with consent, mirrored to the clinician. The plan has six sections: personal warning signs, internal coping strategies, social distractions and settings, trusted people, professional contacts, means-restriction steps. The plan is edited collaboratively in-session and re-reviewed at every appointment. Notification routing into the plan opens it at the section most relevant to the current warning signs.

Regional emergency endpoints are maintained as a signed manifest:

  • 🇺🇸 United States: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline; 911.
  • 🇯🇵 Japan: いのちの電話 0120-783-556.
  • 🇪🇺 European Union: 116 123 (Samaritans); 112.
  • 🇬🇧 United Kingdom: Samaritans 116 123; 999.
  • 🇦🇺 Australia: Lifeline 13 11 14; 000.

Numbers never leave the device and work offline. The signed manifest is refreshed when network is available and is immutable once signed by the WIA safety CA.

5.5 Trusted-contact protocol

A user may, in calm moments, designate up to three trusted contacts and the precise circumstances in which the platform may reach out to them. The protocol is transparent:

  • Each trusted contact is notified at setup that they have been nominated, and by whom.
  • The user can edit or revoke at any time; the revocation is instant and propagated.
  • An outreach never shares diagnostic data, only the user's pre-authored message (e.g., "I could use some support right now").

5.6 Safe messaging guidelines

All UI copy involving crisis follows the WHO / Samaritans Safe Messaging guidelines: no depiction of method, no sensationalisation, no single-cause attribution, hope-first framing, immediate access to help. Translations are validated per-locale — again, no machine translation for life-critical copy. Designers update the copy with input from people with lived experience of suicidality, whose feedback is treated as definitive, not advisory.
