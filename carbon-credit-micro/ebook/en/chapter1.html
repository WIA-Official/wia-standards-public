Every individual action counts—from cycling to work to using renewable energy—and this standard ensures that these actions are measurable, verifiable, and economically rewarding.

1.1 What are Micro Carbon Credits?

A Micro Carbon Credit (MCC) is a fractional carbon offset unit that represents small-scale greenhouse gas emission reductions or removals. Unlike traditional carbon credits that typically represent 1 tCO2e (metric ton of CO2 equivalent) and are traded in bulk by corporations, micro carbon credits can be as small as 0.001 tCO2e (1 kg CO2e).

Definition: A Micro Carbon Credit is a standardized digital token representing verified greenhouse gas emission reductions or removals at scales accessible to individuals and small entities, enabling participation in carbon markets previously restricted to large organizations.

1.1.2 Why Micro Carbon Credits Matter Now

Global climate action requires participation from all levels of society. Current challenges include:

  • Individual Exclusion: 8 billion people worldwide cannot participate in traditional carbon markets
  • Untapped Potential: Individual carbon footprints represent 60-70% of global emissions but lack economic incentives
  • Verification Gap: No standardized method to verify and reward small-scale climate actions
  • Economic Barrier: High transaction costs make small-scale carbon trading economically unviable
  • Technology Enabler: IoT, blockchain, and AI now make micro-level tracking and verification possible

Scenario: Sarah cycles 10 km to work instead of driving, reducing CO2 emissions by approximately 2.3 kg (0.0023 tCO2e).

Traditional Market: This activity is too small to participate in carbon markets (minimum 1 tCO2e = 435 bike rides).

With WIA Micro Credits: Sarah's bike (equipped with GPS tracker) automatically logs her ride. She instantly earns 0.0023 micro carbon credits (~$0.046 at $20/tCO2e). Credits accumulate daily and can be traded or cashed out monthly.

1.6.2 Micro-Credit Specific Considerations

  • Additionality: Automated verification that credited activities wouldn't have occurred anyway
  • Double Counting: Blockchain ledger prevents duplicate credits
  • Permanence: Real-time tracking ensures emission reductions are verified
  • Leakage: GPS and IoT data prevent false claims
