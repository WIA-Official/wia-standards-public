Executive Summary

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released WIA-TIME-024, an international standard for precision time measurement in multi-timeline environments, as free and open source under MIT License. This standard supports attosecond (10⁻¹⁸ seconds) precision, atomic clock standards, relativistic time correction, and multi-timeline synchronization, positioning the Republic of Korea as a leader in time measurement technology.

🚨 Current Situation: Why This Standard Is Needed

In multi-timeline environments, accumulated time measurement errors lead to inability to return. Without relativistic corrections, time errors occur during high-speed travel, time flow varies with gravitational fields, and even ±100ms error can lead to arriving in a different timeline. Accurate return is impossible without standardized time measurement.

Dr. Yeon Sam-Heum, CEO:
"Time is not absolute. At 30% of light speed, time slows down. Near black holes, time warps. Only by measuring with attosecond precision and correcting relativistically can accurate time travel be possible. This is the science of time measurement."

WIA's Solution: WIA-TIME-024 Time Measurement

WIA-TIME-024 standardizes attosecond (10⁻¹⁸ seconds) precision measurement, Cesium-133 atomic clock standards, special/general relativity corrections, gravitational time dilation calculations, and multi-timeline synchronization. Supports resolution up to Planck time (5.39×10⁻⁴⁴ seconds).
