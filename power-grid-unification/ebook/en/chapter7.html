Chapter 7: Phase 4 - Integration

7.1 National Grid Integration

South Korea's KEPCO grid interfaces seamlessly via HVDC terminals at strategic locations. North Korean grid modernization occurs in phases, starting with border regions and expanding to major industrial centers.

7.2 Northeast Asia Supergrid Vision

The unified Korean grid serves as a cornerstone for regional integration connecting China's renewable capacity (solar/wind), Russia's hydroelectric resources, Japan's advanced technology, Mongolia's wind power potential. Combined capacity could exceed 1 TW, enabling true continental-scale renewable energy.

7.3 International Standards Mapping

WIA-UNI-007 aligns with ISO 50001 (Energy Management), IEC 61970/61968 (Energy Management System APIs), IEEE 1547 (Distributed Energy Resources), ITU-T standards for telecommunications.

7.4 WIA Ecosystem Integration

Connects with WIA-UNI-005 (Infrastructure Integration), WIA-FIN-* (Energy Trading Finance), WIA-DATA-* (Secure Data Exchange). Certificates issued via WIA registry with blockchain verification.
