Chapter 3: Standard Overview

3.1 Four-Phase Architecture

WIA-UNI-007 implements a layered approach ensuring interoperability, security, and scalability:

Phase 1: Data Format - Standardized schemas for grid topology, power flow measurements, renewable energy metrics, asset registry, and real-time monitoring data.

Phase 2: API Interface - RESTful APIs for grid management, energy trading, load balancing, renewable integration, and monitoring/alerting.

Phase 3: Protocol - IEC 61850 compliance for substations, DNP3 for SCADA systems, MQTT for IoT sensors, HVDC control protocols, and TLS 1.3 encryption.

Phase 4: Integration - National grid connections, Northeast Asia Supergrid interfaces, international standards mapping, WIA ecosystem integration, certification framework.

3.2 Core Design Principles

Sovereignty & Security: Each side maintains control over domestic grid, secure encrypted communication, audit trails for all transactions.

Interoperability: Standards-based protocols, vendor-neutral APIs, backward compatibility with existing systems.

Scalability: Support for future capacity growth, extensible data models, regional grid expansion capability.

Sustainability: Prioritize renewable energy sharing, carbon tracking and reporting, environmental impact monitoring.

3.3 Key Components

HVDC Terminals with 765kV capacity at strategic border crossings, SCADA Integration Centers for real-time grid monitoring, Energy Management Systems for optimization, Trading Platforms for power exchange, Cybersecurity Operations Centers protecting critical infrastructure.
