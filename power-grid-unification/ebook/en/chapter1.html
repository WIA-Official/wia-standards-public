⚡ WIA-UNI-007: Power Grid Unification

Chapter 1: Introduction to Power Grid Unification

1.1 Vision and Strategic Importance

The unification of power grids across the Korean Peninsula represents one of the most transformative infrastructure projects of the 21st century. This initiative goes beyond technical integration—it embodies a commitment to peace, shared prosperity, and environmental sustainability through collaborative energy management.

By connecting North and South Korean electrical systems via High-Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) transmission, we create opportunities for renewable energy sharing, enhanced grid stability, and economic cooperation that can catalyze broader reconciliation efforts.

1.2 Current Energy Situation

South Korea: Highly developed grid with 120+ GW capacity, heavy reliance on imported fossil fuels (LNG, coal), ambitious renewable energy targets (40% by 2034), advanced smart grid infrastructure.

North Korea: Estimated 7-10 GW capacity with significant supply gaps, abundant hydroelectric and renewable potential (wind, solar), aging infrastructure requiring modernization, frequent power shortages limiting economic development.

1.3 Benefits of Integration

Energy Security: Mutual backup capacity during emergencies, diversified supply sources, reduced dependency on imports.

Renewable Energy: Utilize North Korea's hydroelectric and wind resources, balance intermittent solar/wind with pumped storage, achieve peninsula-wide carbon neutrality faster.

Economic Development: Enable industrialization in North Korea, create joint infrastructure projects, reduce energy costs through economies of scale.

Regional Connectivity: Foundation for Northeast Asia Supergrid linking Korea, China, Russia, Japan, and Mongolia.

1.4 The Role of WIA-UNI-007

The WIA-UNI-007 standard provides the technical framework to make grid unification practical, secure, and sustainable. It addresses data formats, APIs, communication protocols, and integration requirements needed for seamless power exchange while maintaining sovereignty and security for both parties.
