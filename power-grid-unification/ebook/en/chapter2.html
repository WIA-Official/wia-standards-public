Chapter 2: Current Energy Landscape

2.1 South Korea Power Infrastructure

South Korea operates one of the world's most advanced power systems with 120+ GW installed capacity. The grid features 765kV and 345kV AC transmission networks managed by KEPCO. However, the country faces challenges: 95% energy import dependency, limited renewable integration capacity, and growing demand from data centers and electric vehicles.

2.2 North Korea Energy Situation

North Korea's power infrastructure suffers from decades of underinvestment, with estimated capacity of only 7-10 GW serving 25 million people. Frequent blackouts hamper economic activity. However, the country possesses significant untapped renewable resources: major hydroelectric sites, excellent wind conditions in coastal areas, and solar potential.

2.3 Technical Barriers to Integration

Grid Incompatibility: Different frequencies (SK: 60Hz, NK: unknown/variable), voltage standards, and control systems require HVDC conversion.

Infrastructure Gap: NK transmission lines need modernization, limited metering and monitoring capabilities, SCADA systems require development.

Cybersecurity: Protection against attacks on critical infrastructure, secure communication protocols essential, sovereignty concerns for both sides.

2.4 Renewable Energy Potential

Combined peninsula renewable capacity could exceed 200 GW: North Korea's hydroelectric dams (15+ GW potential), East Coast offshore wind (50+ GW potential), DMZ solar farms (10+ GW potential), pumped storage for load balancing (20+ GW capacity).
