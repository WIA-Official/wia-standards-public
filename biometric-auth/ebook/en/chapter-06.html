Anti-Spoofing Measures

Liveness detection and presentation attack countermeasures

Understanding Presentation Attacks

A presentation attack (also called a spoof attack) involves presenting a fake or altered biometric sample to a sensor with the intent to interfere with system operation. Attackers may attempt to impersonate another individual (identity fraud) or evade recognition (identity concealment).

As biometric systems become more prevalent in high-security applications, sophisticated presentation attacks have emerged. Effective countermeasures are essential to maintain system integrity and user trust.

Security Reality

Without presentation attack detection (PAD), even highly accurate biometric systems can be defeated by relatively simple spoofs. PAD must be considered an integral component of any biometric authentication system, not an optional enhancement.

Common Attack Types

👆
Fingerprint Spoofs

Materials: Gelatin, silicone, Play-Doh, wood glue, 3D printed molds

Methods: Latent print lifting, consensual mold creation, cadaver fingers

🎭
Face Spoofs

3D Attacks: Masks, mannequins, 3D-printed faces, deepfakes

👁️
Iris Spoofs

Methods: Printed iris patterns, textured contact lenses, prosthetic eyes

Video: Display of iris image or video on screen

🎤
Voice Spoofs

Replay: Pre-recorded genuine voice samples

Synthesis: AI-generated voice clones, voice conversion

ISO/IEC 30107 PAD Framework

ISO/IEC 30107 provides the international standard framework for presentation attack detection:

  • Part 1: Framework covering PAD concepts, terminology, and general requirements
  • Part 2: Data formats for presenting PAD test results and reporting
  • Part 3: Testing and reporting methodology for PAD evaluation
  • Part 4: Profile for mobile device biometric verification
PAD Performance Metrics (ISO/IEC 30107-3)
=========================================

Attack Presentation Classification Error Rate (APCER)
├── Proportion of attack presentations incorrectly accepted
├── APCER = (Accepted Attacks / Total Attacks) × 100%
└── Lower is better; target depends on security level

Bona Fide Presentation Classification Error Rate (BPCER)
├── Proportion of genuine presentations incorrectly rejected
├── BPCER = (Rejected Genuine / Total Genuine) × 100%
└── Lower is better; affects usability

Detection Error Trade-off (DET) Curve
├── Plots BPCER against APCER across thresholds
├── Enables selection of operating point
└── Area under curve indicates overall PAD performance

Presentation Attack Instrument Species (PAIS)
├── Categorize attacks by creation method
├── Report APCER per species for comprehensive evaluation
└── Examples: printed photo, silicone mold, deepfake video

Fingerprint Liveness Detection

Hardware-Based Methods
  • Multispectral Imaging: Capture at multiple wavelengths; genuine skin has distinct spectral signature
  • OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography): Subsurface imaging reveals skin layers and sweat pores
  • Pulse Detection: Measure blood flow using photoplethysmography
  • Temperature Sensing: Genuine fingers have characteristic temperature patterns
  • Electrical Impedance: Measure skin conductivity differences
Software-Based Methods
  • Perspiration Pattern: Genuine fingers show moisture changes over time
  • Skin Distortion: Analyze elasticity when finger pressed against sensor
  • Pore Analysis: High-resolution examination of sweat pore patterns
  • Deep Learning: CNN-based classification of genuine vs. spoof samples

Face Liveness Detection

Facial PAD Techniques
=====================

PASSIVE METHODS (Single Frame)
├── Texture Analysis
│   ├── LBP (Local Binary Patterns) for micro-texture
│   ├── Frequency analysis (printed photos lack high frequencies)
│   └── Deep CNN feature extraction
├── Depth Estimation
│   ├── Structured light projection (Face ID)
│   ├── Time-of-flight sensors
│   └── Stereo camera depth calculation
└── Reflection Analysis
    ├── Specular highlight patterns
    └── Screen moire pattern detection

ACTIVE METHODS (Challenge-Response)
├── Random Challenge
│   ├── "Turn head left"
│   ├── "Blink twice"
│   └── "Say the words on screen"
├── Motion Analysis
│   ├── Natural micro-movements
│   ├── Parallax from head motion
│   └── Eye gaze tracking
└── Illumination Challenge
    ├── Change screen color, observe reflection
    └── Measure response timing characteristics

3D DEPTH METHODS
├── Structured Light (Apple Face ID)
│   ├── Project 30,000+ dots
│   ├── Create depth map
│   └── Detect flat surfaces (photos, screens)
├── Time-of-Flight
│   ├── Measure light travel time
│   └── Build 3D face model
└── Dual Camera Stereo
    ├── Calculate disparity
    └── Reconstruct depth

Multi-Algorithm Fusion

The most robust PAD implementations combine multiple detection methods. Hardware and software approaches complement each other—hardware methods are harder to circumvent but more expensive; software methods are adaptable but potentially vulnerable to sophisticated attacks. Fusion of multiple weak classifiers creates strong overall detection.

Minimum PAD Requirements by Security Level
==========================================

LEVEL 1 (Basic)
├── APCER: ≤ 15% for common attack types
├── BPCER: ≤ 5%
├── Methods: At least 1 PAD technique
└── Use Case: Low-risk consumer applications

LEVEL 2 (Standard)
├── APCER: ≤ 5% for known attack species
├── BPCER: ≤ 3%
├── Methods: Multiple complementary techniques
└── Use Case: Enterprise, financial services

LEVEL 3 (High Security)
├── APCER: ≤ 1% including sophisticated attacks
├── BPCER: ≤ 2%
├── Methods: Hardware + software fusion
└── Use Case: Government, critical infrastructure

TESTING REQUIREMENTS
├── Evaluate against ISO/IEC 30107-3 test plan
├── Test multiple PAIS (presentation attack instruments)
├── Report per-species APCER
└── Independent third-party evaluation recommended
