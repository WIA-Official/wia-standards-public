WIA-SPACE-LAW v1.0
Governing Humanity's Activities Beyond Earth's Atmosphere Through International Legal Frameworks

WIA-SPACE-LAW establishes a comprehensive international legal framework for space activities, encompassing treaties, national legislation, commercial regulations, and emerging governance models for outer space. As humanity expands its presence beyond Earth—with SpaceX's Starship program, Blue Origin's New Glenn, and Virgin Galactic's space tourism—the need for robust, adaptive legal frameworks has never been more critical.

This standard addresses the Outer Space Treaty (1967), which has 118 state parties as of 2025, alongside national space laws like Italy's comprehensive Space Law (2025), and the U.S. Executive Order on commercial space competitiveness. It covers space resource utilization, liability regimes, registration requirements, space traffic management, and the evolving role of UN COPUOS in coordinating international space governance.
