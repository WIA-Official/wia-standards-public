Chapter 6

Phase 3: Sampling and Analysis Protocols

Field Sampling, DNA Extraction, Sequencing, Bioinformatics, and MRV Verification

6.1 Introduction: From Field to Data

Standardized data formats (Phase 1) and APIs (Phase 2) are only valuable if the underlying data is collected and analyzed consistently. Phase 3 of the WIA-SOIL-MICROBIOME standard defines rigorous protocols for every step from field sampling through bioinformatics analysis, ensuring that data generated anywhere in the world is comparable and verifiable.

"弘益人間 (Hongik Ingan) - Standardized protocols democratize soil science, enabling a smallholder farmer in Kenya to contribute data as reliable as that from a world-class laboratory." — WIA Philosophy

This chapter provides detailed, step-by-step protocols that laboratories can implement immediately. Each protocol has been validated through inter-laboratory comparisons and is designed to be achievable with commonly available equipment while maintaining scientific rigor.

Level Requirements Data Quality

6.2 Field Sampling Protocol

Proper sampling is the foundation of reliable soil microbiome analysis. The WIA protocol balances spatial representativeness, temporal consistency, and practical feasibility.

6.2.1 Sampling Design

Application Sampling Density Pattern Subsamples
Farm monitoring 1 composite per 5-10 ha W-pattern or grid 5-10 cores
Carbon verification 1 composite per 2-5 ha Stratified random 8-12 cores
Research study 1 per plot/treatment Systematic grid 10-15 cores
Detailed mapping 1 per 1 ha Grid points 5-8 cores

6.2.2 Step-by-Step Sampling Protocol

Step 1: Pre-Sampling Preparation
  • Sterilize soil probes with 70% ethanol or 10% bleach, rinse with distilled water
  • Label sample bags with unique IDs, GPS coordinates, and date
  • Prepare field forms or mobile app for metadata capture
  • Bring cooler with ice packs for sample preservation
  • Ensure GPS device is calibrated and functioning
Step 2: Site Selection and Documentation
  • Record GPS coordinates (±5m accuracy minimum)
  • Document land use, crop, recent management (tillage, fertilizer, irrigation)
  • Note soil texture, color, and visible characteristics
  • Photograph sampling location and surrounding area
  • Record weather conditions (temperature, recent rainfall)
Step 3: Core Collection (Standard 0-30 cm depth)
  • Remove surface debris and vegetation without disturbing soil
  • Insert probe vertically to 30 cm depth (or soil auger)
  • Extract core and place in clean collection bucket
  • Clean probe between subsamples
  • Repeat for 5-12 subsamples across sampling area (W-pattern or grid)
  • Avoid field edges, waterways, or obvious anomalies unless specifically targeted
Step 4: Composite and Subsample
  • Homogenize all cores thoroughly in collection bucket
  • Remove visible roots, rocks, and large organic debris
  • Fill labeled sample bag with ~500g of composite soil
  • For microbiome analysis, collect additional 50g subsample
  • Seal bags tightly, removing excess air
Step 5: Preservation and Transport
  • Critical: Place microbiome subsample immediately on ice or in cooler
  • Keep samples at 4°C during transport (max 24 hours)
  • For longer transport, flash-freeze with dry ice
  • Record time from collection to refrigeration
  • Submit chain-of-custody form with samples
Step 6: Laboratory Receipt and Storage
  • Upon receipt, log sample ID, condition, and temperature
  • Store at -80°C for DNA analysis (or -20°C if -80°C unavailable)
  • Store remaining soil at 4°C for chemical analysis
  • Process DNA extraction within 30 days of collection
  • Maintain detailed storage logs

⚠️ Common Sampling Errors to Avoid

  • Temperature abuse: DNA degrades rapidly above 10°C. Always use ice/coolers.
  • Cross-contamination: Clean probes between sites, use new bags for each sample
  • Insufficient subsampling: Minimum 5 cores; more is better for heterogeneous fields
  • Poor metadata: GPS coordinates and land use are mandatory, not optional
  • Wet sampling: Avoid sampling saturated soils; wait 24-48h after heavy rain

6.3 DNA Extraction Protocol

DNA extraction is the critical link between soil samples and sequencing data. The WIA protocol is optimized for high yield, purity, and reproducibility across diverse soil types.

6.3.1 WIA-Certified Extraction Methods

Method Principle Advantages Best For
PowerSoil/DNeasy Bead beating + column High yield, commercial kit Standard applications, most soil types
CTAB extraction Chemical lysis Lower cost, scalable Large studies, budget constraints
Phenol-chloroform Organic extraction High purity DNA Clay-rich or challenging soils
Maxwell automated Magnetic bead High throughput, minimal handling Commercial labs, large projects

6.3.2 PowerSoil Protocol (WIA Standard)

Step 1: Sample Preparation
  • Thaw soil sample at room temperature for 30 minutes
  • Mix thoroughly and weigh 0.25g into PowerBead tube
  • Include extraction blank (no soil) for contamination control
  • Include mock community standard for quality control
Step 2: Cell Lysis
  • Add 60 μL Solution C1 (cell lysis buffer)
  • Vortex briefly to mix
  • Bead beat at maximum speed for 10 minutes (65°C)
  • Centrifuge at 10,000 × g for 30 seconds
  • Transfer supernatant to clean tube (~400-500 μL)
Step 3: Inhibitor Removal
  • Add 250 μL Solution C2 (protein precipitation)
  • Vortex for 5 seconds
  • Incubate at 4°C for 5 minutes
  • Centrifuge at 10,000 × g for 1 minute
  • Transfer supernatant to clean tube
Step 4: DNA Binding
  • Add 200 μL Solution C3 (humic acid precipitation)
  • Vortex briefly and incubate 4°C for 5 minutes
  • Centrifuge at 10,000 × g for 1 minute
  • Transfer up to 750 μL to spin column
  • Centrifuge at 10,000 × g for 1 minute, discard flow-through
Step 5: Washing
  • Add 500 μL Solution C4 (wash buffer)
  • Centrifuge at 10,000 × g for 30 seconds
  • Discard flow-through and repeat wash
  • Centrifuge at 10,000 × g for 2 minutes to dry column
Step 6: Elution
  • Place column in clean 1.5 mL tube
  • Add 100 μL Solution C6 (elution buffer, prewarmed to 55°C)
  • Incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes
  • Centrifuge at 10,000 × g for 30 seconds
  • Store DNA at -20°C (short-term) or -80°C (long-term)

6.3.3 Quality Control Metrics

Metric Method Acceptable Range Action if Outside Range
DNA Concentration Qubit fluorometer 5-100 ng/μL Re-extract if <5 ng/μL
260/280 Ratio NanoDrop 1.8-2.0 Protein contamination if <1.8
260/230 Ratio NanoDrop 2.0-2.2 Humic acid contamination if <1.5
Fragment Size Gel electrophoresis High MW smear (>10 kb) Degradation if <5 kb

🧬 DNA Quality Troubleshooting

Low yield: Increase input soil to 0.5g, extend bead beating to 15 min, or use fresh samples

Humic acid contamination (brown color): Repeat Solution C3 precipitation step, use soil wash pre-treatment

Degraded DNA: Improve cold chain, process samples within 30 days, avoid freeze-thaw cycles

PCR inhibition: Dilute DNA 1:10, use additional cleanup (AMPure beads), or OneStep PCR inhibitor removal

6.4 Sequencing Methods

The WIA standard supports multiple sequencing approaches, each optimized for specific applications and budgets.

6.4.1 Sequencing Method Comparison

Method Target Resolution Cost/Sample Applications
16S rRNA (Bacteria) V4 or V3-V4 region Genus level $30-50 Standard bacterial profiling
ITS (Fungi) ITS1 or ITS2 region Species level $30-50 Fungal community analysis
18S rRNA V4 or V9 region Family level $40-60 Eukaryotic microbes, protozoa
Shotgun Metagenomics Whole genome Strain + function $200-500 Functional profiling, novel organisms
Metatranscriptomics Active genes (RNA) Functional activity $300-600 Active metabolism, real-time function

6.4.2 16S rRNA Protocol (WIA Standard for Bacteria)

Primer Set: 515F-806R (V4 Region)

515F: 5'-GTGCCAGCMGCCGCGGTAA-3'
806R: 5'-GGACTACHVGGGTWTCTAAT-3'

Target: V4 hypervariable region (~250 bp)
Coverage: >90% of bacterial and archaeal taxa
Platform: Illumina MiSeq (2×250 bp paired-end)
Step 1: PCR Amplification (First Round)
  • Template: 1-10 ng genomic DNA
  • Primers: 515F/806R with Illumina adapters (0.2 μM each)
  • Polymerase: Q5 High-Fidelity (or equivalent)
  • Reaction volume: 25 μL
  • Cycling: 98°C 30s; 25 cycles of (98°C 10s, 55°C 20s, 72°C 20s); 72°C 2min
  • Include no-template control and mock community
Step 2: PCR Cleanup
  • Use AMPure XP beads at 0.8× ratio
  • Wash 2× with 80% ethanol
  • Elute in 25 μL TE buffer or nuclease-free water
  • Quantify with Qubit dsDNA HS assay
Step 3: Index PCR (Second Round)
  • Template: 5 μL cleaned PCR product
  • Add Illumina dual-index primers (unique per sample)
  • Cycling: 98°C 30s; 8 cycles of (98°C 10s, 55°C 20s, 72°C 20s); 72°C 2min
  • Cleanup with AMPure beads (0.8×)
Step 4: Library Pooling and QC
  • Quantify each indexed library with Qubit
  • Normalize to equal molarity (typically 4 nM)
  • Pool all samples in equal volumes
  • Verify pool with Bioanalyzer or TapeStation (peak at ~380 bp)
  • qPCR quantification for accurate loading concentration
Step 5: Sequencing
  • Platform: Illumina MiSeq with v2 or v3 chemistry
  • Run type: 2×250 bp paired-end
  • Loading: 10-12 pM with 5% PhiX spike-in
  • Target depth: 30,000-50,000 reads per sample
  • Include positive (mock community) and negative (extraction blank) controls

6.4.3 Shotgun Metagenomics Protocol

For functional profiling and high-resolution taxonomy:

Library Prep: Nextera DNA Flex or similar tagmentation-based
Insert Size: 350-500 bp
Sequencing: Illumina NovaSeq or HiSeq (2×150 bp)
Depth: 5-10 Gb per sample (minimum), 20-50 Gb for deep profiling
Assembly: MEGAHIT or metaSPAdes
Annotation: Prokka, DRAM, or IMG/M

6.5 Bioinformatics Pipeline

The WIA standard specifies a reference bioinformatics pipeline to ensure consistent taxonomic assignment and diversity calculations across laboratories.

6.5.1 QIIME2 Pipeline (WIA Reference)

# Step 1: Import sequences
qiime tools import \
  --type 'SampleData[PairedEndSequencesWithQuality]' \
  --input-path manifest.tsv \
  --output-path paired-end-demux.qza \
  --input-format PairedEndFastqManifestPhred33V2

# Step 2: Quality filtering and denoising (DADA2)
qiime dada2 denoise-paired \
  --i-demultiplexed-seqs paired-end-demux.qza \
  --p-trunc-len-f 240 \
  --p-trunc-len-r 240 \
  --p-max-ee-f 2 \
  --p-max-ee-r 2 \
  --p-n-threads 16 \
  --o-table feature-table.qza \
  --o-representative-sequences rep-seqs.qza \
  --o-denoising-stats denoising-stats.qza

# Step 3: Taxonomic classification (SILVA database)
qiime feature-classifier classify-sklearn \
  --i-classifier silva-138-99-nb-classifier.qza \
  --i-reads rep-seqs.qza \
  --p-n-jobs 16 \
  --o-classification taxonomy.qza

# Step 4: Diversity analysis
qiime diversity core-metrics-phylogenetic \
  --i-phylogeny rooted-tree.qza \
  --i-table feature-table.qza \
  --p-sampling-depth 10000 \
  --m-metadata-file metadata.tsv \
  --output-dir diversity-core-metrics

# Step 5: Alpha diversity
qiime diversity alpha \
  --i-table feature-table.qza \
  --p-metric shannon \
  --o-alpha-diversity shannon.qza

# Step 6: Export to WIA JSON format
qiime tools export \
  --input-path taxonomy.qza \
  --output-path exported-taxonomy

python wia_convert.py \
  --taxonomy exported-taxonomy/taxonomy.tsv \
  --feature-table feature-table.qza \
  --diversity shannon.qza \
  --output wia-microbiome-profile.json

6.5.2 Quality Control Checkpoints

Checkpoint Metric Pass Threshold Action if Fail
Read Quality Mean Q-score >30 Adjust trimming parameters
Sequencing Depth Reads per sample >30,000 Re-sequence or exclude sample
Taxonomic Assignment Assignment rate >85% Check database version, re-classify
Mock Community Expected taxa detected >95% Investigate contamination or bias
Negative Control Read count <1% of samples Review contamination sources

6.6 MRV (Measurement, Reporting, Verification) Protocols

For carbon credit applications, the WIA standard includes rigorous MRV protocols that meet international standards (ISO 14064, Verra VCS, Gold Standard).

6.6.1 MRV Workflow

Phase 1: Baseline Establishment
  • Collect baseline samples at project start (minimum 3 samples per 10 ha)
  • Measure SOC, microbial biomass C, CUE, and respiration
  • Document management practices for previous 5 years
  • Generate WIA-compliant baseline report
  • Store samples and DNA at -80°C for future verification
Phase 2: Monitoring (Annual)
  • Collect samples at same locations (±5m GPS accuracy)
  • Same depth, same time of year (±2 weeks)
  • Measure all baseline parameters plus diversity indices
  • Submit data via WIA API with blockchain timestamp
  • Calculate sequestration rate vs. baseline
Phase 3: Verification (Annual or Per Credits Issuance)
  • Third-party auditor reviews sampling protocol compliance
  • Re-analysis of subset of samples (~10%) for quality control
  • Statistical analysis of sequestration claims
  • Uncertainty quantification and confidence intervals
  • Issue verification statement for credit issuance

6.6.2 MRV Data Requirements

Parameter Frequency Precision Uncertainty Target
SOC Stock (t C/ha) Annual ±0.5 t/ha <10% at 95% CI
Bulk Density (g/cm³) Annual ±0.05 <5%
Microbial Biomass C Annual ±20 mg/kg <15%
CUE Annual ±0.03 <10%
Shannon Diversity Annual ±0.2 <5%

6.6.3 Uncertainty Quantification

Total Uncertainty = √(Sampling² + Analytical² + Temporal² + Spatial²)

Where:
• Sampling = coefficient of variation from replicate samples
• Analytical = laboratory measurement precision
• Temporal = variation due to sampling timing
• Spatial = field-scale heterogeneity

Example Calculation:
Sampling CV = 12%
Analytical CV = 5%
Temporal CV = 8%
Spatial CV = 15%

Total = √(0.12² + 0.05² + 0.08² + 0.15²) = 0.21 (21%)

For 95% confidence interval: ±2 × 21% = ±42%

Carbon credits issued: Measured sequestration × (1 - 0.42) = Conservative estimate

6.7 Laboratory Certification

Laboratories can obtain WIA certification by demonstrating protocol compliance and passing proficiency tests.

6.7.1 Certification Requirements

Level Requirements Annual Cost Benefits
Registered Self-declaration of protocol use Free Listed in WIA directory
Certified Proficiency testing (2× annually), QC metrics $2,500 Use WIA Certified logo, priority support
MRV-Accredited Certified + on-site audit + ISO 17025 accreditation $10,000 Issue carbon credit verification statements

6.7.2 Proficiency Testing

WIA distributes blinded soil samples twice annually. Laboratories must achieve:

  • Taxonomic accuracy: >90% agreement with consensus at phylum level, >80% at family level
  • Diversity indices: Within 10% of consensus mean for Shannon, Simpson
  • SOC measurement: Within ±5% of true value (characterized reference soils)
  • Mock community: Detect >95% of expected taxa, <5% false positives

📚 Chapter Summary: Key Takeaways

  1. Sampling is foundational: Proper field protocols, including GPS documentation, composite sampling (5-12 cores), and immediate cold storage, ensure representative, high-quality samples.
  2. DNA quality determines success: Standardized extraction (PowerSoil protocol), rigorous QC (concentration, purity ratios), and proper storage prevent downstream failures.
  3. Multiple sequencing options: 16S for bacteria ($30-50/sample), ITS for fungi, shotgun metagenomics ($200-500) for functional profiling—choose based on application and budget.
  4. QIIME2 reference pipeline: Standardized bioinformatics (DADA2 denoising, SILVA classification) ensures comparable results across laboratories and studies.
  5. MRV for carbon credits: Rigorous baseline establishment, annual monitoring, third-party verification, and blockchain timestamping make soil carbon credits credible and tradeable.
