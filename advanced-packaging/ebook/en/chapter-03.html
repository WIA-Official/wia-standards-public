Chapter 3: Interconnect Technologies

Interconnect technologies form the backbone of advanced semiconductor packaging, enabling high-density electrical connections between dies, interposers, and substrates. This chapter provides comprehensive coverage of Through-Silicon Vias (TSVs), microbumps, redistribution layers (RDL), and hybrid bonding technologies that enable modern 2.5D and 3D packaging.

3.1 Through-Silicon Via (TSV) Technology

Through-Silicon Vias represent one of the most transformative technologies in advanced packaging, enabling vertical electrical connections through silicon substrates. TSVs are essential for 3D stacking and silicon interposer applications.

Definition: A Through-Silicon Via (TSV) is a vertical electrical interconnect that passes completely through a silicon wafer or die, providing electrical connection between the front and back surfaces of the silicon.

TSV Process Integration

TSV fabrication can occur at different stages in the manufacturing flow, each approach offering distinct advantages for specific applications:

TSV Process Integration Options
===============================

Via-First (Before FEOL)
-----------------------
Wafer -> TSV Etch -> TSV Fill -> FEOL -> BEOL -> Thin -> Reveal
Advantages: High aspect ratio achievable
Challenges: Contamination risk, thermal budget limits
Applications: Image sensors, MEMS

Via-Middle (After FEOL, Before BEOL)
------------------------------------
Wafer -> FEOL -> TSV Etch -> TSV Fill -> BEOL -> Thin -> Reveal
Advantages: Standard BEOL compatibility
Challenges: BEOL thermal budget consideration
Applications: Logic/memory integration

Via-Last (After BEOL, Front Side)
---------------------------------
Wafer -> FEOL -> BEOL -> TSV Etch (front) -> Fill -> Thin -> Reveal
Advantages: Known good die possible
Challenges: Aspect ratio limited, alignment
Applications: Interposers

Via-Last (After BEOL, Back Side)
--------------------------------
Wafer -> FEOL -> BEOL -> Thin -> TSV Etch (back) -> Fill
Advantages: Decoupled from BEOL
Challenges: Thin wafer handling
Applications: 3D stacked memory

TSV Design Parameters

Parameter Typical Range Impact
Diameter 2-10 um (logic), 5-20 um (interposer) Density, fill capability
Depth 50-100 um (typical), up to 300 um Process complexity
Aspect Ratio 5:1 to 20:1 Fill uniformity, void risk
Pitch 10-50 um Interconnect density
Liner Thickness 100-500 nm SiO2 Isolation, capacitance
Barrier Metal 10-50 nm Ta/TaN Cu diffusion prevention

TSV Electrical Model

TSV Equivalent Circuit Model
============================

            Top Contact
                |
        +-------+-------+
        |       |       |
       [R]     [L]     [C]
        |       |       |
        +-------+-------+
                |
            Bottom Contact

Typical Values (10um dia, 100um depth):
- Resistance (R): 10-50 mohm
- Inductance (L): 10-30 pH
- Capacitance (C): 20-50 fF

Calculation Formulas:
---------------------
R_tsv = rho * h / (pi * r^2)
    where rho = Cu resistivity (1.7e-8 ohm-m)
          h = TSV height
          r = TSV radius

L_tsv = (mu_0 * h / 2pi) * [ln(2h/r) - 1 + r/h]
    where mu_0 = permeability of free space

C_tsv = 2 * pi * epsilon * h / ln(r_ox/r_cu)
    where epsilon = oxide permittivity
          r_ox = outer radius (with liner)
          r_cu = copper radius

3.2 Microbump Technology

Microbumps provide fine-pitch interconnection between dies in 2.5D and 3D configurations. These miniaturized solder or copper pillar connections enable the high interconnect density required for advanced packaging.

Evolution: Microbump pitch has decreased from 80um in early implementations to 25-55um in current production, with research demonstrating sub-10um pitches using advanced bonding techniques.

Microbump Structures

Microbump Architecture Comparison
=================================

Traditional Solder Bump (C4):
+---------------------------+
|          Solder           | (~80um diameter)
|      (SnAg or SnAgCu)     |
+---------------------------+
|           UBM             | (Under Bump Metallization)
+---------------------------+
|      Al or Cu Pad         |
+---------------------------+

Copper Pillar Microbump:
+---------------------------+
|       Solder Cap          | (~15um)
|      (SnAg)               |
+---------------------------+
|                           |
|       Cu Pillar           | (~25um)
|                           |
+---------------------------+
|      Ni Barrier           | (~5um)
+---------------------------+
|        Cu Pad             |
+---------------------------+

Copper Pillar Advantages:
- Finer pitch capability (25-55um)
- Better current carrying capacity
- Reduced electromigration risk
- Improved standoff height control

Microbump Reliability Considerations

Failure Mode Root Cause Mitigation Strategy
Electromigration High current density Cu pillar, larger diameter
Thermal fatigue CTE mismatch Underfill, compliant layers
IMC growth Solder-Cu reaction Ni barrier, joint design
Bridging Solder spread Pitch/gap control, flux
Open/weak joint Poor wetting Surface prep, reflow profile

3.3 Redistribution Layer (RDL) Technology

Redistribution Layers provide lateral routing that redistributes I/O connections from the die pad locations to the desired bump positions. RDL is fundamental to fan-out packaging and enables escape routing from fine-pitch die pads to coarser package-level pitches.

RDL Process and Structure
=========================

Multi-Layer RDL Stack:
+------------------------------------------+
|            Bump/Pad Layer                |
+------------------------------------------+
|         Dielectric (PI/PBO)              |
+------------------------------------------+
|            Metal Layer 3                 | (Cu, 2-5um thick)
+------------------------------------------+
|         Dielectric (PI/PBO)              | (5-10um thick)
+------------------------------------------+
|            Metal Layer 2                 |
+------------------------------------------+
|         Dielectric (PI/PBO)              |
+------------------------------------------+
|            Metal Layer 1                 |
+------------------------------------------+
|         Passivation                      |
+------------------------------------------+
|              Die                         |
+------------------------------------------+

RDL Design Rules (Fine-Pitch):
- Line width: 2-5 um
- Line spacing: 2-5 um
- Via diameter: 5-10 um
- Via pitch: 10-20 um
- Layer count: 2-8 layers

RDL Materials and Processes

Material Selection: RDL dielectrics must balance electrical properties (low Dk, low loss), mechanical properties (good adhesion, low stress), and processability (photodefinable, low cure temperature).

3.4 Hybrid Bonding Technology

Hybrid bonding represents the frontier of interconnect density, enabling direct copper-to-copper connections at sub-micron pitch. This technology combines metal-metal bonding with dielectric-dielectric bonding in a single process step.

Hybrid Bonding Process Flow

Hybrid Bonding Process Steps
============================

1. Surface Preparation
   +------------------+
   | Cu pad recessed  |  (5-10nm dish)
   | in SiO2 surface  |
   +------------------+
   - CMP to achieve sub-nm roughness
   - Cu recess: 2-5nm typical
   - Oxide RMS roughness: <0.5nm

2. Surface Activation
   - Plasma activation (N2, Ar, or O2)
   - Creates reactive surface bonds
   - Must bond within hours

3. Alignment and Contact
   +------------------+
   |      Die 1       |
   |   Cu    SiO2     |
   +------------------+  <- Initial contact
   |   Cu    SiO2     |
   |      Die 2       |
   +------------------+
   - Alignment accuracy: <200nm 3-sigma
   - Room temperature oxide bond

4. Anneal
   - Temperature: 200-400C
   - Time: 30min - 2hrs
   - Cu expansion fills gap
   - Cu-Cu interdiffusion creates bond

5. Final Structure
   +------------------+
   |      Die 1       |
   |=====Cu=====SiO2==|  <- Bonded interface
   |      Die 2       |
   +------------------+

Hybrid Bonding Specifications

Parameter Current Production Advanced R&D
Bond Pitch 9-10 um 1-3 um
Cu Pad Size 5-6 um 0.5-2 um
Alignment Accuracy 500 nm 50-100 nm
Bond Strength >2.5 J/m2 >3.0 J/m2
Contact Resistance <10 mohm <5 mohm
Critical Requirement: Hybrid bonding demands extreme surface cleanliness and flatness. Even nanometer-scale particles or surface variations can cause bond voids leading to electrical opens or reliability failures.

3.5 Advanced Wire Bonding

Despite the focus on flip-chip and 3D technologies, wire bonding remains relevant for specific applications and continues to evolve with finer pitch capabilities and copper wire adoption.

Wire Bond Technology Comparison
===============================

Gold Wire Bonding:
- Wire diameter: 15-25 um
- Bond pad pitch: 35-50 um
- Advantages: Mature, reliable, soft metal
- Challenges: Gold cost, limited current

Copper Wire Bonding:
- Wire diameter: 15-25 um
- Bond pad pitch: 40-60 um
- Advantages: Lower cost, better conductivity
- Challenges: Harder metal, Al splash risk

Ribbon Bonding:
- Dimensions: 50-500 um wide x 12-50 um thick
- Applications: High current (power devices)
- Advantages: Lower inductance, higher current
- Challenges: Larger footprint

Bond Pad Structure for Cu Wire:
+---------------------------+
|      Cu Wire Ball         |
+---------------------------+
|        Al Pad             | (1-2um)
+---------------------------+
|      TiN Barrier          |
+---------------------------+
|        Cu Metal           |
+---------------------------+
|    Low-k Dielectric       |
+---------------------------+

3.6 Interconnect Electrical Performance

High-speed signal integrity requires careful analysis of interconnect parasitics. This section provides the models and guidelines for achieving target performance.

Signal Integrity Design Guidelines
==================================

Impedance Targets:
- Single-ended: 50 ohm +/- 10%
- Differential: 85-100 ohm +/- 10%

Interconnect Loss Budget (56 Gbps PAM4):
+----------------------------------+
| Component         | Loss (dB)    |
+----------------------------------+
| Die TSV/bump      | 0.5-1.0      |
| Interposer RDL    | 1.0-3.0      |
| Interposer TSV    | 0.5-1.0      |
| Package substrate | 3.0-6.0      |
| PCB trace         | 5.0-10.0     |
| Connectors        | 1.0-2.0      |
+----------------------------------+
| Total budget      | 11-23 dB     |
+----------------------------------+

Crosstalk Mitigation:
- Adjacent signal spacing: >2x line width
- Ground shielding between pairs
- Orthogonal routing between layers
- Differential signaling where possible

Return Loss Target: < -10 dB in band

The interconnect technologies presented in this chapter form the physical foundation enabling advanced packaging architectures. Selection and optimization of these technologies directly impacts system performance, reliability, and manufacturing yield.
