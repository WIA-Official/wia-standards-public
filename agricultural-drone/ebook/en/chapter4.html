Chapter 4: Precision Crop Spraying

Chapter Overview: Spray system design, nozzle types, application rates, safety protocols, and regulatory compliance for agricultural drone spraying.

4.1 Agricultural Spray Drone Systems

Modern agricultural spray drones integrate sophisticated systems for precise chemical application:

Core Components

  • Tank: 5-30 liters capacity, typically HDPE or stainless steel
  • Pump: Diaphragm or centrifugal, 1-5 L/min flow rate
  • Nozzles: 2-8 nozzles, spray width 3-7 meters
  • Flow Sensor: Real-time flow monitoring
  • Level Sensor: Tank level monitoring
  • Control System: Automated flow rate adjustment based on speed/altitude

4.2 Nozzle Types and Selection

Flat Fan Nozzles

  • Pattern: Elliptical, uniform distribution
  • Best for: Herbicides, contact fungicides
  • Spray angle: 80-110°
  • Example: TeeJet 11001

Hollow Cone Nozzles

  • Pattern: Ring-shaped spray
  • Best for: Insecticides requiring foliage penetration
  • Droplet size: Fine to medium
  • Example: Albuz ATR

Air Induction Nozzles

  • Pattern: Large droplets with air bubbles
  • Best for: Drift reduction (windy conditions)
  • Droplet size: Coarse
  • Example: Hypro Guardian Air

4.3 Droplet Size and Drift Management

Droplet size is critical for efficacy and drift control:

Very Fine<100Fumigants, ULVExtreme

Very Coarse>500FertilizersVery Low

Drift Reduction Strategies

  1. Fly Low: 2-3 meters above canopy (vs. 5-10m for manned aircraft)
  2. Use Coarser Nozzles: Air induction or larger orifice
  3. Add Drift Retardants: Polymers that increase droplet size
  4. Monitor Wind: Stop spraying if wind >10 km/h (3 m/s)
  5. Adjust Pressure: Lower pressure = larger droplets

4.4 Application Rate Calculations

Basic Formula

Application Rate (L/ha) = (Flow Rate (L/min) × 60) / (Speed (m/min) × Swath Width (m)) × 10000

Example:

  • Flow rate: 1.5 L/min
  • Speed: 3.5 m/s = 210 m/min
  • Swath width: 4.5 m
  • Application rate = (1.5 × 60) / (210 × 4.5) × 10000 = 95.2 L/ha

Modern drones automatically adjust flow rate to maintain target application rate as speed varies.

4.5 Weather Considerations

Ideal Spray Conditions

  • Temperature: 10-25°C (optimum 15-20°C)
  • Humidity: >50% (ideally 60-80%)
  • Wind Speed: 3-10 km/h (0.8-2.8 m/s)
  • Time of Day: Early morning or late afternoon
  • Avoid: Rain, fog, temperature inversions

Temperature Inversion

Temperature inversion (warm air above cool air) traps spray droplets near ground, causing drift. Signs:

  • Calm conditions with clear skies
  • Smoke/dust hanging low
  • Typically 2-3 hours after sunset to 1 hour after sunrise

4.6 Safety Protocols

Operator Safety

  • PPE: Gloves, protective clothing when handling chemicals
  • Mixing Station: Designated area away from water sources
  • Emergency Plan: Know location of eyewash, first aid
  • Training: Pesticide applicator certification required

Environmental Safety

  • Buffer Zones: Minimum 15-30m from water bodies, sensitive crops
  • Re-entry Interval: Follow label requirements (typically 4-24 hours)
  • Spray Records: Maintain detailed application logs

4.7 Regulatory Compliance

South Korea (Example)

  • Aviation Law: Drone operator certification required
  • Agricultural Chemicals Act: Aerial application permit
  • Environmental Conservation Act: Spraying near protected areas prohibited
  • Buffer Zones: 200m from residential areas, 300m from schools

United States (Example)

  • FAA Part 107: Remote pilot certificate
  • EPA FIFRA: Pesticide application regulations
  • State Regulations: Vary by state (e.g., California has strict VOC rules)

4.8 Spray Patterns and Coverage

Common drone spray patterns:

  • Parallel Swaths: Most common, 10% overlap between passes
  • Contour Following: Maintains altitude above terrain on slopes
  • Spot Treatment: Treats only specific problem areas
  • Perimeter First: Spray field edges first, then fill interior

4.9 Performance Metrics

Key Performance Indicators

  • Coverage Efficiency: >95% area covered (use water-sensitive papers to verify)
  • Application Accuracy: ±10% of target rate
  • Chemical Savings: 30-50% vs. broadcast application
  • Work Rate: 4-8 hectares/hour (10L tank drone)
  • Drift: <5% beyond target area

4.10 Summary

  • Agricultural spray drones achieve 30-50% chemical savings through precision
  • Nozzle selection affects droplet size and drift potential
  • Fly at 2-3m above crop canopy for optimal coverage
  • Weather conditions critical: 10-25°C, >50% humidity, <10 km/h wind
  • Regulatory compliance requires pilot certification + pesticide applicator license
  • Proper calibration ensures accurate application rates
