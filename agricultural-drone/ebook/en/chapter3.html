Chapter 3: NDVI and Vegetation Health Analysis

Chapter Overview: Deep dive into NDVI calculation, interpretation, and practical application for crop health assessment.

3.1 Understanding NDVI

NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) is the most widely used vegetation index in precision agriculture. It exploits the fact that healthy vegetation strongly reflects NIR light while absorbing red light for photosynthesis.

The Science Behind NDVI

  • Red Light (650nm): Chlorophyll absorbs for photosynthesis
  • NIR Light (850nm): Plant cell structure reflects (protects from overheating)
  • Healthy Plants: High NIR reflection + Low red reflection = High NDVI
  • Stressed Plants: Lower NIR reflection + Higher red reflection = Lower NDVI

NDVI Formula

NDVI = (NIR - Red) / (NIR + Red)

Example Calculation:

  • NIR pixel value: 200
  • Red pixel value: 80
  • NDVI = (200 - 80) / (200 + 80) = 120 / 280 = 0.43

3.3 NDVI Mapping Workflow

  1. Data Collection: Fly drone with multispectral camera (50-120m altitude)
  2. Image Processing: Stitch images into orthomosaic
  3. NDVI Calculation: Software applies formula pixel-by-pixel
  4. Colorization: Apply color ramp (red=low, yellow=medium, green=high)
  5. Zone Delineation: Create management zones based on NDVI ranges
  6. Prescription Generation: Variable rate application maps

3.4 Other Vegetation Indices

EVI (Enhanced Vegetation Index)

EVI = 2.5 × ((NIR - Red) / (NIR + 6×Red - 7.5×Blue + 1))

  • Advantages: Reduces atmospheric and soil background effects
  • Best for: Dense canopy conditions where NDVI saturates

SAVI (Soil-Adjusted Vegetation Index)

SAVI = ((NIR - Red) / (NIR + Red + L)) × (1 + L)

  • L factor: 0.5 (typical), adjusts for soil brightness
  • Best for: Early season crops, sparse vegetation

NDRE (Normalized Difference Red Edge)

NDRE = (NIR - RedEdge) / (NIR + RedEdge)

  • More sensitive to chlorophyll content
  • Best for: Nitrogen deficiency detection, late season monitoring

3.5 Practical Applications

Early Disease Detection

NDVI can detect stress from diseases 7-14 days before visual symptoms:

  • Fungal diseases: Create localized low-NDVI spots
  • Viral diseases: Gradual NDVI decline across affected area
  • Bacterial diseases: Rapid NDVI drop in infected zones

Nitrogen Management

NDVI correlates strongly with crop nitrogen status:

  • High NDVI (>0.7): Adequate nitrogen
  • Medium NDVI (0.5-0.7): Monitor closely
  • Low NDVI (<0.5): Nitrogen deficiency likely

Variable Rate Nitrogen: Apply more N to low-NDVI zones, less to high-NDVI zones, optimizing fertilizer use and reducing environmental impact.

Yield Prediction

NDVI at critical growth stages correlates with final yield:

  • Wheat: NDVI at heading stage (R² = 0.75-0.85)
  • Corn: NDVI at tasseling (R² = 0.70-0.80)
  • Rice: NDVI at panicle initiation (R² = 0.65-0.75)

3.6 Limitations and Considerations

  • Saturation: NDVI saturates at high biomass (LAI >3), use EVI instead
  • Soil Influence: Bare or sparse soil affects values, use SAVI for early season
  • Atmospheric Effects: Haze, clouds affect measurements, fly in clear conditions
  • Sun Angle: Different times of day produce different values, maintain consistent timing
  • Species Variation: Different crops have different "normal" NDVI ranges

3.7 NDVI-Based Management Zones

Create 3-5 management zones based on NDVI statistics:

Zone Classification Method:

  1. Calculate field mean and standard deviation
  2. Zone 1 (Low): NDVI < (mean - 1×SD)
  3. Zone 2 (Medium-Low): (mean - 1×SD) to mean
  4. Zone 3 (Medium-High): mean to (mean + 1×SD)
  5. Zone 4 (High): NDVI > (mean + 1×SD)

3.8 Case Study: Rice Farm NDVI Analysis

Scenario: 20-hectare rice field, 60 days after planting

NDVI Results:

  • Mean NDVI: 0.65
  • Range: 0.35 - 0.82
  • Standard deviation: 0.12

Analysis:

  • 18 hectares (90%): NDVI 0.55-0.75 (healthy)
  • 1.5 hectares (7.5%): NDVI 0.35-0.50 (stressed - low-lying area, poor drainage)
  • 0.5 hectares (2.5%): NDVI 0.75-0.82 (excellent - near irrigation inlet)

Action Taken:

  • Investigated low-NDVI zone: Discovered nitrogen deficiency + waterlogging
  • Applied targeted fertilizer to stressed area (saved 300kg N vs. broadcast)
  • Adjusted irrigation schedule to improve drainage
  • Follow-up flight 14 days later showed NDVI recovery to 0.60

Outcome: Prevented 10% yield loss, saved $850 in fertilizer costs

3.9 Summary

  • NDVI is the gold standard vegetation index, calculated from NIR and Red bands
  • Values range from -1 to +1, with 0.6-0.9 indicating healthy vegetation
  • NDVI maps reveal stress 1-2 weeks before visible symptoms
  • Applications include disease detection, nitrogen management, and yield prediction
  • Complement NDVI with other indices (NDRE, EVI, SAVI) for comprehensive analysis
  • Proper interpretation requires understanding crop type, growth stage, and environmental factors
