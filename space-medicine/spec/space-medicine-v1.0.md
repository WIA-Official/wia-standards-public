## Executive Summary

The WIA-SPACE-023 Space Medicine Standard establishes comprehensive protocols, guidelines, and best practices for the delivery of medical care in space environments, with particular emphasis on long-duration missions to the International Space Station (ISS), lunar surface, and Mars.

This standard addresses the unique challenges of space medicine including microgravity effects, radiation exposure, psychological health, autonomous medical operations, and post-flight rehabilitation.

## Scope

### In Scope
- Human physiological adaptation to microgravity and partial gravity environments
- Radiation exposure monitoring, protection, and risk assessment
- Cardiovascular and musculoskeletal health maintenance
- Neuro-vestibular and vision health (including SANS prevention/treatment)
- Psychological health and crew dynamics management
- Medical operations in microgravity (diagnostics, treatment, emergency procedures)
- Long-duration mission medical protocols (Mars and beyond)
- Pre-flight medical screening and selection
- Post-flight rehabilitation and recovery
- Pharmaceutical logistics and stability in space
- Telemedicine and autonomous medical decision support

### Out of Scope
- Launch and re-entry vehicle design (covered by aerospace engineering standards)
- Life support systems engineering (covered by environmental control standards)
- Nutrition and food systems (covered by space nutrition standards)
- Exercise equipment engineering specifications (covered by equipment standards)

## Terminology and Definitions

### Key Terms

**Microgravity**: Gravitational acceleration less than 10^-4 g, typically experienced in orbital spaceflight

**SANS**: Spaceflight-Associated Neuro-Ocular Syndrome; constellation of vision changes including optic disc edema, globe flattening, and choroidal folds

**GCR**: Galactic Cosmic Radiation; high-energy particles from outside the solar system

**SPE**: Solar Particle Event; burst of radiation from solar flares

**Orthostatic Intolerance**: Inability to maintain blood pressure when standing due to cardiovascular deconditioning

**ARED**: Advanced Resistive Exercise Device; resistance training equipment on ISS

**CMO**: Crew Medical Officer; astronaut with advanced medical training

**LBNP**: Lower Body Negative Pressure; countermeasure device that pulls fluids toward lower body

**Telemedicine**: Remote medical consultation via telecommunications

**Career Exposure Limit**: Maximum cumulative radiation dose permissible over astronaut's career

## Technical Requirements

### 1. Astronaut Medical Selection and Certification

#### 1.1 Pre-Flight Medical Evaluation

**Requirements:**
- Comprehensive medical history review (MED-001)
- Physical examination according to Table 1 standards (MED-002)
- Cardiovascular assessment including stress ECG and echocardiography (MED-003)
- Vision testing: uncorrected 20/200 or better, correctable to 20/20 (MED-004)
- Baseline DEXA scan for bone density (MED-005)
- Psychological evaluation including MMPI and clinical interview (MED-006)
- Blood and urine laboratory panels (MED-007)
- Immunization verification and update (MED-008)

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- No uncontrolled chronic diseases
- Cardiovascular fitness appropriate for mission demands
- Psychological stability and stress tolerance
- Vision within acceptable parameters
- Bone density within normal range for age/gender

#### 1.2 Age and Anthropometric Requirements

- Height: 157-190 cm (adjustable based on spacecraft/spacesuit constraints)
- Weight: Proportional to height, BMI 18.5-30
- Age: No strict limit, but radiation exposure limits affect younger astronauts more significantly

### 2. Physiological Monitoring and Countermeasures

#### 2.1 Cardiovascular Health

**Monitoring Requirements:**
- Daily blood pressure and heart rate (PHYS-001)
- Weekly resting and orthostatic vital signs (PHYS-002)
- Monthly echocardiogram (PHYS-003)
- Quarterly maximal exercise testing (PHYS-004)

**Countermeasures:**
- Aerobic exercise: minimum 150 minutes/week moderate intensity (COUNT-001)
- Resistance exercise: minimum 3 sessions/week (COUNT-002)
- LBNP sessions: 2-3 times/week, 30-45 minutes (COUNT-003)
- Pre-landing fluid loading: 1-2 L saline solution (COUNT-004)

#### 2.2 Musculoskeletal Health

**Monitoring Requirements:**
- Quarterly DEXA scans of lumbar spine, hip, femur (PHYS-005)
- Monthly biomarkers: bone-specific alkaline phosphatase, CTX, osteocalcin (PHYS-006)
- Strength testing: monthly or quarterly (PHYS-007)

**Countermeasures:**
- High-load resistance exercise: 6 days/week using ARED (COUNT-005)
- Target: 70-85% of 1-repetition maximum (COUNT-006)
- Exercises must include: squats, deadlifts, heel raises, rowing (COUNT-007)
- Calcium: 1000-1200 mg/day (COUNT-008)
- Vitamin D: 800-1000 IU/day (COUNT-009)

#### 2.3 Vision and Neuro-Ocular Health

**Monitoring Requirements:**
- Pre-flight comprehensive ophthalmologic exam (PHYS-008)
- In-flight: monthly vision testing, quarterly OCT imaging (PHYS-009)
- Optic nerve sheath diameter measurement via ultrasound (PHYS-010)
- Post-flight: comprehensive exam within 3 days, follow-up at 30, 90, 180 days (PHYS-011)

**Countermeasures (experimental):**
- LBNP sessions to reduce headward fluid shifts (COUNT-010)
- Thigh cuffs to occlude venous return (COUNT-011)
- Carbon dioxide control: maintain ISS CO₂ <0.3% (COUNT-012)

### 3. Radiation Protection

#### 3.1 Dosimetry Requirements

**Monitoring:**
- Passive dosimeters (TLD, OSL) worn continuously (RAD-001)
- Active dosimeters providing real-time dose measurement (RAD-002)
- Area radiation monitors throughout spacecraft (RAD-003)
- Tissue-equivalent proportional counters for LET spectrum (RAD-004)

**Limits:**
- Career limits: Age and sex-dependent, based on 3% REID threshold (RAD-005)
- Annual limit: Not to exceed 500 mSv in any single year (RAD-006)
- 30-day limit: Not to exceed 250 mSv in any 30-day period (RAD-007)

#### 3.2 Protection Measures

**Requirements:**
- Water shielding in sleeping quarters: minimum 10 g/cm² (RAD-008)
- Storm shelter with 20-30 g/cm² shielding for SPE events (RAD-009)
- Solar weather monitoring with 15-60 minute warning capability (RAD-010)
- Mission planning to minimize radiation exposure (optimal trajectories, solar cycle consideration) (RAD-011)

### 4. Psychological Health and Crew Dynamics

#### 4.1 Pre-Flight Psychological Assessment

**Requirements:**
- Comprehensive psychological evaluation (PSY-001)
- Personality assessment (e.g., MMPI-2) (PSY-002)
- Stress tolerance testing (PSY-003)
- Team dynamics assessment (PSY-004)
- Family support system evaluation (PSY-005)

#### 4.2 In-Flight Monitoring and Support

**Requirements:**
- Private Psychological Conferences (PPCs): biweekly minimum (PSY-006)
- Mood and sleep questionnaires: weekly (PSY-007)
- Behavioral observations by flight surgeon (PSY-008)
- Family communication support: scheduled private calls (PSY-009)

**Support Resources:**
- 24/7 access to behavioral health officers (PSY-010)
- Psychoactive medications available: SSRIs, anxiolytics, hypnotics (PSY-011)
- Crew entertainment and recreation resources (PSY-012)

### 5. Medical Operations

#### 5.1 Medical Equipment and Supplies

**Required Capabilities:**
- Diagnostic ultrasound with cardiac, abdominal, vascular probes (MED-020)
- Basic laboratory: glucose, electrolytes, CBC, urinalysis, cultures (MED-021)
- Vital signs monitoring: BP, HR, SpO₂, temp, ECG (MED-022)
- Pharmacy: minimum 200 medications covering major drug classes (MED-023)
- Emergency equipment: airway management, resuscitation drugs, defibrillator (MED-024)
- Surgical kit: minor surgery capability (suturing, I&D, limited procedures) (MED-025)

#### 5.2 Crew Medical Training

**Crew Medical Officer (CMO) Requirements:**
- Minimum 40 hours basic medical training (MED-030)
- Ultrasound proficiency certification (MED-031)
- CPR and ACLS certification (MED-032)
- Telemedicine protocol training (MED-033)
- Annual refresher training (MED-034)

**All Crew Requirements:**
- Basic first aid and CPR (8 hours) (MED-035)
- Medical emergency response procedures (MED-036)

#### 5.3 Telemedicine Protocols

**Requirements:**
- Real-time video/audio communication with flight surgeons (MED-040)
- Secure data transmission for medical images and test results (MED-041)
- Decision support systems for autonomous diagnosis (MED-042)
- Medical event reporting within 15 minutes of occurrence (MED-043)

### 6. Long-Duration Mission Requirements (Mars)

#### 6.1 Autonomous Medical Capability

**Requirements:**
- AI-based diagnostic decision support system (MARS-001)
- Comprehensive procedural databases and just-in-time training (MARS-002)
- Advanced CMO training: 1-2 years including emergency procedures (MARS-003)
- Surgical capability: appendectomy, exploratory laparotomy, basic procedures (MARS-004)

#### 6.2 Pharmaceutical Requirements

**Requirements:**
- 3-year pharmaceutical inventory with 20% margin (MARS-010)
- Radiation-shielded medication storage (MARS-011)
- Stability testing and lot rotation protocols (MARS-012)
- On-demand synthesis capability (desirable) (MARS-013)

#### 6.3 Communication Delay Protocols

**Requirements:**
- Asynchronous telemedicine protocols for non-urgent consultations (MARS-020)
- Emergency decision authority granted to CMO with ground notification (MARS-021)
- Comprehensive diagnostic data transmission: 4-hour turnaround (MARS-022)

### 7. Post-Flight Rehabilitation

#### 7.1 Immediate Post-Landing Care

**Requirements:**
- Medical team on-site at landing zone (REHAB-001)
- Cardiovascular assessment within 2 hours (REHAB-002)
- Orthostatic tolerance testing (REHAB-003)
- Initiation of rehabilitation protocols (REHAB-004)

#### 7.2 Recovery Monitoring

**Requirements:**
- DEXA scans: landing, 30 days, 90 days, 180 days, annually (REHAB-010)
- Cardiovascular testing: landing, 30 days, 90 days (REHAB-011)
- Vision assessment: landing, 3 days, 30 days, 90 days, 180 days (REHAB-012)
- Strength and functional testing: monthly until baseline recovery (REHAB-013)

#### 7.3 Rehabilitation Programs

**Requirements:**
- Progressive resistance training: 3-5 times/week (REHAB-020)
- Aerobic reconditioning: 150+ minutes/week (REHAB-021)
- Balance and proprioception training (REHAB-022)
- Nutritional support: high protein, calcium, vitamin D (REHAB-023)

## Compliance and Certification

### Certification Levels

**Level 1: Basic Compliance**
- Pre-flight medical screening completed
- Basic countermeasures implemented (exercise, nutrition)
- Telemedicine capability established
- Post-flight rehabilitation protocols in place

**Level 2: Standard Compliance**
- All Level 1 requirements
- Comprehensive physiological monitoring
- Radiation dosimetry and protection
- Psychological support systems
- Medical operations capability for routine care

**Level 3: Advanced Compliance**
- All Level 2 requirements
- Long-duration mission capability (autonomous medical operations)
- Surgical capability
- Pharmaceutical stability management
- Communication delay protocols

### Audit and Verification

Organizations claiming compliance must:
1. Document adherence to requirements in Quality Management System
2. Undergo third-party audit by WIA-certified auditors
3. Maintain records of medical events, countermeasure compliance, and outcomes
4. Participate in industry data sharing for continuous improvement
