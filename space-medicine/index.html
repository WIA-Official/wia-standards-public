🔬 Standard Overview

About WIA-SPACE-023

The Space Medicine standard provides comprehensive guidelines and protocols for medical care in space environments. It addresses the unique physiological challenges of spaceflight, including microgravity adaptation, radiation exposure, psychological stress, and medical emergency response in isolated environments.

This standard is essential for space agencies, commercial spaceflight operators, astronaut training programs, and researchers working on long-duration space missions. It integrates clinical medicine, aerospace physiology, and preventive healthcare to ensure crew safety and mission success.

🚀 Applications

  • ISS Medical Operations - Crew health monitoring and medical protocols
  • Commercial Spaceflight - Medical screening and passenger health management
  • Deep Space Missions - Mars missions and beyond-LEO medical planning
  • Astronaut Training - Medical preparedness and emergency response training
  • Space Habitat Design - Medical facility requirements for space stations
  • Research & Development - Pharmaceutical studies and medical device testing

🌐 About WIA (World Certification Industry Association)

WIA develops international standards for emerging technologies and industries. Our mission, guided by the philosophy of 弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity), is to create accessible, practical standards that advance human progress and well-being across all domains.

The SPACE series standards focus on space exploration, technology, and infrastructure, supporting humanity's expansion into the cosmos while maintaining health, safety, and sustainability.
