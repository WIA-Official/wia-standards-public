4.1 Storage Systems

Patient storage systems form the core of any cryopreservation facility. These systems must maintain cryogenic temperatures reliably for indefinite periods while providing protection against environmental hazards.

Dewar Specifications

STORAGE DEWAR REQUIREMENTS:
+------------------+----------------------------------+
| Specification    | Requirement                      |
+------------------+----------------------------------+
| Material         | Stainless steel, vacuum insulated|
| Temperature      | Maintain below -196C             |
| Capacity         | Based on patient configuration   |
| Hold Time        | Minimum 30 days static           |
| Boil-off Rate    | Maximum 0.5% per day             |
| Pressure Relief  | Dual redundant safety valves     |
| Monitoring       | Continuous temperature sensing   |
+------------------+----------------------------------+

Dewar Types

  • [ID:WHOLE_BODY] Whole-body dewars: Vertical or horizontal orientation
  • [ID:NEURO] Neuropreservation dewars: Smaller capacity, higher density
  • [ID:INTERMEDIATE] Intermediate dewars: Temporary storage during transfers
  • [ID:BIGFOOT] Large-capacity dewars: Multi-patient storage systems

Storage Unit Configuration

STORAGE CONFIGURATION OPTIONS:
+------------------+-------------+-------------------+
| Type             | Patients    | Footprint         |
+------------------+-------------+-------------------+
| Individual       | 1           | 2.5 m2            |
| Dual             | 2           | 3.5 m2            |
| Quad             | 4           | 5.0 m2            |
| Bigfoot          | 4-8         | 8.0 m2            |
+------------------+-------------+-------------------+

4.4 Monitoring Systems

Temperature Monitoring

Continuous temperature monitoring is the most critical function:

TEMPERATURE MONITORING SPECS:
+------------------+----------------------------------+
| Parameter        | Requirement                      |
+------------------+----------------------------------+
| Sensor Type      | Platinum RTD or thermocouple     |
| Accuracy         | +/- 0.5C                         |
| Sampling Rate    | Minimum every 60 seconds         |
| Sensors per Dewar| Minimum 2 (top and bottom)       |
| Alert Threshold  | Configurable, typically -185C    |
| Data Retention   | Perpetual                        |
+------------------+----------------------------------+

Level Monitoring

  • [OK] Bulk tank levels with trend analysis
  • [OK] Low-level alerts with escalation
  • [OK] Consumption rate calculations

ENVIRONMENTAL SENSORS:
+------------------+----------------------------------+

Chapter Summary

Infrastructure standards define the physical and technical systems that enable safe, reliable cryopreservation. From storage dewars to power systems, monitoring to security, every component must be designed for decades or centuries of continuous operation.

Meeting infrastructure standards requires significant investment but provides the foundation for facility certification and the protection of preserved individuals.
