6.1 Inspection Framework

Regular inspections ensure facilities maintain compliance with CRYO-FACILITY standards throughout their operational lifetime. The inspection framework balances thorough verification with practical operational considerations.

Inspection Types

INSPECTION CATEGORIES:
+----------------------+----------------------------------+
| Type                 | Purpose                          |
+----------------------+----------------------------------+
| Initial Certification| First-time certification         |
| Annual Audit         | Regular compliance check         |
| Unannounced          | Random verification              |
| Complaint Investigation| Response to reported issues    |
| Renewal              | Certification renewal            |
| Special              | Focused follow-up                |
+----------------------+----------------------------------+

Inspection Authority

Inspections are conducted by:

  • [OK] Certified CRYO-FACILITY inspectors
  • [OK] Recognized third-party auditors
  • [OK] Government regulatory agencies (where applicable)
  • [OK] Peer review teams (Level 4 facilities)

6.2 Inspection Scheduling

Scheduled Inspections

INSPECTION FREQUENCY BY LEVEL:
+--------+------------------+--------------------+
| Level  | Annual Audits    | Minimum Notice     |
+--------+------------------+--------------------+
| 1      | 1 per year       | 30 days            |
| 2      | 1 per year       | 30 days            |
| 3      | 2 per year       | 14 days            |
| 4      | Continuous       | N/A                |
+--------+------------------+--------------------+

Unannounced Inspections

Unannounced inspections are conducted:

  • [i] Randomly throughout the certification period
  • [i] More frequently for facilities with past issues
  • [i] Following complaints or incident reports
  • [i] As part of industry-wide compliance verification
UNANNOUNCED INSPECTION TARGETS:
+--------+----------------------------------+
| Level  | Annual Unannounced Visits        |
+--------+----------------------------------+
| 1      | Random selection (10% of sites)  |
| 2      | Minimum 1 per year               |
| 3      | Minimum 2 per year               |
| 4      | Minimum 4 per year               |
+--------+----------------------------------+

6.5 Inspection Findings

Finding Categories

FINDING SEVERITY LEVELS:
+-----------+----------------------------------+
| Category  | Description                      |
+-----------+----------------------------------+
| Observation| Opportunity for improvement     |
| Minor NC  | Does not directly impact safety  |
| Major NC  | Significant impact on compliance |
| Critical  | Immediate safety concern         |
+-----------+----------------------------------+
NC = Non-Conformance

Finding Examples

EXAMPLES BY SEVERITY:
+-----------+----------------------------------+
| Severity  | Example Finding                  |
+-----------+----------------------------------+
| Observation| Training records not centralized|
| Minor NC  | PM slightly overdue on HVAC     |
| Major NC  | Generator test records missing   |
| Critical  | O2 monitor not functioning       |
+-----------+----------------------------------+

Immediate Actions

Critical findings require:

  • [!] Immediate corrective action during inspection
  • [!] Inspector verification of correction
  • [!] Incident report documentation
  • [!] Root cause analysis within 72 hours
  • [!] Follow-up inspection if warranted

6.7 Corrective Action Process

Corrective Action Plan Requirements

For each non-conformance, the CAP must include:

  • [OK] Description of the finding
  • [OK] Root cause analysis
  • [OK] Corrective action to address immediate issue
  • [OK] Preventive action to prevent recurrence
  • [OK] Responsible person and timeline
  • [OK] Verification method

6.9 Non-Conformance Management

Escalation Path

ESCALATION PROCESS:
+------------------+----------------------------------+
| Trigger          | Escalation Action                |
+------------------+----------------------------------+
| CAP not submitted| Warning, 15-day extension        |
| CAP inadequate   | Revision required, meeting       |
| Deadline missed  | Probation, increased monitoring  |
| Repeat finding   | Major NC upgrade, root cause     |
| Multiple majors  | Suspension consideration         |
+------------------+----------------------------------+

Suspension Process

Certification suspension may result from:

  • [!] Uncorrected critical findings
  • [!] Pattern of repeat non-conformances
  • [!] Failure to submit required reports
  • [!] False or misleading information
  • [!] Denial of inspector access

Reinstatement Requirements

REINSTATEMENT PROCESS:
+------------------+----------------------------------+
| Step             | Requirements                     |
+------------------+----------------------------------+
| 1. Application   | Written request for reinstatement|
| 2. Evidence      | Documentation of corrections     |
| 3. Inspection    | Verification inspection          |
| 4. Review        | Committee review of evidence     |
| 5. Decision      | Reinstatement or revocation      |
+------------------+----------------------------------+

Chapter Summary

Inspections and compliance monitoring ensure facilities maintain the standards required for safe cryopreservation operations. Through scheduled audits, unannounced visits, and continuous monitoring, the certification system provides assurance that facilities remain capable of protecting preserved individuals.

The corrective action process addresses findings while the continuous improvement framework ensures the entire industry advances together.
