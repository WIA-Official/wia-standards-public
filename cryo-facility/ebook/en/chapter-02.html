2.1 Location Selection Criteria

The physical location of a cryopreservation facility significantly impacts its long-term viability. Location selection must consider geological stability, climate factors, accessibility, and regulatory environment.

Geological Stability

SEISMIC ZONE REQUIREMENTS:
+-------------+----------------------------------+
| Seismic Zone| Requirements                     |
+-------------+----------------------------------+
| Zone 0-1    | Standard construction            |
| Zone 2      | Enhanced structural support      |
| Zone 3      | Specialized seismic isolation    |
| Zone 4      | Not recommended; special waiver  |
+-------------+----------------------------------+

[!] Facilities in seismic zones 2 and above must implement:

  • Base isolation systems for storage dewars
  • Flexible cryogen supply lines
  • Automatic shutdown protocols for seismic events
  • Post-earthquake inspection procedures

Climate Considerations

  • [i] Ambient temperature affects cooling system efficiency
  • [i] Humidity impacts condensation and ice formation
  • [i] Extreme weather events require resilient design
  • [i] Climate change projections should inform site selection

Accessibility Requirements

  • [OK] Road access for liquid nitrogen delivery vehicles
  • [OK] Emergency vehicle access from multiple directions
  • [OK] Proximity to transportation hubs for patient transfers
  • [OK] Helicopter landing capability for Level 3+ facilities

Storage Area Design

The patient storage area requires specialized construction:

  • [!] Thermal mass to slow temperature changes during emergencies
  • [!] Oxygen monitoring for nitrogen displacement detection
  • [!] Emergency egress with positive pressure vestibules
  • [!] Spill containment for cryogen releases
  • [!] Anti-static flooring to prevent electrical discharge

2.3 Environmental Control Systems

Storage Environment

Patient storage areas must maintain controlled conditions:

  • [OK] Temperature: 18-22C ambient (dewar exterior)
  • [OK] Humidity: 30-50% relative humidity
  • [OK] Air Quality: HEPA filtered, positive pressure
  • [OK] Lighting: Low-UV for preservation of materials

HVAC Requirements

HVAC SPECIFICATIONS BY LEVEL:
+--------+---------------+----------------+-------------+
| Level  | Redundancy    | Backup Time    | Filtration  |
+--------+---------------+----------------+-------------+
| 1      | N+1           | 4 hours        | MERV 13     |
| 2      | 2N            | 8 hours        | MERV 15     |
| 3      | 2N+1          | 24 hours       | HEPA        |
| 4      | 2N+2          | 72 hours       | HEPA + UV   |
+--------+---------------+----------------+-------------+

Atmospheric Monitoring

Continuous monitoring for hazardous conditions:

  • [!] Oxygen level: Alert below 19.5%, alarm below 18%
  • [!] Carbon dioxide: Alert above 0.5%, alarm above 1%
  • [!] Nitrogen concentration: Derived from oxygen displacement
  • [!] Temperature: Multiple sensors per zone
  • [!] Humidity: Protection against condensation

Chapter Summary

Facility requirements establish the physical and operational foundation for safe cryopreservation. From site selection through construction, environmental control, security, and staffing, every aspect must be designed for decades or centuries of reliable operation.

Meeting these requirements demonstrates an organization's commitment to the highest standards of preservation care and provides the basis for certification at the appropriate level.
