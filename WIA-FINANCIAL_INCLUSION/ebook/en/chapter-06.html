弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity)

The Cost of Moving Money

In the modern economy, the ability to send and receive money quickly, securely, and affordably is fundamental. Yet for billions of people, especially those in developing countries and migrant workers sending money home, moving money remains expensive, slow, and cumbersome. Traditional remittance services charge an average of 6-7% in fees—far above the UN Sustainable Development Goal target of 3%.

These high costs disproportionately affect the world's poorest people. A migrant worker sending $200 home to support family might pay $14 in fees—money that could have purchased food, medicine, or school supplies. Annually, these fees extract over $30 billion from some of the world's most vulnerable populations. Digital payment systems and mobile money offer the potential to dramatically reduce these costs while improving speed and accessibility.

Global Remittance Statistics

  • $656 billion in remittances sent to low and middle-income countries in 2024
  • $30+ billion lost annually to high remittance fees
  • 6.2% average cost to send $200 internationally (Q3 2024)
  • 200 million migrant workers globally sending money to families
  • 800 million people receive remittances, representing 1 in 9 globally

Digital Payment Infrastructure

The WIA-FINANCIAL_INCLUSION standard defines a comprehensive payment infrastructure supporting domestic and international transfers, merchant payments, bill payments, and more.

Merchant Payment Acceptance

Enabling merchants—from street vendors to large retailers—to accept digital payments is crucial for financial inclusion. When merchants accept digital payments, consumers have more reason to use digital wallets, creating a virtuous cycle.

Merchant Payment Methods

  • QR Code Payments: Customer scans merchant QR code, enters amount, confirms payment
  • NFC/Contactless: Tap phone to merchant terminal for instant payment
  • Payment Links: Merchant sends payment request link via SMS or messaging apps
  • USSD Merchant Code: Customer dials *123*merchant_code*amount#
  • Agent-Assisted: Agent processes payment on behalf of merchant or customer
  • Sound-Based Payment: Data transmitted via sound waves for basic phones

Bill Payment Systems

Bill payment is often the first use case that brings people into digital financial services. The ability to pay utilities, school fees, and government services digitally provides immediate, tangible value.

Common Bill Payment Categories

  • Utilities: Electricity, water, gas, internet, cable TV
  • Education: School fees, examination fees, hostel fees
  • Government: Taxes, licenses, permits, fines
  • Insurance: Health insurance, life insurance, vehicle insurance
  • Telecommunications: Postpaid bills, mobile data, landline
  • Transportation: Public transit cards, toll payments, parking
  • Healthcare: Hospital bills, prescription payments

Security and Fraud Prevention

Payment systems are prime targets for fraud. The WIA standard mandates comprehensive security measures while maintaining usability for low-literacy users.

Fraud Detection Mechanisms

  • Velocity Checks: Limit number of transactions in time period
  • Amount Limits: Flag transactions exceeding user's normal pattern
  • Geo-Location Analysis: Detect impossible travel patterns
  • Device Fingerprinting: Identify transactions from unknown devices
  • Behavioral Biometrics: Analyze typing patterns, swipe speed, pressure
  • Network Analysis: Identify suspicious transaction networks
  • Machine Learning: Real-time scoring of transaction fraud risk
  • Multi-Factor Authentication: Require additional verification for high-risk transactions

Real-Time Fraud Scoring

Each transaction receives a fraud risk score (0-100) based on multiple factors. High-risk transactions trigger additional authentication or blocking.

Score 0-30 (Low Risk): Process normally

Score 31-60 (Medium Risk): Additional SMS OTP required

Score 61-80 (High Risk): Biometric verification + SMS OTP required

Score 81-100 (Very High Risk): Transaction blocked, manual review required

Key Takeaways

  • $656 billion in remittances flow to low and middle-income countries annually, with over $30 billion lost to high fees
  • Digital payment infrastructure with real-time processing can reduce remittance costs from 6-7% to under 2%
  • The WIA standard supports multiple payment methods including QR codes, NFC, USSD, and payment links for universal merchant acceptance
  • Bill payment systems for utilities, education, and government services often serve as the entry point to digital financial services
  • Interoperability between payment platforms through central switches or API gateways enables seamless cross-platform transactions
  • Cross-border remittances benefit from direct corridors between compliant providers, eliminating intermediaries and reducing costs
  • Merchant payment acceptance with low fees (0.5-2.5%) drives adoption of digital wallets among consumers
  • Real-time fraud detection using ML models, behavioral analysis, and risk scoring protects users while maintaining usability
