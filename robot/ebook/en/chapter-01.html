Chapter 1: Introduction to Assistive Robot Standards

1.1 The WIA Robot Standard Landscape

Assistive robotics spans a remarkable breadth of human need — from powered exoskeletons that restore walking after spinal cord injury, to collaborative surgical robots that extend a surgeon's precision beyond natural limits. The WIA Robot Standard (WIA-ROBOT) provides a unified interoperability layer across all these domains, built on three pillars: safety (ISO 10218-1/2, ISO 15066), data exchange (ROS 2 REP-2000 lifecycle, HL7 FHIR R5 for health data), and open interfaces (OpenAPI 3.1 REST gateway, DDS-RTPS at the middleware layer).

1.3 The ROS 2 Foundation

All WIA Robot implementations are expected to expose a ROS 2 interface. ROS 2 (Robot Operating System 2), standardised under REP-2000 (platform support policy) and REP-2004 (package quality categories), provides the inter-process communication backbone via DDS/RTPS. As of 2025, the active LTS release is ROS 2 Jazzy Jalisco (EOL 2029-05); prior LTS releases include Humble Hawksbill (EOL 2027-05) and Iron Irwini (EOL 2024-11).

The minimum ROS 2 node lifecycle required by WIA-ROBOT:

ros2 lifecycle list /wia_robot_node
# Managed lifecycle states (REP-146):
# unconfigured → configuring → inactive → activating → active
# active → deactivating → inactive → cleaning_up → unconfigured
# any state → errorprocessing → unconfigured

1.4 DDS/RTPS Middleware

The DDS (Data Distribution Service, OMG standard) transport layer handles publish/subscribe communication between robot nodes. WIA-ROBOT mandates QoS profiles calibrated to safety-critical constraints:

  • RELIABLE + TRANSIENT_LOCAL for all command and E-Stop topics — guaranteed delivery with late-joiner durability.
  • BEST_EFFORT + VOLATILE for high-rate sensor streams (joint states, IMU at 50 Hz) — minimise latency, accept occasional drops.
  • Deadline of 100 ms on command topics per ISO 10218-1 §5.4.2 response budget.

Supported RMW (ROS Middleware) implementations: rmw_fastrtps_cpp (eProsima FastDDS 3.x), rmw_cyclonedds_cpp (Eclipse Cyclone DDS 0.10), rmw_connextdds (RTI Connext DDS 7.x).

1.5 Functional Safety Overview

WIA-ROBOT safety requirements are grounded in two IEC standards:

  • IEC 61508 — Functional Safety of E/E/PE Safety-related Systems. Assistive robots targeting SIL-2 require a probability of dangerous failure per hour (PFH) of 10⁻⁷ to 10⁻⁶.
  • IEC 62061:2021 — Safety of Machinery: Functional Safety. Defines Safety Integrity Levels for machine control systems; Care Robots typically target SIL 1–2.

Emergency stop (E-Stop) signals are published to /emergency_stop with RELIABLE/TRANSIENT_LOCAL QoS and must be processed within 10 ms of publication. The hardware watchdog must verify E-Stop circuit continuity at a minimum of 10 Hz.

1.6 Structure of This Ebook

This ebook follows the WIA four-phase architecture:

  • Chapter 2 — Data Formats: URDF/XACRO robot models, joint state schemas, sensor message types.
  • Chapter 3 — API Interface: OpenAPI 3.1 REST gateway, WebSocket telemetry streaming, JWT authentication.
  • Chapter 4 — Communication Protocols: DDS QoS profiles, ROS 2 topic architecture, MQTT 5 bridge for clinical systems.
  • Chapter 5 — MoveIt 2 Motion Planning: OMPL planners, STOMP, PILZ industrial profiles, collision checking.
  • Chapter 6 — SLAM and Navigation: Cartographer 2D, ORB-SLAM3, ROS 2 Nav2 planner stack.
  • Chapter 7 — Isaac Sim Integration: NVIDIA Omniverse, sim-to-real transfer, synthetic data for training.
  • Chapter 8 — Compliance and Certification: ISO 10218, ISO 15066, IEC 61508, FDA 510(k)/PMA pathways.
