【 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY 】

The World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released the next-generation software development and operations integration standard 'WIA-COMP-011: DevOps Standard' under the MIT license for free public use. This standard provides a comprehensive framework that improves software quality, reduces deployment friction, and enhances team collaboration through complete integration of development and operations, including CI/CD pipelines, Infrastructure as Code (IaC), monitoring, container orchestration, GitOps, and DevSecOps.

The Dev vs Ops War: Throwing Code Over the Wall

[IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER_2: Problems with Traditional Dev-Ops Silos]

Many organizations in the Republic of Korea and worldwide suffer from deep divides between development and operations teams. Developers throw code over the wall saying "it works on my machine," operations teams struggle with 3 AM failure recovery, and quarterly deployments erode competitiveness.

Key Issues:

  • Team Silos: Development and operations opposing with different goals
  • Manual Deployment: All deployment processes performed manually by humans
  • Slow Releases: Quarterly deployments causing slow market response
  • Deployment Failures: Frequent failures from manual process errors
  • Rollback Hell: Hours required for rollback when problems occur
  • Lack of Visibility: Difficulty understanding system state
  • 3 AM Incident Response: Manual recovery without automation
"Every software team has the right to have development and operations collaborate toward one goal. From code commit to production deployment should be automated, teams should safely deploy dozens of times per day, and incidents should be automatically detected and recovered. DevOps is not just tools—it's a revolution in culture and practices."
- Sangmin Park, CEO, SmileStory Inc. / WIA

WIA-COMP-011: Fully Automated DevOps

[IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER_3: Future of WIA DevOps Solution]

The WIA-COMP-011 standard provides a complete DevOps framework realizing continuous integration/deployment, infrastructure automation, and real-time monitoring:

Core Features:

  • CI/CD Pipelines: Automated build, test, and deployment workflows
  • Infrastructure as Code: Version-controlled infrastructure automation
  • Monitoring & Observability: Real-time system health tracking
  • Configuration Management: Automated server and application configuration
  • Container Orchestration: Docker, Kubernetes deployment patterns
  • GitOps: Git-based infrastructure and application deployment
  • DevSecOps: Security integration and automated scanning
  • Incident Management: Automated detection and response

About WIA

The World Certification Industry Association (WIA) is an international standards organization dedicated to researching, developing, and distributing next-generation technology standards. Based on the philosophy of 弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity), WIA releases all technology standards under the MIT license for free use by developers and enterprises worldwide.

WIA is developing over 600 standards, from Rust-based high-performance systems to the TypeScript/JavaScript ecosystem. All standards include production-ready SDKs, CLIs, and detailed documentation.

About the CEO

Sangmin Park is the founder of SmileStory Inc. and Chief Architect of WIA, leading the Republic of Korea's first one-person standards organization. As a DevOps, cloud-native, and Rust expert, he personally designs over 600 WIA standards, believing "technology should be shared equally with everyone," and releases all standards for free.
