4.1 TSMC Advanced Packaging Portfolio

TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company) has established itself as the global leader in advanced semiconductor packaging, with a comprehensive portfolio of technologies that address the full spectrum of integration requirements. From high-performance computing to mobile devices, TSMC's packaging platforms enable the world's most advanced semiconductor products.

TSMC's advanced packaging journey began in 2012 with the introduction of the Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate (CoWoS) platform and the Integrated Fan-Out (InFO) technology in 2016. Since then, the company has continuously expanded and refined these platforms to meet the demanding requirements of AI accelerators, high-performance computing, and advanced mobile applications.

TSMC Packaging Capacity Expansion

In response to explosive demand driven by AI, TSMC has announced massive capacity expansions for advanced packaging. CoWoS capacity is projected to more than double from 2023 to 2025, with additional fabs in Taiwan and planned facilities in Japan and the United States. This investment reflects the critical role of advanced packaging in enabling next-generation AI systems.

Platform Overview

TSMC's advanced packaging portfolio can be broadly categorized into two families:

  • CoWoS Family: Silicon interposer-based platforms for the highest bandwidth and performance applications. Includes CoWoS-S, CoWoS-R, and CoWoS-L variants.
  • InFO Family: Fan-out wafer-level packaging platforms offering compact form factors and cost-effective integration. Includes InFO, InFO_oS, InFO_SoW, and InFO-LSI variants.

4.2 CoWoS-S: Silicon Interposer Platform

CoWoS-S (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate with Silicon interposer) represents TSMC's flagship advanced packaging platform for the most demanding high-performance applications. The platform uses a silicon interposer manufactured on TSMC's 65nm process to provide ultra-high-density interconnects between logic dies and HBM memory stacks.

CoWoS-S Technical Specifications

The silicon interposer enables fine-pitch routing that cannot be achieved with organic substrates, supporting the bandwidth requirements of modern AI accelerators.

CoWoS-S Manufacturing Process

The CoWoS-S manufacturing process involves three main phases: interposer fabrication, chip-on-wafer assembly, and final packaging.

  1. Interposer Fabrication: Silicon wafers are processed through TSMC's 65nm backend line to create TSVs and RDL layers. Multiple metal layers (typically 4-8) provide routing capacity.
  2. Chip-on-Wafer (CoW): Known-good dies (logic and HBM stacks) are bonded to the interposer wafer using thermocompression bonding or mass reflow processes.
  3. Wafer-on-Substrate (WoS): The assembled CoW units are singulated and flip-chip bonded to organic substrates with controlled collapse chip connection (C4) bumps.
  4. Underfill and Encapsulation: Capillary underfill protects micro-bumps, and molding compound encapsulates the assembly.

Notable CoWoS-S Implementations

CoWoS-S has enabled some of the most advanced semiconductor products:

  • NVIDIA H100/H200: Integrates a 814 mm² GPU with 6 HBM3/HBM3E stacks, achieving over 3 TB/s memory bandwidth. The package represents one of the largest and most complex CoWoS implementations.
  • NVIDIA B100/B200: The Blackwell architecture pushes CoWoS further with dual GPU dies and 8 HBM3E stacks in a single package.
  • AMD MI300X: Combines multiple GPU and I/O chiplets with HBM3 stacks, leveraging CoWoS for heterogeneous integration.
  • Google TPU v5: Custom AI accelerator using CoWoS for optimal memory bandwidth configuration.

4.3 CoWoS-R: RDL Interposer Platform

CoWoS-R (Redistribution Layer interposer) represents a cost-optimized variant of the CoWoS platform that replaces the silicon interposer with an organic RDL interposer. While offering somewhat lower interconnect density than CoWoS-S, CoWoS-R provides a more cost-effective solution for applications that don't require the ultimate bandwidth of silicon interposers.

The RDL interposer is fabricated using polymer dielectric layers and copper redistribution, similar to advanced fan-out packaging but with higher layer counts and finer features. This approach eliminates the cost and complexity of silicon interposer manufacturing while still enabling multi-die integration with high bandwidth.

Target Applications

CoWoS-R is well-suited for applications that benefit from multi-die integration but don't require the extreme bandwidth of HBM integration:

  • High-performance networking ASICs
  • Data center switches and routers
  • Multi-chiplet compute platforms
  • Applications transitioning from monolithic to chiplet designs

4.4 CoWoS-L: Large Scale Integration

CoWoS-L (Local Silicon Interconnect) represents TSMC's solution for the largest and most complex multi-die systems. By combining an organic RDL substrate with embedded silicon interconnect bridges (similar in concept to Intel's EMIB), CoWoS-L enables integration of multiple chiplets and HBM stacks across package sizes exceeding 5,000 mm².

CoWoS-L Architecture

CoWoS-L uses small silicon bridges embedded in an organic RDL interposer to provide high-density connections where needed (e.g., between compute chiplets and HBM stacks) while using lower-cost organic routing for longer-distance connections. This hybrid approach optimizes both cost and performance for large-scale integration.

Local Silicon Interconnect (LSI) Technology

The LSI bridges in CoWoS-L are small pieces of silicon (typically less than 100 mm²) that provide high-density interconnects in specific locations. Key characteristics include:

  • Bridge Size: Typically 6mm × 16mm or similar dimensions
  • Interconnect Density: Comparable to full silicon interposers (~0.5 μm L/S)
  • Placement: Strategically located at high-bandwidth interfaces
  • Embedding: Bridges are embedded in organic substrate cavities

Advantages Over Full Silicon Interposers

CoWoS-L offers several advantages for large-scale integration:

  • Scalability: Organic substrates can be manufactured at sizes exceeding practical silicon interposer limits
  • Cost Efficiency: Silicon is used only where high-density routing is essential
  • Thermal Management: Organic substrates can include enhanced thermal features
  • Flexibility: Bridge placement can be optimized for specific chiplet arrangements

4.5 InFO Technology Family

Integrated Fan-Out (InFO) technology represents TSMC's wafer-level packaging platform, originally developed for mobile applications but now expanded to address a wide range of integration needs. Unlike CoWoS, InFO does not use an interposer; instead, dies are embedded in a molding compound and connected through RDL layers built directly on the reconstituted wafer.

InFO Manufacturing Process

The InFO process flow differs fundamentally from traditional flip-chip packaging:

  1. Die Placement: Known-good dies are placed face-down on a temporary carrier with precise alignment.
  2. Molding: Epoxy molding compound encapsulates the dies, creating a reconstituted wafer.
  3. Carrier Removal: The temporary carrier is released, exposing die pads.
  4. RDL Formation: Multiple redistribution layers are built up, connecting die pads to package I/O.
  5. Ball Attach: Solder balls are attached for board-level connection.
  6. Singulation: Individual packages are cut from the reconstituted wafer.

4.6 SoIC: System on Integrated Chips

SoIC (System on Integrated Chips) represents TSMC's most advanced 3D integration technology, using hybrid bonding to achieve chip-to-chip connections with pitches below 10 micrometers. This technology enables near-monolithic integration of separately manufactured dies.

SoIC Technology Variants

  • SoIC-F (Face-to-Face): Two dies bonded with active surfaces facing each other. No TSVs required for the top die.
  • SoIC-B (Face-to-Back): Multiple dies stacked vertically with TSVs connecting through the stack.
  • SoIC-X: Combination of SoIC with CoWoS or InFO for complete system integration.

Hybrid Bonding Process

TSMC's hybrid bonding technology enables direct copper-to-copper connections between dies without traditional bumps or pillars. The process involves:

  • Ultra-flat surface preparation (CMP to sub-nm roughness)
  • Plasma activation for dielectric-to-dielectric bonding
  • Precise alignment (sub-100nm accuracy)
  • Room-temperature pre-bonding followed by thermal anneal
  • Copper interdiffusion creating metallic bonds

4.7 Platform Selection Guidelines

Selecting the appropriate TSMC packaging platform requires careful consideration of application requirements, cost constraints, and time-to-market objectives.

Cost Considerations

Platform costs vary significantly based on complexity and volume:

  • CoWoS-S: Highest cost due to silicon interposer; justified for premium HPC/AI products
  • CoWoS-L: Moderate cost with scalability for large integration
  • CoWoS-R: Lower cost alternative for applications not requiring HBM
  • InFO: Cost-effective for high-volume mobile applications

Summary

TSMC's advanced packaging portfolio provides a comprehensive set of solutions for the full spectrum of integration requirements. From the ultra-high-bandwidth CoWoS-S platform enabling AI accelerators to the cost-effective InFO technology powering mobile devices, these platforms form the foundation of modern heterogeneous integration.

Key Takeaways

  • CoWoS-S provides the highest bandwidth through silicon interposers, enabling AI accelerator products
  • CoWoS-L extends integration scale using embedded silicon bridges in organic substrates
  • InFO technology offers cost-effective wafer-level packaging for mobile and embedded applications
  • SoIC enables near-monolithic integration through hybrid bonding at sub-10 μm pitches
  • Platform selection depends on bandwidth requirements, die count, form factor, and cost targets
