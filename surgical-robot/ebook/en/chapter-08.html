Implementation & Certification

8.1 Introduction to Implementation Strategy

Successful WIA-ROB-006 implementation requires careful planning, systematic execution, thorough testing, and formal certification. This chapter provides manufacturers, healthcare institutions, and third-party developers with comprehensive guidance for achieving WIA-ROB-006 compliance—from initial assessment through certification and ongoing maintenance.

8.4 WIA Certification Process

Certification Levels Review

  • Level 1 (Core): Phase 1 data formats + basic safety protocols
  • Level 2 (Advanced): Levels 1 + Phase 2 APIs + enhanced safety
  • Level 3 (Complete): All phases including full OR integration

Certification Steps

  1. Pre-Application Consultation: WIA advises on requirements, scope, timeline
  2. Formal Application: Submit application with preliminary documentation
  3. Document Review: WIA engineering team reviews technical documentation (4-8 weeks)
  4. Laboratory Testing: Independent facility conducts validation testing (8-12 weeks)
  5. Clinical Validation: Real-world procedures at certified sites (20-50 cases)
  6. Certification Board Review: Expert panel evaluates all evidence
  7. Certification Decision: Grant appropriate level or request remediation
  8. Certificate Issuance: WIA-ROB-006 certification valid for 3 years
  9. Annual Surveillance: Ongoing monitoring, random testing, outcome data review
  10. Recertification: Every 3 years, or upon major system updates

8.6 Post-Market Surveillance and Updates

Continuous Monitoring

  • Performance Metrics: Track operative times, conversion rates, complications
  • Technical Failures: Monitor system errors, E-stop activations, malfunctions
  • Software Updates: Notify WIA of major updates requiring re-validation
  • Adverse Events: Report patient harm incidents to WIA and regulators
  • Field Actions: Document recalls, safety notices, corrective actions

Standard Evolution

WIA-ROB-006 undergoes continuous improvement:

  • Annual minor updates (version 1.1, 1.2, etc.) for clarifications and bug fixes
  • Major revisions (version 2.0, 3.0) every 3-5 years incorporating technological advances
  • Public comment periods for proposed changes
  • Backward compatibility requirements ensuring smooth transitions
  • Grace periods for compliance with new requirements

8.8 Success Stories and Case Studies

Academic Medical Center Implementation

Institution: University Hospital (1,000 beds)
Challenge: Operating two incompatible robotic platforms (da Vinci Xi and Hugo RAS)
Solution: Adopted WIA-ROB-006 Level 3 certified robots enabling unified data platform
Results:

  • 50% reduction in staff training time (cross-platform skills)
  • Unified research database with 2,000+ procedures
  • 3 multi-institutional studies launched using standardized data
  • AI-based complication prediction model developed
  • 20% reduction in per-procedure costs through competitive consumables

Rural Hospital Access Expansion

Institution: Regional Hospital (200 beds, rural area)
Challenge: Could not justify $2M da Vinci investment for limited case volume
Solution: Adopted lower-cost WIA-ROB-006 certified platform ($500K)
Results:

  • Robotic surgery available to underserved population
  • Patient transfers to urban centers reduced 40%
  • Surgeon recruitment improved (advanced technology available)
  • Remote proctoring from academic center enabled safe program launch
  • Break-even achieved after 18 months (vs. never with higher-cost system)

弘益人間 · Benefit All Humanity

WIA-ROB-006 embodies the ancient Korean philosophy of 弘益人間 (Hongik Ingan) by democratizing access to advanced surgical technology. Through standardization, we reduce costs, enable competition, foster innovation, and ensure that the benefits of robotic surgery extend beyond wealthy institutions to serve all patients—regardless of geography, economic status, or institutional resources. This is technology serving humanity, not humanity serving technology.
