Chapter 3: WIA-AGRI-014 Standard Overview

Overview: Architecture, core components, and the four-phase implementation approach of the WIA Agricultural Supply Chain Standard.

The WIA-AGRI-014 standard provides a layered architecture for comprehensive farm-to-fork traceability:

3.2 Core Principles

Interoperability First

All data formats and APIs follow open standards (JSON, REST, GS1 EPCIS) ensuring compatibility with existing ERP, WMS, and POS systems.

Blockchain Immutability

Critical events (harvest, processing, shipping) are recorded on distributed ledgers, creating tamper-proof audit trails.

Real-Time Visibility

IoT sensors provide sub-second updates on temperature, humidity, location, and product condition.

Privacy by Design

Sensitive business data (pricing, contracts) remains private while public provenance data (origin, certifications) is transparent.

3.4 Key Components

Shipment Tracking

End-to-end visibility from farm gate to retail shelf with real-time location updates and estimated arrival times.

Cold Chain Monitoring

Continuous temperature/humidity tracking with automated alerts for threshold violations and predictive analytics for quality estimation.

Blockchain Provenance

Immutable records of product journey including harvest date, processing steps, quality checks, and certifications.

Quality Assurance

Digital quality control records with laboratory test results, visual inspection data, and automated grade assignment.

Consumer Verification

QR codes linking to blockchain-verified provenance, enabling consumers to scan products and view complete farm-to-fork journey.

3.6 WIA Ecosystem Integration

WIA-AGRI-014 integrates seamlessly with other WIA standards:

  • WIA-BLOCKCHAIN: Provenance recording and verification
  • WIA-VC: Verifiable credentials for certifications
  • WIA-IoT: Device registration and management
  • WIA-CARBON: Carbon footprint calculation
  • WIA-IDENTITY: Farmer and operator identity management
