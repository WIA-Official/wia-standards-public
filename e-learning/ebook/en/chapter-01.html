Chapter 1

Introduction to E-Learning Standards

E-learning has revolutionized education by breaking down geographical barriers and making knowledge accessible to anyone with an internet connection. The WIA-EDU-002 standard provides a comprehensive framework for building modern, scalable, and interoperable e-learning platforms.

What is E-Learning?

E-learning (electronic learning) refers to the delivery of educational content through digital means. It encompasses:

  • Online courses and degree programs
  • Video lectures and recorded content
  • Interactive simulations and exercises
  • Live virtual classrooms
  • Mobile learning applications
  • Corporate training and professional development

Why Standardization Matters

Without standards, e-learning systems become isolated silos. The WIA-EDU-002 standard addresses critical challenges:

  • Interoperability: Content created in one platform should work in another
  • Portability: Students should be able to transfer their progress and credentials
  • Quality: Consistent learning experiences across different providers
  • Scalability: Systems that can grow from hundreds to millions of users
  • Accessibility: Ensuring content is available to learners with disabilities

The 弘益人間 (Hongik Ingan) Philosophy

The WIA-EDU-002 standard is built on the principle of 弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity). Education should:

  • Be accessible to everyone, regardless of location or economic status
  • Preserve and share knowledge across generations
  • Empower individuals to reach their full potential
  • Foster global collaboration and cultural exchange

Key Components of WIA-EDU-002

The standard is organized into four major phases:

  1. Content Format: SCORM, xAPI, and multimedia content standards
  2. Learning Management: Course catalogs, enrollment, and progress tracking
  3. Interactive Learning: Live classrooms, gamification, and collaboration
  4. Certification & Integration: Digital credentials and third-party integrations

Who Should Use This Standard?

  • Educational Institutions: Universities, K-12 schools, training centers
  • Corporate Training: Employee onboarding and skill development
  • Content Creators: Course developers and instructional designers
  • Platform Developers: LMS and edtech software companies
  • Standards Bodies: Governments and certification authorities
