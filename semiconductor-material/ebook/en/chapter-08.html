8.1 Scope and Purpose

8.1.1 Scope

This standard establishes specifications, testing methods, and qualification requirements for materials used in advanced semiconductor manufacturing at technology nodes of 5nm and below. It covers the complete materials ecosystem including:

  • Silicon wafer substrates and epitaxial materials
  • High-k dielectric and metal gate materials
  • Interconnect metals (Cu, Co, Ru, Mo) and barriers
  • Photoresists and patterning materials for EUV lithography
  • Specialty gases and wet chemicals
  • CMP consumables and cleaning materials
  • Advanced materials for GAA and CFET architectures

8.1.2 Purpose

The WIA-SEMI-MAT-001 standard aims to:

  1. Establish uniform specifications for semiconductor materials across the global supply chain
  2. Define testing methodologies that ensure consistent quality assessment
  3. Provide qualification frameworks for new material introduction
  4. Enable supply chain interoperability through standardized requirements
  5. Support continued technology advancement through materials innovation
  6. Promote sustainability and safety in materials handling

Relationship to Other Standards

WIA-SEMI-MAT-001 complements and extends existing SEMI standards (M1, M55, C1, etc.) with additional requirements specific to sub-5nm technology nodes. It incorporates requirements from ISO 9001 quality management systems and ISO 14001 environmental management systems.

8.9 Qualification Requirements

8.9.1 New Material Introduction Process

  1. Phase 1 - Initial Assessment: Material data sheet review, preliminary compatibility assessment
  2. Phase 2 - Laboratory Qualification: Small-scale testing against WIA specifications
  3. Phase 3 - Process Integration: Equipment trials, process window determination
  4. Phase 4 - Device Validation: Electrical testing, reliability assessment
  5. Phase 5 - Production Qualification: Full-scale manufacturing validation

8.9.2 Documentation Requirements

  • Certificate of Analysis (CoA) for each lot
  • Safety Data Sheet (SDS) per GHS requirements
  • Process of Record (POR) documentation
  • Change notification procedure compliance
  • Traceability records (lot number, manufacturing date, location)

8.9.3 Ongoing Qualification

  • Annual re-qualification for critical materials
  • Change notification and assessment for any modification
  • Continuous monitoring through SPC programs
  • Failure analysis and corrective action procedures

8.10 Environmental and Safety Requirements

8.10.1 Environmental Compliance

  • GWP reporting for fluorinated gases
  • Abatement requirements for PFC emissions
  • Wastewater treatment specifications
  • Hazardous waste classification and disposal
  • Energy efficiency targets for material processing

8.10.2 Safety Requirements

  • SDS compliance with GHS (Globally Harmonized System)
  • Exposure limit documentation (TLV, PEL)
  • Emergency response procedures
  • PPE requirements specification
  • Spill containment and cleanup procedures

Compliance Certification

Materials meeting all WIA-SEMI-MAT-001 specifications may display the WIA compliance mark and be listed in the WIA Certified Materials Registry. Certification requires annual audit by accredited third-party assessors.
