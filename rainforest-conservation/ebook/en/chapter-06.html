Chapter 06: Phase 3 — Federation Protocol

6.1 Phase 3 in one paragraph

Phase 3 specifies how independent forest-data hosts (satellite vendors, NGOs, indigenous community organisations, national forest agencies, REDD+ verification bodies) build trust over time, exchange observations without merging databases, defend against replay, and enforce the indigenous-community-consent boundary.

6.2 Roles

Satellite vendor, ground survey team, indigenous community custodian, national forest agency, REDD+ verification body, research aggregator. A single legal entity MAY play multiple roles; trust is established by federation handshake and recorded in signed receipts.

6.3 Indigenous community consent envelope

The community consent envelope is the gating control on cross-host data flow within community-claimed territory. It is signed by the community custodian (typically a designated community council or recognised representative), declares the audiences (NGO partners, government agencies, REDD+ verification bodies) authorised to read observations within the territory, declares the scopes (basic_landcover, biodiversity_survey, carbon_stock, full_research_dataset) of each authorisation, and is revocable.

6.4 Federation handshake

Reuses the WIA-SOCIAL Phase 3 §5 receipt shape for cross-WIA-family library reuse. State machine: IDLE → PENDING (origin verifies signature) → ACCEPTED (origin issues receipt) → optional REVOKED. Receipts persist for at least 25 years per typical international forest data retention norms.

6.5 Replay defence

Each signed envelope carries a 96-bit nonce + RFC 3339 timestamp. Receivers MUST reject envelopes with skew greater than ±300 seconds, reject envelopes whose (signer, nonce) tuple has been seen within the last 600 seconds, and maintain the seen-nonce cache for at least 600 seconds.

6.6 Audience matrix

Public sees aggregate forest statistics only. Community custodian sees full data within their territory. NGO partners see the per-scope subset their consent grants. National forest agency sees full data within national jurisdiction (subject to community consent). REDD+ verification body sees verifiable carbon-stock evidence per scope. Research aggregator sees de-identified data per explicit research consent.

Indigenous-Sovereignty First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through the question: does this make community-claimed territory data more useful while honouring indigenous sovereignty, or just more accessible to outside actors? When the two conflict, sovereignty wins. That is why the standard mandates explicit community consent before cross-host data flow within community territory, refuses to allow national agencies to bypass community custodians, and requires implementations to surface custodian provenance so downstream consumers can verify whose data they are working with.
