Chapter 07: Phase 4 — Integration with Earth Observation Pipelines

7.1 Phase 4 in one paragraph

Phase 4 specifies how WIA Rainforest Conservation integrates with existing forest-data systems: REDD+ MRV reporting templates, FAO Global Forest Resources Assessment vocabularies, GBIF Darwin Core biodiversity data, Sentinel-2 / Landsat satellite imagery pipelines, OpenStreetMap landcover layers, and the WIA family standards.

7.2 REDD+ MRV bridge

The bridge maps WIA forest_condition_observation + deforestation_event + carbon_stock_estimate envelopes to REDD+ MRV reporting templates. National forest agencies subscribing to the bridge can ingest community and vendor data through one pipe, then submit national-level MRV reports to the UNFCCC.

7.3 FAO Global Forest Resources Assessment vocabulary

FAO FRA publishes a country-level forest taxonomy every five years. WIA Phase 1 vocabulary aligns with FAO FRA terms; the alignment table is at spec/profiles/fao-fra.md. When FAO publishes a new FRA edition, the alignment table is updated; the standard supports both the new and the prior edition vocabulary tokens via the additive minor-version rules.

7.4 GBIF Darwin Core bridge

The Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF) accepts species-occurrence data in Darwin Core Archive format. The bridge transforms WIA biodiversity_survey envelopes to Darwin Core for direct GBIF submission. Indigenous community consent flows into the Darwin Core dataset metadata so GBIF consumers can verify the provenance.

7.5 Satellite imagery pipelines

Sentinel-2 (via Copernicus / ESA) and Landsat (via USGS) are the public-domain satellite imagery sources most commonly used for deforestation detection. The bridge ingests scene metadata and links it to WIA forest_condition_observation envelopes via the scene_id field. Vendor satellite imagery (Planet Labs, Maxar) follows similar pattern with vendor-specific scene id formats.

7.6 WIA family integration

WIA-OMNI-API holds community custodian identity, government agency credentials, satellite vendor accounts. WIA-AIR-SHIELD provides transport hardening for cross-organisation federation. WIA-ACCESSIBILITY drives community-facing UI accommodations including low-bandwidth modes for remote community access and multilingual labels in indigenous languages.

7.7 Compliance mappings

The patient-consent pattern from WIA medical standards is reused here as community-consent. UNDRIP (UN Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples) Article 18 right to participate in decision-making is encoded in the indigenous community consent envelope (Phase 3 §3). UNFCCC REDD+ MRV reporting requirements are met through the Phase 4 REDD+ bridge.

Indigenous-Sovereignty First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through the question: does this make community-claimed territory data more useful while honouring indigenous sovereignty, or just more accessible to outside actors? When the two conflict, sovereignty wins. That is why the standard mandates explicit community consent before cross-host data flow within community territory, refuses to allow national agencies to bypass community custodians, and requires implementations to surface custodian provenance so downstream consumers can verify whose data they are working with.
