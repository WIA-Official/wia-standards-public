Chapter 4: Soil Moisture Monitoring

Water is essential for plant growth, and soil moisture monitoring is critical for irrigation management. This chapter explores moisture sensing technologies and their application in water conservation.

Moisture Measurement Methods

The WIA-AGRI-005 standard supports three primary methods: capacitance sensors measure dielectric constant changes with moisture content, TDR (Time Domain Reflectometry) measures signal travel time through soil, and FDR (Frequency Domain Reflectometry) measures impedance at specific frequencies.

Precision Irrigation

Real-time moisture data enables irrigation only when needed, where needed. Studies show 20-50% water savings compared to scheduled irrigation. Smart irrigation systems can automatically activate based on moisture thresholds customized for specific crops and growth stages.

Drought Management

Continuous moisture monitoring helps farmers respond proactively to drought conditions, prioritizing water allocation and implementing deficit irrigation strategies when necessary.
