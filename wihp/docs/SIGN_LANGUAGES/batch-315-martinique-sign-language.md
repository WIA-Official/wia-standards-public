# Martinique Sign Language
## Sign Language of Martinique (French Caribbean)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | French Sign Language (LSF) |
| **Native Name** | Langue des Signes Française |
| **Country** | Martinique (France) |
| **Status** | French Overseas Region |
| **Capital** | Fort-de-France |

### Language Situation
- French Sign Language (LSF) primary sign language
- No deaf school on island historically
- Many deaf Martinicans educated in France
- Part of French national deaf education system
- Part of Windward Islands deaf community

### Key Organizations
- **AMEIS** (LAssociation Martiniquaise pour lEducation et lInsertion des Sourds)
  - Founded by parents of deaf children
  - Education in French Sign Language (LSF)
  - Employment training for deaf people
- **Surdus Antilles** (Fort-de-France)
  - LSF educational courses for public
  - Interpreter training programs
  - LSF interpreter certification

### Educational Challenge
- Historically no local deaf school
- Deaf individuals sent to France for education
- Return to work in Martinique after education
- Deaf Martinicans reported dissatisfaction with this system
- (Source: Society for Caribbean Linguistics)

### French Sign Language (LSF)
- ~100,000 native signers in France
- One-handed manual alphabet
- Abbé Charles-Michel de lÉpée: Key figure
- LSF recognized as language in France (2005)
- Related to ASL, NGT, RSL, LSQ

### Geographic Context
- French Overseas Region in Caribbean
- Part of Windward Islands
- Lesser Antilles chain
- French territory since 1635
- Population: ~375,000

### Official Languages
- **French** - Official language
- **Martinican Creole** - Widely spoken
  - French-based Creole
  - Local cultural identity
  - Mutually intelligible with Guadeloupe Creole

### Windward Islands Deaf Context
Martinique part of Windward Islands deaf community including:
- Dominica
- Barbados
- Saint Lucia
- Saint Vincent and the Grenadines
- Trinidad and Tobago
- Grenada

### Regional Sign Languages
Sign languages in Caribbean French territories:
- **Martinique**: LSF
- **Guadeloupe**: LSF
- **French Guiana**: LSF
- (All French overseas regions)

### Early Hearing Detection
- EHDI not universally implemented in Caribbean
- Cost barriers for hearing screenings
- High rate of congenital hearing loss in region
- Limited data on deaf population

### Cultural Context
- French Caribbean culture
- Creole cultural identity
- Very little English spoken
- French language essential for visitors
- Smile universal communication

### French Deaf Resources
- LSF courses available
- Interpreter services through Surdus Antilles
- Connection to French national deaf organizations
- Same legal protections as mainland France

### LSF Legal Status in France
- **1991 Fabius Law**: LSF authorized for education
- **2005 Law**: Full recognition as language
- Applies to all French territories including Martinique

### Tourism Note
- French is essential language
- English limited to tourist areas/hotels
- Phrase book/dictionary recommended
- French efforts appreciated by locals
