## Historical Development

### Colonial Era
- French Sign Language (LSF) introduced during French colonial period
- First school founded in Algiers (1972) by French Catholic fathers

### Post-Independence
- LSA evolved independently from LSF
- Approximately 50% lexical difference from LSF
- Influenced by Arabic and Berber spoken languages

### Legal Recognition
- Law No. 02-09 of May 8, 2002
- Protection and promotion of persons with disabilities
- Designated principal language for deaf Algerians

## Linguistic Features
- Strongly influenced by French Sign Language (LSF)
- LSF appears intelligible to LSA signers
- May be variety of Francophone African Sign Language (LSAF)
- Unique adaptations for Arabic/Berber context

## Related Languages
- Algerian Jewish Sign Language (Ghardaia Sign Language) - separate village sign language
- Influence on Oujda region (northern Morocco)

## Education and Services
- LSA not systematically used in education
- Implementation of recognition inconsistent
- ~300 interpreters (mainly volunteers)
- TV news interpretation available once daily

## Organizations
- Government pledge to provide adequate education (WFD 2008)
- No formal interpreter qualifications
- Family members often serve as interpreters
