# Tunisian Sign Language / Lughat al-Ishara al-Tunisiya (LST)

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Tunisian Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Tunisiya (لغة الإشارة التونسية) |
| **French Name** | Langue des signes tunisienne (LST) |
| **ISO 639-3** | tse |
| **Region** | Tunisia |
| **Users** | ~21,000 signers (2008) |
| **Legal Status** | Officially recognized since 2006 |

## Historical Development

### Colonial Period
- French colonization (1881-1956) influenced sign language development
- Linguistic contact between French and Tunisian deaf communities

### Association Era (1970s)
- 1970: Association Tunisienne dAide aux Sourds (ATAS) founded
- 1979: Association de Soutien aux Deficients Auditifs (ASDA) established
- 1983: Association de la Voix des Sourds Tunisiens (AVAST) - first deaf-led organization

### Modern Development
- 2000s: Partnerships with French organizations (INJS, Centre Jacques-Cartier)
- 2006: Official recognition of LST
- No standardized dictionary yet published

## Linguistic Features

### Origins and Influences
- Derived from Italian Sign Language (LIS)
- Heavy borrowing from French Sign Language (LSF)
- Influence from Arab Unified Sign Language (LSAU)
- Mixed with indigenous signs

### Characteristics
- Unique cultural signs reflecting Tunisian traditions
- Code-switching between LST and LSF common
- Research shows distant genetic relationship with LSF

## Education
- 70 schools for the deaf in Tunisia
- Mix of oral and sign language methods
- ATAS oversees education (Ministry of Social Affairs)
- Primarily oralist tradition in classrooms

## Organizations
- ATAS - Tunisian Association for Aid to the Deaf
- AVST - Voice of the Deaf Association of Tunisia
- ICHARA - Research Institute of Tunisian Sign Language

## Notable Achievements
- First English-TSL textbook: Lets Hand Speak English (2017) by Manel Bergaoui
- LetsApp educational app (2018)
- TSL hospital in Djebel Lahmar slum

## Village Sign Language
- Cap Bon region: High incidence of deafness
- Burj as-Salh deaf village sign language (relationship unclear)
