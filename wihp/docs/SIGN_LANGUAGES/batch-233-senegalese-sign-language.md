# Senegalese Sign Language / Langue des Signes Senegalaise

## Overview

| **Language Family** | Mixed (ASL influence + indigenous signs) |
| **Legal Status** | Not officially recognized |

## Historical Development
- Indigenous sign languages existed before formal deaf education
- Local sign systems developed in various communities
- Mbour neighborhood developed its own sign language (documented by Gwenaelle Jirou)
- American Sign Language introduced by deaf American missionary Andrew Foster
- ASL became the dominant language in formal deaf education
- 2018: FENASSEN stated goal to promote a national sign language and its recognition
- 2024: First electoral lexicon in sign language created for deaf voters

## Language Ecology
- ASL (American Sign Language) - used in education
- Local/Indigenous signs - used by non-schooled deaf
- Mbour Sign Language - village sign language
- West African Sign Language - term used by some schools
