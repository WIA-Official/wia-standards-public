## Legal Recognition

### 2007 Language Act
**Officially recognized March 1, 2007**

Key provisions:
- EVK recognized as "independent language"
- NOT just generic "sign language"
- Signed Estonian = mode of Estonian (not EVK)
- State promotes EVK use and development
- Similar status to Estonian language

### Importance
- Separates EVK from minority languages
- Explicit regulation of EVK use
- Distinguishes EVK from Signed Exact Estonian

## Language Origins and Classification

### Primary Influence: Russian Sign Language
**Taniroo (2007) Study**:
- 61% of EVK signs identical to RSL
- 200-word Swadesh list comparison
- Confirms significant RSL influence

### Possible Family
- French Sign Language family (via RSL)
- Historical connections: German, Russian education
- RSL connected to LSF and ASL historically

### Other Influences
| Source | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Finnish SL | "butterfly" from Finnish "bird" sign |
| German tradition | Oral teaching methods |
| Russian tradition | Soviet-era education |

### Latvian Sign Language Comparison
**Mahoney (2017)**:
- 100-word Swadesh-Woodward comparison
- Possible relationship uncertain
- Similar core lexicons observed
- Shared handshape variations
- NO mother-daughter relationship established

## Linguistic Features

### Colour Terms
Research by Hollman (2010):
- Generic term: derived from "paint"
- Black: most dominant colour sign
- Yellow: possibly from ASL/French (Y/J handshape)
- Blue: loan from Russian SL

### Place Name Signs
#### ESTONIA
- Old sign: Based on "large robust chin" (Estonian physiognomy)
- Contemporary sign: Different, origin debated
- NOT from Swedish SL as sometimes claimed

#### TARTU
- Historical changes in form
- Related to Latvian SL sign (cannons reference)
- EVK sign possibly loaned to Latvian SL

### Kinship Terms
- Studied as part of lexicon research

### Numerals
- Part of basic vocabulary documentation
