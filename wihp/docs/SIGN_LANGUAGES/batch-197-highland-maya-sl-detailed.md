# Batch 197: Highland Maya Sign Language (Meemul Tziij)

## Overview
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Native Names** | Meemul Chabal, Meemul Tziij (Kiche: "mute language") |
| **Region** | Guatemala Highlands, Southern Mexico |
| **Type** | Village Sign Language Complex |
| **Status** | Endangered / Undocumented |

## Geographic Distribution
- Guatemala highlands
- Multiple Maya communities
- Kiche, Mam, and other Maya regions
- Southern Mexico (possible extension)

## Key Research

### Erich Fox Tree (2009)
- "Meemul Tziij: An Indigenous Sign Language Complex of Mesoamerica"
- Sign Language Studies 9(3): 324-366
- First major academic description

### Fox Tree Hypothesis
- Possible pre-Hispanic pan-Mayan sign language complex
- Spread across Mesoamerica
- Historical links between communities (debated)

## Social Context

### Contrast with Yucatec Maya SL

| Feature | Highland Maya SL | Yucatec Maya SL |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| Location | Guatemala | Mexico |
| Attitude | Sometimes oppressive | Positive |
| Integration | Variable | Full |
| Community Use | Limited in some areas | Widespread |

### Reported Uses
- Deaf rural Maya throughout highlands
- Traders (hearing)
- Traditional storytellers (hearing)
- Ceremonies and rituals

### Concerning Reports
- In at least one village, used as means of "social segregation and oppression"
- Contrast with positive attitudes in Yucatan
- Variable community attitudes

## Linguistic Status

### Classification
- Unrelated to Guatemalan Sign Language (LENSEGUA)
- Unrelated to Mexican Sign Language (LSM)
- Unrelated to Spanish
- Unrelated to spoken Mayan languages
- Possible relation to Yucatec Maya SL (debated)

### Documentation Level
- Minimal systematic documentation
- Few academic studies
- No comprehensive grammar
- No published dictionary

## Threats

1. **Guatemalan Sign Language (LENSEGUA)** dominance
2. **Spanish** influence
3. **Limited documentation**
4. **Variable community attitudes**
5. **Rural isolation** - both protection and risk
6. **No educational recognition**

## Research Challenges

### Access Issues
- Remote highland communities
- Multiple distinct varieties possible
- Community permission required
- Political instability history
- Indigenous rights concerns

### Methodological Issues
- Distinguishing varieties
- Determining historical connections
- Documenting variation
- Community-appropriate methods

## Possible Sub-varieties

- Kiche region varieties

- Other highland Maya communities
