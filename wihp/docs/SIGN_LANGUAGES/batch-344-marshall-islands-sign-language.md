### Language Description
Marshall Islands Sign Language is the developing sign language of the Republic of the Marshall Islands, a nation of 29 coral atolls and 5 islands in the North Pacific. The language shows significant influence from American Sign Language (ASL) due to the islands' close political relationship with the United States through the Compact of Free Association.

The Marshallese deaf community has been shaped by unique historical factors, including nuclear testing on Bikini and Enewetak atolls (1946-1958) which may have contributed to higher rates of congenital deafness in affected populations. This tragic history has created a relatively larger deaf community than might otherwise exist.

The language is developing with ASL as a major influence, but local signs and Marshallese cultural elements are increasingly incorporated.

### Nuclear Legacy and Deafness
The nuclear testing program's health impacts:
- 67 nuclear tests conducted 1946-1958
- Bikini and Enewetak atolls most affected
- Radiation exposure linked to birth defects
- Higher rates of hearing loss documented
- Intergenerational health effects continue
- Compensation programs ongoing

### Key Characteristics
- **ASL Foundation**: Strong American influence through education
- **Local Adaptation**: Marshallese cultural signs developing
- **Nuclear Legacy**: Community shaped by historical tragedy
- **US Connection**: Compact relationship maintains ASL contact
- **Island Variation**: Signs may differ across atolls

### Deaf Community
- **Majuro** (capital): Largest concentration
- **Ebeye**: Secondary center near Kwajalein
- **Outer Atolls**: Isolated deaf individuals
- **Nuclear-affected Communities**: Higher deaf populations
- **US Migration**: Many Marshallese live in US (Hawaii, Arkansas, Oklahoma)

### Diaspora
The Compact of Free Association allows free migration to US:
- Large Marshallese communities in US states
- Deaf Marshallese in US may use full ASL
- Return migrants bring ASL influence
- Transnational deaf community emerging

### Education
- **Marshall Islands Public Schools**: Mainstream with support
- **Special Education Program**: ASL-based instruction
- **Teacher Training**: US-connected programs
- **College of the Marshall Islands**: Some services available

### Challenges
1. Nuclear testing legacy and ongoing health issues
2. Geographic dispersion across vast ocean area
3. Limited resources in small nation
4. Brain drain to United States
5. Climate change threatens low-lying atolls
6. Balancing ASL use with local development
