### Language Description
Maltese Sign Language (Lingwa tas-Sinjali Maltija, LSM) is the sign language of the Deaf community in Malta, a small island nation in the Mediterranean Sea. With a total population of approximately 520,000, Malta has a relatively small but organized deaf community.

LSM has developed as a distinct language, though it shows historical influence from British Sign Language (BSL) due to Malta's history as a British colony (1800-1964). Italian Sign Language (LIS) has also had some influence due to geographic proximity and cultural connections.

The language has its own grammar, vocabulary, and structure, with unique signs reflecting Maltese culture, the Catholic faith, and Mediterranean island life.

### Key Characteristics
- **BSL Influence**: Historical British connection visible
- **Mediterranean Character**: Expressive, gestural style
- **Catholic Signs**: Rich religious vocabulary
- **Compact Community**: All signers know each other
- **Bilingual Context**: Maltese/English environment reflected

### Legal Recognition
Malta has strong LSM legal framework:
- **2015**: LSM Recognition Act passed
- **2016**: Constitutional recognition as national language
- **Sign Language Council**: Advises government
- **Interpretation Services**: Required in public services
- **Education Rights**: LSM in deaf education guaranteed

### Deaf Community
The Maltese Deaf community is concentrated:
- **Valletta/Grand Harbour area**: Historical center
- **Deaf Village Malta**: Major community center
- **Church Communities**: Strong Catholic deaf groups
- **Sports**: Active deaf sports programs

### Cultural Elements
Unique LSM signs reflect:
- **Festa**: Village feast celebrations
- **Fishing**: Traditional luzzu boats
- **Religion**: Catholic practices, saints
- **Food**: Pastizzi, rabbit, local dishes
- **Architecture**: Limestone, churches
- **Weather**: Mediterranean climate terms

### Linguistic Features
- **Two-handed alphabet**: Similar to BSL
- **Non-manual markers**: Facial grammar important
- **Classifiers**: For boats, buildings, people
- **Religious register**: Formal church signing style
- **Fingerspelling**: Used for Maltese and English

### Interpreter Services
- **National**: Government provides interpreters
- **TV**: News interpretation available
- **Medical**: Healthcare access improving
- **Legal**: Court interpreters available
- **Education**: School interpreters provided

### Challenges
1. Small community limits development
2. Need for more interpreters
3. LSM teaching materials limited
4. Documentation ongoing
5. Competition with BSL/ISL influence
