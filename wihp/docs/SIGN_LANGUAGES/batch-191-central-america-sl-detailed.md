# Batch 191: Central American Indigenous Sign Languages

## Overview
Central America has indigenous village sign languages beyond ASL-based national sign languages.
Most originated in indigenous communities and are severely threatened.

## Costa Rica Sign Language System (Woodward)
At least 4 distinct sign languages:
1. LESCO (New Costa Rican SL) - National
2. Original LESCO / Old LESCO - Pre-1945
3. Brunca Sign Language - Indigenous village
4. Bribri Sign Language - Indigenous village

## Panama Sign Languages

### 1. Chiriqui Sign Language (LSCH)
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Glottolog** | chir1296 |
| **Local Name** | Lengua de Senas de Chiriqui |
| **Region** | Chiriqui Province, Panama |
| **Main City** | David |
| **Status** | Threatened |

Panama has about 6,000 deaf people.
Two sign languages coexist: LSCH (Chiriqui) + LSP (Panama City)
Not mutually intelligible.

## Guatemala

## Nicaragua

### Nicaraguan Sign Language (ISN)
Famous case of spontaneous language emergence in 1970s-80s.

## Threats to Central American Sign Languages

1. ASL spread through missionaries/education
2. Educational mainstreaming
3. Lack of documentation
4. Indigenous community decline
5. Youth migration to cities
