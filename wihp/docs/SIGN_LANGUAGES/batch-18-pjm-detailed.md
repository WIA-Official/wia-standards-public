# WIA Talk Sign Language Mapping - PJM (Polski Język Migowy / Polish Sign Language)
## Batch 18: 20 Signs with Full WIA Talk Component Analysis

**Language:** PJM - Polski Język Migowy (Polish Sign Language)
**Region:** Poland
**Estimated Users:** 50,000+ (one of largest minority languages in Poland)
**Language Family:** Related to French Sign Language; influences from RSL, DGS, Austrian SL
**Recognition:** Legal recognition 2011

---

## PJM Characteristics

### Historical Background
- **~1817:** PJM emerged with founding of Warsaw Institute of the Deaf-Mute and the Blind
- Founded by **Jakub Falkowski** after meeting deaf boy Piotr Gąsowski
- **1879:** First PJM dictionary published (Hollak et al.)
- **1957:** Kazimierz Kirejczyk: "It is forbidden to use signs!"
- **1984:** Wacław Kur visited Stockholm, saw better results with sign language
- **1994:** Term "Polski Język Migowy (PJM)" introduced by Michael Farris
- **2008:** First professional interpreter training at University of Warsaw
- **2011:** Legal recognition passed
- **2014:** PLM identified 6,610 lexemes in 200,000+ uses

### Language Family & Relationships
- **One-handed manual alphabet** based on Old French Sign Language
- Related to **French Sign Language (LSF)**
- Influences from history of Poland's Partitions:
  - **Russian Sign Language** (Russification period)
  - **German Sign Language** (Germanization period)
  - **Austrian Sign Language** (Austrian partition)
- Lexicon and grammar distinct from Polish language

### Two Systems
1. **PJM (Polski Język Migowy):** Natural sign language with own grammar
2. **SJM (System Językowo-Migowy / Signed Polish):** Manually coded Polish
   - Used by TV interpreters
   - Used in schools
   - Hybrid artificial system

### Key Features
- **SVO word order** in longer sentences
- **SOV word order** also appears in shorter sentences
- **Rich morphology** - both inflection and derivation
- **Non-manual elements essential:**
  - Torso and head position/movement
  - Facial expressions
  - Grammaticalized (part of language system)
- **Simultaneous articulation** possible (left hand, right hand, face)
- **No sound-based phonemes** - visual realizations instead
- **Diacritic phonology** rather than phoneme-based

### Organizations & Resources
- **PLM** - Sign Language Linguistics Laboratory (University of Warsaw)
  - One of 4 largest sign language research databases in world!
  - 300+ hours of video material
- **Korpusowy Słownik PJM** - Corpus-based Dictionary (slownikpjm.uw.edu.pl)
- **University of Warsaw** - Master's degree in PJM Philology (only in Poland!)
- **Świat Głuchych Foundation** - "We teach sign language" video series

### Challenges
- No official curricula for teaching PJM
- No standards for PJM teachers
- Students cannot learn PJM at school
- Long history of sign language suppression

### 20. BRAWO (Bravo/Applause)

**PJM Concept:** Applause - unique cultural sign!

**Detailed Motion:**
- Deaf applause is NOT clapped but SHOWN
- Both hands up, fingers spread
- Waving/shaking motion in air
- Also spelled with W+W+W (www)

**WIA Talk Code:** `HS03-LC10-MV17-OR01-FE01`

| Component | Code | Description |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| HS | 03+03 | Both hands, fingers spread |
| LC | 10 | Above shoulder level |
| MV | 17 | Waving/shaking in air |
| OR | 01 | Palms forward |
| FE | 01 | Excited/happy expression |

**Cultural Note:** "Applause is not 'clapped' but shown" - This is visual applause!

**Source:** swiatgluchych.pl - "We teach sign language" series

|------|-------|-------------|
| HS01 | Fist | Yes |

| HS03 | 5 fingers spread | Number 5, Number 10, Bravo |
| HS04 | V-shape | Number 2, Name |
| HS07 | Index finger | Number 1 |
| HS10 | Four fingers | Number 4 |
| HS45-47 | PJM 7-9 shapes | Numbers 7-9 |
| HS48 | O-shape | Number 0 |
| HS52 | Three fingers | Number 3 |
| HS53 | PJM 6 shape | Number 6 |

### Unique PJM Features
- **One-handed manual alphabet** (from Old French SL)
- **SVO/SOV** word order flexibility
- **Diacritic phonology** - unique linguistic analysis
- **Non-manual markers** grammaticalized
- **Visual applause (BRAWO)** - hands wave, not clap!
- **One of 4 largest SL research databases** (PLM)

### Alphabet Position
- Letters signed near dominant shoulder
- Palm facing viewer
- Polish diacritics have movement markers
