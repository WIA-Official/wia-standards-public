# Saint Vincent and the Grenadines Sign Language
## Sign Language of SVG

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | American Sign Language (ASL) |
| **Alternate Name** | Sign America (local term) |
| **Country** | Saint Vincent and the Grenadines |
| **Population** | ~110,872 total |
| **Deaf Population** | ~500 estimated |
| **CRPD Status** | Ratified (with optional protocol) |
| **Status** | ASL-dominant with localized signs |

### Language Situation
- **American Sign Language (ASL)** primary sign language
- Locally called Sign America
- Learned at school
- Many rural deaf have localized sign languages
- No distinct Saint Vincent Sign Language documented

### SIL International Finding
> Although deaf people indicated that there may be a few local signs and facial expressions confined to Saint Vincent, they all indicated that they have grown up using Sign America that they learned at school and that there is no Saint Vincent Sign Language.

### Geographic Context
- Eastern Caribbean at southern end of Windward Islands
- Archipelago: Main island Saint Vincent + Grenadines
- Capital: Kingstown (population 25,000)
- Total area: ~384 km²
- Independence: October 27, 1979

### Demographics
- Population: ~110,872 (2022)
- Majority African descent
- English: Official language
- Vincentian Creole: Widely spoken
- 300+ inhabitants/km² density

### Village Sign Languages
- Rural deaf populations have localized sign systems
- Undocumented by linguists
- Limited to specific communities
- Not officially recognized

### Healthcare Challenges
- Lacked early hearing screenings (2020 study)
- Little data on deaf population
- Journal of Early Hearing Detection reported gaps
- No comprehensive EHDI program

### Legal Framework
- **CRPD**: Ratified
- Optional protocol also ratified
- Part of UN Convention on Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Member of CARICOM
- Member of OECS

### Regional Connections
- Barbados: 100 miles east
- Saint Lucia: 21 miles north
- Grenada: 28 miles south
- Caribbean regional deaf organizations

### Education
- ASL taught in schools
- Schools for deaf use Sign America
- English primary language of instruction
- Vincentian Creole informal settings

### Linguistic Heritage
- English: Official language
- Vincentian Creole: English-based creole
  - Contains Spanish, Antillean Creole elements
  - Kalinago/Garifuna influences
  - African language influences
- French patois also spoken

### Historical Context
- Indigenous Kalinago (Carib) and Arawak peoples
- African populations from 1600s shipwrecks
- Garifuna culture developed
- British colony 1783-1979
- Commonwealth realm

### Challenges
- Limited deaf-specific organizations
- No documented indigenous sign language
- Healthcare communication barriers
- Early intervention gaps
- Small deaf population (estimated 500)
