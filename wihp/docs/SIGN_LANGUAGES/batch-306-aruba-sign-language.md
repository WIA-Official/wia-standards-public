# Aruba Sign Language
## Sign Language of Aruba (Dutch Caribbean)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | Sign Language of the Netherlands (NGT) |
| **Alternate Name** | Nederlandse Gebarentaal |
| **Country** | Aruba |
| **Status** | Part of Kingdom of Netherlands |
| **Political Status** | Constituent country |

### Language Situation
- NGT (Nederlandse Gebarentaal) used by deaf community
- Dutch Sign Language influence through Netherlands connection
- Sign Supported Dutch (SSD) also used
- Part of Dutch colonial sign language sphere
- Government recognizes sign language for communication

### Historical Context
- Former Netherlands Antilles (dissolved 2010)
- Became separate constituent country of Kingdom of Netherlands
- Historical ties to Dutch deaf education
- Contact with educators from Netherlands

### NGT (Dutch Sign Language)
- Indigenous sign language of Netherlands
- ~7,500 native users in Netherlands
- ~13,000 total users worldwide
- Also used in Aruba, Curacao, Suriname
- Originated from 1790 Groningen deaf school
- Influenced by French Sign Language

### Geographic Context
- Caribbean island off coast of South America
- Near Venezuela and Colombia
- Part of ABC islands (Aruba, Bonaire, Curacao)
- Population: ~100,000
- Area: ~69 square miles

### Official Languages of Aruba
1. **Dutch** - Official, legal/administrative
2. **Papiamento** - Official, everyday use
3. English - Widely spoken (tourism)
4. Spanish - Common (proximity to South America)

### Multilingual Society
- Most Arubans speak 4+ languages
- Children learn English from 4th grade
- Spanish from 5th grade
- Papiamento in curriculum since 1998-1999

### Deaf Community
- Organization exists for deaf community
- Government recognition of sign language
- Connection to Netherlands deaf education network
- Limited specific documentation available

### Related Sign Languages in Region
- Dutch Sign Language (NGT) - Primary
- Sign Supported Dutch (SSD)
- Used also in Curacao (Scola Myrna Dovale school)
- Used in Suriname (Kennedy School)

### Caribbean Sign Language Context
Nine sign languages found in Caribbean region:
1. American Sign Language (ASL)
2. British Sign Language (BSL)
3. Danish Sign Language
4. Dutch Sign Language (NGT)
5. French Sign Language (LSF)
6. Guyanese Sign Language
7. Kajana Sign Language
8. Quebec Sign Language (LSQ)
9. Surinamese Sign Language

### Cultural Context
- Diverse ancestry: European, Amerindian, African
- Various ethnic groups: Dutch, Colombian, Venezuelan, Dominican, Haitian
- Tourism industry significant
- Close ties to Venezuela/Colombia

### Papiamento Phrases
- Bon bini! = Welcome!
- Bon dia = Good morning
- Danki = Thank you
- Aruba ta dushi! = Aruba is lovely!
