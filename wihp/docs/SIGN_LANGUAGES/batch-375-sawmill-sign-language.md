### Language Description
Sawmill Sign Language was an occupational sign system used by workers in lumber mills of the Pacific Northwest United States, particularly in Washington, Oregon, and British Columbia. The extreme noise of sawmill machinery made spoken communication impossible, leading to the development of a workplace sign system.

This represents one of several documented occupational sign languages that emerged in industrial settings before modern hearing protection and communication technology.

### Why Signs Were Needed
Sawmill environment:
- Deafening machinery noise (120+ dB)
- No electronic communication
- No hearing protection (early era)
- Safety-critical communication
- Coordination needed between workers
