# Bangla Sign Language / BdSL

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|

| **Native Name** | বাংলা ইশারা ভাষা |
| **Region** | Bangladesh, West Bengal (India) |
| **Users** | 200,000-3,000,000+ (varying estimates) |
| **Deaf Population** | 13.7 million deaf/hard of hearing (9.6% of population) |

## Historical Development

### Origins (1893)
- Originated at Calcutta Deaf and Dumb School (1893)
- Students created signing system independent of Delhi
- Spread to communities across Bengal region
- Pre-dates partition of India (1947)

### Partition Impact (1947-1971)
- 1947: India partition separated West Bengal and East Pakistan
- Deaf Hindus and Muslims migrated between regions
- Signs remained similar due to migration contact
- 1971: Bangladesh independence from Pakistan
- Post-1971: Divergence into BdSL and WBSL

### Modern Recognition
- 1962: Four schools for blind and deaf established
- 1963: BNFD founded (December 25)
- 1994: First Bangla Sign Language Dictionary published
- **February 1, 2009: Official language recognition by PM Sheikh Hasina**
- 2013: February 7 declared Bangla Sign Language Day
- 2025: First Mental Health Sign Language Bank launched

## Linguistic Features

### Relationship to Other Languages
- Closely related to West Bengal Sign Language (WBSL)
- 75% lexical similarity between Kolkata and Delhi signs
- Possibly distinct from Indo-Pakistani Sign Language (IPSL)
- Identity differences drive separate language recognition

### Unique Characteristics
- NOT signed version of spoken Bangla
- Unique grammar and syntax
- Complex alphabet system (36+ Bangla alphabets)
- Many words and expression techniques

## Education

### Schools and Institutions
- Dhaka Deaf High School
- 1962 schools for blind and deaf
- SDSL - organized sign language teaching

### Challenges
- 99.5% without education access
- Lack of sign language interpreters in courts
- Limited research on BdSL evolution
- Need for language institute under Ministry of Education

## Recent Developments (2025)
- First Bangla Mental Health Sign Language Bank launched
- Co-designed with deaf community members
- Published in The Lancet Psychiatry
- New signs for depression, panic, mental care
- Led by Monash University and Team Inclusion Bangladesh

## Health Statistics
- 9.6% population deaf or hard of hearing
- 34.6% have hearing loss in any degree
- 1.2% have profound hearing loss (90+ dB)
- Second most common disability in Bangladesh
- Higher rates in people over 60 and lower socioeconomic status

## Technology Initiatives
- Machine learning sign language recognition
- YOLOv5-based BdSL recognition systems
- CNN achieving 98-99% accuracy
- KU-BdSL dataset (30 classes, 1500 images)
- Multiple research projects at universities

## Challenges
- 40% unemployment rate among deaf
- Limited interpreter availability
- Dictionary not updated since 1994
- Foreign sign language influence concerns
- Rape victims need court interpreters

## Cultural Significance
- Bangla - 7th most spoken language globally
- UNESCO: one of sweetest languages
- Strong identity with Bengali heritage
- Active deaf poets and artists
- Linguistic research ongoing
