# Curaçao Sign Language
## Sign Language of Curaçao (Dutch Caribbean)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | Dutch Sign Language (NGT) |
| **Previous Language** | American Sign Language (before 1996) |
| **Country** | Curaçao |
| **Status** | Constituent country of Kingdom of Netherlands |
| **School** | Scola Myrna Dovale |

### Language History
- **Before 1996**: American Sign Language (ASL) used
- **Since 1996**: Dutch Sign Language (NGT) adopted
- Transition to NGT for Netherlands connection
- Sign Supported Dutch (SSD) also used
- Contact with educators from Netherlands

### Current Advocacy
- **NTIC** (National Language Institute of Curaçao)
  - Calling for sign language recognition
  - Promote as official language
  - International Week of the Deaf activities
- **HAG** (Stichting Hulp aan Auditief Gehandicapten)
  - Foundation for Assistance to Hearing Impaired
  - Also called Fundashon Hendikèp Ouditivo
- Special commission established for recognition

### Deaf Education
- **Scola Myrna Dovale**
  - Only deaf school on island
  - Uses NGT (Dutch Sign Language)
  - Sign Supported Dutch (SSD) used
  - Spoken Papiamentu supplemented
  - Contact with Netherlands educators

### Language Recognition Goals
- Recognize sign language as official language
- Value alongside Dutch, Papiamentu, English
- Remove communication barriers for deaf
- Support cognitive and social skill development

### Geographic Context
- Largest of ABC islands
- Area: 171 square miles
- Population: ~150,000
- Part of Lesser Antilles
- Independence from Netherlands Antilles: 2010

### Official Languages of Curaçao
1. **Dutch** - Official, administrative/legal
2. **Papiamentu** - Official, everyday (~82% native speakers)
3. **English** - Co-official

### Multilingual Society
- Most speak 2+ languages
- Dutch, English, Spanish common
- Papiamentu most widely spoken
- Portuguese influence historical

### Political History
- Former Netherlands Antilles (dissolved 2010)
- Now constituent country of Kingdom of Netherlands
- Dutch colonial history since 1634
- Slavery abolished 1863

### Visual Communication Tips (NTIC)
- Clearly articulate while making eye contact
- Write short sentences for reading
- Use sign language
- Manual alphabet (26 gestures)

### Regional Sign Language Context
- NGT used in Netherlands, Aruba, Curaçao, Suriname
- Part of Dutch colonial sign language sphere
- Educational contact with Netherlands
- Children may use other signs among themselves

### Cultural Context
- Jewish community oldest in Americas (1651)
- Synagogue since 1732
- African, Dutch, Spanish influences
- Rich literary tradition
- Willemstad UNESCO World Heritage

### Papiamentu Phrases
- Kon ta bai? = How are you?
- Por fabor = Please
- Danki = Thank you
