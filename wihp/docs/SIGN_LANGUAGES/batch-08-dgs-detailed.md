# WIA Talk Sign Language Mapping - DGS (Deutsche Gebärdensprache / German Sign Language)
## Batch 08: 20 Signs with Full WIA Talk Component Analysis

**Language:** DGS - Deutsche Gebärdensprache (German Sign Language)
**Region:** Germany, Luxembourg, German-speaking Belgium
**Estimated Users:** ~200,000 regular users; ~50,000 native signers
**Language Family:** German Sign Language family (related to Israeli Sign Language)
**Recognition:** 2002 legally recognized (Behindertengleichstellungsgesetz)

---

## DGS Characteristics

### Historical Background
- Development began in 18th-19th century deaf education
- 1887: Deutsches Gehörlosen-Institut established
- 1880 Milan Conference: Strong oralist suppression of sign language
- 2002: Legal recognition in Germany (Federal Disability Equality Act)
- 2024: Heike Heubach became first deaf member of German Bundestag

### Strong Oralist Tradition
Germany has historically suppressed sign language:
- Children punished for signing in schools (historically)
- Lip-reading emphasized over signing
- Parents encouraged to use only spoken language
- This resulted in DGS being more "oral-accompanied" than ASL

### Key Features
- **One-handed manual alphabet (Fingeralphabet)** - derived from French manual alphabet
- **Mouthing (Mundbild)** - More pronounced than ASL; lips form German words
- **SOV word order** - Subject-Object-Verb (like Turkish, Japanese, Latin)
- **No copula** - "to be" does not exist in DGS
- **No articles** - No der, die, das
- **Regional dialects** - North vs South, East vs West differences

### Alphabet - Fingeralphabet
- One-handed (unlike BSL/Auslan)
- Derived from French Sign Language alphabet (1700s)
- Includes special characters: Ä, Ö, Ü, ß

### Related Languages
**DGS Family:**
- Deutsche Gebärdensprache (DGS)
- Israeli Sign Language (ISL) - developed from DGS by refugees
  - Teachers from Berlin-Weißensee deaf school fled Nazi Germany
  - Established schools in Israel, evolved into ISL

**NOT Related:**
- Austrian Sign Language (ÖGS) - French Sign Language family
- Swiss-German Sign Language (DSGS) - French Sign Language family

**DGS Concept:** Wave greeting

**Detailed Motion:**
- Flat open hand
- Wave side to side
- Friendly facial expression
- Often accompanied by lip movement "Hallo"

**WIA Talk Code:** `HS02-LC09-MV06-OR01-FE02`

## SECTION B: Numbers 1-10 (Zahlen)

### DGS Number System Overview

**Structure:** Main-base-10, sub-base-5 system

DGS numbers 0-10 are performed:
- In signing space in front of chest
- **Palm toward signer** (unique to DGS!)
- Fingertips pointing upward
- Numbers 6-10 combine both hands

**Key Feature:** One-to-one correspondence between fingers extended and numerical value

**Source:** researchgate.net, dreipunkt-verlag.de, grundschul-blog.de

| FE | 03 | Neutral |

**Structure:** 5 + 1 = 6

### 16. ICH LIEBE DICH (I Love You)

**DGS Concept:** Three-part phrase

**Detailed Motion:**
- ICH (I): Point to self
- LIEBE (Love): Hands cross over heart, or stroking motion
- DICH (You): Point to person

**WIA Talk Code (LIEBE):** `HS02-LC07-MV26-OR05-FE01`

| Component | Code | Description |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| HS | 02 | Flat hands crossed |
| LC | 07 | Chest/heart area |
| MV | 26 | Cross arms over heart or stroke |
| OR | 05 | Palms toward body |
| FE | 01 | Loving expression |

**Note:** ILY (🤟) handshape is recognized internationally but not traditional DGS

**Source:** sltranslator.com

**DGS Concept:** Name question

**Detailed Motion:**
- WIE (How): Question gesture
- NAME: Tap chest (like name badge) or use specific sign
- DU (You): Point to person
- Question facial expression

**WIA Talk Code:** `HS04-LC07-MV15-OR05-FE06`

| Component | Code | Description |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| HS | 04 | Two fingers |
| LC | 07 | Chest area |

| FE | 06 | Question expression |

**Grammar:** DGS may use: "NAME DU WIE?" (SOV order)

## DGS Linguistic Features Summary

### Grammar Structure
- **SOV Order:** Subject-Object-Verb
  - German: "Ich lerne Deutsch"
  - DGS: "ICH DEUTSCH LERNEN"
- **No copula:** "To be" doesn't exist
- **No articles:** No der, die, das
- **Time at beginning:** Temporal markers first
- **Questions:** W-words often at end or both positions

### Example Complex Sentence
German: "Why did you want to give my mother money at my father's house last week?"
DGS Order: LETZTE-WOCHE (Time) → VATER HAUS (Location) → DU (Subject) → MUTTER (Indirect Object) → GELD (Direct Object) → GEB- (Verb) → WÜNSCH- (Auxiliary) → WARUM (Wh-question)

### Personal Agreement Marker (PAM)
- Unique grammatical feature
- Indicates location of animate objects
- Functions like object pronouns
- May be accompanied by mouthing "auf"

### Mouthing (Mundbild)
DGS is more oral-accompanied than ASL:
- Signs accompanied by lip movements forming German words
- Example: Sign MUTTER + mouth "Mutter" silently
- Result of strong oralist tradition in German deaf education

### Regional Dialects
- North Germany vs South Germany differences
- East Germany vs West Germany (from division)
- Monthly signs show clear dialectal variation
- 13 speech collection zones identified in DGS Corpus

### Writing Systems
- **HamNoSys:** Hamburg Notation System
- **SignWriting:** Visual notation
