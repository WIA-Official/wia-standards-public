# Emirati Sign Language / Lughat al-Ishara al-Imaratiya

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Emirati Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Imaratiya (لغة الإشارة الإماراتية) |
| **Region** | United Arab Emirates |
| **Users** | ~3,000+ deaf individuals |
| **Language Family** | Arab sign-language family |
| **Status** | Recognized as minority language |

## Historical Development

### Early History
- Deaf community existed in UAE before formal documentation
- Al Amal School for the Deaf founded in Sharjah (1979)
- First and only school solely for deaf education until today

### Dictionary Development (2014-2018)
- Four years of collaborative development
- 8 authorities involved with 60 deaf people and specialists
- Information collected from 60 areas across UAE
- 2018: First UAE Sign Language Dictionary launched (5,000+ words)
- Available online, smartphone app, and CD

### Modern Developments
- 2009: First certified Emirati sign language specialist (Abeer Al Shahi)
- 2011: Integration of deaf pupils into government schools
- 2021: Dubais first sign language cafe opened
- 2027: Abu Dhabi to host World Deaf Congress

## Linguistic Features

### Development
- Created by Zayed Higher Organization for People of Determination
- First deaf sign language for UAE dialect
- Unified reference language across emirates

### Regional Variations
- Different signs between emirates previously
- Dictionary aimed to unify variations
- Cultural elements: traditional clothes, local food, historical places

### Cultural Signs
- Coffee sign: shaking imaginary cup (different from Levant)
- Signs for ghutra (headdress), henna, oud perfume
- Multiple gestures for same cultural items accommodated

## Education and Integration
- Al Amal School: 93 high school graduates by 2021
- Mohammed bin Rashid Smart Learning Program integrated (2013)
- Deaf pupils integrated into government schools (2011)
- ESL courses offered in educational institutions

## Cultural Significance
- Part of UAE Vision 2021
- Preserves Emirati cultural identity
- Sign language cafe promotes inclusion
- Growing employment opportunities
