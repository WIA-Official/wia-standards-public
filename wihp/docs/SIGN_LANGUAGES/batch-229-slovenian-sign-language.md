## Historic Achievement

### Constitutional Recognition (June 4, 2021)
**FIRST country to constitutionally recognize deafblind language!**

- National Assembly amendment
- Unanimous MP endorsement
- 5th EU country for sign language constitutional recognition
- Others: Austria, Finland, Hungary, Portugal

### Unique Feature
- Recognizes SZJ
- Recognizes Hungarian minority SL
- Recognizes Italian minority SL
- **FIRST for deafblind language**

## Legal Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2002 | Law on Use of Slovenian Sign Language |
| 2018 | Campaign for constitutional recognition begins |
| 2019 | Government endorses proposal (April 18) |
| 2021 | Constitutional amendment (June 4) |

### 2002 Law
- Right to interpreter (limited extent)
- Did NOT grant full language status
- Article 2: "Sign language is the language of deaf people and the natural means of communication"

### 2019 Proposal
- Association of Deaf and Hearing-Impaired initiative
- Aims: Employment and education improvement
- Minister Ksenija Klampfer support

> "Communication and language as well as the right to their use are essential for social inclusion and implementation of basic human rights. Without communication an individual cannot fully participate in society."

## Linguistic Features

### Word Order
Locative constructions studied:
- Ground-Predicate-Figure patterns
- Similar to ASL, Croatian, Australian, Flemish, Irish, Russian SL

### Classifier Predicates
- Two-handed signs
- Movement distinguishes nouns from verbs
- Spatial encoding

### L1/L2 Variation
- Small proportion acquire SZJ as L1 without delay
- Many delayed L1 users
- L2 users common
- Competence varies significantly

## Challenges

### Endangered Status
- Small user population (~900-1,500)
- Not well documented
- Not systematic in classroom
- L1 acquisition often delayed

### Education Gap
- Limited interpreter rights (2002 law)
- Not fully integrated in schools
- Teacher training needs
