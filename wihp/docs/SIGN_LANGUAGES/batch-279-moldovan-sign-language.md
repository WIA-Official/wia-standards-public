# Moldovan Sign Language (MSL)
## Limba Semnelor Moldovenesti

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Moldovan Sign Language / Moldova Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Limba Semnelor Moldovenesti |
| **ISO 639-3** | vsi |
| **Country** | Moldova |
| **Users** | ~17,500 estimated |
| **Status** | Recognized language (2012) |
| **Language Family** | Related to Russian Sign Language |

### Legal Status
- **2012**: Legally recognized under Article 25(1)
- Law on Inclusion of Persons with Disabilities
- Glottolog status: 5 (Developing)

### Historical Development
- **Soviet Era**: Russian Sign Language introduced
- Deaf community developed through Soviet institutions
- Post-Soviet: MSL developed distinct features
- Strong relationship with Russian Sign Language (RSL)
- 70% similarity with USL and RSL (2005 study)

### Linguistic Features
- Related to Russian Sign Language family
- Influences from Romanian/Moldovan spoken language
- Regional variations across Moldova
- Used by deaf community across generations
- Sign language version of common greetings

### Education System
- School and orphanage for deaf children
- Cahul Hipoacuzi Orphanage notable institution
- Combination of residential and day programs
- American volunteer programs support education
- ASL interpreters facilitate cross-language communication

### Organizations
- Association of the Deaf of Republic Moldova

### Challenges
- Poorest country in Europe economically
- Limited resources for deaf education
- Most deaf people secular or Christian
- Difficulty accessing faith communities
- Limited Bible translation available

### International Support
