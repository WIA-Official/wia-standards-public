# Nicaraguan Sign Language (ISN / NSL)
## Idioma de Senas de Nicaragua

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Nicaraguan Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Idioma de Senas de Nicaragua (ISN) |
| **ISO 639-3** | ncs |
| **Country** | Nicaragua |
| **Users** | ~60,000 deaf population (ANSNIC estimate) |
| **Status** | Official language (2009) |
| **Language Family** | Unique - spontaneously emerged |

### Legal Recognition (February 12, 2009)
- Ley del Lenguaje de Senas Nicaraguenses enacted
- Designated as official language for persons with hearing disabilities
- Mandates use in education and government

### Unique Linguistic Significance
**The worlds only documented spontaneously emerged language**
- No influence from existing languages
- Created by deaf children themselves
- Studied from near-beginning by linguists
- Evidence for innate language acquisition (Universal Grammar)

### Historical Timeline
| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| Pre-1970s | No deaf community; isolated individuals using home signs |
| 1977 | Special education center opened in San Judas, Managua (50 students) |
| 1979 | Student population grew to 100 (Sandinista Revolution year) |
| 1980 | Vocational school for deaf adolescents in Villa Libertad |
| 1986 | Judy Kegl (MIT linguist) invited by Ministry of Education |
| 1997 | Diccionario del Idioma de Senas de Nicaragua published |
| 2009 | Official legal recognition |
| 2018 | Third edition NSL Manual (SignWriting) - 1,100+ signs |

### Language Development Stages
1. **Home signs (mimicas)**: Pre-1977, isolated family gestures
2. **Contact pidgin**: Older students initial gestural communication
3. **Creolized ISN**: Younger children added grammatical complexity
4. **Modern ISN**: Full language with syntax, spatial modulation, phonology

### Key Research Findings
- **Judy Kegl**: Avoided contaminating ISN with ASL
- **Senghas & Coppola**: Children transform language as they learn
- **Steven Pinker**: Called it unique opportunity in history
- **William Stokoe**: Debated language acquisition device theory
- Younger signers show more spatial modulation than older peers

### Linguistic Features
- Complex syntax
- Spatial modulation for tense and aspect
- Systematic phonology
- Verb agreement conventions
- Own grammar distinct from any other language

### Organizations
- **ANSNIC** (Asociacion Nacional de Sordos de Nicaragua)
- **NSLP** (Nicaraguan Sign Language Projects)
- **Manos Unidas**: Non-profit for deaf education
- ASB Latin America: Humanitarian support

### Educational Initiatives
- Ministry of Education bilingual programs
- Immersion clinics by NSLP
- ~1,000 underserved deaf children reached annually
- All-deaf-staffed schools established
- Rural outreach programs

### Research Institutions
- MIT (Judy Kegl, founder)
- Wesleyan University (Anna Shusterman)
- University of Nebraska Omaha
- Yale University

### Preservation Philosophy
- NSLP prohibits introducing foreign sign vocabulary
- Borrowing must occur naturally within community
- Deaf Nicaraguans train as teachers and role models
- Nationalization of indigenous signed language prioritized

### ISN vs Other Sign Languages
- NOT a pidgin (no base languages combined)
- NOT derived from ASL or Spanish
- Emerged de novo from human communication instinct
- Continues to evolve with each generation
