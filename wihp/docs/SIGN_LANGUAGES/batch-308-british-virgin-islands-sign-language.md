# British Virgin Islands Sign Language
## Sign Language of the British Virgin Islands (BVI)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | American Sign Language (ASL) / British Sign Language (BSL) |
| **Country** | British Virgin Islands |
| **Status** | British Overseas Territory |
| **Official Language** | English |
| **Population** | ~31,500-35,800 |

### Language Situation
- Limited documentation on deaf community
- ASL influence through US proximity
- BSL influence through British connection
- Part of Caribbean sign language sphere
- English-based Virgin Islands Creole spoken
- Included in SIL 24-location Western Hemisphere study

### Geographic Context
- British Overseas Territory in Caribbean
- East of Puerto Rico
- East of US Virgin Islands
- North-west of Anguilla
- Part of Virgin Islands archipelago
- Leeward Islands, Lesser Antilles

### Main Islands
- **Tortola** - Largest, ~23,491 residents
- **Virgin Gorda**
- **Anegada**
- **Jost Van Dyke**
- 50+ smaller islands and cays
- ~16 islands inhabited

### Official Languages
- **English** (British English) - Official
- **Virgin Islands Creole** - Widely used
- Spanish - Hispanic immigrant community

### Sign Language in Region
Nine sign languages documented in Caribbean:
1. American Sign Language (ASL)
2. British Sign Language (BSL)
3. Danish Sign Language
4. Dutch Sign Language (NGT)
5. French Sign Language
6. Guyanese Sign Language
7. Kajana Sign Language
8. Quebec Sign Language
9. Surinamese Sign Language

  - ASL-certified crew member (Nikki Schletz)
  - Nationally certified ASL interpreter
  - Unique Caribbean service
  - Safety briefings in ASL
  - Snorkeling interpretation

### Cultural Context
- British Commonwealth traditions
- Creole influences on music/dance
- Easygoing island lifestyle
- Custom greetings: good day / good evening
- Island time culture

### Historical Background
- England took over in 1672
- Native people before European colonization
- African descent majority population
- European descent minority
- US/British immigrant growth since 1960

### Virgin Islands Creole
- English-based Creole
- Blend of African, European, Caribbean dialects
- Different from Negerhollands (Dutch-based)
- Many expressions known only to older population
- Continuously evolving with slang/idioms

### Economy
- Tourism: ~45% national income
- Financial services: >50% income
- Popular sailing destination
- Charter boats common

### Regional Connections
- CARICOM Associate Member (since 1991)
- Caribbean Single Market participation
- Close ties to US Virgin Islands
- Shared Virgin Islands heritage
