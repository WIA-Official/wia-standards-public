## Discovery Story

### Roger Blench Encounter
In October 2003, linguist Roger Blench was conducting a survey in the Bura-speaking area of Northeast Nigeria when he encountered an unreported deaf language:

> "I was in the village of Kukurpu when I saw two men signing to one another. When I expressed an interest, a third man was called, who apparently lost hearing later in life and could articulate Bura fairly intelligibly."

### Follow-up Visit
- December 13, 2003: Andy Warren (UBS Bible translator)
- Assisted by Joshua Pakshar (Bura Bible translator)
- Made videotape of signers

## Characteristics

### High Congenital Deafness
- Area has high degree of hereditary deafness
- Multiple deaf individuals in village
- Deaf community established

### Independence
Likely quite independent from other sign languages:
- No signers have attended school
- Remote area
- No exposure to Nigerian Sign Language
- Isolated development

## Linguistic Features

### West African Signing Style
Bura SL shows characteristics of indigenous West African signing:
- Lax hand shapes (relaxed articulation)
- Large signing space
- Outstretched arms when pointing
- Not tense like Western sign languages

### Lexical Similarities
Many signs identical or similar to other West African sign languages:

| Concept | Sign | Also in |
|---------|------|---------|
| GO | Same motion | Adamorobe SL, Nanabin SL |
| WORK | "Sweat" metaphor | Ghana, Mali gestures |
| NEXT DAY | "Sleep" metaphor | Common West African |

### Shared Gestural Origins
Signs appear based on gestures common to hearing populations across West Africa:
- Conventionalized metaphors
- Regional gesture patterns
- Cultural iconicity shared

## Religious Context

### Animism
- Most common religion among Bura people
- Spirits in natural objects
- Fear-based ritual system

### Deaf Religious Understanding
- Deaf tend to have no religious understanding
- May imitate outward forms
- Lack access to religious teaching

### Christianity
- Few Christians in Bura area
- Complete Bible exists in Bura (print, audio)
- JESUS Film available
- Audio recordings exist
- **NONE accessible to Deaf**
