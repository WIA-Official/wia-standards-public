# Kuwaiti Sign Language / Lughat al-Ishara al-Kuwaytiya (KSL)

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Kuwaiti Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Kuwaytiya |
| **Glottolog Code** | kuwa1252 |
| **Region** | Kuwait |
| **Users** | ~7,000 deaf individuals |
| **Language Family** | Arab sign-language family |

## Historical Development

### Early Education Era (1960s)
- 1960: Al-Amal (Hope) School for people with special needs established
- 1965: Ministry of Education law made education mandatory for special needs
- Kuwait second Arab country to introduce deaf education

### Regional Influence
- GCC countries sign languages closely related
- Older generation of deaf in Gulf countries studied in Kuwait before 1990
- Kuwait influenced development of sign language across Gulf region

### Modern Development
- 1975: Kuwait Society for Deaf and Dumb established (now Kuwait Sport Club for the Deaf)
- 1999: Arab countries unified sign languages - dictionary with 3,000+ signs
- Kuwait first country to interpret three TV news bulletins in sign language
- 2020: Bulletins increased to eight per day

## Linguistic Features
- Rich, expressive, and complex like spoken language
- Own grammar framework
- Closely related to other Gulf sign languages
- Contact signs used with other Arab countries

### Relationship with Other Sign Languages
- Very close to other GCC sign languages
- Related to Arab sign-language family
- American Sign Language learned as second language for international use

## Pan-Arab Sign Language Controversy
- 1999: Unified Arabic Sign Language dictionary created
- Deaf community prefers KSL or combination
- Research shows preference against ArSL alone
- National sign languages have sociocultural importance

## Achievements
- First country with 3 interpreted TV news bulletins
- Crown Prince directed National Guards to learn SL
- Active advocacy for government appointments of SL experts
