# WIA Talk Sign Language Mapping - LSE (Lengua de Signos Española / Spanish Sign Language)
## Batch 10: 20 Signs with Full WIA Talk Component Analysis

**Language:** LSE - Lengua de Signos Española (Spanish Sign Language)
**Region:** Spain (primarily central-interior Iberian Peninsula, Madrid epicenter)
**Estimated Users:** ~100,000+ speakers (20-30% as second language)
**Language Family:** Distinct; NOT related to ASL or LSF
**Recognition:** Law 27/2007 officially recognized LSE

---

## LSE Characteristics

### Historical Background
- Developed within Spanish deaf community
- 1936: Spanish Deaf People Confederation (CNSE) established
- 2007: Law 27/2007 officially recognized Spanish sign languages
- June 14th: National Day of Spanish Sign Languages (commemorates CNSE founding)
- CNLSE: Center for the Linguistic Normalization of Spanish Sign Language

### Regional Variations in Spain
Spain has multiple sign language varieties:
- **LSE** - Lengua de Signos Española (Madrid-centered, most widespread)
- **LSC** - Catalan Sign Language (Catalonia)
- **Valencian Sign Language** (Valencia)
- **Andalusian dialects** (eastern vs western differences)
- **Canary Islands** variant
- **Galician** variant
- **Basque Country** variant

**Mutual intelligibility** is generally HIGH due to shared lexicon, but LSC and eastern variants are most distinctive (10%+ lexical difference).

### Legal Recognition
- **Law 27/2007:** Recognizes "Spanish sign languages"
- **Statute of Autonomy of Catalonia:** LSC has normative autonomy
- **Statutes of Andalusia and Aragon:** Explicitly recognize LSE

### Key Features
- **One-handed manual alphabet** (like ASL, unlike BSL)
- **Different from ASL** - Completely separate languages
- **Mouth movements emphasized** - More than ASL's eyebrow emphasis
- **Name signs required** - Teachers assign name signs in class
- **More signs than fingerspelling** - Each month has a sign (unlike ASL)
- **Reversed word order** from spoken Spanish

### Organizations
- **CNSE** - Confederación Estatal de Personas Sordas (National)
- **CNLSE** - Center for Linguistic Normalization
- **Royal Board on Disability** - Oversees CNLSE
- **WFD** - World Federation of the Deaf (International)

### LSE vs ASL Differences (noted by learners)
| Feature | ASL | LSE |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Letter "U" | Standard U | Looks like ASL "V" |
| Letter "V" | Standard V | Shaken V |
| Non-manual | Strong eyebrow use | More mouth/tongue use |
| Months | Fingerspell (3 letters) | Each has unique sign |
| Name signs | Optional | Required - teacher assigns |
| Numbers | Palm often toward viewer | Palm facing forward |

**LSE Concept:** Salute-like greeting

**Detailed Motion:**
- Raise hand near forehead
- Move outward, like a small salute
- Friendly facial expression
- Often accompanied by smile

|-----------|------|-------------|
| HS | 02 | Flat open hand |

## SECTION B: Numbers 1-10 (Números)

### LSE Number System Overview

LSE numbers differ from ASL numbers:
- **One-handed** system
- **Palm often faces forward** (toward viewer)
- Based on research: "Colours and Numerals in Spanish Sign Language" (Báez-Montero & Fernández-Soneira, 2016)

**Key Differences from ASL (Fund for Education Abroad observation):**
- LSE numbers have palm forward more often
- Different finger configurations for some numbers
- Numbers 1-5 similar to many languages
- 6-10 have specific LSE patterns

## LSE Linguistic Features Summary

### Grammar
- **Reversed word order** from spoken Spanish
- **Non-manual markers:** Mouth movements emphasized
- **Mouthing:** Specific mouth patterns (e.g., "-mo" for "cómo")
- **Facial expressions:** Critical for grammar

### Non-Manual Features Unique to LSE
- **Tongue protrusion:** For sign "tener" (to have)
- **Repeated mouthing:** For "cómo" (how)
- Less eyebrow emphasis than ASL
- More mouth/tongue involvement

### Cultural Features
- **Name signs required:** Teachers assign them in LSE classes
- **Every month has a sign:** Unlike ASL which fingerspells
- **Strong regional identity:** LSC (Catalan) has separate status

### Related Sign Languages
- **LSC** - Catalan Sign Language (autonomous)
- **LSM** - Mexican Sign Language (from LSF, not LSE)
- **Venezuelan Sign Language** - Based on LSE
- **Latin American varieties** - Various origins
