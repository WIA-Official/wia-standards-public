# Macedonian Sign Language
## Makedonski znakoven jazik / Makedonski gestoven jazik

### Legal Recognition (2009)
Law defines sign language as:
- Natural way of communication equal to oral communication
- Means for mutual understanding between hearing impaired
- Natural means of communication for deaf and others
- Visual system of signs including hand positions, movements, facial mimicry

### Law Provisions
- Defines the language itself
- Establishes rights of deaf people
- Regulates language study and interpreter training
- Defines National Association tasks and financing
- Secures implementation of deaf rights
- Right to request interpreter (institution must provide)

### Manual Alphabet
- 31 signs matching 31 Macedonian sounds
- Each letter has its own sign
- Two types: one-hand and two-hand alphabets

### Education System
- Elementary school in Bitola
- High school in Skopje
- Specializations: locksmith (men), textile (women)
- University access limited (only ~5 graduates)
- Most teachers do not know sign language
- Graduate education level equivalent to 11-year-old

### Challenges
- Children separated from parents from age 7
- Limited educational options
- No Bible translation in Macedonian SL
- No Christian congregations serving deaf
- Half of population poorly educated

### Television Access
- Signed news available on Macedonian TV
- 6,000 people request signed broadcasts
