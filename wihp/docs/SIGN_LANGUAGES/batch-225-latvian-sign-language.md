## Legal Recognition

### Official Language Law (1999/2000)
**Section 3.3**:
> "The State shall ensure the development and use of the Latvian Sign Language for communication with people with impaired hearing."

### Status Clarification
- LSL is MENTIONED in law
- Does NOT fully recognize LSL as language
- Gives deaf people RIGHTS regarding their language
- State required to ensure development and use

### Comparison with Estonia
- Similar Baltic context
- Different recognition level
- LSL less formally recognized

## Language Classification

### Manual Alphabet Study
76 sign language manual alphabets analyzed:
- LSL shows characteristics of BOTH:
  - "Russian Group" (Russian, Ukrainian, Bulgarian, Estonian)
  - "Polish Group" (Polish, Lithuanian)
- LSL placed BETWEEN these groups

### Relationship to Estonian Sign Language
**Mahoney (2017)** - First comparison study:
- 100-word Swadesh-Woodward list
- Core lexicons appear similar
- Shared parameters with small handshape variations
- NO mother-daughter relationship established
- Possible shared parent language
- Soviet-era dissemination unclear

### Possible Origins
- May descend from Old French Sign Language (VLFS)
- Via Austrian SL (ÖGS) and/or Russian SL
- Soviet influence significant
- Historical connections to Estonian SL

## Education System

### Deaf Education
- 2 specialist schools for DHI children
- 10-12 years of elementary education
- Newborn hearing screening since 2008

### School Types
| School | Method |
|--------|--------|
| School 1 | LSL + Signed Latvian |
| School 2 | Spoken language |

### Notable Pattern
- Children communicate in sign language among themselves
- Even in spoken language schools
- Shows natural preference for visual communication

### Living Situation
- Majority of deaf children in boarding schools
- Separated from families
- Important community building effect

## Challenges

### Recognition Gap
- Mentioned but not fully recognized
- Not used as first language in all education
- Not recognized as main communication means (historically)

### Small Population
- ~2,000 users
- Limited resources
- Interpreter shortage likely

### Boarding School System
- Children separated from families
- Language transmission affected
- Community building vs family involvement
