# Bahraini Sign Language / Lughat al-Ishara al-Bahrainiya

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Bahraini Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Bahrainiya (لغة الإشارة البحرينية) |
| **Region** | Kingdom of Bahrain |
| **Users** | ~8,000+ deaf individuals |
| **Language Family** | Arab sign-language family (Gulf cluster) |
| **Status** | Developing, community supported |

## Historical Development

### Bahrain Deaf Society (2007)
- Official registration: July 7, 2007
- Registration number: 6/c/ag
- 50 founding members (half deaf)
- 243 members as of recent count (135 males, 108 females)
- Board of Directors: 9 members (4 deaf, gender equal)

### Modern Developments
- 2009 Haualand Survey: 4 out of 5 accessibility points
- Regular celebration of International Day of Sign Languages
- Corporate partnerships (e.g., Zain Bahrain)
- Medical sector sign language training programs

## Linguistic Features

### Gulf Arabic SL Family
- Related to other Gulf sign languages
- Part of Arab sign-language family
- Non-diglossic (unlike spoken Arabic)
- Flexible sentence structure (VSO, SVO, OVS, VOS)

### Cultural Elements
- Reflects Bahraini culture and society
- Workshop programs for Bahraini Sign Language basics
- Professional interpreter training available

## Accessibility (2009 Survey)
- Scored 4 out of 5 points (second highest in MENA)
- Has national deaf/hard of hearing organization ✓
- Has sign language interpreter training ✓
- Has interpreter service ✓
- Has government funding for interpreters ✓
- Lacks recognized interpreter profession ✗

## Challenges
- Only 2% employment rate in institutions
- Communication barriers cited for job rejection
- Limited career advancement opportunities
- Cost barriers: hearing aids, translation, alert devices
- Dependence on limited support resources

## Goals and Initiatives
- Integration of deaf into society
- Educational, cultural, athletic development
- Full equality with hearing population
- Job opportunities with government/private cooperation
- Rights and facilities for deaf individuals

## Cultural Significance
- Annual Ghabga celebrations
- Partnership between deaf and hearing communities
- Growing corporate social responsibility
- Medical community engagement
