# Bouakako Sign Language / LaSiBo

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Bouakako Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Langue des signes Bouakako (LaSiBo) |
| **ISO 639-3** | Not assigned |
| **Region** | Bouakako village, near Hire, southern Cote dIvoire |
| **Type** | Emerging Village Sign Language |
| **Age** | At least 50 years (as of 2016) |

## Historical Development
- Emerged spontaneously in response to high deafness incidence in Bouakako
- Village located 6 km west of Hire town
- Used by several generations of deaf people, most related
- Hearing community (Yocoboue Dida speakers) also knows the language

## Linguistic Features
- Variable vocabulary between speakers (indicates young language)
- Similar to Nanabin Sign Language (Ghana) and Mardin Sign Language (Turkey)
- Shows characteristics of emerging sign languages
- Concentrated among one or a few families

## Research
- Documented by Dr. Angoua Tano (Leiden University)
- Part of corpus project Un corpus de reference de LaSiBo (2014)
- PhD thesis: Etude dune langue des signes emergente (2016)
- Published research on Size and Shape Specifiers (2018)

## Significance
- Important example of village sign language emergence
- Shows natural sign language development in West Africa
- Provides insights into sign language genesis
- Shared by both deaf and hearing community members
