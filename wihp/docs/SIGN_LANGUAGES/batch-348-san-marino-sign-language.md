### Language Description
San Marino, the world's oldest republic and one of the smallest countries (61 km², population ~34,000), does not have a distinct national sign language. The Deaf community in San Marino uses Italian Sign Language (Lingua dei Segni Italiana, LIS), reflecting the country's geographic, cultural, and linguistic integration with Italy.

The republic is entirely surrounded by Italy and shares Italian as its primary language. Deaf Sammarinese participate in the broader Italian deaf community and access Italian deaf services, education, and media.

Any local variation in signing would be minimal and represent regional variation within LIS rather than a separate language.

### Key Characteristics
- **LIS Usage**: Same language as surrounding Italy
- **Italian Integration**: Services accessed in Italy
- **Microstate Context**: Too small for distinct language
- **Regional Variation**: May have minor local signs
- **Cultural Identity**: Sammarinese identity within LIS community

### Deaf Community
The tiny Sammarinese deaf population:
- Estimated 50-100 deaf individuals
- Integrated with Italian deaf community
- Access services in Rimini (nearest Italian city)
- May study in Italian deaf schools
- Participate in Italian deaf organizations

### Education
- **Local Schools**: Limited, may include deaf students
- **Italian System**: Most deaf students educated in Italy
- **Special Education**: Accessed through Italian system
- **University**: Study at Italian universities

### Legal Status
- San Marino has no specific sign language legislation
- Italy's 2021 LIS recognition affects Sammarinese deaf
- Cross-border service access well established
- EU association agreement supports mobility

### Healthcare
San Marino has agreement with Italy for:
- Specialist healthcare access
- Audiological services
- Early intervention programs
- Hearing technology

### Challenges
1. Microstate cannot support full deaf services
2. Reliance on Italy for all specialized needs
3. No distinct language documentation needed
4. Small community limits local organizing
5. Young deaf often relocate to Italy

### Cultural Notes
While using LIS, Sammarinese deaf may have signs for:
- **Monte Titano**: The mountain and towers
- **Captains Regent**: Unique government system
- **Local Festivals**: Traditional celebrations
- **Historic Sites**: Three towers, churches

### Regional Context
San Marino is part of Emilia-Romagna regional deaf community:
- Connections to Rimini, Bologna, Ravenna deaf
- Participates in regional deaf events
- Access to regional interpreter services
- Part of Italian deaf cultural sphere
