# Qatari Sign Language / Qatari Unified Sign Language (QSL)

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Qatari Unified Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Qatariya |
| **Region** | Qatar |
| **Language Family** | Arab sign-language family |
| **Legal Status** | Recognized since 2001 |

## Historical Development

### Government Recognition (2001)
- Qatar government first recognized Qatari Sign Language in 2001
- Pioneering step in the Arab region

### Institutional Development (2005)
- 2005: Qatari Center of Social Cultural for the Deaf (QCSCD) established
- Part of civil rights reformations for underserved groups
- Affiliated with Ministry of Culture, Arts and Heritage
- Member of World Federation of the Deaf
- Chaired by Ali Al-Sennari (as of 2015)

### Modern Developments
- Supreme Council for Family Affairs proposed unified sign language
- Dictionary of Qatari Sign Language published
- BuHamad Project - Qatars first 3D signing avatar using AI

## Linguistic Features
- Emerged organically within community
- Influenced by local culture and communication needs
- Own grammatical rules different from spoken Arabic
- Uses spatial grammar and facial expressions
- Regional variations exist within Qatar
- Cultural elements incorporated (traditional customs)

## Organizations
- QCSCD - Qatari Center of Social Cultural for the Deaf
- Supreme Council for Family Affairs
- Ministry of Education and Higher Education
- Qatar Foundation - accessibility support

### QCSCD Objectives
- Help deaf people assimilate into hearing society
- Offer unique services to deaf individuals
- Inform deaf individuals of their rights
- Advocate via media for anti-discrimination laws

## Education
- QSL used in schools for the deaf
- Introduced into curriculum by Ministry of Education
- Lack of education after primary school level
- Limited Sign Language interpreter availability

## Accessibility Survey Results
- Qatar: Only Arab country with 5/5 accessibility points (2009 survey)
- Satisfied all criteria for interpreter access and deaf support

## Cultural Significance
- Cornerstone of Deaf culture in Qatar
- Social connection among deaf individuals
- Part of national identity alongside Arabic and English
- Ongoing standardization efforts
