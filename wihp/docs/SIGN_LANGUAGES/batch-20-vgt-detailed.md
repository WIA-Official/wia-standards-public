# WIA Talk Sign Language Mapping - VGT (Vlaamse Gebarentaal / Flemish Sign Language)
## Batch 20: 20 Signs with Full WIA Talk Component Analysis

**Language:** VGT - Vlaamse Gebarentaal (Flemish Sign Language)
**Region:** Flanders (northern Belgium), Brussels-Capital Region
**Estimated Users:** 5,000-6,000 sign language users
**Language Family:** French Sign Language family (shared origin with NGT)
**Recognition:** April 26, 2006 (Flemish Parliament decree)

---

## VGT Characteristics

### Historical Background
- **Origin:** Based on French Sign Language (same as NGT)
- **1980s-early 1990s:** Lexical unification efforts in Signed Dutch
- **Late 1990s:** Flemish Deaf community rejected Signed Dutch, promoted VGT
- **1998:** First bilingual-bicultural education with VGT as first language
- **2001:** Name "Vlaamse Gebarentaal" officially given
- **2003:** French Belgian Sign Language (LSFB) recognized
- **2004:** First electronic VGT/Dutch dictionary published online
- **2004:** First deaf Member of Flemish Parliament elected (Helga Stevens)
- **2004:** Doof Actie Front (Deaf Action Front) founded
- **April 26, 2006:** Official recognition by Flemish Parliament (unanimous!)
- **2012:** Daily news broadcasts interpreted into VGT

### Five Regional Dialects
Developed in different deaf schools:
1. **Antwerpen** (Antwerp)
2. **Limburg**
3. **Oost-Vlaanderen** (East Flanders)
4. **Vlaams-Brabant** (Flemish Brabant)
5. **West-Vlaanderen** (West Flanders)

Also: intra-regional variation related to gender and age

### Relationship with Other Sign Languages
- **NOT the same as NGT** (Dutch Sign Language) despite shared spoken Dutch!
- **Most similar to French Belgian Sign Language (LSFB)**
- Both VGT and NGT share **French Sign Language** origin
- Fingerspelling system similar to **French Sign Language**
- Influence from spoken Dutch (particularly in mouthing)

### Belgium Sign Language Situation
- **VGT:** For Dutch/Flemish community (Flanders)
- **LSFB:** For French-speaking community (Wallonia)
- Each has own grammar and vocabulary

### Key Features
- **Regional variation included** in dictionaries (labeled as such)
- **No standardization from above** - community decision
- Influence from spoken Dutch in mouthing
- Limited influence from Signed Dutch

### Organizations & Resources
- **Vlaams GebarentaalCentrum** - Knowledge and information centre
- **Fevlado** (Federatie van Vlaamse DovenOrganisaties)
- **Doof Vlaanderen vzw** (2017 merger)
- **Ghent University** - VGT research
- **KU Leuven** - VGT research

### Deaf Schools (7 schools)
Most still use Signed or spoken Dutch
- **Spermalie** - offers Deaf Culture and VGT
- **Sint-Lievenspoort** - offers Deaf Culture and VGT  
- **Sint-Gregorius** - bilingual-bicultural education

### 2006 Recognition Details
1. Cultural recognition as language of Deaf Community
2. Advisory committee (max 15 members, half must be deaf)
3. Establishment of knowledge/information centre

## WIA Talk 5-Component System Reference

| Code | Component | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| HS | Hand Shape | Configuration of fingers and palm |
| LC | Location | Where the sign is performed |
| MV | Movement | Direction and type of motion |

| HS03 | 5 fingers spread | Number 5, Number 10 |
| HS04 | V-shape | Number 2, Name |
| HS07 | Index finger | Number 1 |

| HS48 | O-shape | Number 0 |
| HS52 | Three fingers | Number 3 |
| HS53 | VGT 6 shape | Number 6 |

### Unique VGT Features
- **5 regional dialects** (developed in different deaf schools)
- **No top-down standardization** - community choice
- **Regional variation labeled** in dictionaries
- **NOT same as NGT** despite shared spoken Dutch
- **Most similar to LSFB** (French Belgian Sign Language)
- **First deaf MP** elected 2004 (Helga Stevens)
- **97% newborn hearing screening** (1998+)
- **Mouthing from spoken Dutch**

### Belgium's Two Sign Languages
| Language | Region | Recognition |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| VGT | Flanders (Dutch) | April 26, 2006 |
| LSFB | Wallonia (French) | October 2003 |
