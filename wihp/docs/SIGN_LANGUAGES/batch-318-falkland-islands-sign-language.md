# Falkland Islands Sign Language
## Sign Language of the Falkland Islands (South Atlantic)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | British Sign Language (BSL) influence likely |
| **Country** | Falkland Islands (UK) |
| **Status** | British Overseas Territory |
| **Capital** | Stanley |
| **Population** | ~3,000 (2016 census: 2,563) |

### Language Situation
- British Sign Language (BSL) presumed primary due to UK ties
- Very small population (~3,000 total)
- Part of SIL 24-location Americas survey
- Geographically isolated South Atlantic location
- Strong British linguistic and cultural heritage

### Geographic Context
- British Overseas Territory in South Atlantic
- 300 miles (500 km) east of South America
- 752 miles (1,210 km) from Antarctic Peninsula
- Archipelago of 778 islands
- Total area: 4,700 sq mi (12,200 km²)
- Latitude: ~52°S

### Main Islands
- **East Falkland** (largest, includes Stanley)
- **West Falkland**
- 776 smaller islands

### Official Language
- **English** - Only official language (89% speakers)
- Falkland Islands English dialect
  - British character with local features
  - Non-rhotic pronunciation
  - Influences: Australian, New Zealand, West Country, Norfolk, Lowland Scots
- **Spanish** - 10% (mostly Chilean immigrants)
- **Filipino** - 1.5%
- **Shona** (Zimbabwe) - small community

### Historical Languages
- French (first colonizers, 1764)
- Scottish Gaelic (early settlers)
- Yaghan (extinct, Tierra del Fuego missionaries)

### Political Status
- British Overseas Territory
- Internal self-governance
- UK responsible for defense/foreign affairs
- 2013 Referendum: 99.8% voted to remain British
- Governor appointed by British Crown
- Legislative Assembly (elected members)

### Historical Context
- First undisputed landing: John Strong (1690)
- French settlement: Port Louis (1764)
- British settlement: Port Egmont (1765)
- Spanish period (1774-1811)
- Argentine claim dispute
- **1982 Falklands War**: UK retook islands from Argentina
- RAF Mount Pleasant military base established after war

### Population Demographics
- Primarily British descent
- 47% born locally
- Many with international roots
- Called Kelpers (from kelp seaweed)
- Nine generations of pioneering families

### Deaf Community
- Very small due to tiny population
- Limited documentation available
- Connection to UK deaf services likely
- Isolation challenges significant
- BSL probably used but not confirmed

### Economy
- Sheep farming traditional
- Fishing (squid)
- Tourism (wildlife, penguins)
- Government services
- Oil exploration potential

### Wildlife (Unique Environment)
- 1+ million penguins (5 species)
- Black-browed albatrosses
- Sea lions, elephant seals
- 65+ bird species

### Local Terms
- Kelper = Falkland Islander
- Smoko = smoking break (like Australia/NZ)
- Camp = rural areas (from Spanish campo)
- Yomp = military march term

### Regional Sign Language Context
- Nearest: Argentina (LSA - Argentine Sign Language)
- UK connection: BSL (British Sign Language)
- Geographically isolated from both
