# Mayan Sign Languages
## Meemul Tziij / Meemul Chaabal Complex

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Language Complex** | Meemul Tziij / Meemul Chaabal |
| **Meaning** | Mute language (Kiche) |
| **Region** | Yucatan, Chiapas, Guatemala |
| **Countries** | Mexico, Guatemala, Belize |
| **Language Family** | Ancient Mesoamerican sign language family |
| **Status** | Indigenous village sign languages |

### Language Description
- Complex of indigenous sign languages in Mesoamerica
- Used by Maya communities with high deaf populations
- Unrelated to Mexican Sign Language or Guatemalan Sign Language
- Unrelated to spoken Mayan languages
- Possibly derived from ancient signed lingua franca
- Pre-Hispanic origins proposed by researchers

### Geographic Distribution
Three main documented varieties:
1. **Yucatan, Mexico** (Yucatec Maya Sign Language)
2. **Chiapas, Mexico** (Chiapas Maya Sign Language)
3. **Guatemala Highlands** (Highland Mayan Sign Language)

### Yucatec Maya Sign Language
- **Location**: Yucatan Peninsula
- **Community**: Village of Chican
- **Discovery**: 1976 (Hubert Smith film project)
- **Population**: 13 deaf in 450 total (1976)
- **ISO 639-3**: msd
- **Status**: Natural, complex language

**Key Features**:
- Used by both deaf and hearing villagers
- Deaf well-integrated into community
- Confirmed as language by Gallaudet researchers (1987)

### Highland Mayan Sign Language (Guatemala)
- **Location**: Kiche region, Guatemala highlands
- **Community**: Nahuala (Department of Solola)
- **Local Name**: Meemul Chaabal, Meemul Tziij
- **Users**: Deaf rural Maya, traders, storytellers
- **Distance from Yucatan**: 600 kilometers
- **Mutual intelligibility**: Reported with Yucatec Maya SL

### Key Researchers
- **Erich Fox Tree**: Primary researcher on Meemul Tziij (2009)
- **Hubert Smith**: Maya Film Project (1977-2006)
- **Robert Johnson and Carol Erting**: Gallaudet University
- **Malcolm K. Shuman**: Early documentation (1980)

### Community Integration
- Deaf persons fully participate in community life
- Both deaf and hearing use sign language
- Deafness is positive constitutive feature
- All eligible deaf men have married

### Historical Context
- Possibly pre-Hispanic origins
- Ancient gestures in Maya artwork
- May hold key to ancient Maya iconography
