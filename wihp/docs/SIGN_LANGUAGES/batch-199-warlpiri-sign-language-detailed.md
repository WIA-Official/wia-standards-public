## The Most Studied Aboriginal Sign Language

Warlpiri Sign Language is:
- Most elaborate of all Aboriginal sign languages
- Most extensively documented
- Complete system of communication (not simplified)
- As expressive as spoken Warlpiri

## Why So Developed

### Mourning Practice (Key Factor)
- Widows must not speak during mourning
- Mourning period: months to YEARS
- Communicate SOLELY by sign during this time
- Widows live together with other widows
- Creates intensive sign language environment

### Result: Gender Difference
- Women have BETTER sign language command
- Older women use sign constantly
- Even when NOT under speech ban
- Men understand but use less frequently

## Linguistic Features

### Relationship to Spoken Warlpiri
Adam Kendon argues rdaka-rdaka is:
- Manual representation of spoken Warlpiri
- Signs represent morphemes from speech
- Same word order as oral language

### What IS Signed
- Content words
- Most vocabulary items
- Directional information

### What is NOT Signed
- Case relation markers
- Tense markers
- Cliticised pronouns

### Unique Features
- Some spatial grammar NOT in spoken Warlpiri
- Lips used for pointing (north direction)
- Finger clicks for emphasis
- Body location articulations

## Example Sentence

Spoken: "ga na jani jadidjara gura binga-dju"
(I am going a long way towards the north)

Signed sequence:
1. Touch chest (I)
2. Move right index finger (going)
3. Point north with lips
4. Click fingers towards north (long way, emphatic)

## Communities

### Yuendumu
- Main research site (Kendon 1978-1986)
- Strongest sign language tradition
- Population: approximately 800

### Lajamanu
- Northern dialect area
- Young people developed Light Warlpiri language

### Willowra
- Central dialect area
- Near Lander River

### Nyirrpi
- Smaller community
- Sign language tradition maintained

## Current Status

### Still Used For
- Mourning practices
- Hunting
- Private communication
- Ceremonies
- Daily conversation among older women

### Threats
- Younger generation less fluent
- Modern lifestyle reduces traditional contexts
- Competition from Auslan
- Movement away from Country

### Deaf Warlpiri Today
- Some develop home sign systems
- Some learn Auslan at distant schools
- Traditional sign less automatically adopted
- 1980s: deaf naturally used rdaka-rdaka
- Today: more variation in deaf experience

## Warlpiri People Context

### Population
- 5,000-6,000 Warlpiri people
- 3,000 still speak Warlpiri language
- Multilingual community

### Other Languages
- English (usually 2nd-5th language)
- Light Warlpiri (new mixed language)
- Arrernte, Jaru, Western Desert Language
- Warumungu

### Kinship System (Subsection)
- 8 subsections govern social relationships
- Sign language respects avoidance rules
- Mother-in-law avoidance extends to Europeans
