# Panamanian Sign Language (LSP)
## Lengua de Senas Panamenas

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Panamanian Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lengua de Senas Panamenas (LSP) |
| **ISO 639-3** | lsp |
| **Country** | Panama |
| **Users** | ~6,000 deaf people estimated |
| **Status** | Stable; Recognized (1992) |
| **Language Family** | ASL-derived, Salvadoran SL influence |

### Legal Recognition (1992)
- Panama officially recognized Panamanian Sign Language
- Recognized as natural language
- Significant step for deaf community rights

### Two Sign Languages in Panama
1. **LSP (Lengua de Senas Panamenas)**: Urban areas
2. **LSCH (Lengua de Senas de Chiriqui)**: Chiriqui province

### Historical Development
- Derived from American Sign Language
- Influenced by Salvadoran Sign Language
- Closest relative: Honduras Sign Language
- Continuous ASL influence documented

### Geographic Distribution
- **Panama City**: Main hub, Albrook Mall gatherings
- **David (Chiriqui)**: Second major hub, Central Park
- Two-thirds of deaf live in rural areas without SL access

### Language Acquisition
- Most learn from deaf friends, not family or school
- Majority mainstreamed in hearing schools without interpreters
- Sign language classes mainly for hearing people
- Deaf community primary transmission method

### Research and Documentation
- **2009**: SIL rapid-appraisal survey
- **2011**: Parks, Williams, Parks sociolinguistic profile
- **1990**: First LSP book by Pimentel, Walker, Fajardo
- **2019**: EnSenias platform developed

### EnSenias Platform
- Online learning tool for Panamanian Sign Language
- Developed at Panamanian Institute for Special Enabling
- Functions as dictionary and translator
- Universal accessibility features
- Supports deaf, hearing, blind, and deaf-blind users

### Challenges
- Limited interpreter availability
- Few deaf employed independently
- Rural isolation from deaf community
- Deaf associations not yet unified
- Dictionary development ongoing

### Organizations
- SENADIS (National Secretariat for Disability)
- Panamanian Institute for Special Enabling
- Deaf associations in Panama City and David

### Linguistic Relationship
- Related to Honduras Sign Language
- ASL and Salvadoran SL influences
- Chiriqui SL also part of same family
