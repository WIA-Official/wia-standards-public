### Language Description
Grand Cayman Sign Language was an indigenous sign language that developed in the Cayman Islands, particularly in the community of Old Man Bay on Grand Cayman. The language emerged due to a high incidence of hereditary deafness in certain families, creating a situation similar to Martha's Vineyard where both deaf and hearing community members used sign language.

The language is now believed to be extinct or nearly so, with deaf Caymanians now using American Sign Language (ASL) or British Sign Language (BSL) through education and media.

### Old Man Bay Community
The village of Old Man Bay on Grand Cayman:
- Small fishing community
- Hereditary deafness in several families
- High proportion of deaf residents
- Entire community learned to sign
- Hearing people signed fluently

### Language Shift
Factors causing decline:
1. Modern deaf education (ASL-based)
2. Television and media exposure
3. Integration with broader community
4. Death of elderly signers
5. Immigration and emigration
6. British connection (BSL influence)
