# Saint Pierre and Miquelon Sign Language
## Sign Language of Saint Pierre and Miquelon (North Atlantic)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | French Sign Language (LSF) |
| **Native Name** | Langue des Signes Française |
| **Country** | Saint Pierre and Miquelon (France) |
| **Status** | French Overseas Collectivity |
| **Capital** | Saint-Pierre |
| **Population** | ~5,819 (2022) |

### Language Situation
- French Sign Language (LSF) presumed primary sign language
- Very small population (~5,819 total)
- Part of French national system
- Part of SIL 24-location Americas survey
- Last remnant of French North American empire

### Geographic Context
- French territorial collectivity in North Atlantic
- 15 miles (25 km) south of Newfoundland, Canada
- Archipelago of eight islands
- Total area: 242 km² (93 sq mi)
- Near Grand Banks of Newfoundland

### Main Islands
- **Saint-Pierre**: 26 km², 90% of population
- **Miquelon-Langlade**: 215 km² (connected by Isthmus of Langlade)
- Isle-aux-Marins (absorbed 1945)

### Official Language
- **French** - Official language (97.6% speakers)
- Metropolitan French variety (not Canadian French)
- Influences from Basque, Norman, Breton origins
- English also spoken (2.4%)
- Basque extinct on islands (late 1950s)

### Political Status
- French Overseas Collectivity (since 1985)
- EU Overseas Country and Territory (OCT)
- Part of Eurozone (uses Euro)
- French citizens with EU citizenship
- Residents elect deputy to National Assembly

### Historical Context
- First settled by French 1604
- Discovered by Portuguese José Alvarez Faguendez (1520)
- Exchanged between French and British multiple times
- Permanently French since 1816 (Treaty of Paris)
- Used in Prohibition-era smuggling (Al Capone connection)
- WWII: Free French control (1941)

### Cultural Identity
- French heritage maintained
- Maritime/fishing traditions
- Basque, Breton, Norman ancestry
- Distinct from Quebec/Acadian French culture
- Most western portion of Europe culturally
- Unique local expressions and vocabulary

### French Sign Language (LSF)
- ~100,000 native signers in France
- LSF recognized as language in France (2005)
- One-handed manual alphabet
- Related to ASL (~60% lexical similarity)
- Same legal protections as mainland France

### Deaf Community Size
- Very small due to tiny population
- Limited documentation available
- Connection to French national deaf services
- Potential isolation challenges

### Education System
- Free and compulsory (ages 6-16)
- French education model
- Primary: 5 years
- Secondary: 4+3 years (baccalauréat)
- 99% literacy rate

### Economy
- Fishing industry (declining)
- Tourism development
- Disputes with Canada over fishing rights
- Uses Euro currency

### Media
- L'Echo des Caps (weekly)
- 5 radio stations
- No daily newspaper
- French metropolitan media access

### Neighboring Sign Languages
- **Canada**: ASL (English areas), LSQ (French areas)
- Geographically near but culturally distinct
- No shared sign language with Newfoundland
