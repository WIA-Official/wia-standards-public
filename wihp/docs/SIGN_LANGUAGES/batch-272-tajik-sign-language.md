### Historical Development
- **~1940**: RSL imported by hearing Soviet educators
- **1940s**: First deaf school established (Leninsky School) near Dushanbe
- **Soviet Era**: Residential school model adopted
- **~80 years**: Continuous development at Leninsky School
- **2020**: First comprehensive linguistic research published

### Unique Emergence Pattern
- Hearing educators taught L2 varieties of RSL to children
- Neither educators nor students formed pre-existing language community
- Children came from heterogeneous geographic/linguistic backgrounds
- Contact situation led to unique language development
- Complex mix of RSL influence and local innovations

### Research Findings (Justin M. Power, 2020)
- First systematic study of signed language origins in Tajikistan
- Examined historical sources and transmission patterns
- Investigated educational institutions and social contexts
- Found >50% deaf Tajiks have limited institutional exposure
- Many signers communicate primarily with family/neighbors

### Language Characteristics
- Roots in Russian Sign Language varieties
- Developed unique features through contact situation
- Family-based signing coexists with school-based varieties
- Rural/urban variation significant
- Limited standardization

### Current Challenges
- Lack of qualified sign language interpreters
- National Union of the Deaf raised interpreter shortage
- Many deaf struggle to find interpretation services
- No official government recognition
- Limited educational resources

### Education System
- Residential school model
- Variable exposure among deaf population
- Some deaf never attend specialized institutions
- Home sign systems common in rural areas
