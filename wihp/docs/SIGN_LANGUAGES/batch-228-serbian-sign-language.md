## Legal Recognition

### Law on the Use of Sign Language (2015)
**April 28, 2015**: Serbian Parliament adopted the law

### Parliamentary Vote
- 164 voted in favor
- 2 abstained
- 166 total MPs
- 30 amendments made
- Live broadcast with SL interpretation

### 7-Year Journey
- Working group established
- National Organisation of the Deaf representatives
- Rigorous debate and differing opinions
- Final support from Minister of Labour

### Key Supporters
- Minister Aleksandar Vulin
- Assistant Vladimir Pesic
- Law based on deaf community proposals

### First Deaf School
**Stefan Dečanski**
- Established: 1896
- Location: Center of Belgrade
- Founder: Ministry of Education
- Method: "German system" (oralism)
- Exclusive speech method (glasovna metoda)

### German vs French Systems
| System | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| German (Heinicke) | Oralism, spoken language |
| French (de l'Épée) | Sign language education |
Serbia adopted German system → lasting oralist ideology

## Education Challenge

### Core Problem
> "SZJ: officially recognised, yet not used in deaf education"

### Research Findings (2021)
**Discrepancy in language ideologies**:
- Deaf signers: SZJ is "most important building block"
- Teachers: Hearing as "major factor in learning"
- Conflicting attitudes and practices

### FASPER Influence
- Faculty of Special Education and Rehabilitation
- Established surdopedagogy programs (1988, 1990, 1998)
- Oralist ideology perpetuated
- Most Belgrade deaf school teachers graduated from FASPER

### Strategy 2030
- Government Strategy on Education until 2030
- Action Plan for implementation
- Potential turning point being analyzed

## Challenges

### Education-Recognition Gap
- Officially recognized since 2015
- Still not used in deaf education
- Oralist ideology persists
- Teacher training needs reform

### Deaf Perspective vs System
