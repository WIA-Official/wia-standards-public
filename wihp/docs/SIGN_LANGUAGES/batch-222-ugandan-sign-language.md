## Historic Achievement

### Second Country in the World!
**Uganda was the SECOND country in the world to recognize sign language in its constitution (1995)**

### Constitutional Protection
- Right to respect and dignity for all with disabilities
- Persons with Disabilities Act 2020
- Uganda Communications Act 2013: All TV stations must have interpreters

## Historical Development

### Timeline

|------|-------|

| 1962 | Ugandan schools for deaf opened |
| 1969 | Ngora school opened |
| 1973 | Uganda National Association of the Deaf (UNAD) created |
| 1988 | Sign allowed in classroom - languages merged |

| 1995 | USL recognized in Constitution |
| 1998 | Manual of Ugandan Signs (UNAD) |
| 1999 | Manual revised |
| 2006 | Uganda Sign Language Dictionary |

### Language Origins

#### Possible Creole
- Several sign languages merged in 1988
- Local languages created in different schools
- Home signs evolved into USL
- First generation combined various systems

#### External Influences
| Source | Influence |
|--------|-----------|
| BSL | Early classroom instruction |
| ASL | Educational influence |
| Kenyan SL | Ugandans studying in Kenya |

#### Current Classification
- Glottolog: ASL most probable main progenitor
- BSL was first language in deaf schools
- Systematic lexical investigation still needed

## Education System

### Schools (as of 2014)
| Type | Number |
|------|--------|
| Primary schools for deaf | 11 |
| Secondary schools for deaf | 2 |
| Units in mainstream schools | ~40 |
| Districts with units | <40 of 100 |

### Access Crisis
- **Fewer than 2% of deaf children attend school**
- Knowledge of USL primarily urban
- Rural deaf have poor education access
- Not all deaf children can access schools

### Educational Approach
- Bilingual education
- Total communication methods
- English writing taught
- ~40% of deaf children learn to write English
- Preschool to university available

## Challenges

### Employment
- Discrimination in job market
- Communication barriers in interviews
- Many unemployed despite qualifications

### Education
- Poor primary completion rates
- Lack of school access
- Insufficient funding
- Rural-urban gap

### Social
- Negative attitudes from hearing people
- Social stigma in rural areas
- Abuse and discrimination
- Poverty cycle

### Covid-19 Impact
- School lessons on TV/radio were spoken only
- No sign language interpretation
- Deaf students excluded

## Linguistic Features

### Signed English Interference
- Some mixing with signed English
- Noted by Diane Brentari

### Communication Modes
- USL primary in education and social settings
- Lip reading varies by age of deafness onset
- Born deaf: shorter sentences
- Later deaf: better grammar, lip reading
