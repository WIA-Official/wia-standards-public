# Batch 217: Turkey Sign Languages

## Sign Languages in Turkey

Turkey has multiple sign languages:

| Language | Type | Location |
|----------|------|----------|
| Turkish Sign Language (TİD) | National | Nationwide |
| Central Taurus SL (CTSL) | Village | Taurus Mountains |
| Mardin SL (MarSL) | Family | Mardin region |

## 2. Central Taurus Sign Language (CTSL)

### Basic Information
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Turkish Name** | Orta Toroslar İşaret Dili |
| **Location** | Central Taurus Mountains, Southern Turkey |
| **Villages** | 3 neighboring villages |
| **Age** | ~50+ years (3-4 generations) |
| **Status** | ENDANGERED |

### Population
| Type | Number |
|------|--------|
| Deaf native signers | 36 |
| Deaf (CTSL only language) | 25 |
| Hearing signers | ~100 |
| Village population | ~450 |
| Deafness rate | ~4.6% |

### Discovery Story

**Rabia Ergin** - The Researcher's Personal Connection:
- Turkish cognitive science PhD student at Tufts
- Mother grew up in the village
- Spent summers visiting relatives
- Communicated with ~21 deaf relatives
- Seven generations of hereditary deafness in family
- Mentioned at seminar (2013) → "collective Wow!"

### Academic Interest

**Ray Jackendoff** (Tufts, Center for Cognitive Studies):
> "Here's something that starts with zero input from the culture, and yet it develops into a language over time."

### Why CTSL Matters

#### Unique Research Opportunity
- Very first creators still alive today
- Can track development from origins
- Three successive cohorts documented
- Window into language emergence

#### Independence from TİD
- No influence from Turkish Sign Language
- Developed in isolation
- Verified as distinct by TİD native signer
- Geographic, cultural, economic isolation

### Genetic Background
- Recessive deafness in community
- Consanguineous marriages common
- Small gene pool (7+ generations)
- High incidence of hereditary deafness

### Three Cohorts of Signers

| Cohort | Description | Age Range | Input |
|--------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| CTSL-1 | First deaf in family | 49-61 | No linguistic model |
| CTSL-2 | Younger siblings of CTSL-1 | 42-54 | Had signing siblings |
| CTSL-3 | Children of CTSL-1/2 | 24-30 | Full sign from birth |
| CTSL-4 | 4 deaf children | Young | Not yet documented |

### Linguistic Findings

#### Generational Development
- CTSL-1: Basic, variable signing
- CTSL-2: More combinatorial, morphologically complex
- CTSL-3: Further regularization

#### Grammatical Features Emerging
- Argument structure development
- Handshape classifiers
- Verb classes
- Word order patterns
- Reciprocal/symmetrical marking

#### Iconic Strategies
- Object-based strategies
- Action-based strategies
- Combinations becoming more systematic

### Community Characteristics

#### Isolation Factors
- Mountainous region (most mountainous in Turkey)
- Self-sustaining villages
- Agriculture and animal husbandry
- Economic isolation
- Limited contact with outside world

#### Social Structure
- Deaf connected by birth and marriage
- Extended family networks
- Hearing relatives sign with varying fluency

## Theoretical Significance

### Language Emergence Studies
CTSL provides evidence for:
1. How languages emerge from scratch
2. How structure develops over generations
3. How iconicity evolves into convention
4. How grammar conventionalizes

### Comparison with Other Emerging SL
- Nicaraguan Sign Language (community)
- Al-Sayyid Bedouin SL (village)
- CTSL (village/family)

Different transmission patterns:
- NSL: School-based
- ABSL: Village-wide
- CTSL: Extended family across villages
