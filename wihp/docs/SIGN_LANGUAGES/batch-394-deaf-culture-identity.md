### Introduction
Deaf culture refers to the social beliefs, behaviors, art, literary traditions, history, values, and shared institutions of communities influenced by deafness and sign language. The capital "D" in "Deaf" signifies cultural identity, distinct from "deaf" which refers to the audiological condition.

Deaf culture challenges the medical model of deafness, instead viewing being deaf as a cultural and linguistic identity to be celebrated.

### Medical vs. Cultural Models
| Model | Perspective |
|-------|-------------|
| Medical | Deafness as deficit to fix |
| Cultural | Deafness as identity, difference |
