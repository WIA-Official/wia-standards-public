# Batch 205: Al-Sayyid Bedouin Sign Language (ABSL)

## Overview
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Arabic Name** | لغة الإشارة لعشيرة السيد |
| **Location** | Al-Sayyid village, Negev Desert, Israel |
| **Village Population** | ~4,500 (2023: 5,775) |
| **Deaf Population** | 130-150 (4% of village) |
| **Age** | ~90 years (emerged 1920s-1930s) |
| **Generations** | 4+ generations |
| **Status** | VULNERABLE (contact with ISL increasing) |

## Significance for Linguistics

### The Forbidden Experiment
ABSL is the closest researchers can get to observing language birth:
- Brand NEW language (only 90 years old)
- Emerged WITHOUT influence from other sign languages
- Deaf people never exposed to ISL, Arabic, or Hebrew signs
- Allows study of how human language naturally forms

### 2005 Landmark Study
Published in Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences
- International team: Sandler, Meir, Padden, Aronoff
- Brought ABSL to worldwide attention
- Showed complex grammar emerged spontaneously

## Origin Story

### Founding Couple (mid-19th century)
- Egyptian man + local Bedouin woman settled in Negev
- Low social status (called foreign fellahin)
- Other tribes refused intermarriage
- Sons married women from Gaza area

### The Deaf Gene
- 2 of 5 founding sons carried recessive deafness gene
- Cousin marriage became common (27% of marriages)
- 65% of marriages between relatives
- Gene became widespread in population

### First Deaf Generation
- First deaf siblings born 1924-1940
- 10 deaf members in that generation
- They created the first signs
- Language passed to children and grandchildren

## Community Features

### No Deaf Stigma
- Deaf NOT marginalized
- Marriage between deaf and hearing common
- No separate Deaf culture or politics
- Full social integration

### Shared Signing Community
- 4% of village is deaf (50x normal rate)
- Hearing family members sign fluently
- Infants (deaf and hearing) exposed from birth
- Multiple adult signing models available

## Linguistic Features

### Word Order Discovery
ABSL uses SOV (Subject-Object-Verb):
- Example: woman child feed

This is DIFFERENT from:
- Spoken Arabic (SVO)
- Hebrew (SVO)
- Classical Arabic (VSO)
- Israeli Sign Language (SVO)
- Jordanian Sign Language

### Evidence of Natural Grammar
- SOV emerged in FIRST generation
- No influence from surrounding languages
- Suggests innate human grammar tendency

### Generational Development
| Generation | Features |
|------------|----------|
| 1st | Basic signs, SOV word order |
| 2nd | More conventionalized, faster |
| 3rd | 2x faster, longer sentences |
| 4th | Increasing complexity |

### Phonology Emergence
- Signs vary across community (understood through iconicity)
- Within families: more conventionalized forms
- Assimilation processes emerging in compounds
- Full phonology developing over generations

### Compounding
- New signs created by combining two nouns
- Length affix: touching wrist at different points
- Shows grammatical abstraction developing

## Current Threats

### Israeli Sign Language Contact
- Schools expose children to ISL
- Some deaf men work outside village
- Occasional intermarriage with non-Al-Sayyid deaf
- Code-switching and mixing increasing

### Language Change Concerns
- Younger signers incorporating ISL
- Pure ABSL may become mixed
- Older and younger generations may diverge
- Distinctiveness being corrupted

### The Dilemma
Learning ISL helps integration into Israeli society
BUT threatens unique ABSL features

## Martha Vineyard Parallel

### Similar Patterns
| Feature | Martha Vineyard | Al-Sayyid |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|
| Hereditary deafness | Yes | Yes |
| Shared signing | Yes | Yes |
| External contact | Led to decline | Beginning |
| Final outcome | Extinct (1952) | Still alive |

### Warning from History
Martha Vineyard SL disappeared within decades of mainland school contact.
ABSL may follow same path.
