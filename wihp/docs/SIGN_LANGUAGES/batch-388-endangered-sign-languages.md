### Introduction
Like spoken languages, sign languages can become endangered and extinct. The UN estimates that many of the world's 300+ sign languages are at risk, with some having fewer than 100 users. Sign language endangerment often receives less attention than spoken language loss, but represents an equally significant loss of human linguistic heritage.

### Endangerment Categories
| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| Vulnerable | Some children learn |
| Definitely Endangered | Children not learning |
| Severely Endangered | Only grandparents use |
| Critically Endangered | Fewer than 10 speakers |
| Extinct | No speakers remain |

### Causes of Sign Language Endangerment

#### Demographic Factors
| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Small community | Limited transmission |
| Aging signers | No new learners |
| Migration | Community dispersal |
| Intermarriage | Language shift |

#### Educational Factors
| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Oralism | Sign suppressed |
| Cochlear implants | Reduced signing |
| Mainstreaming | Away from deaf community |
| Dominant SL | Replaces local varieties |

#### Social Factors
| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Stigma | Sign devalued |
| Assimilation | Hearing culture pressure |
| Media | Dominant SL exposure |
| Urbanization | Rural communities decline |
