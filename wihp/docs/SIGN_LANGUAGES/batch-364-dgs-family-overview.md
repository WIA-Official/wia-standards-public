### Introduction
The German Sign Language family comprises sign languages descended from or significantly influenced by Deutsche Gebärdensprache (German Sign Language, DGS). This family developed primarily through German deaf education institutions and spread through migration and educational connections.

The family's most notable member outside Germany is Israeli Sign Language (ISL), which developed largely from German Sign Language brought by deaf Jewish refugees fleeing Nazi persecution in the 1930s.

### Israeli Sign Language Development
The unique history of ISL:
- German deaf Jews fled Nazi persecution
- Brought DGS to British Mandate Palestine
- Mixed with local signs and other influences
- Hebrew influence on vocabulary
- Developed as national Israeli language

### Oralism's Impact
Germany was significantly affected:
- Strong oralist tradition developed
- Sign language suppressed 1880-1980s
- Underground signing continued
- Late 20th century revival

### Israeli Sign Language Uniqueness
ISL distinctives:
- DGS foundation
- Hebrew influence
- Middle Eastern gestures incorporated
- Unique military vocabulary (IDF)
- Religious signs (Judaism)
- Immigration continued to bring new influences

### Polish Sign Language
PJM characteristics:
- Independent development primary
- Some DGS educational contact
- Socialist era isolation
- Post-1989 international contact
- Strong deaf community

### Shared Features
Across family members:
- Some shared vocabulary
- Similar grammatical structures
- European sign language features
- Classifier systems
- Non-manual markers

### Key Differences
What distinguishes members:
- National vocabulary
- Cultural adaptations
- Religious vocabulary (ISL - Jewish; DGS - Christian)
- Environmental signs
- Historical divergence

### Holocaust Impact
The German deaf community:
- Nazi persecution of deaf people
- T4 euthanasia program affected deaf
- Forced sterilization
- Community devastated
- Post-war slow recovery
- ISL carries refugee language heritage

### Contemporary Status
The family today:

- ISL strong and recognized
- Academic research expanding
- Media presence increasing
- International connections
