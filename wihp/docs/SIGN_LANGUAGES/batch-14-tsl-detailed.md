# WIA Talk Sign Language Mapping - TSL (臺灣手語 / Taiwan Sign Language)
## Batch 14: 20 Signs with Full WIA Talk Component Analysis

**Language:** TSL - 臺灣手語 (Táiwān Shǒuyǔ / Taiwan Sign Language)
**Region:** Taiwan (Republic of China)
**Estimated Users:** ~30,000 native signers; 124,938 hearing-impaired (2020)
**Language Family:** Japanese Sign Language (JSL) family
**Recognition:** National language status (2019 Development of National Languages Act)

---

## TSL Characteristics

### Historical Background
- **1895-1945:** Japanese colonial period introduced JSL
- **1915:** First deaf school established in Tainan
- Post-1945: ROC took over, CSL influence via mainland educators
- **1970s:** Serious linguistic research began
- **March 1-2, 2003:** First International Symposium on TSL Linguistics (National Chung Cheng University)
- **2019:** Included in Development of National Languages Act
- **2020:** Movie "The Silent Forest" (無聲) featured Taipei TSL dialect

### Language Family & Relationships
- **Parent:** Japanese Sign Language (JSL)
- **Siblings:** Korean Sign Language (KSL)
- **60% lexical similarity with JSL** - partial mutual intelligibility!
- **52-74% lexical similarity with JSL** depending on comparison method
- **30-58% lexical similarity with CSL** (contact influence, not genetic)
- **NOT related to Chinese Sign Language (CSL)** - different family!

### Key Features
- **S-O-V word order** (Subject-Object-Verb) - same as JSL, different from Mandarin (S-V-O)
- **Negation appears AFTER verb** (sentence-final position)
  - Example: "TODAY-I-SCHOOL-GO-NOT" = "I'm not going to school today"
- **Four phonological parameters:**
  1. Handshape (配置)
  2. Location (位置)
  3. Movement (動作)
  4. Palm orientation (方向)
- **Non-manual markers:** Brow raising/furrowing, head shake/nod, torso movement
- **Numeral incorporation:** Numbers can fuse with signs (e.g., 7-PEOPLE)

### Two Main Dialects
1. **Taipei dialect** (北部) - Northern
2. **Tainan dialect** (南部) - Southern
3. Taichung variant - similar to Tainan

### Handshape System
- Handshapes referenced by Chinese characters
- Example: "B/胡hu" handshape, "X/十shi" handshape
- PLEASE = B handshape at forehead
- HOPE = X handshape at forehead

### Distinct from Signed Chinese
- **TSL (自然手語):** Natural sign language with own grammar
- **Signed Chinese (文法手語 wénfǎ shǒuyǔ):** Manually coded Mandarin

### Organizations & Resources
- **National Chung Cheng University** - Sign Language Research Group
- **Taiwan Sign Language Online Dictionary** - 3,500+ signs (tsl.ccu.edu.tw)
- **Signing Village Workshop** - Founded 2021 by Dr. Fanghuai Ku (CODA)
- **National Association of the Deaf ROC**

### Challenges
- **Aging user base** - only 27% learned TSL as first language in childhood
- **Cochlear implants:** 775 infants identified with hearing impairment in 2019
- Limited transmission to younger generations

## WIA Talk 5-Component System Reference

| Code | Component | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| HS | Hand Shape | Configuration of fingers and palm |
| LC | Location | Where the sign is performed |
| MV | Movement | Direction and type of motion |
| OR | Orientation | Which way palm/fingers face |
| FE | Facial Expression | Required non-manual markers |

### 1. 你好 (Hello)

**TSL Concept:** Greeting - similar to JSL heritage

**Detailed Motion:**
- Wave hand in greeting gesture
- Or: Bow-like gesture from JSL influence
- Friendly facial expression
- Eye contact important

**WIA Talk Code:** `HS02-LC09-MV06-OR01-FE02`

| Component | Code | Description |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| HS | 02 | Flat open hand |
| LC | 09 | Neutral space |

| OR | 01 | Palm forward |
| FE | 02 | Friendly smile |

**Note:** TSL greeting shows JSL influence

## SECTION B: Numbers 1-10 (數字)

### TSL Number System Overview

TSL numbers share heritage with JSL:
- Numbers can incorporate into other signs (numeral incorporation)
- Example: 7-JIN (7 PEOPLE), 12-APPROACH-CAR
- Fused numbers possible: 17 = combination of 10 and 7
- Part of JSL family number system

**Source:** researchgate.net (Fischer & Gong 2010)

- **Non-manual markers** essential for questions
