### Language Description
FSM Sign Language refers to the sign language varieties used across the four states of the Federated States of Micronesia: Yap, Chuuk, Pohnpei, and Kosrae. Due to the nation's geographic spread across 600+ islands over 2,700 km of ocean, and distinct cultural identities of each state, sign language use varies considerably.

American Sign Language (ASL) serves as the primary educational model due to the US Compact relationship, but each state has developed local signs reflecting their unique cultures and languages. The degree of mutual intelligibility between state varieties is unknown.

### Key Characteristics
- **Four Distinct Cultures**: Each state has unique language/culture
- **ASL Foundation**: US relationship brings ASL model
- **Geographic Fragmentation**: Islands spread over vast ocean
- **State Autonomy**: Education managed at state level
- **Inter-State Variation**: Significant differences likely

### Deaf Community
The deaf community is fragmented by geography:
- **Weno, Chuuk**: Largest concentration
- **Kolonia, Pohnpei**: Capital region community
- **Colonia, Yap**: Traditional culture context
- **Tofol, Kosrae**: Smallest, most isolated
- **Outer Islands**: Deaf often never meet others

### Education
- **State-Level Programs**: Each state manages own education
- **US Support**: Special education funding through Compact
- **Teacher Training**: US-based training programs
- **Resources**: Varies significantly by state
- **COM-FSM**: College of Micronesia serves region

### Challenges
1. Extreme geographic fragmentation
2. Four distinct state systems
3. Limited inter-state coordination
4. No unified sign language standard
5. Outer island isolation severe
6. Brain drain to US territories/mainland
7. Limited resources across all states

### Research Needs
- Comparative study across four states
- Documentation of state-specific signs
- Outer island sign systems
- Historical sign language development
- Mutual intelligibility assessment

### Cultural Considerations
Each state has unique cultural signs for:
- **Yap**: Stone money, traditional navigation
- **Chuuk**: Lagoon life, navigational terms
- **Pohnpei**: Sakau ceremony, traditional titles
- **Kosrae**: Traditional foods, church culture
