# Batch 213: Hausa Sign Language (Maganar Hannu)

## Overview
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Local Name** | Maganar Hannu (Hand Talk) |
| **Location** | Kano State, Northern Nigeria |
| **Type** | Indigenous Urban Sign Language |
| **Community** | Hausa-speaking deaf |
| **Status** | ENDANGERED |

## Origins

### Independent Development
- Emerged within Hausa deaf community
- Indigenous to Kano city and region
- NOT derived from American Sign Language
- Developed through deaf community interaction

### Hausa Context
- Kano is major northern Nigerian city
- Hausa is lingua franca of Northern Nigeria
- Large urban population
- Historic trade center

## Linguistic Features

### Name Meaning
"Maganar Hannu" = "Talk of the Hand" or "Hand Talk"
- Maganar = talk, speech, language
- Hannu = hand

### Independence from Nigerian SL
- Nigerian Sign Language is ASL-based
- Maganar Hannu developed independently
- Different vocabulary
- Different grammatical structures

## Research

### Key Researcher
**Constanze Schmaling** (1997)
- First to document Maganar Hannu
- Identified as indigenous sign language
- Distinct from Nigerian SL

### Documentation Status
- Limited research
- Basic description exists
- No comprehensive grammar
- No full dictionary

## Current Situation

### Threats
1. Nigerian Sign Language dominance in schools
2. ASL influence through deaf education
3. Limited institutional support
4. No official recognition

### Nigerian Sign Language Pressure
- Schools use Nigerian SL (ASL-based)
- Andrew Foster schools spread ASL
- Younger deaf learn Nigerian SL
- Maganar Hannu declining

## Comparison

### Nigeria's Sign Languages
| Language | Origin | Status |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Nigerian SL | ASL-based (1960s) | Dominant |
| Maganar Hannu | Indigenous Kano | Endangered |
| Bura SL | Village, Borno | Critically Endangered |

### Other Indigenous Urban SL
Similar pattern seen in:
- Bamako SL (Mali) - urban tea circles
- Old Bangkok SL (Thailand) - urban deaf
- Pre-ASL local varieties worldwide
