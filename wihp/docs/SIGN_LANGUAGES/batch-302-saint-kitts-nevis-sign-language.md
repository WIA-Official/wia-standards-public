## Sign Language of the Federation of Saint Kitts and Nevis

### Language Situation
- American Sign Language (ASL) is primary sign language
- Wikipedia lists ASL among languages of Saint Kitts and Nevis
- Vital communication tool for deaf community
- Government-supported inclusive language policies

### ASL Background
- Connected to French Sign Language (LSF) origins
- Originated from American School for the Deaf (Hartford, 1817)
- Spread through deaf schools and organizations
- Features phonemic components: hand movements, torso, hands
- Grammar distinct from English

### Government Policies
- Inclusive language policies implemented
- Accessibility during government events
- Public budget readings include interpretation
- Digital accessibility initiatives
- Commitment to information access for deaf community

### Demographics
- Estimated deaf population: Small (specific numbers unavailable)
- Total population: ~54,961
- 75% African ancestry
- 12% Afro-European
- 5.3% mixed race
- >98% literacy rate

### Geographic Context
- Twin-island nation in Caribbean
- Part of Leeward Islands
- British colony until 1983 independence
- English colonial legacy
- West Indian inflection in accent

### Languages of Saint Kitts and Nevis
1. **English** (Official)
2. **Saint Kitts Creole** (~40,000 speakers)
3. **American Sign Language**

### Cultural Significance
- ASL bridges communicative gap
- Ensures hearing impairments not barrier
- Promotes community participation
- Part of inclusive language policy vision

### Regional Connections
- Part of Caribbean deaf community network
- Included in SIL International profiles
- SAGE Deaf Studies Encyclopedia coverage
- Caribbean Sign Language Centre services

### Challenges
- Limited educational access
- Scripture access difficulties
- Need for deaf missionaries
- Training programs needed
- Small deaf community

### Linguistic Heritage
- English from British colonial period
- Saint Kitts Creole: African, European, indigenous elements
- British spelling and vocabulary conventions
- Unique local linguistic tapestry
