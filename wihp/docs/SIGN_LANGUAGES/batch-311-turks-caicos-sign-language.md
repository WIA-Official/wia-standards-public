# Turks and Caicos Islands Sign Language
## Sign Language of the Turks and Caicos Islands

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Languages** | American Sign Language (ASL), British Sign Language (BSL) |
| **Country** | Turks and Caicos Islands |
| **Status** | British Overseas Territory |
| **Capital** | Grand Turk (Cockburn Town) |
| **Population** | ~32,000+ |

### Language Situation
- Both ASL and BSL reported in use
- Part of SIL 24-location Caribbean survey (2011)
- Part of SAGE Deaf Studies Encyclopedia coverage
- British territory with American influence
- Limited documentation on deaf community

### SIL Survey Report (2011)
- **Title**: Sociolinguistic Profiles of Twenty-four Deaf Communities in the Americas
- **Authors**: Parks, Elizabeth and Holly Williams
- Video summaries available in ASL
- Subject languages: ASL and BSL
- Part of Americas Area Sign Language Survey

### Geographic Context
- Archipelago of 40 low-lying coral islands
- Atlantic Ocean, southeast of Bahamas
- Main islands: Grand Turk, Providenciales (Provo)
- Area: 430 km²
- Historical Lucayan (Taino) inhabitants

### Official Language
- **English** - Official language
- Turks and Caicos Creole - Widely spoken (similar to Bahamian Creole)
- Haitian Creole - Immigrant community
- Spanish - Dominican immigrants

### Political Status
- British Overseas Territory
- Not a sovereign country
- British Overseas Territories Citizenship
- One of 14 British Overseas Territories
- Uses US Dollar as currency

### Deaf Community Challenges
- Limited educational access
- Scripture access difficulties
- Need for deaf missionaries and leaders
- Small island community
- Limited documentation available

### Regional Sign Language Context
Nine sign languages found in Caribbean region:
1. American Sign Language (ASL)
2. British Sign Language (BSL)
3. Danish Sign Language
4. Dutch Sign Language (NGT)
5. French Sign Language
6. Guyanese Sign Language
7. Kajana Sign Language
8. Quebec Sign Language
9. Surinamese Sign Language

### Caribbean Sign Language Education
- **UWI Mona** offers Caribbean Sign Language Interpreting BA
- Focus on Caribbean Sign Language fluency
- Understanding of Deaf culture
- Addresses critical interpreter shortage
- Caribbean Sign Languages mutually intelligible

### Cultural Diversity
- Over 60 nationalities represented
- Mix of Caribbean, European, other cultures
- Rich heritage of migration and tradition
- British overseas territory identity

### Tourism and Economy
- Top 10 diving destination in Caribbean
- Grace Bay Beach (Providenciales)
- Grand Turk Cruise Center
- Carnival Cruise Line visits
- Strong tourism industry

### Historical Context
- Lucayan (Taino) original inhabitants (~600 AD)
- European discovery late 1400s/early 1500s
- Possible Columbus 1492 landfall site
- British colonial history
