# Kurdish Sign Language / Zmani Hemay Kurdi (ZHK/KuSL)

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Kurdish Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Zmani Hemay Kurdi (زبانەی هەماي کوردی) |
| **Abbreviation** | ZHK, KuSL |
| **Glottolog Code** | kurd1260 |
| **Region** | Kurdistan Region, Iraq |
| **Users** | ~1,000 students in deaf schools |
| **Status** | ENDANGERED |

## Historical Development

### Origins (1982)
- First Kurdish school for deaf established in Sulaymaniyah (1982)
- First teachers apparently did not know Iraqi Sign Language
- Unclear if based on existing community sign language or created from home signs

### Development
- Three regional dialects: Sulaymaniyah, Erbil, Duhok
- Each associated with deaf schools in those cities
- 2023: Interpreter Training Pilot supported by US Embassy in Baghdad

## Linguistic Features

### Relationship to Other Languages
- Unintelligible with Iraqi Sign Language
- Lexical similarities with ISL (may be from 1990s influence or later)
- Baghdad Iraqi Sign is closest relative (Jaza 2015)

### Regional Variations
- Rich regional variations across Erbil, Sulaymaniyah, Duhok
- Three dialects associated with three schools

## Organizations and Education
- Kurdish deaf schools in Sulaymaniyah, Erbil, Duhok
- Education only until sixth grade
- AUIS offers Deaf Studies and KuSL courses
- Deaf Worlds, Inc - KuSL Project

## Key Figures
- Ms. Bahar Mahmood Mohammed - pioneer in sign language teaching
- Ms. Aveen Sleman - deaf Kurdish sign language instructor
- Mr. Zana Abbas - deaf classroom facilitator
- Ms. Shara Kamal - hard of hearing facilitator

## Challenges
- NOT officially recognized by Iraqi or KRG government
- Fewer than 5 interpreters for all of Kurdistan
- Deaf people isolated socially, economically, politically
- Many deaf Kurds use only 5% of hearing peoples language
- Language at risk of becoming endangered

### Barriers
- Limited educational support
- Insufficient resources
- Lack of official recognition
- Risk to younger generations

## Recent Developments
- KRG Ministry of Culture asked TV stations to provide SL interpretation
- AUIS offering KuSL courses since 2020s
- US Embassy supported interpreter training (2023)
