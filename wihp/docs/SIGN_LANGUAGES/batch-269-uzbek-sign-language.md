# Uzbek Sign Language (UzSL / USL)
## Oʻzbek imo-ishora tili / Узбекский жестовый язык

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Uzbek Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Oʻzbek imo-ishora tili |
| **ISO 639-3** | Not assigned |
| **Country** | Uzbekistan |
| **Users** | ~21,212 registered (est. 80,000-200,000 total) |
| **Status** | "Means of interpersonal communication" (not official) |
| **Language Family** | Dialect of Russian Sign Language |

### Historical Development
- **Soviet Era**: Uzbek Society of the Deaf established under USSR Ministry of Social Welfare
- **1991**: Society of the Deaf became non-profit after Soviet collapse
- **Post-Soviet**: Many deaf lost jobs and homes due to funding cuts
- **2022**: Presidential decree on development of UzSL and Braille
- **2023**: Sign language education introduced in schools
- **2024**: Machine learning research for UzSL recognition began

### Presidential Decree (2022)
- Special training courses in general secondary schools
- "Ishga Merhamat" monocenters offer free training
- 5% of TV broadcast time with sign language/subtitles
- From 2024/2025: Teachers required to know sign language
- Annual "Best Sign Language Interpreter" contest established

### Linguistic Features
- Dialect of Russian Sign Language with local variations
- Many unique phrases different from standard RSL
- Written form developed for educational purposes
- Fingerspelling based on Cyrillic alphabet
- Active research on CNN/RNN recognition systems

### Education Challenges
- Only 3-4 colleges adapted for sign language instruction
- Limited to crafts and service professions
- Most specialized school teachers lack sign language training
- Students learn lip-reading and articulation instead
- Graduates finish one year later than hearing students
