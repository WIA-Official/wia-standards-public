# WIA Talk Sign Language Mapping - NGT (Nederlandse Gebarentaal / Dutch Sign Language)
## Batch 19: 20 Signs with Full WIA Talk Component Analysis

**Language:** NGT - Nederlandse Gebarentaal (Dutch Sign Language)
**Also Known As:** Sign Language of the Netherlands (SLN)
**Region:** Netherlands, also Suriname, former Netherlands Antilles
**Estimated Users:** 60,000 (10,000 early onset deaf signers)
**Language Family:** Old French Sign Language family
**Recognition:** Official recognition October 13, 2020; Law effective March 16, 2021

---

## NGT Characteristics

### Historical Background
- **Mid-18th century:** Origins from Old French Sign Language (VLSF)
- **Abbé Charles-Michel de l'Épée** learned Parisian deaf community's signs
- **1790:** First school for the deaf established
- **1840:** 's-Hertogenbosch school
- **1853:** Rotterdam school  
- **1888:** The Hague school
- **1911:** Amsterdam school
- **Until 1915:** Sign language used in education
- **1915-1980:** NGT FORBIDDEN in all deaf schools (Milan Resolution 1880)
- **1980s:** "Total Communication" period began
- **2009:** Van Dale Dictionary published (3,000 signs)
- **October 13, 2020:** Official recognition
- **March 16, 2021:** Law came into force

### Language Family
- Common ancestor: **Old French Sign Language** (late 18th century)
- Related to **Flemish Sign Language (VGT)** but NOT mutually intelligible!
- 200 years of divergence between NGT and VGT
- Also used in **Suriname** and former Netherlands Antilles

### Two Systems in Netherlands
1. **NGT (Nederlandse Gebarentaal):** Natural sign language with own grammar
2. **NmG (Nederlands met Gebaren / Signed Dutch):** Uses Dutch grammar with signs

### Key Linguistic Features
- **Topic-prominent language** - sentences start with topic
- **Topic agreement** - clause-final anaphoric pronouns
- **No case marking**
- **Two derivational negative affixes** - one prefix, one suffix (unique!)
- **Classifier predicates** - complex predicates
- **Non-manual grammatical markers** - for questions, negations, topicalizations

### Organizations & Resources
- **Dutch Sign Centre** - National center for NGT
- **Van Dale Basiswoordenboek** - First national dictionary (2009)
- **Corpus NGT** - Large online video corpus (2008)
- **HU University of Applied Sciences** - Interpreter training
- **640 interpreters** registered (July 2020)
- **SIGN-HUB** - Digital grammar platform

### Five Historical Schools
1. Groningen (first, 1790)
2. 's-Hertogenbosch (1840)
3. Rotterdam (1853)
4. The Hague (1888)
5. Amsterdam (1911)

## SECTION C: Basic Phrases (4 signs)

### 17. IK HOU VAN JOU (I Love You)

**NGT Concept:** Love expression

**Detailed Motion:**
Two options in Netherlands:
1. Traditional: IK (point self) + HOUDEN-VAN (love sign) + JOU (point to person)
2. ILY handshape (borrowed from ASL - popular internationally)

**Traditional HOUDEN-VAN:**
- Cross arms over heart area
- Loving expression

**WIA Talk Code (Traditional):** `HS02-LC07-MV26-OR05-FE01`

| Component | Code | Description |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| HS | 02 | Flat hands |
| LC | 07 | Heart/chest area |
| MV | 26 | Cross/embrace motion |
| OR | 05 | Palms toward body |
| FE | 01 | Loving expression |

**ILY Handshape (International):** `HS55-LC09-MV00-OR01-FE01`
- Combines I + L + Y handshapes from ASL

### 18. HOE HEET JE? (What is your name?)

**NGT Concept:** Name question

**Detailed Motion:**
- Name sign combined with question
- Non-manual question markers essential
- Furrowed eyebrows (whq marker)

| Component | Code | Description |

| HS | 04 | Two fingers |

### Unique NGT Features
- **Topic-prominent language** with topic agreement
- **Two derivational negative affixes** (unique among sign languages!)
- **Furrowed eyebrows** = canonical WH-question marker
- **NOT mutually intelligible** with Flemish Sign Language (VGT) despite shared ancestor
- **1915-1980:** 65 years of prohibition in schools!
- **Van Dale Dictionary (2009):** 3,000 standard signs
- **640 registered interpreters** (2020)

### Alphabet & Manual System
- Based on Old French Sign Language alphabet
- Different from VGT (Flemish) system
