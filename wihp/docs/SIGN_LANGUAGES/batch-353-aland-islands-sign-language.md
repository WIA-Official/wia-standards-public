### Language Description
The Åland Islands, an autonomous Swedish-speaking region of Finland in the Baltic Sea, have a tiny deaf population that uses either Finnish Sign Language (Suomalainen viittomakieli, FinSL) or Swedish Sign Language (Svenskt teckenspråk, STS), depending on educational background and personal connections.

With a population of approximately 30,000 and Swedish as the sole official language, Åland represents a unique linguistic situation. The deaf community is too small to have developed a distinct sign language, but the question of which sign language to use—Finnish or Swedish—reflects the islands' complex identity.

- **Swedish-Speaking**: Only Swedish official in Åland
- **Finnish Connection**: Part of Finland politically
- **Dual Influence**: Both FinSL and STS used
- **Tiny Community**: ~30-50 deaf individuals
- **Identity Question**: Which sign language represents Åland?

### Deaf Community
The Åland deaf community:
- Extremely small population
- May be split between FinSL and STS users
- Individual choice often determines language
- Historical education location matters
- Intermarriage with Finnish or Swedish deaf

### Education
- **Åland Schools**: Limited special education
- **Finnish Options**: Deaf schools in Finland (FinSL)
- **Swedish Options**: Deaf schools in Sweden (STS)
- **Historical Pattern**: Varied by era and individual
- **Current**: Inclusive education trend

### Legal Status
- Finland recognizes FinSL constitutionally (2015)
- Sweden recognizes STS
- Åland autonomous government has no specific SL policy
- Both languages potentially accessible

### Challenges
1. Too small for any deaf infrastructure
2. Split between two sign languages
3. Interpreters not locally available
4. Young deaf often relocate
5. No documentation of local signing
6. Identity question unresolved

### Cultural Identity
Ålanders have strong local identity:
- Swedish-speaking but not Swedish
- Finnish citizens but culturally distinct
- Demilitarized status (1856)
- Self-governance tradition
- Deaf identity within this context complex
