### Legal Recognition (2022)
- Disability rights legislation amended to include sign language
- Recognized through amendments to disability rights law
- Part of broader accessibility improvements
- World Federation of the Deaf acknowledged recognition

### Historical Development
- **Soviet Era**: Russian Sign Language introduced through Soviet institutions
- **Post-Soviet**: Continued use of RSL variant
- **2022**: Official recognition achieved
- Limited documentation of distinct Belarusian features

### Linguistic Context
- Close relationship with Russian Sign Language
- Belarusian spoken language historically marginalized
- Only ~11.9% of population actively uses spoken Belarusian
- Sign language community similarly influenced by Russian

### Current Situation
- Belarus maintains two official spoken languages (Belarusian/Russian)
- Sign language community reflects this bilingual context
- Limited research on Belarusian-specific sign variants
- Recognition represents significant step for deaf rights

### Challenges
- Limited documentation and standardization
- Russian Sign Language dominance in region
- Fewer resources than neighboring countries
- Need for interpreter training programs

### Cultural Notes
- Deaf community part of broader Eastern European deaf network
- Shared history with Soviet-era deaf education
- Growing international connections post-recognition
