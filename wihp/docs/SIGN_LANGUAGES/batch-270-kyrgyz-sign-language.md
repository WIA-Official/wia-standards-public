# Kyrgyz Sign Language
## Кыргыз жаңылыгы тили

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Kyrgyz Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Кыргыз ым-ишара тили |
| **ISO 639-3** | Not assigned |
| **Country** | Kyrgyzstan |
| **Users** | ~9,507 registered (Society of Deaf and Blind, 2017) |
| **Status** | Not officially recognized |
| **Language Family** | Russian Sign Language variant |

### Historical Development
- **Soviet Era**: Russian Sign Language introduced through Soviet institutions
- **1989**: Kyrgyz language acquired official status in spoken form
- **Post-Soviet**: Sign language classes conducted via bilingual method
- **Current**: Translation from Russian gestures into Kyrgyz at National Deaf School

### Linguistic Features
- Based on Russian Sign Language
- Bilingual education method (Russian SL to Kyrgyz)
- Limited research on indigenous sign variations
- Classes taught through gesture translation
- Significant similarities with other CIS sign languages

### Research Status
- Sign Language Issues identified as unstudied topic
- National Deaf School serves as primary research site
- 2018 CESS Conference paper on SL issues
- No comprehensive linguistic documentation yet
- Need identified for comparison with spoken Kyrgyz

### Education System
- National Deaf School (primary institution)
- Bilingual teaching methodology
- Russian Sign Language as instruction medium
- Kyrgyz translation provided alongside
- Society of Deaf and Blind oversees services

### Challenges
- Limited academic research
- No standardized Kyrgyz-specific sign system
- Reliance on Russian Sign Language
- Lack of trained interpreters
- Minimal government recognition

### Organizations
- Society of Deaf and Blind in Kyrgyz Republic
- National Deaf School
- Ministry of Education (limited involvement)
