## Fiji Sign Language (FJSL)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Native Name** | Fiji Sign Language |
| **Country** | Fiji |
| **Status** | Developing, undocumented linguistically |
| **Recognition** | Recognized via CRPD ratification (2017) |

### Language Features
- Own word order and structure (distinct from English)
- Noun order flexible (beginning, middle, or end of sentence)
- Strong English influence in schools
- Many variations due to late language acquisition
- Currently being researched by USP graduates

### Key Organizations
- **Fiji Association of the Deaf (FAD)**: Founded 2002
- **Fiji Disabled Peoples Federation (FDPF)**: Founded late 1970s
- **Gospel School for the Deaf (GSD)**: Education for deaf children
- **Hilton Special School**: Deaf education

### Research
- Inise Tawaketini (hearing linguist, MA from Chinese University of Hong Kong)
- Krishneer Sen (deaf researcher, MA from CUHK)
- Research presented at USP (August 2023)

### Bible Translation
- Fiji Sign Language Bible team active
- Mark completed (2019, 12-step video process)
- Partnership with Wycliffe Associates

### Advocacy
- International Day of Sign Languages celebrated annually
- TV news sign language interpretation (since April 2020, COVID-19)
- Fiji Access to Justice Project (EU/UNDP funded)

## Rennellese Sign Language (Extinct)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **ISO 639-3** | [rsi] (retired 2017) |
| **Country** | Solomon Islands |
| **Location** | Rennell Island (Mugaba) |
| **Status** | Extinct |
| **Type** | Home sign (not community language) |

### History
- **Created**: ~1915 by Kagobai (deaf-mute)
- **Documented**: 1974 by Rolf Kuschel
- **Died with**: Kagobai (only deaf person on island)
- First deaf person in islands oral tradition (24 generations)

### Research
- Rolf Kuschel: "A Lexicon of Signs from a Polynesian Outlier Island" (1974)
- 217 signs documented
- Important study of isolated deaf individual sign creation

### Linguistic Significance
- Created without contact with any other sign language
- Mix of universal gestures and culturally-specific signs
- Shows human capacity for linguistic invention

## Regional Challenges

### Common Issues Across Pacific
1. **No early language access**: Most deaf children miss critical acquisition period
2. **Family signing limited**: Hearing families know little/no sign
3. **English influence**: Sign often follows English word order
4. **Interpreter shortage**: Very few trained interpreters
5. **Employment discrimination**: Lower wages, limited opportunities
6. **Access to services**: Justice, health, education barriers
7. **Domestic violence**: Deaf women face compound challenges

### Organizations Working in Region
- **Pacific Disability Forum (PDF)**: Regional umbrella
- **World Federation of the Deaf (WFD)**: International support
- **UNDP Pacific Office**: Development programs
- **European Union**: Funding (Access to Justice)
- **Wycliffe Associates**: Bible translation

### Research Needs
- No comprehensive documentation of Pacific sign languages
- Unknown genetic relationships between languages
- Hawaii SL research provides foundation for future work
- Urgent need for indigenous sign language documentation before ASL/Auslan dominance

## Pacific Sign Language Research Priority

James Woodward (2014) identified undocumented Pacific sign languages:
- Western Samoa
- Fiji
- Vanuatu
- Rennell Island (now documented as extinct)
- Many others unknown

### Threat Assessment
- ASL and Auslan encroachment
- Limited institutional support
- Lack of research funding
- No comprehensive survey conducted
