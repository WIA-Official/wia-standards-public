# Guyanese Sign Language (GSL)
## Sign Language of Guyana

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Guyanese Sign Language |
| **Alternate Name** | GSL, Guyana Sign Language |
| **ISO 639-3** | gys |
| **Glottolog** | guya1253 |
| **Country** | Guyana |
| **Status** | ASL-influenced, possibly distinct |

### Language Situation
- GSL recognized as valid sign language in its own right
- ASL used for education in Guyana
- May be local name for ASL variety
- Indigenous sign language exists in rural areas
- DAG advocates for GSL recognition

### Indigenous Sign Language Discovery
- **South Rupununi region**: Indigenous sign language documented
- Used for several generations
- Used among deaf people and with hearing community
- Contact between deaf people in different villages
- Research for documentation and preservation

### Research Project (2017)
- Culturally specific materials developed
- Core vocabulary elicitation
- Lexical variation study across region
- Naturalistic conversation recordings:
  - Deaf-to-deaf signing
  - Deaf-to-hearing signing
  - Deaf-to-researcher signing
- Initial structural sketch developed

### Educational Initiatives
- Train the ASL Trainer workshop

### Challenges Identified
- Shortage of trained teachers
- Lack of special curriculum
- Misconception that deafness disables
- Low school enrollment for deaf
- Healthcare communication barriers
- Employment difficulties
- Driving rights issues

### Documentary: Silent Voices
- Human Rights Film Festival 2017
- Premiered October 3, 2017
- Brazilian Embassy and GEF collaboration
- Addresses medical access, education, employment
- Features deaf community members

### Geographic Context
- Only South American country with English official
- Capital: Georgetown
- Languages: English (official), Guyanese Creole
- Amerindian languages also spoken
- Independence from England: 1966

### ASL Influence History
- ASL brought by Christian missionaries
- Peace Corps volunteers
- Educational textbooks
- Spread through 20th century
- Concern about linguistic imperialism

### Cultural Significance
