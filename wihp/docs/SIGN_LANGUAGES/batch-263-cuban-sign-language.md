## HISTORIC MILESTONE 2024
- **September 17, 2024**: Council of State approved Decree Law On Cuban Sign Language
- LSC officially recognized as the official language of Deaf Community in Cuba
- Guarantees non-discriminatory access to information
- Promotes bilingual education
- Protects deaf cultural identity

## Historical Development

### Early History (Late 1800s-1900s)
- Late 1800s: First school for the deaf (Havana)
- Early 1900s: Oralist teaching methods dominant
- Alfabeto Manual Antiguo (old manual alphabet) emerged
- Manual alphabet rejected due to oralist policies

### Revival Period (1970s-1990s)
- 1977: Manual alphabet returned to deaf schools
- 1978: ANSOC (National Association of Deaf) founded
- 1990s: LSC recognized in deaf school curricula
- 1994: LSC introduced as pedagogical alternative

### Modern Era (2000s-2024)
- 2004: Modelo Cubano de Educación Bilingüe launched
- 2008: CENDSOR created (National Center for Improvement)
- 2024: LSC becomes official national language

  - Trains interpreters and instructors

## Education System

### Schools
- 17 schools for the deaf
- One per province
- Three in Havana
- Bilingual approach increasingly popular

### Educational Approaches
1. **Bimodal**: Sign and Spanish simultaneously
2. **Bilingual**: LSC as L1, Spanish as L2

### Modelo Cubano de Educación Bilingüe (2004)
- Experimental bilingual project
- Three pilot schools
- LSC as first language
- Spanish (especially written) as second language

## Linguistic Features

### Manual Alphabet
- Dactilema - current fingerspelling system
- Two-handed with facial articulation (historical)
- Used to teach reading/writing
- Used for words without signs

### Grammar
- Personal pronouns through movement and position
- inform moves toward/away from referents
- Tense through space and movement:
  - Present: Near signers front
  - Past: Toward signers back
  - Future: In front of signer

### Influences
- French Sign Language
- Spanish
- Indigenous signs
- Distinct from Spanish Sign Language

## Cultural Identity
- Strong Deaf community culture
- Shared language, history, struggles
- Identity and solidarity
- Social and expressive culture
- Applause with raised hands
- Toasting with finger touch (not cups)

## Media Access
- **Noticiero ANSOC** - Only LSC news program in Cuba
- Provides deaf awareness to hearing viewers
- Sign language interpreters on national broadcasts

## Health System
- Early infant screening for hearing loss
- Risk factor assessment
- Professional team assignment:
  - Language pathologists
  - Neurophysicians
  - Audiology technicians
  - Psychologists
- Hearing aids: 35 Cuban pesos (subsidized if needed)
- Bilateral hearing loss >35 HL: Amplification provided

## Technology
- First bilingual digital dictionary (Las Tunas)
- Multimedia sign language tools
- Created by special school teachers

## Government Recognition
- Constitutional protection
- Decree Law guarantees:
  - Broad access to information
  - Bilingual education
  - Full inclusion in society
  - Deaf cultural identity values

## Cultural Significance
- Native language of deaf community
- Part of Cuban revolutionary humanistic tradition
- Symbol of inclusion and integration
- Distinct from Spanish Sign Language
