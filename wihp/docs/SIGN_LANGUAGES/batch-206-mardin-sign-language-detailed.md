# Batch 206: Mardin Sign Language (MarSL)

## Overview
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **ISO 639-3** | dsz |
| **Location** | Mardin, Southeastern Turkey |
| **Type** | Family Sign Language |
| **Community** | Extended "Dilsiz" (mute) family |
| **Status** | ENDANGERED |

## Unique Characteristics

### Family-Based Sign Language
Unlike village sign languages with community-wide deafness:
- Centered on ONE extended family
- "Dilsiz" family = "mute family" in Turkish
- Several hundred family members over generations
- Deaf members distributed across family branches

### Geographic Spread
- Originally concentrated in Mardin compound
- Children moved out as adults
- Established homes elsewhere in Mardin region
- Language spread with family dispersal

## Linguistic Status

### Relationship to TİD
Turkish Sign Language (TİD) is dominant in region:
- MarSL users aware of TİD
- Attitude: "TİD is used everywhere"
- MarSL seen as local, less important
- Language shift pressure exists

### Independence
- Emerged independently within family
- Different from Turkish Sign Language
- Unique vocabulary and structures
- Family-internal conventions

## Community Attitudes

### Initial Skepticism
When researcher arrived:
- "What is this research?"
- "Why is it important?"
- "TİD is used everywhere anyway"

### Growing Interest
After engagement:
- Family became enthusiastic
- Want materials widely disseminated
- Proud of their unique language
- Cooperative with documentation

### Family Consultant
- One family member particularly helpful
- Good command of MarSL
- Contacts other relatives for verification
- Helps with data analysis

## Challenges

### Threats
1. Turkish Sign Language dominance
2. Family dispersal across region
3. Younger generation TİD exposure
4. No formal recognition
5. Limited documentation

### Research Challenges
- Explaining research concept to community
- Building trust as deaf researcher
- Communication across sign languages
- Family politics and conflicts
