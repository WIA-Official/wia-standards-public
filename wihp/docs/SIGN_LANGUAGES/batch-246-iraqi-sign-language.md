# Iraqi Sign Language / Lughat al-Ishara al-Iraqiya (ISL)

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Iraqi Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Iraqiya (لغة الإشارة العراقية) |
| **Glottolog Code** | iraq1246 |
| **Region** | Iraq (Baghdad and other cities) |
| **Language Family** | Arab sign-language family |
| **Status** | Vulnerable, threatened |

## Historical Development

### Early Development
- Developed in early 20th century (pathway unclear)
- Primarily used by deaf community in Iraq
- Used in 7 schools or deaf associations in Baghdad and 5 other cities

### Modern Era
- 2010: Iraq ratified UN Convention on Rights of Persons with Disabilities
- Not officially recognized as national language
- Efforts to support ISL increasing

## Linguistic Features

### Relationship to Other Sign Languages
- Close to Levantine Arabic Sign Language
- Over 50% lexical similarity with Jordanian and Syrian SL
- Part of Arab sign-language family (Hendriks and Zeshan 2009)

### Characteristics
- Distinct from Kurdish Sign Language
- Used in deaf schools and some educational institutions
- Limited usage due to lack of national support

## Organizations and Education
- Used in deaf schools across Iraq
- Deaf community organizations in Baghdad
- Sharif Farhan - established Falluja charity for deaf

## Challenges
- No official recognition by government
- Quality of deaf education poor
- Limited early intervention services
- Very few interpreters available
- Warfare-related hearing damage (Battle of Mosul)

## Key Figures
- Sharif Farhan - deaf activist, charity founder
- Intisar Abdulhamid - 30 years helping deaf community
