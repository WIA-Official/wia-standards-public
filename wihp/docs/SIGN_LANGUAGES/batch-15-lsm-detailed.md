# WIA Talk Sign Language Mapping - LSM (Lengua de Señas Mexicana / Mexican Sign Language)
## Batch 15: 20 Signs with Full WIA Talk Component Analysis

**Language:** LSM - Lengua de Señas Mexicana (Mexican Sign Language)
**Region:** Mexico (Mexico City, Guadalajara, Monterrey + US border areas)
**Estimated Users:** 49,000-300,000 native signers; 800,000+ deaf/hard-of-hearing
**Language Family:** French Sign Language (LSF) family - derived from Old French Sign Language (OFSL)
**Recognition:** Official national language since 2005 (Ley General de las Personas con Discapacidad)

---

## LSM Characteristics

### Historical Background
- **1869:** Escuela Nacional de Sordomudos (ENS) established in Mexico City
- Deaf French educator **Ernest Huet** introduced OFSL methods
- Pre-existing home signs + OFSL → LSM formation
- **1880:** Milan Congress → global oralism shift, but LSM persisted
- **Mid-1980s:** First TV signed news segment (5 minutes)
- **1992:** Signed TV discontinued
- **1997:** 2-minute signed news summary resumed
- **2005:** LSM declared **official national language** alongside Spanish and indigenous languages

### Language Family & Relationships
- **Parent:** Old French Sign Language (OFSL)
- **Siblings:** American Sign Language (ASL), French Sign Language (LSF)
- **Mutually unintelligible** with ASL despite shared origin!
- **Manual alphabet almost identical** to American alphabet
- **80-90% lexical similarity across Mexico**
- **25% lexical overlap with ASL** in some corpora

### Key Features
- **5 phonological parameters:**
  1. Handshape (configuración de mano)
  2. Location (ubicación)
  3. Movement (movimiento)
  4. Palm orientation (orientación)
  5. Facial expressions (expresiones faciales) - crucial for grammar!
- **37% initialized signs** (vs 12-14% in ASL) - from Spanish letter initials!
- **S-V-O word order** typical (subjects precede verbs)
- **Little use of verb "to be"**
- **Spatial grammar:** Distance from body = significance level
- **Regional variations:** Age and gender affect numeral system!

### Two Systems
1. **LSM (Natural):** Own grammar, syntax independent of Spanish
2. **Signed Spanish (Español Señado):** LSM lexicon + Spanish word order
   - Uses suffixes like -dor, -ción
   - Articles and pronouns fingerspelled
   - Used by interpreters, late-deafened

### Other Mexican Sign Languages
- **Yucatec Maya Sign Language (LSMY)** - indigenous community
- **Albarradas Sign Language** - indigenous community
- **Chatino emerging sign language** (Oaxaca)
- **Tzotzil Maya sign language** (Chiapas)

### Organizations & Resources
- **DIELSEME (2004):** Diccionario Español-Lengua de Señas Mexicana
- **Diccionario de LSM:** 1,113 words in 15 categories
- **GDLSM:** Glosario Digital (INDISCAPACIDAD CDMX)
- **Federación Mexicana de Sordos**
- **SIL Mexico** - research and documentation

### Challenges
- **Only 42 certified interpreters** for 300,000-500,000 signers!
- ~200 with partial training, ~150 informal interpreters
- Limited rural access
- Shortage of LSM-fluent teachers
- Debate over initialized signs (language autonomy)

## WIA Talk 5-Component System Reference

| Code | Component | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| HS | Hand Shape | Configuration of fingers and palm |
| LC | Location | Where the sign is performed |
| MV | Movement | Direction and type of motion |
| OR | Orientation | Which way palm/fingers face |
| FE | Facial Expression | Required non-manual markers |

### Unique LSM Features
- **37% initialized signs** - highest rate among sign languages!
- **Number variations** by age, gender, region
- **Spatial significance** - distance from body matters
- **Signed Spanish (Español Señado)** exists as separate system
- **Numbers transform to C** for hundreds

### Related Sign Languages
- **LSF** - French Sign Language (ancestor)
- **ASL** - American Sign Language (cousin from OFSL)
- **LSMY** - Yucatec Maya Sign Language (indigenous)
