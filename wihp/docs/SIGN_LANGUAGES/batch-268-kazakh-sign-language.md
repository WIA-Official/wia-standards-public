# Kazakh Sign Language (KSL / K-RSL)
## Қазақ ым-ишара тілі / Казахско-русский жестовый язык

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Kazakh Sign Language (KSL) / Kazakh-Russian Sign Language (K-RSL) |
| **Native Name** | Қазақ ым-ишара тілі (Qazaq ym-ishara tili) |
| **ISO 639-3** | Not assigned (related to RSL) |

### Historical Development
- **Soviet Era**: Sign language introduced through Soviet centralized language policy
- **1940s-1990s**: Russian Sign Language (RSL) established as base
- **Post-Independence**: K-RSL developed local vocabulary and expressions
- **2015**: Kazakh-Russian Sign Language Corpus project initiated
- **2025**: Government announced plans for official recognition

### Linguistic Features
- Based on Russian Sign Language with significant local adaptations
- High mutual intelligibility with RSL and other CIS sign languages
- Manual alphabet originally based on Russian fingerspelling system
- Local vocabulary influenced by Kazakh culture and terminology
- Government provides 60 hours/year of free interpreting services

### Current Status
- **2025 Development**: Deputy PM confirmed plans for official recognition
- **e-Ymdau**: Interactive video dictionary used in special schools
- **Kostanay Dictionary**: First electronic KSL dictionary developed
- **17 Universities**: Train teachers of the deaf
- **SignBridge Project**: AI-based translation system in development

### Education and Resources
- Specialized boarding schools for deaf children
- Dactyl alphabet taught alongside sign language
- Handbooks and study guides in development
- Research ongoing at Nazarbayev University
- Gumilyov Eurasian National University AI research
