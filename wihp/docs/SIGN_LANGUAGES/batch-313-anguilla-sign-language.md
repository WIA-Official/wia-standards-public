# Anguilla Sign Language
## Sign Language of Anguilla

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | Likely ASL/BSL influenced |
| **Country** | Anguilla |
| **Status** | British Overseas Territory |
| **Capital** | The Valley |
| **Population** | ~15,753 (2021) |

### Language Situation
- Part of SIL 24-location Caribbean survey
- British territory with likely ASL/BSL influence
- Limited documentation on deaf community
- Very small island population
- Part of Leeward Caribbean sign language sphere

### Geographic Context
- Most northerly of Leeward Islands
- East of Puerto Rico and Virgin Islands
- North of Saint Martin
- 16 miles (26 km) long
- 3 miles (5 km) wide at widest
- Name means eel (Italian: anguilla) due to shape

### Official Language
- **English** (British-influenced variety) - Official
- **Anguillan Creole** - Widely spoken (~10,000 speakers)
  - Also called Anguilla Talk or dialek
  - Variety of Antiguan and Barbudan Creole
  - English-lexifier Creole
  - Not official status

### Other Languages
- Spanish (immigrant communities)
- Chinese (immigrant communities)
- Other immigrant languages

### Political History
- British Overseas Territory
- 1967 secession from Saint Kitts and Nevis
- Republic of Anguilla briefly declared
- British authority restored 1969
- Separate British Crown colony since 1980
- Anguilla Day: May 30

### Regional Sign Language Context
Nine sign languages found in Caribbean region:
1. American Sign Language (ASL)
2. British Sign Language (BSL)
3. Danish Sign Language
4. Dutch Sign Language (NGT)
5. French Sign Language
6. Guyanese Sign Language
7. Kajana Sign Language
8. Quebec Sign Language
9. Surinamese Sign Language

### Demographics
- 90.08% West African ancestry
- Descendants of enslaved people
- Native Arawak name: Malliouhana
- High literacy rate (>90%)

### Education
- Free and compulsory (ages 5-17)
- Government-run schools
- University of West Indies distance learning
- Some students receive full scholarships

### Economy
- Tourism major industry
- Offshore financing
- Tax haven status
- 33 beaches
- No bus service (car/taxi required)

### Cultural Features
- Summer Festival (August)
- Fishing traditional livelihood
- Shipbuilding tradition
- Eastern Caribbean Dollar (XCD)
- US Dollar also accepted

### Local Expressions
- Booloonjee = eggplant
- Boot-up = to collide
- Creole rules distinct from Standard English
