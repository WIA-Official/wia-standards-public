### Introduction
Sign language legal recognition refers to official acknowledgment of sign languages in national laws, constitutions, or policies. Recognition ranges from constitutional status to limited mention in disability laws. Full recognition treats sign languages as legitimate languages with rights comparable to spoken languages.

### Types of Recognition
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Constitutional | Highest level, in constitution |
| Official language | Equal to spoken official |
| Legal recognition | Specific law recognizes |
| Educational | Right to use in schools |

| None | No legal status |
