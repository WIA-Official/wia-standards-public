# Batch 200: Yolngu Sign Language (YSL)

## Overview
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Other Names** | Penguin Sign Language, Murngin Sign Language |
| **Native Term** | Gonu djama (hand work), Actions |
| **Region** | Northeast Arnhem Land, Northern Territory |
| **Area** | 40,000 square kilometers |
| **Type** | Alternate AND Primary Sign Language |
| **Status** | Endangered |

## Unique Dual Function

YSL is BOTH:
1. **Alternate sign language** - for hearing Yolngu
2. **Primary sign language** - for deaf Yolngu

This dual role makes it special among Australian Indigenous sign languages.

## NOT Just Mourning-Based

Unlike Central Desert sign languages:
- NOT primarily connected to speech taboos
- NOT used during extended mourning periods

### Primary Uses
1. Communication at distance (fishing, hunting)
2. Communication with deaf/hard of hearing Yolngu
3. Ceremonies requiring silence
4. Avoidance relationships
5. Secret/private exchanges

## Cultural Significance

### Ancestral Heritage
- Rich connections to dance, ritual, kinship
- Reflects intimate relationship with natural world
- Embodies Yolngu view of universe
- Pre-existing ancestral geography encoded

### Modern Evolution
- Continues adapting to contemporary life
- Full ceremonial participation by deaf Yolngu
- Part of bimodal-bilingualism tradition

## Michael Ganambarr - Example

Famous deaf Yolngu artist:
- Born deaf
- Uses YSL as primary language
- Renowned painter
- Talented dancer
- Full ceremonial and social participant

Demonstrates deaf inclusion in Yolngu society.

## Linguistic Features

### Yolngu Matha Context
- Yolngu Matha = collective term for spoken languages
- Pama-Nyungan family group
- Multiple clan languages exist

### Sign Language Characteristics
- Not signed version of any spoken Yolngu language
- Independent communication system
- Own grammatical structures
- Articulations on lower body (unusual globally)

### Endangered Status
- Intergenerational transmission declining
- Modern lifestyle reducing traditional contexts
- 70 signs from 1929 still in use today
- Urgent preservation needed

## Yolngu Society Context

### Population
- Yolngu speakers: approximately 6,000
- Multiple clan groups
- Strong cultural continuity

### Ceremonial Life
YSL used in:
- Ritual contexts
- Religious ceremonies
- Sacred site approaches
- Avoidance/poison kin interactions

## Threats

1. **Modernization** - lifestyle changes
2. **Education** - Auslan often taught instead
3. **Urbanization** - movement to cities
4. **Technology** - reduces traditional contexts
5. **Intergenerational gap** - youth less fluent

## Preservation Efforts

### Handbook Project
- Teaching materials for children

### Philosophy
"As each indigenous language disappears all our worlds are diminished."
