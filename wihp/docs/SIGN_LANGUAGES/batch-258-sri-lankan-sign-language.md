## Important Note
There is no single fully developed common sign language. Multiple regional varieties exist across 25+ deaf schools. Relationship between varieties unknown.

## Linguistic Classification
- Language isolate (prototype sign language)
- Developed through stimulus diffusion from existing sign language
- Related to British Sign Language family
- Connected to Australian, New Zealand, Indian sign languages

## Historical Development

### Dictionary Publications
- 2007: An Introduction to Sri Lankan Sign Language
  - First conversational sign language dictionary
  - 350+ signs documented
  - Published locally in Sinhala and English
  - Created by volunteers and Rohana Special School staff
- 2008: Sri Lanka Sign Dictionary (Volume I)
  - Created by deaf Sri Lankans
  - Central Federation of the Deaf collaboration
- Volume II: Subsequently published
- Volume III: In development (NLF project)

## Linguistic Features

### Iconicity
- Many signs can be guessed or mimed
- Signs for money, time, book, phone recognizable
- Action verbs: write, run, swim, drive - intuitive
- Local Sri Lankan body language incorporated

### Regional Variation
- Different varieties evolved in different parts of island
- Separate deaf communities developed distinct signs
- Dialects collected and archived in SignBank

## Challenges

### Current Issues
- No fully developed common sign language
- Severe shortage of interpreters
- Few resources for teachers
- Lack of SLSL vocabulary
- Public ignorance about deaf community

### Social Marginalization
- DHH community under-supported
- Underutilization as productive workforce
- Limited access to education
- Barriers in government buildings, healthcare, markets
