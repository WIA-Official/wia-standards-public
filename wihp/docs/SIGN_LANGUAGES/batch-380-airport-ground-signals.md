### Introduction
Airport marshalling signals are internationally standardized hand signals used by ground crew to guide aircraft on the ground. These signals are regulated by the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) and are legally required for international aviation.

This represents one of the most strictly standardized sign systems in the world, with identical signals used at every commercial airport globally.

### ICAO Standardization
The International Civil Aviation Organization:
- Sets global standards
- Annex 2 specifies signals
- All member states comply
- Regular updates
- Safety-critical regulation

### Aircraft Guidance Signals
| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Wands raised, beckoning | Proceed to this marshaller |
| Wands down, outward | Proceed straight ahead |
| Wands move left | Turn left |
| Wands move right | Turn right |
| Arms crossed overhead | Stop |
| Thumbs up | Chocks in place |
