### Introduction
Trading floor sign languages were sophisticated manual communication systems developed in stock and commodity exchanges worldwide. In the chaos of open outcry trading pits, where shouting was supplemented by hand signals, elaborate sign systems evolved to communicate buy/sell orders, prices, and quantities.

With the shift to electronic trading, these occupational sign languages have largely disappeared, representing a significant cultural and linguistic loss.

### Sign Language Components

#### Numbers (Critical)
| Concept | General Form |
|---------|--------------|
| 1-5 | Fingers extended (facing out = buy, in = sell) |
| 6-9 | Combinations |
| 10s | Various conventions |
| 100s | Different gestures |
| Fractions | Specific signs |

#### Buy/Sell
| Action | Signal |
|--------|--------|
| Buy | Palm facing self |
| Sell | Palm facing out |
