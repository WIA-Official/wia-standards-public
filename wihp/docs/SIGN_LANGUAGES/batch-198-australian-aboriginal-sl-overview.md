# Batch 198: Australian Aboriginal Sign Languages - Overview

## Unique Characteristics

### The Worlds Oldest Sign Language Tradition
- Aboriginal sign languages are among the oldest in the world
- 250,000+ years of continuous cultural tradition
- 12+ distinct sign languages still in use

### Alternate vs Primary Sign Languages
Most Australian Indigenous sign languages are ALTERNATE sign languages:
- Developed by HEARING people
- Used when speech is forbidden or impractical
- Also used by deaf community members

### Bimodal Bilingualism
- Entire communities use BOTH spoken and signed languages
- Rare phenomenon globally
- Strong cultural and kinship connections support this

## Speech Taboo Origins

### Mourning Practices
- Widows forbidden to speak for months to years
- Sign language sole communication during mourning
- Women have better command than men

### Male Initiation
- Speech forbidden during ceremonies
- Sacred knowledge transmitted in sign

### Avoidance Relationships
- Certain kin cannot speak directly
- Mother-in-law avoidance common

### Practical Uses
- Silent hunting communication
- Long-distance communication
- Private conversations

## Deaf Aboriginal Experience

### Double Isolation
1. Isolation from mainstream hearing Australia
2. Isolation from mainstream Deaf/Auslan community
3. Sometimes isolation from own Indigenous community

### Health Context
- Hearing loss rates: 10x higher than non-Indigenous
- High rates of otitis media

## Threats

1. Modern lifestyle changes
2. Urbanization
3. Auslan taught instead of Indigenous sign
4. Intergenerational transmission decline
5. Colonization effects
