### Introduction
Whistled languages and sign languages both demonstrate that human language is not limited to spoken speech. While sign languages are visual-manual and used primarily by deaf communities, whistled languages are auditory and typically developed by hearing communities in mountainous terrain for long-distance communication.

Comparing these modalities reveals insights about human language capacity.
