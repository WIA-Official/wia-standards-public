# Saudi Sign Language / Lughat al-Ishara al-Saudiya (SSL)

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Saudi Sign Language / Saudi Arabian Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Saudiya (لغة الإشارة السعودية) |
| **Abbreviations** | SSL, SASL |
| **ISO 639-3** | sdl |
| **Region** | Saudi Arabia |
| **Status** | Indigenous, stable |

## Historical Development

### Early History
- Informal sign systems in family and community settings
- Natural emergence among deaf individuals

### Formal Education Era (1964)
- First two schools for deaf established in Riyadh (1964)
- Separate schools for boys and girls (gender-segregated system)
- Schools founded before many other Arab countries

### Foreign Influences
- British Sign Language (BSL) influence - teachers from UK
- American Sign Language (ASL) influence - Saudi specialists studying abroad
- Teachers from Jordan and Egypt added cultural signs
- Gender-based vocabulary differences due to segregated education

## Linguistic Features
- Own unique grammar, syntax, and lexicon
- Complex features: verb agreement, nominal case marking, aspectual markers
- Facial expressions, gestures, finger spelling
- Distinct from other Arab sign languages

### Cultural Elements
- Has own literature, humor, and poetry
- Can be studied as foreign language in colleges
- Older generations use more traditional SASL

## Pan-Arab Unification Efforts
- Unified Arabic Sign Language dictionary (2004)
- Created by Council of Arab Ministers of Social Affairs (CAMSA)
- Combined signs from Saudi, Egyptian, Jordanian sign languages
- Used by Al-Jazeera for news interpretation
- Met with resistance from deaf communities

## Organizations
- King Salman Center for Disability Research
- Saudi Association for Hearing Impairment
- Saudi Committee for Sign Language Experts and Interpreters
- Ministry of Health initiatives

### Key Achievements
- Largest Saudi Sign Language database developed
- Interpreter certification programs established
- Alshams Private School (2007) - teaches SASL to hearing students
- Prince Sattam bin Abdulaziz University offers SASL course (2019)

## Education
- Government recognizes educational rights of deaf
- Ongoing challenges with interpreter services
- Teacher SSL skills need improvement
- Integration efforts ongoing
