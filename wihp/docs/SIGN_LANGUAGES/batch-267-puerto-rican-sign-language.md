# Puerto Rican Sign Language / PRSL

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Puerto Rican Sign Language |
| **Abbreviation** | PRSL |
| **ISO 639-3** | psl |
| **Region** | Puerto Rico (US Territory) |
| **Deaf Population** | 8,000-340,000 (varying estimates) |
| **Status** | 6b Threatened / Vulnerable |

## Historical Development

### Origins (1902-1907)
- 1902/1907: ASL introduced to Puerto Rico
- 1907: Catholic nuns introduced signs to schools
- Possible indigenous sign language predating ASL
- Contact with US deaf community

### ASL Dominance
- Signed English and ASL used in deaf schools
- High contact with US deaf community
- Americans with Disabilities Act applies
- ASL becoming sole sign language

## Linguistic Classification

### Debate on Status
- Dialect of ASL?
- Separate stand-alone language?
- Creole or pidgin language?
- 54% lexical similarity with ASL documented

### Unique Characteristics
- Slower signing rate than ASL
- More repetition of signs
- Different palm orientation
- Puerto Rican cultural elements

## Geographic Distribution
- Urban areas (especially San Juan metro): ASL dominant
- Rural/mountainous areas: PRSL may persist
- Western and central parts: Traditional PRSL
- Only 20 of 78 municipalities have deaf education

## Puerto Rican Signs
Unique vocabulary includes:
- Mofongo (local food)
- Tostones (fried plantains)
- Presentao (cultural term)
- Buen provecho (cultural greeting)
- All 78 municipality names
- Home signs used within families

## Education System

### Schools
- Colegio San Gabriel
- Evangelical School for the Deaf (Betsy Hoke, administrator)
- Deaf schools concentrated in northeastern area

### Current Situation
- ASL taught in deaf schools
- ASL taught at Puerto Rican university
- Limited PRSL interpretation services
- Only one agency offers PRSL interpretation

## Organizations
- CUACS (Campus organization for deaf awareness)
- Four interpreting agencies
- Deaf ministries and associations
- Video relay services

## Challenges

### Language Shift
- ASL poised to replace PRSL entirely
- New generation influenced by internet ASL resources
- Social media spreading ASL
- Urban-rural divide in language use

### Interpreter Shortage
- Significant lack of interpreters
- Growing services but insufficient
- ASL interpreters more available than PRSL
