# Haitian Sign Language (LSH)
## Langue des Signes Haitienne

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Haitian Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Langue des Signes Haitienne (LSH) |
| **ISO 639-3** | hks |
| **Country** | Haiti |
| **Users** | ~72,000 deaf population (WHO 2003) |
| **Status** | Not officially recognized |
| **Language Family** | Related to ASL but distinct |

### Language Status
- **NOT officially recognized** by government
- Government recognizes some schools using sign language
- Not recognized as official national language
- Not official language of instruction
- 2018 disability act provisions largely unobserved

### Historical Documentation
- **2014**: LSHDoP (Documentation Project) began
- **2016**: First linguistic description published
- Hochgesang and McAuliff first formal research
- Collaboration between Deaf Haitians and Gallaudet University
- OAS (Organization of American States) support

### Key Research
- **Julie A. Hochgesang & Kate McAuliff** (2016)
  - An Initial Description of the Deaf Community in Haiti and Haitian Sign Language
  - Sign Language Studies, Vol. 16, No. 2, pp. 227-294
- Grammatical Sketch available in multiple languages
- YouTube documentation channel

### Language Characteristics
- Similar to ASL but considered distinct by Deaf Haitians
- Own unique features and expressions
- Topic of keen discussion: relationship with ASL
- Transmitted through deaf schools and peer interaction

### Cultural Context
- Deafness seen as punishment/curse in traditional culture
- Deaf people fear using sign language around hearing
- Stigmatization and discrimination common
- Violence against deaf (2016 murders of 3 deaf women)
- Poverty prevalent among deaf community

### Healthcare Challenges
- No early hearing detection screening system
- Families discover deafness between infancy and age 6-8
- Some seek traditional/voodoo remedies
- No government-sponsored programs for deaf children
- More access to hearing aids than sign language resources

### Education System
- Five government-run deaf schools
- LSH spread through schools and social areas
- Most deaf dont make it past secondary school
- Limited interpreter services

### Organizations
- **Federation Nationale des Sourds dHaiti** (Jonas Cadet, President since 2010)
- **Association des Sourds de Leveque**: Risk/emergency advocacy
- **IHLS** (Institut Haitien de Langue des Signes): Training institute
- **LSHDoP**: Documentation project with Gallaudet
- **CASAS**: State special education agency

### IHLS Training Institute
- Founded February 2019 in Port-au-Prince
- Year-long course (3 semesters)
- 200+ enrolled since opening
- Cost: 13,000 gourdes (~91)
- Co-founders: Fenel Bellegarde, Jean Evens Pierre

### Legal Barriers
- Courts and police lack communication resources
- No interpreting services in justice system
- No statistics on services provided
- Article 11 CRPD on emergencies not implemented

### Research Partners
- **Gallaudet University** Linguistics Department
- **Organization of American States** (OAS)
- **Gerald Oriol Jr.** - Secretary of State for Disability Integration
- Sylvie Marc-Charles Weir (Gallaudet/Haitian heritage)

### Disability Population
- ~1 million disabled Haitians total
- Chronic poverty and marginalization
- Natural disasters compound challenges
- Limited government health spending
