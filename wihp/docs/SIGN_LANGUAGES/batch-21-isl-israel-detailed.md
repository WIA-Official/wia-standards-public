# WIA Talk Sign Language Mapping - ISL (Israeli Sign Language / שפת הסימנים הישראלית)
## Batch 21: 20 Signs with Full WIA Talk Component Analysis

**Language:** ISL - Israeli Sign Language (שפת הסימנים הישראלית / Shassi)
**Region:** Israel (used by Jewish, Muslim, Christian Arabs, Druze, Bedouin)
**Estimated Users:** 10,000-20,000 (5,000 daily users)
**Language Family:** Related to German Sign Language (DGS)
**Recognition:** November 29, 2020 - Government established ISL unit at Hebrew Language Academy

## ISL Characteristics

### Historical Background
- **1873:** Marcus Reich (German Jew) opened school for Jewish deaf children in Germany
- **Ottoman Empire period:** Earliest information about ISL
- **1932:** First school for deaf established in Jerusalem (Jewish School for Deaf Mutes)
  - Strict boarding school teaching oralism
  - Headed by teacher from Jewish School for the Deaf in Berlin
- **1941:** Tel Aviv school established
- **1940s:** ISL (Shassi) became language of established community in Jerusalem and Tel Aviv
- **1943-1944:** Deaf association formed with 44 members
- **1949:** Haifa school established
- **1970s:** Izchak Schlesinger began ISL research
- **1973:** Fourth International Conference on Deafness in Israel
- **1970s-80s:** Formal linguistic research began (Sandler, Aronoff, Padden, Meir)
- **November 29, 2020:** Government established ISL unit at Hebrew Language Academy

### Language Family & Origins
- **Descended from German Sign Language (DGS)** via Jewish deaf school in Berlin
- A local **creole** gradually emerged
- Still shares features with DGS but now independent
- Influences from:
  - German Sign Language (DGS)
  - Algerian Jewish Sign Language (AJSL)
  - Moroccan Sign Language
  - Various immigrant sign languages

### NOT "Hebrew Sign Language"!
- Common myth: ISL is "Hebrew Sign Language" or "Jewish Sign Language"
- **FALSE** - ISL is used by:
  - Hebrew speakers
  - Arabic speakers
  - Russian speakers
  - Jewish, Muslim, Christian communities
  - Arab, Druze, Bedouin communities
- ISL is **independent** from spoken Hebrew

### Other Sign Languages in Israel
- **Al-Sayyid Bedouin Sign Language (ABSL)** - unique village sign language
- Kufr Qassem village sign language
- Ein Mahel village sign language
- Other less-documented community sign languages

### Key Phonological Features (5 parameters)
1. **Handshape:** Over 30 distinct handshapes
2. **Location:** Neutral space or body parts (face, chest)
3. **Movement:** Path or internal hand motion
4. **Orientation:** Palm/finger direction
5. **Nonmanual features:** Facial expressions, body position

### Manual Alphabet
- Similar to ASL alphabet
- Based on French-origin fingerspelling family
- Unique correspondences:
  - ASL 'F' → Hebrew ט (tet)
  - ASL '1' → ו (vav)
  - ASL 'C' and 'K' → כ (kaf) and ק (qof)
  - ASL '3' → צ (tsade)
  - ASL 'W' → ש (shin) - reflects shape!

### Organizations & Resources
- **Hebrew Language Academy** - ISL Department (2020)
- **Sign Language Research Lab** - University of Haifa
- **Deaf Association in Israel (ADI)**
- **ISL-LEX v.1** - Database with 961 video entries (2022)
- Bar-Ilan University
- Ma'agalei Shema

### Unique Features
- **Young language** - less than 100 years old
- Used in research on **language evolution**
- **Multi-ethnic deaf community** - diverse users
- New signs being created (e.g., 20 historical terms for Tower of David Museum)
- Former Knesset member **Shirley Pinto** (deaf) advocated for ISL

## WIA Talk 5-Component System Reference

| Code | Component | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| HS | Hand Shape | Configuration of fingers and palm |
| LC | Location | Where the sign is performed |
| MV | Movement | Direction and type of motion |
| OR | Orientation | Which way palm/fingers face |
| FE | Facial Expression | Required non-manual markers |

## Summary: ISL WIA Talk Code Inventory

### Hand Shapes Used (HS)
| Code | Shape | Signs Using |
|------|-------|-------------|
| HS01 | Fist | Yes |
| HS02 | Flat hand | Shalom, Thank you, Please, No, Love, Don't understand, Goodbye |
| HS03 | 5 fingers spread | Number 5, Number 10 |
| HS04 | V-shape | Number 2, Name |
| HS07 | Index finger | Number 1 |
| HS10 | Four fingers | Number 4 |
| HS45-47 | ISL 7-9 shapes | Numbers 7-9 |
| HS48 | O-shape | Number 0 |
| HS52 | Three fingers | Number 3 |
| HS53 | ISL 6 shape | Number 6 |

### Unique ISL Features
- **Young language** (~80-90 years old)
- **Creole origin** from German Sign Language
- **Multi-ethnic user community** (Jewish, Arab, Druze, Bedouin)
- **NOT "Hebrew Sign Language"** - independent language
- **ASL-similar manual alphabet** (French-origin family)
- **Over 30 distinct handshapes**
- **Hebrew Language Academy** ISL Department (2020)
- **ISL-LEX database** - 961 video entries
- **Key figure:** Moshe Bamberger (first chairman, arrived 1934 from Germany)
- **Research hub:** University of Haifa Sign Language Research Lab

### Manual Alphabet Unique Correspondences
| ASL | Hebrew | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| F | ט (tet) | Analogous to Greek theta |
| 1 | ו (vav) | Not U or V |
| W | ש (shin) | Reflects letter shape! |
| 3 | צ (tsade) | Resembles Arabic numeral 3 |
