# Yemeni Sign Language / Lughat al-Ishara al-Yamaniya

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Yemeni Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Yamaniya (لغة الإشارة اليمنية) |
| **Glottolog Code** | yeme1237 |
| **Region** | Yemen |
| **Language Family** | Arab sign-language family |
| **Status** | Not widely recognized |

## Historical Context

### Historical Division
- May have separate sign languages from former North and South Yemen
- Unclear how close the two variants are
- Unified Yemen since 1990, but linguistic diversity may persist

### Research Status
- Belongs to Arab sign-language family (Hendriks 2008)
- Lexicostatistically established as part of Arab Sign family
- Regional dialects exist across Yemen

## Linguistic Features
- Part of Arab sign-language family
- Has its own alphabet and vocabulary
- Similarities with other Arab sign languages due to shared cultural values
- Regional dialects highlighted in recent artwork projects

## Current Situation

### Challenges
- Not widely recognized in Yemen
- Limited resources for deaf community
- Ongoing conflict affects access to services
- Limited formal educational opportunities

### Activism
- Maisaa Ahmed - Yemeni activist for hearing disability rights
- German-Yemeni exchange project on science communication
- Art projects promoting awareness of Yemeni Sign Language

## Cultural Characteristics
- Reflects Yemeni culture and values
- Passed from generation to generation
- Often learned by hearing family members
- Survival ensured through family transmission

## Regional Context
- Part of MENA deaf community
- Yemen included in Arab Sign Language accessibility surveys
- Less institutional support compared to Gulf neighbors

## Documentation
- Regional dialects showcased in art projects (2024)
- Limited formal linguistic research
- Part of broader Arab sign language studies
