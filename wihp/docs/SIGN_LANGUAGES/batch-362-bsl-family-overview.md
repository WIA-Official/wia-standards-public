### Introduction
The British Sign Language family (sometimes called BANZSL) comprises sign languages descended from or significantly influenced by British Sign Language. This family spread through British colonial education for the deaf and migration patterns throughout the British Empire.

The family is distinct from the larger French Sign Language family (which includes ASL) and represents an independent development of sign language in the British Isles.

### Two-Handed Alphabet
The BSL family uses a two-handed manual alphabet:
- Distinct from one-handed ASL alphabet
- Shared across BSL, Auslan, NZSL
- Some regional variations
- Historical marker of family membership
