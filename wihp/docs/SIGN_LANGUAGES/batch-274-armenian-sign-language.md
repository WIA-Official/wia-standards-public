### Historical Development
- **Traditional Origins**: Developed independently within Armenian deaf community
- **Wittmann (1991)**: Classified as language isolate / prototype sign language
- **1990s**: First academic research initiated (CSUN)
- **First Dictionary**: Limited 100-sign dictionary compiled
- **Ongoing**: Armenian Deaf Society advocacy continues

### Linguistic Classification
- Classified by Henri Wittmann as prototype sign language
- Language isolate - not directly related to European sign languages
- Possible historical connections to monastic sign systems
- Independent grammatical structure from Armenian spoken language

### Research History
- First systematic study: California State University, Northridge
- Three weeks fieldwork in Yerevan, Armenia
- 10 participants: 8 Deaf Armenians, 1 Deaf Russian (control), 1 CODA
- Both genders and various ages studied
- 100 signs documented with photos and descriptions

### Related Language: Harsneren
- Also called: Armenian Womans Sign Language / Caucasian Sign Language
- Armenian: harsneren (brides language)
- Traditional gestural communication among Armenian women
- May have historical connections to ArSL

### Organizations
- Armenian Deaf Society
- Central Governing Board of the Deaf Society
- Focus: Integration of disabled youth
- Spiritual/cultural development programs
- Public life participation initiatives

### Current Status
- Used as first language by deaf people of all ages
- Stable but under-researched
- Limited interpreter availability
- Need for more comprehensive documentation
