### Language Description
Warlpiri Sign Language, known as "rdaka-rdaka" (hand-hand) in Warlpiri, is an alternate sign language used by the Warlpiri people of Central Australia. Like Yolngu Sign Language, it is used by both hearing and deaf individuals and serves cultural functions beyond deaf communication.

The Warlpiri are one of the largest Aboriginal groups in Australia, with approximately 3,000 speakers of the spoken Warlpiri language. The sign language has been used traditionally during speech taboos, particularly those associated with mourning and initiation ceremonies.

### Key Characteristics
- **Alternate Sign Language**: Used by hearing community
- **Speech Taboo Contexts**: Mourning, ceremony, avoidance
- **Women's Predominance**: Strong female signing tradition
- **Semantic System**: Based on Warlpiri spoken language categories
- **Gesture Integration**: Blends with natural gesture

### Cultural Functions

#### Mourning (Sorry Business)
- Widows observe speech bans (historically 1-2 years)
- Close relatives may restrict speech
- Sign language primary communication during this time
- Important for maintaining social connections

#### Ceremonial Use
- Men's initiation restrictions
- Sacred knowledge transmission
- Avoidance relationships (in-laws, etc.)

#### Practical Use
- Hunting silence
- Long-distance communication
- Noisy environments

### Deaf Community
Within Warlpiri society:
- Deaf individuals use extended sign vocabulary
- Family and community can communicate
- Social inclusion traditional
- Deaf may develop expanded signing

### Linguistic Features
- **Semantic Domains**: Signs organized by Warlpiri categories
- **Minimal Pairs**: Distinctions similar to spoken language
- **Directional Modification**: Signs show direction of action
- **Compound Signs**: Complex concepts formed by combination

### Challenges
1. Younger generation less proficient
2. Spoken Warlpiri also endangered
3. Limited documentation
4. Urbanization and mobility
5. Competition from Auslan
6. Loss of traditional practices

### Language Vitality
Factors affecting survival:
- **Positive**: Strong Warlpiri identity, community cohesion
- **Negative**: Social change, reduced ceremonial practice
- **Trend**: Declining but efforts to maintain
- **Key**: Connection to sorry business maintains use

### Integration with Spoken Warlpiri
- Sign vocabulary parallels spoken categories
- Not a translation of spoken language
- Independent grammatical system
- Code-switching between modalities

### Educational Context
- Bilingual education programs in communities
- Some integration of sign traditions
- Auslan used for deaf education (different system)
- Cultural programs may include sign
