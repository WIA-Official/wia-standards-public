# US Virgin Islands Sign Language
## Sign Language of the United States Virgin Islands (USVI)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | American Sign Language (ASL) |
| **Country** | United States Virgin Islands |
| **Status** | US Territory |
| **Deaf Population** | ~2,000+ (2020 census) |
| **Total Population** | ~87,100 |

### Language Situation
- American Sign Language (ASL) is primary sign language
- Part of US deaf community sphere
- ADA requirements apply (often not fully implemented)
- Growing advocacy for interpreter services
- Bill 36-0190 proposed for government interpretation

### Organizations
- **VIUCEDD** (Virgin Islands University Center for Excellence in Developmental Disabilities)
  - ASL workshops (FUN-damentals of ASL)
  - Allied Help Coalition program
  - Established 1994 at University of Virgin Islands
- **Virgin Islands Deaf & Hard of Hearing Advocates**
  - Deaf Awareness events
  - Community education
- **Virgin Islands Association for Independent Living**

### Educational Programs
- Free virtual ASL workshops via Zoom
- 6-week in-person ASL courses
- Early childhood intervention workshops
- Disability sensitivity training sessions
- University of Virgin Islands programs

### Deaf Awareness Events
- **September**: Deaf Awareness Month
- **April**: Deaf History Month
- Community events at UVI
- Trivia and education about deaf culture

### Challenges Identified
- Hospital/medical access without interpreters
- No policy for interpreter provision at appointments
- TTY emergency services issues
- Limited interpreter pool (6-7 in territory)
- Most interpreters work for Dept of Education
- Government broadcasts lack ASL interpretation
- Closed captioning alone insufficient

### Legislative Efforts (Bill 36-0190)
- Requires ASL interpretation at government events
- Calls for at least one interpreter per district
- Governor Bryan supports proposal
- Held in committee for funding/staffing issues
- May need 2 interpreters per island minimum

### VI Deaf Tours
- Accessible tours for deaf visitors
- St. Thomas and St. John based
- History and culture of Virgin Islands
- Sign language teaching
- Owned by Shanelle Lawrence

### Geographic Context
- US unincorporated territory
- Main islands: St. Thomas, St. John, St. Croix
- Caribbean Sea location
- East of Puerto Rico
- Purchased from Denmark in 1917

### Languages Spoken
- **English** (American English) - Official
- **Virgin Islands Creole** - Widely spoken
- Spanish - Immigrant communities

### ADA Compliance Issues
- Americans with Disabilities Act applies
- Requirements often not followed
- Effective communication mandate
- Government and private sector gaps
- Advocacy for full compliance ongoing

### National Context
- ~48 million deaf/hard of hearing in US
- Part of broader US deaf community
- ASL recognized nationally
- Federal accessibility standards apply
