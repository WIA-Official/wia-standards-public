# Greenland Sign Language
## Greenlandic Sign Language (Grønlandsk Tegnsprog)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | Danish Sign Language (DSL) dialect |
| **Native Name** | Grønlandsk Tegnsprog |
| **Country** | Greenland (Denmark) |
| **Status** | Autonomous territory within Kingdom of Denmark |
| **Capital** | Nuuk |
| **Deaf Population** | ~325 |

### Language Situation
- Greenlandic Sign Language is dialect of Danish Sign Language (DSL)
- No officially recognized separate Greenlandic Sign Language
- Deaf community primarily uses DSL
- Historical ties to Denmark
- Efforts to develop localized signs reflecting Greenlandic culture

### Nordic Sign Language Family
Greenlandic Sign Language part of Western Nordic group:
- **Danish Sign Language (DTS)** - Parent language
- Faroese Sign Language (dialect of DSL)
- **Greenlandic Sign Language** (dialect of DSL)
- Icelandic Sign Language (ÍTM)
- Norwegian Sign Language (NTS)

### Historical Development
- 1807: First deaf school in Denmark (Peter Atke Castberg)
- Deaf children from Greenland sent to Denmark before 1978
- 1978: First school for deaf in Greenland established
- DSL exported to Greenland through education system

### Inuit Sign Language Connection
- Historically hearing Inuit used sign language for trade
- **Inuit Sign Language (IUR)** exists in Nunavut (Canada)
- May have historical connection to Greenlandic deaf community
- Used since 18th century for hunting communication
- High incidence of hereditary deafness in Arctic regions

### Geographic Context
- Worlds largest island
- Autonomous territory of Denmark
- North Atlantic location
- Population: ~56,000 total
- Arctic/subarctic climate

### Official Languages
- **Greenlandic (Kalaallisut)** - Official language
- Three main dialects:
  - Kalaallisut (West Greenlandic) - 44,000 speakers
  - Tunumiit (East Greenlandic) - 3,000 speakers
  - Inuktun (North Greenlandic) - 800 speakers
- **Danish** - Widely spoken
- English - Studied in schools

### Political Status
- Autonomous country within Kingdom of Denmark
- Home Rule since 1979
- Self-Government since 2009
- Denmark handles foreign affairs, defense, currency
- Own parliament (Inatsisartut)

### Deaf Organizations
- **Greenlandic Deaf Association**
  - Promotes sign language education
  - Works on accessibility
  - Developing localized signs

### Danish Deaf Support
- **Danish Deaf Association (DDL)** - Founded 1935
- Online DSL dictionary
- Interpreter training programs
- News interpretation requirements

### Danish Sign Language Features
- French Sign Language family influence
- One-handed manual alphabet (1808)
- Unique grammar (spatial, facial expressions)
- Different from spoken Danish grammar

### Education
- Deaf education in Greenland since 1978
- Previously children sent to Denmark
- Bilingual-bicultural approach adopted
- Total communication methods used

### Legal Protections
- Denmark ratified UN CRPD (2009)
- Optional Protocol (2014)
- Sign language interpretation for authorities
- Medical interpretation guaranteed
- News broadcast interpretation required

### Cultural Context
- Kalaallit (Greenlandic Inuit) indigenous culture
- Protestant Christianity predominant
- High literacy tradition
- KNR (public broadcaster)
- Strong media presence since 1861
