# Mbour Sign Language / Langue des signes de Mbour

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Mbour Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Langue des signes de Mbour |
| **ISO 639-3** | Not assigned |
| **Region** | Mbour, Senegal |
| **Type** | Village Sign Language |
| **Status** | Emerging/Informal |

## Historical Development
- Emerged spontaneously in a neighborhood of Mbour city, Senegal
- Deaf people in the neighborhood meet regularly
- Developed independently from formal deaf education system
- Documents an important example of indigenous sign language emergence

## Linguistic Features
- Informal sign language used outside institutional settings
- Developed through natural community interaction
- Shows variation in iconicity typical of emerging sign languages

## Documentation
- Documented by Gwenaelle Jirou-Sylla (2001)
- DEA thesis: Description dune langue des signes informelle en dehors du milieu institutionnel
- Analyzes lexical aspects of the gestural speech of Mbour

## Significance
- Important example of indigenous West African sign language
- Shows natural sign language emergence outside formal education
- Contrasts with ASL-based education in urban Senegal
