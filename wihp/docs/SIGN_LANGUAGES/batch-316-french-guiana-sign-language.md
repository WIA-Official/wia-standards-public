### Language Situation
- French Sign Language (LSF) primary sign language
- Only French territory in South America
- Part of French national deaf education system
- LSF recognized as language in France (2005)
- Part of SIL 24-location Caribbean/Americas survey

### Geographic Context
- French Overseas Region in South America
- Northeast coast of South America
- Borders Brazil and Suriname
- Only European Union territory in continental Americas
- Home to Guiana Space Centre (Kourou)
- Population: ~300,000

- **Guianese Creole** - Widely spoken
- Multiple indigenous languages
- Immigrant languages (Portuguese, Chinese, etc.)

### French Sign Language (LSF)
- ~100,000 native signers in France
- One-handed manual alphabet
- Related to ASL (~60% lexical similarity)
- Abbé Charles-Michel de lÉpée: Key figure
- Laurent Clerc brought LSF to America (1817)

### LSF Legal Recognition
- **1991 Fabius Law**: LSF authorized for education
- **2005 Law**: LSF fully recognized as language
- Applies to all French territories including French Guiana

### Deaf Community Access
- ARS (Regional Health Agency) SMS service
- Originally for deaf/hard of hearing
- Extended during COVID-19 pandemic
- Telemedicine apps developed
- Digital inclusion efforts

### Regional Sign Language Context
French Caribbean/South American territories using LSF:
- Guadeloupe (Caribbean)
- Martinique (Caribbean)
- **French Guiana** (South America)
- Saint Pierre and Miquelon (North Atlantic)

### Historical Context
- Originally French colony
- Infamous Devils Island penal colony
- French overseas department since 1946
- European Space Agency launch site

### Deaf Community Challenges
- Limited educational access
- Scripture access difficulties
- Small population spread over large area
- Indigenous community access barriers
- Multilingual environment complexity

### LSF Historical Significance
- LSF ancestor to ASL (American Sign Language)
- ASL ~60% lexicon from 19th century LSF
- Laurent Clerc: French deaf educator
- Thomas Gallaudet: Brought LSF to USA (1816)
- Cistercian Sign Language possible earlier influence

### Neighboring Sign Languages
- **Brazil**: Libras (Brazilian Sign Language) - LSF-related
- **Suriname**: Surinamese Sign Language (SSL)
- Different language families despite geography
