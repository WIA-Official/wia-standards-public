### Language Description
Liechtenstein, a tiny principality of 160 km² and approximately 39,000 people between Switzerland and Austria, does not have a distinct national sign language. The Deaf community uses Swiss-German Sign Language (Deutschschweizer Gebärdensprache, DSGS), reflecting the country's German-speaking population and close integration with Switzerland.

Some influence from Austrian Sign Language (Österreichische Gebärdensprache, ÖGS) may also be present due to proximity to Austria. However, DSGS is the primary language of the deaf community.

Liechtenstein has a customs union with Switzerland and uses the Swiss franc, with most services closely integrated with the Swiss system.

### Deaf Community
With ~39,000 total population:
- Estimated 50-80 deaf individuals
- Extremely small, intimate community
- Most access services in Switzerland
- Some may have connections to Austrian deaf
- Full integration with Swiss German-speaking deaf

### Education

### Swiss-German Sign Language (DSGS)
DSGS characteristics relevant to Liechtenstein:
- One of three Swiss sign languages
- Used in German-speaking Switzerland
- Different from German Sign Language (DGS)
- Various regional varieties exist
- Liechtenstein may use St. Gallen regional variety
