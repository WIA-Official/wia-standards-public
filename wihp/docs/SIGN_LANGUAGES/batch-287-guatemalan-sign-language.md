# Guatemalan Sign Language (LENSEGUA)
## Lengua de Senas de Guatemala

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Guatemalan Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lengua de Senas de Guatemala (LENSEGUA) |
| **ISO 639-3** | gsm |
| **Country** | Guatemala |
| **Users** | 28,000-256,000 estimated |
| **Status** | Stable; Recognized (2020) |
| **Language Family** | Mixed origins (Old LESCO, ASL, indigenous) |

### Legal Recognition (January 2020)
- Guatemalan Congress officially adopted LENSEGUA
- Recognized as first language for deaf people
- Previous bills (2009, 2016, 2019) considered but not voted
- Landmark decision for inclusivity

### Population Statistics
- ~640,000 Guatemalans with hearing loss
- 40% use sign language
- Greatest concentration in Guatemala City
- Many in northern Peten never exposed to full language

### Dialects
1. **Eastern dialect**: Guatemala City and surrounding areas (Ladino/mestizo)
2. **Western dialect**: Quetzaltenango and western highlands

### Historical Development
- **2000**: First LENSEGUA dictionary published
- Formed from Old Costa Rican SL, ASL, and indigenous signs
- Distinct from neighboring countries sign languages
- Has manual alphabet with initialized signs

### Linguistic Features
- Own grammar structure (not Spanish translation)
- Uses finger-spelling
- Distinct from Mayan sign languages (Meemul Tziij complex)
- Indigenous SLs separate from LENSEGUA

### Related Sign Languages in Region
- Mayan sign languages (not part of Mayan language family)
- Village sign languages
- Home sign systems (especially rural areas)

### Education
- Schools use LENSEGUA as primary instruction language
- Less than 50% of deaf receive formal education
- Few Deaf-led ministries
- Limited access in northern regions
