### Language Description
Sint Maarten Sign Language refers to the sign language variety used on the Dutch side of the island of Saint Martin/Sint Maarten. The island is divided between France (Saint-Martin) and the Netherlands (Sint Maarten), creating a unique linguistic situation for the deaf community.

The sign language shows heavy influence from American Sign Language (ASL) due to proximity to the United States and educational connections. There is also interaction with French Sign Language (LSF) varieties used on the French side of the island.

The deaf community often crosses the open border between the two sides, creating a mixed signing environment where ASL-based and LSF-based signing may both be encountered.

### Key Characteristics
- **Border Island**: Unique Dutch-French bilingual context
- **ASL Dominance**: Strong American influence
- **French Interaction**: Contact with LSF on north side
- **Tourism Context**: High exposure to international visitors
- **English Base**: English widely spoken, affecting mouth movements

### Deaf Community
The Sint Maarten deaf community is relatively small but benefits from:
- Open border allowing interaction with French side
- Strong English language environment
- Tourism economy providing employment
- Regional Caribbean Deaf connections

### The Border Factor
The Treaty of Concordia (1648) established an open border:
- Deaf individuals move freely between Dutch and French sides
- May learn both ASL-influenced and LSF-influenced signs
- Creates natural bilingualism in sign languages
- Community gatherings may include signers from both sides

### Education
- **Dutch Side**: Sister Marie Laurence School for Special Education
- **French Side**: French educational system
- **Approach**: Primarily ASL-based on Dutch side
- **Challenges**: Limited resources, small population

### Post-Hurricane Recovery
Hurricane Irma (2017) severely impacted Sint Maarten:
- Infrastructure damage affected deaf services
- Some deaf individuals relocated
- Rebuilding process ongoing
- International aid supported recovery

### Challenges
1. Small community divided by international border
2. Competition between ASL and LSF influences
3. Hurricane vulnerability
4. Limited documentation
5. Economic instability affects services
