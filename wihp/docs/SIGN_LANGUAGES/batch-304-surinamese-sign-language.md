# Surinamese Sign Language (SSL)
## Sign Language of Suriname

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Surinamese Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Surinaamse Gebarentaal (SSL) |
| **Country** | Suriname |
| **Status** | Official language for deaf community |
| **Language Family** | NGT-influenced, with local development |

### Multiple Sign Languages in Suriname
According to research (Parks & Williams 2011), three sign languages in use:
1. **Surinamese Sign Language (SSL)** - Primary community language
2. **Sign Language of the Netherlands (NGT)** - Educational use
3. **Sign Supported Dutch (SSD)** - Educational use

Additionally:
- **Kajana Sign Language** - Village sign language (3 families)

### Official Recognition
- SSL recognized as official language for deaf community
- Improved access to education and medical services
- Government sign language interpreters during press conferences
- Efforts to standardize and codify SSL

### Language Development
- Developed naturally among deaf individuals and families
- Kennedy School for Deaf Children (Paramaribo) influence
- Contact with Dutch educators and professionals
- Children use signs among themselves distinct from teachers

### Organizations
- **SUDOBE** (Surinaamse Doven Bond voor Emancipatie)
  - Surinamese society for deaf rights
- **Kennedy School for Deaf Children** (Paramaribo)
  - Only deaf school in Suriname

### Key Figures
- **Igsan Karijowidjojo**: SUDOBE member, vlogger
- **Stephanie Waridjan**: SSL interpreter (in training)
- **Mrs. Rosita**: Sign language course organizer

### Cultural Features
- Name signs (personal signs based on appearance/character)
- The Smiley Deaf comedy group (YouTube, Facebook, Instagram)
- Vlogs teaching Surinamese Sign Language
- International sign language (I Love You) also used

### Challenges
- Only 2 official sign language interpreters in entire country
- No official SSL interpreter education system
- Limited access to information and services
- Need for interpreter recognition as official service
- Limited employment opportunities
- Social stigmatization

### COVID-19 Impact
- Sign language interpreters added to press conferences
- Deaf community appreciation for accessibility
- Call for more interpretation services
- Request for subtitles when interpretation unavailable

### Education
- Kennedy School teaches NGT and SSD
- Students use different signs among themselves
- Bilingual approach: SSL and written Dutch
- Need for inclusive education expansion

### Geographic Context
- South American country
- Population: ~560,000
- Dutch: Official language
- Sranan Tongo: Widely spoken creole
- Former Dutch colony (independence 1975)

### Related Languages
- NGT (Sign Language of the Netherlands)
- NGT also used in former Netherlands Antilles
- Historical Dutch colonial connections

### Kajana Sign Language
- Village sign language
- Spoken in Kajana village
- Only three families
- Distinct from SSL
