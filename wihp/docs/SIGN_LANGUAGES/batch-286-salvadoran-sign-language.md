# Salvadoran Sign Language (LESSA)
## Lengua de Senas Salvadorena

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Salvadoran Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lengua de Senas Salvadorena (LESSA) |
| **ISO 639-3** | esn |
| **Country** | El Salvador |
| **Users** | ~15,000 (0.25% of population) |
| **Status** | Vulnerable; Recognized (2005) |
| **Language Family** | Language isolate (prototype SL) |

### Legal Recognition (2005)
- Recognized by Salvadoran government
- Used for official government interpretation
- Public deaf education conducted in LESSA

### Language Distribution
| Language Group | Percentage |
|----------------|------------|
| LESSA users | ~65% |
| ASL variety (Salvadoran) | ~20% |
| LESCO + LESSA combination | ~10% |
| Home signs (rural) | ~5% |

### Three Forms of Sign Language
1. **Pure LESSA**: Indigenous Salvadoran signs
2. **ASL**: Brought by American missionaries
3. **ASL-LESSA hybrid**: Combined form commonly used

### Historical Development
- ASL introduced by US missionaries
- Government schools developed modified LESSA
- Henri Wittmann: LESSA is language isolate
- Possible stimulus diffusion from French Sign Language
- Community developed distinct linguistic identity

### Linguistic Classification
- Classified as prototype sign language
- Language isolate per Wittmann analysis
- Influenced Panamanian Sign Language
- Used alongside ASL and LESCO

### Education System
- Government-run formal deaf school
- Teachers visit villages every ~5 years
- Five public schools serve deaf exclusively
- One private deaf school
- Education advocacy improving over 15+ years

### Current Developments
- **FUDIPES**: Deaf-run NGO creating video-books
- LESSA/Spanish video-books in Bloom Library
- **TBLESSA**: Biblical LESSA-Spanish dictionaries
- First LESSA grammar booklet in development
- LESSA linguistic analysis ongoing

### Community Needs (Identified)
1. Improved deaf education
2. Developed and expanded LESSA use
3. Interpreter standardization and certification
4. Spanish literacy programs
5. Bible translation in LESSA

### Organizations
- Three deaf associations in El Salvador
- FUDIPES (Deaf-run NGO)
- TBLESSA (Bible translation team)
- Public and private deaf schools

### Employment
- Most work as low-paid laborers
- Some are teachers or computer workers
- Community advocates for equal access
- Fighting for employment rights

### Research
- **2009**: SIL sociolinguistic survey (Ciupek-Reed, Epley)
- University of North Dakota thesis
- Multiple wordlist and text studies
- Participatory research methodology applied
