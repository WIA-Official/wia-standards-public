### Language Description
Old Kent Sign Language is a hypothesized historical sign language that may have existed in Kent, England, prior to the establishment of formal deaf education and the development of British Sign Language (BSL). The evidence for this language comes from historical records of deaf communities in Kent and the presence of hereditary deafness in certain families.

The existence of distinct regional sign varieties in England before BSL standardization is well-documented, and Kent's relative isolation and documented deaf populations suggest a local sign system may have existed.
