# Ukrainian Sign Language (USL)
## Ukrainska zhestova mova (UZhM)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Ukrainian Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Ukrainska zhestova mova |
| **Cyrillic** | Українська жестова мова (УЖМ) |
| **ISO 639-3** | ukl |
| **Country** | Ukraine |
| **Users** | 43,108 registered (2015); est. 200,000 total |
| **Status** | Stable indigenous language |
| **Language Family** | French Sign Language family |

### Historical Development
- **1805**: First deaf school opened in Romaniv (Volyn)
- **1830**: Halychyna School for Deaf in Lviv
- **1843**: Odesa School for the Deaf established
- **1933**: Ukrainian Society of the Deaf (UTOG) founded
- **1950**: Stalin banned sign language in USSR education
- **1957**: UTOG joined World Federation of the Deaf
- **2006**: USL reintroduced in deaf education
- **2014**: Film The Tribe raised global awareness
- **2022**: UTOG began de-Russification of USL

### Soviet Impact
- Stalin called sign language surrogate language
- Deaf people labeled abnormal human beings
- USL education banned; oralism enforced
- Linguistic genocide of Ukrainian deaf culture
- Soviet Sign Language replaced authentic USL

### Current De-Russification
- Half of 44,000 UTOG members switching to USL
- New Ukrainian signs replacing Russian ones
- Muscle memory makes transition difficult
- Some interpreters conservative about changes
- Youth leading language revival efforts

### Education Infrastructure (2015)
- 39 preschools for deaf children
- 61 specialized secondary schools
- Most schools emphasize oral proficiency
- Department of Sign Language at Institute of Special Education
- Laboratory of Sign Language established 2006

### International Research
- University of Alberta Ukrainian Sign Language Project (2007)
- Dr. Debra Russell headed recognition efforts
- 70% similarity with RSL and Moldovan SL (2005 study)
- Ongoing documentation and curriculum development

### Cultural Significance
- The Tribe (2014) - entirely in USL, no subtitles
- Highlighted deaf community alienation
- Explored Ukrainian-Russian cultural identity

### War Impact (2022-present)
- Deaf cannot hear air raid sirens
- Limited government support
- Anti-Russian sentiment accelerated de-Russification
- Many deaf Ukrainians emigrated

### Organizations
- Ukrainian Society of the Deaf (UTOG)
- 38,746 members (90% of registered deaf)
- EUD affiliated member since 2022
- Chair: Iryna Chepchina
