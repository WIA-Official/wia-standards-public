### Introduction
The UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) is the first international human rights treaty to explicitly recognize sign languages and the linguistic rights of deaf people. It provides a comprehensive framework for deaf rights globally.

### CRPD Overview
| Aspect | Detail |

### Sign Language in CRPD

#### Article 2: Definitions
| Content | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| "Language" | Includes sign languages |
| Explicit | Clear recognition |
| Equal | Same status as spoken |

#### Article 9: Accessibility
| Content | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Communication | Access required |
| Sign language | Part of access |
| Interpreters | Must be provided |

#### Article 21: Expression
| Content | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Right | To expression in SL |
| Official | Accept SL in official |
| Promote | Use of sign language |
| Identity | Linguistic identity |

#### Article 24: Education
| Content | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Language | Learn in SL |
| Teachers | Who sign |
| Environment | Maximize development |
| Deaf culture | Facilitate |

#### Article 30: Culture
| Content | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Deaf culture | Recognized |
| Linguistic identity | Protected |
| Sign language | Cultural expression |
