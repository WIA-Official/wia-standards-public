- School established with USSR assistance
- Many similarities with Russian Sign Language (RSL)
- Languages have since diverged significantly
- Deaf community actively differentiating from RSL

## Language Characteristics

### Relationship to Russian Sign Language
- Initially very similar due to Soviet influence
- Active divergence since 1990s
- Deaf community prefers differentiation
- Kinship terms show intentional dissimilation

### Kinship Terminology
#### Native MSL System
- Lineal system
- Elaborated for immediate family
- Grandparents included
- Unified terms for collaterals (aunts, cousins)

#### Preferred System
- Fingerspelled Mongolian kinship terms
- Influenced by spoken/written Mongolian
- Shows cultural identity assertion

### Variation Sources
1. Age of signers
2. Time spent abroad (e.g., Gallaudet University)
3. Regional differences
4. Language contact with ASL
5. Individual preferences

## Challenges

### Interpreter Shortage
- Professional interpreters scarce
- Translator applications limited
- Hinders freedom of expression
- Affects political/public participation

### Variation and Change
- Rapid language change occurring
- Mongolians studying abroad bring variations
- ASL influence increasing
- Generational differences noted

## Language Features

### Fingerspelling
- 51 distinct hand gestures documented
- Used for Mongolian words
- Sometimes preferred over signs
- Shows spoken language influence
