# Egyptian Sign Language / Lughat al-Ishara al-Misriya

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Egyptian Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Misriya (لغة الإشارة المصرية) |
| **ISO 639-3** | esl |
| **Region** | Egypt |
| **Users** | ~1.2 million deaf/hard of hearing (5 years+) |
| **Legal Status** | Not formally recognized by government |

## Historical Development

### Early History
- Emerged organically from indigenous signing systems
- Influences from regional sign languages
- Early dictionary dates from 1984

### Modern Era
- 1982: Deaf Unit Cairo established by Episcopal Diocese
- National Association of the Deaf created online dictionary
- 113 schools for the deaf exist (quality varies)
- Some schools use ESL, others are oral

## Linguistic Features
- NOT related to other Arab sign languages
- Distinct from Jordanian, Palestinian, Libyan Sign Languages
- Unique grammar and vocabulary
- Uses Sign Writing notation to some extent

### Unique Characteristics
- Independent development from other Arab sign languages
- Attempts at unification with Arabic Sign Language met resistance
- Research on mouthing in ESL conducted

## Organizations
- National Association of the Deaf (Egypt)
- Deaf Unit Cairo - school and programs
- EIDR (Giza) - legal rights for deaf Egyptians
- Resala - Islamic charitable organization
- Deaf Ministries International

## Education
- 113 schools for the deaf (variable standards)
- Mix of ESL and oral methods
- Limited interpreter services in higher education
- Deaf Unit offers programs for deaf and hearing

## Cultural Significance
- Egyptian Deaf community gradually gaining visibility
- KFC Cairo outlet employs deaf staff using ESL
- Sign language interpretation on some TV programs
- Advocacy for accessibility ongoing
