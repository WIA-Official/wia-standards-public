# Batch 209: Algerian Jewish Sign Language (AJSL)

## Overview
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Also Known As** | Ghardaia Sign Language |
| **Origin** | Ghardaïa, M'zab region, Algeria |
| **Current Location** | Israel (possibly France) |

| **Status** | MORIBUND |

## Historical Background

### Ghardaïa Jewish Community
- Founded 11th century by Ibadiyya Muslim sect
- Jews brought for trade skills (4 families initially)
- After 1492 expulsion from Spain, more Jews settled
- Jews lived in mellah (walled Jewish quarter)
- Isolated from Muslim majority except commercially

### Origins of Deafness
- Consanguineous (relative) marriage common
- High hereditary deafness rate: 2.5% of community
- Social isolation enabled genetic concentration
- No intermarriage with Muslim community

### Sign Language Development
- Developed WITHOUT outside sign language influence
- Deaf children did not attend school in Algeria
- Standardized Algerian SL only recognized in 2002
- AJSL emerged independently over generations

## Immigration History

### Three Waves to Israel
| Wave | Period | Trigger |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1st | 1943-1948 | WWII, Zionism |
| 2nd | 1950s | Israel established, Algerian War |
| 3rd | 1962 | Algeria independence, anti-Jewish laws |

### 1962 Crisis
- Algeria won independence from France
- Parliament denied citizenship to non-Muslims
- ALL Jews forced to flee
- No Jews remain in Algeria today

### Displacement
- Some went to Israel
- Some relocated to Marseilles, France
- Ship problems caused unplanned relocations
- Poor conditions in young Israel caused some to leave

## Unique Linguistic Features

### Extreme Iconicity
AJSL preserved more iconicity than other sign languages:
- Signs more visually transparent
- Less abstract than even Israeli Sign Language
- Example: DEAF sign = cutting off tongue with scissors
- BOY/GIRL signs reference sex organs (normal in AJSL)

### Why More Iconic?
- Never underwent standardization
- Used mostly in informal environments
- Isolation preserved original forms
- ISL (younger language) is less iconic

### Distinctiveness
- Mutually unintelligible with all other sign languages
- Unique signs from isolation
- Only share few pan-Arabic signs (e.g., WATER = drinking from well)

## Current Situation in Israel

### Language Use Patterns
| Context | Language Used |
|---------|---------------|
| Nuclear family | AJSL |
| Deaf community | ISL |
| Spouses (non-Algerian) | ISL |
| Children | ISL |
| Public settings | ISL |

### Survival Paradox
AJSL survived when other immigrant sign languages died because:
1. High deaf percentage meant hearing relatives knew AJSL
2. Hearing relatives had no motivation to learn ISL
3. AJSL remained family language

### Unique Transmission Pattern
**Most unique aspect of AJSL:**
- Now passed down MOSTLY by HEARING people
- Deaf learned ISL quickly in Israel
- Hearing never learned ISL
- Hearing teach AJSL to children/grandchildren

## Key Researcher

### Sara Lanesman
- Haifa University Sign Language Research Lab
- Personal connection: fled Algeria as child (age 6)
- Three deaf sisters
- Discovered AJSL during research

### Discovery Story
- Studying village sign language signers
- Asked standard questions
- Participant responded "In Ghardaia we signed differently"
- Led to rediscovery of unknown language

## Endangered Status

### Why Moribund
1. Intergenerational transmission ceased
2. Community relocated and dispersed
3. Small community size
4. Deaf Algerians married non-Algerians
5. ISL dominance in Israel

### Social Factors
- AJSL perceived negatively by Israeli Deaf community
- Algerian culture marginalized
- AJSL relegated to minority language status
- Only used with friends and family

## Cultural Significance

### Lost Jewish Community
- 3,000 years of North African Jewish history
- Communities now extinct or near-extinct
- AJSL is linguistic heritage of vanished world
- Documents unique deaf-Jewish experience

### Religious Context
- Jews practiced Judaism freely in mellah
- Synagogue in quarter
- Ceremonial traditions maintained
- Sign language developed within religious community

## Comparison with Other Israeli Sign Languages

| Language | Origin | Status |
|----------|--------|--------|
| ISL | Israel 1930s | Dominant |
| ABSL | Al-Sayyid village | Active research |
| KQSL | Kafr Qasem | ~100 years old |
| AJSL | Algeria → Israel | Moribund |

### Why AJSL Survived, Others Didn't
Other immigrant sign languages (Moroccan, etc.) disappeared because:
- Fewer deaf in those communities
- No hearing signer population to preserve
- AJSL had critical mass of hearing users
