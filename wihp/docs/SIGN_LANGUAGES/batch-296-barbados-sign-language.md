# Barbados Sign Language
## Sign Language of Barbados (Bajan Sign)

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Barbados Sign Language |
| **Alternate Names** | Bajan Sign, ASL-Bajan |
| **Country** | Barbados |
| **Users** | ~2,900 deaf (estimates vary) |
| **Status** | ASL-based with local variants |
| **Language Family** | ASL variety with Bajan influence |

### Language Characteristics
- Primary base: American Sign Language (ASL)
- Blend of signs representing Bajan dialect and culture
- Village sign languages also exist
- Incorporates hands, facial expressions, gestures, body language
- Some lip reading component

### Historical Development
- **1957**: Barbados Association for the Blind and Deaf established by Parliament
- **1995**: New Life Deaf Club (NLDC) formed
- **2019**: Renamed to Barbados Horizon Deaf Charity (BHDC)

### Population Statistics
- ~2,900 deaf persons (Joshua Project estimate)
- No official government figures available
- BHDC has 200+ members including elderly and hearing loss

### Education
- **Irving Wilson School for the Blind and Deaf**
  - Only deaf educational institution (as of 2011)
  - Originally operated under different name
  - Sign language used in instruction

### Key Figures
- **Lionel Smith**: President of BHDC
- **Bonnie Leonce**: Experienced interpreter and advocate

### Community Challenges
- Limited educational access and certification
- Job acquisition difficulties
- Many deaf Barbadians migrate overseas (USA)
- Mask-wearing during COVID impeded communication
- Limited interpreter training standards
- Basic education levels upon leaving school

### Interpreting Services
- Provided by Barbados Council for Disabilities
- Sign language classes available
- Interpreters trained at various levels
- Some trained via US scholarships

### Cultural Initiatives
- **2022**: NCF released cultural content in ASL
  - All Aboard animation series (Barbados Landship)
  - Bush Tea documentary series
  - Barbados Teachers Training Manual
- Emergency responder training in sign language
- Partnership with Department of Emergency Management

### Regional Connections
- CARPHA (Caribbean Public Health Agency) COVID videos
- Deaf Pioneers Life Centre (Trinidad and Tobago)
- Caribbean Deaf News network
- OACPS and EU partnerships

### Accessibility Advocacy
- International Day of Sign Languages observance
- International Week of the Deaf participation
- Theme: Reaffirming Deaf Peoples Human Rights
- Building an Inclusive Community initiatives

### Communication Quote
> Sign Language is the language used by deaf people and is recognised like any other language in the world. In Barbados, the Deaf use American Sign Language (ASL) with a blend of signs representing Bajan dialect and culture.
> -- Lionel Smith, BHDC President
