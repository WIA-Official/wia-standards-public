# Ivorian Sign Language / Langue des Signes de Cote dIvoire (LSCI)

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |

| **ISO 639-3** | Not assigned |

| **Legal Status** | Not officially recognized (2025 advocacy ongoing) |

## Historical Development

### Pre-Colonial Era
- Indigenous sign languages existed before formal education
- Local deaf communities developed their own signs

### Andrew Foster Era (1974)
- First deaf school established in Abidjan (1974) by Andrew Foster
- American Sign Language (ASL) introduced as language of instruction
- ASL modified to accommodate French and local cultural concepts
- Over 1,000 deaf children educated since foundation

### Modern Era (2000s-present)
- LSAF (Langue des Signes de lAfrique Francophone) became dominant
- Indigenous LSCI marginalized by ASL/LSAF
- 2011: Project documentation des langues des signes de Cote dIvoire
- December 2025: Declaration of Dabou - advocacy for LSCI recognition
- FENASOCI (Federation Nationale des Sourds de Cote dIvoire) active

## Language Ecology

### Sign Languages in Use
1. **LSAF/ASL variant** - High prestige, used in education
2. **LSCI (local signs)** - Used by non-schooled deaf
3. **Bouakako Sign Language (LaSiBo)** - Village sign language

### Status
- LSAF associated with education and economic opportunities
- LSCI viewed negatively but attitudes changing
- World Federation of the Deaf promoting local sign language maintenance
