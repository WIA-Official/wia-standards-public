## Community Profile

### Village Setting
- Rural village in southern India
- Karnataka state
- High hereditary deafness rate
- Longstanding deaf population

### Deaf Distribution
- Multiple deaf families
- Mapped locations of deaf inhabitants
- Concentrated in specific areas of village
- Multi-generational presence

## Linguistic Features

### Independence from ISL
APSL is quite different from Indian Sign Language:
- Developed independently
- Not derived from urban ISL
- Unique vocabulary
- Different grammatical structures

### Contrast with Home Signs
Unlike ad-hoc home sign systems:
- Full language with grammar
- Community-wide use
- Intergenerational transmission
- Both deaf and hearing users

### Numeral System
Research on APSL numerals shows:
- Non-decimal bases (rare in sign languages)
- Subtractive sub-systems (rare in any language)
- Extends known typological range
- Cross-modal typology implications

## User Profile

### Deaf Signers
- Tend to be monolingual in APSL
- Minimal literacy (until recently)
- No access to formal education (historically)
- Primary language is APSL

### Hearing Signers
- Majority of villagers use APSL
- Varying proficiency levels
- Family members most fluent
- Community-wide basic competence

## Research History

### Key Researcher
**Sibaji Panda** - Deaf Indian researcher
- Native of India
- Conducted fieldwork in Alipur
- Part of international village SL project
- Published in Zeshan & de Vos (2012)

### Research Challenges
Initial community reaction:
- Concept of "research" was alien
- Presence of foreigner, cameras unfamiliar
- Video documentation new concept
- Computers and Internet unknown

### Impact of Deaf Researcher
- Deaf researcher made particular impact
- Built trust with local deaf people
- Communication through sign more effective
- Sometimes needed hearing interpreters for precise info

## Recent Developments

### Deaf School Project (2008)
- School established in village
- Children acquiring literacy in English
- APSL used as medium of instruction
- First formal education for deaf

### Television Channel
- Alipur has own TV channel
- Recently began broadcasting Alipur signs
- Community initiative
- Access to other channels restricted
