# Kosovar Sign Language (KSL / GjShK)
## Gjuha e Shenjave Kosovare

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Kosovar Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Gjuha e Shenjave Kosovare (GjShK) |
| **ISO 639-3** | Not assigned |
| **Country** | Kosovo |
| **Users** | ~5,000 deaf people; 9,800 D/HH estimated |
| **Status** | Officially recognized (2010, confirmed 2014) |
| **Language Family** | Yugoslav Sign Language family |

### Legal Recognition
- **September 29, 2010**: Government of Kosovo recognized KSL
- 146th regular government meeting
- Achieved through Group for Recognition of Sign Language
- Kosovar Association of the Deaf played key role
- **2014**: Further confirmed by government

### Historical Development
- **19th Century**: Deaf Kosovars studied in other Yugoslav schools
- **1950**: First deaf school opened in Mitrovica
- **1951**: Deaf school opened in Prizren (still operating)
- **Post-WWII**: Josip Colakic founded deaf education in Kosovo
- **2006**: Documentation project began with Finnish support
- **2012**: First KSL dictionary published (book and DVD)

### Josip Colakic - Father of Kosovo Deaf Community
- Born in Janjevo, Kosovo
- Himself deaf
- Created and managed first deaf schools
- Founded local deaf associations from 1955
- Established model for deaf community organization

### Linguistic Features
- Variant of Yugoslav Sign Language family
- Similar to other ex-Yugoslav sign languages
- 99% of deaf prefer sign language for communication
- 1% prefer combination of spoken and signed
- Natural language with own grammar and structure

### Documentation and Resources
- First dictionary produced 2012 (ShKSh and FAD collaboration)
- Electronic dictionary (gjshk.org) continuously updated
- Video resources available online
- Supported by Finnish Association of the Deaf
- Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Finland funding

### Organizations
- Kosovar Association of the Deaf (ShKSh)
- Schools for the Deaf in Prizren
- International partnerships with FAD (Finland)

### Education System
- Schools operate in Prizren and other locations
- Inclusive education guidelines (2019)
- Deaf education recognized in national framework
- Interpreter training programs developing
