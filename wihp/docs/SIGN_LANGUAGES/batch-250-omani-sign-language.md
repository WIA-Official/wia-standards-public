# Omani Sign Language / Lughat al-Ishara al-Umaniya

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Omani Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lughat al-Ishara al-Umaniya (لغة الإشارة العمانية) |
| **Region** | Sultanate of Oman |
| **Language Family** | Arab sign-language family |
| **Status** | Developing, not officially recognized |

## Historical Development

### Early History
- Sign language used by deaf population of Oman
- Passed from generation to generation within families
- Hearing family members often learn to improve communication
- High endogamy rates contributed to deaf community formation

### Modern Developments
- 2018: First International Day of Sign Languages celebrated
- Ministry of Social Development awareness campaigns
- Royal Oman Police provides sign interpreters to all units
- Al-Amal School for the Deaf provides education

### Technology Initiatives
- Sohar University research: AI translation system for OSL
- Neural networks and image processing for gesture recognition
- Sign Translation app - won Mada-ALECSO award (2023)
- 742+ visual content materials translated into sign language
- 90+ printed materials with QR-Code sign language access

## Linguistic Features

### Cultural Elements
- Reflects Omani culture and traditions
- Gestures rooted in Omani society
- Regional variations across Oman
- Signs mirror landscape, traditional attire, history

### Relationship to Other Languages
- Not clear if single language across country
- May have similarities with neighboring Gulf sign languages
- Authorities refer to Arabic Sign Language for training

## Education and Integration
- Al-Amal School for the Deaf - main educational institution
- Vocational guidance and skill training
- Integration programs with government and private institutions
- Higher education and labor market participation programs

## Accessibility Survey (2009)
- Oman scored 3 out of 5 points
- Has national deaf/hard of hearing organization
- Has sign language interpreter training
- Has interpreter service
- Lacks government funding for interpreters
- Lacks recognized interpreter profession

## Challenges
- No official legal recognition
- Limited documentation and research
- Public awareness gaps about deaf capabilities
- Scarcity of labor market opportunities
- Psychological and social challenges for deaf individuals

## Cultural Significance
- Family and community central to deaf individuals lives
- Families learn OSL to communicate with deaf relatives
- Growing inclusivity in schools, mosques, workplaces
- Emphasis on hospitality, cooperation, and respect
