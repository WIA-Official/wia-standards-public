## Legal Recognition

### 1995 Recognition
**May 4, 1995**: Government officially recognized LGK as native language of the deaf

### Law of Social Integration of the Disabled (1991)
- Based on Constitution of Lithuania
- Implements international rights
- States: "sign language is a native language of the deaf"

### National Programme (1997-2003)
**National Programme of Using Sign Language**:
- Ministry of Social Security and Labour
- Ministry of Education and Science
- Lithuanian Union of the Deaf
- 6-year implementation (1998-2003)

### Programme Goals
1. Establish effective sign language
2. Meet international standards
3. Secure interpretation services
4. Research demand for interpretation
5. Support sign language research
6. Nurture deaf culture and traditions

## Historical Development

### Timeline
| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| Pre-1945 | Very little known |
| 1945-1990 | Used alongside Russian SL |
| 1991 | Social Integration Law |
| 1995 | Official recognition (May 4) |
| 1995+ | Dictionary volumes published |
| 1996 | Linguistic research begins |
| 1997-2003 | National Programme |
| 2020 | COVID: First interpreter next to President |

### Soviet Era (1945-1990)
- Used with Russian Sign Language
- Two languages became very similar
- Russian lip-patterns in some LGK signs
- Signs borrowed from Russian SL

### Post-Independence (1990+)
- Contact with RSL almost cut off
- Developing as independent language
- International Sign influence
- Active differentiation from Russian

## Language Characteristics

### Signs and Meaning
- Signs denote NOTIONS (not letters or words)
- Like spoken words representing concepts
- Finger spelling for special terms
- Names, titles, new terms fingerspelled

### Calque Language
**Not true LGK**:
- Lithuanian shown in signs
- Uses spoken Lithuanian grammar
- Signs denote words directly
- Difficult for deaf to understand
- Used by late-deafened, hard of hearing

### True LGK Features
- Own grammar system
- Verb direction inflection
- Quantitative noun features
- Different from Lithuanian structure

## Documentation

### Dictionaries
- 5 volume dictionary published (1995+)
- ~3,000 signs included
- Traditional word-to-sign format
- Thematic vocabularies
- Bilingual texts (LGK-Lithuanian)

### Standardization Attempt
**"Vocabulary of Lithuanian sign language" (1995)**:
- Books 1-5
- Deaf community did not accept it
- Many signs did not consolidate
- Lesson: Cannot create standard artificially

- LGK database development

## Language Learning

### How Deaf Learn LGK
1. From deaf parents (if available)
2. From peers at school
3. From "brilliant" signers in groups
4. From friends in community

### Natural Leaders
- One person with excellent sign language
- Others absorb their signs
- Organic spread of vocabulary
