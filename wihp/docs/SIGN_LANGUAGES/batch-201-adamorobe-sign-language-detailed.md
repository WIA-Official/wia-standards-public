# Batch 201: Adamorobe Sign Language (AdaSL)

## Overview
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Local Name** | Mumu kasa (deaf language) |
| **Location** | Adamorobe village, Eastern Region, Ghana |
| **Distance from Accra** | 30-40 km north (Akuapem Hills) |
| **Village Population** | ~1,400 (2003) |
| **Deaf Users** | ~30 |
| **Hearing Users** | ~1,370 |
| **Age** | 200+ years (since ~1733) |
| **Status** | ENDANGERED |

## Unique Features

### Shared Sign Language
- Majority of users are HEARING
- Both deaf and hearing use AdaSL daily
- Full integration of deaf in village life
- Unlike national sign languages which are deaf-centered

### Historical Significance
- One of the oldest documented African village sign languages
- Earliest formal sign language in Africa (Miles, 2004)
- Used as early as 1733

### Hereditary Deafness
- Genetic mutation passed through generations
- Originally 2% deaf population (2001)
- Now 1.8% (decline due to marriage ban)

## The Marriage Ban

### 1975 Law
Chief Nana Kwaakwaa Asiampong instituted a ban:
- Deaf people CANNOT marry each other
- Goal: reduce hereditary deafness
- Still technically in effect

### Consequences
- Denial of marriage partners
- Denial to have children
- Stigmatization
- Divorces
- Unstable relationships
- Economic hardship
- Loneliness

### Ironic Effect
The law threatens the very culture that made deaf people valued members of society.

## Linguistic Features

### Unique Grammar
AdaSL differs from most sign languages:
- NO entity classifiers (motion/location type)
- Uses serial verb constructions instead
- Influenced by spoken Akan grammar
- Shared prosodic features with Bura Sign Language (Nigeria)

### Independence
- Unrelated to Ghanaian Sign Language (GSL)
- GSL is ASL-derived
- AdaSL developed independently
- Now contains some GSL loan signs

## Current Situation

### Language Shift
Children now attend boarding school in Mampong-Akuapem
- GSL is used at school
- GSL becoming primary language for children
- AdaSL command decreasing in younger generation

### Threats
1. GSL through education
2. Urbanization and migration
3. Marriage ban reducing deaf population
4. Death of elder fluent signers
5. Stone quarry attracting outsiders
6. No formal preservation initiatives

## Cultural Context

### Origin Myths
Several traditional stories explain deafness:
1. Punishment myth - deafness as divine punishment
2. Gift myth - positive characteristics with deafness
3. Totem myth - animals transformed into deaf children
4. Natural inheritance - passed through generations

### Deaf Identity in Adamorobe
- Hardworking
- Strong
- Unafraid
- Productive members of society
- Fully integrated in farming life

### Thursday Rest Day
Traditional custom:
- No farming on Thursdays
- Earth goddess rests
- Trading day for community

## Related Sign Languages

### Nanabin Sign Language
- Nearby village in Ghana
- Family with 3 generations of deafness
- 2 hours documented in AdaSL corpus

### Bura Sign Language (Nigeria)
- Kukurpu village, northeast Nigeria
- Some shared features with AdaSL
- Possibly from cultural gestures, not genetic relation
