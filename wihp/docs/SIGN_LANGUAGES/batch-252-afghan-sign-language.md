# Afghan Sign Language / AFSL

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Afghan Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Zaban-e Ishara-e Afghanistani (زبان اشاره افغانستانی) |
| **Alternative Names** | AFSL, Afghan Sign, Jalalabad Sign Language |
| **ISO 639-3** | afg |
| **Region** | Afghanistan (multiple provinces) |
| **Users** | ~34,700 signers; 394,000 deaf population |
| **Status** | Stable, Institutional |

## Historical Development

### Origins and Early History
- Originated from Deaf-community sign language of Jalalabad
- Standardization began mid-1990s
- 1989: Initial work in Peshawar (refugee context)
- 1999: Systematic sign collection started
- ASL influence from Holy Land Institute for the Deaf training

### Dictionary Development
- 2003: First dictionary published (2,000 signs)
- 2009: Expanded to 4,000 signs (February 2009)
- 2010 Goal: 6,000 signs target
- Sign for Afghanistan created by group deliberation (10 days)

### Modern Context
- 2018: Ministry of Education oversaw 10 Inclusive Education Schools
- 2021: Taliban return severely impacted deaf community
- Ongoing language development continues

## Geographic Distribution
Provinces with AFSL users:
- Kabul, Nangarhar (schools)
- Balkh, Bamyan, Faryab, Ghor
- Herat, Kandahar, Laghman
- Parwan, Takhar, Badakhshan

### Other City Sign Languages (may differ)
- Kabul, Mazar-e Sharif, Herat, Kandahar
- Connection to Jalalabad variety unclear

## Education System
- Schools in Kabul and Nangarhar provinces
- Primary and secondary education in AFSL
- 250+ students attend classes (5 days/week)
- Saturday volunteer training for families
- Inclusive Education Schools (2018: 10 schools)

## Challenges

### Current Crisis
- Taliban rule (since 2021) severely restricts women
- Deaf women banned from education and employment
- Women cannot leave house without male relative
- Prison-like conditions for disabled women

### Historical Challenges
- 40+ years of war increased deaf population
- Stigma and discrimination persistent
- Women viewed as unfit for marriage
- Social isolation and outcast status
- 69.7% of disabled live in rural areas

### Resource Issues
- Inconsistent donor support
- Transportation problems for students
- Discontinued lunch programs
- Short-term funding (6 months to 1 year)

## Disability Statistics
- 747,500 to 867,100 people with disabilities (2005-2006)
- 34,000 deaf or blind aged 7-18
- Over half of disabled in Central, Western, Southern regions
- 30.3% in cities, 69.7% in rural areas

## Linguistic Features
- Language isolate (not related to regional sign languages)
- Stable indigenous language
- First language for deaf of all ages
- Some ASL influence from refugee training

## Cultural Significance
- Represents tribal diversity of Afghanistan
- Multiple sign variations reflect regional identity
- Handshape for Afghanistan mirrors country shape
- Community-developed linguistic standards
