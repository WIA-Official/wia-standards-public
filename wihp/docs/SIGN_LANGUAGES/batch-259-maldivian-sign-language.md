# Maldivian Sign Language / MvSL

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Maldivian Sign Language |
| **Abbreviation** | MvSL |
| **Native Name** | Dhivehi Sign Language |
| **Region** | Republic of Maldives |
| **Users** | Deaf community of Maldives |
| **Dictionary Size** | ~650 words |
| **Status** | Developing, Spontaneously emerged |

## Historical Development

### Emergence (2000s)
- Developed largely spontaneously by deaf children
- Emerged in number of schools in Maldives
- Unique opportunity to study birth of a new language
- Of particular interest to linguists

### 2009 Dictionary Launch
- October 28, 2009: President Mohamed Nasheed launched dictionary
- Project leader: Amaresh Gopalakrishnan
- Supported by Jamaaluddin School
- 650 words with English and Dhivehi descriptions
- Hand-shape and movement explanations included

## Linguistic Significance
- Offers opportunity to study language emergence
- Spontaneous development by deaf children
- New language being born and documented
- Example of creolization and language genesis

## Dictionary Features
- ~650 signs documented
- English descriptions
- Dhivehi (ދިވެހި) descriptions
- Hand-shape illustrations
- Movement instructions

## Education
- Schools throughout Maldives teach MvSL
- Dictionary enables school and family communication
- President stressed importance for all students to learn

## Challenges

### Documented Issues
- Abuse of disabled people
- Deaf women mistaken for mentally ill
- Cases of exploitation and abuse in atolls
- Need for widespread awareness

### Development Needs
- Limited dictionary size (~650 words)
- Fewer signs than other South Asian languages
- Ongoing development required
