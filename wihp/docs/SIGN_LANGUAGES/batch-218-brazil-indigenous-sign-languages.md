## 2. Ka'apor Sign Language

### Basic Information
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Also Known As** | Urubu Sign Language, Urubu-Ka'apor SL |
| **Portuguese** | Língua de sinais caapor brasileira |
| **ISO 639-3** | uks |
| **Location** | Maranhão state |
| **Ethnic Group** | Ka'apor people |
| **Status** | EXTINCT (Ethnologue) |

### Note on Names
"Urubu" is considered pejorative - prefer "Ka'apor"

### Historical Documentation (1968)
**Jim Kakumasu** (linguist, SIL) observed:
- 7 deaf people in community of ~500
- Ratio: 1 in 75 (very high)
- Both deaf and hearing used the language
- Hearing children bilingual in spoken and signed

### Unique Linguistic Features

#### Word Order
- **OSV** (Object-Subject-Verb)
- Unusual among sign languages

#### Time Orientation
- **Past = in FRONT of signer**
- **Future = BEHIND signer**
- Opposite to most European sign languages!
- Similar to some spoken Aymara patterns

### Inter-Tribal Signing
- Other indigenous tribes also reported using sign
- Sign language pidgins used between tribes
- Regional signing network existed

### Current Status
- Listed as EXTINCT in Ethnologue
- Current state unknown
- Community changes over decades
- Need for verification research

## 3. Terena Sign Language (LTS)

### Basic Information
| Item | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Portuguese** | Língua Terena de Sinais (LTS) |
| **Location** | Terra Indígena Cachoeirinha |
| **State** | Mato Grosso do Sul |
| **Nearest City** | Miranda-MS |
| **Status** | ACTIVE (under research) |

### Terena People
| Item | Data |
|------|------|
| Total population | 28,845 (IBGE 2010) |
| Indigenous areas | 17 |
| States | Mato Grosso do Sul, São Paulo |

### Discovery and Research

#### Key Researcher
**Priscilla Alyne Sumaio Soares**
- Master's thesis (2014)
- PhD dissertation (2018)
- Documented LTS extensively

#### Research Questions
Initial goals:
1. Are these home signs or a language?
2. Is it autonomous or LIBRAS variety?
3. What is its grammatical structure?

#### Findings
- LTS is its own linguistic system
- Has own grammar
- Capable of expressing any thought
- NOT just a variety of LIBRAS

### Language Situation

#### Deaf Terena
- Found in Cachoeirinha Indigenous Land
- Four villages with deaf individuals
- Population: ~4,920 in the land

#### Bilingualism Pattern
| Context | Language |
|---------|----------|
| Village with relatives | LTS |
| Village with other deaf | LTS |
| School in city | LIBRAS |
| With hearing family | LTS |

#### LIBRAS Contact
- Young deaf studying in city learn LIBRAS
- But use LTS when returning to village
- Code-switching observed
- LTS remains primary home language

### Linguistic Features

#### Phonological Parameters
Same parameters as LIBRAS:
- Handshape
- Movement
- Location
- Orientation
- Non-manual expression

#### But distinct signs and grammar
- LTS signs differ from LIBRAS
- Own lexicon
- Own grammatical structures

### Documentation Status
- Photography and video collected
- Master's and PhD research
- Comparative studies ongoing
- Dictionary development needed

## Indigenous Sign Language Research in Brazil

### Historical Context
- First research: Kakumasu (1968) on Ka'apor
- Long gap in research
- Recent revival with Soares' work on Terena
- Growing interest in indigenous sign languages

### Challenges
1. Remote indigenous territories
2. LIBRAS dominance in education
3. Limited funding
4. Need for community partnerships
5. Documentation before language shift

### Significance
- Cultural preservation
- Linguistic diversity
- Rights of indigenous deaf
- Understanding sign language emergence
