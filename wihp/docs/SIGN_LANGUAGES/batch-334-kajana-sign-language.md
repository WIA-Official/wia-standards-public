### Community Background
- **Saramaccan Maroons**: Descendants of escaped African slaves (17th-18th century)
- **Kadosi-nengre**: Village self-name (from plantation owner Cardoso)
- **Cultural traditions**: 18th century West African traditions preserved
- **Isolation**: Very remote, accessible only by small aircraft and canoe

### Saramaccan Context
The Saramaccans are one of six Maroon peoples in Suriname:
- **Population**: ~24,000 speakers of Saramaccan language
- **History**: Escaped slavery, signed treaty with Dutch (1762)
- **Territory**: Upper Suriname River and tributaries
- **Cultural preservation**: Strong oral traditions

### Language Situation in Suriname

#### Official Languages
- **Dutch**: Official government/education language
- **Surinamese Sign Language (SSL)**: Official deaf community language

#### Maroon Languages
- **Saramaccan**: Saramaka and Matawai tribes
- **Ndjuka (Ndyuka)**: Djuka, Aluku, Paramaka tribes
- Both are creole languages with African, English, Portuguese, Dutch elements

### Kajana Sign Language Status
- Listed as village sign language by Wikipedia
- Limited to three families in village
- Relationship to SSL (Surinamese Sign Language) unknown
- No detailed linguistic documentation available
- Part of Caribbean indigenous sign language heritage
