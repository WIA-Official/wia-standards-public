# Dominica Sign Language
## Sign Language of the Commonwealth of Dominica

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary Language** | American Sign Language (ASL) |
| **Country** | Commonwealth of Dominica |
| **Deaf Population** | 75-320 estimated (2011) |
| **CRPD Status** | Signed 2007, Ratified |
| **Status** | ASL-dominant |

### Important Note
**NOT to be confused with Dominican Republic Sign Language (LSD)**

This document covers the island nation of **Dominica** (Commonwealth of Dominica), a separate country from the **Dominican Republic**.

### Language Situation
- American Sign Language (ASL) most common
- Other sign languages either unknown or unofficial
- Home signs and gestures used by older deaf (40+)
- No documented indigenous Dominican sign language

### Geographic Context
- Part of Windward Islands, Caribbean
- Located between Guadeloupe (north) and Martinique (south)
- Eastern Caribbean island nation
- Different from Dominican Republic (shares Hispaniola with Haiti)

### Demographics
- Small deaf population (75-320 estimated)
- General population: ~72,000
- English: Official language
- Dominican Creole French also spoken

### Organizations
- **Dominica Association of Disabled Persons (DADP)**
  - Established: 1983
  - Provides sign language training
  - Communication training programs
  - Youth employment assistance
  - Independent living preparation
  - Funded by grants, donations, government subsidies
  - 400+ members

### Healthcare Challenges
- Lacked early hearing screenings (2020 study)
- Little data reported
- Healthcare communication barriers
- Limited audiology services

### Legal Framework
- **CRPD**: Signed 2007, Ratified
- Optional protocol ratified
- Among first Windward Islands to sign CRPD
- Disability rights protections

### Age-Based Language Use
| Age Group | Primary Communication |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Under 40 | American Sign Language |
| Over 40 | Home signs, gestures |

### Regional Context
- Part of Caribbean deaf community network
- Regional services from Trinidad organizations
- CARPHA health initiatives
- Caribbean Sign Language Centre access

### Research Coverage
- SIL International profiles
- SAGE Deaf Studies Encyclopedia (Caribbean chapter)
- Limited specific documentation
- Included in regional Caribbean studies

### Challenges
- Very small deaf population
- Limited local services
- No deaf school on island
- Older generation isolated with home signs
- Need for interpreter development
