# Bosnian Sign Language
## Bosanski znakovni jezik

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Bosnian Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Bosanski znakovni jezik |
| **ISO 639-3** | Not assigned (part of Yugoslav SL family) |
| **Country** | Bosnia and Herzegovina |
| **Users** | Unknown (significant deaf community) |
| **Status** | Officially recognized (2009) |
| **Language Family** | Yugoslav Sign Language family |

### Legal Recognition (2009)
- Sign language recognized as official language
- Deaf have same language rights as hearing with oral language
- Interpreters must be provided for government dealings
- Government TV broadcasts translated into sign language
- Commission for Sign Language established

### Legal Framework
- Three-nation commission representation:
  - Education representatives
  - Linguistics/pedagogy experts
  - Representatives from Bosniak, Croat, Serb communities

### Historical Development
- Part of Yugoslav Sign Language family
- Schools for deaf established in Yugoslav era
- Post-war development as distinct variety
- Active research since 2000s at University of Tuzla

### Research Publications
- 1986: Zimmerman identified 27 hand shapes
- 2002: Dictionary for the Deaf (Tuzla)
- 2005-2007: Govorite li znakovni? publications
- 2008: Masters thesis on Bosnian Sign Language formation
- 2013-2014: 14 new hand shapes discovered
- 425 signs analyzed in linguistic study

### Linguistic Features
- Two-handed manual alphabet in widespread use
- One-handed alphabet (American-based) has official status
- Mutually intelligible with Croatian and Serbian SL
- Dialectical distinctions remain
- 41 total hand shapes identified (27 original + 14 new)

### Organizations
- SUGOSG BiH (Union of Deaf and Hard of Hearing)
- Center for Sign Language Interpreters (Sarajevo)
- University of Tuzla - Faculty of Education and Rehabilitation

### Education and Research
- Master thesis programs on sign language
- Ongoing standardization efforts needed
- Dictionary development continues
- Interpreter training centers active

### Challenges
- Need for comprehensive standardization
- Limited systematic documentation
- Cross-entity coordination required
- Distinct from but related to neighboring SLs
