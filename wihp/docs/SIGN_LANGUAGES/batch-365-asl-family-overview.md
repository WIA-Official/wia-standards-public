### Introduction
The American Sign Language family is arguably the most widely spread sign language family in the modern world. While ASL itself is descended from French Sign Language (LSF), ASL has become a major source language influencing deaf education and sign language development across the globe.

Through American deaf education exports, missionary work, international development programs, and media influence, ASL has shaped sign languages on every inhabited continent.

### The Fingerspelling Factor
ASL's one-handed alphabet:
- Easy to learn
- Different from BSL family (two-handed)
- Spread with ASL education
- Adapted to local orthographies
- Some countries created alternatives
