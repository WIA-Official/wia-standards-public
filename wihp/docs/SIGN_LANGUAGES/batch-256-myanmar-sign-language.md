# Myanmar Sign Language / Burmese Sign Language

## Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Official Name** | Myanmar Sign Language |
| **Alternative Names** | Burmese Sign Language, Yangon Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | မြန်မာလက်သင်္ကေတမြဘာသာစကား |
| **ISO 639-3** | ysm |
| **Region** | Myanmar (Burma) |
| **Deaf Population** | ~2.7 million |
| **Users** | ~270,000 |
| **Status** | Stable, Institutional, Incipient national standard |

## Important Note
It is unclear if there are ONE or TWO sign languages in Myanmar. Yangon and Mandalay sign languages are different, but their relationship is undetermined.

## Historical Development

### Early History (1904-1964)
- 1904: Mary Chapman School for the Deaf established (Yangon)
  - Named after Methodist missionary
  - Oldest deaf school in Myanmar
- 1964: School for the Deaf, Mandalay established
- 2005: Immanuel School for the Deaf (Kalay) established

### Modern Standardization (2007-2010)
- 2007: Myanmar Sign Language Conversation Book published
  - Ministry of Social Welfare collaboration
  - Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) support
- 2010: Government project to establish national sign language
  - Japanese Federation of the Deaf assistance
  - Goal: unified national standard

## Educational Approach
- Oral instruction in Burmese language
- Sign used to support oral education
- Different sign systems between cities

## Language Influences
Multiple foreign sign language influences documented:
- American Sign Language (all schools)
- Korean Sign Language
- Australian Sign Language (Auslan)
- Thai Sign Language
- Possible local indigenous substratum

## Manual Alphabets
Two fingerspelling systems used in Yangon:
1. American Manual Alphabet (limited use)
2. Burmese-based alphabet (taught 1970s-1980s)

## Linguistic Features

### Structure
- Different grammatical structure from spoken Burmese
- Manual components: handshapes, position, movements
- Non-manual components included
- 33 alphabets (Ka to Ah)
- 11 numbers (0 to 10)

### Regional Variation
- Yangon Sign Language
- Mandalay Sign Language
- Mutual intelligibility status unknown
