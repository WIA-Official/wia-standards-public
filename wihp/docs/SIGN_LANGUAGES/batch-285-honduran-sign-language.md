# Honduran Sign Language (LESHO)
## Lengua de Senas Hondurenas

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Honduran Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lengua de Senas Hondurenas (LESHO) |
| **ISO 639-3** | hds |
| **Country** | Honduras |
| **Users** | 30,000-60,000 (2021: ~40,000) |
| **Status** | Developing (Recognized 2013) |
| **Language Family** | Independent; not related to ASL |

### Legal Recognition (2013)
- **Decreto No. 321-2013**: Official recognition
- Recognized as communication means for deaf and general population
- Provides for use in deaf education
- Mandates interpreter training
- Requires interpreters for official government communications

### Historical Development
- **Pre-1980s**: Deaf isolated, no unified language
- **1980s**: National Deaf Association established
- **1980s**: Deaf ministries began organizing community
- Community language developed distinct from ASL
- ASL used historically but LESHO evolved independently

### Geographic Distribution
- **Major hubs**: Tegucigalpa, San Pedro Sula, La Ceiba
- **Departments**: Comayagua, Francisco Morazan, Intibuca, La Paz, Lempira, Valle
- Rural deaf often isolated using home signs

### Language Acquisition Challenges
- Many families dont know LESHO
- Rural isolation prevents community access
- Deaf often considered mentally weak
- Limited interpreter availability
- Language development started relatively recently

### Research and Documentation
- **2009-2010**: SIL Sociolinguistic Survey (Holly Williams)
- **2017**: Spanish version published
- Multiple wordlist studies (2009)
- Variety documentation ongoing

### Organizations and Programs
- National Deaf Association of Honduras
- ABC Hearing (La Entrada, Copan) - hearing aids
- Signs of Love ministry
- IFMSA-Honduras LESHO courses for medical students
- Universidad Nacional Autonoma de Honduras (UNAH)

### Healthcare Access Initiative
- LESHO courses for health career students
- Training at UNAH Faculty of Medical Sciences
- Goal: Reduce language barriers in healthcare
- Targets: Medicine, nursing, dentistry, pharmacy students

### Other Sign Languages in Honduras
- **Bay Islands Sign Language (BISL)**: Village SL in French Harbour
- High incidence of Usher syndrome on Roatan Island
- Spread to Guanaja island
