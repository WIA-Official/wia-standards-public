### Language Situation
- French Sign Language (LSF) primary sign language
- Part of French national deaf education system
- LSF recognized as language in France (2005)
- Part of SIL 24-location Caribbean survey
- French overseas territory, same rights as mainland

### French Sign Language (LSF)
- ~100,000 native signers in France
- One-handed manual alphabet
- Developed in 18th century Paris
- Abbé Charles-Michel de lÉpée: Key figure
- First public school for deaf (1755/1760)
- Related to ASL, NGT, RSL, LSQ

### LSF Legal Recognition
- **1991 Fabius Law**: LSF authorized for deaf education
- **2005 Law**: LSF fully recognized as language
- Late 1970s: Deaf community activism began
- Milan Congress (1880) had suppressed sign language

### Geographic Context
- French Overseas Region in Caribbean
- Part of Lesser Antilles (Eastern Caribbean)
- Southeast of Puerto Rico, north of Dominica
- Two main islands: Basse-Terre and Grande-Terre
- Butterfly Island (butterfly shape)
- Annexed to Kingdom of France 1674

### Official Languages
- **French** - Official language
- **Guadeloupean Creole** - Widely spoken (~430,000 speakers)
  - French-based with English, Bantu, Amerindian vocabulary
  - Also called Patwa, Patois, Kreyol
  - Mutually intelligible with Martinique, Haiti Creoles
  - Now taught in schools

### Cultural Context
- French, African, Indian, Caribbean influences
- Gwo ka music (percussion, call-and-response)
- Zouk music genre
- Carnival major celebration
- Rich Creole identity maintained

### Regional Sign Language Context
Nine sign languages in Caribbean (SIL survey):
1. American Sign Language (ASL)
2. British Sign Language (BSL)
3. Danish Sign Language
4. Dutch Sign Language (NGT)
5. **French Sign Language (LSF)**
6. Guyanese Sign Language
7. Kajana Sign Language
8. Quebec Sign Language (LSQ)
9. Surinamese Sign Language

### LSF Family Languages
LSF is related to/ancestral to:
- American Sign Language (ASL)
- Quebec Sign Language (LSQ)
- Dutch Sign Language (NGT)
- Belgian-French Sign Language (LSFB)
- Irish Sign Language (ISL)
- Brazilian Sign Language (Libras)
- Russian Sign Language (RSL)
