### Introduction
Signed Exact English (SEE, specifically SEE-2) is an artificial sign system designed to represent English grammar manually. Unlike American Sign Language (ASL), which is a natural language with its own grammar, SEE was created to help deaf children learn English by matching signs exactly to English words and grammar.

SEE is one of several Manually Coded English (MCE) systems and has been controversial in deaf education.

### How SEE Works
| Feature | English Representation |
|---------|----------------------|
| Word order | English word order |
| Affixes | Signs for -ed, -ing, -tion |
| Articles | Signs for a, an, the |
| Pronouns | English pronoun system |
| Be verbs | Signed explicitly |

### SEE vs. ASL Comparison
| Feature | SEE | ASL |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Grammar | English | ASL (unique) |
| Word order | English SVO | Flexible/OSV |
| Affixes | Added signs | Incorporated |
| Articles | Included | Generally absent |
| Be verbs | Signed | Not needed |
| Naturalness | Artificial | Natural language |

### Example: "The boy is walking to school"
| System | Signed |
|--------|--------|
| SEE | THE BOY IS WALK-ING TO SCHOOL |
| ASL | BOY WALK SCHOOL (spatial) |

### Criticism of SEE
| Criticism | Issue |
|----------|-------|
| Unnatural | Violates visual language principles |
| Slow | Takes longer than ASL |
| Deaf community | Not their natural language |
| Research | Limited evidence of effectiveness |
| Identity | May separate from deaf culture |

### Deaf Community Perspective
| Concern | Position |
|---------|----------|
| Not a real language | Artificial construction |
| Cultural imposition | Hearing values imposed |
| ASL devalued | Implies ASL is inadequate |
| Child development | May delay natural acquisition |
| Identity | Connection to deaf community |
