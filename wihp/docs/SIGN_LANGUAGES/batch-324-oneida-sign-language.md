# Oneida Sign Language (OSL)
## Indigenous Sign Language of Oneida Nation

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Abbreviation** | OSL |
| **Country** | Canada (Ontario), USA (New York, Wisconsin, Michigan) |
| **Community** | Oneida Nation of the Thames |
| **Language Family** | Northeast Hand Talk branch (PISL related) |
| **Status** | Newly developed/revitalized |
| **Signs Developed** | 200+ |

### Language Description
- Recently developed sign language for Oneida Nation
- Combines Plains Indian Sign Language (Hand Talk) elements
- Connected to spoken Oneida language
- More literal and gesture-based than ASL
- Emphasis on Oneida cultural concepts
- Used by deaf and hearing community members

### Geographic Distribution
- **Primary**: Oneida Nation of the Thames (near London, Ontario)
- **Extended**: New York, Michigan, Wisconsin (Oneida communities)
- Oneida Language & Cultural Centre: 2207 Elm Ave, Southwold, Ontario

### Development History
- Spearheaded by **Marsha Ireland** (deaf Elder, Turtle Clan)
- Supported by **Max Ireland** (hearing husband)
- Pilot project at Oneida Language and Cultural Centre
- Uses 6,000-word Oneida dictionary as reference
- Leans toward Plains Indian method/Hand Talk

### Project Modules
Five complete modules developed:
1. Introducing myself
2. My family
3. Thanksgiving Address
4. Office phrases
5. Council phrases

### Linguistic Features
- More literal and gesture-based than ASL
- Cultural emphasis in signs
- Example: celebration sign includes:
  - Fire gesture
  - People gesture
  - Talk Creator gesture
- Connected to spoken Oneida language structure

### Relationship to Other Languages
- **Plains Indian Sign Language**: Primary influence (400+ year history)
- **American Sign Language**: Distinct from ASL
- **Spoken Oneida**: Connected through sign development
- Part of **Northeast Hand Talk** regional family

### Community Use
- Approximately two dozen deaf users from First Nation
- Taught to community members (deaf and hearing)
- Used in community meetings
- Interpreter services provided (Debbie Parliament)

### Training Program
- Five community individuals trained as OSL interpreters
- YouTube video resources available
- Classes offered by Marsha Ireland
