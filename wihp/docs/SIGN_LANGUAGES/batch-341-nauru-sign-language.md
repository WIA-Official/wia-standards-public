### Language Description
Nauru Sign Language refers to whatever sign communication system exists among deaf individuals on the island nation of Nauru, the world's third smallest country by area (21 km²) and smallest republic by population (~10,000).

Due to Nauru's extremely small population and geographic isolation, there is virtually no documentation of sign language use on the island. Deaf individuals likely use home signs or have been educated abroad (primarily in Australia), potentially bringing back Auslan (Australian Sign Language) influence.

The linguistic situation for deaf Nauruans is largely undocumented and represents a significant gap in global sign language research.

### Key Characteristics
- **Undocumented**: No formal linguistic description
- **Extremely Small**: Perhaps world's smallest deaf "community"
- **Australian Connection**: Historical ties may bring Auslan influence
- **Home Signs**: Likely predominant communication mode
- **Isolation**: Geographic remoteness limits contact

### Deaf Community
With a total population of approximately 10,000:
- Estimated deaf population: 20-50 individuals
- No organized deaf community documented
- Individuals likely scattered across small island
- Family-based support systems probable

### Education
- **Local**: Very limited special education
- **Overseas**: Some deaf individuals may study in Australia
- **Resources**: Extremely scarce
- **Teachers**: No known specialized deaf educators

### Healthcare Context
Nauru faces significant health challenges:
- High diabetes rates may cause acquired deafness
- Limited medical facilities
- Australian support for medical needs
- Evacuation to Australia for serious conditions

### Research Priority
Nauru represents one of the world's least documented linguistic situations for deaf people. Research needs include:
- Basic demographic survey of deaf population
- Documentation of any existing signs
- Study of home sign systems
- Historical research on deaf Nauruans

### Regional Context
As part of Micronesia, Nauru's deaf situation may relate to:
- Other Micronesian island deaf communities
- Pacific regional deaf networks
- Australian influence throughout region
