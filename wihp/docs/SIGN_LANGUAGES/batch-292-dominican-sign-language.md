# Dominican Sign Language (LSD)
## Lengua de Senas Dominicana

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Dominican Sign Language |
| **Native Name** | Lengua de Senas Dominicana (LSD) |
| **ISO 639-3** | doq |
| **Country** | Dominican Republic |
| **Users** | ~18,000 estimated |
| **Status** | Stable; Recognized (2023) |
| **Language Family** | ASL-related with French SL origins |

### Legal Recognition (2023)
- **Law No. 43-23**: Official recognition
- 12-year advocacy effort by deaf community
- Separate from disability law 5-13
- Recognizes deaf identity and sign language needs
- WFD and DDW supported the process

### Historical Development
- French Sign Language introduced by missionaries
- Combined with local gesture traditions
- ASL influence through American contacts
- Dominican man studied in New York, promoted ASL
- ~70% overlap with ASL reported

### Language Characteristics
- Own unique grammar and lexicon
- Spanish initialization (e.g. WATER with A for agua)
- Distinct from American Sign Language
- Vocabulary from other Spanish-speaking countries
- Indigenous signs being reclaimed

### Community Attitudes
- Moving toward distinguishing LSD from ASL
- Indigenous signs previously called wrong
- Shift toward valuing Dominican-specific signs
- Quote: We really want a Dominican Republic sign language that is separate from ASL

### Education System
- Deaf schools are primary acquisition sites
- Mix of LSD, ASL, and Signed English used
- Schools located Santo Domingo to Puerto Plata
- Deaf clubs and associations active

### Dictionary Development
- **November 2023**: Official Dictionary launched
- Introduced at Autonomous University of Santo Domingo
- Spearheaded by Conadis (National Disability Council)
- Collaborative effort among institutions
- Distributed to universities, inclusive schools
