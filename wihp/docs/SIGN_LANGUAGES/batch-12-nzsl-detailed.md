# WIA Talk Sign Language Mapping - NZSL (New Zealand Sign Language / Te Reo Rotarota)
## Batch 12: 20 Signs with Full WIA Talk Component Analysis

**Language:** NZSL - New Zealand Sign Language (Māori: Te Reo Rotarota o Aotearoa)
**Region:** New Zealand (Aotearoa)
**Estimated Users:** 20,000+ (2013 Census)
**Language Family:** BANZSL (British-Australian-New Zealand Sign Language)
**Recognition:** Official language since April 2006 (NZSL Act 2006)

---

## NZSL Characteristics

### Historical Background
- 1868-1877: Dorcas Mitchell taught sign language (Charteris Bay) - 42 pupils
- 1878: Sumner Deaf and Dumb Institution opened (first deaf school)
- 1880: Milan Congress banned sign language in deaf education
- 1878-1979: Sign language forbidden in schools (100+ years!)
- Children developed NZSL secretly, largely without adult intervention
- 1979: "Total Communication" adopted (but with artificial Australasian Signed English)
- 1985: Dr. Marianne Ahlgren proved NZSL is fully-fledged language (PhD, Victoria University)
- 1992: NZSL Teachers Association (NZSLTA) established
- 1994: NZSL adopted for teaching
- 1998: First comprehensive NZSL dictionary (4,000 signs)
- 2006: **NZSL became official language of New Zealand** (April 11)

### BANZSL Family
NZSL is part of the BANZSL family:
- **BSL** - British Sign Language (ancestor)
- **Auslan** - Australian Sign Language (sibling)
- **NZSL** - New Zealand Sign Language

**Lexical similarity:**
- 62.5% with BSL
- 33% with ASL (American Sign Language)

### Official Language Status
- **2006:** NZSL Act passed (119-2 votes)
- **Three official languages of NZ:** English, Te Reo Māori, NZSL
- Purpose: Rights in legal system, government access for Deaf community

### Key Features
- **Two-handed manual alphabet** (same as BSL and Auslan)
- **More lip-patterns** than BSL (oralist education history)
- **Māori concepts included:** marae, tangi, kia ora
- **NZ placenames:** Rotorua (mudpools), Christchurch (ChCh - 2 Cs)
- **5 elements make up each sign:** Hand shape, location, movement, orientation, facial expression
- **"Old Sign" vs "New Sign":** Traditional NZSL vs Australasian-influenced modern NZSL
- **Regional variations:** Different areas, different signing styles
- **Two variations for number 9** in teaching materials

### Cultural Features
- **Name signs:** Given by Deaf community (not appropriate to self-assign)
- **Waving hands:** Applause in Deaf culture
- **Eyebrows as question marks:** Raised = yes/no, Lowered = wh-questions
- **Furrowed eyebrows:** May be misunderstood by hearing people
- **Māori Deaf culture:** Work ongoing to create signs for Te Reo concepts

### Organizations
- **Deaf Aotearoa** - National organization
- **NZSLTA** - NZ Sign Language Teachers Association
- **NFDHH** - National Foundation for Deaf and Hard of Hearing
- **Victoria University Wellington** - Deaf Studies programme

### Resources
- **NZSL Online** - nzsl.nz (4,500+ dictionary entries)
- **Learn NZSL** - Free learning portal
- **Thumbs Up!** - NZ curriculum resource
- **Sign Ninja** - Interactive learning game

### 1. HELLO (Kia Ora)

**NZSL Concept:** Greeting (includes Māori concept)

**Detailed Motion:**
- Wave hand in greeting
- Or: Specific NZSL greeting sign
- Friendly facial expression
- Eye contact important

**WIA Talk Code:** `HS02-LC09-MV06-OR01-FE02`

| Component | Code | Description |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| HS | 02 | Flat open hand |
| LC | 09 | Neutral space |
| MV | 06 | Wave |
| OR | 01 | Palm forward |

## SECTION B: Numbers 1-10

### NZSL Number System Overview

NZSL numbers have notable characteristics:
- **Regional and generational variation** exists
- **Auslan numbers introduced 1979** (with Australasian Signed English)
- **"Old" vs "New" number signs** - traditional vs modern
- **Two variations for number 9** recognized in teaching materials
- Numbers can be modified for:
  - Quantity (1-5, sometimes 1-9)
  - Time expressions
  - Age expressions

**Source:** nzsl.nz/numbers, nzsl.tki.org.nz

## NZSL Linguistic Features Summary

### Grammar
- **4D language:** Uses whole body, space around body, non-manual signs
- **Classifiers:** Handshapes represent objects, movements represent actions
- **Eyebrow grammar:**
  - Raised eyebrows = yes/no questions
  - Lowered eyebrows = wh-questions (what, where, when, how)
- **No written form:** Recorded on video

### 5 Elements of NZSL Signs
1. Hand shape
2. Location
3. Movement
4. Hand/finger orientation
5. Facial expression and lip-patterns

### Cultural Features
- **Name signs:** Given by Deaf community (culturally inappropriate to self-assign)
- **Applause:** Waving hands (not clapping)
- **Deaf Clubs:** Historical havens for NZSL during oral-only period
- **Māori concepts:** Signs for marae, tangi, etc.
- **Regional variations:** Different areas, different styles

### Māori Connection
- Patrick Wikiriwhi Thompson - Māori Deaf activist
- Stephanie Awheto - Creates culturally meaningful Māori signs
- Ongoing work to integrate Te Reo Māori into NZSL
- "Kia ora" sign reflects blessing concept (not just "hello")

### Related Sign Languages
- **BSL** - British Sign Language (62.5% similarity)
- **Auslan** - Australian Sign Language (BANZSL sibling)
- **ASL** - American Sign Language (33% similarity - different family)
