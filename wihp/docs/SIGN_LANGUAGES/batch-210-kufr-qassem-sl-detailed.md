## Community Context

### Palestinian-Arab Town
- Located 20 km northeast of Tel Aviv
- Existed for hundreds of years
- Southern Triangle area, Central Israel
- Arabic-speaking population

### Origin Story
KQSL emerged when:
- Deaf woman from Negev area
- Married hearing man from Kufr Qassem
- Had deaf children together
- Deafness gene passed down generations

## Historical Isolation

### Military Rule Period (1948-1966)
- Israeli military imposed restrictions on Palestinian citizens
- People could NOT leave hometowns without permit
- Movement between towns severely limited
- This isolation helped KQSL develop independently

### Result of Isolation
- KQSL emerged without ISL influence
- Unique vocabulary developed
- Distinct grammatical structures
- Shared signing community formed

## Deaf Population Growth

| Era | Deaf Population |
|-----|-----------------|
| 1960s | ~12 |
| 1970s | ~30 |
| Today | ~120 |

### Four Generations
1. **1st Generation (1934-1959)**: No school, worked in agriculture/sewing
2. **2nd Generation**: Some education, KQSL dominant
3. **3rd Generation**: ISL introduced in school (1985)
4. **4th Generation**: Bilingual KQSL-ISL

## Education History

### 1979: Deaf Class Opened
- Local school in Kufr Qassem
- First formal education for deaf

### 1985: ISL Introduction
- ISL-competent teacher joined class
- Beginning of ISL contact
- Bilingualism emerged

## Language Features

### Shared Sign Language
- Used by deaf AND hearing community members
- Both populations sign with varying proficiency
- Historical integration of deaf in community

### Linguistic Research Areas
- Word order and sentence structure
- Use of space
- Mouthing patterns
- Code-switching with ISL

### Dictionary
- KQSL Dictionary published (2013)
- Documents distinct vocabulary
- Shows differences from ISL

## Language Contact Situation

### Two Sign Languages in Contact
| Feature | KQSL | ISL |
|---------|------|-----|
| Origin | Local, 1930s | National, 1930s |
| Users | 120 deaf in town | 10,000+ nationwide |
| Status | Local minority | Dominant |

### Contact Phenomena
- Code-switching between KQSL and ISL
- Lexical borrowing from ISL
- ~15% ISL signs in KQSL speech (average)
- Younger generations more bilingual

### Interesting Finding: The "Wife" Mystery
Researchers found:
- KQSL monolinguals produce: GIRL(ISL) + GIRL(KQSL)
- NOT code-switching
- Actually a compound meaning "wife"
- Shows ISL sign borrowed into KQSL compound

## Mouthing Patterns

### Multilingual Context
KQSL community in contact with:
- KQSL (local sign)
- ISL (national sign)
- Hebrew (spoken)
- Palestinian Arabic (spoken)
- Modern Standard Arabic (written)

### Findings
- More mouthing with ISL interlocutor
- More mouthing with ISL signs
- Mouthing in Palestinian Arabic (not MSA)
- Diachronic changes reflect education/mobility changes

## Current Threats

### Language Shift Evidence
- Younger generations prefer ISL
- Third/fourth generations exposed to ISL through:
  - School
  - Wider deaf community
  - Medical services
  - Sports
  - Legal/interpreting services
  - Social media

### Mergence Risk
- KQSL facing "mergence" with ISL
- May not remain distinct language
- Younger signers increasingly bilingual
