# Belizean Sign Language
## Sign Language of Belize

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Belizean Sign Language |
| **Primary Base** | American Sign Language (ASL) |
| **Country** | Belize |
| **Deaf Population** | ~13,000-15,000 estimated |
| **Status** | ASL variety with regional variation |
| **Introduction** | 1958 |

### Language Situation
- ASL used by educated deaf community
- High comprehension of US/Canada ASL varieties
- Some regional variation unique to Belize
- Some prefer term Belizean Sign Language
- Still considered ASL variety
- No conventionalized sign language before 1958

### Historical Development
- **1958**: Deaf education and ASL introduced
- No prior conventionalized sign language
- ASL brought through educational programs
- Sustained contact with US/Canada ASL

### Organizations
- **Belize National Association of the Deaf (BNAD)**
- **Belize Association of the Deaf**
- **Inter-American Confederation for Deaf Professionals**
- Deaf associations have closed in recent years

### 2024 Conference
- **First Inter-American Sign Language Linguistics Conference**
- Hosted in Belize (first time)
- Participants from Central/South America and Caribbean
- Organized by Inter-American Confederation for Deaf Professionals
- Organized by Svetlana Romero (Belizean, deaf)
- Day after International Day of Sign Languages

### Key Figures
- **Svetlana Romero**: Deaf Belizean organizer

### Religious Organizations
- **Jesus Deaf Church Life Changing Ministries** (Orange Walk Town)
- Belize Evangelical Mennonite Church support
- Various church-based deaf services

### Challenges
- Closure of deaf associations
- Loss of key deaf community leaders
- No central meeting place
- No advocacy center
- Rural areas lack educational resources
- Northern and southern areas underserved
- Family members often dont know sign language

### Development Needs
- Further education options
- Interpreter training programs
- Employment opportunities
- Greater social access
- Rural area services

### Geographic Context
- Central Americas only English-speaking country
- English: Official language
- Spanish: 57% speak
- Belizean Creole (Kriol): 45% speak
- Former British Honduras
- Population: ~400,000

### Languages in Belize
- English (official)
- Spanish
- Kriol (Belizean Creole)
- Qeqchi (Kekchi)
- Mopan Maya
- Yucatec Maya

### Regional Connections
- Contact with US/Canada ASL communities
- Inter-American deaf professional networks
- Central American sign language connections
