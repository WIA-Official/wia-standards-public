# Grenada Sign Language
## Sign Language of Grenada

### Overview
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Official Name** | Grenadian Sign Language |
| **Primary Language** | American Sign Language (ASL) |
| **Country** | Grenada |
| **Status** | ASL-based, developing |
| **CRPD Status** | Signed 2010, Ratified |

### Language Situation
- American Sign Language (ASL) is primary sign language used
- Listed among Languages of Grenada (Wikipedia)
- No distinct documented Grenadian sign language
- Possible village sign languages exist (undocumented)
- Caribbean regional sign language influences

### Geographic Context
- Island nation in far south Caribbean Sea
- Near Trinidad and Tobago
- Part of Windward Islands
- Independence from Britain in 1974
- 82% African descent population

### Deaf Voices Grenada
- Non-profit organization
- Mission: Improve living standards of deaf population
- Contact: deafvoices.sign@gmail.com
- Facebook: facebook.com/deafvoicesgrenada

### Healthcare Access
- Primary care free to citizens
- 36 public health facilities nationwide
- Every household within 3 miles of provider
- Challenge: Finding interpreters for medical communication
- Doctors often ill-equipped for deaf patients

### International Support
- **Starkey Hearing Foundation**
  - US-based nonprofit
  - 2019 mission to Grenada
  - Provided hearing aids
  - Partnered with local organizations

### Regional Connections
- **Caribbean Sign Language Centre** (Trinidad-based)
  - Interpreting services
  - Sign language tuition (Levels 1-3)
  - Training by deaf persons
- CARPHA regional health initiatives
- UNESCO Caribbean inclusion efforts

### Legal Framework
- **CRPD** (Convention on Rights of Persons with Disabilities)
  - Signed: 2010
  - Ratified: Yes
- Part of UN Disability Inclusion Strategy

### Challenges
- Limited interpreter availability
- Healthcare communication barriers
- ASL dominance may affect local signs
- Small deaf community size
- No documented indigenous sign language

### UNESCO Initiatives
- International Day of Sign Languages observance
- Knowledge Series on Empowering Persons with Disabilities
- Caribbean SIDS inclusion focus
- Sign language recognition advocacy

### Cultural Context
- English official language
- Grenadian Creole English widely spoken
- Grenadian French Creole in some areas
- Colonial history (French 1649-1783, British periods)
- African linguistic influences

### Quote on Inclusion
