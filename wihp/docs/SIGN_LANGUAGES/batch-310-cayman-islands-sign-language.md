### Old Cayman Sign Language
- Historical deaf sign language of Grand Cayman
- Possibly related to Providence Island Sign Language
- Documented by Washabaugh (1981)
- Now possibly extinct or severely endangered
- Developed in isolated community with high deafness incidence

### Current Language Situation
- ASL now dominant sign language
- Part of Caribbean ASL sphere
- British connection but American sign language influence
- Limited documentation on current deaf community

### Historical Significance
- Similar to other Caribbean village sign languages:
  - Providence Island Sign Language (Colombia)
  - Jamaican Country Sign Language (St. Elizabeth, Jamaica)
  - Kajana Sign Language (Suriname)
- High incidence of hereditary deafness historically
- Unique indigenous language developed

### Research Reference
- **Washabaugh, William (1981)**: The Deaf of Grand Cayman, British West Indies
- Published in Sign Language Studies 31: 117-134
- Early documentation of deaf community

### Geographic Context
- British Overseas Territory in western Caribbean
- Three islands: Grand Cayman, Cayman Brac, Little Cayman
- South of Cuba, northeast of Honduras
- Between Jamaica and Yucatán Peninsula
- Population: ~81,546 (2022 estimate)
- Area: 264 km²

### Official Language
- **English** - Official language
- Caymanian English: British English + Jamaican Patois + West African Creole
- Spanish, Tagalog, Hindi also spoken by expats

### Political Status
- Self-governing British Overseas Territory
- Largest British Overseas Territory by population
- Internal self-government from Jamaica until 1962
- Direct British rule after Jamaican independence

### Regional Sign Language Context
Nine sign languages documented in Caribbean:
1. American Sign Language
2. British Sign Language
3. Danish Sign Language
4. Dutch Sign Language (NGT)
5. French Sign Language
6. Guyanese Sign Language
7. Kajana Sign Language
8. Quebec Sign Language
9. Surinamese Sign Language

### Deaf Community Challenges
- Limited educational access
- Scripture access difficulties
- Need for deaf missionaries/leaders
- Small island community size

### Economic Context
- Major offshore financial center
- GDP per capita: 7,750 (2023)
- Highest standard of living in Caribbean
- Tax-exempt destination

### Caribbean Village Sign Languages
Similar indigenous sign languages emerged in:
- **Grand Cayman** (Old Cayman Sign Language)
- **Providence Island, Colombia** (Providencia Sign Language)
- **St. Elizabeth, Jamaica** (Konchri Sain/Country Sign)
- **Kajana, Suriname** (Kajana Sign Language)

All developed in communities with high hereditary deafness rates.

### Language Endangerment
- Old Cayman Sign Language possibly extinct
- ASL dominance affecting indigenous varieties
- Documentation and revitalization needed
- Caribbean deaf communities recognizing heritage importance
