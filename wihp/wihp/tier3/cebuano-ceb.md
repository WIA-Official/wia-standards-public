# WIHP: Cebuano (Sinugbuanon) - ceb

## Language Overview
- **Native Name:** Sinugbuanon / Bisaya
- **ISO 639-3:** ceb
- **Speaker Population:** ~27 million
- **Language Family:** Austronesian → Malayo-Polynesian → Philippine → Bisayan
- **Writing System:** Latin alphabet
- **Phonological Type:** Philippine-type voice system

## Phonological Features
- **Focus/Voice System:** Actor, Patient, Locative, Benefactive
- **Vowel System:** 3 vowels (a, i, u)
- **Glottal Stop:** Phonemic
- **Stress:** Affects meaning

## Phonological Rules

### Three-Vowel System
Cebuano has only /a, i, u/ as phonemes; [e, o] are allophones in Spanish loans.

### Glottal Stop
Phonemic and common word-medially and finally.

### Stress
Stress can distinguish meaning and is often on penultimate or final syllable.

### Voice System
Complex verbal morphology marking focus (actor, patient, etc.).

---

## WIHP Implementation Notes

1. **Three Vowels:** i and u have [e, o] allophones
2. **Glottal Stop:** Mark with ㅇ
3. **Spanish Loans:** Many Spanish-origin words
4. **Velar Nasal:** ng is single phoneme
5. **Stress:** Not marked in Hangul
