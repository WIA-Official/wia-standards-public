# WIHP: Xhosa (isiXhosa) - xho

## Language Overview
- **Native Name:** isiXhosa
- **ISO 639-3:** xho
- **Speaker Population:** ~8 million
- **Language Family:** Niger-Congo → Bantu → Nguni
- **Writing System:** Latin alphabet
- **Phonological Type:** Agglutinative with click consonants

## Phonological Features
- **Click Consonants:** Three click types (c, q, x) with more variants than Zulu
- **Tone System:** High and low tones
- **Breathy Voice:** Distinctive feature
- **Noun Classes:** 15+ grammatical classes

## Phonological Rules

### Click Consonants
Xhosa has the most extensive click system among the Nguni languages, with 15+ click phonemes including aspirated, ejective, voiced, and nasalized variants.

### Breathy Voice
Xhosa distinguishes breathy voiced stops from plain voiced stops:
- b vs bh, d vs dh, etc.

### Tone System
Two-level tone system affecting meaning and grammar.

### Prenasalization
Common in word-initial and medial positions:
- mb, nd, ng, nj, nc, nq, nx
