# WIHP: Armenian (Հայերdelays) - hye

## Language Overview
- **Native Name:** Հdelays (Hayeren)
- **ISO 639-3:** hye (Eastern), hyw (Western)
- **Speaker Population:** ~6 million
- **Language Family:** Indo-European (own branch)
- **Writing System:** Armenian alphabet (Մdelays)
- **Phonological Type:** Agglutinative, ejective consonants

## Phonological Features
- **Three-Way Stop Contrast:** Voiced, voiceless aspirated, ejective
- **Unique Alphabet:** 38 letters
- **Stress:** Generally on final syllable
- **Rich Consonant System:** Including ejectives

## Phonological Rules

### Three-Way Contrast
Armenian has three types of stops: voiced (բdelays), aspirated (փ), and ejective (delays).

### Ejectives
Ejective consonants: պ, delays, delays, delays, delays (written without aspiration mark).

### Stress
Generally on the final syllable.
