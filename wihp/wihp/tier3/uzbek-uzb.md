# WIHP: Uzbek (Oʻzbekcha) - uzb

## Language Overview
- **Native Name:** Oʻzbek tili / Ўзбек тили
- **ISO 639-3:** uzb
- **Speaker Population:** ~35 million
- **Language Family:** Turkic → Karluk
- **Writing System:** Latin (official), Cyrillic, Arabic
- **Phonological Type:** Agglutinative, weakened vowel harmony

## Phonological Features
- **Vowel Harmony:** Weakened compared to other Turkic
- **6 Vowels:** Simpler than Kazakh
- **Pharyngealized Vowels:** In Arabic loans
- **Stress:** Generally on final syllable

## Phonological Rules

### Vowel Harmony (Weakened)
Unlike Kazakh, Uzbek vowel harmony is largely lost in modern speech.

### Uvular Consonants
q /q/ and gʻ /ɣ/ are distinctive.

### Final Devoicing
Voiced consonants may devoice word-finally.

---

## WIHP Implementation Notes

1. **Uvular:** q→ㅋ, gʻ→ㄱ
2. **Affricates:** ch→ㅊ, j→ㅈ
3. **Oʻ Vowel:** Map to ㅗ
4. **Velar Nasal:** ng→ㅇ
5. **Simple Harmony:** Less complex than Kazakh
