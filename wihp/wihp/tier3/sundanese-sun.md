# WIHP: Sundanese (Basa Sunda) - sun

## Language Overview
- **Native Name:** Basa Sunda / ᮘᮞ ᮞᮥᮔ᮪ᮓ
- **ISO 639-3:** sun
- **Speaker Population:** ~42 million
- **Language Family:** Austronesian → Malayo-Polynesian → Sundanese
- **Writing System:** Latin, Sundanese script (Aksara Sunda)
- **Phonological Type:** Speech levels (loma, sedeng, lemes)

## Phonological Features
- **Speech Levels:** Multiple politeness registers
- **Vowel System:** 7 vowels
- **Syllable Structure:** (C)V(C)
- **No Tones:** Stress-timed

## Standard Sentences (50 Sentences)

### Basic Communication (1-10) - Loma (Informal)
1. **Kumaha damang?** /kumaha damaŋ/
   - 쿠마하 다망
   - "How are you?"

2. **Pangésto, sae.** /paŋesto sae/
   - 팡에스토 사에
   - "Fine, thank you."

3. **Hatur nuhun.** /hatur nuhun/
   - 하투르 누훈
   - "Thank you."

4. **Enya.** /əɲa/
   - 으냐
   - "Yes."

5. **Henteu.** /hənteʔu/
   - 흔트우
   - "No."

6. **Hampura.** /hampura/
   - 함푸라
   - "Sorry."

7. **Mangga.** /maŋga/
   - 망가
   - "Please."

8. **Abdi teu ngartos.** /abdi təʔu ŋartos/
   - 압디 트우 응아르토스
   - "I don't understand."

9. **Tiasa nyarios basa Inggris?** /tiasa ɲarios basa iŋgris/
   - 티아사 냐리오스 바사 잉그리스
   - "Can you speak English?"

10. **Wilujeng énjing.** /wiluɟəŋ enɟiŋ/
    - 윌루즁 엔징
    - "Good morning."

### Personal Information (11-20) - Lemes (Formal)
11. **Nami anjeun saha?** /nami anɟəʔun saha/
    - 나미 안즌 사하
    - "What is your name?"

12. **Nami abdi...** /nami abdi/
    - 나미 압디
    - "My name is..."

13. **Anjeun ti mana?** /anɟəʔun ti mana/
    - 안즌 티 마나
    - "Where are you from?"

14. **Abdi ti Sunda.** /abdi ti sunda/
    - 압디 티 순다
    - "I'm from Sunda."

15. **Yuswa anjeun sabaraha?** /juswa anɟəʔun sabaraha/
    - 유스와 안즌 사바라하
    - "How old are you?"

16. **Abdi tilu puluh taun.** /abdi tilu puluh taʔun/
    - 압디 틸루 풀루 타운
    - "I am thirty years old."

17. **Pagawéan anjeun naon?** /pagawean anɟəʔun naon/
    - 파가웨안 안즌 나온
    - "What is your occupation?"

18. **Abdi guru.** /abdi guru/
    - 압디 구루
    - "I am a teacher."

19. **Anjeun cicing di mana?** /anɟəʔun tʃitʃiŋ di mana/
    - 안즌 치칭 디 마나
    - "Where do you live?"

20. **Abdi cicing di Bandung.** /abdi tʃitʃiŋ di banduŋ/
    - 압디 치칭 디 반둥
    - "I live in Bandung."

### Daily Life (21-30)
21. **Tabuh sabaraha ayeuna?** /tabuh sabaraha ajəʔuna/
    - 타부 사바라하 아유나
    - "What time is it now?"

22. **Poé naon ayeuna?** /poe naon ajəʔuna/

23. **Hawana hadé poé ieu.** /hawana hade poe iəʔu/

24. **Abdi lapar.** /abdi lapar/
    - 압디 라파르
    - "I'm hungry."

25. **Abdi hanaang.** /abdi hanaʔaŋ/

26. **Abdi capé.** /abdi tʃape/

27. **Kamar cai di mana?** /kamar tʃaʔi di mana/
    - 카마르 차이 디 마나
    - "Where is the bathroom?"

28. **Sabaraha hargana?** /sabaraha hargana/
    - 사바라하 하르가나
    - "How much does it cost?"

29. **Mahal.** /mahal/
    - 마할
    - "It's expensive."

30. **Murah.** /murah/
    - 무라
    - "It's cheap."

### Travel & Directions (31-40)
31. **Stasiun di mana?** /stasiun di mana/
    - 스타시운 디 마나
    - "Where is the station?"

32. **Kumaha carana ka kota?** /kumaha tʃarana ka kota/
    - 쿠마하 차라나 카 코타
    - "How do I get to the city?"

33. **Terus waé.** /tərus wae/
    - 트루스 와에
    - "Go straight."

34. **Belok kénca.** /beloʔ kentʃa/
    - 벨록 겐차
    - "Turn left."

35. **Belok katuhu.** /beloʔ katuhu/
    - 벨록 카투후
    - "Turn right."

36. **Jauh?** /ɟauh/
    - 자우
    - "Is it far?"

37. **Deukeut.** /dəʔukəʔut/
    - 드우크읏
    - "It's close."

38. **Abdi hoyong meuli karcis.** /abdi hojoŋ məʔuli kartʃis/
    - 압디 호용 므울리 카르치스
    - "I want to buy a ticket."

39. **Karéta indit tabuh sabaraha?** /kareta indit tabuh sabaraha/
    - 카레타 인딧 타부 사바라하
    - "When does the train leave?"

40. **Turun di mana?** /turun di mana/
    - 투룬 디 마나
    - "Where do I get off?"

### Social & Cultural (41-50)
41. **Wilujeng!** /wiluɟəŋ/
    - 윌루즁
    - "Cheers!"

42. **Wilujeng tepang taun!** /wiluɟəŋ təpaŋ taʔun/

    - "Happy birthday!"

46. **Abdi sono ka anjeun.** /abdi sono ka anɟəʔun/

    - 무기아 숙세스
    - "Good luck!"

48. **Wilujeng angkat.** /wiluɟəŋ aŋkat/
    - 윌루즁 앙깟
    - "Goodbye." (to one leaving)

49. **Wilujeng wengi.** /wiluɟəŋ wəŋi/
    - 윌루즁 웅이
    - "Good night."

50. **Tepang deui.** /təpaŋ dəʔui/
    - 트팡 드우이

## Phonological Rules

### Eu Vowel
The /ə/ vowel written as 'eu' is distinctive to Sundanese.

### Glottal Stop
Common between vowels and word-finally.

### Nasal Assimilation
Nasals assimilate to following consonant place.

### Speech Levels
Three levels: loma (informal), sedeng (middle), lemes (formal).

---

## WIHP Implementation Notes

1. **Eu Vowel:** Map to ㅡ
2. **Palatal Stops:** c→ㅊ, j→ㅈ
3. **Glottal Stop:** Use ㅇ
4. **Speech Levels:** Same phonology, different vocabulary
5. **Stress:** Generally penultimate
