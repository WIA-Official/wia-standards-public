# WIHP: Malay (Bahasa Melayu) - msa

## Language Overview
- **Native Name:** Bahasa Melayu
- **ISO 639-3:** msa (macrolanguage)
- **Speaker Population:** ~77 million (native), 200M+ (L2)
- **Language Family:** Austronesian → Malayo-Polynesian → Malayic
- **Writing System:** Latin (Rumi), Arabic script (Jawi)
- **Phonological Type:** Agglutinative, simple phonology

## Phonological Features
- **Vowel System:** 6 vowels
- **Syllable Structure:** (C)(C)V(C)
- **No Tones:** Stress-timed
- **Reduplication:** Common morphological process

## Standard Sentences (50 Sentences)

### Basic Communication (1-10)
1. **Apa khabar?** /apa xaˈbar/
   - 아파 카바르
   - "How are you?"

2. **Khabar baik.** /xaˈbar baʔiʔ/
   - 카바르 바익
   - "I'm fine."

3. **Terima kasih.** /tərima kaˈsih/
   - 트리마 카시
   - "Thank you."

4. **Ya.** /ja/
   - 야
   - "Yes."

5. **Tidak.** /tiˈdaʔ/
   - 티닥
   - "No."

6. **Maafkan saya.** /maʔafkan saˈja/
   - 마아프칸 사야
   - "I'm sorry."

7. **Tolong.** /toˈloŋ/
   - 톨롱
   - "Please."

8. **Saya tidak faham.** /saˈja tiˈdaʔ faˈham/
   - 사야 티닥 파함
   - "I don't understand."

9. **Awak boleh cakap Inggeris?** /awaʔ boleh tʃaˈkap iŋˈgəris/
   - 아왁 볼레 차캅 잉그리스
   - "Can you speak English?"

10. **Selamat pagi.** /səlaˈmat paˈgi/
    - 슬라맛 파기
    - "Good morning."

## Phonological Rules

### Final Glottal Stop
Final /k/ is often realized as glottal stop [ʔ].

### Schwa
The schwa /ə/ is very common (written 'e').

### Nasal Assimilation
Nasals assimilate to following consonant.

### Arabic/English Loans
Many borrowed words from Arabic, English, Sanskrit, Tamil.

---

## WIHP Implementation Notes

1. **Schwa:** Map to ㅓ
2. **Affricates:** c→ㅊ, j→ㅈ
3. **Final k:** Often glottal stop [ʔ]
4. **Arabic Sounds:** kh, sy preserved in loans
5. **Simple Syllables:** CV(C) structure
