# Classical Latin (Latina) - WIHP A-Grade Mapping

## Language Information
- **Language**: Classical Latin (Latina Classica)
- **ISO 639-3**: lat
- **Period**: 75 BCE - 200 CE (Golden/Silver Age)
- **Region**: Roman Empire
- **Status**: Extinct (liturgical, academic use)
- **Script**: Latin alphabet
- **WIHP Category**: Special/Historical

## Phonological Notes

Classical Latin pronunciation is **reconstructed** based on:
- Comparative linguistics
- Ancient grammarians' descriptions
- Latin loanwords in other languages
- Spelling conventions and poetry metrics

## Phonological Rules

1. **C always /k/**: Caesar = /ˈkae̯.sar/ 카이사르 (not /siːzər/)
2. **V = /w/**: Vēnī = /ˈweː.niː/ 웨니
3. **Vowel length matters**: mālum (apple) ≠ malum (evil)
4. **AE/OE diphthongs**: Caesar /ˈkae̯.sar/, poena /ˈpoe̯.na/
5. **Geminate consonants**: Long, not repeated
6. **Stress rules**: Based on syllable weight

## Cultural Context

- **Golden Age**: Cicero, Caesar, Virgil, Horace
- **Silver Age**: Seneca, Tacitus, Pliny
- **Church Latin**: Later pronunciation differs
- **Scientific Latin**: Neo-Latin terminology
