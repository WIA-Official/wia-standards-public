# Classical Arabic (العربية الفصحى) - WIHP A-Grade Mapping

## Language Information
- **Language**: Classical Arabic (العربية الفصحى Al-ʿArabiyyah al-Fuṣḥā)
- **ISO 639-3**: arb
- **Period**: 7th century CE - present
- **Region**: Arab World, Islamic civilization
- **Status**: Liturgical, literary, formal standard
- **Script**: Arabic (العربية)
- **WIHP Category**: Special/Historical

## Phonological Notes

Classical Arabic preserves the original phonological system of 7th century Arabic, including sounds that have merged in many modern dialects.

## Phonological Rules

1. **Emphatic consonants**: ط ظ ص ض affect neighboring vowels
2. **Sun/Moon letters**: Assimilation of ال article
3. **ʿAyn and Ḥāʾ**: Distinctive pharyngeal sounds
4. **Hamzah rules**: Glottal stop orthography
5. **Case endings**: Full case system (ـُ ـَ ـِ)
6. **Tanwīn**: Nunation on indefinite nouns
7. **Vowel length**: Distinctive (a vs aː)
8. **Root system**: Triliteral consonant roots
