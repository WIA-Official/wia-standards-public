# Classical Sanskrit (संस्कृतम्) - WIHP A-Grade Mapping

## Language Information
- **Language**: Classical Sanskrit (संस्कृतम् Saṃskṛtam)
- **ISO 639-3**: san
- **Period**: ~500 BCE - present (liturgical)
- **Region**: Ancient India, South Asia
- **Status**: Classical/liturgical language
- **Script**: Devanagari (देवनागरी), also Romanized (IAST)
- **WIHP Category**: Special/Historical

## Phonological Notes

Sanskrit has one of the most **precisely documented** phonological systems of any ancient language, thanks to ancient grammarians like Pāṇini.

## Phonological Rules

1. **Sandhi**: Sound changes at word/morpheme boundaries
2. **Aspiration series**: Each stop has 4 variants (k/kh/g/gh)
3. **Retroflex series**: Tongue curled back (ṭ/ḍ/ṇ/ṣ)
4. **Three sibilants**: Palatal (ś), retroflex (ṣ), dental (s)
5. **Vowel length**: Distinctive (a vs ā)
6. **Syllabic r**: ऋ functions as vowel
7. **Visarga**: Final aspiration ः
