Chapter 02

AMMs and Liquidity Pools

弘益人間 - Democratizing Market Making

2.1 Introduction to Automated Market Makers

Automated Market Makers (AMMs) represent one of DeFi's most revolutionary innovations. Before AMMs, cryptocurrency trading required centralized exchanges with order books - a model that necessitated market makers, high liquidity requirements, and permission-based access. AMMs changed everything.

An AMM is a type of decentralized exchange (DEX) protocol that uses mathematical formulas to price assets algorithmically, rather than relying on order books. Instead of matching buyers and sellers directly, AMMs use liquidity pools - smart contracts holding reserves of tokens - and mathematical curves to determine prices and execute trades automatically.

Why AMMs Matter

AMMs democratized market making, allowing anyone to become a liquidity provider and earn fees. This eliminated the need for sophisticated trading firms and large capital requirements that dominated traditional markets. By 2026, AMMs facilitate over $500 billion in monthly trading volume across thousands of token pairs.

2.2 The Constant Product Formula (x × y = k)

The foundation of modern AMMs is the constant product formula, pioneered by Uniswap. This elegant equation enables automated, permissionless trading:

x × y = k

where x = reserve of token A, y = reserve of token B, k = constant

This formula ensures that the product of the two token reserves remains constant before and after each trade (minus fees). Let's break down how it works:

  • x represents the quantity of token A in the pool
  • y represents the quantity of token B in the pool
  • k is the constant product, which only changes when liquidity is added or removed

2.2.1 How Trades Work

When a trader wants to swap token A for token B:

  1. They deposit Δx amount of token A into the pool
  2. The AMM calculates how much token B (Δy) to give them such that (x + Δx) × (y - Δy) = k
  3. The trade is executed automatically, with the pool's reserves adjusted

Practical Example: ETH/USDC Pool

Initial State:

  • Pool contains 100 ETH (x) and 200,000 USDC (y)
  • k = 100 × 200,000 = 20,000,000
  • Implicit price: 200,000 / 100 = 2,000 USDC per ETH

Trade: Swap 10 ETH for USDC

  • New ETH in pool: 100 + 10 = 110 ETH
  • Calculate new USDC: y = k / x = 20,000,000 / 110 = 181,818 USDC
  • USDC received: 200,000 - 181,818 = 18,182 USDC
  • Effective price: 18,182 / 10 = 1,818 USDC per ETH (price impact!)

After Trade:

  • Pool now has 110 ETH and 181,818 USDC
  • New implicit price: 181,818 / 110 = 1,653 USDC per ETH
  • The large trade moved the price significantly

2.2.2 Price Impact and Slippage

As shown in the example above, larger trades relative to pool size cause greater price impact. This is because the constant product formula creates a hyperbolic curve - the more you buy, the exponentially more expensive it becomes.

Slippage is the difference between expected price and execution price. In AMMs, slippage is deterministic and calculable before trade execution. Users typically set a slippage tolerance (e.g., 0.5%) to protect against excessive price movement.

2.3 Uniswap Evolution: v1 → v2 → v3 → v4

2.3.1 Uniswap v1 (2018)

  • Introduced constant product AMM to Ethereum
  • Only supported ETH/ERC20 pairs (no direct ERC20/ERC20)
  • 0.3% fixed fee on all trades
  • Proof of concept that revolutionized DEXs

2.3.2 Uniswap v2 (2020)

  • Direct ERC20/ERC20 pairs (no forced ETH intermediary)
  • Price oracles: time-weighted average prices (TWAP)
  • Flash swaps: borrow tokens, use them, repay in same transaction
  • Became the dominant DEX, facilitating billions in volume

2.3.3 Uniswap v3 (2021)

V3 introduced concentrated liquidity, the biggest advancement in AMM technology:

  • Concentrated Liquidity: LPs can specify price ranges for their capital, achieving up to 4000x capital efficiency
  • Multiple Fee Tiers: 0.01%, 0.05%, 0.3%, and 1% fee options for different volatility pairs
  • Non-Fungible Positions: Each LP position is unique (NFT), unlike v2's fungible LP tokens
  • Advanced Oracles: Geometric mean oracles for improved manipulation resistance

2.3.4 Uniswap v4 (2024-2026)

Uniswap v4 represents a paradigm shift with its hooks system, making it the most flexible and powerful AMM to date:

Key Features:

  • Hooks: Custom code that executes at specific points in the pool lifecycle (before/after swaps, LP actions, etc.)
  • Singleton Architecture: All pools in one contract, dramatically reducing gas costs (~99% cheaper to create pools)
  • Flash Accounting: Only net balances settled at end of transaction, enabling complex multi-hop routes
  • Native ETH Support: More efficient than using WETH
  • Governance-Controlled Fees: Fee tiers can be customized per pool

Hook Capabilities:

// Hooks enable unlimited customization: ✓ beforeInitialize / afterInitialize - Custom pool setup logic ✓ beforeSwap / afterSwap - Dynamic fees, restrictions ✓ beforeAddLiquidity / afterAddLiquidity - LP incentives, caps ✓ beforeRemoveLiquidity / afterRemoveLiquidity - Exit fees, locks // Example use cases: - Time-weighted average market maker (TWAMM) - On-chain limit orders - Dynamic fees based on volatility - MEV-resistant pools - Geoblocking or KYC requirements - Auto-compounding fee reinvestment - Circuit breakers for extreme volatility

Real-World v4 Hook: Volatility-Adjusted Fees

A hook could monitor price volatility and automatically adjust fees:

  • Low volatility (stable trading): 0.01% fee to encourage volume
  • Medium volatility: 0.05% fee (standard)
  • High volatility: 0.3% fee to protect LPs from adverse selection
  • Extreme volatility: 1% fee + temporary trading pause

This protects liquidity providers from toxic flow while maximizing fee revenue and capital efficiency.

2.4 Impermanent Loss: The LP's Challenge

Impermanent loss (IL) is the reduction in value that liquidity providers experience compared to simply holding the tokens, caused by price divergence between the two assets in the pool.

2.4.1 Understanding Impermanent Loss

When you provide liquidity to an AMM, your position's value changes as prices move, due to the constant product formula's automatic rebalancing. If one token appreciates relative to the other, arbitrageurs will trade against your position until pool prices match external markets, leaving you with more of the depreciated token and less of the appreciated one.

IL = 2√(price_ratio) / (1 + price_ratio) - 1

Impermanent Loss by Price Change:

  • 1.25x price change: -0.6% loss
  • 1.5x price change: -2.0% loss
  • 2x price change: -5.7% loss
  • 3x price change: -13.4% loss
  • 4x price change: -20.0% loss
  • 5x price change: -25.5% loss
Why "Impermanent"?

The loss is "impermanent" because if prices return to their original ratio, the loss disappears. However, if you withdraw liquidity while prices are diverged, the loss becomes permanent. In practice, prices rarely return exactly to original ratios, making the "impermanent" label somewhat misleading.

2.4.2 Mitigating Impermanent Loss

Strategies to minimize IL exposure:

  1. Correlated Pairs: Provide liquidity for pairs that move together (e.g., ETH/stETH, USDC/USDT) to minimize price divergence
  2. Stablecoin Pairs: Stable/stable pairs have minimal IL but also lower fee revenue
  3. Concentrated Liquidity (v3/v4): Narrow ranges earn more fees to offset IL, but require active management
  4. Fee Capture: Ensure trading fees earned exceed impermanent loss (high-volume pairs)
  5. Liquidity Mining Incentives: Additional token rewards can offset IL
  6. IL Protection: Some protocols offer IL protection/insurance products

2.5 Alternative AMM Designs

2.5.1 Curve: Stableswap

Curve pioneered a formula optimized for similarly-priced assets (stablecoins, wrapped tokens):

A × n^n × Σx_i = A × D × n^n + D^(n+1) / (n^n × Πx_i)

This complex formula creates a flatter curve that keeps prices closer to 1:1 for stablecoins, dramatically reducing slippage. Curve v2 extended this to volatile pairs with dynamic pegs.

2.5.2 Balancer: Weighted Pools

Balancer generalizes AMMs to pools with arbitrary token ratios (not just 50/50):

  • Create pools with 80/20, 60/40, or any ratio
  • Support up to 8 tokens in a single pool
  • Custom weight = custom exposure to different assets
  • Acts like auto-rebalancing index fund

2.5.3 Solidly/Velodrome: ve(3,3) Model

ve(3,3) aligns incentives between LPs, traders, and governance:

  • Voters direct liquidity mining emissions to pools
  • Pool LPs share fees with voters
  • Vote-locking creates protocol-owned liquidity
  • Sustainable tokenomics without infinite inflation

2.6 Concentrated Liquidity Deep Dive

Concentrated liquidity deserves special attention as it fundamentally changed how LPs operate:

2.6.1 Capital Efficiency

By concentrating liquidity in specific price ranges, LPs can achieve capital efficiency improvements of up to 4000x. This means $1M of capital in a concentrated range can provide the same depth as $4B in a full-range v2 position.

Example: ETH/USDC Pool

Scenario: ETH trading at $2,000

v2 Full Range:

  • Liquidity spread from $0 to $∞
  • $100,000 TVL provides thin liquidity across all prices
  • Earning fees only on trades that occur

v3 Concentrated ($1,800 - $2,200):

  • Same $100,000 TVL concentrated in 20% price range
  • Provides ~10x deeper liquidity within range
  • Earns fees 10x faster when price in range
  • BUT: Earns nothing if price exits range

2.6.2 Active Management

Concentrated liquidity positions require active management:

  • Monitoring: Track if current price is within your range
  • Rebalancing: When price exits range, withdraw and re-deploy in new range
  • Fee Compounding: Periodically claim and reinvest earned fees
  • Gas Costs: Frequent rebalancing can be expensive on mainnet

This complexity led to the rise of liquidity management protocols like Gamma Strategies, Arrakis, and Charm Finance that automate position management for a fee.

2.7 Providing Liquidity: Practical Guide

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Choose a Pool: Research TVL, volume, and fees of different pairs
  2. Calculate Expected Returns:
    • APR from fees = (24h_fees × 365) / TVL
    • Consider additional incentives (liquidity mining)
    • Estimate potential impermanent loss
  3. Prepare Assets: Need equal value of both tokens (v2) or strategic ratio (v3/v4)
  4. Approve Tokens: Grant router contract permission to spend your tokens
  5. Add Liquidity:
    • v2: Specify amounts or total value
    • v3/v4: Select price range and concentration
  6. Receive LP Token/NFT: Represents your position and claim on fees
  7. Monitor Position: Track performance, fees earned, impermanent loss
  8. Claim Fees: Some protocols auto-compound, others require manual claiming
  9. Remove Liquidity: Burn LP token to withdraw proportional share
