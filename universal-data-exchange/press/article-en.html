Executive Summary

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released the international standard WIA-CORE-003 for seamless data exchange between heterogeneous systems under the MIT License, completely free. This announcement marks Korea's leadership in data interoperability standards.

🚨 Current Situation: Why This Standard Is Needed

Global enterprises spend trillions annually on integrating heterogeneous systems. Due to different data formats, protocols, and schemas, medical records cannot be shared between hospitals, financial data transfers are delayed between banks, and IoT devices cannot communicate with each other. Data silos have become the biggest barrier to digital transformation.

CEO Sam-Heum Yeon:

WIA's Solution: WIA-CORE-003 Universal Data Exchange

WIA-CORE-003 provides a framework for securely exchanging all types of data through a JSON-LD based Universal Data Envelope. It includes built-in schema evolution, data transformation, integrity verification, and supports all protocols including HTTP, WebSocket, gRPC, and MQTT.

🏢 About WIA

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) is an international standards organization whose mission is to provide equal opportunities to all humanity through technology and certification.

WIA is developing over 600 international standards in various fields including accessibility, security, AI, environment, and healthcare, and releases all standards as free open source.

🦀 Tech Stack: WIA's core APIs are developed in Rust. Rust is a systems programming language developed by Mozilla, the only language that simultaneously achieves memory safety and zero-cost abstractions.

👤 About CEO

Dr. Sam-Heum Yeon is the world's only solo standardization architect who built over 60 international standard infrastructures in collaboration with AI, and is recognized as a living legend in lifelong education.
