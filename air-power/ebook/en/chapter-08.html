Chapter 8: Certification & Compliance

8.1 Certification Overview

WIA AIR-POWER certification ensures that transmitters, receivers, and complete systems meet safety, performance, and interoperability requirements. Certification is mandatory for commercial deployment.

Compliance Requirement: All AIR-POWER devices sold commercially must obtain WIA certification and applicable regional regulatory approvals (FCC, CE, KC, etc.).

Certification Levels

Level Scope Requirements Validity
Component Individual TX or RX module Safety, EMC, performance specs 3 years
System Complete power zone deployment Integration, coverage, safety Annual review
Manufacturer Production facility and QA process Quality management, testing capability 2 years
Integrator Installation and deployment services Technical competency, safety training 2 years

8.3 Safety Certification Requirements

Comprehensive safety testing ensures AIR-POWER systems are safe for continuous operation around humans and animals.

Safety Test Categories

1. SAR Testing (Specific Absorption Rate)

  • Whole body SAR measurement at maximum power
  • Localized SAR testing at 1g and 10g averaging
  • Testing at multiple frequencies and modes
  • Worst-case exposure scenario validation
  • Compliance with ICNIRP/IEEE/FCC guidelines

2. EMC Testing (Electromagnetic Compatibility)

  • Radiated emissions measurement
  • Conducted emissions on power lines
  • Immunity to external interference
  • Harmonic distortion analysis
  • Co-existence with WiFi, Bluetooth, cellular

3. Electrical Safety

  • Insulation resistance and dielectric strength
  • Overcurrent and overvoltage protection
  • Grounding and leakage current testing
  • Thermal runaway prevention
  • Fire and explosion hazard assessment

4. Human Safety Systems

  • Human detection system accuracy (>99.9%)
  • Emergency shutdown response time (<100ms)
  • Failsafe behavior validation
  • Long-term exposure safety studies
  • Children and vulnerable populations assessment

5. Device Safety

  • Battery overcharge protection verification
  • Thermal management effectiveness
  • Foreign object detection accuracy
  • Receiver compatibility testing
  • Power delivery precision and stability

8.4 Performance Certification

Performance testing validates that AIR-POWER systems deliver the promised power transfer efficiency and coverage.

Performance Test Requirements

Test Category Metric Minimum Standard Measurement Method
Power Transfer Efficiency End-to-end efficiency > 40% at optimal distance Precision power meter
Coverage Area Effective power zone > 90% of claimed area Grid measurement across zone
Multi-Device Support Simultaneous devices > 80% of rated capacity Load testing with real devices
Discovery Time Device discovery latency < 5 seconds Protocol analyzer
Power Delivery Accuracy Actual vs. negotiated power ± 10% tolerance Real-time power monitoring
Uptime Reliability Operational availability > 99.5% over 30 days Continuous monitoring

8.5 Interoperability Certification

Interoperability testing ensures AIR-POWER devices from different manufacturers work together seamlessly.

Interoperability Test Suite

  • Protocol compliance: Discovery, negotiation, power transfer sequences
  • Cross-vendor compatibility: TX from vendor A with RX from vendor B
  • Multi-mode operation: Seamless switching between RF, resonance, infrared
  • API compatibility: RESTful endpoints match WIA specification
  • Data schema validation: JSON payloads conform to WIA schemas
  • Security handshake: Authentication and encryption interoperability
  • Backward compatibility: Newer devices work with older infrastructure
  • Edge cases: Handling of malformed packets and error conditions

8.6 Certification Process

Step-by-step process for obtaining WIA AIR-POWER certification.

Certification Steps

Step 1: Pre-Certification (2-4 weeks)

  1. Register product with WIA Certification Board
  2. Submit technical documentation and design files
  3. Complete pre-assessment questionnaire
  4. Receive initial feedback and testing requirements

Step 2: Lab Testing (4-8 weeks)

  1. Ship prototype units to accredited test lab
  2. Complete safety testing (SAR, EMC, electrical)
  3. Complete performance testing (efficiency, coverage)
  4. Complete interoperability testing with reference devices
  5. Receive detailed test reports

Step 3: Review & Remediation (2-4 weeks)

  1. WIA Certification Board reviews test results
  2. Address any failures or deficiencies
  3. Re-test modified units if necessary
  4. Final documentation submission

Step 4: Certification Grant (1-2 weeks)

  1. Board approval and certification issuance
  2. Receive WIA AIR-POWER certification mark
  3. Product listed in WIA certified devices database
  4. Marketing materials approval
Total Timeline: 9-18 weeks from application to certification, depending on product complexity and test results.

8.7 Deployment Certification

For large-scale deployments (commercial, public infrastructure), additional system-level certification is required.

Deployment Certification Checklist

  • Site survey and coverage planning documentation
  • Transmitter placement and power output configuration
  • Zone configuration and access control policies
  • Safety monitoring systems operational and tested
  • Human detection systems calibrated for environment
  • Emergency shutdown procedures documented and drilled
  • System monitoring and alerting configured
  • User signage and safety information posted
  • Staff training completed for operation and emergency response
  • Regional regulatory approvals obtained (FCC/CE/KC/etc.)
  • Insurance coverage for wireless power operations
  • Incident reporting and logging systems active
  • Maintenance schedule and procedures established
  • Periodic safety audits planned (quarterly minimum)

8.8 Ongoing Compliance

Maintaining certification requires ongoing monitoring, reporting, and periodic recertification.
