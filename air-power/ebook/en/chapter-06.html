Chapter 6: Security & Privacy

6.1 Safety Overview

The safety of wireless power transmission is paramount. WIA AIR-POWER implements multiple layers of protection to ensure that power delivery is as safe as WiFi for humans, animals, and devices.

Safety First Principle: All AIR-POWER systems must prioritize safety over power delivery. When in doubt, the system shuts down.

Safety Layers

Layer Protection Response Time
Detection Human presence, foreign objects, interference < 50ms
Monitoring SAR levels, temperature, power output Continuous
Adjustment Reduce power, redirect beam, change frequency < 100ms
Shutdown Emergency stop when safety limits exceeded < 100ms
Recovery Safe restart after incident resolution Manual approval

SAR Monitoring System

  • Automatic Compliance: Never exceed limits, reduce power proactively
  • Logging: All SAR measurements logged for regulatory audits

  methods: [radar, thermal, motion, camera_ai]

  detection_logic:
    # Multi-sensor fusion
    confidence_threshold: 95%

    radar_detected: boolean
    thermal_detected: boolean
    motion_detected: boolean
    camera_detected: boolean

    human_present = (
      (radar_detected AND thermal_detected) OR
      (thermal_detected AND camera_detected) OR

  response_actions:

      immediate:
        - calculate_distance_and_direction
        - if_in_beam_path:
            - redirect_beam_away (< 50ms)

                - if_still_unsafe:
                    - complete_shutoff

      create_exclusion_zone:
        radius: 0.5m around detected human
        beam_redirect: mandatory
        power_limit: ambient_safe_level

  false_positive_handling:
    verify_detection: multi_sensor
    timeout: 5s for confirmation
    err_on_side_of_safety: true

6.4 Device Safety

Protection mechanisms ensure that receiving devices are not damaged by wireless power.

Device Protection Features

Overcharge Protection

  • Stop charging at 100% (or user-configured level)
  • Switch to trickle mode to maintain charge without overcharging
  • Monitor battery health and adjust charging strategy
  • Prevent battery degradation from continuous charging

Thermal Management

  • Continuous temperature monitoring of receiving device
  • Throttle charging rate if temperature exceeds 40°C
  • Stop charging if temperature reaches 45°C
  • Resume only after cooling to safe level
  • Alert user if persistent overheating detected

Foreign Object Detection (FOD)

  • Detect metallic objects in magnetic resonance field
  • Prevent heating of coins, keys, jewelry
  • Immediate shutoff if foreign object detected
  • Resume only after object removal confirmed

Device Safety Thresholds

Parameter Safe Range Warning Level Critical Level
Battery Temp 20-40°C > 40°C (throttle) > 45°C (stop)
Voltage Device specified ± 5% of nominal ± 10% of nominal
Current Device specified ± 10% of nominal ± 20% of nominal
Charge Cycle < 500 cycles/year > 500 cycles/year > 800 cycles/year

6.5 Privacy in Charging Zones

Wireless power zones collect data for operation and optimization. Privacy protections ensure this data is used responsibly.

Data Collection & Privacy

Data Type Purpose Retention Privacy Protection
Device ID Session management, billing 90 days Hashed, not reversible to personal identity
Location Data Power zone optimization 30 days Aggregated, anonymized after 24h
Power Usage Billing, analytics 1 year Associated with account, not device
Battery Level Power allocation optimization Real-time only Not logged, not stored
Device Type Compatibility, statistics Indefinite Aggregated statistics only

Privacy Principles

  • Data Minimization: Collect only what's necessary for operation
  • Purpose Limitation: Use data only for stated purposes
  • Anonymization: Remove personal identifiers as soon as possible
  • User Control: Users can opt out of analytics while still charging
  • Transparency: Clear disclosure of what data is collected and why
  • Secure Storage: Encrypted at rest and in transit
  • Right to Deletion: Users can request deletion of their data

6.7 Incident Response

Procedures for handling safety incidents and system failures.

Incident Response Plan

  1. Detection: System automatically detects safety violation or failure
  2. Immediate Response: Emergency shutdown within 100ms
  3. Isolation: Affected TX unit isolated from network
  4. Notification: Alerts sent to system admin and safety officer
  5. Investigation: Logs reviewed to determine root cause
  6. Remediation: Fix issue, verify safety before restart
  7. Documentation: Incident recorded for regulatory compliance
  8. Prevention: System updates to prevent recurrence

Reportable Incidents

  • SAR level exceeded regulatory limits
  • Device overheating above 50°C
  • Foreign object heating detected
  • Human detection system failure
  • Emergency shutdown triggered
  • Unauthorized access to private zone
  • Data breach or privacy violation
  • System malfunction causing safety risk
