Chapter 5: Integration Guide

5.1 Integration Overview

This chapter provides practical guidance for integrating WIA AIR-POWER technology into various environments including homes, offices, commercial spaces, public infrastructure, and vehicles.

Integration Philosophy: Start small, validate safety, scale gradually. Always prioritize user safety and experience over rapid deployment.

5.2 Home Integration

Residential deployment brings wireless power to everyday living spaces, eliminating cable clutter and charging anxiety.

Home Deployment Scenarios

Room Type TX Placement Coverage Typical Devices
Living Room Ceiling center, integrated with light fixture Full room (~20㎡) Phones, tablets, remotes, smart speakers
Bedroom Ceiling or nightstand TX units Sleeping area focus Phones, watches, earbuds, e-readers
Kitchen Under-cabinet or ceiling mount Counter and table areas Tablets, phones, smart displays
Home Office Desk-mounted or overhead Workspace coverage Laptop, phone, mouse, keyboard
Whole Home Mesh network of 3-5 TX units Complete coverage All portable devices, IoT sensors

Home Setup Guide

  1. Planning: Map your home and identify high-use areas
  2. TX Selection: Choose appropriate power class (typically 10-30W for home)
  3. Installation: Mount TX units in optimal locations (ceiling preferred)
  4. Configuration: Set up zones, access control (private), and preferences
  5. Device Registration: Add all compatible devices to the system
  6. Testing: Verify coverage in all areas and measure charging rates
  7. Optimization: Adjust TX placement or add units to fill coverage gaps
Home Configuration Example
home_setup:
  name: "Smith Family Home"
  zones:
    - zone_id: "home-living-room"
      transmitters: ["tx-lr-001"]
      access: "private"
      priority_devices: ["family-phones", "tablets"]

    - zone_id: "home-bedrooms"
      transmitters: ["tx-bed-001", "tx-bed-002"]
      access: "private"
      schedule:
        night_mode: true  # Lower power 10pm-6am

  device_policies:
    smartphones:
      priority: "high"
      target_level: 80%  # Stop at 80% for battery health

    wearables:
      priority: "normal"
      continuous: true

    iot_sensors:
      priority: "low"
      power_mode: "trickle"

5.3 Commercial Space Integration

Offices, cafes, coworking spaces, and retail locations can attract and retain customers by providing wireless charging as an amenity.

Commercial Deployment Types

Coffee Shop / Restaurant

  • Strategy: Ceiling-mounted TX units covering seating areas
  • Power Class: 50-100W commercial TX
  • Coverage: All customer seating zones
  • Access: Open to paying customers
  • Business Value: Increased dwell time, customer loyalty, competitive advantage
  • Marketing: "Our cafe charges your devices while you relax"

Office / Coworking Space

  • Strategy: Comprehensive coverage of all workspaces
  • Power Class: High-power TX supporting laptops (100W+)
  • Coverage: Desks, meeting rooms, common areas
  • Access: Authenticated members only
  • Business Value: Cable-free desks, flexibility, productivity boost
  • Cost Savings: Eliminate desk power outlets and cable management

Retail Store

  • Strategy: Power zones in try-on areas, lounges, checkouts
  • Power Class: Medium TX (30-50W)
  • Coverage: Strategic customer touchpoints
  • Access: Open to shoppers
  • Business Value: Extended shopping time, premium experience
  • Analytics: Track dwell time correlation with charging usage

Commercial Integration Checklist

  • Conduct site survey and power needs assessment
  • Calculate required TX units based on coverage area and expected device count
  • Design TX placement for optimal coverage without dead zones
  • Install electrical infrastructure for TX power supply
  • Configure access control and authentication system
  • Set up usage analytics and monitoring dashboard
  • Test coverage in all customer areas
  • Train staff on system operation and troubleshooting
  • Create customer-facing signage and instructions
  • Monitor usage patterns and optimize allocation strategies

5.5 Vehicle Integration

Cars, buses, trains, and airplanes can integrate wireless power to keep passengers' devices charged during transit.

Vehicle Deployment Guide

Personal Vehicle

  • TX Placement: Dashboard, center console, or headliner
  • Power Source: Vehicle 12V system with DC-DC converter
  • Coverage: Front seats and back seats
  • Power Class: 20W vehicle TX
  • Devices Supported: 5+ (phones, tablets, wearables)
  • Auto-Start: Power on when engine starts

Public Transit (Bus, Train)

  • TX Placement: Ceiling-mounted along passenger cabin
  • Power Source: Vehicle electrical system
  • Coverage: All passenger seating areas
  • Power Class: 100W+ commercial TX
  • Devices Supported: 50-100+ passengers
  • Passenger Benefit: Productive commute time, no cable hassle

Aircraft

  • TX Placement: Overhead bins or seatback integration
  • Power Source: Aircraft electrical system
  • Coverage: All passenger seats
  • Certification: FAA/EASA approval required
  • Passenger Value: Eliminate seat power ports, reduce weight
  • Airlines Benefit: Premium feature, reduced maintenance
