Chapter 4: Hospital Systems Network

Interoperable Hospital Systems

Hospital network integration creates a unified healthcare infrastructure where facilities across North and South Korea can seamlessly collaborate to provide optimal patient care.

Hospital Information System (HIS) Integration

Key integration capabilities include:

  • Patient Registration: Unified patient identification across all facilities
  • Appointment Scheduling: Cross-border appointment booking and coordination
  • Bed Management: Real-time capacity monitoring and patient placement
  • Order Entry: Standardized medication and procedure ordering
  • Billing Integration: Interoperable insurance and payment systems

Medical Imaging Exchange

DICOM-Based Image Sharing

Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine (DICOM) standard enables seamless medical image exchange between facilities:

  • X-rays, CT scans, MRI, ultrasound
  • Picture Archiving and Communication Systems (PACS)
  • 3D reconstruction and advanced visualization
  • Secure cloud-based storage and access

Laboratory Information Systems

Integrated laboratory systems enable:

  • Standardized test ordering and result reporting
  • Reference range harmonization across facilities
  • Quality control and proficiency testing coordination
  • Specialized test referral to advanced laboratories

Telemedicine Infrastructure

Telemedicine bridges connect medical expertise across the Korean Peninsula, enabling remote consultations, second opinions, and emergency guidance.

Specialist Consultations

  • Real-time video consultations with specialists
  • Remote diagnosis and treatment planning
  • Asynchronous case reviews and second opinions
  • Multi-disciplinary team conferences

Remote Monitoring

  • ICU telemedicine for critical care support
  • Chronic disease management and follow-up
  • Post-surgical monitoring and complication detection
  • Home health monitoring integration

Patient Transfer Protocols

Standardized processes for cross-border patient transfers:

  • Medical Necessity Assessment: Clear criteria for transfer authorization
  • Facility Matching: Automated matching with appropriate receiving facility
  • Transport Coordination: Medical evacuation and ambulance services
  • Continuity of Care: Complete medical record transfer and care handoff

Emergency Medical Services Integration

Unified emergency response systems enable coordinated care during medical emergencies and disasters:

  • Joint emergency dispatch and triage protocols
  • Cross-border ambulance and medical helicopter access
  • Real-time emergency department capacity monitoring
  • Trauma center network and specialty care referral
  • Mass casualty incident response coordination

Quality & Accreditation Standards

Harmonized quality standards ensure consistent, high-quality care across all facilities:

  • Joint accreditation and certification programs
  • Unified clinical practice guidelines
  • Shared quality metrics and benchmarking
  • Cross-border peer review and learning
