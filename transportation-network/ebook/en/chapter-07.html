Chapter 07: Phase 4 — Integration with Mobility Stacks

7.1 Phase 4 in one paragraph

Phase 4 specifies how WIA-UNI-008 integrates with existing transit data standards: GTFS, GTFS-Realtime, NeTEx (CEN/TS 16614), SIRI (CEN/TS 15531), GBFS (NABSA), MDS (Open Mobility Foundation), and the WIA family.

7.2 GTFS bridge

GTFS static feeds (CSV) ingest into WIA route + stop + schedule envelopes. The bridge handles GTFS extensions (fares, shapes, transfers) via the standard\x27s extension mechanism.

7.3 GTFS-Realtime bridge

GTFS-Realtime Protocol Buffer messages ingest into WIA vehicle_position, trip_update, service_alert envelopes. The bridge supports both push (GTFS-Realtime publisher) and pull (GTFS-Realtime polling consumer) modes.

7.4 NeTEx + SIRI bridges (Europe)

NeTEx (network timetable exchange) and SIRI (real-time service interface) are CEN-standardised European transit data exchange. The bridges enable EU public transport authorities to ingest and emit WIA feeds without changing existing infrastructure.

7.5 GBFS bridge

GBFS (General Bikeshare Feed Specification) defines micromobility availability. The bridge maps WIA shared_vehicle envelopes to GBFS station_information and free_bike_status feeds.

7.6 MDS bridge

MDS (Mobility Data Specification) is the city-side data exchange for shared micromobility. The bridge enables cities to ingest WIA shared_vehicle telemetry through their existing MDS pipelines for permit compliance and infrastructure planning.

7.7 WIA family integration

WIA-OMNI-API holds agency credentials, operator credentials, rider identity. WIA Time-Series Data composes — high-cadence vehicle telemetry rides as time-series envelopes when bulk historical analysis is needed. WIA-AIR-SHIELD provides transport hardening for cross-organisation federation.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in transportation network infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, vehicle_position update frequency: most US transit agencies emit every 15-30 seconds; high-frequency rail systems emit every second. Hosts MUST provision sufficient throughput. Second, rider trip consent revocation: when a rider revokes consent, the data must stop flowing within minutes; the consent envelope MUST carry an explicit revocation timestamp. Third, GTFS feed updates: agencies typically publish weekly GTFS updates; the standard\x27s schedule envelope versioning lets downstream consumers detect new feeds without polling.

Multi-Modal Trip Planning Pattern

The standard's core use case is multi-modal trip planning: a traveller wants to go from point A to point B and the planner needs to combine bus, rail, micromobility, and walking. The standard makes this possible without per-vendor integration: the traveller's app subscribes to one WIA-UNI-008 host that aggregates feeds from all participating providers in the city. Trip search returns multi-modal options ranked by total time, total cost, or carbon footprint. The traveller selects an option and the app initiates rider_trip envelopes with explicit consent for the trip duration only.
