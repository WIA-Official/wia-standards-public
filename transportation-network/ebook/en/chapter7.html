CHAPTER 7

Phase 4 - International Integration

7.1 Trans-Siberian Railway Connection

Connecting the Trans-Korean Railway to the Trans-Siberian Railway creates a land bridge from Busan to Europe:

Route Architecture

Busan → Seoul → Pyongyang → Sinuiju → Dandong (China) → Beijing → Ulaanbaatar (Mongolia) → Irkutsk (Russia) → Moscow → Berlin → Paris

Technical Challenges

  • Track Gauge: Korea/China use 1435mm (standard), Russia/Mongolia use 1520mm (Russian gauge)
  • Solution: Bogie exchange stations at border (e.g., Zabaikalsk)
  • Benefit: 13-day freight transit Busan-Rotterdam vs. 30 days by sea

7.2 Unified Ticketing System

Trans-Eurasian rail tickets spanning multiple operators and countries with integrated pricing, reservations, and digital credentials.

7.3 Customs and Border Control

Integration with international customs frameworks:

  • WCO Data Model: Standardized customs declarations
  • AEO Certification: Authorized Economic Operator for expedited clearance
  • Single Window: One-stop submission for all border agencies
  • Pre-clearance: Data submission before arrival for faster processing

7.4 Regional Cooperation

Northeast Asia Transportation Network

Multilateral cooperation among Korea, China, Russia, Mongolia, and Japan for unified standards, joint infrastructure investment, and cross-border facilitation.

ASEAN Connectivity

Korea-ASEAN Rail-Road-Maritime Corridor connecting Busan to Singapore via China, Laos, Thailand, and Malaysia.

7.5 WIA Ecosystem Integration

Connection with other WIA standards:

  • WIA-UNI-001: Inter-Korean data exchange protocols
  • WIA-UNI-004: Economic integration for trade facilitation
  • WIA-UNI-005: Infrastructure integration standards
  • WIA-FIN-*: Payment systems for ticketing and toll collection

7.6 International Standards Mapping

  • ISO 28000: Supply chain security management
  • UIC RIC: Railway interoperability codes
  • ICAO Annex 10: Aeronautical telecommunications
  • IMO SOLAS: Maritime safety regulations
