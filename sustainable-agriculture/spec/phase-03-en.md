# WIA-ENE-070: Sustainable Agriculture Standard
## Phase 3: Verification & Testing

### 3.1 Overview

Phase 3 validates that implemented practices are delivering intended sustainability outcomes and identifies areas for improvement before formal certification.

**Duration**: 6-12 months  
**Key Outcome**: Verified compliance with sustainability standards and performance data

### 3.2 Performance Measurement

#### 3.2.1 Environmental Metrics
- **Soil Health**: Re-test organic matter, pH, nutrients, compare to baseline (target: +0.2-0.5% OM)
- **Water Use**: Calculate water use efficiency, compare to baseline (target: 20-40% reduction)
- **Biodiversity**: Species counts, compare to baseline (target: 30-50% increase)
- **Carbon**: Estimate GHG emissions and sequestration (target: net reduction 40-60%)
- **Energy**: Track fuel and electricity use (target: 15-30% reduction per unit output)

#### 3.2.2 Agronomic Performance
- **Yields**: Compare to baseline and regional averages
- **Crop quality**: Visual quality, nutrient content, market grades
- **Pest/disease incidence**: Document severity, control effectiveness
- **Weed pressure**: Assess weed populations, management success

#### 3.2.3 Economic Performance
- **Income**: Revenue by product, compare to baseline
- **Costs**: Input costs, labor costs, total per hectare
- **Profitability**: Net income, profit margins
- **ROI**: Return on sustainability investments

#### 3.2.4 Social Indicators
- **Worker welfare**: Wages, working conditions, safety incidents
- **Community impact**: Local employment, contributions, engagement
- **Knowledge sharing**: Training provided, field days hosted

### 3.3 Record Review

#### 3.3.1 Documentation Audit
- **Completeness**: All required records present and up-to-date
- **Accuracy**: Cross-check records with physical evidence
- **Traceability**: Follow products from field to sale
- **Compliance**: All inputs approved for use, no prohibited substances

#### 3.3.2 Common Documentation Issues
- Incomplete field activity logs
- Missing input labels/receipts
- Gaps in harvest records
- Unclear product segregation
- Insufficient training documentation

**Remediation**: Implement corrective actions, update procedures, additional training

### 3.4 Third-Party Pre-Assessment

Before formal certification, engage certifying body for pre-assessment:
- **Mock audit**: Simulate certification inspection
- **Gap identification**: List non-conformances, minor vs. major
- **Corrective action plan**: Address gaps before official audit
- **Timeline**: Allow 3-6 months between pre-assessment and certification audit

Benefits: Reduces risk of failed certification, identifies overlooked issues, builds confidence

### 3.5 Verification Methods

#### 3.5.1 On-Site Inspection
- Field observations: Visual assessment of practices, soil quality, biodiversity
- Infrastructure review: Storage, equipment, buffers, signage
- Input verification: Check inputs against allowed lists, inspect labels
- Interview: Discuss practices with farmer and workers
- Record review: Examine documentation on-site

#### 3.5.2 Sampling and Testing
- **Soil sampling**: Verify organic matter, residue testing if suspected contamination
- **Product sampling**: Test for pesticide residues (especially if transitioning)
- **Water testing**: If quality concerns
- **Tissue testing**: Plant nutrient status

#### 3.5.3 Remote Verification
- **Satellite imagery**: Verify land use, cover cropping, deforestation monitoring
- **Digital records**: Review uploaded field data, photos
- **Video calls**: Virtual farm tours for preliminary assessment

### 3.6 Non-Conformance Management

When issues identified:
1. **Document**: Describe non-conformance clearly
2. **Classify**: Minor (easily corrected) vs. Major (threatens certification)
3. **Root cause analysis**: Understand why it occurred
4. **Corrective action**: Immediate fix
5. **Preventive action**: Systemic changes to prevent recurrence
6. **Verification**: Confirm issue resolved
7. **Timeline**: Major non-conformances must be resolved before certification

Common non-conformances:
- Use of prohibited substance (major)
- Inadequate buffer zones (major)
- Incomplete records (minor-major depending on extent)
- Lack of staff training (minor)
- Improper composting (minor-major)

### 3.7 Continuous Improvement Plan

Based on verification results:
- **Strengths**: Practices performing well, maintain and share
- **Weaknesses**: Areas needing improvement, prioritize actions
- **Opportunities**: New practices, technologies to explore
- **Threats**: Emerging challenges (new pests, market changes, climate)

**SWOT Analysis** guides refinement of management plan

### 3.8 Stakeholder Reporting

Share results with:
- **Internal**: Farm management, workers - celebrate successes, discuss challenges
- **Technical assistance**: Consultants, extension - get advice on improvements
- **Buyers**: Demonstrate sustainability performance, build trust
- **Community**: Transparency, show commitment to sustainability
- **Funders**: Grant/loan providers, carbon projects - report on outcomes

### 3.9 Data Management and Analysis

- **Database**: Compile all monitoring data in organized system
- **Trends**: Analyze multi-year trends in soil health, yields, costs
- **Benchmarking**: Compare to other farms, regional averages, standards
- **Visualization**: Charts, graphs, maps to communicate results
- **Interpretation**: What do the data mean? Lessons learned?

### 3.10 Preparation for Certification Audit

- **Schedule audit**: Contact certifying body, arrange date (typically during growing season)
- **Prepare records**: Ensure all documentation accessible, organized
- **Site preparation**: Clean, signage, easy inspector access
- **Staff preparation**: Brief workers on audit process, role
- **Confidence**: Mock audit has addressed gaps, ready for real thing

### 3.11 Phase 3 Deliverables

✓ Performance measurement report (environmental, economic, social)  
✓ Complete, verified records  
✓ Pre-assessment findings and corrective actions  
✓ Continuous improvement plan  
✓ Stakeholder communication materials  
✓ Certification audit readiness  

### 3.12 Phase 3 Success Criteria

- [ ] All sustainability metrics measured and documented
- [ ] Environmental performance meets or exceeds targets
- [ ] Economic viability maintained or improved
- [ ] Social indicators positive
- [ ] Records complete, accurate, compliant
- [ ] Pre-assessment conducted, gaps addressed
- [ ] Continuous improvement plan in place
- [ ] Farm and staff ready for certification audit

**Upon completion of Phase 3, proceed to Phase 4: Certification & Maintenance**
