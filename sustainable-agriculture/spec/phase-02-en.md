# WIA-ENE-070: Sustainable Agriculture Standard
## Phase 2: Implementation

### 2.1 Overview

Phase 2 focuses on the actual implementation of sustainable agricultural practices identified in Phase 1. This is the action phase where planned changes are put into practice.

**Duration**: 1-2 years  
**Key Outcome**: Sustainable practices operational across the farm

### 2.2 Soil Health Implementation

#### 2.2.1 Reduced/No-Till Systems
- Transition from conventional tillage to reduced or no-till
- Acquire or adapt equipment for direct seeding
- Manage crop residues for surface cover
- Monitor soil structure improvements

#### 2.2.2 Cover Cropping
- Select appropriate cover crop species for climate and goals
- Establish cover crop planting schedule
- Integrate into crop rotation
- Manage termination timing and methods

#### 2.2.3 Organic Matter Management
- Apply compost at recommended rates (5-20 tons/ha)
- Source or produce quality organic amendments
- Incorporate into soil management plan
- Track organic matter changes over time

### 2.3 Water Management Implementation

#### 2.3.1 Irrigation Efficiency
- Install high-efficiency irrigation (drip, micro-sprinklers)
- Implement soil moisture monitoring
- Adopt ET-based irrigation scheduling
- Reduce water usage by 20-40%

#### 2.3.2 Water Conservation
- Install rainwater harvesting systems
- Improve soil water retention through organic matter
- Mulch to reduce evaporation
- Implement contour farming on slopes

### 2.4 Integrated Pest Management

#### 2.4.1 Monitoring and Scouting
- Establish regular field scouting protocols (2-3x/week)
- Install monitoring traps (pheromone, sticky, etc.)
- Train staff on pest identification and thresholds
- Maintain scouting records

#### 2.4.2 Cultural Controls
- Implement crop rotations disrupting pest cycles
- Plant resistant varieties where available
- Adjust planting dates to avoid peak pest periods
- Maintain field sanitation

#### 2.4.3 Biological Control
- Create habitat for beneficial insects
- Release biological control agents as needed
- Conserve natural enemies through reduced pesticides
- Monitor beneficial insect populations

#### 2.4.4 Selective Pesticide Use
- Transition to organic-approved pesticides
- Use only when economic thresholds exceeded
- Choose selective, short-residual products
- Rotate modes of action to prevent resistance

### 2.5 Nutrient Management

#### 2.5.1 Organic Fertility
- Apply organic fertilizers based on soil tests
- Implement 4R nutrient stewardship
- Integrate leguminous cover crops for nitrogen
- Compost manure to reduce pathogens and nutrient loss

#### 2.5.2 Precision Application
- Variable rate application where appropriate
- Banding or fertigation for efficiency
- Split applications to match crop uptake
- Reduce synthetic fertilizer use by 30-50%

### 2.6 Biodiversity Enhancement

#### 2.6.1 Habitat Creation
- Plant pollinator strips (3-5% of area)
- Establish hedgerows along field margins
- Create beetle banks for beneficial insects
- Install bird boxes, bee hotels

#### 2.6.2 Crop Diversity
- Diversify crop rotations (4+ crops)
- Intercropping where appropriate
- Maintain field borders with native plants
- Preserve on-farm wild areas

### 2.7 Climate-Smart Practices

#### 2.7.1 Carbon Sequestration
- Implement practices sequestering 0.5-1.5 tCO₂e/ha/year
- No-till, cover crops, agroforestry, compost
- Track soil carbon changes
- Potentially enroll in carbon credit programs

#### 2.7.2 GHG Emission Reduction
- Optimize nitrogen fertilizer use (reduce N₂O)
- Improve manure management
- Increase energy efficiency
- Adopt renewable energy where feasible

### 2.8 Record Keeping and Documentation

#### 2.8.1 Required Records
- **Field activities**: Tillage, planting, fertilizer, pesticides, irrigation, harvest
- **Inputs**: Purchases with labels/receipts, organic compliance verification
- **Yields**: By field, crop, date
- **Sales**: Buyers, quantities, prices, organic vs. conventional segregation
- **Training**: Staff education, dates, topics

#### 2.8.2 Record Keeping Systems
- Paper logs (minimum)
- Spreadsheets (better organization)
- Farm management software (ideal)
- Daily or weekly updating (timely, accurate)

### 2.9 Training During Implementation

- Hands-on practice training as new techniques introduced
- Troubleshooting sessions when challenges arise
- Peer farmer exchanges to learn from others' experience
- Consultant or extension agent site visits for guidance
- Participation in farmer networks and learning groups

### 2.10 Adaptive Management

- Regular review of practice performance (monthly/quarterly)
- Adjust based on observations (pest pressure, yields, costs)
- Document lessons learned
- Modify Organic System Plan as needed
- Stay informed about new practices, research, technologies

### 2.11 Buffer Zone Establishment

For organic/sustainable certification:
- **Buffer zones**: 7-30 meters between certified and conventional areas
- **Physical barriers**: Hedgerows, ditches, roads
- **Temporal buffers**: Time separation if equipment shared
- **Purpose**: Prevent contamination from drift, runoff, inadvertent mixing

### 2.12 Equipment and Infrastructure

- Clean equipment before use in certified areas
- Dedicated storage for organic vs. conventional inputs
- Proper composting infrastructure if using manure
- Irrigation infrastructure for efficiency
- Processing/packing facilities meeting standards

### 2.13 Phase 2 Milestones

**Month 3**: Core practices implemented (cover crops, reduced tillage)  
**Month 6**: Monitoring systems operational, first data collected  
**Month 12**: Full integration across farm, initial results visible  
**Month 18-24**: Practices refined, ready for verification

### 2.14 Phase 2 Success Criteria

- [ ] All planned sustainable practices operational
- [ ] Monitoring data shows practice adoption
- [ ] Soil health improvements measurable
- [ ] Pest/disease managed without prohibited inputs
- [ ] Water use efficiency improving
- [ ] Complete, accurate records maintained
- [ ] Staff trained and competent in new practices
- [ ] Crop yields maintained or improving
- [ ] Input costs reduced
- [ ] Ready for third-party verification

**Upon completion of Phase 2, proceed to Phase 3: Verification & Testing**
