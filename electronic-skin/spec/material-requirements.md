# WIA-SEMI-016 Material Requirements

## Specifications for Electronic Skin Materials

**Version**: 1.0
**Date**: 2025-01-01

---

## 1. Substrate Materials

### 1.1 Elastomeric Substrates

**PDMS (Polydimethylsiloxane)**:
- Purity: ≥99%
- Crosslinker ratio: 10:1 to 50:1 (base:crosslinker)
- Curing: 60-80°C for 2-4 hours or RT for 48 hours
- Shore hardness: 40-60 A
- Tensile strength: ≥3 MPa
- Biocompatibility: USP Class VI for medical applications

**Thermoplastic Polyurethane (TPU)**:
- Medical grade for Class A devices
- Shore hardness: 70A to 85A
- Melt flow index: 10-30 g/10min
- Water absorption: <1%
- Compliance: ISO 10993, FDA master files

**Ecoflex Silicone**:
- Shore hardness: 00-30
- Elongation: ≥900%
- Platinum-catalyzed (biocompatible)

### 1.2 Hydrogel Substrates

**Polyvinyl Alcohol (PVA)**:
- Molecular weight: 50,000-100,000 g/mol
- Degree of hydrolysis: 87-99%
- Water content: 70-90%
- Freeze-thaw cycles for crosslinking: 3-5

## 2. Conductive Materials

### 2.1 Silver Nanowires (AgNW)

**Specifications**:
- Diameter: 30-50 nm
- Length: 10-30 μm
- Aspect ratio: >200
- Purity: ≥99.9% Ag
- Dispersion: Isopropanol or water-based
- Concentration: 0.5-5 mg/mL
- Shelf life: 6 months at 4°C

**Performance Requirements**:
- Sheet resistance: <100 Ω/sq
- Optical transmittance: >85% at 550 nm
- Adhesion: Pass tape test (ASTM D3359)

### 2.2 Carbon Nanotubes (CNT)

**Specifications**:
- Type: Single-wall (SWCNT) or multi-wall (MWCNT)
- Purity: ≥95% (metal catalyst <5%)
- Diameter: 1-2 nm (SWCNT), 10-30 nm (MWCNT)
- Length: 5-30 μm
- Conductivity: ≥10³ S/cm in bulk
- Dispersion: Requires surfactant (SDS, Triton X-100)

### 2.3 Conductive Polymers

**PEDOT:PSS**:
- Grade: Clevios PH1000 or equivalent
- Conductivity: 800-1200 S/cm (with additives)
- Viscosity: 50-200 mPa·s
- Solid content: 1.0-1.3 wt%
- Additives: DMSO, EG (5-10 vol% for enhanced conductivity)
- Biocompatibility: Suitable for skin contact

---

## 3. Dielectric Materials

**For Capacitive Sensors**:
- Permittivity (εᵣ): 2-10
- Dielectric loss tangent: <0.01
- Breakdown voltage: >10 MV/m
- Compressibility: High (for enhanced sensitivity)

**Recommended Materials**:
- Porous PDMS (εᵣ ≈ 2-3, highly compressible)
- PVDF (εᵣ ≈ 10-12, ferroelectric)
- Silicone foams

---

## 4. Encapsulation Materials

### 4.1 Polymer Encapsulation

**Medical-grade Silicone**:
- Biocompatibility: ISO 10993 compliant
- Water vapor transmission: <10 g/m²/day
- Thickness: 10-50 μm

**Parylene-C**:
- Deposition: CVD process
- Thickness: 0.5-10 μm
- Pinhole density: <1 per cm²
- Biocompatibility: USP Class VI

**Polyurethane**:
- Medical grade for skin contact
- Flexibility: Must not crack at substrate strain levels

### 4.2 Barrier Layers

For long-term stability:
- Multi-layer: Organic/inorganic alternating
- Inorganic: Al₂O₃, SiO₂ (ALD deposited)
- WVTR: <10⁻⁴ g/m²/day for implantable devices

---

## 5. Adhesives

### 5.1 Medical Adhesives

**For Skin Contact**:
- Type: Medical-grade acrylic adhesive
- Peel strength: 0.5-2 N/cm
- Tack: Immediate or repositionable
- Wear time: Up to 7 days for extended wear
- Biocompatibility: ISO 10993-10 compliant (no sensitization/irritation)

**Examples**:
- 3M 1524 medical tape
- Scapa Health series

### 5.2 Structural Adhesives

**For Layer Bonding**:
- Silicone adhesives for PDMS bonding
- UV-curable adhesives (low temperature cure)
- Oxygen plasma bonding (no adhesive required)

---

## 6. Functional Additives

### 6.1 Self-Healing Agents

**Dynamic Bond Systems**:
- Hydrogen bonding polymers (PU with high urea content)
- Disulfide crosslinks
- Metal-ligand coordination (Fe³⁺ with carboxylates)

**Microcapsule Systems**:
- Core: Healing agent (DCPD, epoxy)
- Shell: Urea-formaldehyde, melamine-formaldehyde
- Size: 1-100 μm
- Loading: 5-15 wt%

---

## 7. Prohibited Materials

The following materials shall NOT be used in Class A devices:
- Lead-containing materials (except where specifically allowed by regulations)
- Mercury and mercury compounds
- Cadmium and cadmium compounds
- Latex (high allergen risk)
- Materials with known cytotoxicity or sensitization

---

## 8. Material Sourcing and Traceability

### 8.1 Supplier Requirements
- ISO 9001 certified suppliers preferred
- Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) required
- Certificates of Analysis (CoA) for each lot
- Traceability to raw material batch

### 8.2 Incoming Inspection
- Visual inspection: No contamination, correct labeling
- Identity confirmation: FTIR, Raman, or equivalent
- Key property verification: Conductivity, viscosity, etc.
- Accept/reject criteria documented

### 8.3 Storage Conditions
- Temperature: Per supplier specification (typically 4-25°C)
- Humidity: <60% RH for moisture-sensitive materials
- Light protection: For UV-sensitive materials
- Shelf life tracking: FIFO (First In, First Out)

---

## 9. Environmental and Sustainability

### 9.1 Preferred Characteristics
- Bio-derived or recyclable materials preferred
- Low environmental impact processing
- Minimal hazardous waste generation
- Biodegradable for temporary applications (when appropriate)

### 9.2 Conflict Minerals
Compliance with Dodd-Frank Act and EU Conflict Minerals Regulation

---

## 10. Material Safety

### 10.1 Handling Precautions
- Nanomaterials: Avoid inhalation, use fume hood, PPE
- Solvents: Proper ventilation, fire safety
- Curing agents: Some are skin sensitizers, wear gloves

### 10.2 Disposal
- Follow local regulations for hazardous waste
- Nanomaterial waste: Special handling may be required
- Document disposal per ISO 14001 (if certified)
