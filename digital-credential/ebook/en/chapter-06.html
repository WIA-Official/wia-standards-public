Chapter 6: Verification Systems

How Credential Verification Works

Digital credential verification is a multi-step process that cryptographically proves the authenticity, integrity, and validity of educational credentials in seconds.

Verification Steps

1. Credential Presentation

The holder presents their credential to the verifier via QR code, link, or direct API call.

2. Signature Verification

The verifier checks the cryptographic signature to ensure the credential hasn't been tampered with.

3. Issuer Resolution

The verifier looks up the issuer's DID to get their public key and verify they're a legitimate institution.

4. Revocation Check

The system checks if the credential has been revoked or suspended.

5. Validity Period Check

Confirms the credential is being used within its valid date range.

6. Trust Registry Check

Verifies the issuer is accredited and authorized to issue this type of credential.

Verification Results

Verification returns detailed information:

  • Valid: Credential is authentic and current
  • Invalid: Signature doesn't match or data tampered
  • Revoked: Credential has been cancelled
  • Expired: Validity period has passed
  • Suspended: Temporarily invalid pending review

Trust Registries

Trust registries are authoritative lists of accredited credential issuers. They answer: "Is this institution authorized to issue this type of credential?"

National Education Registries

Government-maintained lists of accredited universities and colleges.

Professional Certification Registries

Industry bodies maintaining lists of authorized certifying organizations.

WIA Global Registry

International registry of verified educational institutions and their DIDs.

Privacy-Preserving Verification

Zero-Knowledge Proofs

Prove facts about credentials without revealing the credentials themselves.

Examples:

  • Prove GPA > 3.5 without revealing exact GPA
  • Prove graduation after 2020 without revealing exact year
  • Prove degree in STEM field without revealing specific major

Selective Disclosure

Share only the specific fields needed for verification. Hide sensitive information like student ID numbers, birth dates, or home addresses.
