About This Standard

The WIA-SLR-001 standard provides a comprehensive framework for understanding, monitoring, and responding to sea level rise - one of the most significant challenges of our time. As global temperatures continue to rise, thermal expansion of ocean water and melting of land-based ice sheets are causing sea levels to increase at an accelerating rate.

This ebook serves as a complete guide to the WIA-SLR-001 standard, covering everything from the fundamental science of sea level rise to practical implementation strategies for coastal communities, engineers, policymakers, and researchers. Each chapter builds upon the previous, creating a comprehensive understanding of this critical environmental challenge.

The standard draws upon the latest research from the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), satellite altimetry data from missions like Jason-3 and Sentinel-6, and tide gauge networks spanning over a century of measurements. It incorporates best practices from leading institutions worldwide and provides actionable guidance aligned with the WIA philosophy of benefiting all humanity - 弘益人間 (Hongik Ingan).
