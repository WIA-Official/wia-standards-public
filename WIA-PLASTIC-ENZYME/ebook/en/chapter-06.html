6.2 Pre-treatment Protocol

Effective pre-treatment is crucial for enzymatic degradation efficiency. The protocol addresses cleaning, size reduction, and crystallinity optimization.

1

Cleaning and Decontamination

Remove labels, adhesives, and contaminants that could inhibit enzyme activity.

  • Hot water wash: 80°C for 10 minutes to remove residues
  • Alkaline treatment: 1% NaOH for label removal
  • Rinse: Deionized water to neutral pH
  • Drying: Hot air at 60°C to <0.5% moisture
  • Visual inspection for remaining labels
  • pH verification (6.5-7.5)
  • Moisture content measurement
2

Size Reduction

Increase surface area for better enzyme access to polymer chains.

  • Primary grinding: Reduce to <10mm flakes
  • Secondary milling: Further reduce to <2mm particles
  • Optional micronization: <0.5mm for difficult substrates
Particle Size Surface Area Degradation Rate
10mm flakes Baseline 1.0x
2mm particles 5x higher 2.5x faster
0.5mm powder 20x higher 4x faster
3

Crystallinity Optimization

Reduce crystallinity to increase amorphous regions accessible to enzymes.

  • Thermal treatment: Brief heating above Tg (>75°C) followed by rapid cooling
  • Target: Reduce crystallinity from 30-40% to 15-25%
  • Verification: DSC analysis or FTIR crystallinity index

6.3 Enzymatic Reaction Protocol

The core degradation process requires careful control of enzyme loading, temperature, pH, and reaction time.

4

Reactor Preparation

  • Buffer: 50mM Tris-HCl or phosphate buffer, pH 8.0
  • Temperature: Pre-heat to 50-55°C
  • Agitation: Set impeller speed for gentle mixing (100-200 RPM)
  • Substrate loading: 10-15% (w/v) plastic substrate
5

Enzyme Addition

  • Primary enzyme (PETase): 2-3 mg/g substrate
  • Secondary enzyme (MHETase): 1-1.5 mg/g substrate
  • Addition method: Gradual addition over 30 minutes
  • Homogenization: Ensure complete dispersion before reaction start
6

Reaction Monitoring

  • Temperature logging: Continuous monitoring, maintain ±2°C
  • pH adjustment: Add buffer if pH deviates >0.5 units
  • Sampling: Every 6-12 hours for progress analysis
  • HPLC analysis: Monitor TPA, MHET, and BHET concentrations
7

Reaction Completion

  • Target: >95% substrate conversion
  • Endpoint criteria: TPA concentration plateau for 4+ hours
  • Typical duration: 24-96 hours depending on conditions

6.4 Monomer Recovery Protocol

Recovery of high-purity monomers is essential for circular economy applications.

8

Solid-Liquid Separation

  • Filtration: Remove undegraded residue
  • Enzyme inactivation: Heat to 80°C for 15 minutes
  • Clarification: Centrifugation at 10,000g for 20 minutes
9

TPA Crystallization

  • Acidification: Lower pH to 2.5 with HCl
  • Cooling: Reduce temperature to 4°C
  • Crystal collection: Filter precipitated TPA
  • Washing: Cold water wash to remove salts
  • Drying: Vacuum dry at 60°C
10

Ethylene Glycol Recovery

  • Evaporation: Concentrate remaining solution
  • Distillation: Vacuum distillation at 80°C
  • Purification: Molecular sieves for water removal

6.6 Safety Protocols

Safety is paramount in enzymatic degradation facilities:

Personal Protective Equipment

  • Lab coat or protective clothing
  • Safety glasses or face shield
  • Chemical-resistant gloves
  • Respiratory protection for powder handling

Chemical Hazards

  • Acids (pH adjustment): Corrosive, handle with care
  • Alkalis (cleaning): Caustic, avoid skin contact
  • Solvents (if used): Flammable, ensure ventilation

Biological Hazards

  • Enzyme proteins: Potential allergens with repeated exposure
  • Microbial contamination: Maintain sterility in production

6.7 Environmental Protocols

The WIA standard requires environmentally responsible operations:

  • Wastewater treatment: Neutralize pH before discharge
  • Enzyme inactivation: Ensure no active enzymes in effluent
  • Solid waste: Residue may contain non-degradable additives
  • Energy efficiency: Optimize heating/cooling cycles
  • Carbon accounting: Track emissions per kg of plastic processed

6.8 Documentation Requirements

All protocol steps must be documented for traceability and certification:

  • Batch records: Complete process parameters for each batch
  • Quality certificates: Analytical results for each product lot
  • Deviation reports: Document any departures from protocol
  • Equipment logs: Maintenance and calibration records
  • Training records: Personnel qualification documentation

6.9 Chapter Summary

Phase 3 protocols provide the operational framework for enzymatic plastic degradation:

  • Pre-treatment: Cleaning, size reduction, crystallinity optimization
  • Enzymatic reaction: Controlled conditions for efficient degradation
  • Monomer recovery: Purification to meet repolymerization specifications
  • Quality control: Analytical testing to ensure product quality
  • Safety and environmental: Responsible operation protocols
