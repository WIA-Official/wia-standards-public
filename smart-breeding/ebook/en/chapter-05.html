Chapter 5: Phase 3 — Federation Protocol

Phase 3 specifies how independent breeding organisations exchange data over time without merging into a central database. Federation matters because national herd-books, breeding cooperatives, AI/ET service providers, and academic genetic-evaluation centres each hold portions of the picture, and each is bound by different sharing agreements with their members.

5.1 Roles

RoleDescription
OriginHolds the canonical record (individual, genotype, phenotype) for animals it owns / registers
CooperativeAggregates phenotype records from members for joint genetic evaluation
Evaluation centreComputes GEBV from federated genotype + phenotype data
AI/ET serviceSubscribes to mating plans for semen / embryo distribution
AuditorHolds the audit log of every data exchange

5.2 Federation Handshake

   IDLE
     │ peer presents credential + ephemeral key
     ▼
   PENDING (origin verifies signature)
     │ valid
     ▼
   ACCEPTED (origin issues federation receipt; both sides persist)
     │ optional revocation
     ▼
   REVOKED

Receipts persist for at least 30 years per typical herd-book retention norms.

5.3 Replay Defence

Every signed envelope carries a 96-bit nonce and an RFC 3339 timestamp. Receivers MUST reject envelopes with skew > ±300 s, reject envelopes whose (signer, nonce) tuple has been seen within the last 600 s, and maintain the seen-nonce cache for at least 600 s. For high-volume cooperative aggregations the cache MAY be persistent across restarts.

5.4 Owner Consent

Animal-owner consent is the gating control on cross-organisation data sharing. Consent envelopes are signed by the owner and reference specific scopes (cooperative aggregation, public publication, commercial valuation, research). Origins MUST refuse cross-organisation reads whose owner consent does not cover the requested scope.

5.7 Conformance

A Phase 3 conformant implementation MUST implement the handshake state machine, honour replay-defence bounds, enforce owner consent before serving any non-public data, apply audience-based read controls, and maintain an append-only audit log of every cross-organisation read.
