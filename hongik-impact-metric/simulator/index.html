Deep Dive: The 7 Dimensions

❤️

1. Social Good

Social Good measures how your work strengthens communities and creates positive social connections. This dimension focuses on the quality and depth of human relationships fostered, community resilience built, and the degree of social inclusion achieved.

Key Metrics: People reached, engagement depth, inclusion score, community building initiatives, social capital generated, bridge-building between diverse groups.

♿

2. Accessibility

Accessibility evaluates how well your solution removes barriers for people with disabilities, language differences, economic constraints, or geographical limitations. True benefit means universal access.

Key Metrics: WCAG compliance level, multi-language support, economic accessibility, geographical reach, assistive technology compatibility, literacy-independent design.

3. Sustainability

Sustainability assesses environmental impact, resource efficiency, and long-term ecological viability. 弘益人間 extends beyond current humanity to include future generations and the planet that sustains us all.

Key Metrics: Carbon footprint reduction, renewable energy usage, circular economy practices, waste reduction, biodiversity protection, regenerative design principles.

🏥

4. Health & Wellbeing

Health & Wellbeing encompasses physical health, mental wellness, and emotional balance. This dimension recognizes that human flourishing requires holistic wellness across all aspects of life.

Key Metrics: Healthcare access improved, mental health support, stress reduction, work-life balance impact, preventive health measures, wellness program quality.

💰

5. Economic Equity

Economic Equity measures fair distribution of resources, opportunities, and economic benefits. True benefit to humanity requires reducing inequality and ensuring economic systems serve all people equitably.

Key Metrics: Wage equality, opportunities created, support for underserved communities, wealth distribution impact, financial literacy promotion, economic mobility enabled.

6. Education

Education evaluates knowledge sharing, skill development, and capacity building. Empowering people with education multiplies their ability to contribute to humanity and improve their own lives.

Key Metrics: Educational resources provided, people educated, skill transferability, curriculum quality, learning accessibility, long-term knowledge retention.

7. Innovation

Innovation assesses creative problem-solving, breakthrough thinking, and novel approaches to human challenges. This dimension recognizes that advancing humanity often requires thinking beyond conventional solutions.

Key Metrics: Solution novelty, scalability potential, open-source contribution, technology transfer, interdisciplinary collaboration, paradigm-shifting potential.
