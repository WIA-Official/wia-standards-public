Chapter 6: Certification Process

Understanding the journey from application to certification and beyond

Hongik Impact Certification isn't just a badge—it's a commitment to continuously measuring and improving your benefit to humanity. This chapter details the certification process, requirements for each level, and what certified organizations can expect.

Certification Levels Overview

The five certification levels represent progressive stages in an organization's impact journey. Each level builds on the previous one, requiring higher scores, more rigorous verification, and deeper commitment to 弘益人間.

🥉 Bronze Certification (HIS: 100-299)

Philosophy: Foundational commitment to impact measurement

Requirements:

  • Complete self-assessment and formal application
  • Minimum 1 year of operational history
  • Basic data across all seven dimensions
  • Self-reported metrics (verification not required)
  • Minimum stakeholder feedback (25+ responses or 10% of users, whichever is smaller)
  • HIS score 100-299

Assessment Process:

  • Document review (2-3 weeks)
  • Clarification questions as needed
  • No site visit required
  • Single assessor review

Cost: $500-$5,000 depending on organization size

Validity: 1 year; annual renewal required

Best For: Emerging organizations, startups, small nonprofits beginning their impact journey

🥈 Silver Certification (HIS: 300-499)

Philosophy: Established impact practices with clear improvement trajectory

Requirements:

  • Minimum 2 years operational history
  • Comprehensive data across all dimensions
  • Third-party verification for key metrics (carbon, wages, outcomes)
  • Robust stakeholder feedback (100+ responses or 20% of users)
  • Demonstrated improvement from previous assessment (if applicable)
  • HIS score 300-499

Assessment Process:

  • Comprehensive document review (3-4 weeks)
  • Stakeholder interviews (10+ conducted by WIA)
  • Optional site visit for organizations requesting it
  • Dual assessor review with consensus requirement

Cost: $1,000-$10,000 depending on organization size

Validity: 1 year; annual renewal required

Best For: Growing organizations with maturing impact programs

🥇 Gold Certification (HIS: 500-699)

Philosophy: Strong, verified impact with excellence in multiple dimensions

Requirements:

  • Minimum 3 years operational history
  • Longitudinal data showing sustained impact
  • Third-party verification for all quantitative metrics
  • Extensive stakeholder engagement (200+ responses or 30% of users)
  • Excellence (80+) in at least 3 dimensions
  • No dimension below 60
  • Published impact report available publicly
  • HIS score 500-699

Assessment Process:

  • In-depth document review (4-6 weeks)
  • Mandatory site visit (1-2 days)
  • Stakeholder interviews (20+ conducted by WIA)
  • Expert panel review for technical dimensions
  • Triple assessor review with consensus

Cost: $2,000-$15,000 depending on organization size

Validity: 2 years; biennial reassessment required

Best For: Established organizations seeking credible third-party validation of strong impact

⭐ Platinum Certification (HIS: 700-899)

Philosophy: Outstanding comprehensive impact; industry leadership

Requirements:

  • Minimum 5 years operational history
  • Multi-year longitudinal data with clear trends
  • Independent third-party audit of all impact claims
  • Comprehensive stakeholder feedback (500+ responses or 40% of users)
  • Excellence (85+) in at least 5 dimensions
  • No dimension below 70
  • Published annual impact reports with independent verification
  • Demonstrated thought leadership in impact measurement
  • HIS score 700-899

Assessment Process:

  • Rigorous document review (6-8 weeks)
  • Multi-day site visit (3-5 days)
  • Extensive stakeholder interviews (50+)
  • Expert panel reviews across all relevant domains
  • Comparison with best-in-class benchmarks
  • Senior assessor team review

Cost: $5,000-$30,000 depending on organization size

Validity: 2 years; annual in-depth monitoring with biennial full reassessment

Best For: Industry leaders seeking highest level of impact validation

💎 Diamond Certification (HIS: 900+)

Philosophy: Exceptional, transformative impact; embodiment of 弘益人間

Requirements:

  • Minimum 7 years operational history
  • Long-term impact data with proven sustained excellence
  • Continuous independent monitoring and verification
  • Comprehensive multi-stakeholder feedback
  • Excellence (90+) in at least 5 dimensions
  • No dimension below 75
  • Demonstrated systemic change and field advancement
  • Global recognition and thought leadership
  • Published research and knowledge sharing
  • HIS score 900+

Assessment Process:

  • Comprehensive review (8-12 weeks)
  • Extended site visit (1 week+)
  • Global stakeholder engagement
  • Multi-disciplinary expert panels
  • Academic research review of impact claims
  • WIA Board-level review and approval

Cost: $10,000-$50,000+ depending on organization size and complexity

Validity: 3 years; continuous monitoring with triennial full reassessment

Best For: Truly exceptional organizations setting the standard for impact

Note: Diamond certification cannot be directly applied for. Organizations must first achieve Platinum certification and demonstrate sustained excellence before being invited to pursue Diamond level.

Application Process

Step 1: Create WIA Account
Register at wia.org/register and complete your organization profile.
Step 2: Self-Assessment
Use the free self-assessment tool to estimate your HIS and identify data gaps.
Step 3: Choose Certification Level
Based on self-assessment and readiness, select target certification level.
Step 4: Collect Data
Gather required data across all seven dimensions (timeline varies by level).
Step 5: Submit Application
Complete online application, upload documentation, pay assessment fee.
Step 6: Assessment Period
WIA conducts review, verification, and stakeholder engagement (4-12 weeks depending on level).
Step 7: Receive Results
Comprehensive impact report with HIS, dimension scores, and recommendations.
Step 8: Certification Decision
If HIS meets threshold, certification granted. If not, detailed improvement roadmap provided.
Step 9: Ongoing Compliance
Annual or biennial reassessment required to maintain certification.

Certification Benefits

  • Listing in WIA Global Impact Directory
  • Comprehensive impact report for stakeholder communication
  • Access to WIA community of practice and learning network
  • Benchmarking data for continuous improvement

  • Featured case studies and thought leadership opportunities
  • Speaking opportunities at WIA conferences and events
  • Priority consideration for impact investment and funding
  • Media and PR support from WIA communications team
  • Executive coaching and strategic advising
  • Partnership opportunities with other certified organizations

Maintaining Certification

Certification is not permanent—it requires ongoing commitment and continuous improvement:

Level Renewal Frequency Renewal Requirements
Bronze Annual Updated data, maintained or improved HIS
Silver Annual Updated data, maintained or improved HIS, continued verification
Gold Biennial Full reassessment, demonstrated improvement, published report
Platinum Annual monitoring + Biennial full assessment Continuous data reporting, sustained excellence, thought leadership
Diamond Continuous monitoring + Triennial full assessment Real-time impact data, field advancement, global recognition
Important: If HIS drops below certification threshold at renewal, organization receives 6-month grace period to address issues. If HIS remains below threshold after grace period, certification is revoked (though organization can reapply once improvements are made).

Appeals & Disputes

Organizations may appeal certification decisions if they believe:

  • Factual errors in assessment
  • Procedural irregularities
  • Assessor bias or misconduct
  • Context not adequately considered

Appeals are reviewed by senior assessors not involved in original assessment. Decision timeline: 30 days. Appeal fee: $500 (refunded if appeal succeeds).
