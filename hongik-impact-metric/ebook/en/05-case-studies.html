Chapter 5: Case Studies

Real organizations, real impact, real lessons learned

Theory becomes meaningful through practice. This chapter presents detailed case studies of organizations that have implemented the Hongik Impact Metric across diverse sectors, geographies, and scales. Each case reveals unique insights about what it means to truly benefit all humanity.

Case Study 1: Sunrise Community Health

Sector: Healthcare | Location: Rural India | Scale: 50 employees, 25,000 patients/year

Background

Sunrise Community Health provides primary healthcare in underserved rural communities across three states in India. Founded in 2018, they use a hub-and-spoke model with mobile clinics reaching remote villages.

Implementation Journey

Sunrise began with WIA's self-assessment tool in early 2024. Their preliminary HIS estimate was 420, suggesting Silver certification potential. However, they discovered they were collecting minimal data on several key dimensions.

Over four months, they:

  • Implemented systematic patient outcome tracking
  • Conducted comprehensive carbon footprint analysis
  • Surveyed patients in multiple local languages
  • Documented their community health worker training program
  • Analyzed wage equity and staff wellbeing

Final Results

Hongik Impact Score: 672 (Gold Certification)

Social Good:89/100
Accessibility:85/100
Sustainability:64/100
Health & Wellbeing:96/100
Economic Equity:78/100
Education:91/100
Innovation:72/100

Key Strengths

  • Health & Wellbeing (96): Exceptional patient outcomes, holistic care approach, mental health integration, community health worker program
  • Education (91): Extensive health education, community health worker training, patient empowerment programs
  • Social Good (89): Deep community trust, strong social networks, inclusive governance

Growth Opportunities

  • Sustainability (64): High transportation emissions from mobile clinics; opportunity to transition to electric vehicles and renewable energy
  • Innovation (72): Strong execution but limited novel approaches; opportunity to document and share learnings more widely
Key Insight: Sunrise's excellence in health outcomes and community engagement compensated for lower sustainability scores. The assessment helped them recognize sustainability as their next frontier. They're now piloting solar-powered clinics and electric motorcycles for community health workers.
"The assessment validated our strengths and gave us a clear roadmap. We always knew we provided excellent care, but we hadn't considered our environmental impact. Now we're on track to be carbon-neutral by 2026."

— Dr. Priya Sharma, Founder & Medical Director

Case Study 2: OpenLearn Global

Sector: Education Technology | Location: Global (HQ: Kenya) | Scale: 15 employees, 2M+ users

Background

OpenLearn Global develops open-source educational software with a focus on African languages and contexts. Their platform provides free K-12 curriculum aligned with national standards across 12 African countries.

Implementation Journey

As a tech company, OpenLearn had extensive usage data but limited qualitative feedback. They invested heavily in stakeholder engagement, conducting surveys and interviews across their user base.

The most revealing finding: while they had millions of users, actual learning outcomes were mixed. Many students accessed content but weren't completing lessons or demonstrating mastery. This honest assessment temporarily lowered their Education score but led to critical product improvements.

Final Results

Hongik Impact Score: 781 (Platinum Certification)

Social Good:82/100
Accessibility:97/100
Sustainability:88/100
Health & Wellbeing:71/100
Economic Equity:76/100
Education:94/100
Innovation:95/100

Key Strengths

  • Accessibility (97): Works offline, 18 languages, low bandwidth, free, screen reader compatible, designed for basic smartphones
  • Innovation (95): Novel approach to contextualized education, open-source model, AI-powered adaptive learning
  • Education (94): After product improvements, demonstrated strong learning outcomes and high completion rates

Lessons Learned

The Honesty Paradox: OpenLearn's willingness to honestly assess their limitations initially resulted in lower scores but ultimately led to genuine improvements. By their second assessment cycle, their Education score jumped from 78 to 94 based on real product enhancements.
"We thought we were measuring impact through usage numbers. The Hongik framework forced us to ask harder questions: Are students actually learning? Are outcomes equitable? Are we reaching the most marginalized? The answers weren't always comfortable, but they made us better."

— James Mwangi, CEO

Case Study 3: Regenerative Harvest Cooperative

Sector: Agriculture | Location: United States (Midwest) | Scale: 200 farmer members, 50,000 acres

Background

Regenerative Harvest is a farmer-owned cooperative practicing and promoting regenerative agriculture— farming methods that restore soil health, sequester carbon, and enhance biodiversity while producing food.

Implementation Journey

The cooperative saw the Hongik Impact Metric as an opportunity to differentiate themselves in a crowded "sustainable agriculture" market and to communicate their value to customers, investors, and policymakers.

Their assessment process was collaborative, involving farmers, agronomists, soil scientists, and community members. They conducted extensive soil testing, carbon sequestration measurement, and economic impact analysis.

Final Results

Hongik Impact Score: 847 (Platinum Certification)

Social Good:88/100
Accessibility:71/100
Sustainability:98/100
Health & Wellbeing:85/100
Economic Equity:92/100
Education:83/100
Innovation:87/100

Key Strengths

  • Sustainability (98): Net carbon negative (sequestering more than emitting), biodiversity increase, water quality improvement, soil health restoration
  • Economic Equity (92): Worker-owned cooperative model, fair profit distribution, living wages, support for beginning farmers
  • Social Good (88): Strong cooperative culture, community supported agriculture programs, rural community revitalization

Business Impact

The Platinum certification opened new markets:

  • Partnership with major food retailer seeking verified sustainable suppliers
  • $5M impact investment at favorable terms
  • 20% price premium from sustainability-conscious consumers
  • Policy advocacy platform based on proven impact data
Key Insight: The cooperative discovered that their regenerative practices created cascading benefits across multiple dimensions—carbon sequestration (Sustainability), healthier food (Health), farmer prosperity (Economic Equity), and knowledge sharing (Education). This integration boosted their overall HIS significantly.

Case Study 4: TechBridge Foundation

Sector: Technology Access | Location: Southeast Asia (6 countries) | Scale: 120 employees, 500+ partner organizations

Background

TechBridge provides technology infrastructure, training, and support to nonprofits and social enterprises across Southeast Asia, helping them leverage technology for greater impact.

Implementation Challenge

TechBridge faced a unique measurement challenge: their impact is largely indirect—through the organizations they serve rather than direct beneficiaries. How do you measure impact when you're an impact enabler?

They developed an innovative approach:

  • Surveyed partner organizations about their enhanced capacity
  • Calculated aggregate impact of partner organizations
  • Attributed appropriate portion to TechBridge's enabling role
  • Measured both direct impact (training, tech provision) and indirect (partners' amplified impact)

Final Results

Hongik Impact Score: 723 (Platinum Certification)

Social Good:91/100
Accessibility:94/100
Sustainability:77/100
Health & Wellbeing:68/100
Economic Equity:81/100
Education:96/100
Innovation:84/100

Measurement Innovation

The Multiplier Effect: TechBridge's approach to measuring indirect impact has become a model for other intermediary organizations. WIA now includes their methodology in assessment guidelines for capacity-building organizations.
"We always knew we were having impact, but we struggled to quantify it. The Hongik framework's flexibility allowed us to capture our unique value proposition while maintaining rigor. Our Platinum certification has been transformative for fundraising and partnerships."

— Lin Chen, Executive Director

Cross-Case Insights

Common Success Factors

  • Stakeholder-Centricity: Organizations that excel gather extensive feedback from those they serve
  • Honest Self-Assessment: Willingness to acknowledge limitations leads to genuine improvement
  • Integration Across Dimensions: The highest-impact organizations create synergies between dimensions
  • Long-Term Commitment: Treating assessment as beginning of journey, not one-time validation
  • Data-Informed Culture: Using measurement to drive continuous improvement

Common Challenges

  • Collecting historical data retroactively
  • Balancing quantitative metrics with qualitative depth
  • Engaging beneficiaries in meaningful feedback
  • Measuring long-term impact with limited time horizons
  • Attributing impact in complex, multi-stakeholder contexts

Sector-Specific Patterns

Sector Typical Strengths Common Growth Areas
Healthcare Health & Wellbeing, Social Good Sustainability, Innovation
Education Education, Accessibility Economic Equity, Sustainability
Environmental Sustainability, Innovation Economic Equity, Health
Technology Innovation, Accessibility, Education Social Good, Health & Wellbeing
Economic Development Economic Equity, Social Good Sustainability, Health

These patterns aren't limitations—they're opportunities. Organizations can differentiate themselves by excelling in dimensions their sector typically overlooks.
