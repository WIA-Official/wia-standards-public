The pathway from foundational impact to exceptional 弘익人間 embodiment.

5.1 The Certification Ladder

The Hongik Impact Metric certification system is structured as a progressive ladder, with each level representing increasingly sophisticated impact measurement practices and verified performance. This design serves multiple purposes:

  • Accessibility: Organizations at any stage can engage meaningfully
  • Aspiration: Clear pathway for improvement and growth
  • Differentiation: Recognition of genuine excellence at higher levels
  • Credibility: Verification rigor scales with claims magnitude
  • Continuous improvement: Built-in incentive to enhance impact over time
Level HIS Range Philosophy Operational History Validity Period
Bronze 100-299 Foundational commitment 1+ years 1 year
Silver 300-499 Established practices 2+ years 1 year
Gold 500-699 Strong verified impact 3+ years 2 years
Platinum 700-899 Outstanding leadership 5+ years 2 years
Diamond 900-1000+ Exceptional embodiment 7+ years 3 years

5.2 Bronze Certification (HIS 100-299)

Bronze: Foundation

Bronze certification recognizes organizations taking their first serious steps in impact measurement. It establishes baseline practices and demonstrates commitment to the 弘익人間 philosophy.

Requirements

  • Minimum 1 year operational history
  • Complete self-assessment across all seven dimensions
  • Basic quantitative and qualitative data collection
  • Stakeholder feedback: 25+ responses OR 10% of users
  • HIS score: 100-299
  • Good faith attestation of data accuracy

Assessment Process

  • Document review by single WIA assessor (2-3 weeks)
  • Clarification questions as needed
  • No site visit required
  • Self-reported metrics acceptable (conservative estimates required)

Cost Structure

Small organizations (<$500K annual budget) $500-$1,500
Medium organizations ($500K-$5M) $1,500-$3,000
Large organizations (>$5M) $3,000-$5,000

Benefits

  • Digital certification badge for website/materials
  • Basic impact report with dimension breakdown
  • Access to WIA community and learning resources
  • Impact improvement roadmap
  • Annual newsletter with best practices

Maintaining Bronze

  • Annual renewal required
  • Updated metrics across dimensions
  • Renewed stakeholder feedback
  • Improvement plan if HIS declines

5.3 Silver Certification (HIS 300-499)

Silver: Established Practice

Silver certification demonstrates mature impact measurement practices with clear improvement trajectory and third-party verification of key claims.

Requirements

  • Minimum 2 years operational history
  • Comprehensive data across all dimensions
  • Third-party verification for key metrics (carbon, wages, major outcomes)
  • Stakeholder feedback: 100+ responses OR 20% of users
  • Demonstrated improvement from previous assessment (if applicable)
  • HIS score: 300-499

Assessment Process

  • Comprehensive document review (3-4 weeks)
  • Dual assessor review with consensus requirement
  • Stakeholder interviews (10+ conducted by WIA)
  • Optional site visit if organization requests
  • Third-party verification audit of key claims

Excellence Criteria (must meet 3+)

  • At least one dimension scoring 80+
  • No dimension below 40
  • Year-over-year improvement in weighted score
  • Stakeholder satisfaction >75%
  • Published annual impact report

Cost Structure

Small organizations $1,000-$3,000
Medium organizations $3,000-$6,000
Large organizations $6,000-$10,000

Additional Benefits

  • All Bronze benefits, plus:
  • Detailed improvement roadmap with specific recommendations
  • Benchmarking data against similar organizations
  • Case study consideration for WIA publications
  • Enhanced community participation (monthly roundtables)
  • Impact measurement training discount (20%)

5.4 Gold Certification (HIS 500-699)

Gold: Strong Verified Impact

Gold certification represents strong, independently verified impact with excellence across multiple dimensions. Organizations at this level demonstrate impact measurement best practices.

Requirements

  • Minimum 3 years operational history with longitudinal data
  • Third-party verification for ALL quantitative metrics
  • Stakeholder feedback: 200+ responses OR 30% of users
  • Excellence (80+) in at least 3 dimensions
  • No dimension below 60
  • Published impact report available publicly
  • HIS score: 500-699

Assessment Process

  • In-depth document review (4-6 weeks)
  • Mandatory site visit (1-2 days)
  • Stakeholder interviews (20+ conducted by WIA)
  • Expert panel review for technical dimensions
  • Triple assessor review with consensus
  • Comparative benchmarking against best-in-class
  • Total timeline: 16-20 weeks

Excellence Criteria (must meet all)

  • At least 3 dimensions scoring 80+
  • All dimensions above 60
  • Published annual impact reports (2+ years)
  • Stakeholder satisfaction >80%
  • Evidence of field contribution (publications, presentations, methodology sharing)

Cost Structure

Small organizations $2,000-$5,000
Medium organizations $5,000-$10,000
Large organizations $10,000-$15,000

Additional Benefits

  • All Silver benefits, plus:
  • Premium certification badge (animated digital badge)
  • Featured in WIA showcase and annual report
  • Invitation to WIA advisory councils
  • Speaking opportunities at WIA events
  • Priority support for impact challenges
  • Two-year certification validity (biennial renewal)

Maintaining Gold

  • Biennial renewal (every 2 years)
  • Annual progress reports required
  • Maintained or improved HIS
  • Continued stakeholder engagement
  • Public impact reporting

5.5 Platinum Certification (HIS 700-899)

Platinum: Outstanding Leadership

Platinum certification recognizes outstanding comprehensive impact and industry leadership in translating the 弘익人間 philosophy into measurable results.

Requirements

  • Minimum 5 years operational history with multi-year trend data
  • Independent third-party audit of ALL impact claims
  • Stakeholder feedback: 500+ responses OR 40% of users
  • Excellence (85+) in at least 5 dimensions
  • No dimension below 70
  • Published annual impact reports with independent verification
  • Demonstrated thought leadership in impact measurement
  • Contribution to field advancement
  • HIS score: 700-899

Assessment Process

  • Rigorous document review (6-8 weeks)
  • Multi-day site visit (3-5 days) including operations observation
  • Extensive stakeholder interviews (50+)
  • Expert panel reviews across all relevant domains
  • Supply chain assessment (if applicable)
  • Unintended consequences analysis
  • Long-term sustainability evaluation
  • Senior assessor team review
  • Board-level review for borderline cases
  • Total timeline: 20-24 weeks

Thought Leadership Requirements (must meet 3+)

  • Published peer-reviewed research on impact measurement
  • Presented at 3+ conferences/events on impact practices
  • Open-source methodology/tools shared with field
  • Training/mentoring provided to other organizations
  • Policy influence or advocacy for impact standards

Cost Structure

Small organizations $5,000-$10,000
Medium organizations $10,000-$20,000
Large organizations $20,000-$30,000

Distinguished Benefits

  • All Gold benefits, plus:
  • Platinum badge and public recognition
  • Guaranteed case study in WIA publications
  • Permanent seat on relevant WIA advisory councils
  • Keynote speaking opportunities
  • Direct line to WIA leadership
  • Co-branding opportunities for initiatives
  • Impact measurement methodology certification

5.6 Diamond Certification (HIS 900-1000+)

Diamond: Exceptional Embodiment

Diamond certification represents the highest level of verified impact—exceptional, transformative benefit to humanity that genuinely embodies 弘益人間. This level cannot be directly applied for; organizations must first achieve Platinum and be invited.

Requirements

  • Minimum 7 years operational history with proven sustained excellence
  • Continuous independent monitoring and verification
  • Comprehensive multi-stakeholder feedback (1000+ OR 50%)
  • Excellence (90+) in at least 5 dimensions
  • No dimension below 75
  • Demonstrated systemic change and field advancement
  • Global recognition and thought leadership
  • Published research contributions
  • HIS score: 900-1000+

Exceptional Impact Criteria (must demonstrate 3+)

  • Paradigm-shifting impact in relevant field
  • Replication by others demonstrating field-wide influence
  • Policy change or market transformation attributable to work
  • Recognition by academic/professional communities
  • Innovative methodologies shared openly and adopted widely
  • Multi-generational or permanent impact trajectory

Assessment Process

  • Comprehensive review (8-12 weeks)
  • Extended site visit (1 week+)
  • Global stakeholder engagement (100+ interviews)
  • Multi-disciplinary expert panels
  • Academic research review of impact claims
  • Comparative analysis with historical high-performers
  • WIA Board-level review and approval
  • External validation from field experts
  • Total timeline: 24-48 weeks (invitation-only)

Cost Structure

By invitation only, costs negotiated based on scope: $10,000-$50,000+

Lifetime Benefits

  • All Platinum benefits, plus:
  • Diamond badge—highest recognition
  • Permanent recognition in WIA hall of excellence
  • Dedicated profile page on WIA website
  • Documentary/media feature consideration
  • Global speaking tour support
  • Methodology archived as WIA best practice
  • Legacy contribution to 弘益人間 knowledge base
  • Three-year certification validity

Maintaining Diamond

  • Continuous monitoring with real-time data sharing
  • Triennial full reassessment
  • Ongoing thought leadership
  • Field advancement contributions
  • Ambassador role for WIA standards

Key Takeaways

  • The five certification levels (Bronze through Diamond) form a progressive ladder, with operational history requirements increasing from 1 year to 7+ years and verification rigor scaling proportionally.
  • Bronze certification (HIS 100-299) provides accessible entry point with self-assessment, while Silver (300-499) requires third-party verification of key metrics.
  • Gold certification (500-699) demands excellence in 3+ dimensions, mandatory site visits, and expert panel reviews—representing true impact measurement best practices.
  • Platinum certification (700-899) requires thought leadership contributions and comprehensive independent audits, recognizing industry leaders who advance the entire field.
  • Diamond certification (900-1000+) is invitation-only for exceptional organizations demonstrating transformative, systemic impact that genuinely embodies 弘익人間.
  • Certification validity periods increase at higher levels (1 year for Bronze/Silver, 2 years for Gold/Platinum, 3 years for Diamond) reflecting greater investment and stability.
  • Cost structures scale with organization size and certification level, ranging from $500 (small Bronze) to $50,000+ (large Diamond), balancing accessibility with sustainability of rigorous assessment.
