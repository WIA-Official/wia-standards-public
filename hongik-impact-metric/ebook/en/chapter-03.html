Chapter 3: Measurement Methodology and Calculations

From philosophy to numbers: The technical framework for calculating the Hongik Impact Score.

3.1 The Master Formula

The Hongik Impact Score (HIS) synthesizes data across all seven dimensions into a single comprehensive metric. While simple metrics have their place, genuinely assessing whether something "benefits all humanity" requires accounting for multiple factors and their interactions.

The master formula balances several competing demands:

  • Comprehensiveness: Capturing impact across all relevant dimensions
  • Simplicity: Remaining understandable and communicable
  • Objectivity: Using verifiable data and transparent calculations
  • Nuance: Accounting for context, scale, and temporal factors
  • Comparability: Enabling meaningful comparison across organizations
HIS = [Σ(Di × Wi) × SRF × TF] × 1000 Components: Di = Dimension score i (0-1 normalized) Wi = Weight for dimension i (Σ Wi = 1.0) SRF = Stakeholder Reach Factor (1-10) TF = Temporal Factor (0.5-1.5) Final result multiplied by 1000 for 0-1000+ scale Dimension Weights: W1 = 0.20 (Social Good) W2 = 0.20 (Accessibility) W3 = 0.15 (Sustainability) W4 = 0.15 (Health & Wellbeing) W5 = 0.10 (Economic Equity) W6 = 0.10 (Education & Growth) W7 = 0.10 (Innovation & Progress) ──────── Sum = 1.00

This formula has several important properties. It's multiplicative rather than purely additive—the Stakeholder Reach Factor and Temporal Factor multiply the base score, meaning that greater scale and sustainability amplify impact rather than simply adding to it.

3.2 Calculating Dimension Scores

Each of the seven dimension scores (D1 through D7) is calculated independently using dimension-specific methodologies detailed in Chapter 2. All dimension scores are normalized to the 0-1 scale before being combined in the master formula.

The normalization process accounts for different measurement units and scales across dimensions:

Dimension Score Normalization: Di_normalized = (Di_raw - Di_min) / (Di_max - Di_min) Where: Di_raw = Raw calculated score for dimension i Di_min = Minimum possible score (typically 0) Di_max = Maximum possible score (context-dependent) For most dimensions: Di_min = 0 (no positive impact, or net negative) Di_max = 1 (exceptional impact, best-in-class performance) Example: Social Good raw score: 83/100 Normalized: (83 - 0) / (100 - 0) = 0.83

3.3 Stakeholder Reach Factor (SRF)

The Stakeholder Reach Factor accounts for the scale of impact. An initiative that benefits 100 people with the same per-capita quality as one benefiting 100 million people should be distinguished in the overall score. However, the relationship isn't linear—benefiting twice as many people doesn't simply double the impact score.

The SRF uses a logarithmic scale to balance recognizing greater reach while avoiding overwhelming small-scale but high-quality impact:

SRF = 1 + 9 × [log10(AB) / log10(8,000,000,000)] Where: AB = Affected Beneficiaries (direct + indirect) 8,000,000,000 = Approximate global population Range: 1 (minimal reach) to 10 (global reach) Calculation Examples: 100 beneficiaries: SRF = 1 + 9 × [log10(100) / log10(8,000,000,000)] = 1 + 9 × [2 / 9.903] = 1 + 9 × 0.202 = 1 + 1.818 = 2.82 10,000 beneficiaries: SRF = 1 + 9 × [log10(10,000) / log10(8,000,000,000)] = 1 + 9 × [4 / 9.903] = 1 + 9 × 0.404 = 1 + 3.636 = 4.64 1,000,000 beneficiaries: SRF = 1 + 9 × [log10(1,000,000) / log10(8,000,000,000)] = 1 + 9 × [6 / 9.903] = 1 + 9 × 0.606 = 1 + 5.454 = 6.45 100,000,000 beneficiaries: SRF = 1 + 9 × [log10(100,000,000) / log10(8,000,000,000)] = 1 + 9 × [8 / 9.903] = 1 + 9 × 0.808 = 1 + 7.272 = 8.27 1,000,000,000 beneficiaries: SRF = 1 + 9 × [log10(1,000,000,000) / log10(8,000,000,000)] = 1 + 9 × [9 / 9.903] = 1 + 9 × 0.909 = 1 + 8.181 = 9.18

Counting Direct and Indirect Beneficiaries

Determining the number of affected beneficiaries requires careful methodology:

Direct Beneficiaries: Individuals who directly use, receive, or participate in the service/product. Must be counted conservatively with verification.

Indirect Beneficiaries: People who benefit secondarily—family members of direct beneficiaries, communities affected by changes, future generations affected by sustainable practices. Counted at 0.3x weight compared to direct beneficiaries to account for reduced impact intensity.

Total Affected Beneficiaries Formula: AB = DB + (IB × 0.3) Where: AB = Total Affected Beneficiaries DB = Direct Beneficiaries IB = Indirect Beneficiaries Example - Healthcare Clinic: Direct patients treated: 5,000 Family members affected (3 per patient): 15,000 Community health improvement: 50,000 AB = 5,000 + (15,000 × 0.3) + (50,000 × 0.3 × 0.5) = 5,000 + 4,500 + 7,500 = 17,000 total affected beneficiaries Note: Second-order indirect effects (community) weighted at 0.5× to account for more attenuated impact

3.4 Temporal Factor (TF)

The Temporal Factor adjusts for impact sustainability and duration. 弘益人間 emphasizes long-term benefit to humanity, not just short-term gains. An initiative with lasting effects should score higher than one with identical quality but temporary impact.

TF = 0.5 + (Impact_Duration / 60) Capped at: Minimum: 0.5 (very temporary impact) Maximum: 1.5 (permanent/generational impact) Impact Duration Categories: Temporary (<1 year): TF = 0.5 to 0.6 Example: Emergency relief, one-time event Short-term (1-3 years): TF = 0.7 to 0.8 Example: Short training program, temporary service Medium-term (3-10 years): TF = 0.9 to 1.0 Example: Multi-year education, infrastructure with limited lifespan Long-term (10-30 years): TF = 1.1 to 1.3 Example: Educational degrees, sustainable systems, policy change Permanent (>30 years): TF = 1.4 to 1.5 Example: Fundamental research, ecosystem restoration, cultural preservation Calculation Examples: 6-month emergency program: TF = 0.5 + (0.5 / 60) = 0.5 + 0.008 = 0.508 ≈ 0.5 2-year skills training: TF = 0.5 + (2 / 60) = 0.5 + 0.033 = 0.533 ≈ 0.5-0.6 Adjusted to category: 0.75 7-year education program: TF = 0.5 + (7 / 60) = 0.5 + 0.117 = 0.617 ≈ 0.9-1.0 Adjusted to category: 0.95 20-year policy reform impact: TF = 0.5 + (20 / 60) = 0.5 + 0.333 = 0.833 Adjusted to category: 1.2 50-year environmental restoration: TF = 0.5 + (50 / 60) = 0.5 + 0.833 = 1.333 Capped at permanent category: 1.5

The Temporal Factor is assessed based on expected duration of impact, not just current age of the initiative. A newly launched program designed for permanent impact can claim higher TF if evidence supports sustainability claims.

3.5 Complete Calculation Example

Example: Community Health Cooperative

Step 1: Calculate Individual Dimension Scores

Social Good (D1): Community Engagement: 0.85 Social Equity: 0.90 Public Benefit: 0.75 Cultural Impact: 0.80 D1 = (0.85×0.25) + (0.90×0.30) + (0.75×0.25) + (0.80×0.20) = 0.2125 + 0.27 + 0.1875 + 0.16 = 0.83 Accessibility (D2): 0.78 Sustainability (D3): 0.64 Health & Wellbeing (D4): 0.96 Economic Equity (D5): 0.76 Education & Growth (D6): 0.91 Innovation & Progress (D7): 0.72

Step 2: Calculate Weighted Dimension Sum

Σ(Di × Wi) = (D1×W1) + (D2×W2) + (D3×W3) + (D4×W4) + (D5×W5) + (D6×W6) + (D7×W7) = (0.83×0.20) + (0.78×0.20) + (0.64×0.15) + (0.96×0.15) + (0.76×0.10) + (0.91×0.10) + (0.72×0.10) = 0.166 + 0.156 + 0.096 + 0.144 + 0.076 + 0.091 + 0.072 = 0.801

Step 3: Calculate Stakeholder Reach Factor

Direct Beneficiaries: 12,000 patients Indirect Beneficiaries: 36,000 family members + 80,000 community AB = 12,000 + (36,000 × 0.3) + (80,000 × 0.3 × 0.5) = 12,000 + 10,800 + 12,000 = 34,800 total affected beneficiaries SRF = 1 + 9 × [log10(34,800) / log10(8,000,000,000)] = 1 + 9 × [4.542 / 9.903] = 1 + 9 × 0.459 = 1 + 4.131 = 5.13

Step 4: Determine Temporal Factor

Organization age: 8 years Expected continuation: Indefinite (sustainable model) Impact duration category: Long-term (10-30 years) TF = 1.2

Step 5: Calculate Final HIS

HIS = [Σ(Di × Wi) × SRF × TF] × 1000 = [0.801 × 5.13 × 1.2] × 1000 = [0.801 × 6.156] × 1000 = 4.931 × 1000 = 493 Final Score: HIS = 493 Certification Level: Silver (300-499 range)

Interpretation:

This Community Health Cooperative achieves a solid Silver certification with an HIS of 493. The organization excels particularly in Health & Wellbeing (0.96) and Education (0.91), while having moderate performance in Sustainability (0.64). The scale (34,800 beneficiaries) provides a meaningful SRF of 5.13, and the long-term sustainable model earns a TF of 1.2.

To reach Gold certification (500+), the organization could focus on improving its weakest dimension (Sustainability) or expanding reach to increase the SRF.

3.6 Per-Capita Impact

While the HIS accounts for scale through the SRF, sometimes comparing the intensity of impact is valuable. Per-Capita Impact (PCI) normalizes the score by the number of beneficiaries:

PCI = HIS / AB Where: PCI = Per-Capita Impact HIS = Hongik Impact Score AB = Affected Beneficiaries Example from above: PCI = 493 / 34,800 = 0.0142 Interpretation: Higher PCI indicates more intensive impact per person Lower PCI indicates broader but less intensive impact Use Cases: - Comparing intensive vs. extensive strategies - Assessing efficiency of resource allocation - Identifying highly concentrated impact initiatives
Strategy Type Beneficiaries HIS PCI Characteristics
Deep Impact 500 350 0.70 Intensive, personalized, transformative
Balanced 35,000 493 0.014 Moderate scale and intensity
Broad Reach 1,000,000 650 0.00065 Extensive, scalable, systemic

Neither high nor low PCI is inherently better—both intensive and extensive approaches can embody 弘益人間. The key is intentional strategy aligned with the problem being addressed.

Key Takeaways

  • The HIS master formula is multiplicative, with Stakeholder Reach Factor and Temporal Factor amplifying the base dimensional score rather than simply adding to it.
  • All seven dimension scores are normalized to 0-1 scale before being combined using their assigned weights (Social Good and Accessibility at 20% each, others ranging from 10-15%).
  • The Stakeholder Reach Factor uses logarithmic scaling (1-10 range) to balance recognizing greater scale while not overwhelming small but high-quality initiatives.
  • Direct beneficiaries count at full weight, while indirect beneficiaries count at 0.3× weight, and second-order indirect effects at 0.15× weight to account for attenuated impact.
  • The Temporal Factor (0.5-1.5 range) rewards sustainability and long-term impact, with permanent initiatives earning up to 3× the adjustment of temporary ones.
  • Per-Capita Impact provides an alternative lens for comparison, normalizing by beneficiary count to assess impact intensity versus extensiveness.
  • Complete HIS calculation requires careful data collection, transparent assumptions, and conservative estimates to maintain credibility and prevent score inflation.
