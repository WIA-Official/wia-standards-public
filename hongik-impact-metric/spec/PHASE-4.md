## 2. Standard Evolution Process

### 2.1 Version Control

**Current Version:** 1.0 (Released: 2025-01-15)

**Version Numbering:**
- **Major versions (X.0):** Fundamental changes to framework or philosophy
- **Minor versions (1.X):** New methodologies, dimension adjustments, certification level changes
- **Patches (1.0.X):** Clarifications, corrections, minor improvements

### 2.2 Release Cycle

**Annual Review:** Every January, WIA Research Committee reviews:
- Feedback from assessments
- Research findings
- Field developments
- Stakeholder input

**Proposal Development:** Based on review, develop proposals for changes

**Public Comment Period:** 60-day public comment on proposed changes

**Revision and Approval:** Incorporate feedback, finalize changes

**Release:** New version released July 1 each year (if changes warrant)

**Transition Period:** 6-month transition where old or new version acceptable

**Full Implementation:** January 1 following release, new version required

### 2.3 Backward Compatibility

Major principles:
- Organizations certified under previous version remain certified during transition
- Methodology changes documented clearly
- Conversion guidelines provided for moving between versions
- Historical scores comparable with adjustments documented

### 2.4 Community Input

Anyone can propose changes via:
- GitHub issues (technical changes)
- WIA website feedback form
- Annual stakeholder forums
- Research publications
- Direct communication with WIA Research Committee

All proposals publicly tracked and responded to.

## 3. Research Agenda

### 3.1 Priority Research Questions (2025-2027)

#### 3.1.1 Measurement Methodology

**Question:** How can we better measure long-term and intergenerational impact?

**Rationale:** Current methods favor short-term measurable outcomes. 弘益人間 extends across generations.

**Research Priorities:**
- Develop validated proxies for long-term impact
- Study organizations with 10+ year track records
- Model intergenerational benefit
- Account for time preference and discounting

**Expected Output:** Improved temporal weighting methodology (target: Version 1.2)

#### 3.1.2 Causation and Attribution

**Question:** How can we accurately assess contribution in complex, multi-actor systems?

**Rationale:** Most important change happens in complex ecosystems. Better attribution methods needed.

**Research Priorities:**
- Comparison of contribution analysis approaches
- Development of standardized counterfactual estimation
- System dynamics modeling for systemic change
- Network analysis of multi-stakeholder impact

**Expected Output:** Enhanced contribution analysis framework (target: Version 1.3)

#### 3.1.3 Equity and Justice

**Question:** How can we better assess whether benefits are equitably distributed?

**Rationale:** 弘益人間 means benefiting ALL humanity, not just some. Need better equity metrics.

**Research Priorities:**
- Develop intersectional impact analysis
- Study differential outcomes across demographics
- Measure empowerment and agency, not just services
- Assess power dynamics and participatory governance

**Expected Output:** Expanded Economic Equity dimension; new equity analysis requirements (target: Version 2.0)

#### 3.1.4 Planetary Boundaries

**Question:** How can we integrate planetary boundary science into Sustainability assessment?

**Rationale:** Current Sustainability metrics important but incomplete. Need systemic view.

**Research Priorities:**
- Integration with planetary boundaries framework
- Absolute sustainability vs. relative improvement
- Regenerative vs. sustainable approaches
- True cost accounting

**Expected Output:** Enhanced Sustainability dimension (target: Version 2.0)

### 3.2 Medium-Term Research (2027-2032)

- AI ethics and algorithmic impact measurement
- Measuring collective consciousness and cultural shift
- Integration of indigenous knowledge systems
- Universal basic wellbeing metrics
- Post-scarcity economics and impact measurement
- Measuring prevention (impact of problems avoided)
- Spiritual and meaning-making dimensions of wellbeing

### 3.3 Long-Term Research (2032+)

- Measuring wisdom (not just knowledge or skills)
- Collective human flourishing beyond individual metrics
- Interplanetary considerations (if/when humanity expands beyond Earth)
- Post-human and transhumanist considerations
- Integration with potential future forms of consciousness

## 4. Open Questions and Frontier Topics

### 4.1 Unresolved Challenges

#### 4.1.1 The Measurement Paradox

**Challenge:** The more you measure, the more you distort what you're measuring.

Example: Organizations optimizing for HIS score rather than genuine impact.

**Current Approach:** Multiple metrics, qualitative assessment, unannounced observations, peer review

**Open Question:** Is there a point where measurement becomes counterproductive? How do we know when we've crossed it?

#### 4.1.2 Incommensurability

**Challenge:** How do you compare fundamentally different types of benefit?

Example: Is education more important than healthcare? Environmental protection more than economic opportunity?

**Current Approach:** Weight all dimensions, but weights somewhat arbitrary

**Open Question:** Do universal weights make sense? Should they vary by context? By whom should they be set?

#### 4.1.3 The Goodhart Problem

"When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure."

**Challenge:** As HIS becomes important (for funding, recognition, etc.), gaming increases.

**Current Approach:** Multiple data sources, verification, unannounced elements, assessor judgment

**Open Question:** Can we design a measurement system resistant to Goodhart's Law? Or must we constantly adapt?

#### 4.1.4 Unknowable Futures

**Challenge:** Most important impacts may not be measurable for decades or generations.

Example: Education's full impact unfolds across a lifetime; ecosystem restoration across centuries.

**Current Approach:** Temporal weighting, leading indicators, theory of change validation

**Open Question:** How do we value the distant future appropriately? How do we avoid short-termism?

### 4.2 Philosophical Dilemmas

#### 4.2.1 Paternalism vs. Autonomy

**Dilemma:** Who defines "benefit"? Outside assessors or those being served?

**Tension:** Respecting autonomy (people define their own wellbeing) vs. addressing false consciousness or adaptive preferences (people accepting harm as normal).

**Current Approach:** Stakeholder voices central, but framework provides structure

**Open Question:** Where is the boundary between respectful assessment and paternalistic imposition?

#### 4.2.2 Aggregation vs. Distribution

**Dilemma:** 1000 people experiencing small benefit vs. 10 people experiencing transformative benefit. Which is greater?

**Tension:** Utilitarian aggregation vs. prioritarian focus on worst-off.

**Current Approach:** Both quantity and depth matter; depth multipliers provide some prioritarian weighting

**Open Question:** What is the right balance?

#### 4.2.3 Present vs. Future

**Dilemma:** How much should we discount future benefits?

**Tension:** Urgency of current need vs. responsibility to future generations.

**Current Approach:** Temporal multipliers value sustained impact, but still weighted toward present

**Open Question:** What discount rate (if any) is consistent with 弘益人間?

### 4.3 Emerging Considerations

- **Artificial General Intelligence (AGI):** If/when AGI emerges, how do we measure impact on non-human intelligence?
- **Radical Life Extension:** If lifespan extends dramatically, how does this change impact calculation?
- **Climate Tipping Points:** How to account for non-linear catastrophic risks?
- **Post-Work Society:** If automation eliminates most jobs, how do we measure economic impact?
- **Simulated Realities:** If we inhabit simulated/virtual worlds, what constitutes "real" impact?

## 7. Integration with Other Standards

### 7.1 WIA Family of Standards

WIA-CORE-005 is one of a family:

**Relationship to other WIA standards:**
- **WIA-INTENT:** How intentions are expressed; CORE-005 measures if they're achieved
- **WIA-OMNI-API:** Technical infrastructure; CORE-005 measures impact of implementations
- **WIA-SOCIAL:** Social connection standard; CORE-005 includes Social Good dimension
- **WIA-AIR-POWER & AIR-SHIELD:** Security/capability standards; CORE-005 assesses their impact

All WIA standards embody 弘益人間.

### 7.2 Integration with External Standards

**UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs):**
- All seven HIS dimensions map to SDGs
- HIS provides measurement framework for SDG progress
- SDG targets inform HIS benchmarks

**ESG Frameworks:**
- Environmental (E) → Sustainability dimension
- Social (S) → Social Good, Health, Economic Equity, Education dimensions
- Governance (G) → Assessed within Economic Equity and context multipliers

**B Corp Assessment:**
- Similar philosophy, different approach
- B Corp can use HIS for impact measurement
- WIA accepts B Corp certification as supporting evidence

**GRI Standards:**
- GRI provides reporting framework
- HIS provides impact assessment
- Compatible and complementary
