## 1. Overview

### 1.1 Purpose

PHASE 2 extends the foundational framework established in PHASE 1 to cover advanced assessment methodologies, rigorous verification standards, and higher certification levels (Gold, Platinum, Diamond). This phase is designed for organizations with mature impact practices seeking credible third-party validation of excellence.

### 1.2 Scope

This phase covers:
- Gold, Platinum, and Diamond certification requirements
- Advanced measurement methodologies
- Longitudinal impact assessment
- Systemic change measurement
- Third-party verification protocols
- Site visit assessment procedures
- Expert panel review processes

### 1.3 Prerequisites

Organizations must have:
- Achieved Bronze or Silver certification, OR
- 3+ years of documented impact work with comprehensive data
- Robust internal systems for ongoing data collection
- Stakeholder engagement infrastructure
- Commitment to continuous improvement

## 2. Advanced Certification Levels

### 2.1 Gold Certification (HIS 500-699)

**Philosophy:** Strong, verified impact with excellence in multiple dimensions

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 3 years operational history with longitudinal data
- Third-party verification for ALL quantitative metrics
- Extensive stakeholder engagement (200+ responses or 30% of users)
- Excellence (80+) in at least 3 dimensions
- No dimension below 60
- Published impact report available publicly
- HIS score 500-699

**Assessment Process:**
- In-depth document review (4-6 weeks)
- Mandatory site visit (1-2 days)
- Stakeholder interviews (20+ conducted by WIA)
- Expert panel review for technical dimensions
- Triple assessor review with consensus
- Comparative benchmarking

**Timeline:** 16-20 weeks from application to certification decision

**Cost:** $2,000-$15,000 (based on organization size and complexity)

**Validity:** 2 years; biennial reassessment required

**Maintaining Certification:**
- Annual progress reports
- Maintained or improved HIS
- Continued stakeholder engagement
- Public impact reporting

### 2.2 Platinum Certification (HIS 700-899)

**Philosophy:** Outstanding comprehensive impact; industry leadership

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 5 years operational history with multi-year trend data
- Independent third-party audit of all impact claims
- Comprehensive stakeholder feedback (500+ responses or 40% of users)
- Excellence (85+) in at least 5 dimensions
- No dimension below 70
- Published annual impact reports with independent verification
- Demonstrated thought leadership in impact measurement
- Contribution to field advancement (publications, presentations, etc.)
- HIS score 700-899

**Assessment Process:**
- Rigorous document review (6-8 weeks)
- Multi-day site visit (3-5 days) including operations observation
- Extensive stakeholder interviews (50+)
- Expert panel reviews across all relevant domains
- Comparison with best-in-class benchmarks
- Senior assessor team review
- Board-level review for borderline cases

**Special Considerations:**
- Supply chain assessment (if applicable)
- Unintended consequences analysis
- Long-term sustainability evaluation
- Replication and scaling potential

**Timeline:** 20-24 weeks from application to certification decision

**Cost:** $5,000-$30,000 (based on organization size and complexity)

**Validity:** 2 years

**Maintaining Certification:**
- Annual in-depth monitoring with data submission
- Biennial full reassessment
- Sustained excellence across dimensions
- Continued thought leadership

### 2.3 Diamond Certification (HIS 900+)

**Philosophy:** Exceptional, transformative impact; embodiment of 弘益人間

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 7 years operational history with proven sustained excellence
- Continuous independent monitoring and verification
- Comprehensive multi-stakeholder feedback
- Excellence (90+) in at least 5 dimensions
- No dimension below 75
- Demonstrated systemic change and field advancement
- Global recognition and thought leadership
- Published research contributions
- HIS score 900+

**Special Requirements:**
- Evidence of paradigm-shifting impact
- Replication by others demonstrating field-wide influence
- Policy change or market transformation attributable to work
- Recognition by academic/professional communities
- Innovative measurement methodologies shared openly

**Assessment Process:**
- Comprehensive review (8-12 weeks)
- Extended site visit (1 week+)
- Global stakeholder engagement
- Multi-disciplinary expert panels
- Academic research review of impact claims
- Comparative analysis with historical high-performers
- WIA Board-level review and approval
- External validation from field experts

**Timeline:** 24-48 weeks from invitation to certification decision

**Cost:** $10,000-$50,000+ (based on organization size and complexity)

**Validity:** 3 years

**Important Note:** Diamond certification cannot be directly applied for. Organizations must first achieve Platinum certification and demonstrate sustained excellence before being invited to pursue Diamond level.

**Maintaining Certification:**
- Continuous monitoring with real-time data sharing
- Triennial full reassessment
- Ongoing thought leadership
- Field advancement contributions

## 3. Advanced Measurement Methodologies

### 3.1 Longitudinal Impact Assessment

#### 3.1.1 Purpose
Assess sustained impact over time, distinguishing temporary effects from lasting change.

#### 3.1.2 Requirements by Level
- **Gold:** 3-year trend data for key metrics
- **Platinum:** 5-year trend data with demonstrated sustained or improving impact
- **Diamond:** 7+ year data showing transformative, lasting change

#### 3.1.3 Key Elements
- Baseline measurements
- Regular tracking (quarterly or more frequent)
- Long-term outcome assessment
- Attrition analysis (understanding why people disengage)
- Sustainability indicators (will impact continue without intervention?)

#### 3.1.4 Temporal Weighting

| Impact Duration | Multiplier | Example |
|----------------|-----------|---------|
| < 1 year | 1.0x | Emergency relief |
| 1-3 years | 1.2x | Skills training |
| 3-7 years | 1.5x | Education programs |
| 7-15 years | 1.8x | Systemic change initiatives |
| 15+ years | 2.0x | Ecosystem restoration, policy reform |

### 3.2 Systemic Change Measurement

#### 3.2.1 Levels of Change

| Level | Description | Impact Multiplier |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Direct Service | Providing services to individuals | 1.0x |
| Capacity Building | Enabling others to provide services | 1.3x |
| Institutional Change | Changing how institutions operate | 1.7x |
| Systemic Change | Changing rules, norms, or structures | 2.5x |

#### 3.2.2 Evidence Requirements for Systemic Change Claims

To claim systemic change impact:
1. **Documentation** of system before and after
2. **Evidence** of contribution to the change
3. **Proof** that change is institutionalized (not dependent on continued intervention)
4. **Evidence** of sustained operation of new system
5. **Independent validation** from field experts

#### 3.2.3 Example: Food Waste Reduction

- **Direct Service:** Operating a food bank (feeds 500 families)
- **Capacity Building:** Training 10 food banks to operate efficiently
- **Institutional Change:** Partnering with grocery chains to change waste practices (eliminating 1000 tons/year waste)
- **Systemic Change:** Passing legislation requiring waste reduction (affecting entire state/country)

### 3.3 Attribution vs. Contribution Analysis

#### 3.3.1 The Challenge
In complex systems with multiple actors, determining specific causation is difficult and often counterproductive.

#### 3.3.2 WIA Approach: Contribution Analysis

Rather than claiming sole attribution, organizations demonstrate contribution through:

1. **Theory of Change:** Document how work is supposed to lead to impact
2. **Evidence for Each Link:** Gather proof for each causal connection
3. **Identify Other Factors:** Transparently acknowledge all contributing factors
4. **Assess Strength of Evidence:** Evaluate confidence in contribution claim
5. **Transparent Communication:** Present contribution alongside other factors

#### 3.3.3 Scoring
- Clear, evidence-based contribution: Full credit
- Contribution with mixed evidence: Partial credit (proportional to evidence strength)
- Unclear or weak contribution: Minimal credit
- Exaggerated attribution: Penalty applied

### 3.4 Unintended Consequences Assessment

Required for Gold+. Organizations must systematically identify and assess unintended consequences (positive and negative).

#### 3.4.1 Process
1. **Pre-mortem:** Before launch, imagine failure/harm scenarios
2. **Stakeholder Blind Spots:** Seek perspectives from non-consulted groups
3. **System Mapping:** Identify all affected stakeholders and ripple effects
4. **Ongoing Monitoring:** Create feedback loops for detecting unexpected effects
5. **Honest Reporting:** Include in impact reports

#### 3.4.2 Impact on Scoring
- Organizations acknowledging and addressing negative unintended consequences score higher than those ignoring them
- Significant unaddressed negative consequences can result in certification denial
- Positive unintended consequences add to overall score

## 4. Verification and Quality Assurance

### 4.1 Third-Party Verification

#### 4.1.1 Requirements
- **Gold:** Third-party verification of all quantitative metrics
- **Platinum:** Independent audit of all impact claims
- **Diamond:** Continuous independent monitoring

#### 4.1.2 Acceptable Verifiers
- Independent auditing firms
- Academic researchers
- Government agencies (for certain metrics)
- Other certified third-party assessors
- NOT: Internal staff, board members, or direct beneficiaries

#### 4.1.3 Verification Standards
- Financial data: Audited financials by certified accountants
- Carbon footprint: GHG Protocol-compliant calculation
- Accessibility: WCAG testing by certified professionals
- Health outcomes: Peer-reviewed methodology
- Education outcomes: Independent assessment of learning

### 4.2 Site Visit Protocols

#### 4.2.1 Purpose
Direct observation of operations, culture, and impact delivery to validate documentation and deepen understanding.

#### 4.2.2 Site Visit Components

**Pre-Visit:**
- Document review
- Stakeholder interview scheduling
- Observation planning
- Site visit agenda shared with organization

**During Visit:**
- Operations observation
- Facility tour
- Staff interviews (random selection)
- Beneficiary interactions (with consent)
- Document verification
- Surprise elements (unscheduled observations)

**Post-Visit:**
- Debrief with organization
- Assessor team synthesis
- Follow-up question identification
- Integration into overall assessment

#### 4.2.3 Duration
- Gold: 1-2 days
- Platinum: 3-5 days
- Diamond: 1 week+ (may include multiple sites)

### 4.3 Expert Panel Reviews

For technical dimensions requiring specialized knowledge, WIA convenes expert panels.

#### 4.3.1 When Used
- Gold+: For dimensions where organization claims high performance in technical areas
- Platinum+: For all technical claims
- Diamond: Comprehensive expert review across all dimensions

#### 4.3.2 Panel Composition
- 3-5 experts per dimension
- Diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- No conflicts of interest
- Academic, practitioner, and lived experience representation

#### 4.3.3 Review Process
1. Review documentation
2. Interview organization
3. Independent assessment
4. Panel consensus discussion
5. Recommendation to assessors

## 5. Stakeholder Engagement Requirements

### 5.1 Quantitative Thresholds

| Certification Level | Minimum Responses | OR | Percentage of Users |
|---------------------|-------------------|----|--------------------|
| Gold | 200 | OR | 30% |
| Platinum | 500 | OR | 40% |
| Diamond | 1000+ | OR | 50% |

### 5.2 Qualitative Requirements

Beyond numbers, Gold+ requires:
- Multiple methods (surveys + interviews + focus groups)
- Diverse stakeholder types (beneficiaries + workers + community + partners)
- Accessible formats (multiple languages, audio options, etc.)
- Representative sampling (not just satisfied customers)
- Anonymous options to encourage honesty
- Action on feedback (demonstrate responsiveness)

### 5.3 WIA-Conducted Interviews

| Level | Minimum WIA Interviews |
|-------|------------------------|
| Gold | 20 |
| Platinum | 50 |
| Diamond | 100+ |

These are in addition to organization-collected feedback.

## 6. Benchmarking and Comparative Analysis

### 6.1 Purpose
Assess performance relative to best practices and industry standards.

### 6.2 Benchmark Categories

1. **Sector Benchmarks:** Comparison with similar organizations in same sector
2. **Geographic Benchmarks:** Comparison accounting for regional context
3. **Best-in-Class:** Comparison with highest performers regardless of sector
4. **Historical:** Organization's performance over time

### 6.3 Use in Assessment

Benchmarking informs but doesn't determine scores. An organization can excel relative to poor-performing sector while still having modest absolute impact.

However, for Platinum and Diamond:
- Must demonstrate performance at or near best-in-class in multiple dimensions
- Must show innovation or leadership advancing the field

## 7. Continuous Improvement Requirements

### 7.1 Annual Monitoring (Gold+)

**Platinum organizations submit annually:**
- Updated metrics across all dimensions
- Stakeholder feedback summary
- Progress on improvement goals
- Major changes or challenges
- Narrative update on impact work

**Diamond organizations submit:**
- All Platinum requirements, plus:
- Real-time data dashboard access
- Quarterly impact reports
- Ongoing stakeholder satisfaction tracking

### 7.2 Reassessment

**Gold:**
- Full reassessment every 2 years
- Must demonstrate maintained or improved HIS
- New impact report required

**Platinum:**
- Annual monitoring review
- Full reassessment every 2 years
- Must demonstrate sustained excellence and field leadership

**Diamond:**
- Continuous monitoring
- Full reassessment every 3 years
- Must demonstrate transformative, sustained impact

### 7.3 Improvement Expectations

Organizations are expected to improve over time. Static performance may result in:
- Certification maintained but with improvement plan
- Downgrade to lower level (if HIS drops below threshold)
- Certification revocation (if significant problems emerge)
