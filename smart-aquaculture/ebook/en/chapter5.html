🌊 Environmental Monitoring

Weather, tides, currents, and climate impacts on aquaculture operations.

5.1 Weather Monitoring

Critical parameters for aquaculture:

  • Wind Speed: >15 m/s dangerous for marine cages, can damage nets
  • Rainfall: Heavy rain reduces salinity in surface waters, stresses marine fish
  • Air Temperature: Affects water temperature, particularly in shallow systems
  • Barometric Pressure: Rapid drops indicate storms, trigger fish feeding behavior

5.2 Tidal & Current Monitoring

For marine cage systems:

  • Tidal Range: Affects water exchange, DO levels. Spring tides (new/full moon) have largest range
  • Current Speed: Optimal 0.2-0.5 m/s. Too slow = poor water exchange. Too fast = fish exhaustion
  • Tidal Predictions: Plan feeding during slack tide (slow current) for better feed retention

5.3 Harmful Algal Blooms (HABs)

Toxic algae can kill entire fish farms. Monitor via:

  • Chlorophyll sensors (>10 μg/L = high risk)
  • Satellite imagery (NASA/ESA chlorophyll maps)
  • Regional HAB forecasts from fisheries authorities

Action: If HAB detected, stop feeding, increase aeration, consider early harvest or temporary relocation.

5.4 Climate Change Impacts

  • Ocean Acidification: CO₂ absorption lowers pH. Impacts shell formation in mollusks (oysters, abalone)
  • Temperature Rise: 1-2°C increase shifts species ranges. Warmwater species move poleward
  • Extreme Weather: Typhoons/hurricanes damage cages. Need storm preparedness plans
