Chapter 02: Water Quality Drivers and Fish Health

2.1 Water quality drivers

Aquaculture water quality is dominated by five parameters: temperature (drives metabolism + dissolved oxygen capacity), salinity (species-dependent), dissolved oxygen (must stay above species-specific minimum), pH (typically 6.5-8.5 for most farmed species), and ammonia (toxic above species-specific thresholds). The standard\x27s water_quality_reading envelope preserves all five plus optional turbidity, alkalinity, and nitrite for advanced setups.

2.2 Sensor placement and accuracy

Sensor placement matters: surface vs depth-stratified profiles, near-cage vs far-field, hourly vs continuous. The standard\x27s envelope captures the sensor location and depth so cross-farm comparisons are valid. Sensor calibration drifts over weeks-to-months; the calibration_chain_id field links to the most recent calibration record so downstream analytics can detect overdue sensors.

2.3 Fish health indicators

Mortality count is the dominant fish-health indicator; growth rate is the dominant production indicator. Modern farms add biomass cameras (deep learning weight estimation) and behaviour cameras (lice detection, disease indicators). The standard\x27s fish_health_observation envelope captures cohort-level aggregates plus optional per-fish detail when underwater computer vision systems are deployed.

2.4 Disease management

Sea lice (salmon farming\x27s dominant pest), bacterial gill disease, amoebic gill disease, and various viral diseases require monitoring and intervention. Vaccination and biological control (cleaner fish, freshwater treatment) replace chemical treatments where possible. The standard\x27s vaccination_record envelope tracks treatment history per cohort.

2.5 Feed conversion ratio (FCR)

FCR is the single most important production efficiency metric: kg of feed per kg of fish gained. Modern salmon FCR is around 1.1-1.3; tilapia 1.5-1.8; shrimp 1.2-1.5. The standard\x27s feeding_event envelope captures feed_kg and the cohort\x27s growth so FCR can be computed cross-farm without bespoke data extraction.

2.6 Environmental impact

Aquaculture impacts are localised (benthic enrichment under cages, escapes, disease transfer to wild populations) and basin-scale (effluent nutrients in semi-enclosed seas). The standard\x27s farm envelope optionally carries environmental_impact_assessment_id linking to a periodic compliance record.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in aquaculture infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, water quality sensor drift: sensors drift over weeks-to-months and require regular calibration. The standard\x27s calibration_chain_id field links to the most recent calibration so downstream analytics can detect overdue sensors. Second, mortality reporting timeliness: regulators require mortality reporting within 24 hours of observation; the standard\x27s fish_health_observation envelope supports this via the observed_at timestamp + immediate POST. Third, traceability chain handoffs: retailer audit requirements mean every handoff (farm → processor → distributor → retail) must be signed; the traceability_chain envelope structure forces this.

Operator-First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through: does this give the farm operator more leverage with feed suppliers and certification bodies, or does it just make farm data more accessible to outside actors? When the two conflict, operator leverage wins. That is why the standard mandates explicit operator consent for cross-organisation data sharing, refuses to allow vendor terms-of-service to bypass the consent envelope, and requires all certification audit data flows to ride through operator-controlled audience scopes.

Korea-Specific Application Note

Korea's aquaculture industry — flatfish (광어), rockfish (우럭), abalone (전복), oyster (굴), seaweed (김·다시마·미역) — concentrates along the southern coast (Geoje, Tongyeong, Namhae, Wando). The Korea Fisheries Resources Agency (FIRA) and National Institute of Fisheries Science (NIFS) coordinate aquaculture data; the Korean seafood traceability scheme (KFTS) provides retail-side chain-of-custody. The standard's Phase 4 traceability_chain envelope maps cleanly onto KFTS via the destination + handoff_event fields, and Korea's eco-friendly aquaculture certification (HACCP / 친환경 인증) maps via the certifications array on the farm envelope.
