💧 Water Quality Management

Master the critical parameters that determine fish health. Learn sensor deployment, automated alerts, and predictive analytics for optimal water conditions.

2.1 Critical Water Quality Parameters

Dissolved Oxygen (DO)

The most critical parameter. Fish require dissolved oxygen for respiration. Levels below 5 mg/L cause stress; below 3 mg/L is lethal.

DO Level Impact Action
> 7.0 mg/L Optimal growth Normal operations
5-7 mg/L Acceptable, slower growth Monitor closely
3-5 mg/L Stress, immune suppression Increase aeration immediately
< 3 mg/L Lethal within hours Emergency aeration, reduce feeding

Temperature

Controls fish metabolism, growth rate, and disease susceptibility. Each species has an optimal range.

Species Optimal Range Max Tolerance
Flatfish (넙치) 16-22°C 28°C
Sea Bass (농어) 18-25°C 30°C
Abalone (전복) 15-20°C 26°C
Shrimp (새우) 26-30°C 35°C

pH

Affects ammonia toxicity and fish osmoregulation. Most fish prefer pH 6.5-8.5. Sudden changes are more dangerous than absolute values.

Salinity

For marine species, maintain 30-35 ppt (parts per thousand). Freshwater species require <0.5 ppt. Brackish species tolerate 5-25 ppt.

Ammonia (NH₃/NH₄⁺)

Fish excrete ammonia, which is toxic. Total ammonia >0.5 mg/L causes stress. Un-ionized ammonia (NH₃) is 100x more toxic than ionized (NH₄⁺). Ratio depends on pH and temperature.

2.2 Sensor Deployment

Multiparameter Sondes

Commercial sondes measure temperature, DO, pH, salinity, and turbidity simultaneously. Deploy one sonde per 500-1000 m³ of water volume. Position at mid-depth in tanks, near bottom in cages.

Best Practice: Deploy sensors in zones of poorest water quality (bottom of cages, corners of tanks) to ensure early warning of problems.

Optical DO Sensors vs. Electrochemical

  • Optical: No consumables, 1-2 year calibration interval, stable in biofouling conditions. Higher initial cost ($800-2000).
  • Electrochemical: Lower cost ($200-500), but requires membrane replacement every 1-3 months. Drift in high biofouling.

Biofouling Prevention

Algae and bacteria grow on sensors, causing false readings. Solutions:

  • Copper alloy sensor guards (prevent algae growth)
  • Automated wipers (mechanical cleaning every 1-4 hours)
  • UV anti-fouling systems
  • Manual cleaning every 7-14 days

2.3 Automated Alert Systems

Three-Tier Alert System

Level Trigger Example Action
INFO DO drops to 6.5 mg/L SMS notification to manager
WARNING DO drops to 5.5 mg/L Phone call + auto-increase aeration 50%
CRITICAL DO drops to 4.0 mg/L Emergency call + max aeration + stop feeding

Predictive Alerts

Machine learning models predict DO crashes 2-4 hours before they occur by analyzing:

  • Temperature trends (DO solubility decreases as temp rises)
  • Time of day (photosynthesis stops at night)
  • Cloud cover (reduces algae oxygen production)
  • Biomass (higher fish density = faster oxygen depletion)
  • Feeding schedule (metabolic spike 2-3 hours post-feeding)

2.4 Water Exchange & Aeration

Recirculating Aquaculture Systems (RAS)

Land-based systems that filter and reuse water. Components:

  • Mechanical Filtration: Removes solids (feces, uneaten feed)
  • Biofilter: Nitrifying bacteria convert ammonia → nitrite → nitrate
  • Aeration: Oxygenation via pure oxygen injection or air diffusers
  • UV Sterilization: Kills pathogens
  • Denitrification: Optional anaerobic filter removes nitrate

RAS reuses 95-99% of water, enabling aquaculture in water-scarce regions. Energy-intensive (aeration, pumping), but allows precise environmental control.

Flow-Through Systems

Continuous fresh water supply from rivers, springs, or ocean currents. Lower operating costs but requires abundant clean water. Used in marine cages and land-based trout/salmon farms.

2.5 Case Study: Korean Flatfish Farm

Location: Tongyeong, South Korea
Species: Flatfish (넙치)
Scale: 50 tanks, 15,000 kg biomass
System: Land-based RAS with real-time monitoring

Challenge: Summer DO crashes during heat waves killed 10-15% of fish annually.

Solution: Deployed IoT DO sensors in every tank, connected to automated aeration and emergency oxygen injection. Predictive AI alerts farm manager 3 hours before anticipated crashes.

Results:

  • Mortality reduced from 12% to 2%
  • 30% reduction in energy costs (aeration only when needed)
  • FCR improved from 1.3 to 1.15 (less stress = better feed efficiency)
  • ROI: 14 months payback period
