🎣 Harvest & Traceability

Optimal harvest timing, blockchain traceability, and seafood certification.

6.1 Harvest Timing Optimization

Balance growth rate vs. market price. ML models predict:

  • Growth Rate: Fish gain 2-5g/day depending on species, temp, feed
  • Market Price Forecast: Seasonal patterns (higher in winter for some species)
  • Optimal Harvest Window: Maximize revenue = (weight × price) - (days × daily costs)

Example: If flatfish price rises 15% in 30 days but fish only grow 8%, delay harvest.

6.2 Harvest Methods

  • Seine Netting: Gradual gathering, low stress. Best for tanks/ponds
  • Pumping: Pneumatic or vacuum pumps. Fast, suitable for high-volume operations
  • Crowding: Reduce cage volume, net fish out. Common in marine cages

Quality Preservation: Immediate ice immersion (1:1 fish to ice). Reduces bacterial growth, maintains freshness.

6.3 Blockchain Traceability

Korean Seafood Traceability System (수산물이력제) example:

  • Hatchery: Record fingerling batch ID, genetics, vaccinations
  • Farm: Water quality logs, feed records, antibiotic usage (if any)
  • Harvest: Date, weight, quality grade
  • Processing: Filleting, packaging
  • Distribution: Transport logs, cold chain monitoring
  • Retail: Consumer scans QR code, sees full history

Blockchain ensures immutability - data cannot be altered retroactively. Builds consumer trust, premium pricing for traceable products (+10-25%).

6.4 W3C Verifiable Credentials

Digital certificates proving:

  • Farm location and license number
  • Species and harvest date
  • Antibiotic-free, organic certification
  • Water quality compliance
  • ASC/BAP certification status

Verifiable by any party without central authority. Enables global seafood fraud prevention.
