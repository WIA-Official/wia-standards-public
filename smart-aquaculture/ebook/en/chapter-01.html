Chapter 01: Introduction & The Aquaculture Production Question

1.1 Why this book exists

WIA Smart Aquaculture is the open standard for aquaculture operations data exchange — water quality, fish health, feeding, biomass tracking, and harvest records. This book is the practitioner introduction.

1.2 The aquaculture production question

Aquaculture now produces more than half of the seafood consumed globally; capture fisheries are flat or declining. Salmon farming alone is a multi-billion-dollar industry; tilapia, sea bass, oysters, and shrimp follow. Production efficiency, fish welfare, and environmental impact are increasingly tied to digital monitoring — water quality sensors, automated feeders, biomass cameras, mortality counters. But the data produced by these systems is locked into vendor platforms; cross-vendor analytics, certification body audits, and consumer traceability are difficult.

1.3 What WIA Smart Aquaculture changes

The standard collapses vendor sensor networks, automated feeder telemetry, biomass cameras, and farm management software into one open wire format. A salmon farm running AKVA cages, Steinsvik feeders, Cargill SmartFarm software, and Optoscale biomass cameras can subscribe to all four data streams through one pipe.

1.4 The 4-Phase architecture

Phase 1 (Data Format) defines JSON envelopes for farms, water quality readings, fish health observations, feeding events, harvest records, and traceability chain records. Phase 2 (API Interface) specifies the HTTP surface. Phase 3 (Federation Protocol) handles cross-organisation data flow. Phase 4 (Integration) bridges to FAO ALDFI vocabularies, ASC certification, BAP audits, Global GAP traceability, and the WIA family.

1.5 Operator sovereignty as a baked-in primitive

The standard treats farmer/operator data sovereignty as a Phase 3 primitive: every cross-organisation data flow involving farm operations MUST be gated on an operator-signed consent envelope. Vendors cannot share farmer data with feed suppliers, certification bodies, or insurance providers without the operator\x27s explicit signature.

1.6 How this book is organised

Chapter 2 covers water quality and fish health. Chapter 3 introduces the standard at a glance. Chapters 4-7 walk the four phases. Chapter 8 closes with conformance, traceability, and roadmap.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in aquaculture infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, water quality sensor drift: sensors drift over weeks-to-months and require regular calibration. The standard\x27s calibration_chain_id field links to the most recent calibration so downstream analytics can detect overdue sensors. Second, mortality reporting timeliness: regulators require mortality reporting within 24 hours of observation; the standard\x27s fish_health_observation envelope supports this via the observed_at timestamp + immediate POST. Third, traceability chain handoffs: retailer audit requirements mean every handoff (farm → processor → distributor → retail) must be signed; the traceability_chain envelope structure forces this.

Operator-First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through: does this give the farm operator more leverage with feed suppliers and certification bodies, or does it just make farm data more accessible to outside actors? When the two conflict, operator leverage wins. That is why the standard mandates explicit operator consent for cross-organisation data sharing, refuses to allow vendor terms-of-service to bypass the consent envelope, and requires all certification audit data flows to ride through operator-controlled audience scopes.

Korea-Specific Application Note

Korea's aquaculture industry — flatfish (광어), rockfish (우럭), abalone (전복), oyster (굴), seaweed (김·다시마·미역) — concentrates along the southern coast (Geoje, Tongyeong, Namhae, Wando). The Korea Fisheries Resources Agency (FIRA) and National Institute of Fisheries Science (NIFS) coordinate aquaculture data; the Korean seafood traceability scheme (KFTS) provides retail-side chain-of-custody. The standard's Phase 4 traceability_chain envelope maps cleanly onto KFTS via the destination + handoff_event fields, and Korea's eco-friendly aquaculture certification (HACCP / 친환경 인증) maps via the certifications array on the farm envelope.
