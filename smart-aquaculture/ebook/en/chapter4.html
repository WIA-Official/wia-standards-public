CHAPTER 4

🍽️ Feeding Systems & FCR Optimization

Master Feed Conversion Ratio optimization, automated feeding, and appetite-based ration control.

4.1 Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR)

FCR = Total Feed Fed (kg) / Biomass Gained (kg)

Lower FCR = better efficiency. Target FCR by species:

  • Flatfish: 1.1-1.3
  • Sea Bass: 1.2-1.4
  • Shrimp: 1.4-1.6
  • Tilapia: 1.3-1.5

Feed costs represent 50-60% of total production cost. Reducing FCR from 1.5 to 1.2 saves $0.45/kg produced (@ $1.5/kg feed price).

4.2 Factors Affecting FCR

Water Temperature

Fish are cold-blooded. Metabolism doubles for every 10°C rise. Optimal feeding temps:

  • Cold-water (trout, salmon): 12-16°C
  • Temperate (flatfish, sea bass): 18-24°C
  • Warm-water (tilapia, shrimp): 26-30°C

Dissolved Oxygen

Below 6 mg/L, fish reduce feeding. Below 4 mg/L, stop feeding entirely. Ensure DO >7 mg/L during feeding times.

Fish Size & Growth Stage

Smaller fish have higher metabolic rates. Feeding rate (% of biomass per day):

  • Fingerlings (1-10g): 5-8%
  • Juveniles (10-100g): 2.5-4%
  • Adults (100-500g): 1.5-2.5%

Feed Quality

Protein content affects FCR. Marine carnivores require 40-50% protein. Herbivores: 28-35%. Higher protein = better FCR but higher cost.

4.3 Automated Feeding Systems

Time-based Feeders

Dispense fixed amounts at scheduled times (e.g., 8AM, 12PM, 5PM). Simple but doesn't account for appetite variation.

Demand Feeders

Fish trigger feeding by activating a sensor (pendulum, optical). Pro: Fish eat when hungry. Con: Dominant fish trigger more, uneven growth.

Camera-based Smart Feeders

Underwater cameras with AI assess appetite in real-time:

  • Count pellets reaching bottom (uneaten feed)
  • Analyze fish behavior (active feeding vs. satiation)
  • Adjust feed rate every 30 seconds
  • Stop when appetite drops below threshold

Results: 25-35% reduction in feed waste, FCR improvement of 0.1-0.2 units.

4.4 Feeding Strategies

Satiation Feeding

Feed until fish show satiation behavior. Maximizes growth but risks overfeeding and water quality issues.

Restricted Feeding

Feed 80-90% of satiation amount. Slower growth but better FCR and water quality. Used in high-density systems.

Multi-frequency Feeding

More frequent small meals improve FCR vs. large infrequent meals. 3-6 times daily optimal for most species.
