Chapter 6: Phase 3 - Protocols

Measurement protocols and clinical assessment procedures

6.1 TMS-EEG Measurement Protocol

Equipment Requirements

  • TMS System: Navigated TMS with biphasic pulse capability (Nexstim, Magstim, MagVenture)
  • EEG System: ≥60 channels, ≥5000 Hz sampling, TMS-compatible amplifiers
  • Neuronavigation: MRI-based head positioning
  • Ancillary: EMG for motor threshold, ECG for artifact removal

Standard PCI Protocol Steps

  1. 1. Subject Preparation: Position comfortably, apply EEG cap, verify impedances <5 kΩ
  2. 2. Neuronavigation: Co-register head position with structural MRI
  3. 3. Target Selection: Identify left premotor cortex (BA6) as primary target
  4. 4. Baseline Recording: 5 minutes resting EEG with fixation cross
  5. 5. TMS Stimulation: 200 biphasic pulses at 0.5-1 Hz, 80-120% motor threshold
  6. 6. EEG Recording: 500ms pre- and post-stimulus epochs
  7. 7. Quality Check: Verify ≥150 artifact-free epochs

6.2 PCI Computation Pipeline

1. PREPROCESSING
   ├── Artifact Removal
   │   ├── TMS artifact: interpolation (-2 to 15 ms)
   │   ├── Muscle: ICA/SSP
   │   ├── Eye: ICA regression
   │   └── Cardiac: template subtraction
   ├── Filtering: 1-80 Hz bandpass
   └── Epoching: -100 to 500 ms

2. BINARIZATION
   ├── Source localization
   ├── Threshold: 3.5 SD from baseline
   └── Time window: 20-300 ms post-TMS

3. COMPRESSION
   ├── Create spatiotemporal matrix
   ├── Flatten to 1D string
   └── Apply Lempel-Ziv algorithm

4. NORMALIZATION
   └── PCI = L(SS) / (s × t × H(p))

6.3 Clinical Assessment Protocol

Disorders of Consciousness Evaluation

Important: PCI should complement, not replace, behavioral assessment (CRS-R).

Standard evaluation workflow:

  1. Conduct Coma Recovery Scale-Revised (CRS-R) - 5 sessions over 2 weeks
  2. Perform TMS-EEG during maximal arousal
  3. Calculate PCI and classify state
  4. Compare behavioral and electrophysiological findings
  5. Consider additional tests if discrepancy exists

6.5 Quality Standards

Quality Criteria:
  • Impedances: <5 kΩ all channels
  • Artifact-free epochs: ≥150
  • Signal amplitude: <500 μV
  • Test-retest reliability: ICC >0.7

6.6 Interpretation Guidelines

Important caveats:

  • Single measurement should not determine diagnosis alone
  • Medications (especially sedatives) can affect PCI
  • Repeat measurements recommended across multiple sessions
  • Clinical behavioral assessment remains essential
  • PCI measures capacity for consciousness, not content

For borderline cases (PCI within 0.05 of threshold):

  1. Repeat measurement 3x over 1 week
  2. Report range and trend
  3. Do not assign definitive classification
  4. Recommend continued monitoring
