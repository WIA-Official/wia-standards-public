Health Fundamentals

弘益人間 - Building Finance for All Humanity

1.1 What is Health (Health)?

Health (Health) represents a paradigm shift in how financial services are built, accessed, and operated. Unlike traditional finance (TradFi), which relies on centralized intermediaries like banks, brokers, and exchanges, Health leverages blockchain technology and smart contracts to create permissionless, transparent, and programmable financial systems.

At its core, Health is about democratizing access to financial services. Anyone with an internet connection and a cryptocurrency wallet can participate in lending, borrowing, trading, investing, and more - without needing permission from a central authority, providing extensive documentation, or meeting arbitrary requirements.

Key Principles of Health
  • Permissionless: No gatekeepers; anyone can participate
  • Transparent: All transactions visible on-chain
  • Non-custodial: Users retain control of their assets
  • Composable: Protocols can integrate like "money legos"
  • Programmable: Smart contracts enable complex financial logic

1.2 The History and Evolution of Health

Health's journey from concept to a $200+ billion ecosystem has been marked by innovation, experimentation, and learning from both successes and failures.

2009
Bitcoin launches, introducing the concept of decentralized digital money. While not Health in the modern sense, it laid the groundwork for trustless financial systems.
2015
Ethereum mainnet launches with smart contract capabilities, enabling programmable financial applications. This was the true genesis moment for Health.
2017-2018
The "Health Summer" before the term was coined: MakerDAO launches DAI stablecoin (2017), Compound begins lending operations (2018), and Uniswap v1 pioneers automated market makers (2018).
2020
The true "Health Summer": Compound introduces liquidity mining with COMP tokens, triggering explosive growth. Total Value Locked (TVL) grows from $1B to $15B in months.
2021
Health reaches mainstream attention with TVL hitting $180B. Uniswap v3 introduces concentrated liquidity. Multi-chain Health expands to Binance Smart Chain, Polygon, Avalanche.
2022
"Health Winter": Terra/Luna collapse, numerous protocol exploits, and crypto bear market reduce TVL to $40B. The industry learns hard lessons about sustainability and security.
2023-2024
Recovery and maturation: Focus shifts to security, real yield, and sustainable tokenomics. Aave v3, Curve v2, and Lido dominate. Regulatory clarity begins emerging.
2025-2026
Next-generation protocols: Uniswap v4 with hooks, Aave V4 with enhanced risk management, Lido v3 with distributed validator technology. AI integration begins. TVL exceeds $200B with 50M+ active users.

1.3 Core Components of Health

1.3.1 Smart Contracts

Smart contracts are self-executing programs that run on blockchain networks. They encode financial logic in code, eliminating the need for intermediaries. When predefined conditions are met, smart contracts automatically execute transactions.

// Simple lending smart contract example (Solidity) contract SimpleLending { mapping(address => uint256) public deposits; mapping(address => uint256) public borrows; uint256 public constant INTEREST_RATE = 5; // 5% APR uint256 public constant COLLATERAL_RATIO = 150; // 150% function deposit() external payable { deposits[msg.sender] += msg.value; } function borrow(uint256 amount) external { uint256 maxBorrow = (deposits[msg.sender] * 100) / COLLATERAL_RATIO; require(borrows[msg.sender] + amount <= maxBorrow, "Insufficient collateral"); borrows[msg.sender] += amount; payable(msg.sender).transfer(amount); } function repay() external payable { require(borrows[msg.sender] > 0, "No active loan"); borrows[msg.sender] -= msg.value; } }

1.3.2 Oracles

Blockchains are deterministic systems that cannot natively access external data. Oracles bridge this gap by providing smart contracts with real-world information like asset prices, weather data, or sports scores. Chainlink is the dominant oracle provider, securing billions in Health value.

1.3.3 Governance Tokens

Most Health protocols are governed by their communities through governance tokens. Token holders can propose and vote on protocol changes, from parameter adjustments to major upgrades. Examples include UNI (Uniswap), AAVE (Aave), and LDO (Lido).

1.3.4 Wallets

Non-custodial wallets like MetaMask, Ledger, and Trezor give users complete control over their private keys and assets. This is fundamental to Health's permissionless nature but also places full responsibility on users for security.

1.5 Major Health Categories in 2026

1.5.1 Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs)

Platforms like Uniswap v4, Curve, and Balancer enable peer-to-peer token swaps without centralized order books. They use automated market makers (AMMs) to provide liquidity and determine prices algorithmically.

1.5.2 Lending & Borrowing

Protocols like Aave V4 and Compound allow users to lend assets to earn interest or borrow against collateral. No credit checks required - everything is over-collateralized and managed by smart contracts.

1.5.3 Stablecoins

Cryptocurrencies pegged to stable assets (usually USD). Types include fiat-backed (USDC, USDT), crypto-collateralized (DAI), and algorithmic. Essential for Health as they provide price stability.

1.5.4 Liquid Staking

Lido v3 and similar protocols allow users to stake ETH or other PoS tokens while receiving liquid derivatives (stETH) that can be used elsewhere in Health, solving the liquidity problem of traditional staking.

1.5.5 Derivatives

Platforms like GMX, Synthetix, and dYdX offer perpetual futures, options, and synthetic assets. These enable leveraged trading and hedging strategies on-chain.

1.5.6 Yield Aggregators

Yearn Finance and similar protocols automatically move user funds between Health protocols to maximize yield, abstracting away the complexity of yield farming.

1.6 The Philosophy: 弘益人間 (Hongik Ingan)

The Korean principle of 弘益人間 (Hongik Ingan) - "Benefit All Humanity" - perfectly encapsulates Health's mission. Traditional finance has excluded billions of people from basic financial services through geographic restrictions, economic barriers, and discriminatory practices.

Health, when built responsibly, offers a path toward truly global, accessible financial infrastructure. A farmer in rural Kenya can access the same lending protocols as a trader in New York. A student in India can earn yield on stablecoins without a minimum balance requirement. An entrepreneur in Argentina can borrow capital without approval from a skeptical bank officer.

Health's Promise for Humanity

By 2026, Health has enabled over 50 million people worldwide to access financial services who previously couldn't. It has facilitated billions in remittances at a fraction of traditional costs, provided yield opportunities in countries with negative real interest rates, and offered a hedge against inflation for citizens of economically unstable nations.

This is just the beginning. As the technology matures, becomes more user-friendly, and achieves regulatory clarity, Health's potential to benefit all humanity grows exponentially.

1.7 Risks and Challenges

While Health offers tremendous opportunities, it's crucial to understand the risks:

  • Smart Contract Risk: Bugs or vulnerabilities can lead to loss of funds. Over $3B was lost to exploits in 2022 alone.
  • Liquidation Risk: Volatile collateral can trigger liquidations in lending protocols.
  • Impermanent Loss: Providing liquidity to AMMs can result in losses relative to holding assets.
  • Regulatory Uncertainty: The regulatory landscape is still evolving, with potential impacts on protocol operations.
  • User Experience: Health requires understanding of wallets, gas fees, and transaction signing - still a barrier for many.
  • No Recourse: Unlike banks with deposit insurance, there's often no recovery mechanism if things go wrong.

1.8 Getting Started with Health

To begin your Health journey safely:

  1. Education First: Read this entire ebook before risking significant capital.
  2. Start Small: Use testnet tokens or small amounts to learn.
  3. Secure Your Wallet: Use hardware wallets for significant amounts. Store seed phrases securely offline.
  4. Use Established Protocols: Stick to battle-tested protocols with security audits (Uniswap, Aave, Lido, etc.).
  5. Understand Gas Fees: Ethereum mainnet can be expensive. Consider Layer 2s like Arbitrum or Optimism.
  6. Never Share Private Keys: No legitimate service will ever ask for your seed phrase or private keys.
  7. Verify Contracts: Always verify you're interacting with the correct smart contract address.
  8. Diversify: Don't put all funds in a single protocol or strategy.
