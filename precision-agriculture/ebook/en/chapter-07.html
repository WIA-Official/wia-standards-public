Case Study 1: GreenField Farms - Large-Scale Row Crops

GreenField Farms

Nebraska, USA | 4,500 hectares | Corn & Soybeans

Gold Certified

Background

GreenField Farms operates 4,500 hectares of irrigated row crops in central Nebraska, producing corn and soybeans in annual rotation. Prior to precision agriculture adoption, the operation used uniform management practices across all fields despite significant soil variability ranging from sandy loam to silty clay. Yield monitors revealed substantial within-field variation (6-16 tonnes/ha for corn) that uniform management could not address.

Implementation Journey

Year 1 - Assessment & Foundation

Comprehensive soil sampling (1 sample per hectare), yield data analysis (5 years), and management zone development. Installed 45 soil moisture sensor stations across representative fields.

Year 2 - Variable Rate Nitrogen

Implemented zone-based nitrogen management on 2,000 hectares. Conducted strip trials comparing variable rate to uniform application.

Year 3 - Precision Irrigation

Upgraded center pivot systems with variable rate irrigation (VRI) capability. Integrated soil moisture sensors with irrigation scheduling.

Year 4 - Full Integration

Expanded VRT to all inputs (seed, P, K, micronutrients). Implemented predictive analytics for yield forecasting and marketing decisions.

Case Study 2: Sunshine Vineyards - Precision Viticulture

Sunshine Vineyards

Napa Valley, California | 180 hectares | Premium Wine Grapes

Platinum Certified

Background

Sunshine Vineyards produces premium Cabernet Sauvignon and Chardonnay grapes for ultra-premium wine production where quality commands significant price premiums. The hillside vineyard terrain creates extreme variability in sun exposure, soil depth, and water availability. Traditional uniform management resulted in inconsistent grape quality that limited winemaking options.

Precision Viticulture Implementation

The vineyard implemented comprehensive precision viticulture focused on quality optimization rather than yield maximization. High-resolution soil mapping identified 23 distinct soil units within the 180-hectare property. Drone-based multispectral imagery captured vine vigor patterns at individual vine resolution throughout the growing season.

Soil moisture sensors at 120 locations monitor water status across soil types and topographic positions. These sensors drive variable rate deficit irrigation that induces controlled water stress to concentrate grape flavors while preventing excessive stress that degrades quality.

Yield monitors on mechanical harvesters create vine-by-vine yield maps that inform selective harvesting. Different quality blocks within single vineyard blocks are harvested separately based on remotely-sensed ripeness indicators and historical quality correlations.

Technology Stack

  • EM38 and gamma-ray soil sensing for detailed soil mapping
  • Weather stations with 500-meter spacing for microclimate monitoring
  • Multispectral drone flights weekly during growing season
  • Wireless soil moisture sensors with hourly data transmission
  • NDVI-based selective harvester with individual vine tracking
  • Custom WIA-compliant analytics platform for quality prediction

Quality-focused precision management increased the proportion of grapes meeting ultra-premium standards from 45% to 78%. The resulting wine quality improvements enabled price increases that more than offset the modest yield reduction from deficit irrigation. Overall revenue per hectare increased 32% while water use decreased 25%.

Case Study 3: AgriCoop Kenya - Smallholder Aggregation

AgriCoop Kenya

Central Kenya | 2,400 smallholders | Mixed Crops

Silver Certified

Background

AgriCoop Kenya aggregates production from 2,400 smallholder farmers averaging 0.8 hectares each. Traditional extension services could not provide timely, site-specific guidance to dispersed farmers. Input recommendations were generic despite significant soil and climate variation across the cooperative's operating area spanning three counties.

Mobile-First Precision Agriculture

AgriCoop implemented a mobile-first precision agriculture platform designed for feature phone access via SMS and USSD alongside smartphone apps for farmers with Android devices. The platform delivers personalized recommendations based on farmer location, registered crops, and satellite-derived field conditions.

Satellite imagery from Sentinel-2 provides field-level vegetation monitoring without requiring on-farm sensors. Cloud-based algorithms process imagery weekly during growing seasons and generate alerts when individual fields show stress symptoms or development anomalies. Farmers receive SMS notifications with specific guidance in Swahili or English.

Soil testing campaigns sampled representative locations across the operating area, creating interpolated soil fertility maps that inform fertilizer recommendations. Recommendations account for both soil conditions and economic constraints, optimizing limited fertilizer investments for maximum return.

Case Study 4: AquaGrow Netherlands - Greenhouse Production

AquaGrow Netherlands

Westland, Netherlands | 12 hectares | Hydroponic Tomatoes

Platinum Certified

Background

AquaGrow operates high-tech glass greenhouses producing premium tomatoes year-round using hydroponic systems. In the controlled greenhouse environment, precision extends to individual plant management with comprehensive monitoring of climate, nutrition, and plant development. The Dutch greenhouse sector leads globally in technology adoption, creating intense competitive pressure for optimization.

Integrated Precision Systems

Every aspect of the growing environment is monitored and controlled through an integrated precision platform. Climate sensors measure temperature, humidity, CO2, and light at 50 locations within each hectare. Substrate sensors monitor EC, pH, and moisture for each irrigation zone. Camera systems capture plant images for automated growth tracking and disease detection.

Machine learning models predict optimal climate setpoints based on weather forecasts, energy prices, and crop development stage. The system continuously adjusts heating, ventilation, CO2 enrichment, and supplemental lighting to maximize photosynthesis while minimizing energy costs. Dynamic optimization reduces energy consumption by 18% compared to fixed setpoint control.

Nutrient management uses real-time EC and pH monitoring with automatic adjustment. Ion-selective sensors track individual nutrient concentrations, enabling precise recipe adjustments that maintain optimal nutrition throughout crop development. Drain water recycling captures and treats runoff for reuse, achieving near-zero nutrient discharge.

Automation and Robotics

  • Autonomous harvesting robots with computer vision for ripeness detection
  • Automated crop registration linking individual plants to production data
  • Disease scouting drones that identify problems before human-visible symptoms
  • Integrated pest management with automated beneficial insect release
  • Robotic leaf pruning and plant maintenance operations

Lessons Learned Across Case Studies

Analysis across these diverse case studies reveals common success factors and lessons applicable to precision agriculture implementations regardless of scale or crop type.

Universal Success Factors

  1. Start with clear objectives: Each successful implementation began with specific, measurable goals rather than technology adoption for its own sake
  2. Phase implementation appropriately: Gradual rollout with learning between phases outperformed big-bang implementations
  3. Invest in data quality: Successful operations prioritized sensor calibration, data validation, and quality assurance
  4. Combine data with expertise: Technology augmented rather than replaced agronomic knowledge
  5. Measure and adapt: Systematic performance tracking enabled continuous improvement
  6. Scale to context: Technology choices matched operational scale and capability
