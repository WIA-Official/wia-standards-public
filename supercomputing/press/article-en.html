Executive Summary

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released WIA-COMP-001, an international standard for exascale (10^18 FLOPS) supercomputing systems covering architecture, performance benchmarking, and cluster management, as free and open source under MIT License. This announcement positions the Republic of Korea as a leader in high-performance computing international standards.

🚨 Current Situation: Why This Standard Is Needed

Over 80% of the world's top 500 supercomputers are concentrated in the United States, China, and Japan. Supercomputing resources essential for climate simulation, drug discovery, and AI training cost millions of dollars annually, accessible only to large corporations and major research institutions. Developing countries and smaller research organizations are denied opportunities for innovation due to lack of computing resources.

WIA's Solution: WIA-COMP-001 Supercomputing

WIA-COMP-001 standardizes every aspect of supercomputing from exascale computing architecture to cluster management and performance benchmarking. It provides a heterogeneous computing framework integrating CPU, GPU, FPGA, and TPU, along with programming models based on MPI, OpenMP, and CUDA.

🏢 About WIA

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) is an international standards organization dedicated to providing equal opportunities for all humanity through technology and certification.

WIA develops over 600 international standards across accessibility, security, AI, environment, healthcare, and more—all released as free and open source.

🦀 Tech Stack: WIA's core APIs are built in Rust—the systems programming language that achieves both memory safety and zero-cost abstractions.

👤 About the CEO

Dr. Yeon Sam-Heum is the world's only one-person standards architect who has single-handedly built infrastructure for over 60 international standards in collaboration with AI, and is recognized as a living legend in the lifelong education field.

Since founding SmileStory Inc. in 2009, he has operated 11 business domains including travel, insurance, publishing, education, media, web/software development, medical devices, medical tourism, and business consulting.
