Executive Summary

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released international standard WIA-AUTO-021 covering specifications, testing methods, and computational frameworks for vehicle lightweight materials including carbon fiber composites, aluminum alloys, high-strength steel, and advanced polymers, under MIT license for free. This announcement positions Korea as a leader in vehicle material innovation international standards.

🚨 Current Situation: Why This Standard Is Needed

Traditional vehicles have heavy steel-based bodies resulting in low fuel efficiency and high CO₂ emissions. In the electric vehicle era, lightweighting is even more critical due to battery weight, but lack of standardization for lightweight materials like carbon fiber and aluminum leads to different specifications across manufacturers, degrading interoperability and recyclability while increasing costs. Particularly, unclear crash safety validation standards raise concerns that lightweighting may compromise safety.

Sam-Heum Yeon, CEO:
"100kg vehicle weight reduction means 6-8% fuel efficiency improvement. But lightweighting should not sacrifice safety. We created material standards that achieve both weight reduction and safety enhancement simultaneously."

WIA's Solution: WIA-AUTO-021 Lightweight Materials

WIA-AUTO-021 provides material property databases for CFRP, aluminum 6061/7075, magnesium alloys, high-strength steel (AHSS), and titanium alloys, weight reduction calculation algorithms, strength-to-weight ratio analysis, manufacturing process standards, crash safety validation, and recyclability assessment.
