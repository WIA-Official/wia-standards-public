## What is Space Tourism?

Space tourism is commercial spaceflight for recreational purposes. The industry has evolved into three tiers:

### 🌌 Suborbital Tourism
- **Altitude:** 80-110 km (50-68 miles)
- **Duration:** 11-90 minutes
- **Experience:** 3-4 minutes of weightlessness, view Earth's curvature
- **Providers:** Virgin Galactic, Blue Origin
- **Price:** $200,000 - $500,000

### 🛸 Orbital Tourism
- **Altitude:** 400-600 km (250-375 miles)
- **Duration:** 3-14 days
- **Experience:** Extended weightlessness, Earth observation, ISS visits
- **Providers:** SpaceX, Axiom Space, Space Adventures
- **Price:** $50-60 million

### 🌙 Lunar Tourism
- **Distance:** 380,000 km to the Moon
- **Duration:** 6-14 days
- **Experience:** Lunar flyby, orbit, or surface landing
- **Providers:** SpaceX (Dear Moon mission planned)
- **Price:** $100 million+ (estimated)

## Technical Requirements

### Medical Screening

**Suborbital Missions:**
- Good cardiovascular health (BP <140/90 mmHg)
- Age 18+ (no upper limit if healthy)
- Ability to tolerate 3-6 G forces
- Normal EKG

**Orbital Missions:**
- All suborbital requirements PLUS
- Comprehensive cardiovascular evaluation
- Bone density scan (DEXA)
- Vestibular function testing
- Psychological evaluation
- 3-6 months training required

### Training Duration

- **Suborbital:** 2-3 days orientation
- **Orbital (Free Flight):** 3-4 months comprehensive training
- **Orbital (ISS Visit):** 4-6 months NASA-level training
- **Lunar (Future):** 6-12 months extensive preparation

## Environmental Considerations

### Current Impact

- Suborbital flight: ~1.2 tons CO2 per passenger (comparable to transatlantic business class)
- Blue Origin: Zero carbon emissions (hydrogen/oxygen → water vapor only)
- SpaceX Falcon 9: ~425 tons CO2 per launch
- Scale: Even with 1,000x growth, space launches <1% of aviation industry impact

### Sustainability Measures

✅ Reusable vehicles (90-95%+ cost reduction)
✅ Green propulsion research (hydrogen/oxygen, methane)
✅ Space debris mitigation (25-year deorbit rule)
✅ Carbon offset programs
✅ Continuous environmental monitoring

## Legal Framework

### Key Regulations

- **Outer Space Treaty (1967):** Foundation of international space law
- **FAA Commercial Space Transportation:** U.S. launch licensing
- **Informed Consent:** Required disclosure of risks to participants
- **Cross-Waiver of Liability:** Standard protection for operators and participants
- **ITAR Export Control:** Technology transfer restrictions

### Passenger Rights

- Full disclosure of risks before purchase
- Medical privacy protection (HIPAA)
- Flight cancellation/rescheduling for safety
- Refund for medical disqualification
- Right to information on safety records
