2.1 The Five Freedoms

Origin and Development
======================

The Five Freedoms were developed by the UK Farm Animal Welfare Council
in 1979, derived from the Brambell Report of 1965. They have since
become the global standard for assessing animal welfare.

The Five Freedoms:
  1. Freedom from Hunger and Thirst
  2. Freedom from Discomfort
  3. Freedom from Pain, Injury, or Disease
  4. Freedom to Express Normal Behavior
  5. Freedom from Fear and Distress

WIA Adaptation for Companion Animals:
  - Species-specific requirements
  - Behavioral enrichment emphasis
  - Social needs recognition
  - Emotional well-being inclusion

2.2 Freedom from Hunger and Thirst

Definition
==========
Ready access to fresh water and a diet to maintain
full health and vigor.

Assessment Indicators
=====================

Water Availability (10 points)
  10: Fresh water 24/7, multiple sources
   8: Fresh water always available
   6: Water available most of time
   4: Water available at set times only
   2: Irregular water access
   0: No reliable water access

Diet Appropriateness (10 points)
  10: Species and age-specific, high quality
   8: Appropriate commercial diet
   6: Adequate nutrition
   4: Basic nutrition met
   2: Nutritional deficiencies possible
   0: Inadequate nutrition

Feeding Schedule (10 points)
  10: Optimal schedule for species/age
   8: Consistent, appropriate timing
   6: Regular feeding
   4: Inconsistent timing
   2: Irregular feeding
   0: No feeding schedule

Body Condition Monitoring (10 points)
  10: Regular assessment, records, action plans
   8: Regular monitoring with records
   6: Periodic monitoring
   4: Occasional monitoring
   2: Rare monitoring
   0: No monitoring

Special Dietary Needs (10 points)
  10: Full accommodation, medical diets available
   8: Good accommodation of needs
   6: Basic accommodation
   4: Limited accommodation
   2: Poor accommodation
   0: No accommodation

Maximum Score: 50 points

2.3 Freedom from Discomfort

Definition
==========
An appropriate environment including shelter
and a comfortable resting area.

Assessment Indicators
=====================

Shelter Adequacy (10 points)
  10: Climate-controlled, species-appropriate
   8: Good shelter from elements
   6: Basic shelter provided
   4: Minimal shelter
   2: Inadequate shelter
   0: No shelter

Resting Area (10 points)
  10: Comfortable bedding, appropriate surfaces
   8: Good resting options
   6: Basic resting area
   4: Minimal comfort
   2: Uncomfortable surfaces
   0: No appropriate resting area

Temperature Control (10 points)
  10: HVAC with species-appropriate ranges
   8: Good temperature management
   6: Adequate temperature control
   4: Basic heating/cooling
   2: Poor temperature control
   0: No temperature control

Ventilation (10 points)
  10: Excellent air quality, proper air changes
   8: Good ventilation
   6: Adequate air flow
   4: Minimal ventilation
   2: Poor air quality
   0: No ventilation system

Space Per Animal (10 points)
  10: Exceeds minimum by 50%+
   8: Exceeds minimum standards
   6: Meets minimum standards
   4: Near minimum
   2: Below minimum
   0: Severely overcrowded

Maximum Score: 50 points

2.6 Freedom from Fear and Distress

Definition
==========
Conditions and treatment which avoid
mental suffering.

Assessment Indicators
=====================

Calm Environment (10 points)
  10: Quiet, low-stress, noise managed
   8: Generally calm environment
   6: Moderate noise levels
   4: Noisy environment
   2: High-stress environment
   0: Chaotic, frightening

Gentle Handling (10 points)
  10: All staff trained, gentle methods
   8: Good handling practices
   6: Adequate handling
   4: Inconsistent handling
   2: Rough handling observed
   0: Abusive handling

Predictable Routines (10 points)
  10: Consistent schedules, minimal disruption
   8: Good routine consistency
   6: Basic routines
   4: Inconsistent routines
   2: Chaotic schedules
   0: No routines

Stress Monitoring (10 points)
  10: Behavior logs, stress signs tracked
   8: Regular stress assessment
   6: Basic monitoring
   4: Occasional monitoring
   2: Rare monitoring
   0: No stress monitoring

Mental Well-being Programs (10 points)
  10: Behavioral support, positive reinforcement
   8: Good mental health focus
   6: Basic behavioral support
   4: Limited support
   2: Poor mental health attention
   0: No mental well-being focus

Maximum Score: 50 points

2.7 Scoring Methodology

Score Calculation
=================

Total Possible: 250 points (50 per freedom)

Overall Score = Sum of all indicators / 250 * 100%

Tier Thresholds:
  PLATINUM: >= 95% (238+ points)
  GOLD:     >= 85% (213+ points)
  SILVER:   >= 75% (188+ points)
  BRONZE:   >= 60% (150+ points)
  FAIL:     < 60%  (<150 points)


Individual Freedom Requirements
===============================

Minimum per Freedom for Certification:
  PLATINUM: >= 45/50 (90%) each freedom
  GOLD:     >= 40/50 (80%) each freedom
  SILVER:   >= 35/50 (70%) each freedom
  BRONZE:   >= 25/50 (50%) each freedom

Note: Overall score AND individual minimums must be met


Critical Indicators
===================

Some indicators are critical - scoring below threshold
in these areas results in automatic failure:

  - Water availability: Minimum 4
  - Veterinary access: Minimum 4
  - Injury prevention: Minimum 4
  - Temperature control: Minimum 4

Scoring 2 or below on any critical indicator = FAIL


Assessment Documentation
========================

Required Evidence:
  - Photographs of facilities
  - Policy and procedure documents
  - Training records
  - Health records
  - Feeding logs
  - Enrichment schedules

Assessor Notes:
  - Specific observations
  - Animal behavior during visit
  - Staff interactions observed
  - Areas of strength
  - Areas for improvement
