4.1 Audit Types

Audit Type Purpose Notice Scope
INITIAL First certification Scheduled Full assessment
SURVEILLANCE Ongoing compliance Scheduled Partial review
RECERTIFICATION Renewal of certification Scheduled Full assessment
UNANNOUNCED Verify true conditions No notice Full or targeted
FOLLOW-UP Verify corrective actions Short notice Specific findings
COMPLAINT Investigate concerns No notice Targeted 
Audit Frequency by Tier
=======================

BRONZE (1-year certification):
  Year 1: Initial audit
  Year 1 (6 months): Unannounced possible
  Year 1 (end): Recertification audit

SILVER (2-year certification):
  Year 1: Initial audit
  Year 1 (12 months): Surveillance audit
  Year 2 (end): Recertification audit

GOLD (3-year certification):
  Year 1: Initial audit
  Year 1 (12 months): Surveillance audit
  Year 2 (24 months): Surveillance audit
  Year 3 (end): Recertification audit

PLATINUM (3-year certification):
  Year 1: Initial audit
  Year 2 (18 months): Light-touch surveillance
  Year 3 (end): Recertification audit

All Tiers:
  Unannounced audits: At least 1 per cycle
  Complaint audits: As triggered

4.2 Audit Process

Pre-Audit Phase
===============

30 Days Before:
  - Audit team assigned
  - Notification sent (for scheduled audits)
  - Document request issued

14 Days Before:
  - Documents received and reviewed
  - Previous findings reviewed
  - Audit plan drafted

7 Days Before:
  - Final audit plan sent
  - Logistics confirmed
  - Questions addressed


On-Site Audit Phase
===================

Opening Meeting (30 minutes):
  - Introductions
  - Audit scope and objectives
  - Schedule confirmation
  - Safety briefing
  - Queries addressed

Facility Tour (1-2 hours):
  - All animal housing areas
  - Support areas (food prep, storage)
  - Veterinary/medical areas
  - Outdoor areas
  - Emergency equipment

Document Review (1-2 hours):
  - Policies and procedures
  - Training records
  - Health records
  - Feeding logs
  - Incident reports

Animal Observation (1-2 hours):
  - Body condition assessment
  - Behavior observation
  - Interaction with handlers
  - Environment adequacy
  - Random selection sampling

Staff Interviews (30-60 minutes):
  - Knowledge verification
  - Practice understanding
  - Concern identification
  - Training adequacy

Five Freedoms Assessment (1-2 hours):
  - Systematic scoring
  - Evidence collection
  - Photograph documentation
  - Gap identification

Closing Meeting (30-60 minutes):
  - Preliminary findings
  - Observations discussed
  - Strengths highlighted
  - Non-conformities explained
  - Next steps outlined


Post-Audit Phase
================

Within 5 Days:
  - Draft report to entity

Within 10 Days:
  - Final report issued
  - Non-conformities documented
  - Corrective action timeline set

Within 30 Days:
  - Certification decision
  - Certificate issued (if approved)
  - Public registry updated

4.3 Finding Classification

Non-Conformity Classifications
==============================

CRITICAL
  Definition:
    Immediate threat to animal welfare
    Violation of law
    Fraudulent practice

  Examples:
    - Animals without water access
    - Active cruelty observed
    - Falsified health records
    - Overcrowding causing injury

  Response:
    - Certification cannot be granted
    - Existing certification suspended
    - Immediate corrective action required
    - May involve authorities


MAJOR
  Definition:
    Significant deviation from requirements
    Systemic failure of controls
    Risk to animal welfare

  Examples:
    - Inadequate veterinary care
    - Significant space shortfalls
    - No training program
    - Multiple minor issues in same area

  Response:
    - 30-day corrective action required
    - May require follow-up audit
    - Certification decision delayed


MINOR
  Definition:
    Isolated deviation from requirements
    Low risk to animal welfare
    Easy to correct

  Examples:
    - Incomplete records
    - Minor space shortfall
    - Single equipment issue
    - Procedure not documented

  Response:
    - 90-day corrective action
    - Self-declaration of correction
    - Verified at next surveillance


OBSERVATION
  Definition:
    Opportunity for improvement
    Not a requirement violation
    Best practice recommendation

  Examples:
    - Enhanced enrichment options
    - Better documentation format
    - Process efficiency
    - Staff development ideas

  Response:
    - No mandatory action
    - Consider for improvement
    - May become requirement in future

4.4 Corrective Actions

Corrective Action Process
=========================

Step 1: Root Cause Analysis
  - Why did the non-conformity occur?
  - What systems failed?
  - Is this isolated or systemic?
  - What conditions contributed?

Step 2: Immediate Correction
  - Stop the immediate problem
  - Protect animal welfare now
  - Contain the issue
  - Document actions taken

Step 3: Corrective Action Plan
  - Address root cause
  - Prevent recurrence
  - Define specific actions
  - Assign responsibilities
  - Set realistic timelines

Step 4: Implementation
  - Execute the plan
  - Document progress
  - Collect evidence
  - Address obstacles

Step 5: Verification
  - Self-verify effectiveness
  - Gather evidence
  - Submit to certification body
  - Accept follow-up audit if required


Corrective Action Timeline
==========================

CRITICAL:
  Immediate: Contain issue
  24 hours: Action plan submitted
  7 days: Critical aspects resolved
  30 days: Full resolution

MAJOR:
  7 days: Action plan submitted
  30 days: Resolution deadline
  45 days: Follow-up verification

MINOR:
  14 days: Action plan submitted
  90 days: Resolution deadline
  Next audit: Verification
Finding Type Plan Due Resolution Due Verification
CRITICAL 24 hours 7-30 days Follow-up audit
MAJOR 7 days 30 days Follow-up audit
MINOR 14 days 90 days Next surveillance
OBSERVATION N/A N/A Optional review

4.5 Certification Decisions

Decision Criteria
=================

GRANT CERTIFICATION:
  - Five Freedoms score meets tier threshold
  - All individual freedom minimums met
  - No critical non-conformities
  - Major non-conformities: max 2, with action plans
  - Minor non-conformities: no limit, with plans

GRANT WITH CONDITIONS:
  - Score meets threshold
  - Major non-conformities being addressed
  - Follow-up audit scheduled
  - Time-limited certification possible

DEFER DECISION:
  - Evidence insufficient
  - Major non-conformities need resolution
  - Follow-up audit required
  - Decision within 90 days

DENY CERTIFICATION:
  - Critical non-conformity found
  - Score below threshold
  - Individual freedom below minimum
  - Fraud or deception identified
  - Legal non-compliance


Decision Committee
==================

Composition:
  - Technical expert (welfare)
  - Lead auditor (not from audit)
  - Quality manager
  - External advisor (as needed)

Process:
  1. Audit report reviewed
  2. Findings discussed
  3. Entity response considered
  4. Decision reached
  5. Rationale documented

Timeline:
  - Standard: within 30 days of audit
  - Complex cases: within 60 days

4.6 Appeals Process

Right to Appeal
===============

Appealable Decisions:
  - Certification denial
  - Certification suspension
  - Certification revocation
  - Tier downgrade
  - Non-conformity classification

Non-Appealable:
  - Audit scheduling
  - Auditor assignment
  - Observations
  - Fee decisions


Appeal Process
==============

Step 1: Notice of Appeal (30 days from decision)
  - Written appeal submitted
  - Grounds for appeal stated
  - Supporting evidence provided
  - Appeal fee paid (refundable if successful)

Step 2: Appeal Review (14 days)
  - Completeness check
  - Validity assessment
  - Additional information requested if needed

Step 3: Appeal Panel (30 days)
  - Independent panel appointed
  - Original materials reviewed
  - Appellant submission considered
  - Hearing conducted (if requested)

Step 4: Appeal Decision (14 days after panel)
  - Written decision issued
  - Reasons provided
  - Decision is final

Outcomes:
  - Appeal upheld: Original decision reversed
  - Appeal partially upheld: Modified decision
  - Appeal denied: Original decision stands


Appeal Panel Composition
========================

Minimum 3 members:
  - Independent chairperson
  - Welfare expert (external)
  - Industry representative (no conflict)

Exclusions:
  - Anyone involved in original decision
  - Anyone with conflict of interest
  - Anyone from same organization as appellant
