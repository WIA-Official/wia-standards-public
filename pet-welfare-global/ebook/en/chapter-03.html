3.1 Housing Requirements

General Housing Principles
==========================

PRIMARY ENCLOSURE
  - Safe, secure, escape-proof
  - Easy to clean and sanitize
  - Non-toxic materials
  - Appropriate size for species
  - Protection from elements

CONSTRUCTION STANDARDS
  - Durable, non-porous surfaces
  - No sharp edges or protrusions
  - Secure latches and doors
  - Proper drainage
  - Easy maintenance access

SPECIES-SPECIFIC DESIGN
  Dogs:
    - Run or kennel with outdoor access
    - Visual barriers optional (reduce stress)
    - Non-slip flooring
    - Raised resting platforms

  Cats:
    - Vertical space (shelving, climbing)
    - Hiding places
    - Scratching surfaces
    - Separate litter areas

  Small Animals:
    - Species-appropriate substrate
    - Hiding houses
    - Exercise equipment
    - Proper enclosure design

3.2 Space Standards

Animal Minimum Primary Exercise Area Notes
Dog (small, <10kg) 2.0 sqm 10 sqm Height: 1.2m min
Dog (medium, 10-25kg) 3.0 sqm 15 sqm Height: 1.5m min
Dog (large, >25kg) 4.0 sqm 20 sqm Height: 1.8m min
Cat (single) 1.5 sqm floor +1.0 sqm vertical Include hiding spots
Cat (group per cat) 1.0 sqm floor +0.5 sqm vertical Max 10 cats/group
Rabbit 0.5 sqm 3.0 sqm Exercise daily
Guinea Pig (pair) 0.4 sqm 1.0 sqm Must house in pairs 
Space Calculation Rules
=======================

Group Housing:
  Total Space = (Per Animal Min) x (Number) x 1.2
  Note: 20% additional for group dynamics

Breeding Facilities Additional:
  Whelping/Kitting Area: 2x primary enclosure
  Puppy/Kitten Socialization: 4 sqm minimum
  Separation Capability: Must be possible

Long-Term Housing (>6 months):
  All minimums increase by 50%
  Outdoor access required where possible

3.3 Environmental Controls

Temperature Requirements
========================

Dogs:
  Target: 18-24C (64-75F)
  Minimum: 10C (50F) with shelter
  Maximum: 29C (84F) with cooling
  Breed considerations apply

Cats:
  Target: 20-25C (68-77F)
  Minimum: 15C (59F)
  Maximum: 29C (84F)
  Senior/young: 22-25C preferred

Small Animals:
  Rabbits: 15-21C (59-70F)
  Guinea Pigs: 18-24C (64-75F)
  Hamsters: 20-24C (68-75F)


Ventilation Requirements
========================

Air Changes:
  Minimum: 10-15 per hour
  Preferred: 15-20 per hour

Ammonia Levels:
  Maximum: 25 ppm
  Target: <10 ppm

Humidity:
  Target: 30-70%
  Avoid: Sustained >80%


Lighting Requirements
=====================

Light Cycle:
  Minimum: 8 hours light
  Maximum: 16 hours light
  Natural light preferred

Intensity:
  General areas: 200-400 lux
  Rest areas: <100 lux available
  Avoid: Direct, harsh lighting

Nocturnal Animals:
  Reverse cycle possible
  Red light for observation

3.4 Sanitation Requirements

Cleaning Frequency
==================

Daily:
  - Remove waste and soiled bedding
  - Clean food and water bowls
  - Spot clean surfaces
  - Check for hazards

Weekly:
  - Full enclosure sanitization
  - Bedding replacement
  - Enrichment item cleaning
  - Drain cleaning

Monthly:
  - Deep cleaning all surfaces
  - Equipment inspection
  - Pest inspection
  - Ventilation cleaning


Disinfection Protocols
======================

Standard Cleaning:
  1. Remove organic matter
  2. Apply detergent, scrub
  3. Rinse thoroughly
  4. Apply disinfectant (contact time per product)
  5. Rinse if required
  6. Dry before animal return

Disease Outbreak:
  1. Isolate affected animals
  2. Enhanced cleaning protocol
  3. Contact surfaces: 2x daily
  4. Footbaths between areas
  5. Staff PPE requirements


Waste Management
================

Waste Types:
  - General waste (packaging, bedding)
  - Biological waste (feces, soiled materials)
  - Medical waste (medications, sharps)
  - Deceased animals

Disposal:
  - Licensed waste contractors
  - Proper segregation
  - Secure storage
  - Documented disposal

3.5 Safety Standards

Physical Safety
===============

Enclosure Safety:
  [OK] No gaps allowing escape or injury
  [OK] Latches secure against animal manipulation
  [OK] No sharp edges or protrusions
  [OK] Flooring non-slip, comfortable
  [OK] No strangulation hazards

Facility Safety:
  [OK] Fire detection and suppression
  [OK] Emergency exits clearly marked
  [OK] Electrical safety (animal-proofed)
  [OK] Chemical storage secured
  [OK] First aid supplies available


Escape Prevention
=================

Primary Barriers:
  - All enclosures secure
  - Double-door entry systems
  - Window/vent screens

Secondary Barriers:
  - Perimeter fencing (facilities)
  - Secure external doors
  - Vehicle entry controls

Emergency Protocols:
  - Escape reporting procedure
  - Search protocols
  - Notification requirements


Bite/Scratch Prevention
=======================

Risk Assessment:
  - New animal evaluation
  - Known behavior documentation
  - Warning systems (color coding)

Handling Protocols:
  - Appropriate restraint equipment
  - Two-person handling when needed
  - Training for all handlers

Incident Response:
  - First aid protocols
  - Reporting requirements
  - Follow-up care

3.6 Entity-Specific Requirements

BREEDING FACILITIES
===================

Additional Requirements:
  - Whelping/kitting areas: separate, quiet
  - Nursery: temperature controlled (warmer)
  - Socialization area: neutral territory
  - Quarantine: for new/sick animals
  - Stud housing: separate from females

Records Required:
  - Breeding pair documentation
  - Litter records
  - Genetic testing results
  - Veterinary clearances


PET STORES
==========

Additional Requirements:
  - Display enclosures: meet space standards
  - Stress reduction: quiet areas, hiding
  - Health monitoring: twice daily checks
  - Sale preparation: health certificates
  - Customer education: materials available

Restrictions:
  - Maximum display time: 4 hours/day
  - Handling by public: limited, supervised
  - Sale age minimums: enforced


SHELTERS
========

Additional Requirements:
  - Intake area: separate from general pop
  - Stray hold area: legal requirements
  - Adoption viewing: comfortable setting
  - Behavioral assessment: designated area
  - Euthanasia: humane, private

Capacity Management:
  - Maximum capacity defined
  - Protocols when at capacity
  - Foster network integration


BOARDING FACILITIES
===================

Additional Requirements:
  - Individual housing: unless from same home
  - Owner belongings: storage/use allowed
  - Diet: owner's food preferred
  - Medication: storage and administration
  - Emergency contact: maintained

Service Standards:
  - Exercise: minimum 2x daily
  - Monitoring: every 4 hours minimum
  - Communication: daily updates available
