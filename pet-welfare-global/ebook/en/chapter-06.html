6.1 Training Requirements

Training by Role
================

FACILITY STAFF
--------------
Required Training:
  - Animal welfare fundamentals (8 hours)
  - Species-specific handling (4 hours per species)
  - Health and safety (4 hours)
  - Emergency procedures (2 hours)

Refresher: Annual (4 hours minimum)

Delivery: Online or in-person


FACILITY MANAGERS
-----------------
Required Training:
  - All staff training (18+ hours)
  - Welfare management (8 hours)
  - Regulatory compliance (4 hours)
  - Staff supervision (4 hours)
  - Record keeping (2 hours)

Refresher: Annual (8 hours minimum)

Delivery: Hybrid (online + workshop)

6.2 Auditor Certification

Auditor Levels
==============

TRAINEE AUDITOR
---------------
Prerequisites:
  - Relevant degree or 3 years experience
  - Complete auditor training program
  - Pass written examination

Scope:
  - Assist in audits only
  - Under supervision of certified auditor
  - Cannot sign audit reports
  - Minimum 5 audits as trainee

Duration: 6-12 months typical


CERTIFIED AUDITOR
-----------------
Prerequisites:
  - Complete trainee period
  - Minimum 5 audits as trainee
  - Positive supervisor evaluations
  - Pass practical assessment
  - No ethical concerns

Scope:
  - Conduct audits independently
  - Sign audit reports
  - Supervise trainee auditors
  - All entity types within competence

Maintenance:
  - Annual CPD (16 hours)
  - Minimum 10 audits annually
  - Quality reviews satisfactory


SENIOR AUDITOR
--------------
Prerequisites:
  - 3+ years as certified auditor
  - 50+ audits completed
  - No quality concerns
  - Advanced training complete
  - Peer review passed

Scope:
  - All certified auditor scope
  - Complex and large facilities
  - Multi-site certifications
  - Technical review of reports

Maintenance:
  - Annual CPD (20 hours)
  - Minimum 15 audits annually
  - Mentor junior auditors


LEAD AUDITOR
------------
Prerequisites:
  - 5+ years audit experience
  - 100+ audits completed
  - Lead auditor training
  - Panel interview passed

Scope:
  - All senior auditor scope
  - Lead audit teams
  - Certification decisions
  - Appeal panel participation

Maintenance:
  - Annual CPD (24 hours)
  - Lead minimum 5 audits annually
  - Quality assurance role


MASTER AUDITOR
--------------
Prerequisites:
  - 10+ years audit experience
  - 200+ audits completed
  - Industry recognition
  - Contribution to standards

Scope:
  - All lead auditor scope
  - Standard development
  - Training delivery
  - International harmonization

Maintenance:
  - Annual CPD (24 hours)
  - Active contribution to profession

6.3 Continuing Education

CPD Requirements
================

Annual Requirements by Role:
  Facility Staff: 4 hours
  Facility Managers: 8 hours
  Trainee Auditors: 12 hours
  Certified Auditors: 16 hours
  Senior Auditors: 20 hours
  Lead/Master Auditors: 24 hours


Acceptable CPD Activities
=========================

Formal Training (1 hour = 1 CPD):
  - Accredited courses
  - Workshops and seminars
  - Conference attendance
  - Webinars (live, interactive)

Self-Study (2 hours = 1 CPD):
  - Reading technical materials
  - Online modules
  - Recorded presentations
  - Research review

Professional Activity (varies):
  - Presenting at conference: 4 CPD
  - Publishing article: 6 CPD
  - Standard development: 8 CPD
  - Mentoring: 1 CPD per 4 hours

Audit Experience:
  - Does not count toward CPD
  - Separate maintenance requirement


CPD Documentation
=================

Required Records:
  - Activity description
  - Date and duration
  - Provider/source
  - Learning outcomes
  - Evidence (certificate, receipt)

Audit:
  - Random CPD audits annually
  - 10% of certified personnel
  - Non-compliance: warning, then suspension

6.4 Accredited Bodies

Certification Body Accreditation
================================

Requirements for Accreditation:
  - Legal entity in good standing
  - Quality management system
  - Competent personnel
  - Impartiality demonstrated
  - Financial stability
  - Complaints handling process
  - Appeals process

Application Process:
  1. Application and documentation
  2. Desktop review (30 days)
  3. Office assessment (1-2 days)
  4. Witness audits (2-4 audits)
  5. Decision and feedback
  6. Accreditation granted (if approved)

Scope of Accreditation:
  - Geographic regions
  - Entity types
  - Species covered
  - Languages supported


Maintaining Accreditation
=========================

Annual Requirements:
  - Surveillance assessment
  - Performance data submission
  - Complaint summary
  - CPD records for auditors
  - Quality indicators

4-Year Reassessment:
  - Full application review
  - Office assessment
  - Witness audits
  - Performance evaluation

6.5 Quality Assurance

Quality Assurance Framework
===========================

AUDITOR QUALITY
---------------
Monitoring:
  - Report quality review (all reports)
  - Witness audits (annual per auditor)
  - Entity feedback collection
  - Complaint analysis

Indicators:
  - Report accuracy
  - Consistency of findings
  - Timeliness
  - Professionalism
  - Entity satisfaction

Action:
  - Feedback provided
  - Additional training if needed
  - Scope restriction possible
  - Certification withdrawal (severe)


AUDIT QUALITY
-------------
Monitoring:
  - Report technical review
  - Decision consistency checks
  - Entity appeal analysis
  - Certification outcome tracking

Indicators:
  - Finding consistency
  - Scoring calibration
  - Decision accuracy
  - Appeal success rate

Action:
  - Calibration sessions
  - Standard interpretation guidance
  - Process improvements
  - Individual corrective action


CERTIFICATION BODY QUALITY
--------------------------
Monitoring:
  - Accreditation surveillance
  - Performance data analysis
  - Stakeholder feedback
  - Peer comparison

Indicators:
  - Certification consistency
  - Complaint handling
  - Timeliness
  - Stakeholder satisfaction

Action:
  - Improvement requirements
  - Enhanced surveillance
  - Scope reduction
  - Accreditation suspension/withdrawal


Calibration Activities
======================

Purpose: Ensure consistency across auditors and bodies

Activities:
  - Annual calibration workshops
  - Case study discussions
  - Video assessment exercises
  - Inter-laboratory comparisons
  - Cross-audit reviews

Frequency:
  - Major calibration: annually
  - Minor calibration: quarterly
  - Ad-hoc: as issues identified
