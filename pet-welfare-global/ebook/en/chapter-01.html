Chapter 1: Certification Framework

"Hongik Ingan: Certification is not merely compliance; it is a commitment to excellence in care."

1.1 Certification Philosophy

Core Principles
===============

ANIMAL-CENTERED
  Every decision prioritizes animal welfare
  Standards based on animal needs, not convenience
  Outcomes measured by animal well-being

EVIDENCE-BASED
  Requirements grounded in scientific research
  Regular updates based on new knowledge
  Measurable, objective criteria

TRANSPARENT
  Public visibility of certification status
  Clear requirements and processes
  Open complaint mechanisms

CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT
  Certification as journey, not destination
  Regular reassessment and development
  Recognition for exceeding standards

GLOBALLY HARMONIZED
  Consistent standards worldwide
  Mutual recognition agreements
  Respect for regional variations

1.3 Certification Tiers

BRONZE - Entry Level
====================
Requirements:
  - Five Freedoms Score: >= 60%
  - No critical non-conformities
  - All legal requirements met
  - Basic documentation in place

Benefits:
  - Listed in certified registry
  - Use of BRONZE certification mark
  - Access to training resources

Validity: 1 year
Review: Annual audit required


SILVER - Standard Level
=======================
Requirements:
  - Five Freedoms Score: >= 75%
  - No critical or major non-conformities
  - Enhanced welfare practices
  - Staff training program

Benefits:
  - All BRONZE benefits
  - Use of SILVER certification mark
  - Priority listing in registry
  - Reduced audit frequency

Validity: 2 years
Review: Surveillance audit at 12 months


GOLD - Advanced Level
=====================
Requirements:
  - Five Freedoms Score: >= 85%
  - Exceeds minimum standards
  - Proactive welfare programs
  - Continuous improvement demonstrated

Benefits:
  - All SILVER benefits
  - Use of GOLD certification mark
  - Featured in registry
  - Eligible for excellence awards

Validity: 3 years
Review: Surveillance audits at 12 and 24 months


PLATINUM - Excellence Level
===========================
Requirements:
  - Five Freedoms Score: >= 95%
  - Industry leadership demonstrated
  - Innovation in welfare practices
  - Community outreach programs

Benefits:
  - All GOLD benefits
  - Use of PLATINUM certification mark
  - Ambassador status
  - Mentor other facilities
  - Speaking opportunities

Validity: 3 years
Review: Light-touch surveillance only

1.4 Application Process

Step 1: Eligibility Check
=========================
  - Confirm entity type is covered
  - Verify legal compliance
  - Review minimum requirements
  - Self-assessment recommended

Step 2: Application Submission
==============================
  Required Documents:
    - Entity registration/license
    - Organizational structure
    - Facility details
    - Species and capacity information
    - Existing certifications
    - Staff qualifications

Step 3: Application Review
==========================
  Timeline: 5-10 business days
  Outcome:
    - Accepted for audit
    - Additional information requested
    - Rejected (with reasons)

Step 4: Audit Scheduling
========================
  - Initial audit date agreed
  - Audit team assigned
  - Pre-audit documentation review
  - Audit plan communicated

Step 5: On-Site Audit
=====================
  Duration: 1-3 days (based on size)
  Coverage:
    - Facility inspection
    - Document review
    - Staff interviews
    - Animal observation
    - Five Freedoms assessment

Step 6: Audit Report
====================
  Timeline: 10 business days after audit
  Contents:
    - Findings and observations
    - Five Freedoms scores
    - Non-conformities identified
    - Recommendation

Step 7: Certification Decision
==============================
  Decision Committee Review
  Outcomes:
    - Certification granted
    - Certification with conditions
    - Certification denied

1.5 Certification Lifecycle

Certification Timeline (GOLD Example)
=====================================

Year 1:
  Month 0: Certification granted
  Month 6: Self-assessment due
  Month 12: Surveillance audit

Year 2:
  Month 18: Self-assessment due
  Month 24: Surveillance audit

Year 3:
  Month 30: Self-assessment due
  Month 33: Recertification application
  Month 36: Recertification audit

Ongoing:
  - Continuous compliance required
  - Significant changes must be reported
  - Complaints may trigger unannounced audits


Maintaining Certification
=========================

DO:
  [OK] Maintain all standards continuously
  [OK] Report significant changes promptly
  [OK] Complete self-assessments on time
  [OK] Cooperate with audits
  [OK] Address non-conformities promptly
  [OK] Keep documentation current

DON'T:
  [!] Allow standards to slip between audits
  [!] Hide problems from auditors
  [!] Ignore non-conformity deadlines
  [!] Make unapproved changes


Suspension and Revocation
=========================

Grounds for Suspension:
  - Critical non-conformity discovered
  - Failure to address major non-conformity
  - Legal violations
  - Verified welfare complaints

Suspension Period:
  - Maximum 6 months
  - Corrective actions required
  - Verification audit before reinstatement

Grounds for Revocation:
  - Failure to resolve suspension
  - Repeated critical violations
  - Fraud in certification process
  - Severe animal welfare breaches

Appeal:
  - 30 days to appeal decisions
  - Independent appeal panel
  - Decision within 60 days
