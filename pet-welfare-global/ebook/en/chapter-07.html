7.1 Global Welfare Landscape

Major Welfare Frameworks
========================

FIVE FREEDOMS (Global)
  Origin: UK Farm Animal Welfare Council, 1979
  Adoption: Worldwide reference standard
  Coverage: All animal types
  Status: Foundation of WIA standard

WORLD ORGANISATION FOR ANIMAL HEALTH (WOAH)
  Formerly: OIE
  Members: 182 countries
  Coverage: Terrestrial and aquatic animals
  Standards: International trade focus

EUROPEAN UNION
  Framework: EU Animal Welfare Strategy
  Coverage: 27 member states
  Standards: Legally binding regulations
  Focus: Farm and companion animals

UNITED STATES
  Federal: Animal Welfare Act (AWA)
  Coverage: Limited (research, dealers, exhibitors)
  State: Varies significantly
  Focus: Minimum standards

WORLD ANIMAL PROTECTION
  Type: NGO
  Coverage: Global advocacy
  Standards: Animal Protection Index
  Focus: Legal and policy reform

7.2 Standards Mapping

WIA to Regional Standards Mapping
=================================

Purpose:
  - Identify equivalences
  - Recognize gaps
  - Enable mutual recognition
  - Facilitate international trade

Mapping Process:
  1. Identify regional standard requirements
  2. Compare to WIA requirements
  3. Assess equivalence level
  4. Document gaps
  5. Define bridging requirements


Equivalence Levels
==================

EQUIVALENT:
  - Regional requirement meets or exceeds WIA
  - No additional requirements needed
  - Direct recognition possible

SUBSTANTIALLY EQUIVALENT:
  - Regional requirement mostly meets WIA
  - Minor gaps identified
  - Bridging requirements defined

PARTIALLY EQUIVALENT:
  - Significant gaps exist
  - Supplementary assessment needed
  - Additional certification required

NOT EQUIVALENT:
  - Fundamental differences
  - Full WIA assessment required
  - No recognition possible

7.3 Mutual Recognition

Mutual Recognition Agreements
=============================

Definition:
  Agreement between WIA and regional body to
  recognize each other's certifications as equivalent

Benefits:
  - Reduced duplication of assessment
  - Lower costs for entities
  - Faster certification process
  - Harmonized welfare standards

Requirements for MRA:
  - Mapped standards with substantial equivalence
  - Comparable audit quality
  - Information sharing agreements
  - Dispute resolution mechanism


MRA Process
===========

Step 1: Exploratory Discussions
  - Initial contact between bodies
  - Interest and feasibility assessment
  - Resource commitment

Step 2: Technical Mapping
  - Detailed standard comparison
  - Gap analysis
  - Bridging requirement identification

Step 3: System Assessment
  - Certification body review
  - Auditor competence assessment
  - Quality system evaluation

Step 4: Pilot Phase
  - Limited mutual recognition
  - Monitoring and evaluation
  - Adjustment as needed

Step 5: Full Agreement
  - Formal MRA signed
  - Public announcement
  - Implementation guidance

Step 6: Ongoing Management
  - Annual review meetings
  - Performance monitoring
  - Agreement updates

7.4 Cross-Border Certification

Cross-Border Certification Process
==================================

Scenario: Entity certified in Country A operates in Country B

WITH MUTUAL RECOGNITION:
========================

Step 1: Verify Source Certification
  - Confirm valid WIA certification
  - Check tier level
  - Verify no suspensions

Step 2: Check MRA Status
  - Confirm MRA exists with target country
  - Identify any bridging requirements

Step 3: Apply for Transfer
  - Submit transfer application
  - Pay administrative fee
  - Provide documentation

Step 4: Issue Transfer Certificate
  - Transfer certificate generated
  - Same tier level recognized
  - Same expiry date

Timeline: 5-10 business days


WITHOUT MUTUAL RECOGNITION:
===========================

Step 1: Identify Target Standard
  - Research local requirements
  - Identify certification body
  - Understand process

Step 2: Gap Assessment
  - Compare current practices to target
  - Identify additional requirements
  - Prepare for supplementary assessment

Step 3: Apply for Certification
  - Full application to local body
  - Provide WIA certification evidence
  - Reduced scope audit possible

Step 4: Supplementary Assessment
  - Focus on gap areas
  - Use existing evidence where possible
  - Complete additional requirements

Step 5: Local Certification
  - Separate certificate issued
  - Local tier level assigned
  - Local renewal cycle

Timeline: 30-90 days


International Transfer Certificate
==================================

Certificate Content:
  - Source certification details
  - Source certification body
  - Transfer destination
  - Equivalence statement
  - Validity period
  - Verification code

Verification:
  - Online verification portal
  - QR code verification
  - Direct inquiry option

7.5 Regional Variations

Permitted Regional Variations
=============================

Core Requirements (Non-Variable):
  - Five Freedoms framework
  - Four certification tiers
  - Audit and compliance process
  - Complaint mechanism
  - Public transparency

Variable Elements:
  - Species-specific standards (local species)
  - Climate-related requirements
  - Language and documentation
  - Local legal integration
  - Regional entity types


Examples of Regional Adaptation
===============================

TEMPERATURE STANDARDS:
  Nordic Region:
    - Higher cold tolerance expected
    - Outdoor access in winter: breed-specific
    - Heating focus in shelters

  Tropical Region:
    - Cooling requirements emphasized
    - Shade and ventilation critical
    - Heat stress protocols

SPECIES COVERAGE:
  East Asia:
    - Specific standards for: Korean Jindo, Shiba Inu
    - Exotic pet standards (more common species)

  Australia:
    - Consideration for native species interaction
    - Specific transport requirements (distance)

LEGAL INTEGRATION:
  EU Member States:
    - Reference to EU regulations
    - National implementation variations
    - Cross-border movement rules

  United States:
    - Federal AWA reference
    - State-specific requirements
    - Municipal ordinance consideration


Requesting Regional Variation
=============================

Process:
  1. Identify need for variation
  2. Submit proposal with justification
  3. Technical committee review
  4. Stakeholder consultation
  5. Approval or rejection
  6. Publication if approved

Criteria for Approval:
  - Does not reduce welfare outcome
  - Scientifically justified
  - Culturally appropriate
  - Practically implementable
  - Does not create trade barrier
