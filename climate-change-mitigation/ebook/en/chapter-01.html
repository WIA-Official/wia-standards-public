1.1 The Climate Crisis

Climate change represents one of the most significant challenges facing humanity. Rising global temperatures, extreme weather events, and ecological disruption demand urgent, coordinated action across all sectors of society.

Key Concept: Climate change mitigation refers to actions that reduce or prevent greenhouse gas emissions, while adaptation refers to adjusting to the effects of climate change that are already occurring or anticipated.

1.2 WIA-CLIMATE-CHANGE-MITIGATION Standard

The WIA-CLIMATE-CHANGE-MITIGATION standard provides a comprehensive framework for measuring, reporting, and reducing greenhouse gas emissions across all sectors.

1.2.1 Standard Scope

  • Measurement: Standardized GHG accounting and reporting
  • Technology: Clean technology integration standards
  • Policy: Carbon pricing and regulatory frameworks
  • Verification: Third-party certification and auditing
  • Transition: Just transition planning and implementation

1.2.2 Four-Phase Architecture

WIA-CLIMATE-CHANGE-MITIGATION Architecture:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Phase 1: Measurement & Reporting                   │
│  • GHG Inventory • Carbon Footprint • Scopes 1-3    │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Phase 2: Reduction Strategies                      │
│  • Energy Efficiency • Renewables • Electrification│
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Phase 3: Technology Integration                    │
│  • Clean Tech • Carbon Capture • Green Hydrogen     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Phase 4: Verification & Certification              │
│  • Third-Party Audits • Carbon Credits • Reporting  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
