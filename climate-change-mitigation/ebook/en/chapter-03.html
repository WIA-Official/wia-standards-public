3.1.1 Phase Overview

Phase Purpose Key Components
Phase 1: Measurement Establish emission baseline GHG inventory, carbon footprint
Phase 2: Reduction Implement reduction strategies Efficiency, renewables, optimization
Phase 3: Technology Deploy clean technologies CCS, hydrogen, electrification
Phase 4: Verification Validate and certify progress Audits, credits, reporting

3.2 Mitigation Assurance Levels (MAL)

WIA-CLIMATE-CHANGE-MITIGATION defines four assurance levels to address different reduction ambitions.

Level Reduction Target Key Requirements
MAL-1 (Basic) 10-20% GHG inventory, reduction plan
MAL-2 (Standard) 30-50% Science-based targets, verified
MAL-3 (Advanced) 70-90% Full scope 3, net-zero pathway
MAL-4 (Net Zero) Carbon neutral All scopes, residual offsets

3.6 Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  • Four-phase architecture covers measurement to verification
  • Four MAL levels address different reduction ambitions
  • GHG accounting covers Scopes 1, 2, and 3 emissions
  • Standard aligns with GHG Protocol, SBTi, and ISO 14064
  • Certification requires third-party verification and annual renewal
