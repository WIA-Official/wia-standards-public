4.1 GHG Inventory Fundamentals

A greenhouse gas inventory is the foundation of any climate mitigation effort. It provides the baseline against which reduction progress is measured.

4.1.1 Greenhouse Gases Covered

Gas Formula GWP (100-year) Primary Sources
Carbon Dioxide CO2 1 Fossil fuel combustion
Methane CH4 28 Agriculture, natural gas
Nitrous Oxide N2O 265 Agriculture, industry
HFCs Various 12-14,800 Refrigeration, AC
PFCs Various 6,500-9,200 Electronics, aluminum
SF6 SF6 23,500 Electrical equipment

4.3 Calculation Methodologies

4.3.1 Basic Formula

// GHG Emission Calculation
Emissions (tCO2e) = Activity Data × Emission Factor × GWP

// Example: Natural Gas
{
  "activityData": {
    "value": 10000,

4.6 Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  • GHG inventories cover seven greenhouse gases
  • Emissions are categorized into Scopes 1, 2, and 3
  • Calculations use activity data, emission factors, and GWP
  • Boundary setting determines which emissions to include
  • Data quality requirements increase with MAL level
