## Overview

### 1.1 Purpose

The WIA Climate Change Mitigation Standard (WIA-ENE-049) defines a comprehensive digital framework for tracking greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, implementing mitigation strategies, and coordinating global climate action. This standard enables countries, organizations, and individuals to align with the Paris Agreement, achieve Nationally Determined Contributions (NDC), and reach carbon neutrality.

**Core Objectives**:
- Track and report GHG emissions across all sectors
- Implement science-based mitigation strategies
- Support Paris Agreement 1.5°C pathway
- Enable NDC compliance and enhancement
- Facilitate carbon neutrality roadmaps
- Coordinate international climate cooperation

### 1.2 Scope

This standard covers:

| Domain | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Emission Tracking | CO2, CH4, N2O, F-gases across all sectors |
| Mitigation Strategies | Renewable energy, efficiency, electrification, nature-based solutions |
| Policy Frameworks | NDCs, Long-term strategies, sectoral policies |
| Carbon Markets | Article 6 mechanisms, carbon pricing, offsets |
| Climate Finance | Green investments, climate funds, technology transfer |
| MRV Systems | Measurement, Reporting, Verification of emissions and reductions |

### 1.3 Target Audience

- National governments implementing NDCs
- Cities and regional authorities
- Corporations setting net-zero targets
- Project developers (renewable energy, efficiency, etc.)
- Carbon market participants
- Climate finance institutions
- International organizations (UNFCCC, IPCC, etc.)

## Terminology

### 2.1 Core Climate Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Climate Change** | Long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns, primarily caused by human activities |
| **Mitigation** | Actions to reduce or prevent emission of greenhouse gases |
| **Greenhouse Gases (GHG)** | Gases that trap heat in the atmosphere (CO2, CH4, N2O, F-gases) |
| **Carbon Dioxide Equivalent (CO2e)** | Universal unit for measuring GHG emissions based on global warming potential |
| **Global Warming Potential (GWP)** | Measure of how much heat a GHG traps over time compared to CO2 |
| **Carbon Neutrality** | Balance between emitting carbon and absorbing carbon (net zero) |
| **Net Zero** | Reducing GHG emissions to as close to zero as possible, with remaining emissions balanced by removal |
| **Carbon Budget** | Maximum amount of CO2 that can be emitted to stay below a temperature target |

### 2.2 Paris Agreement Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Paris Agreement** | 2015 international treaty on climate change (196 Parties) |
| **NDC** | Nationally Determined Contributions - each country's emission reduction pledge |
| **1.5°C Target** | Limiting global warming to 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels |
| **2°C Limit** | Paris Agreement's upper limit for global warming |
| **Article 6** | Paris Agreement mechanisms for international carbon markets |
| **Enhanced Transparency Framework** | Paris Agreement system for reporting and reviewing climate action |
| **Global Stocktake** | 5-year assessment of collective progress toward Paris goals |

### 2.3 Mitigation Sectors

| Sector | Key Strategies |
|--------|----------------|
| **Energy** | Renewable electricity, phase out fossil fuels |
| **Transport** | Electric vehicles, public transit, sustainable aviation fuels |
| **Industry** | Energy efficiency, electrification, green hydrogen, CCUS |
| **Buildings** | Insulation, heat pumps, smart controls, renewable heating |
| **Agriculture** | Sustainable practices, methane reduction, soil carbon |
| **Forestry** | Reforestation, avoided deforestation, sustainable management |
| **Waste** | Circular economy, methane capture, reduce landfill |

## Climate Change Context

### 3.1 The Climate Crisis

**Current State (2025)**:
- Global temperature: +1.2°C above pre-industrial (1850-1900)
- Atmospheric CO2: ~425 ppm (vs. 280 ppm pre-industrial)
- Global emissions: ~40 GtCO2e/year
- Sea level rise: ~20 cm since 1900, accelerating
- Extreme weather events: Increasing frequency and intensity

**Impacts**:
- Heat waves, droughts, wildfires
- Floods, hurricanes, typhoons
- Ecosystem disruption, species extinction
- Food and water security threats
- Human displacement, climate refugees
- Economic damages: Trillions of dollars

### 3.2 The Urgency

| Temperature | Impacts |
|-------------|---------|
| **+1.5°C** | Severe but manageable with adaptation |
| **+2°C** | Significantly worse impacts, many irreversible |
| **+3°C** | Catastrophic for many ecosystems and regions |
| **+4°C+** | Existential threat to human civilization |

**Carbon Budget for 1.5°C** (IPCC AR6):
- Remaining budget (50% chance): ~500 GtCO2 from 2020
- At current emissions: Budget exhausted by ~2032
- **Conclusion**: Immediate, deep emissions cuts required

## Paris Agreement Framework

### 4.1 Core Components

**Article 2 - Goals**:
1. Hold global temperature increase to well below 2°C, pursue 1.5°C
2. Increase ability to adapt to climate impacts
3. Make finance flows consistent with low-GHG pathway

**Article 4 - Mitigation (NDCs)**:
- Each Party prepares, communicates, and maintains NDCs
- NDCs represent progression beyond previous efforts
- Submit new/updated NDC every 5 years
- Long-term low GHG development strategies

**Article 6 - Carbon Markets**:
- 6.2: Cooperative approaches (bilateral trading)
- 6.4: Sustainable Development Mechanism (SDM)
- 6.8: Non-market approaches

**Article 13 - Transparency**:
- Enhanced Transparency Framework (ETF)
- Biennial Transparency Reports (BTRs)
- National inventory reports
- Technical expert review

### 4.2 NDC Requirements

**Elements of an NDC**:
1. **Target**: Quantified emission reduction (e.g., 40% below 2005 by 2030)
2. **Scope**: Sectors and gases covered
3. **Baseline**: Reference year for reductions
4. **Time frame**: Target year(s)
5. **Assumptions**: Methodologies, metrics, GWP values
6. **Fairness**: How target is ambitious and fair
7. **Policies**: Key mitigation measures
8. **Adaptation**: (Optional) adaptation component

**NDC Progression**:
- Each NDC must be more ambitious than previous
- No backsliding allowed
- Regular updates every 5 years (2025, 2030, 2035...)

## IPCC Science Foundation

### 5.1 IPCC Assessment Reports

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) provides the scientific basis for climate action:

**Key Reports**:
- **AR6 (2021-2023)**: Sixth Assessment Report
  - WG1: Physical Science Basis
  - WG2: Impacts, Adaptation, Vulnerability
  - WG3: Mitigation of Climate Change
- **Special Reports**:
  - SR15 (2018): 1.5°C warming
  - SRCCL: Climate Change and Land
  - SROCC: Ocean and Cryosphere

### 5.2 Emission Scenarios (SSPs)

**Shared Socioeconomic Pathways**:

| Scenario | Description | 2100 Warming | Peak Year |
|----------|-------------|--------------|-----------|
| **SSP1-1.9** | Very low emissions, 1.5°C pathway | 1.4°C | Before 2030 |
| **SSP1-2.6** | Low emissions, 2°C pathway | 1.8°C | 2030-2040 |
| **SSP2-4.5** | Intermediate emissions | 2.7°C | 2050-2070 |
| **SSP3-7.0** | High emissions | 3.6°C | No peak |
| **SSP5-8.5** | Very high emissions | 4.4°C | No peak |

**For 1.5°C**:
- Global emissions must peak before 2025
- Reduce by 45% by 2030 (vs. 2010)
- Reach net zero by ~2050
- Likely requires carbon dioxide removal (CDR)

### 5.3 Mitigation Pathways

**Key Strategies for 1.5°C**:

1. **Rapid Coal Phase-Out**: By 2030 in developed countries, 2040 globally
2. **100% Renewable Electricity**: By 2050
3. **Electrify Everything**: Transport, heating, industry where possible
4. **Energy Efficiency**: Double improvement rate
5. **Methane Reduction**: 30% cut by 2030 (Global Methane Pledge)
6. **Nature-Based Solutions**: Reforestation, soil carbon, blue carbon
7. **Carbon Removal**: Needed for residual emissions (BECCS, DACCS, etc.)
8. **Behavioral Change**: Sustainable consumption, reduced waste

## Mitigation Pathways

### 6.1 Sectoral Pathways

**Energy Sector** (40% of global emissions):
- Renewable energy: 70%+ by 2050
- Coal phase-out: 2030-2040
- Oil & gas: Reduced 60-80% by 2050
- Grid modernization: Smart grids, storage
- Access: Universal clean energy access

**Transport** (25% of emissions):
- Electric vehicles: 50%+ of new sales by 2030
- Public transit: Expanded urban networks
- Aviation: Sustainable aviation fuels, efficiency
- Shipping: Hydrogen, ammonia, efficiency
- Active mobility: Walking, cycling infrastructure

**Buildings** (8% of emissions):
- Zero-carbon new buildings: By 2030
- Retrofit existing: Deep energy renovations
- Heat pumps: Replace fossil fuel heating
- Smart systems: Energy management, demand response
- Circular materials: Low-carbon construction

**Industry** (23% of emissions):
- Energy efficiency: 30% improvement
- Electrification: Where technically feasible
- Green hydrogen: Steel, chemicals, cement
- CCUS: Hard-to-abate sectors
- Circular economy: Reduce material demand

**Agriculture & Land** (20% of emissions):
- Sustainable farming: Reduced fertilizer, tillage
- Livestock: Methane reduction, feed additives
- Soil carbon: Regenerative agriculture
- Dietary shifts: Reduced meat consumption
- Food waste: 50% reduction

### 6.2 Cross-Cutting Enablers

**Carbon Pricing**:
- Emissions trading systems (ETS)
- Carbon taxes
- Internal carbon pricing (corporations)
- Price floor: $50-100/tCO2e by 2030

**Climate Finance**:
- $100B/year developed to developing (Paris commitment)
- Green bonds, climate funds
- Divest from fossil fuels
- Invest in clean energy, adaptation

**Technology Transfer**:
- Share clean technologies globally
- Capacity building in developing countries
- Intellectual property cooperation

**Just Transition**:
- Support workers in fossil fuel industries
- Retrain for clean energy jobs
- Ensure equity in transition

## Philosophy

### 7.1 弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity)

Climate change mitigation embodies the principle of 弘益人間:

**Intergenerational Justice**:
- Protect the planet for future generations
- Leave a livable world for our children

**Global Solidarity**:
- Rich countries must act first and fastest
- Support developing countries in transition
- Common but differentiated responsibilities

**Ecosystem Harmony**:
- Protect biodiversity and ecosystems
- Respect planetary boundaries
- Live in balance with nature

**Human Dignity**:
- Climate action that improves health, equity
- Clean air, water, food for all
- Resilient communities

### 7.2 The Path Forward

**We have the solutions**:
- Renewable energy is now cheapest
- Electric vehicles are competitive
- Efficiency technologies are proven
- Nature-based solutions are ready

**What's needed**:
- **Political will**: Bold climate policies
- **Finance**: Redirect trillions to clean solutions
- **Cooperation**: Global collective action
- **Speed**: Deploy solutions at unprecedented scale
- **Justice**: Ensure fair, equitable transition

**The choice is ours**:
- 1.5°C is still achievable (barely)
- Every fraction of a degree matters
- Every ton of CO2 avoided matters
- Every year of delay increases costs and suffering

**弘益人間** - We must act now to benefit all humanity, present and future. Climate change mitigation is not a burden, but an opportunity to build a better, healthier, more equitable world.
