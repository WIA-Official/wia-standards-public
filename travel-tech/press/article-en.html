WIA Announces Travel Tech Standard - Seamless Travel Realized

The World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has announced the WIA-IND-015 Travel Tech standard. This standard provides perfect travel experiences by seamlessly connecting flight bookings, hotel accommodations, multi-modal transportation integration, travel document verification, and automatic payments.

Integrated Travel Platform

Travelers from the Republic of Korea can now plan all their trips from a single platform. WIA-IND-015 integrates global airline APIs, hotel booking systems, and train, bus, and car rental services. It automatically verifies passport and visa requirements, calculates exchange rates in real-time, and handles travel insurance all at once. Accessibility options enable all travelers to move comfortably.

Key Features:
  • Flight Integration: Real-time search and booking of worldwide airlines
  • Hotel Reservations: Standardized accommodation information and booking
  • Multi-Modal Transportation: Integrate trains, buses, rentals into one itinerary
  • Document Verification: Automatic verification of passports, visas, vaccine certificates
  • Accessibility Support: Special assistance and options for people with disabilities

Sustainable Travel

This standard displays the carbon footprint of each travel option to support environmentally conscious choices. It prioritizes public transportation like trains and buses, and integrates carbon offset programs to encourage responsible travel. It also recommends local experiences and accommodations that support local economies.

弘益人間 - Benefit All Humanity

WIA Chairman Yeon Sam-heum stated, "The Travel Tech standard enables everyone to experience the world easily and affordably." He added, "In the spirit of 弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity), this technology will lower barriers to travel, promote cultural exchange, and protect local communities and the environment through sustainable tourism."

"Travel enriches life. The WIA-IND-015 standard helps everyone travel safely and comfortably, meeting diverse cultures and people to experience a broader world."
