Chapter 8: Certification & Compliance

8.1 Overview

Rehabilitation exoskeletons are regulated medical devices requiring certification in most jurisdictions. This chapter outlines the certification requirements, testing procedures, and compliance standards necessary for commercial deployment.

8.2 Medical Device Classification

Region Classification Regulatory Body

European Union Class IIb Medical Device Notified Body (CE marking)
Canada Class III Medical Device Health Canada
Japan Class III Medical Device PMDA

8.3 Safety Standards

8.3.1 Core Safety Standards

Standard Title Scope
IEC 60601-1 Medical electrical equipment - General requirements for safety Basic safety and performance
IEC 60601-1-2 EMC requirements and tests Electromagnetic compatibility
ISO 13849-1 Safety of machinery - Safety-related parts of control systems Functional safety (PLd required)
IEC 62304 Medical device software lifecycle processes Software development (Class B)
ISO 14971 Risk management for medical devices Risk analysis and mitigation

8.3.2 Performance Standards

Key Performance Requirements

  • Joint Accuracy: ±0.5° position accuracy
  • Torque Accuracy: ±2% of rated torque
  • Control Latency: <10ms sensor-to-actuator
  • Emergency Stop: <5ms power cutoff
  • Battery Life: Minimum 2 hours continuous operation
  • MTBF: >2000 hours between failures

8.4 Testing Requirements

8.4.1 Pre-clinical Testing

Test Category Test Items Pass Criteria
Mechanical Testing Durability, fatigue, wear 10,000 gait cycles without failure
Electrical Safety Leakage current, isolation Per IEC 60601-1 limits
EMC Testing Emissions, immunity Per IEC 60601-1-2
Software Validation Unit, integration, system tests 100% requirement coverage
Biocompatibility Skin contact materials ISO 10993 compliance

8.4.2 Clinical Testing

Clinical Trial Requirements

Clinical evidence must demonstrate:

  1. Safety: No serious adverse events attributable to device
  2. Efficacy: Statistically significant improvement in primary outcome measures
  3. Usability: Therapists can operate device with minimal training
  4. Patient Acceptance: >80% satisfaction score

Minimum Sample Size: 30 patients per indication

8.5 Risk Management

8.5.1 Hazard Analysis

Hazard Risk Level (Pre-mitigation) Mitigation Residual Risk
Patient fall High Fall detection, stability control, therapist supervision Low
Joint overextension Medium Soft/hard limits, emergency stop Very Low
Motor overheating Medium Temperature monitoring, automatic derating Low
Software malfunction High Watchdog timer, redundancy, testing Low
Skin irritation Low Biocompatible materials, padding Very Low

8.6 Quality Management System

8.6.1 ISO 13485 Requirements

Quality Management System Components:

1. Management Responsibility
   - Quality policy and objectives
   - Resource allocation
   - Management review

2. Design and Development
   - Design planning and input
   - Verification and validation
   - Design transfer and changes

3. Production and Service
   - Process validation
   - Traceability
   - Calibration and maintenance

4. Measurement and Improvement
   - Internal audits
   - Corrective/preventive actions
   - Post-market surveillance

8.9 Cybersecurity Requirements

8.9.1 FDA Cybersecurity Guidance

Pre-market Requirements

  • SBOM: Software Bill of Materials for all components
  • Vulnerability Management: Process for identifying and patching vulnerabilities
  • Secure Development: Following secure coding practices
  • Threat Modeling: Documented analysis of cybersecurity risks

8.10 Training and Labeling

8.10.1 Operator Training Requirements

User Type Training Duration Competency Assessment
Physical Therapist 8 hours (theory + hands-on) Written exam + practical demonstration
Physician 4 hours (overview + safety) Written exam
Clinical Engineer 16 hours (technical + maintenance) Practical assessment

8.10.2 Device Labeling

Required Labels

  • ✓ Device name and model number
  • ✓ Manufacturer name and address
  • ✓ Serial number and manufacturing date
  • ✓ Medical device symbol
  • ✓ CE marking (EU) or FDA clearance number (US)
  • ✓ Safety warnings and precautions
  • ✓ Electrical safety symbols
  • ✓ Maximum patient weight and height

8.11 Certification Checklist

Pre-submission Checklist

  • ☐ Complete design verification and validation
  • ☐ Risk management file per ISO 14971
  • ☐ Software documentation per IEC 62304
  • ☐ Electrical safety testing per IEC 60601-1
  • ☐ EMC testing per IEC 60601-1-2
  • ☐ Biocompatibility assessment per ISO 10993
  • ☐ Clinical evaluation report
  • ☐ Usability engineering file per IEC 62366
  • ☐ Cybersecurity documentation
  • ☐ Instructions for use and labeling
  • ☐ Quality management system certification (ISO 13485)
