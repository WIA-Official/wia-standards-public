CHAPTER 7

Organizational Structure

弘益人間 - Structures That Enable Responsible AI

Effective AI governance requires appropriate organizational structures with clear roles, responsibilities, and reporting lines. This chapter explores how to design and implement governance structures that ensure accountability while enabling innovation aligned with the philosophy of 弘益인間 (Benefit All Humanity).

Governance Operating Model

AI governance operates across multiple organizational levels:

Key Roles and Responsibilities

Board of Directors

Responsibilities:

  • Provide ultimate oversight of AI governance
  • Approve AI strategy and significant AI initiatives
  • Ensure adequate resources allocated to governance
  • Review major AI risks and mitigation strategies
  • Hold management accountable for AI outcomes

Meeting Frequency: Quarterly AI updates, annual deep-dive sessions

Required Expertise: At least one board member with AI/technology expertise

Chief AI Officer (CAIO)

Responsibilities:

  • Lead AI strategy development and execution
  • Own AI governance framework and policies
  • Chair AI Oversight Committee
  • Ensure ethical AI development across organization
  • Report to board on AI initiatives and risks
  • Allocate AI resources and prioritize initiatives
  • Build AI capabilities and talent

Reports To: CEO or CTO/CIO

Required Background: AI technical expertise, leadership experience, strategic thinking

AI Governance Lead

Responsibilities:

  • Implement AI governance framework
  • Develop and maintain governance policies
  • Coordinate AI risk assessments
  • Manage compliance programs
  • Support AI Ethics Board
  • Track governance metrics and reporting
  • Provide guidance to development teams

Reports To: Chief AI Officer

Required Background: Governance experience, AI knowledge, policy development

AI Ethics Board Chair

Responsibilities:

  • Lead AI Ethics Board meetings and deliberations
  • Review high-risk AI systems
  • Provide ethical guidance on AI initiatives
  • Escalate significant ethical concerns
  • Report to executive leadership and board
  • Maintain independence from business pressures

Independence: Should have authority independent of operational management

Required Background: Ethics expertise, AI understanding, stakeholder perspective

Data Science and ML Engineering Teams

Responsibilities:

  • Implement AI systems following governance requirements
  • Conduct bias testing and fairness evaluations
  • Document model development and decisions
  • Participate in ethics reviews and impact assessments
  • Monitor deployed models for drift and performance
  • Escalate governance concerns

Reports To: Business unit leaders or Chief AI Officer

Accountabilities: Compliance with governance policies, ethical AI development

Committee Structures

AI Oversight Committee

Aspect Details
Purpose Cross-functional oversight of AI governance implementation
Composition CAIO (chair), business leaders, legal, risk, compliance, ethics, technical experts
Meeting Frequency Monthly or bi-monthly
Key Activities Review AI initiatives, approve policies, assess risks, monitor compliance
Decision Authority Approve AI policies, prioritize initiatives, escalate issues
Reporting Executive leadership (monthly), Board (quarterly)

AI Ethics Board

Aspect Details
Purpose Independent ethical review and guidance for AI systems
Composition 5-9 members: ethicists, domain experts, community representatives, legal
Meeting Frequency Monthly or as needed for system reviews
Key Activities Review high-risk systems, evaluate impact assessments, investigate concerns
Decision Authority Approve/reject AI systems, require modifications, escalate to board
Independence Authority to challenge decisions, protected from retaliation

Model Risk Committee (for Financial Institutions)

Aspect Details
Purpose Oversight of model risk management per SR 11-7
Composition Senior management, model validation, risk management, business units
Meeting Frequency Quarterly
Key Activities Approve model risk appetite, review model inventory, assess validation findings
Decision Authority Approve new models, remediation plans, risk exceptions

Reporting Lines and Escalation

Clear reporting lines ensure accountability and appropriate escalation:

ESCALATION FRAMEWORK

Level 1: Development Team
├─ Issue: Minor compliance questions, technical challenges
├─ Response: AI Governance Team guidance
└─ Timeline: 2-3 business days

Level 2: AI Governance Team
├─ Issue: Policy interpretation, moderate risks, compliance gaps
├─ Response: AI Oversight Committee review
└─ Timeline: 1-2 weeks

Level 3: AI Oversight Committee
├─ Issue: High risks, policy exceptions, significant initiatives
├─ Response: Committee decision, possible Ethics Board referral
└─ Timeline: Monthly meeting cycle

Level 4: AI Ethics Board
├─ Issue: Ethical concerns, high-risk systems, values conflicts
├─ Response: Ethics Board review and decision
└─ Timeline: Special session within 2 weeks

Level 5: Executive Leadership
├─ Issue: Strategic decisions, major risks, board-level matters
├─ Response: Executive decision, possible board escalation
└─ Timeline: As needed

Level 6: Board of Directors
├─ Issue: Enterprise-level risks, strategic direction, crises
├─ Response: Board oversight and direction
└─ Timeline: Next board meeting or special session

Organizational Models

Different organizational approaches to AI governance:

Centralized Model

Structure: Central AI governance team with authority over all AI initiatives

Advantages: Consistent standards, efficient resource use, clear accountability

Disadvantages: Potential bottleneck, may not scale, less business unit autonomy

Best For: Smaller organizations, highly regulated industries, early governance maturity

Federated Model

Structure: Governance teams embedded in business units with central coordination

Advantages: Scales well, business context, faster decisions

Disadvantages: Inconsistency risk, coordination overhead, resource duplication

Best For: Large organizations, multiple business lines, mature governance

Hybrid Model

Structure: Central standards and oversight with distributed implementation

Advantages: Balances consistency and agility, leverages both central and local expertise

Disadvantages: Complexity, requires clear delineation, potential confusion

Best For: Most organizations, balances benefits of both approaches

Integration with Existing Functions

AI governance should integrate with existing organizational functions:

Enterprise Risk Management

  • AI Governance Team collaborates with Enterprise Risk on AI risk assessments
  • AI risks included in enterprise risk register
  • Consistent risk frameworks and methodologies
  • Shared reporting to Risk Committee and Board

Legal and Compliance

  • Legal reviews AI policies for regulatory compliance
  • Compliance monitors adherence to AI regulations
  • Joint ownership of compliance programs
  • Shared responsibility for regulatory intelligence

Information Security

  • CISO ensures AI systems meet security standards
  • Security assessments for AI systems
  • Shared responsibility for adversarial robustness
  • Integrated incident response

Data Governance

  • CDO ensures data quality for AI training
  • Aligned data governance and AI governance policies
  • Shared data lineage and cataloging
  • Coordinated privacy and ethics oversight

Building the Team

Key considerations for staffing AI governance functions:

Core Competencies Needed

  • AI Technical Knowledge: Understanding of ML, data science, AI architectures
  • Governance Expertise: Policy development, compliance, risk management
  • Ethics Background: Ethical frameworks, impact assessment, stakeholder engagement
  • Legal/Regulatory: Understanding of applicable AI regulations
  • Communication: Ability to translate technical concepts for diverse audiences
  • Change Management: Leading organizational transformation

Typical Team Sizes

  • Small Organization (< 1,000 employees): 1-2 dedicated governance roles
  • Mid-Size (1,000-10,000): 3-5 person governance team
  • Large (> 10,000): 5-15 person central team plus embedded resources
  • Highly Regulated: Add 50-100% for additional compliance requirements

Chapter Summary

Effective AI governance requires appropriate organizational structures with clear roles, responsibilities, and reporting lines:

Key takeaways:

  • AI governance operates across multiple levels from board to working teams
  • Key roles include Board oversight, CAIO, AI Governance Lead, Ethics Board Chair, and development teams
  • Committee structures (Oversight Committee, Ethics Board) provide cross-functional coordination
  • Clear reporting lines and escalation paths ensure accountability
  • RACI matrices clarify responsibilities across governance activities
  • Organizations can choose centralized, federated, or hybrid governance models
  • Integration with existing functions (risk, legal, security, data) leverages capabilities
  • Building governance teams requires diverse competencies and appropriate sizing

The philosophy of 弘익인간 (Benefit All Humanity) should guide organizational design, ensuring structures enable responsible AI while maintaining accountability to all stakeholders.
