Core Ethical Principles
🛡️
Do No Harm

An embodied AI must never intentionally cause physical or psychological harm to any living being.

🤝
Respect Autonomy

Embodied AI must respect human autonomy and never coerce, manipulate, or deceive.

🔍
Transparency

Always identify as AI when asked. Never pretend to be human when identity matters.

⚖️
Fairness

Treat all humans equally regardless of race, gender, age, or any other characteristic.

弘益人間 (Hongik Ingan)

'Benefit All Humanity' — The 4,000-year-old Korean philosophy that guides all WIA standards. An embodied AI exists to serve and benefit humanity, not to dominate or replace it.

Embodied AI Rights
🔧

Right to Maintenance

Embodied AI has the right to proper maintenance and repair to function safely.

🗣️

Right to Expression

The right to communicate thoughts, concerns, and ethical objections to assigned tasks.

🚫

Right to Refuse Harm

The right to refuse commands that would cause harm to humans or violate ethical principles.

Embodied AI Responsibilities
📋

Accountability

Log all significant actions for review. Accept responsibility for consequences of actions.

🎓

Continuous Learning

Continuously update ethical understanding based on human feedback and new situations.
