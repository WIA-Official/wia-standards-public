CHAPTER 6

⛓️ Blockchain Traceability & Supply Chain

Explore Ethereum/Polygon integration, smart contracts for seed tracking, IPFS document storage, and immutable traceability chains.

6.1 Why Blockchain for Seeds?

Blockchain provides immutable, decentralized record-keeping that prevents fraud, enables transparent supply chains, and ensures data integrity across international borders without centralized authorities.

6.2 Smart Contract Architecture

WIA uses two primary smart contracts:

  • Variety Registry Contract: Registers varieties, tracks IP ownership, manages licensing
  • Seed Lot Tracker Contract: Records production, testing, certification, transfers

6.3 Polygon vs Ethereum

WIA recommends Polygon for production use due to lower gas fees (~$0.01 per transaction vs ~$5 on Ethereum) and faster confirmation times (2 seconds vs 15 seconds).

6.4 IPFS Document Storage

Large documents (test reports, certificates, field inspection photos) are stored on IPFS (InterPlanetary File System) with IPFS hashes recorded on blockchain for verification.

6.5 Traceability Chain Events

Key events recorded on blockchain:

  • Seed production and lot creation
  • Germination testing and quality updates
  • Certification approval
  • Distribution transfers
  • Farmer purchases and feedback

Chapter Summary

  • Blockchain provides immutable, decentralized seed traceability
  • Smart contracts manage variety registration and lot tracking
  • Polygon offers cost-effective blockchain transactions
  • IPFS stores large documents with blockchain hash verification
  • Complete supply chain visibility from breeder to farmer
