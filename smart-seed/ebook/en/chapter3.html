CHAPTER 3

🧪 Germination Testing & Quality Assurance

Explore ISTA-compliant germination testing protocols, seed vigor assessment, purity analysis, and automated quality data submission to certification agencies.

3.1 ISTA Testing Standards

The International Seed Testing Association (ISTA) provides globally recognized germination testing protocols. WIA-AGRI-011 ensures full compatibility with ISTA Rules for Seed Testing.

Standard Germination Test Parameters

Crop Temperature Substrate Duration
Rice 25°C constant Top of Paper (TP) 7-14 days
Wheat 20°C constant Between Paper (BP) 7 days
Corn 25°C constant Rolled Paper (RP) 7 days
Tomato 20-30°C alternating Top of Paper (TP) 14 days

3.2 Seed Vigor Index

Beyond germination percentage, seed vigor measures the speed and uniformity of germination. The WIA system calculates vigor index automatically:

Vigor Index Formula: VI = (Germination %) × (Mean Germination Time)
Example: 95% germination in 5 days = VI of 95

3.3 Purity Analysis

Seed purity testing identifies the percentage of pure seeds versus other crop seeds, weed seeds, and inert matter. Certified seeds typically require ≥98% purity.

3.4 Automated Test Submission

Laboratories can submit test results directly to the WIA platform via API, which automatically:

  • Validates data against ISTA standards
  • Updates seed lot quality metrics
  • Triggers certification workflows
  • Records results on blockchain for immutability

3.5 Quality Prediction Algorithms

The WIA system uses machine learning to predict seed germination degradation based on storage conditions (temperature, humidity, duration), helping producers optimize storage and determine shelf life.
