Chapter 07: Phase 4 — Integration with Seed Certification

7.1 Phase 4 in one paragraph

Phase 4 specifies how WIA Smart Seed integrates with existing seed-system data: UPOV plant variety protection, ISTA International Rules for Seed Testing, AOSA test methods, OECD seed schemes, FAO AgroVoc, EU plant passport, and the WIA family.

7.2 UPOV bridge

UPOV (International Union for the Protection of New Varieties of Plants) maintains plant breeder rights registries. The bridge maps WIA variety_registration envelopes to UPOV PVR application data, enabling breeders to submit through a single pipe.

7.3 ISTA bridge

ISTA publishes International Rules for Seed Testing. The bridge maps WIA germination_test envelopes to ISTA Orange Certificate (international seed lot certificate) data, enabling cross-border seed trade compliance through one pipe.

7.4 AOSA bridge

AOSA (Association of Official Seed Analysts) publishes US seed test methods. The bridge supports parallel mapping for US-domestic seed certification.

7.5 OECD seed scheme bridge

OECD operates international seed schemes for cereals, maize, vegetables, and other crops. The bridge maps WIA seed_lot certification_class to OECD scheme certification stages.

7.6 FAO AgroVoc

The standard\x27s species field uses FAO AgroVoc concept URIs for cross-language species identification.

7.7 EU plant passport bridge

EU Regulation 2016/2031 requires plant passports for many traded plants and seeds. The bridge maps WIA digital_passport envelopes to EU plant passport requirements.

7.8 WIA family integration

WIA-OMNI-API holds breeder credentials. WIA Smart Breeding composes — variety registration informs breeding program evaluation; harvest performance feeds back to breeder. WIA Smart Farm composes — farm-side seed deployment closes the loop.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in seed infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, ISTA accreditation is the gating credential for international seed trade; the standard\x27s lab_id field MUST reference an active ISTA accreditation that downstream consumers can verify via WIA-OMNI-API. Second, passport revocation must propagate quickly when contamination or fraud is detected; the standard\x27s POST /ss/passport/{id}/revoke endpoint is the primary mechanism. Third, farmer-saved seed compatibility varies by jurisdiction; implementations MUST NOT make jurisdiction-specific judgements but MUST preserve the breeder PVR number so downstream consumers can apply local rules.

Korea-Specific Application Note

Korea\x27s seed system is coordinated by the Rural Development Administration (농촌진흥청) and the Korea Seed and Variety Service (국립종자원). Korean rice varieties (e.g., 일품벼, 추청벼, 호품벼) are registered through the national variety listing process. The standard\x27s Phase 4 UPOV bridge maps cleanly onto KSVS PVR records, and Korean ISTA-accredited laboratories (국립종자원 시험분석부 등) can submit germination test data directly. The standard\x27s farmer-sovereignty primitive aligns with Korean farmer expectations of independent verification of purchased seed quality.

Independent Verification Pattern

The standard's defining choice — public-readable digital passport endpoints — implements the farmer-sovereignty primitive at the API layer. A farmer scanning a QR code at the point of purchase resolves to a publicly-readable URL that returns the digital_passport envelope including signature, lab credentials, certification class, and traceability chain. The farmer can verify the signature against the certification body's published key (also public). No vendor authentication required. This pattern lifts cleanly to vendor-locked alternative systems: when a vendor offers a digital passport that requires their app to verify, the farmer should refuse to buy.
