Chapter 02: Seed Quality: Germination, Purity, Vigor

2.1 Germination rate

The dominant seed quality metric. Defined as the percentage of seeds that germinate under controlled conditions per ISTA Rules. Required minimum varies by crop and certification class: certified cereals typically require 85%+, vegetable seeds 70-85%. The standard\x27s germination_test envelope preserves the protocol version and laboratory credentials so cross-jurisdiction comparisons are valid.

2.2 Purity

Physical purity (seeds of the species vs other crop seeds vs weed seeds vs inert matter) and varietal purity (genetic identity to the registered variety). ISTA Rules define standardised purity test methods. The standard\x27s seed_lot envelope carries purity_pct and an optional varietal_purity_pct from molecular markers if available.

2.3 Vigor index

Beyond simple germination, vigor index measures seedling growth rate and uniformity. AOSA publishes vigor test methods (cold test, accelerated aging, conductivity). The standard\x27s germination_test envelope optionally carries vigor_index and the protocol used.

2.4 Moisture content

Critical for seed storage. Above 12-14% (crop dependent) seeds spoil rapidly; below 4-5% seeds suffer storage damage. The standard\x27s seed_lot envelope carries moisture_pct and the measurement method (oven drying ISTA Method 9.1.4 vs near-infrared spectroscopy).

2.5 Certification classes

OECD seed schemes define a certification class hierarchy: pre-basic seed → basic seed → certified seed (1st generation, 2nd generation, etc.). Each class has progressively higher tolerance for off-types and lower required varietal purity. The standard\x27s certification_class field uses OECD-aligned tokens.

2.6 Seed treatment

Pesticide and fungicide seed treatments are increasingly tracked due to environmental concerns (neonicotinoid restrictions in EU). The standard\x27s seed_lot envelope optionally carries a treatments array with treatment_id, active_ingredient, dose, and regulatory authorisation reference.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in seed infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, ISTA accreditation is the gating credential for international seed trade; the standard\x27s lab_id field MUST reference an active ISTA accreditation that downstream consumers can verify via WIA-OMNI-API. Second, passport revocation must propagate quickly when contamination or fraud is detected; the standard\x27s POST /ss/passport/{id}/revoke endpoint is the primary mechanism. Third, farmer-saved seed compatibility varies by jurisdiction; implementations MUST NOT make jurisdiction-specific judgements but MUST preserve the breeder PVR number so downstream consumers can apply local rules.

Korea-Specific Application Note

Korea\x27s seed system is coordinated by the Rural Development Administration (농촌진흥청) and the Korea Seed and Variety Service (국립종자원). Korean rice varieties (e.g., 일품벼, 추청벼, 호품벼) are registered through the national variety listing process. The standard\x27s Phase 4 UPOV bridge maps cleanly onto KSVS PVR records, and Korean ISTA-accredited laboratories (국립종자원 시험분석부 등) can submit germination test data directly. The standard\x27s farmer-sovereignty primitive aligns with Korean farmer expectations of independent verification of purchased seed quality.

Independent Verification Pattern

The standard's defining choice — public-readable digital passport endpoints — implements the farmer-sovereignty primitive at the API layer. A farmer scanning a QR code at the point of purchase resolves to a publicly-readable URL that returns the digital_passport envelope including signature, lab credentials, certification class, and traceability chain. The farmer can verify the signature against the certification body's published key (also public). No vendor authentication required. This pattern lifts cleanly to vendor-locked alternative systems: when a vendor offers a digital passport that requires their app to verify, the farmer should refuse to buy.
