CHAPTER 5

📱 Digital Seed Passports & Verifiable Credentials

Learn about W3C Verifiable Credentials for seeds, QR code verification, and mobile passport validation by farmers.

5.1 What is a Digital Seed Passport?

A digital seed passport is a tamper-proof digital document containing a seed lot's complete history: variety information, production details, test results, certifications, and traceability chain. It uses W3C Verifiable Credential standards for cryptographic proof.

5.2 QR Code Scanning

Every seed bag includes a QR code that farmers can scan with smartphones to instantly verify:

  • Germination rate and test date
  • Certification status and issuing agency
  • Variety authenticity and breeder information
  • Complete supply chain from production to retail

5.3 Verifiable Credential Structure

Seed passports follow W3C VC Data Model 2.0, including:

  • Issuer: Certification agency (DID-based identity)
  • Subject: Seed lot with quality metrics
  • Proof: Cryptographic signature for verification

5.4 Offline Verification

Farmers in remote areas can verify seed passports offline using pre-loaded certificate chains, ensuring authenticity even without internet connectivity.

Chapter Summary

  • Digital seed passports contain complete seed lifecycle history
  • QR codes enable instant smartphone verification by farmers
  • W3C Verifiable Credentials provide cryptographic proof
  • Offline verification works without internet connectivity
