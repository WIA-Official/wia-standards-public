Chapter 7: Clinical Fitting and EMG Training Protocols

7.1 Pre-Fitting Assessment

Before myoelectric fitting, a prosthetic prosthetist performs a systematic sEMG site assessment to identify the best electrode locations on the residual limb. WIA-MYO defines a standardised pre-fitting assessment protocol:

  1. Skin preparation: Light abrasion with fine grit pad + isopropyl alcohol wipe. Target impedance < 10 kΩ.
  2. Manual muscle testing (MMT): Identify muscles with ≥ Grade 3 strength (against gravity). Minimum viable signal for pattern recognition: peak RMS ≥ 30 μV at 50% MVC.
  3. Signal mapping: Grid of 16 electrode positions over residual limb; record peak RMS for each target gesture at each position. Select 8 positions with highest signal-to-noise ratio and muscle independence.
  4. Crosstalk assessment: Correlation coefficient between channel pairs during single-muscle contractions. Reject positions with |r| > 0.6 indicating excessive crosstalk.

7.2 Classifier Training Session Protocol

Pattern recognition classifier training follows a standardised WIA protocol aligned with established clinical fitting guidelines (e.g., Coapt COMPLETE CONTROL workflow): 

training_protocol:
  gestures: ["rest", "open_hand", "close_hand", "pronate",
             "supinate", "wrist_flex", "wrist_ext", "tripod_pinch"]
  repetitions_per_class: 25          # clinical standard: 20–30
  contraction_duration_s: 3.0
  rest_duration_s: 2.0
  contraction_level: 30-50% MVC      # moderate effort, not maximal
  arm_position: neutral_hanging       # reduce posture-dependent variation
  instructions: "Attempt the gesture with your phantom limb
                 OR contract the muscles you would use for that motion"

7.4 Electrode Rejection / Poor SNR Recovery

When one or more electrode channels produce poor SNR (below 20 dB), the WIA-MYO system can:

  • Automatic rejection: Exclude the channel from the feature vector; retrain LDA on remaining channels.
  • Gain adjustment: Increase amplifier gain on low-signal channels (only if saturation not occurring).
  • Re-placement guidance: Dashboard flags channels with impedance > 50 kΩ or SNR < 20 dB and guides prosthetist to alternative SENIAM sites.

7.5 Phantom Limb Pain and sEMG Biofeedback

An important therapeutic application of myoelectric training is biofeedback for phantom limb pain (PLP) management. Mirror visual feedback (Ramachandran 1996) combined with sEMG-driven prosthetic visual feedback has shown efficacy in reducing PLP intensity (Ramachandran & Rogers-Ramachandran 2011). WIA-MYO supports a biofeedback mode where prosthetic motion is displayed in real-time on a screen without requiring actual hardware: 

# WIA-MYO biofeedback mode (prosthetic visualisation without hardware)
ros2 run wia_myo_biofeedback biofeedback_node \
  --ros-args \
  -p emg_topic:=/emg/classification \
  -p display_mode:=mirror_visual \
  -p hand_model:=right_hand_3d \
  -p pain_scale_interval_min:=5

7.7 Home Training App Integration

WIA-MYO Phase 4 integrates with patient-facing home training apps that guide daily practice exercises to maintain classifier accuracy and prevent rejection rate increase (phenomenon of "classifier drift" due to physiological changes, electrode placement variation):

MetricHome Training Alert Threshold
Classifier accuracy (daily 2-min test)< 85% triggers alert to prosthetist
Rejection rate> 20% triggers guided re-calibration
Channel SNR< 20 dB for any channel → electrode check reminder
Daily training adherence< 5/7 days in past week → motivational nudge
