8.1 Regulatory Landscape

Myoelectric prosthetic control systems are classified as medical devices and must comply with regional regulatory requirements before clinical deployment.

Region Regulatory Body Classification Approval Process
United States FDA Class II (510(k)) Premarket Notification, 3-6 months
European Union Notified Bodies Class IIa (MDR) CE Marking, 6-12 months
Canada Health Canada Class II Medical Device License, 4-8 months
Japan PMDA Class II SHONIN approval, 6-12 months
Australia TGA Class IIa TGA Registration, 3-6 months

8.2 FDA Requirements (United States)

8.2.1 510(k) Submission

Required Documentation

  • Device Description: Intended use, technological characteristics, materials
  • Substantial Equivalence: Comparison to legally marketed predicate device
  • Performance Testing: Bench testing, biocompatibility, electrical safety
  • Clinical Evaluation: Literature review or clinical study data
  • Labeling: Instructions for use, warnings, patient information
  • Cybersecurity: Threat model, SBOM, vulnerability management plan

8.2.2 Performance Testing Standards

Test Category Standard Requirements
Electrical Safety IEC 60601-1 Isolation, leakage current, grounding
EMC (Electromagnetic Compatibility) IEC 60601-1-2 Immunity to interference, emissions limits
Biocompatibility ISO 10993 Cytotoxicity, sensitization, irritation
Software Validation IEC 62304 Software lifecycle, risk management
Usability Engineering IEC 62366-1 Use-related risk analysis, validation testing

8.2.3 Cybersecurity Premarket Guidance

FDA requires cybersecurity documentation for networked medical devices:

  • SBOM (Software Bill of Materials): Complete inventory of software components
  • Threat Modeling: STRIDE analysis of potential attack vectors
  • Security Architecture: Authentication, encryption, access controls
  • Vulnerability Management: Plan for post-market security updates
  • Penetration Testing: Third-party security assessment results

8.3 EU MDR Requirements (European Union)

8.3.1 CE Marking Process

  1. Classification: Determine device class (I, IIa, IIb, III)
  2. Quality Management System: Implement ISO 13485
  3. Technical Documentation: Compile complete technical file
  4. Clinical Evaluation: Clinical evidence per MDR Annex XIV
  5. Notified Body Assessment: Third-party conformity assessment
  6. Declaration of Conformity: Manufacturer's declaration
  7. CE Marking: Affix CE mark and register in EUDAMED

8.3.2 Essential Requirements

Category Requirements
General Safety & Performance Risk management, design verification, validation
Design & Manufacturing Materials, biocompatibility, mechanical properties
Information Provided Instructions for use, warnings, training materials
Clinical Evidence Clinical investigation or literature equivalence
Post-Market Surveillance Vigilance reporting, periodic safety updates

8.4 Risk Management

8.4.1 ISO 14971 Compliance

Medical device risk management following ISO 14971 standard:

Risk Management Process:

1. Risk Analysis
   - Identify intended use and hazards
   - Estimate risks (severity × probability)
   - Evaluate risk acceptability

2. Risk Control
   - Implement risk control measures
   - Verify effectiveness
   - Assess residual risk

3. Risk Evaluation
   - Overall residual risk evaluation
   - Risk/benefit analysis
   - Risk acceptability decision

4. Production & Post-Market
   - Manufacturing risk controls
   - Information for safety
   - Post-market surveillance

8.4.2 Example Hazard Analysis

Hazard Severity Probability Risk Mitigation
Incorrect gesture recognition Moderate Medium Medium Require >90% accuracy, confidence thresholds
EMG signal loss during use Moderate Low Low Fallback to safe position, user alert
Skin irritation from electrodes Minor Medium Low Biocompatible materials, usage guidelines
Patient data breach Serious Low Medium Encryption, access controls, audit logs
Prosthetic malfunction injury Serious Very Low Low Force limits, emergency stop, testing

8.5 Clinical Evidence Requirements

8.5.1 Clinical Study Design

Typical Study Parameters

  • Study Type: Prospective, multi-center, controlled
  • Sample Size: 30-50 subjects for 510(k), 50-100 for CE marking
  • Duration: Minimum 3-6 months follow-up
  • Primary Endpoint: Functional performance (e.g., SHAP, ACMC)
  • Secondary Endpoints: User satisfaction, adverse events, device reliability

8.5.2 Clinical Performance Metrics

Metric Assessment Tool Acceptance Criteria
Functional Performance SHAP (Southampton Hand Assessment Procedure) ≥ 80% completion rate
Control Accuracy Gesture recognition success rate ≥ 90% accuracy
User Satisfaction OPUS (Orthotics Prosthetics Users Survey) ≥ 70% satisfaction
Daily Usage Data logging (hours/day) ≥ 8 hours/day average
Safety Adverse event rate < 5% serious AEs

8.6 Quality Management System

8.6.1 ISO 13485 Requirements

Comprehensive quality management system for medical devices:

  • Management Responsibility: Quality policy, planning, management review
  • Resource Management: Personnel competence, infrastructure, work environment
  • Product Realization: Design controls, purchasing, production, validation
  • Measurement & Analysis: Monitoring, measurement, internal audits
  • Improvement: Corrective/preventive actions, continuous improvement

8.7 Post-Market Requirements

8.7.1 Vigilance Reporting

Event Type Definition Reporting Timeline
Death Device caused or contributed to death FDA: 30 days (MDR)
EU: 2 days (FSCA)
Serious Injury Life-threatening injury or permanent impairment FDA: 30 days (MDR)
EU: 10 days (FSCA)
Malfunction Would likely cause death/serious injury if recurred FDA: 30 days (MDR)
EU: Case-by-case
Field Safety Corrective Action Product recall or safety notification EU: Prior to implementation

8.7.2 Post-Market Surveillance Plan

  • Complaint Handling: System for receiving, documenting, investigating complaints
  • Trend Analysis: Monitor complaint patterns for emerging issues
  • Literature Review: Ongoing review of scientific publications
  • Field Performance Data: Collect usage data from deployed devices
  • Periodic Safety Updates: Annual/periodic summaries to regulators

8.8 WIA Myoelectric Certification

8.8.1 Conformance Levels

Level Requirements Capabilities
Level 1: Basic - JSON data format support
- 2+ EMG channels
- 1000 Hz sample rate
- Basic preprocessing 		Direct control (open/close)
Level 2: Standard - JSON + binary formats
- 4-8 channels
- 1000-2000 Hz sample rate
- Full preprocessing pipeline
- Pattern recognition 		Multi-gesture recognition, proportional control
Level 3: Advanced - All formats + streaming
- Real-time quality monitoring
- Adaptive learning
- Security compliance 		Simultaneous DOF control, research-grade data quality

8.8.2 Certification Process

  1. Self-Assessment: Review conformance checklist
  2. Testing: Functional testing against WIA test suite
  3. Documentation: Submit technical documentation
  4. Review: WIA technical committee evaluation
  5. Certification: Issue WIA compliance certificate
  6. Listing: Add to WIA certified devices registry
  7. Maintenance: Annual renewal and update requirements
