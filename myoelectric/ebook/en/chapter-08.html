Chapter 8: Regulatory and Standards Compliance

8.1 ISO 22523:2006 — External Limb Prostheses

ISO 22523:2006 "External limb prostheses and external orthoses — Requirements and test methods" is the primary international standard covering myoelectric prosthetics. It defines:

  • Classification codes (P0–P3, E1–E3, K1–K5) by amputation level — used as the mandatory iso_22523_class field in WIA-MYO session metadata.
  • Static strength: prosthetic hand must withstand 500 N static load in power grip without permanent deformation.
  • Dynamic fatigue: 100,000 open/close cycles at rated grip force without mechanism failure.
  • Biocompatibility: socket materials must comply with ISO 10993-1 (biocompatibility evaluation).
  • Grip force measurement: must accurately reproduce commanded force within ±15% of commanded value at 10–90% rated maximum grip force.

8.2 IEC 60601-1 — Medical Electrical Equipment

Myoelectric prosthetic systems with integrated sEMG amplifiers that are intended for use in clinical settings must comply with IEC 60601-1:2005+AMD2:2020 (Medical electrical equipment — Part 1: General requirements for basic safety and essential performance).

RequirementSpecification
Applied part classificationType BF (Body Floating) for skin surface electrodes
Patient leakage current≤ 10 μA (normal condition), ≤ 50 μA (single fault)
EMC immunityIEC 60601-1-2 — must function in hospital EM environment (2 V/m @ 26–1000 MHz)
Essential performancesEMG signal acquisition within specification limits during EMC testing

IEC 60601-2-77 (Active implantable medical devices) applies only if the prosthetic system interfaces with implanted electrodes (IMES — Implantable Myoelectric Sensors); WIA-MYO currently covers surface electrode systems only.

8.4 EU MDR 2017/745

Under EU MDR, myoelectric prosthetics are classified:

  • Upper-limb myoelectric prosthesis: Class IIa (Rule 9: active therapeutic device; surgically non-invasive).
  • Implanted electrode-based system: Class III (Rule 7: implant).
  • sEMG electrode module: Class IIa (Rule 10: active device for diagnosis).

CE marking requires Notified Body (NB) conformity assessment (DEKRA, TÜV SÜD, BSI), Technical Documentation per Annex II/III, and post-market clinical follow-up (PMCF) plan. ISO 14971 risk management and IEC 62304 (software lifecycle) are mandatory harmonised standards.

8.7 WIA Standards Registry

Manufacturers implementing WIA-MYO submit their device profiles to the WIA Myoelectric Device Registry at https://standards.wia.global/myoelectric/registry. The registry assigns a wia_device_id (format: WIA-MYO-{manufacturer}-{model}-{year}) used as the canonical device identifier in FHIR Device resources and WIA REST API calls.
