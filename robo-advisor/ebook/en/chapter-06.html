Chapter 6: Regulatory Compliance and Security

6.1 U.S. Regulatory Framework

6.1.1 SEC Registration

Robo-advisors providing investment advice must register as Registered Investment Advisors (RIAs) under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940.

Form ADV Requirements

  • Part 1: Basic information, ownership, disciplinary history
  • Part 2A: Brochure with services, fees, conflicts of interest
  • Part 2B: Brochure supplement with advisor backgrounds
  • Annual Update: File within 90 days of fiscal year end

6.1.2 FINRA Oversight

Broker-dealer robo-advisors fall under FINRA jurisdiction:

  • Suitability requirements (FINRA Rule 2111)
  • Know Your Customer obligations (Rule 2090)
  • Best execution requirements (Rule 5310)
  • Recordkeeping (Rule 4511)
  • Supervision systems (Rule 3110)

6.1.3 SEC Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI)

Broker-dealers must act in the best interest of retail customers:

  • Disclosure Obligation: Material facts about relationship and conflicts
  • Care Obligation: Reasonable diligence and prudent judgment
  • Conflict of Interest Obligation: Establish and maintain policies
  • Compliance Obligation: Written policies and procedures

6.2 Fiduciary Duty

RIAs owe fiduciary duty to clients, requiring:

Duty of Care

  • Provide suitable investment advice
  • Conduct reasonable investigation
  • Seek best execution
  • Monitor portfolios continuously

Duty of Loyalty

  • Eliminate or disclose conflicts of interest
  • Obtain informed client consent for conflicts
  • Prioritize client interests over firm profits
  • Avoid self-dealing

6.3 KYC/AML Compliance

6.3.1 Customer Identification Program (CIP)

Required under USA PATRIOT Act:

  • Collect identifying information (name, DOB, address, SSN)
  • Verify identity using documentary or non-documentary methods
  • Maintain records for 5 years after account closure
  • Check against OFAC and terrorist watchlists

6.3.2 Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

  • Identify and verify beneficial owners
  • Understand nature and purpose of customer relationships
  • Conduct ongoing monitoring for suspicious activity
  • Update customer information periodically

6.3.3 Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

File SARs for transactions involving $5,000+ and:

  • Suspected money laundering
  • Terrorist financing
  • Structuring to evade reporting
  • Fraud or insider trading

6.4 Privacy and Data Protection

6.4.1 Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)

  • Financial Privacy Rule: Annual privacy notices to customers
  • Safeguards Rule: Protect customer information
  • Pretexting Protection: Prevent fraudulent information access

6.4.2 GDPR Compliance (EU Clients)

  • Explicit consent for data processing
  • Right to access and portability
  • Right to erasure (right to be forgotten)
  • Data protection impact assessments
  • Mandatory breach notifications within 72 hours

6.4.3 CCPA Compliance (California)

  • Disclosure of data collection practices
  • Right to opt-out of data sales
  • Right to deletion of personal information
  • Non-discrimination for exercising rights

6.5 Cybersecurity Requirements

6.5.1 SEC Cybersecurity Guidelines

RIAs must implement robust cybersecurity programs:

  • Risk assessments and governance
  • Access controls and authentication
  • Data encryption and loss prevention
  • Incident response plans
  • Vendor management
  • Employee training

6.5.2 NIST Cybersecurity Framework

  • Identify: Asset inventory and risk assessment
  • Protect: Access controls, encryption, secure development
  • Detect: Anomaly detection, security monitoring
  • Respond: Incident response plans and communications
  • Recover: Business continuity and disaster recovery

6.6 Recordkeeping Requirements

Books and Records (SEC Rule 204-2)

RIAs must maintain records for specified periods:

  • 6 Years: Accounts, trades, communications
  • 5 Years: First 2 in easily accessible place
  • Lifetime: Articles of incorporation, partnership agreements

Electronic Recordkeeping

  • WORM storage (Write Once Read Many)
  • Tamper-proof audit trails
  • Redundant backups and disaster recovery
  • Ability to produce records promptly

6.7 Marketing and Advertising Rules

SEC Marketing Rule (Rule 206(4)-1)

  • Prohibit false or misleading statements
  • Testimonials must include disclosures
  • Performance advertising requires net-of-fees returns
  • Time-weighted returns for one year+
  • Cherry-picking prohibition

6.8 Technology and Algorithm Governance

Algorithm Validation

  • Document algorithm logic and assumptions
  • Backtest with historical data
  • Stress test under extreme scenarios
  • Regular review and updates
  • Override mechanisms for errors

Model Risk Management

  • Independent validation by quants
  • Assumption documentation
  • Limitation disclosure
  • Ongoing performance monitoring
  • Version control and change logs

6.9 International Regulations

6.9.1 MiFID II (Europe)

  • Suitability and appropriateness assessments
  • Product governance requirements
  • Best execution reporting
  • Inducement restrictions

6.9.2 FCA Regulations (UK)

  • Treating Customers Fairly (TCF)
  • Consumer Duty obligations
  • Automated advice attestation
  • Financial promotions approval

6.10 Compliance Program Elements

Essential Components:
  • Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) appointment
  • Written policies and procedures
  • Annual compliance review
  • Employee training and certification
  • Testing and monitoring
  • Remediation and corrective action
