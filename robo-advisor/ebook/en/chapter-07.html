Chapter 7: Case Studies and Industry Best Practices

7.1 Betterment: The Pioneer

Company Overview

  • Founded: 2008
  • AUM: $40+ billion
  • Customers: 800,000+
  • Fee: 0.25% annually

Key Innovations

  • Tax-Loss Harvesting: First to offer automated TLH at scale
  • Fractional Shares: Enable precise allocations
  • Smart Deposit: Automatically allocate deposits to underweight assets
  • Tax Coordination: Optimize asset location across taxable and IRA accounts

Technology Stack

  • Python for portfolio optimization
  • Scala for high-throughput trading
  • PostgreSQL for transactional data
  • Real-time portfolio monitoring

Lessons Learned

  • User experience matters more than marginal optimization
  • Tax alpha can add 0.7-1.5% annually
  • Simple, transparent pricing builds trust
  • Mobile-first design critical for younger investors

7.2 Wealthfront: Tax Optimization Leader

Company Overview

  • Founded: 2011
  • AUM: $60+ billion
  • Customers: 700,000+
  • Fee: 0.25% annually

Distinctive Features

  • Direct Indexing: Own individual stocks instead of ETFs for $100k+ accounts
  • Stock-Level Tax-Loss Harvesting: Harvest losses on 1,000+ individual stocks
  • Risk Parity Fund: Proprietary fund with leverage
  • Path: Financial planning with home purchase, college savings

Portfolio Approach

  • 0.07-0.16% weighted expense ratios
  • 10-12 asset classes for diversification
  • Automatic rebalancing with 3% threshold
  • VTI →ITOT, VEA → SCHF for tax harvesting pairs

Best Practices

  • Prioritize tax efficiency for high-income clients
  • Direct indexing provides both customization and tax alpha
  • Transparency in methodology builds credibility
  • Free financial planning attracts and retains clients

7.3 Vanguard Digital Advisor: Institutional Scale

Company Overview

  • Launched: 2020
  • AUM: $200+ billion
  • Minimum: $3,000
  • Fee: 0.15% annually

Approach

  • Simple Vanguard index fund portfolios
  • 4-8 funds depending on account type
  • Automatic rebalancing
  • Access to human advisors

Competitive Advantages

  • Lowest costs: 0.15% + ultra-low fund expenses
  • Institutional credibility and trust
  • Extensive mutual fund selection
  • Scale enables continuous cost reduction

Lessons

  • Brand reputation matters enormously
  • Simplicity can win over complexity
  • Cost leadership is defensible moat
  • Hybrid model combines automation with human touch

7.4 Schwab Intelligent Portfolios

Company Overview

  • Launched: 2015
  • AUM: $85+ billion
  • Fee: $0 (no advisory fee)
  • Minimum: $5,000

Business Model

  • No advisory fee - revenue from Schwab ETF expense ratios
  • 6-8% cash allocation generates float income
  • Cross-sell to other Schwab products

Portfolio Construction

  • 17-20 asset classes
  • Primarily Schwab ETFs
  • Monthly rebalancing if drift > 5%
  • Tax-loss harvesting in Premium tier ($25k min, $30/mo)

Strategic Insights

  • Free offering disrupted industry pricing
  • Vertically integrated ETFs enable zero-fee model
  • Cash allocation controversial but profitable
  • Loss leader for client acquisition

7.6 Industry Best Practices

7.6.1 User Experience

  • Onboarding in under 10 minutes
  • Clear, jargon-free language
  • Mobile-optimized interfaces
  • Goal-based visualizations
  • Real-time portfolio updates

7.6.2 Portfolio Management

  • Low-cost ETF portfolios (< 0.15% weighted ER)
  • Broad diversification (8-20 asset classes)
  • Tax-loss harvesting for taxable accounts
  • Automatic rebalancing with drift thresholds
  • Fractional shares for precise allocation

7.6.3 Technology Infrastructure

  • Cloud-native architecture (AWS, Azure, GCP)
  • Microservices for scalability
  • Real-time market data integration
  • Automated testing and deployment
  • Disaster recovery and high availability

7.6.4 Customer Communication

  • Educational content during onboarding
  • Market volatility messaging during downturns
  • Proactive rebalancing notifications
  • Tax-loss harvesting reports
  • Annual performance summaries

7.6.5 Risk Management

  • Robust risk questionnaires (10-15 questions)
  • Periodic re-assessment requirements
  • Stress testing and scenario analysis
  • Downside protection disclosures
  • Emergency liquidity planning

7.7 Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Warning Signs:
  • Over-optimization: Complex strategies that confuse clients
  • Hidden fees: Expense ratios, transaction costs not disclosed
  • Illiquid investments: Hard-to-sell assets in retail portfolios
  • Market timing: Tactical shifts based on predictions
  • Proprietary products: Self-dealing with in-house funds
  • Inadequate disclosures: Omitting conflicts or risks

7.8 Success Metrics

Client-Facing Metrics

  • Net returns (after all fees and taxes)
  • Sharpe ratio and risk-adjusted performance
  • Tax alpha generated
  • Volatility relative to benchmark
  • Tracking error to target allocation

Business Metrics

  • Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
  • Lifetime value (LTV)
  • Churn rate and retention
  • Net promoter score (NPS)
  • Assets under management growth
  • Revenue per client
