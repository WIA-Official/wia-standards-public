💓 Photoplethysmography (PPG)

Photoplethysmography (PPG) is the foundational technology behind heart rate monitoring in smartphones and wearables. It uses light to detect blood volume changes in the microvascular bed of tissue. When the heart beats, blood volume in the peripheral vasculature briefly increases, changing the amount of light absorbed or reflected.

PPG Operating Principles

A PPG sensor consists of an LED light source and a photodetector. The LED illuminates the skin, and the photodetector measures the amount of light that passes through (transmissive mode) or reflects back from (reflective mode) the tissue. The pulsatile component of this signal corresponds to the cardiac cycle.

💚
Green LED (530nm)

Most common for wrist-based heart rate. Good absorption by hemoglobin, lower penetration depth reduces motion artifact sensitivity. Used by Apple Watch, Fitbit.

❤️
Red LED (660nm)

Required for SpO2 measurement. Deeper penetration than green. Used in combination with infrared for pulse oximetry applications.

🔴
Infrared (940nm)

Deepest penetration, used with red for SpO2 calculation. Less sensitive to skin pigmentation. Essential for accurate oxygen saturation measurement.

💡 Multi-LED PPG Systems

Modern wearables use multiple LEDs at different wavelengths and positions to improve accuracy. The Apple Watch Series 9 uses 4 green, 4 red, and 4 infrared LEDs with 4 photodiodes. Advanced algorithms combine signals to reject motion artifacts and improve measurements during activity.

Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

HRV measures the variation in time between successive heartbeats (R-R intervals). Higher HRV generally indicates better cardiovascular fitness and stress resilience. PPG-derived HRV is increasingly used for stress monitoring, sleep quality assessment, and fitness tracking.

📈 Electrocardiography (ECG/EKG)

ECG sensors measure the electrical activity of the heart by detecting the small voltage changes on the skin surface that result from cardiac muscle depolarization and repolarization. Consumer wearables now offer single-lead ECG capability for detecting atrial fibrillation (AFib).

ECG Signal Components

  • P Wave: Atrial depolarization (0.08-0.12s duration)
  • QRS Complex: Ventricular depolarization (0.06-0.10s duration)
  • T Wave: Ventricular repolarization
  • PR Interval: Time from atrial to ventricular activation (0.12-0.20s)
  • QT Interval: Total ventricular activity time

ECG Sensor Technologies

🔋
Wet Electrodes (Ag/AgCl)

Gel-based electrodes provide excellent signal quality. Gold standard for clinical ECG. Single-use, skin preparation needed. Used in Holter monitors and clinical systems.

⚡
Dry Electrodes

Metal or conductive polymer electrodes without gel. Reusable, convenient for wearables. Higher impedance requires better amplifier design. Apple Watch, Samsung Galaxy Watch.

👕
Textile Electrodes

Conductive fibers woven into fabric. Enables garment-integrated ECG. Comfort for long-term wear. Used in smart clothing and chest straps.

🩸 Blood Oxygen (SpO2) Monitoring

Pulse oximetry measures peripheral oxygen saturation (SpO2) by exploiting the different light absorption characteristics of oxygenated and deoxygenated hemoglobin. This non-invasive technique has become standard in wearables since the COVID-19 pandemic highlighted the importance of monitoring oxygen levels.

SpO2 Measurement Principle

Oxygenated hemoglobin (HbO2) absorbs more infrared light and allows more red light to pass through, while deoxygenated hemoglobin (Hb) absorbs more red light and allows more infrared to pass. By measuring the ratio of light absorption at red (660nm) and infrared (940nm) wavelengths, the oxygen saturation can be calculated.

// SpO2 Calculation Algorithm (Simplified)
interface SpO2Measurement {
    redAC: number;     // Pulsatile component at 660nm
    redDC: number;     // DC component at 660nm
    irAC: number;      // Pulsatile component at 940nm
    irDC: number;      // DC component at 940nm
}

function calculateSpO2(measurement: SpO2Measurement): number {
    // Calculate R ratio (modulation ratio)
    const redModulation = measurement.redAC / measurement.redDC;
    const irModulation = measurement.irAC / measurement.irDC;
    const R = redModulation / irModulation;
    
    // Empirical calibration curve (device-specific)
    // SpO2 = A - B * R (linear approximation)
    const A = 110;
    const B = 25;
    const SpO2 = A - (B * R);
    
    return Math.min(100, Math.max(0, SpO2));
}

// Normal SpO2: 95-100%
// Hypoxemia: <90%
// Severe Hypoxemia: <80%
⚠️ SpO2 Accuracy Considerations

Skin Pigmentation: FDA studies show reduced accuracy in individuals with darker skin tones. Algorithm bias is an active area of research and regulation.

Motion Artifacts: Movement during measurement significantly affects accuracy. Most wearables require the user to remain still.

Perfusion: Cold extremities, vasoconstriction, and low perfusion reduce signal quality and accuracy.

Nail Polish: Dark nail polish can interfere with light transmission in fingertip sensors.

🩺 Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM)

Continuous glucose monitors enable real-time tracking of blood glucose levels, transforming diabetes management. While current CGM systems are minimally invasive (requiring a subcutaneous sensor), non-invasive optical glucose sensing is an active area of research.

CGM Technologies

🔬
Electrochemical (Current Standard)

Enzyme-coated electrode measures glucose oxidation current. Subcutaneous sensor replaced every 10-14 days. Leaders: Dexcom G7, Abbott Freestyle Libre 3, Medtronic Guardian.

🔦
Optical/Spectroscopic

Measures glucose through skin using NIR spectroscopy, Raman spectroscopy, or photoacoustic methods. Non-invasive but challenging accuracy. Emerging products from Apple, Rockley.

💧
Interstitial Fluid Extraction

Microneedles or reverse iontophoresis extract interstitial fluid for measurement. Less invasive than subcutaneous sensors. GlucoWatch (discontinued) used this approach.

⌚ Wearable Health Platforms

Modern smartwatches and fitness trackers integrate multiple biometric sensors into comprehensive health monitoring platforms. These devices combine PPG, ECG, accelerometers, gyroscopes, and temperature sensors to provide continuous health insights.

Emerging Health Sensing Capabilities

  • Blood Pressure: Samsung Galaxy Watch offers cuffless BP estimation using pulse transit time (requires cuff calibration)
  • Body Composition: Bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA) estimates body fat %, muscle mass, water
  • Stress/Mental Health: EDA (electrodermal activity) sensors detect stress response through skin conductance
  • Sleep Apnea: SpO2 pattern analysis can screen for sleep-disordered breathing
  • Menstrual Cycle: Skin temperature tracking enables cycle phase prediction
