8.1 Electric and Hybrid Propulsion

Electric Propulsion Architectures

Electric Aviation Propulsion Types:
===================================

1. All-Electric
   └── Battery → Motor → Propeller/Fan
   └── Applications: Short-range, eVTOL, training
   └── Challenge: Battery energy density (250 Wh/kg)

2. Hybrid-Electric (Parallel)
   └── Gas Turbine + Electric Motor → Gearbox → Prop
   └── Applications: Regional aircraft
   └── Benefit: Optimized operation points

3. Hybrid-Electric (Series)
   └── Gas Turbine → Generator → Battery → Motor → Prop
   └── Applications: Distributed propulsion
   └── Benefit: Propulsion/generation decoupled

4. Turboelectric
   └── Gas Turbine → Generator → Motors → Fans
   └── Applications: Large aircraft concepts
   └── Benefit: Boundary layer ingestion

5. Hydrogen Fuel Cell
   └── H2 + FC → Electric → Motor → Prop
   └── Applications: Zero-emission medium range
   └── Challenge: H2 storage volume

8.3 Hydrogen Propulsion

Hydrogen Aviation Concepts

  • H2 Combustion: Modified gas turbines burning hydrogen directly
  • Fuel Cells: Electrochemical conversion to electricity
  • Hybrid: Combined fuel cell + battery systems
  • Storage: Cryogenic liquid H2 (LH2) at -253°C

Challenges: Tank volume (4x kerosene), airport infrastructure, boil-off management
