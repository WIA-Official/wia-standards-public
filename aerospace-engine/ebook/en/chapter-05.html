Chapter 05: Manufacturing Technologies

Manufacturing aerospace engine components requires extraordinary precision and quality control. From investment casting of single-crystal turbine blades to additive manufacturing of fuel nozzles, modern production technologies enable the complex geometries and tight tolerances demanded by propulsion systems. This chapter covers key manufacturing processes per WIA-AEROSPACE_ENGINE specifications.

5.1 Investment Casting

Investment casting, particularly the single-crystal casting process, is fundamental to turbine blade production. The process enables complex internal cooling passages and controlled crystallographic orientation.

Single Crystal Casting Process

Single Crystal Turbine Blade Manufacturing:
==========================================

1. Wax Pattern Assembly
   - Wax injection molding of blade
   - Ceramic core insertion for cooling passages
   - Wax tree assembly (cluster)

2. Shell Building
   - Ceramic slurry dipping
   - Stuccoing with refractory particles
   - 7-10 coating layers
   - Drying between coats

3. Dewax and Preheat
   - Autoclave dewax (flash fire)
   - Shell sintering at 1000°C

4. Single Crystal Growth
   - Vacuum induction melting
   - Alloy pouring at 1500°C
   - Grain selector or seed crystal
   - Controlled withdrawal (200mm/hr)
   - Directional solidification

5. Post-Processing
   - Shell knockout
   - Core leaching
   - HIP treatment
   - Heat treatment
   - Final machining

5.3 Forging Operations

Disk Forging Process

  • Triple Melt: VIM + ESR + VAR for inclusion control
  • Billet Conversion: Cogging and upsetting
  • Isothermal Forging: Controlled strain rate forming
  • Heat Treatment: Solution, aging for microstructure
  • Machining: Rough and finish to final dimensions
