Chapter 02: Gas Turbine Engines

Gas turbine engines are the dominant propulsion technology for modern aviation, powering everything from regional turboprops to the largest widebody aircraft. This chapter provides comprehensive coverage of turbofan and turbojet architecture, component design principles, and performance optimization strategies according to the WIA-AEROSPACE_ENGINE standard.

2.1 Turbofan Engine Architecture

The turbofan engine represents the most efficient air-breathing propulsion system for subsonic flight. By directing a portion of airflow around the core, turbofans achieve high propulsive efficiency while maintaining the power density advantages of gas turbines.

// Compressor Stage Design

  stator: {
    vaneCount: number;
    aspectRatio: number;
    inletAngle: number;
    exitAngle: number;

  };

  performance: {
    pressureRatio: number;    // typically 1.15-1.35

  };
}

2.6 Military Engine Considerations

  • Afterburner: Thrust augmentation for combat maneuvers
  • Variable Cycle: Adaptive bypass for multi-mission capability
  • Stealth: IR signature reduction and radar cross-section
  • Supercruise: Sustained supersonic flight without afterburner
