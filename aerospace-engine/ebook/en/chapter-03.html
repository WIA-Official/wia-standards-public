Chapter 03: Rocket Propulsion Systems

Rocket propulsion enables humanity's access to space, from launching satellites to exploring distant worlds. Unlike air-breathing engines, rockets carry both fuel and oxidizer, allowing operation in the vacuum of space. This chapter covers liquid, solid, and hybrid rocket systems according to WIA-AEROSPACE_ENGINE specifications.

3.1 Rocket Propulsion Fundamentals

The Rocket Equation

Tsiolkovsky Rocket Equation:
============================

Δv = Isp × g₀ × ln(m₀/mf)

Where:
  Δv  = Change in velocity (m/s)
  Isp = Specific impulse (seconds)
  g₀  = Standard gravity (9.81 m/s²)
  m₀  = Initial mass (kg)
  mf  = Final mass (kg)

Example: For Isp = 350s and mass ratio of 10:
Δv = 350 × 9.81 × ln(10) = 7,910 m/s

3.2 Liquid Rocket Engines

Liquid rockets offer high performance and controllability, making them the choice for most orbital launch vehicles and spacecraft maneuvering systems.

3.3 Solid Rocket Motors

Solid rocket motors provide high thrust, simplicity, and long-term storability, making them ideal for boosters, missiles, and emergency escape systems.

Solid Motor Characteristics

  • Advantages: Simplicity, storability, high thrust density
  • Disadvantages: Cannot throttle or restart, lower Isp
  • Propellants: HTPB/AP/Al composite, double-base nitrocellulose
  • Isp range: 240-290 seconds

3.6 Advanced Propulsion Concepts

Beyond chemical rockets, advanced propulsion concepts offer higher efficiency for specific mission profiles. Electric propulsion systems provide extremely high specific impulse for deep space missions, while nuclear thermal propulsion promises to revolutionize crewed Mars missions.

Technology Isp (seconds) Thrust Level Applications
Ion Thruster 2000-4000 mN - N Station keeping, deep space
Hall Effect 1500-3000 mN - N Orbit raising, station keeping
Nuclear Thermal 800-1000 kN - MN Crewed Mars missions
Solar Sail N/A µN - mN Interplanetary exploration
WIA Certification Note: All rocket propulsion systems covered by the WIA-AEROSPACE_ENGINE standard must undergo rigorous testing and certification processes. This includes design validation, component qualification, system-level testing, and flight readiness reviews. Safety is paramount, and systems must demonstrate compliance with range safety requirements before any launch operations.
