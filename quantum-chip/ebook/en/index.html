Quantum Computing Milestones

2019

Google Quantum Supremacy

Google's Sycamore processor with 53 qubits demonstrated quantum supremacy, completing a calculation in 200 seconds that would take classical supercomputers 10,000 years.

2021

IBM 127-Qubit Eagle

IBM unveiled the Eagle processor, the first quantum chip to break the 100-qubit barrier, marking a significant milestone in qubit scaling.

2023

IBM 1,000+ Qubit Condor

IBM's Condor processor reached 1,121 qubits, demonstrating continued progress toward practical quantum advantage.

2024

Google Willow - Below Threshold

Google's Willow chip with 105 qubits achieved below-threshold error correction, a critical milestone for fault-tolerant quantum computing.

2025

Microsoft Majorana 1

Microsoft announced Majorana 1, the first commercial topological quantum processor, utilizing Majorana zero modes for inherent error protection.

2025

IBM Nighthawk Launch

IBM released Nighthawk with 120 qubits and 218 tunable couplers, featuring improved coherence and gate fidelity for complex algorithm execution.

About WIA-QUANTUM-CHIP Standard

The WIA-QUANTUM-CHIP standard establishes universal guidelines for quantum processor certification, testing, and interoperability. Developed by the World Certification Industry Association (WIA), this standard ensures that quantum hardware from different manufacturers can be reliably evaluated, compared, and integrated into hybrid computing systems.

This comprehensive ebook provides the knowledge foundation needed to understand quantum chip technology and work with the WIA certification framework. Whether you're a researcher, engineer, or technology decision-maker, this guide will equip you with the expertise to navigate the rapidly evolving quantum hardware landscape.
