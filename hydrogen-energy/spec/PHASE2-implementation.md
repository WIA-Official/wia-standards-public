## 2.3 Procurement Strategy

### Major Equipment Procurement

**Electrolyzer System:**
- RFP issued to 2-3 pre-qualified vendors (from FEED phase)
- Technical and commercial evaluation (weighted scoring)
- Vendor presentations and site visits (factory acceptance test facilities)
- Contract negotiation: price, delivery, payment terms, warranties
- Typical lead time: 12-18 months from PO to delivery

**Compression & Gas Processing:**
- Compressor: 6-12 month lead time
- Drying system (PSA or TSA): 4-8 months
- Gas analysis equipment: 3-6 months

**Storage Vessels:**
- ASME pressure vessels: 6-10 months (depending on size and quantity)
- Tank fabrication, inspection, hydrostatic testing
- Transportation and lifting plan for large vessels

**Balance of Plant:**
- Water treatment system: 4-6 months
- Cooling system: 3-5 months
- Power supply/rectifier: 4-8 months
- Control system hardware and software: 3-6 months

### Procurement Best Practices

1. **Long-Lead Item Management:**
   - Identify items with lead time >6 months
   - Early procurement (potentially during FEED)
   - Expedite critical path items

2. **Vendor Qualification:**
   - Technical capability (design, manufacturing, quality)
   - Financial stability (Dun & Bradstreet rating)
   - Safety record (TRIR, incident history)
   - References from similar projects

3. **Contract Terms:**
   - Fixed price preferred for major equipment
   - Performance guarantees (capacity, efficiency, availability)
   - Liquidated damages for late delivery or underperformance
   - Warranty: 1-2 years parts and labor (negotiate longer for critical items)
   - Spare parts: Initial spares package + long-term availability commitment

4. **Quality Assurance:**
   - Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) for all major equipment
   - Third-party inspection (ASME code vessels, critical welds)
   - Material certifications and test reports

**Deliverable:** Procurement Log
- Equipment and material list with PO numbers
- Vendor names and contact information
- Delivery dates (planned and forecasted)
- Status tracking (ordered, manufactured, shipped, received)

## 2.4 Construction Management

### Construction Approach

**Option 1: EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction) Contractor**
- Single point of responsibility
- Fixed price or lump sum contract (preferred for cost certainty)
- Contractor manages all subcontractors and construction
- Owner oversight and QA/QC audits

**Option 2: EPCM (Engineering, Procurement, Construction Management) + Direct Hire**
- EPCM provides engineering and construction management services
- Owner procures major equipment directly (cost savings)
- Construction subcontractors hired by owner or EPCM
- More owner involvement but potential cost and schedule control

**Recommended Approach for Hydrogen Projects:**
- EPC for turn-key delivery and risk transfer
- Owner maintains strong technical and safety oversight
- Regular progress reviews and quality audits

### Construction Execution Plan

**Site Preparation (Months 7-9):**
- Mobilization and site fencing
- Clearing and grubbing
- Rough grading and drainage
- Utility installation (water, sewer, power, telecom)
- Temporary facilities (construction office, laydown area)

**Civil and Foundations (Months 9-11):**
- Excavation and compaction
- Foundation concrete placement
- Anchor bolts and embedded items
- Curing time (minimum 28 days for design strength)
- Underground piping and electrical

**Structural and Buildings (Months 10-13):**
- Structural steel erection
- Building shell construction
- Roofing and siding
- HVAC, plumbing, electrical rough-in

**Equipment Setting (Months 12-15):**
- Major equipment delivery and receiving inspection
- Rigging and crane operations (critical lifts require engineered lift plan)
- Equipment placement and leveling
- Anchor bolt grouting and tightening

**Piping and Electrical (Months 13-16):**
- Process piping fabrication and installation
- Pressure testing (hydrostatic or pneumatic)
- Electrical cable pulling and termination
- Instrumentation installation and wiring

**Completion and Pre-Commissioning (Months 16-18):**
- Insulation and paint
- Final connections (power, water, compressed air)
- Punchlist development and closeout
- System cleaning and flushing
- Pre-commissioning checks (see Section 2.6)

### Construction Safety Management

**Goal: Zero Harm**

**Safety Program Elements:**
1. Site-Specific Safety Plan (approved before construction start)
2. Daily safety briefings (toolbox talks)
3. Job Hazard Analysis (JHA) for high-risk activities
4. Permit system (hot work, confined space, excavation, energized electrical)
5. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) requirements
6. Incident reporting and investigation
7. Weekly safety audits and inspections
8. Monthly safety meetings with all contractors

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
- Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR): Target <1.0
- Lost Time Incident (LTI): Target = 0
- Near-miss reporting rate: >10 per month (indicates strong reporting culture)
- Safety observation program: 100% participation

**Deliverable:** Construction Safety Plan
- Site safety rules and procedures
- Emergency response procedures
- Evacuation plan and muster points
- PPE matrix by work area/activity
- Training requirements and records

## 2.5 Quality Assurance and Quality Control

### QA/QC Program

**Objective:** Ensure all work is performed per design specifications and industry standards

**QA/QC Organization:**
- Owner's QA/QC Manager (independent of contractor)
- EPC Contractor QA/QC Department
- Third-party inspection agencies (for code vessels, welds, NDT)

**Inspection and Test Plan (ITP):**

| Activity | Inspection Type | Acceptance Criteria | Responsible Party |
|----------|----------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Foundation concrete | Visual, slump test, strength samples | Per ACI 318, f'c ≥ design strength | Owner QC witness |
| Anchor bolts | Dimensional survey | ±5mm position, ±2mm elevation | Owner QC |
| Pressure vessels | ASME Code inspection, hydrostatic test | ASME BPVC Section VIII, no leaks @ 1.5× design pressure | Authorized Inspector (AI) |
| Piping welds | Visual, radiography (100% for H₂) | ASME B31.12, no unacceptable defects | Third-party NDT |
| Piping pressure test | Pneumatic or hydrostatic | Hold 1.5× design pressure for 4 hours, <2% pressure drop | Contractor, Owner witness |
| Electrical | Megger testing, continuity | >100 MΩ @ 1000V DC, continuity confirmed | Contractor |
| Instrumentation | Calibration verification | ±0.25% of span (or per spec) | Contractor, Owner audit |

**Material Certifications:**
- Material Test Reports (MTRs) for all pressure boundary components
- Certified weld procedures (WPS) and welder qualifications (WPQ)
- Equipment certifications (UL, CE, ATEX as applicable)

**Deliverable:** Quality Assurance File
- Approved ITP with inspection records
- Material certifications and test reports
- Non-conformance reports (NCRs) and resolutions
- As-built drawings (redlined during construction)

## 2.6 Pre-Commissioning

### Pre-Commissioning Objectives

- Verify mechanical completion
- Confirm all systems are ready for energization and operation
- Identify and resolve any installation defects
- Establish baseline conditions for commissioning

### Pre-Commissioning Checklist

**Mechanical Systems:**
- [ ] All equipment installed and anchored per drawings
- [ ] Piping installation complete with proper supports
- [ ] Valves installed in correct orientation and tagged
- [ ] Pressure testing complete with no leaks
- [ ] Flushing and cleaning of all process lines complete
- [ ] Insulation and heat tracing (if applicable) installed

**Electrical Systems:**
- [ ] All electrical equipment installed and connected
- [ ] Cable pulling complete, terminations checked
- [ ] Megger testing passed (insulation resistance >100 MΩ)
- [ ] Grounding system verified (<5Ω earth resistance)
- [ ] Motor rotation direction verified (before coupling)
- [ ] Temporary power available for testing

**Instrumentation & Control:**
- [ ] All instruments installed and wired
- [ ] Loop checks complete (end-to-end verification of I/O)
- [ ] Control valves stroked and calibrated
- [ ] Interlock and safety system functional testing
- [ ] SCADA system configured and tested
- [ ] Alarm testing and setpoint verification

**Safety Systems:**
- [ ] Fire detection and suppression systems installed
- [ ] H₂ gas detectors installed and bump-tested
- [ ] Emergency shutdown system functional test
- [ ] Pressure relief devices installed and set
- [ ] Emergency eyewash and safety showers functional
- [ ] Signage and labeling complete

**Utilities:**
- [ ] Electrical power available and tested
- [ ] Water supply connected and flow-tested
- [ ] Compressed air system operational
- [ ] Nitrogen supply for purging available
- [ ] Telecom and network connectivity established

**Documentation:**
- [ ] As-built drawings updated with field changes
- [ ] O&M manuals received and reviewed
- [ ] Spare parts inventory received and stored
- [ ] Vendor training schedules confirmed

### Punch List Management

**Punch List Categories:**
- **Category A (Critical):** Must be completed before startup
- **Category B (Important):** Complete within 30 days of startup
- **Category C (Cosmetic):** Complete within 90 days

**Punch List Tracking:**
- Item description and location
- Responsible contractor
- Target completion date
- Status (open, in progress, complete, verified)
- Owner acceptance signature

**Deliverable:** Mechanical Completion Certificate
- Signed by EPC contractor, confirmed by owner
- All Category A punch list items closed
- Authorization to proceed to commissioning (Phase 3)

## 2.7 Regulatory Compliance and Inspections

### Building and Safety Inspections

**Typical Inspection Milestones:**
1. Foundation inspection (before concrete pour)
2. Framing and rough-in inspection (building structure, MEP rough-in)
3. Pressure vessel and piping inspection (by Authorized Inspector)
4. Electrical inspection (before energization)
5. Fire protection system inspection (acceptance test)
6. Final inspection (Certificate of Occupancy)

**Inspection Preparation:**
- Schedule inspections 1-2 weeks in advance
- Ensure work is ready and accessible for inspection
- Provide required documentation (calculations, certifications, test reports)
- Contractor representative and engineer present during inspection
- Address any deficiencies immediately

### Environmental and Safety Permits

**Air Quality:**
- Demonstration that equipment meets emission standards
- Opacity and particulate testing (if combustion equipment present)

**Water:**
- Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) implementation
- Erosion and sediment control inspections (weekly during rain events)
- Final stabilization and permit termination

**Hazardous Materials:**
- Hazardous Materials Inventory Statement (HMIS) update
- Hazardous Materials Management Plan (HMMP) if required
- Emergency response coordination with local fire department

**Deliverable:** Inspection and Permit Log
- List of all required inspections with dates and results
- Permit compliance documentation
- Final permits and approvals for operation

## 2.8 Training and Knowledge Transfer

### Operations Team Training

**Training Program Structure:**

**Phase 1: Classroom Training (2-4 weeks)**
- Hydrogen safety fundamentals (properties, hazards, PPE)
- Process overview (P&IDs, equipment function)
- Control system operation (SCADA, local panels)
- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) review
- Emergency response procedures

**Phase 2: Hands-On Training (2-4 weeks)**
- Walkdown of equipment (tagging, function verification)
- Control system simulations (startup, shutdown, upset conditions)
- Pre-commissioning participation (valve lineups, checks)
- Operator rounds and logbook practices

**Phase 3: On-the-Job Training (During Commissioning)**
- Supervised startup and operation
- Shift handover procedures
- Troubleshooting and problem-solving
- Competency assessment and certification

**Vendor Training:**
- Electrolyzer: 1 week (system operation, maintenance, troubleshooting)
- Compressor: 3-5 days
- Control system: 1 week (operator training, separate maintenance training)
- Gas analysis: 2-3 days

**Maintenance Team Training:**
- Equipment-specific maintenance procedures
- Predictive maintenance techniques (vibration, thermography)
- Spare parts management
- Work order system

**Deliverable:** Training Records
- Training curriculum and materials
- Attendance records
- Competency assessments (pass/fail)
- Certification cards for qualified operators

## 2.9 Project Controls and Reporting

### Schedule Management

**Master Schedule:**
- Baseline schedule established at start of Phase 2
- Critical path identified (typically: equipment delivery → installation → testing)
- Monthly schedule updates with actual progress
- Variance analysis (schedule variance, impact assessment)
- Recovery plans for delays (acceleration, re-sequencing)

**Schedule Performance Metrics:**
- Planned vs. Actual milestones
- Critical path duration and float
- Schedule Performance Index (SPI) = Earned Value / Planned Value (target >0.95)

### Cost Management

**Budget Categories:**
- Engineering: 10-15% of total project cost
- Equipment and materials: 50-60%
- Construction labor and subcontracts: 25-35%
- Project management and owner's costs: 5-10%
- Contingency: 10-20% (declining as project progresses)

**Cost Control Measures:**
- Monthly cost reports (committed, actual, forecast)
- Variance analysis (budget vs. actual)
- Change order management (scope, cost, schedule impact)
- Cost Performance Index (CPI) = Earned Value / Actual Cost (target >0.95)

**Deliverable:** Monthly Project Report
- Executive summary (key accomplishments, issues, upcoming milestones)
- Schedule update (Gantt chart with critical path highlighted)
- Cost summary (budget, committed, actual, forecast to complete)
- Safety performance (incidents, near-misses, training hours)
- Quality metrics (inspections, NCRs, punch list status)
- Risk register update
- Photos (construction progress, major equipment deliveries)
