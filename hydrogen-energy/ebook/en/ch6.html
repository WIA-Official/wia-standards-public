6.1 Physical Security Framework

Hydrogen energy infrastructure requires comprehensive physical security measures to protect against unauthorized access, vandalism, and potential security threats while maintaining operational accessibility.

Personnel Security Protocols

  • Background Checks: All personnel with facility access undergo screening appropriate to risk level
  • Training Requirements: Security awareness training for all employees, specialized training for operators
  • Visitor Management: Sign-in/out, escort requirements, restricted area controls
  • Contractor Vetting: Pre-qualification, site-specific orientation, work permit system
  • Insider Threat Program: Behavioral indicators, reporting mechanisms, response procedures

6.2 Cybersecurity for Hydrogen Systems

OT Security Architecture

Operational Technology (OT) systems controlling hydrogen production and distribution require specialized cybersecurity approaches:

IEC 62443 Industrial Cybersecurity Framework

The WIA-ENE-007 standard adopts IEC 62443 as the baseline for hydrogen system cybersecurity:

  • Zone and Conduit Model: Segment networks by security requirements and function
  • Defense in Depth: Multiple layers of security controls
  • Least Privilege: Minimum necessary access for users and systems
  • Security Lifecycle: Continuous assessment, monitoring, and improvement

Network Segmentation

Hydrogen System Network Architecture:

Level 4: Enterprise Network (IT)
    │ DMZ with firewalls and data diodes
    ▼
Level 3: SCADA / HMI Network
    │ Firewalls, intrusion detection
    ▼
Level 2: Control Network (PLCs, controllers)
    │ Firewalls, whitelisting
    ▼
Level 1: Field Device Network
    │ Physical isolation where possible
    ▼
Level 0: Process Equipment

Security Measures by Level:
- Levels 0-1: No direct internet connectivity
- Levels 1-2: Application whitelisting, USB blocking
- Level 2-3: Firewall with strict rules, IDS/IPS
- Level 3-4: DMZ architecture, one-way data flows to enterprise

Authentication:
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for remote access
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Password policies: 12+ characters, complexity, rotation
- Service accounts with unique credentials per system

Process Safety Management (PSM)

For facilities meeting threshold quantities, comprehensive PSM programs are required:

  1. Process Safety Information: Complete documentation of chemistry, technology, equipment
  2. Process Hazard Analysis (PHA): HAZOP, What-If, FMEA every 5 years minimum
  3. Operating Procedures: Written procedures for all normal and emergency operations
  4. Training: Initial and refresher training documented for all operators
  5. Contractors: Selection, evaluation, and oversight of contractor safety performance
  6. Pre-Startup Safety Review (PSSR): Verification before initial startup or after modifications
  7. Mechanical Integrity: Inspection, testing, and maintenance of critical equipment
  8. Hot Work Permits: Controlled ignition source management
  9. Management of Change (MOC): Review and authorization of process changes
  10. Incident Investigation: Root cause analysis and corrective action tracking
  11. Emergency Planning: Response procedures, drills, coordination with first responders
  12. Compliance Audits: Third-party audit every 3 years
  13. Trade Secrets: Protection of confidential process information
  14. Employee Participation: Worker involvement in safety programs

Renewable Energy Correlation

Demonstrating that hydrogen is produced from renewable energy requires robust tracking:

  • Temporal Matching: Hourly (preferred) or monthly matching of production and renewable generation
  • Geographic Correlation: Renewable generation and hydrogen production in same grid region
  • Additionality: Renewable energy capacity added specifically for hydrogen production
  • Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs): Procurement and retirement of RECs matching consumption
  • Power Purchase Agreements: Long-term contracts demonstrating dedicated renewable supply
  • Third-Party Verification: Annual audit by accredited certifier

6.6 Audit and Inspection Readiness

Inspection Preparation Checklist

Pre-Inspection Activities (30 days before):

□ Records Review
  □ Ensure all required records are complete and accessible
  □ Review previous inspection findings and close-out documentation
  □ Prepare summary of any changes since last inspection

□ Physical Inspection Preparation
  □ Conduct internal pre-audit inspection
  □ Address any identified deficiencies
  □ Ensure all safety equipment is functional and accessible
  □ Prepare site for inspector access (safe pathways, lighting)

□ Personnel Preparation
  □ Identify escort/subject matter experts for each area
  □ Brief personnel on inspection procedures and expectations
  □ Review emergency response procedures

□ Documentation Preparation
  □ Organize records by category
  □ Prepare index of key documents
  □ Have copies available for inspector review
  □ Ensure confidential/proprietary information is marked

During Inspection:
□ Professional, cooperative demeanor
□ Answer questions accurately and completely
□ Take notes of inspector comments and observations
□ If answer is unknown, commit to follow-up (don't guess)
□ Request clarification of any unclear findings
□ Daily debriefs with inspection team

Post-Inspection:
□ Review all findings and recommendations
□ Develop corrective action plan with timelines
□ Assign responsibility for each action item
□ Implement high-priority items immediately
□ Track and document corrective action completion
□ Prepare response to regulatory agency (if required)
□ Update procedures based on lessons learned

🎯 Chapter Summary

Security and compliance are foundational to successful hydrogen operations:

  • Physical security: Layered approach from perimeter to process, personnel vetting
  • Cybersecurity: IEC 62443 framework, network segmentation, defense in depth
  • Regulatory compliance: PSM, environmental permits, transportation regulations
  • Green certification: Carbon intensity limits, renewable energy correlation
  • Data management: Automated reporting, record retention, audit readiness
  • Insurance: Comprehensive coverage, loss prevention programs
  • International standards: Harmonized approach across jurisdictions

Robust security and compliance programs protect assets, ensure regulatory standing, and build stakeholder confidence in hydrogen energy systems.
