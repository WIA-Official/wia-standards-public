WIA-SEC-001 Standard Specification

8.1 WIA-SEC-001 Overview

The WIA-SEC-001 Standard represents the World Industry Association's comprehensive security certification framework. This standard establishes requirements for organizations seeking to demonstrate their commitment to security excellence across all operational domains. The specification covers cybersecurity, physical security, identity management, data protection, and security operations.

This chapter provides the official WIA-SEC-001 specification, including detailed control requirements, biometric authentication standards, certification levels, and compliance assessment procedures.

WIA-SEC-001 Mission Statement

To establish a globally recognized security standard that enables organizations to protect their assets, data, and stakeholders while fostering trust and enabling secure digital transformation. Guided by the principle of 弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity), WIA-SEC-001 aims to elevate security practices worldwide for the benefit of all.

8.3 Biometric Authentication Standards

WIA-SEC-001 establishes comprehensive requirements for biometric authentication systems, ensuring security, privacy, and accuracy in biometric implementations.

8.3.1 Approved Biometric Modalities

👆

Fingerprint Recognition

Capacitive or optical sensors with anti-spoofing measures. FAR: <0.001%, FRR: <0.1%

👁️

Facial Recognition

3D depth sensing with liveness detection. FAR: <0.0001%, FRR: <0.5%

👁️‍🗨️

Iris Recognition

Near-infrared imaging with quality checks. FAR: <0.0001%, FRR: <0.2%

🗣️

Voice Recognition

Text-dependent or independent with replay detection. FAR: <0.01%, FRR: <1%

🖐️

Palm Vein Recognition

Infrared vein pattern matching. FAR: <0.00001%, FRR: <0.01%

⌨️

Behavioral Biometrics

Keystroke dynamics, gait analysis, mouse patterns. Continuous authentication.

Biometric Privacy Considerations

Biometric data is considered sensitive personal information under most privacy regulations. Organizations must implement appropriate safeguards, obtain informed consent, provide transparency about data usage, and enable user rights including deletion requests. Biometric templates should be stored locally where feasible and centralized databases must have enhanced protections.

AI Security Best Practices

AI-enhanced security systems should maintain human oversight for critical decisions. Organizations must document AI model training data sources, monitor for model drift, and establish clear escalation paths when AI confidence levels are low. Regular red team exercises should include AI evasion testing.

8.7.3 Maintaining Certification

Certification Maintenance Requirements

To maintain WIA-SEC-001 certification, organizations must:

  • Pass surveillance audits at required intervals (annual for Bronze/Silver, semi-annual for Gold, continuous for Platinum)
  • Report significant security incidents within 72 hours
  • Submit annual attestation of continued compliance
  • Address identified findings within specified timeframes
  • Complete re-certification every 3 years
