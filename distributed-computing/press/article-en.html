Limits of Centralized Computing: The Single Point of Failure Risk

[IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER_2: Overloaded central server with queued requests, red warnings and delay indicators]

Modern applications handle millions of users and petabytes of data daily. However, centralized architectures face fundamental limitations.

Critical Problems of Centralized Systems

  • Single Point of Failure (SPOF): One server failure brings down the entire system
  • Scalability Ceiling: Vertical scaling (Scale-Up) has physical and economic limits
  • Geographic Latency: Distant users experience high latency
  • Bottlenecks: Performance degrades sharply during traffic spikes
  • Data Loss Risk: Single storage failure can be unrecoverable
  • Inefficient Resource Utilization: Resources idle outside peak hours

CAP Theorem: Fundamental Principle of Distributed Systems

Distributed systems can achieve at most 2 of 3 properties simultaneously: Consistency, Availability, and Partition Tolerance.

Property Description Trade-off
Consistency All nodes have the same data vs Availability
Availability System always responds vs Consistency
Partition Tolerance Works despite network splits Required

About WIA (World Certification Industry Association)

WIA is an international standards organization that freely publishes technology standards beneficial to humanity under the MIT License. All WIA standards are delivered with production-ready code implementations in Rust, TypeScript, Python, and other languages.

WIA-COMP-003 is a core standard in the Computing & Software (COMP) category, designed to integrate with WIA-COMP-001 (Supercomputing), WIA-BLOCKCHAIN (Blockchain), WIA-SOCIAL (Social Standards), and others.
