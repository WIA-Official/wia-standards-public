Standard Overview

The WIA Elderly Care Device Standard (WIA-MED-013) provides a comprehensive framework for devices and systems that support safe and independent living for seniors. This standard integrates fall detection, daily activity monitoring, cognitive assistance, voice-controlled interfaces, emergency response systems, social connectivity, medication management, and accessibility features designed specifically for older adults.

With the global population aged 65 and above exceeding 1.4 billion, the need for technological solutions that ensure safety and health while maintaining dignity and autonomy has never been greater. This standard leverages the latest IoT, AI, and sensor technologies to help seniors live longer, safer, and more independently in their own homes.

Key Features

🚨

Fall Detection

AI-powered sensors detect falls in real-time and automatically alert caregivers and emergency services.

📊

Activity Monitoring

Track daily activity patterns to detect health changes early and provide personalized care.

🧠

Cognitive Assistance

Memory aids, schedule management, and cognitive training games to prevent dementia and maintain function.

🎤

Voice Control

Control all functions with simple voice commands to overcome physical limitations.

🆘

Emergency Response

One-touch SOS button, automatic anomaly detection, and 24/7 monitoring center connection for rapid emergency response.

💊

Medication Management

Automatic medication reminders, drug interaction warnings, and medication history tracking for safe pharmaceutical management.
