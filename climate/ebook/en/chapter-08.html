Chapter 8: Implementation & Certification

Overview: A 12-month deployment roadmap, three best-practice patterns, three working case studies, three WIA certification levels, and the ROI math for sovereigns, multinationals, and audit firms.

8.1 Twelve-month roadmap

Months 1–3 — Phase 1 Data Format

  • Inventory existing data: NetCDF holdings, GHG inventories, disclosure spreadsheets, registry records.
  • Map every internal identifier to LEI / CAS / UCUM / CRS84 references.
  • Stand up the Zarr v3 mirror of the NetCDF archive on object storage.
  • Run cf-checker regressions in CI; reject merges that introduce non-CF datasets.

Months 4–6 — Phase 2 API

  • Deploy pygeoapi or geoserver with OGC API — Coverages / EDR / Records / Features.
  • Stand up STAC catalogue (stac-fastapi-pgstac) and ingest the Zarr mirror.
  • Issue OAuth 2.1 tokens; rotate JWKS every 90 days.
  • Activate the disclosure twin endpoint with iXBRL secondary representation.

Months 7–9 — Phase 3 Protocol

  • Deploy WIS 2.0 broker (EMQX 5 + wis2box reference impl).
  • Subscribe to upstream WMO GISCs; transcode BUFR/GRIB into CF-1.10 NetCDF.
  • Spin up Kafka 3 with idempotent + transactional producers; integrate CEMS gateways.
  • Turn on Merkle-root anchoring to WIA Chain PoA / BSC.

Months 10–12 — Phase 4 Integration

  • Wire the corporate disclosure path: facility inventory → IFRS S2 → ESRS E1 → CDP.
  • For sovereigns: assemble first BTR from facility-rolled CRT 2.0 entries.
  • Federate carbon-market registries; require corresponding-adjustment evidence.
  • Deploy ESG dashboard endpoints; publish climate-justice tags.

8.2 Three best-practice patterns

Verifier-first design

Build the audit traversal experience before you build the dashboard. If a verifier can walk from any disclosed total back to a primary record in three clicks, the rest of the system is correct by construction.

Source-of-truth single-write

Facility data is written exactly once — by the meter ingest path. Every other system (disclosure, BTR, ESG dashboard) reads, never re-keys. The minute you introduce a "reconciliation step" you've lost the standard's primary value.

Climate-justice as compile-time check

Reject builds that produce disclosures missing the historical-responsibility, just-transition, and loss-and-damage tags. CI is cheaper than retroactive reporting.

8.3 Case studies

Case 1 — Mid-tier ASEAN sovereign (BTR programme)

  • Challenge: First Biennial Transparency Report due in 12 months, primary data scattered across 7 ministries, 18 industry portals, no consolidated GHG inventory database.
  • Solution: Phase 1 + Phase 2 + Phase 4 ETF endpoint deployed in 9 months; legacy data migrated through automated CRT 2.0 mappers.
  • Result: BTR submitted on time with reasonable-assurance verification, audit cost reduced 62% versus manual reconciliation pilot, < 0.4% discrepancy with independent IEA estimates.

Case 2 — Korean steelmaker (IFRS S2 + K-ETS)

  • Challenge: Simultaneous IFRS S2 first-year disclosure, ESRS E1 EU CSRD compliance, K-ETS Phase 4 allocation reconciliation, and CDP A-list defence.
  • Solution: Single facility inventory in JSON-LD; four downstream renderings auto-generated.
  • Result: 41% staff time reduction in disclosure season; CDP score moved from B to A; K-ETS reconciliation discrepancies fell from 2.3% to 0.1%.

Case 3 — Voluntary carbon registry (corresponding-adjustment trust)

  • Challenge: 2024 ICVCM CCP and VCMI Claims Code raised retirement standards; legacy retirement records did not carry corresponding-adjustment evidence; trade volume halved.
  • Solution: WIA-CLIMATE-022 federation layer over Verra and Gold Standard; W3C VC retirement records with NDA-signed adjustment letters.
  • Result: Volume recovery to 78% of 2022 levels in 6 months; first-of-kind ICVCM CCP-labelled credits cleared at 3× pre-2024 average price.

Certification process

  1. Application — system architecture, data inventory, and existing audit reports submitted.
  2. Technical conformance suite — automated execution of conformance.wia-climate.org against the Bronze/Silver/Gold scope.
  3. Verifier interview — independent review of CI gates, audit-traversal UX, climate-justice tag completeness.
  4. Penetration test — OWASP ASVS L2 (Bronze) or L3 (Silver/Gold).
  5. Issuance — WIA badge + W3C VC + verify URL.
  6. Maintenance — annual surveillance + 3-yearly recertification.

Closing

The science is settled. The accounting frameworks exist. The treaty is signed. What we still owe is the engineering — a wire format that lets a meter reading on a coal-fired boiler in Pohang reach a UNFCCC BTR in Bonn without a single human re-typing a number, while a verifier walks the traversal and signs off in hours instead of weeks. WIA-CLIMATE-022 is that wire format. Build with it carefully; build with it generously.
