Chapter 1: Introduction to the WIA Climate Data Standard

Overview: WIA-CLIMATE-022 is an executable specification that binds IPCC AR6 scenario semantics, UNFCCC Enhanced Transparency Framework reporting, WMO WIS 2.0 + CF-1.10 grids, GHG Protocol corporate inventories, and IFRS S2 climate disclosures into a single, conformance-tested wire format. This chapter explains why such a standard is needed, what it covers, and how to read the rest of the book.

1.1 The state of the world the standard answers to

By the close of 2024 the global mean surface temperature was 1.55°C above the 1850-1900 baseline (WMO Provisional State of the Climate). The IPCC AR6 Synthesis Report (2023) shows that holding warming to 1.5°C now requires reaching net zero CO₂ around 2050 with deep, immediate cuts in this decade. Nearly every country has committed under the Paris Agreement, and yet the data systems that make those commitments verifiable are fragmented, manually reconciled, and often unaudited.

WIA-CLIMATE-022 is the engineering response to that gap. The standard is not a new climate model nor a new emissions accounting framework — those already exist (CMIP6, GHG Protocol, ISO 14064, IFRS S2). It is the interoperability layer that lets a sensor on a Korean cement kiln, a CMIP6 SSP scenario run, an IFRS S2 disclosure submitted to the SEC, a UNFCCC ETF biennial transparency report, and a Verra carbon credit registry all reference the same units, the same spatial reference, the same time anchor, and the same audit trail.

1.2 Five things the standard makes possible

  1. Single-source emissions reporting. A facility-level Scope 1/2 inventory rolls up into a corporate IFRS S2 disclosure, a UNFCCC CRT (Common Reporting Table), and an SBTi target progress report — without re-keying.
  2. Cross-model scenario consumption. Climate-impact dashboards consume CMIP6 SSP1-2.6, SSP2-4.5, and SSP5-8.5 grids through a single OGC API — Coverages endpoint, regardless of which modelling group produced the run.
  3. Verifiable carbon credits. A Verra VCS, Gold Standard, or ACR credit ID resolves through WIA — Verifiable Credentials to a tamper-evident chain of monitoring evidence.
  4. Real-time atmospheric observations. WMO WIS 2.0 broker subscriptions deliver CF-1.10 NetCDF tiles to subscribers at sub-minute latency, with content-based filtering on geography and time.
  5. Article 6 ITMO bookkeeping. Internationally Transferred Mitigation Outcomes traded under Paris Agreement Article 6.2/6.4 carry a wired-in corresponding-adjustment record.

1.4 The four-phase implementation arc

Phase 1 — Data Format     CF-1.10 NetCDF, CRS84, JSON-LD, GeoJSON, units (UCUM/UDUNITS-2)
Phase 2 — API Interface   OGC API (Features, Coverages, EDR), STAC 1.0, OAuth 2.1
Phase 3 — Protocol        WMO WIS 2.0 MQTT broker, Kafka exactly-once, NetCDF over HTTPS
Phase 4 — Integration     UNFCCC ETF, IFRS S2 / ESRS E1, registries, ESG dashboards

1.5 Reference authorities you will see throughout the book

  • IPCC — Sixth Assessment Report (AR6). WG-I physical science basis, WG-II impacts, WG-III mitigation. SPM tables drive risk thresholds in Phase 1.
  • UNFCCC — Enhanced Transparency Framework, Common Reporting Tables (CRT 2.0), Article 6 PACM (6.4 mechanism) and ITMOs (6.2).
  • WMO — WIS 2.0 (in production since 2023, replacing GTS), WMO Information System message broker network, WIS2 Notification Message format.
  • OGC — Open Geospatial Consortium APIs (Features, Coverages, EDR, Records), CRS84, GeoPackage, NetCDF-CF.
  • HL7 FHIR R5 — for cross-domain link to climate-health (used in Phase 4 heat-mortality joins).
  • ISO — 14064-1/2/3 (GHG), 14067 (product carbon footprint), 14068-1 (carbon neutrality), 14083 (transport emissions).
  • IFRS Foundation — IFRS S2 climate-related disclosures (effective for periods beginning 1 Jan 2024 in early-adopter jurisdictions).
  • EFRAG — ESRS E1 (Climate Change) under the EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive.

1.6 What this book is, and is not

This book is a practitioner's reference. Read sequentially, it walks an engineering team from "what is the standard?" to "we just submitted our first IFRS S2 climate disclosure that traces back to facility-level meter data." It is not an introduction to climate science — for that, AR6 WG-I Chapter 1 is the canonical entry point. It is not a substitute for ISO 14064 audit guidance — for that, your verification body's auditor's manual remains authoritative. WIA-CLIMATE-022 makes those frameworks executable; their substantive guidance still applies.

Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  1. Climate commitments outpace climate-data interoperability; WIA-CLIMATE-022 closes that gap.
  2. The standard binds AR6 scenarios, ETF reporting, WIS 2.0 streams, GHG inventories, IFRS S2 disclosures, and Article 6 ITMOs into one wire vocabulary.
  3. Six domains (atmosphere, model output, GHG inventory, disclosure, registries, treaty reporting) share a common identifier and unit system.
  4. Four phases (Data, API, Protocol, Integration) sequence implementation from the bytes outward.
  5. The standard is the interoperability layer; the underlying climate science and accounting frameworks remain authoritative.
