WIA-ENE-006: Wind Energy Standard
Phase 4 Specification

Document Information

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

Last Updated: December 25, 2025

Maintainer: WIA Standards Committee

1. Overview

Phase 4 of the WIA-ENE-006 Wind Energy Standard defines the requirements and guidelines for implementing wind energy systems at scale. This phase focuses on establishing core protocols, data formats, and integration patterns that enable interoperability across different wind energy platforms and systems.

2. Scope

This specification covers:

  • Wind turbine telemetry data formats and transmission protocols
  • Power output forecasting algorithms and accuracy requirements
  • Grid integration standards and requirements
  • Safety and operational protocols
  • Environmental monitoring and reporting standards
  • Maintenance scheduling and lifecycle management

3. Technical Requirements

3.1 Data Format Specifications

All wind energy systems must support the following data format:

3.3 Performance Metrics

  • Availability: ≥98% uptime annually
  • Capacity Factor: Site-specific, typically 30-60%
  • Power Curve Accuracy: ±5% of manufacturer specifications
  • Forecast Accuracy: ≤15% MAPE for day-ahead predictions
  • Response Time: ≤100ms for telemetry data transmission

4. Safety and Compliance

4.1 Operational Safety

  • Compliance with IEC 61400 series standards
  • Automatic shutdown at wind speeds >25 m/s (cut-out speed)
  • Emergency stop response time ≤10 seconds
  • Lightning protection system meeting IEC 61024 requirements
  • Fire detection and suppression systems

4.2 Environmental Compliance

  • Noise limits: ≤45 dBA at 300m distance from residences
  • Shadow flicker: ≤30 hours/year at any residence
  • Bird and bat monitoring protocols
  • Oil spill prevention and containment measures

5. Integration Requirements

5.1 Grid Connection

Wind farms must provide:

  • Low Voltage Ride-Through (LVRT) capability
  • Reactive power control (±0.95 power factor)
  • Frequency response services (±0.5 Hz tolerance)
  • Active power control with 10% ramp rate limits

5.2 SCADA Integration

All turbines must integrate with wind farm SCADA systems supporting:

  • Real-time monitoring of all operational parameters
  • Remote control capabilities (start/stop/reset)
  • Alarm management and event logging
  • Historical data archiving (minimum 5 years)

6. Testing and Validation

6.1 Performance Testing

  • Power curve measurement per IEC 61400-12-1
  • Acoustic emissions testing per IEC 61400-11
  • Structural load validation
  • Grid compliance testing

6.2 Certification Requirements

  • Type certification from accredited certification body
  • Component certification (blades, generators, converters)
  • Project certification for wind farms >10 MW
