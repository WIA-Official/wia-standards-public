Industry Applications and Use Cases

Quantum computing promises to transform industries by solving problems that remain intractable for classical computers. From drug discovery to financial modeling, real-world applications are beginning to emerge.

The Quantum Application Landscape

While universal quantum advantage remains years away, targeted applications are already demonstrating value. The key is identifying problems where quantum approaches offer genuine advantages—not all problems benefit from quantum computing, but those that do may see transformative improvements.

Drug Discovery and Pharmaceutical

Drug discovery is perhaps the most compelling near-term application for quantum computing. Simulating molecular interactions at the quantum level—understanding how drugs bind to proteins, how enzymes catalyze reactions, and how side effects arise—requires computational resources that scale exponentially with molecular size for classical computers but polynomially for quantum computers.

Pharmaceutical Applications

  • Molecular Simulation: Accurate prediction of molecular properties and interactions
  • Protein Folding: Understanding how proteins fold and misfold (relevant to Alzheimer's, Parkinson's)
  • Drug-Target Binding: Predicting how drug candidates interact with biological targets
  • Reaction Mechanisms: Understanding enzymatic reactions for drug design
  • Toxicity Prediction: Identifying potential side effects before clinical trials

Active Research Near-term Impact

Case Study: Biogen-IBM Collaboration

Biogen partnered with IBM to explore quantum computing for drug discovery, focusing on simulating molecular systems relevant to neurological diseases. Using VQE algorithms on IBM quantum hardware, they demonstrated accurate energy calculations for small molecules that serve as stepping stones to larger biological systems. This work establishes the methodology that will scale as quantum hardware improves.

Financial Services

The financial industry faces optimization problems of extraordinary complexity: portfolio optimization, risk assessment, fraud detection, and trading strategies. Many of these problems are NP-hard, meaning classical computers struggle as problem size increases. Quantum approaches—particularly QAOA and quantum annealing—offer potential speedups.

Financial Applications

  • Portfolio Optimization: Finding optimal asset allocations across thousands of instruments
  • Risk Analysis: Monte Carlo simulations for VaR and stress testing
  • Option Pricing: Quantum amplitude estimation for derivative pricing
  • Fraud Detection: Pattern recognition in transaction data
  • Credit Scoring: Machine learning for risk assessment

Pilot Projects Production 2027+

Case Study: JPMorgan Chase

JPMorgan Chase has been at the forefront of quantum finance research, publishing work on quantum algorithms for derivative pricing and portfolio optimization. Their research demonstrated quantum advantage (using simulators) for specific option pricing scenarios, showing paths to practical applications as hardware matures.

Materials Science and Chemistry

Materials science shares the fundamental quantum nature of drug discovery: materials properties arise from quantum mechanical interactions of electrons. Designing new materials—better batteries, more efficient solar cells, room-temperature superconductors, or improved catalysts—requires understanding these interactions at the atomic level.

Materials Science Applications

  • Battery Design: Understanding lithium-ion transport and electrode reactions
  • Catalyst Development: Designing catalysts for nitrogen fixation (fertilizer) and CO2 reduction
  • Superconductors: Searching for room-temperature superconducting materials
  • Photovoltaics: Optimizing solar cell materials and structures
  • Semiconductors: Designing next-generation chip materials

Research Impact 2030+

Cryptography and Security

Quantum computing's impact on cryptography is perhaps the most urgent and well-understood application domain. Shor's algorithm threatens the RSA and elliptic curve cryptography that secures the internet, while quantum key distribution offers provably secure communication.

Post-Quantum Cryptography

NIST finalized its first post-quantum cryptography standards in 2024, marking a critical milestone in preparing for the quantum threat. Organizations worldwide are beginning the multi-year process of transitioning to quantum-resistant algorithms.

NIST PQC Standards (2024):
  • ML-KEM (CRYSTALS-Kyber): Key encapsulation mechanism
  • ML-DSA (CRYSTALS-Dilithium): Digital signature algorithm
  • SLH-DSA (SPHINCS+): Hash-based digital signatures

Quantum Key Distribution

QKD uses quantum mechanics to distribute encryption keys with information-theoretic security—any eavesdropping attempt disturbs the quantum states and is detectable. While limited by distance and infrastructure requirements, QKD is being deployed for high-security applications.

Case Study: China's Quantum Network

China has built the world's largest quantum communication network, spanning over 4,600 kilometers and connecting Beijing, Shanghai, and other major cities. In 2025, Chinese researchers established a 12,900 km quantum satellite link between China and South Africa, demonstrating intercontinental quantum-secure communication.

Optimization and Logistics

Optimization problems pervade industry: routing vehicles, scheduling production, allocating resources, and designing supply chains. Many of these problems are combinatorially explosive, with the number of possible solutions growing factorially with problem size.

Optimization Applications

  • Vehicle Routing: Optimizing delivery routes for logistics companies
  • Manufacturing: Production scheduling, resource allocation
  • Supply Chain: Inventory optimization, demand forecasting
  • Network Design: Telecommunications network optimization
  • Energy Grid: Power distribution and load balancing

Pilot Projects Near-term Value

Case Study: Volkswagen Traffic Optimization

Volkswagen partnered with D-Wave to optimize bus routes in Lisbon using quantum annealing. The project demonstrated practical quantum optimization by reducing congestion and travel times for public transit, showing that even near-term quantum devices can provide value for certain optimization problems.

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

The intersection of quantum computing and AI—quantum machine learning (QML)—is an active research area. While many claims remain speculative, certain machine learning problems may benefit from quantum approaches.

Quantum ML Applications

  • Quantum Kernels: Kernel methods using quantum feature spaces
  • Quantum Neural Networks: Parameterized quantum circuits for learning
  • Quantum Sampling: Generating samples from complex distributions
  • Optimization: Training classical ML models using quantum optimization

Research Uncertain Timeline

Research from NVIDIA and academic partners (2025) suggests that AI may now be the most important tool for solving quantum computing's problems—creating a symbiotic relationship where AI helps design better quantum computers while quantum computers eventually accelerate AI.

Climate and Energy

Addressing climate change requires advances in energy technology that quantum computing may enable: better batteries for energy storage, more efficient solar cells, improved catalysts for carbon capture, and optimized renewable energy grids.

Climate Applications

  • Carbon Capture: Simulating catalysts for CO2 sequestration
  • Hydrogen Production: Optimizing water-splitting catalysts
  • Battery Technology: Designing next-generation energy storage
  • Grid Optimization: Managing renewable energy variability
  • Climate Modeling: Improving atmospheric simulations

Summary

Key Takeaways:

  • Drug Discovery: Near-term impact through molecular simulation
  • Finance: Portfolio optimization and risk analysis show promise
  • Cryptography: PQC transition underway; QKD deployments growing
  • Optimization: Logistics and scheduling problems being tackled
  • Materials Science: Battery and catalyst design for climate solutions
  • Timeline: Most transformative applications still 5-10+ years away
