2.2 Sensor Technologies

The sensor suite is the heart of any pet wearable device. WIA-PET-007 defines requirements for various sensor types based on device category.

2.2.1 Motion Sensors

Three-Axis Accelerometer: Required for all devices. Measures acceleration along X, Y, and Z axes to detect movement, orientation, and activity levels.

+----------------------+------------------+-----------------------------------+ | Specification | Requirement | Purpose | +----------------------+------------------+-----------------------------------+ | Resolution | 16-bit minimum | High precision movement detection | | Range | ±16g | Capture vigorous activity | | Sampling Rate | 25-100 Hz | Balance accuracy and power | | Noise Level | < 150 µg/√Hz | Minimize false detections | | Power Consumption | < 100 µA | Preserve battery life | +----------------------+------------------+-----------------------------------+

Gyroscope (Optional): Three-axis gyroscope enhances motion tracking accuracy, particularly for rotation and orientation changes.

  • Resolution: 16-bit, ±2000 dps range
  • Applications: Precise activity classification, fall detection
  • Power: Active only during high-activity periods

2.2.4 Location Sensors

GPS Module: Required for location-tracking devices, optional for others.

  • Systems Supported: GPS, GLONASS, Galileo, BeiDou (multi-GNSS recommended)
  • Accuracy: 2.5m CEP (Circular Error Probable) in open sky
  • Time to First Fix: < 30 seconds (hot start), < 60 seconds (cold start)
  • Update Rate: Configurable from 1 to 10 Hz
  • Assisted GPS: A-GPS support for faster acquisition
  • Power Modes: Full power, balanced, ultra-low-power tracking

2.3.2 Material Requirements

All pet-contact materials must meet stringent safety standards:

  • Body Materials: Medical-grade silicone, thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU), or food-grade plastics
  • Toxicity Testing: ISO 10993 biocompatibility or equivalent
  • Allergen-Free: Hypoallergenic certification required
  • Durability: Resistant to pet saliva, urine, and environmental exposure
  • Color Fastness: No dye leaching or color transfer to pet fur
⚠️ Material Safety: Devices must not contain BPA, phthalates, lead, cadmium, or other toxic substances. All materials in contact with pets must be explicitly certified as pet-safe and non-toxic.

2.4.2 Battery Safety

Lithium batteries in pet wearables require multiple safety mechanisms:

  • Overcharge Protection: Hardware cutoff at 4.2V per cell
  • Over-discharge Protection: Cutoff at 2.8V per cell
  • Temperature Monitoring: Shutdown if battery exceeds 60°C
  • Short Circuit Protection: Fuse or electronic protection
  • Cell Balancing: Required for multi-cell configurations
  • Crush Resistance: Reinforced enclosure to prevent battery damage

2.5.1 LED Indicators

  • Battery Status: Green (>50%), Yellow (20-50%), Red (<20%), Blinking Red (<10%)
  • Connection Status: Blue (connected), Blinking Blue (searching), Off (disconnected)
  • Activity Indicator: White pulse during active tracking
  • Error States: Red blinking patterns for various error conditions

2.8 Durability and Reliability Testing

2.8.1 Environmental Testing

Devices must pass rigorous environmental stress testing:

  • Temperature Cycling: -20°C to +60°C, 100 cycles minimum
  • Humidity Testing: 85% RH at 85°C for 1000 hours
  • Salt Spray: 48-hour salt fog exposure (coastal environments)
  • UV Exposure: 500 hours UV-A/UV-B to verify material stability
  • Vibration: Random vibration per MIL-STD-810G

2.9 Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC)

2.9.1 Emissions Testing

Devices must not interfere with other electronics:

  • FCC Part 15: Radiated and conducted emissions limits
  • CE EMC Directive: EN 55032 (emissions) compliance
  • Harmonic Distortion: Low harmonic output from switching power supplies

2.9.2 Immunity Testing

Devices must operate reliably in the presence of electromagnetic interference:

  • ESD (Electrostatic Discharge): ±8kV contact, ±15kV air discharge
  • RF Immunity: 10 V/m across 80 MHz to 6 GHz
  • Surge Protection: ±1kV line-to-line, ±2kV line-to-ground

2.10 Modular Design and Repairability

To enhance sustainability and reduce electronic waste, the WIA-PET-007 standard encourages modular design:

2.10.1 Replaceable Components

  • Battery: User-replaceable battery modules (tools allowed)
  • Straps/Bands: Standard attachment system for easy replacement
  • Sensors: Modular sensor pods for repair or upgrades (manufacturer service)

2.10.2 Repair Documentation

Manufacturers should provide:

  • Repair manuals for authorized service centers
  • Exploded view diagrams and parts lists
  • Diagnostic software for troubleshooting
  • Spare parts availability for minimum 5 years post-production
♻️ Sustainability Note: Modular design not only extends product life but also allows for technology upgrades. A collar purchased today could potentially receive a next-generation sensor module in the future, rather than requiring complete replacement.

Chapter Summary: Key Takeaways

  1. Integrated Sensor Suite: WIA-PET-007 devices require accelerometer, health sensors (PPG, temperature), and connectivity modules with specified accuracy and power requirements.
  2. Robust Construction: Devices must achieve IP67/IP68 waterproofing, withstand drops from 1.5m, resist pet bites, and operate across -10°C to 50°C.
  3. Battery Requirements: Minimum 5-14 day battery life depending on size category, with comprehensive safety protections and USB-C or wireless charging.
  4. Material Safety: All pet-contact materials must be food-grade, hypoallergenic, and free from toxic substances, meeting ISO 10993 biocompatibility standards.
  5. Quality Assurance: Comprehensive testing protocols including sensor calibration, environmental stress tests, EMC compliance, and mechanical durability verification ensure reliability.
