4.2.2 Multi-GNSS Support

Supporting multiple Global Navigation Satellite Systems improves accuracy and reliability:

  • GPS (USA): 31 satellites, global coverage
  • GLONASS (Russia): 24 satellites, enhanced high-latitude coverage
  • Galileo (EU): 30 satellites (when fully deployed), high accuracy
  • BeiDou (China): 35 satellites, global and regional services
  • Benefit: Using multiple systems increases satellite visibility by 50-100%

4.2.3 Assisted GPS (A-GPS)

A-GPS dramatically reduces time-to-first-fix by downloading satellite data via cellular or Wi-Fi:

  • Satellite almanac and ephemeris data pre-loaded
  • Approximate location from cell tower triangulation
  • Reduces cold start from 60s to under 5s
  • Lower power consumption due to faster acquisition

4.4 Indoor Positioning

4.4.1 Indoor Location Technologies

GPS signals don't penetrate buildings effectively. Alternative methods for indoor tracking:

  • Wi-Fi Triangulation: Determine position based on visible Wi-Fi access points and signal strength
  • Bluetooth Beacons: Small BLE beacons placed around home provide proximity detection
  • Inertial Navigation: Dead reckoning using accelerometer and gyroscope when GPS unavailable
  • Barometric Altitude: Determine floor level in multi-story buildings

4.10 Power Optimization for Location Services

4.10.1 Intelligent GPS Scheduling

GPS is the most power-hungry component. Smart scheduling extends battery life:

  • Motion-Triggered: GPS activates when significant movement detected
  • Geofence Monitoring: Infrequent checks when pet is inside safe zone, frequent when near boundary
  • Adaptive Sampling: Higher frequency during walks, lower during rest periods
  • Wi-Fi Offloading: Use Wi-Fi location when at home, GPS only when away

  5. Power Optimization: Adaptive GPS sampling, motion-triggered activation, and multiple tracking modes balance accuracy with 7-30 day battery life.
