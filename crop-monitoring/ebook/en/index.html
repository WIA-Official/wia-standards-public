📚 About This Ebook

This comprehensive guide covers the WIA Crop Monitoring Standard (WIA-AGRI-006), a global standard for real-time crop health intelligence through IoT sensors, AI-powered disease detection, and predictive analytics. Learn how to protect harvests, optimize yields, and integrate with weather, marketplace, and insurance systems.

📖 Table of Contents

  • Chapter 1: Introduction to Crop Monitoring
    The evolution of agriculture, precision farming, and the role of real-time crop monitoring in modern food production. Understanding phenology, growth stages, and health assessment.
  • Chapter 2: Data Format & Schema
    Deep dive into the WIA Crop Monitoring data format: growth metrics, BBCH phenology codes, measurement standards, and JSON schema validation for crop health data.
  • Chapter 3: IoT Sensors & Field Devices
    Soil moisture sensors, weather stations, cameras, drones, and smart agriculture equipment. Installation, calibration, and real-time data collection from field to cloud.
  • Chapter 4: AI Disease Detection
    Computer vision and machine learning for crop disease identification. Training models, confidence scoring, and automated treatment recommendations for common plant diseases.
  • Chapter 5: Yield Prediction & Forecasting
    Predictive models for harvest estimation using growth data, weather patterns, and historical yields. Scenario analysis and optimization strategies for maximum productivity.
  • Chapter 6: System Integration
    Connecting crop monitoring with weather APIs, agricultural marketplaces, crop insurance platforms, and farm management software for end-to-end agricultural intelligence.
  • Chapter 7: Implementation Guide
    Step-by-step deployment of crop monitoring systems: sensor networks, cloud infrastructure, API integration, mobile apps, and real-time dashboards for farmers.
  • Chapter 8: Case Studies & Future Trends
    Real-world implementations from rice paddies in Korea, tomato farms in California, and corn fields in Brazil. The future of autonomous farming and climate-resilient agriculture.
