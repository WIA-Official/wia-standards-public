Executive Summary

The World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released the international standard WIA-DEF-016 for military communication systems encompassing tactical radios, satellite communications, secure data links, and network-centric warfare under free MIT license. This announcement marks Korea's leadership in international defense communication standards.

🚨 The Challenge: Why This Standard is Needed

In modern warfare, communication failure means mission failure. Military forces worldwide use incompatible communication systems, severely limiting interoperability during coalition operations. In electronic warfare environments, forces face communication loss and eavesdropping risks. The absence of secure tactical communication standards leads to command and control failures, putting soldiers' lives at risk.

WIA's Solution: WIA-DEF-016 Military Communication

WIA-DEF-016 standardizes all aspects of military communications from VHF/UHF/HF tactical radios to satellite communications (MILSATCOM), secure data links, and C4ISR networks. It provides frequency management, electronic warfare countermeasures, COMSEC encryption, and allied interoperability.

🏢 About WIA

The World Certification Industry Association (WIA) is an international standardization organization whose mission is to provide equal opportunities to all humanity through technology and certification.

WIA is developing over 600 international standards across defense, security, AI, environment, healthcare, and other fields, releasing all standards as free open source.
