Chapter 3

Team Building & Competition

Successful esports teams combine individual skill with exceptional teamwork, communication, and strategic coordination. This chapter covers team formation, role assignments, practice strategies, and competitive preparation.

Team Formation

Roster Building

  • Starting Five: Core competitive roster (game-dependent)
  • Substitutes: 2-3 backup players for flexibility and development
  • Practice Squad: Additional players for scrimmages and growth
  • Support Roles: Analysts, coaches, content creators, managers

Player Assessment

Evaluate potential team members across multiple dimensions:

  • Mechanical Skill: Reaction time, accuracy, game mechanics proficiency
  • Game Knowledge: Understanding of strategies, meta, champion/agent pools
  • Communication: Clear callouts, positive attitude, constructive feedback
  • Teamwork: Willingness to fill needed roles, sacrifice for team goals
  • Coachability: Receptiveness to feedback, growth mindset, dedication

Role Specialization

Most competitive games require distinct roles. Example using MOBA structure:

Core Roles

  • Top Lane/Tank: Frontline, initiation, durability focus
  • Jungle/Flex: Map control, objective management, roaming support
  • Mid Lane/Carry: Damage output, playmaking, team fight impact
  • ADC/Marksman: Consistent damage, positioning, late-game scaling
  • Support: Vision control, protection, utility, shot-calling
Role Assignment Tips:
  • Match player personality and playstyle to role requirements
  • Allow flexibility—players can learn multiple roles
  • Identify natural leaders for shot-calling positions
  • Consider champion/agent pool depth when assigning roles

Communication & Coordination

Voice Communication Best Practices

  • Clear Callouts: Concise information (enemy location, cooldowns, objectives)
  • Positive Tone: Encouragement over blame, constructive feedback
  • Information Priority: Critical info first, discussion after engagement
  • Active Listening: Acknowledge calls, confirm understanding
  • Designated Caller: One primary shot-caller to avoid confusion

Team Synergy Development

  1. Trust Building: Team bonding activities, shared goals, vulnerability
  2. Communication Drills: Practice specific callout scenarios
  3. Conflict Resolution: Established processes for disagreements
  4. Shared Language: Develop team-specific terminology and strategies

Practice Structure

Weekly Practice Plan

  • Monday: Individual skill development, mechanics training
  • Tuesday: Team strategy review, new tactics implementation
  • Wednesday: Scrimmages against other teams
  • Thursday: Replay analysis, performance feedback
  • Friday: Light practice, mental preparation
  • Weekend: Tournaments or rest (alternate weeks)

Effective Practice Activities

  • Drills: Focused mechanics practice (last-hitting, aim training)
  • VOD Review: Analysis of own games and professional matches
  • Scrimmages: Full team practice games with specific objectives
  • Theory Discussions: Meta analysis, strategy development
  • 1v1s: Individual matchup practice and skill development

Tournament Preparation

Pre-Tournament Checklist

  • ✅ Roster confirmed and registered
  • ✅ Equipment tested (hardware, internet, software)
  • ✅ Strategy playbook prepared for opponent matchups
  • ✅ Communication protocols reviewed
  • ✅ Physical and mental preparation (sleep, nutrition, stress management)
  • ✅ Backup plans for technical issues

Match Day Routine

  1. Pre-Match (1 hour before): Equipment check, warm-up games, team huddle
  2. Draft/Selection Phase: Follow prepared strategy, adapt to opponent
  3. In-Game: Execute strategy, communicate clearly, stay positive
  4. Between Games: Quick feedback, adjustments, mental reset
  5. Post-Match: Handshake/GG, brief reflection, detailed analysis later

Handling Competition

Winning with Grace

  • Respect opponents—avoid trash talk or excessive celebration
  • Acknowledge good plays from both teams
  • Review what went well to reinforce positive behaviors
  • Stay humble and focus on next challenge

Learning from Losses

  • Allow brief emotional response, then move to analysis
  • Focus on controllable factors and improvement opportunities
  • Avoid blame—collective responsibility for outcomes
  • Extract specific lessons to apply in future matches
  • Reinforce growth mindset—losses are learning experiences
Sportsmanship Matters:
Educational esports programs emphasize values over victory. Students learn that how they compete matters as much as the competition itself. Model professional conduct and hold all participants to high standards of respect and integrity.

Building Team Culture

  • Team Identity: Name, logo, colors, motto that reflects values
  • Traditions: Pre-match rituals, celebration styles, team bonding
  • Accountability: Clear expectations and consequences
  • Recognition: Celebrate improvements, not just wins
  • Inclusion: Ensure all members feel valued and heard
