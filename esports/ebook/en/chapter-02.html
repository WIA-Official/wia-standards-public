Chapter 2

Program Design & Curriculum

Building a successful educational esports program requires careful planning, clear learning objectives, and integration with academic standards. This chapter provides a framework for designing comprehensive programs that benefit all students.

Program Foundation

1. Mission & Vision

Start by defining your program's core purpose:

  • Mission: What will your program accomplish?
  • Vision: What impact will it have on students and the school?
  • Values: What principles guide the program (sportsmanship, growth, inclusion)?
Example Mission Statement:
"Our esports program develops critical 21st-century skills through competitive gaming while promoting digital citizenship, teamwork, and personal growth for all students."

2. Learning Objectives

Define measurable outcomes aligned with educational standards:

Domain Learning Objectives
Academic Critical thinking, problem-solving, data analysis, communication
Technical Game mechanics, strategy development, performance optimization
Social Teamwork, leadership, conflict resolution, sportsmanship
Career Industry awareness, skill development, professional conduct

Curriculum Development

Semester 1: Foundations

  • Week 1-2: Program orientation, digital citizenship, code of conduct
  • Week 3-4: Game fundamentals, mechanics, basic strategy
  • Week 5-8: Team formation, role identification, communication skills
  • Week 9-12: Practice routines, performance analysis, friendly scrimmages
  • Week 13-16: Tournament preparation, team strategy, competitive play

Semester 2: Advanced Development

  • Week 1-4: Advanced strategies, meta analysis, professional gameplay study
  • Week 5-8: Leadership development, coaching fundamentals, team management
  • Week 9-12: Content creation, streaming basics, personal branding
  • Week 13-16: Career exploration, industry presentations, capstone projects

Game Selection

Choose games that align with educational goals and student interests:

Recommended Educational Titles

  • Rocket League: Physics-based, non-violent, fast-paced teamwork
  • League of Legends: Strategic depth, role specialization, team coordination
  • Valorant: Tactical gameplay, communication-critical, team-based
  • Minecraft Education: Creativity, problem-solving, educational mods
  • Overwatch: Team composition, role diversity, inclusive characters
Selection Criteria:
  • Age-appropriate content (ESRB ratings)
  • Strong competitive scene with organized leagues
  • Emphasis on teamwork and strategy over individual skill
  • Active community and educational resources
  • Platform accessibility (PC, console, mobile)

Program Structure

Class Format (90-minute sessions)

  1. Check-in (5 min): Attendance, objectives review, team updates
  2. Theory/Strategy (15 min): Lesson on game concepts, tactics, skills
  3. Practice (45 min): Structured drills, scrimmages, skill development
  4. Analysis (15 min): Replay review, performance feedback, discussion
  5. Reflection (10 min): Goal setting, journaling, wrap-up

Practice Schedule

  • Beginner Teams: 2-3 sessions per week, 60-90 minutes each
  • Intermediate Teams: 3-4 sessions per week, 90-120 minutes each
  • Advanced/Varsity: 4-5 sessions per week, 120+ minutes each

Assessment & Evaluation

Performance Metrics

  • Individual Skills: Mechanical proficiency, decision-making, game knowledge
  • Team Performance: Communication quality, coordination, win rate
  • Academic Integration: Written reflections, strategic analysis papers
  • Growth Mindset: Improvement over time, resilience, learning from losses

Grading Components

Component Weight
Attendance & Participation 30%
Skill Development & Improvement 25%
Teamwork & Communication 20%
Reflections & Written Work 15%
Sportsmanship & Conduct 10%

Academic Integration

Cross-Curricular Connections

  • Mathematics: Statistics analysis, probability, resource optimization
  • English: Team communication, strategy documents, game analysis essays
  • Social Studies: Esports industry economics, global gaming culture
  • Health/PE: Ergonomics, physical fitness, mental health awareness
  • Computer Science: Game mechanics, networking, technology literacy
