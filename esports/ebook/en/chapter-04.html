Chapter 4

Digital Citizenship & Ethics

Online gaming environments present unique challenges and opportunities for teaching responsible digital behavior. Educational esports programs must prioritize digital citizenship, online safety, and ethical conduct.

Core Principles of Digital Citizenship

1. Respect & Empathy

Treat all players with dignity, regardless of skill level, background, or identity. Remember there are real people behind every screen name.

2. Responsibility

Take ownership of your online actions. Your words and behavior have real consequences for others and reflect on your team and school.

3. Integrity

Play fairly, follow rules, and maintain honesty in all competitive situations. Never cheat, exploit bugs, or engage in unsportsmanlike conduct.

4. Safety

Protect personal information, recognize online threats, and create safe gaming environments for yourself and teammates.

Code of Conduct

Student-Athlete Expectations:
  • Use appropriate language in all communications
  • Respect opponents, teammates, coaches, and officials
  • Report bullying, harassment, or toxic behavior immediately
  • Protect personal and team information
  • Represent your school with pride and professionalism
  • Balance gaming with academics, health, and other responsibilities
  • Accept coaching feedback with a growth mindset

Combating Toxicity

Understanding Toxic Behavior

Toxic behavior in gaming includes:

  • Verbal Abuse: Insults, profanity, harassment
  • Griefing: Intentionally sabotaging teammates
  • Trolling: Provoking emotional responses for amusement
  • Discrimination: Bias based on identity, skill, or background
  • Cheating: Using unfair advantages or exploits

Prevention Strategies

  • Model Positive Behavior: Coaches and leaders set the tone
  • Clear Consequences: Enforce code of conduct consistently
  • Positive Reinforcement: Recognize and reward good sportsmanship
  • Reporting Systems: Anonymous channels to report concerns
  • Education: Regular discussions about online behavior

Response Protocol

  1. Immediate: Remove from situation, ensure safety
  2. Investigation: Gather facts from all parties involved
  3. Consequences: Apply appropriate disciplinary measures
  4. Support: Provide resources for both victim and perpetrator
  5. Follow-up: Monitor for recurring issues, adjust policies as needed

Online Safety

Personal Information Protection

  • Never share real name, address, phone number, or school details publicly
  • Use gamer tags/usernames that don't reveal personal information
  • Be cautious about sharing photos or videos that reveal location
  • Understand privacy settings on gaming platforms and social media
  • Think before clicking links or downloading files from unknown sources

Account Security

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each gaming account
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever available
  • Never share account credentials, even with friends
  • Be wary of phishing attempts and scams
  • Report suspicious activity to platform administrators
Warning Signs of Online Threats:
  • Requests for personal information from strangers
  • Pressure to keep conversations secret
  • Inappropriate sexual content or advances
  • Offers that seem too good to be true (free items, prizes)
  • Threats or intimidation
If you encounter any of these, report immediately to a trusted adult.

Ethical Gaming Practices

Fair Play

  • No Cheating: Never use hacks, aimbots, or unauthorized third-party software
  • No Exploiting: Don't abuse game bugs or unintended mechanics
  • No Account Sharing: Each player uses only their own account
  • No Boosting/Smurfing: Compete at your true skill level

Academic Integrity

  • Maintain eligibility requirements (grades, attendance, conduct)
  • Prioritize academics over gaming when necessary
  • Don't plagiarize strategy guides or analysis in assignments
  • Be honest about time commitments and capabilities

Building Positive Communities

Being an Upstander, Not a Bystander

When you witness toxic behavior:

  1. Speak Up: Calmly address the behavior if safe to do so
  2. Support Victims: Offer encouragement and assistance
  3. Report: Use official channels to document incidents
  4. Model Better: Demonstrate positive behavior as an alternative

Mentorship

  • Experienced players guide newcomers with patience
  • Share knowledge without elitism or gatekeeping
  • Celebrate diverse playstyles and perspectives
  • Create welcoming environments for all skill levels

Diversity & Inclusion

Esports should be accessible and welcoming to all students:

  • Gender Inclusion: Active recruitment and support for underrepresented genders
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Respect diverse backgrounds and perspectives
  • Accessibility: Accommodate different abilities and learning styles
  • Skill Diversity: Value contributions beyond mechanical skill
