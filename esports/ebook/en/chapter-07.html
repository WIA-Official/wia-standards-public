Chapter 7

Health, Wellness & Balance

Sustainable success in esports requires attention to physical health, mental well-being, and life balance. This chapter addresses common health concerns and provides strategies for maintaining wellness while competing at high levels.

Physical Health

Ergonomics & Posture

Proper setup prevents injury and improves performance:

  • Chair: Adjustable height, lumbar support, armrests at desk level
  • Monitor: Top of screen at or slightly below eye level, arm's length away
  • Keyboard/Mouse: Elbows at 90 degrees, wrists neutral (not bent)
  • Feet: Flat on floor or footrest, knees at 90 degrees
  • Lighting: Reduce glare, ambient lighting to prevent eye strain
The 20-20-20 Rule:
Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. This reduces eye strain and prevents digital eye fatigue.

Exercise & Movement

Regular physical activity improves focus, reaction time, and overall health:

  • Breaks: Stand and move for 5-10 minutes every hour
  • Cardio: 30+ minutes, 3-5 times per week (walking, running, cycling)
  • Strength Training: 2-3 times per week (bodyweight, weights, resistance bands)
  • Flexibility: Daily stretching, yoga, or mobility work
  • Wrist/Hand Exercises: Prevent carpal tunnel and RSI

Hand & Wrist Health

Stretches (perform before and after gaming):

  1. Wrist flexion/extension (10 reps each direction)
  2. Finger spreads and curls (15 reps)
  3. Prayer stretch (hold 15 seconds)
  4. Wrist circles (10 each direction)
  5. Shake hands out loosely for 10 seconds
Warning Signs of Injury:
If you experience persistent pain, numbness, tingling, or weakness in hands, wrists, or arms, STOP gaming immediately and consult a healthcare provider. Early intervention prevents serious injury.

Nutrition

  • Hydration: 8+ glasses of water daily, limit energy drinks
  • Balanced Meals: Protein, complex carbs, healthy fats, vegetables
  • Snacks: Nuts, fruit, yogurt instead of chips and candy
  • Timing: Don't game on empty stomach; avoid heavy meals before competing
  • Caffeine: Moderate use (1-2 cups coffee/day), avoid dependence

Sleep

Sleep is critical for learning, reaction time, and performance:

  • Duration: 8-10 hours for teens, 7-9 for adults
  • Consistency: Same bedtime and wake time, even weekends
  • Screen Time: Stop gaming 1-2 hours before bed (blue light affects sleep)
  • Environment: Cool, dark, quiet bedroom
  • Routine: Wind-down activities (reading, stretching, meditation)

Mental Health

Stress Management

Competitive gaming can be stressful. Healthy coping strategies:

  • Breathing Exercises: Deep breathing before matches (4-7-8 technique)
  • Mindfulness: Stay present, don't dwell on past mistakes
  • Perspective: It's a game—performance doesn't define your worth
  • Breaks: Step away when frustrated or tilting
  • Support: Talk to friends, family, coaches about stress

Performance Anxiety

Nervousness before competition is normal. Strategies to manage:

  • Preparation: Thorough practice builds confidence
  • Routine: Consistent pre-match warm-up and rituals
  • Reframing: View anxiety as excitement and readiness
  • Focus Control: Concentrate on process, not outcome
  • Visualization: Mentally rehearse successful performance

Dealing with Losses & Setbacks

  • Allow Emotions: Brief disappointment is healthy
  • Learn Lessons: What can you improve for next time?
  • Avoid Rumination: Don't replay mistakes endlessly
  • Maintain Perspective: One match doesn't define you
  • Move Forward: Focus on next opportunity

When to Seek Help

Talk to a mental health professional if you experience:
  • Persistent sadness, anxiety, or mood changes
  • Gaming interfering with school, relationships, or health
  • Inability to control gaming time or urges
  • Using gaming to escape problems
  • Thoughts of self-harm
Mental health support is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Life Balance

Time Management

Balancing esports with other responsibilities:

  • Academic Priority: Grades first—no practice if failing classes
  • Schedule: Block time for homework, practice, sleep, free time
  • Limits: Set maximum gaming hours per day/week
  • Flexibility: Adjust during exam periods or busy seasons
  • Communication: Keep parents/guardians informed of commitments

Social Relationships

  • Maintain friendships outside of gaming
  • Spend time with family
  • Participate in non-gaming activities and hobbies
  • Face-to-face social interaction is important
  • Don't isolate yourself through excessive gaming

Avoiding Burnout

Burnout symptoms: exhaustion, loss of enjoyment, decreased performance, cynicism

Prevention Strategies:

  • Take Breaks: Rest days from competitive gaming
  • Variety: Play casual games or different titles occasionally
  • Set Limits: Don't grind excessively for marginal improvements
  • Remember Why: Reconnect with what made gaming fun
  • Off-Season: Take extended breaks between competitive seasons

Screen Time Management

Recommended Screen Time Limits:
  • Recreational + Educational: Max 2-3 hours per day for ages 13-15
  • Competitive Practice: Additional 1-2 hours for serious players
  • Tournament Days: Increased time acceptable, not everyday
  • Non-Gaming Screens: Count toward total (phone, TV, etc.)

Healthy Screen Habits

  • Use night mode/blue light filters in evening
  • Take regular breaks (Pomodoro technique: 25 min work, 5 min break)
  • Alternate gaming with other activities
  • Don't game immediately before bed
  • Track total screen time with apps/tools

Building Healthy Habits

Daily Routine Template

  • Morning: Wake up consistent time, breakfast, physical activity
  • School Day: Attend classes, complete homework before gaming
  • After School: Snack, 1 hour break, then practice
  • Practice Session: 2-3 hours with breaks, team coordination
  • Evening: Dinner with family, wind down without screens
  • Bedtime: Consistent time, 8+ hours before wake-up

Weekly Structure

  • Weekdays: Prioritize academics, moderate practice (1-2 hours)
  • Weekends: Longer practice sessions, tournaments, social time
  • Rest Day: At least one day per week with no competitive gaming
  • Variety: Different activities (sports, hobbies, friends, family)

Supporting Student-Athletes

For Parents/Guardians

  • Show interest in their esports participation
  • Set clear expectations and boundaries
  • Monitor for signs of unhealthy gaming
  • Encourage balance with other activities
  • Support healthy habits (sleep, nutrition, exercise)
  • Communicate with coaches about concerns

For Coaches

  • Model healthy behavior and life balance
  • Educate players about health risks and prevention
  • Build rest into practice schedules
  • Monitor for burnout and excessive practice
  • Prioritize player well-being over winning
  • Create supportive team culture
