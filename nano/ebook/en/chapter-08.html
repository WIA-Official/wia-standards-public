8.1 Twelve-month roadmap

Months 1-3 — Phase 1 Data Format

  • Inventory existing substance dossiers, ELN entries, and instrument captures.
  • Map every internal identifier to CAS / InChIKey / ChEBI / ISO/TS 80004.
  • Stand up the Iceberg + Polaris REST catalogue for substance records.
  • Run JSON-LD validation in CI; reject merges that introduce trivially-named substances or measurements without uncertainty.

Months 4-6 — Phase 2 API

  • Deploy pygeoapi or FastAPI with OpenAPI 3.1; STAC catalogue on stac-fastapi-pgstac.
  • Issue OAuth 2.1 + DPoP tokens; rotate JWKS every 90 days.
  • Activate dossier twin endpoints (FDA SPL, EMA eCTD, PMDA QbD, MFDS, ECHA IUCLID).

Months 7-9 — Phase 3 Protocol

  • Wire OPC UA Lab Devices CS to TEM, DLS, BET, ICP-MS instruments.
  • Translate OPC UA captures into MQTT 5 + Allotrope ADF stream.
  • Onboard exposure samplers (NIOSH NMAM 7300, NMAM 0500).
  • Turn on Merkle-root anchoring to WIA Chain PoA / BSC.

Months 10-12 — Phase 4 Integration

  • Connect to FDA / EMA / PMDA / MFDS / ECHA submission portals.
  • Wire EPCIS 2.0 supply-chain traceability for precursors → product.
  • Enable WIA-CLIMATE-022 ESG routing.
  • Publish public verification page with QR support.

8.2 Three best-practice patterns

Verifier-first design

Build the auditor traversal experience before you build the dashboard. If a regulator can walk from any dossier figure back to a primary instrument capture in three clicks, the rest of the system is correct by construction.

Source-of-truth single-write

Substance records are written exactly once — by the measurement upload path. Every other system (dossier twin, IFRS S2, ESG dashboard) reads, never re-keys.

Safety-by-design as compile-time check

Reject builds that produce substance records missing OECD TG 318 dispersion stability or NIOSH NMAM exposure data once a substance enters production. CI gating is cheaper than a recall.

8.3 Case studies

Case 1 — Korean nanomedicine startup

  • Challenge: Pre-IND submission to FDA + parallel MFDS supplementary application; 2 different team workflows.
  • Solution: WIA-NANO-016 with the dossier-twin endpoint; one substance record, two regulator submissions.
  • Result: Pre-IND on time, MFDS supplementary 6 weeks ahead of schedule, audit-trail traversal < 60 s for any figure.

Case 2 — European nanosafety consortium

  • Challenge: 14 labs in NanoCommons round-robin, irreproducible TiO₂ size results.
  • Solution: Allotrope ADF + ISO/TS 80004 + WIA-NANO measurement schema with mandatory uncertainty + n.
  • Result: Cross-lab D₅₀ agreement within 4 nm where method is the same; documented method spread for cross-method differences.

Case 3 — Korean ICT/nano researcher

  • Challenge: KIMS pilot line A produces ZnO nanoparticles for industrial sponsors with EHS reporting requirements.
  • Solution: NIOSH NMAM 7300 + ISO/TR 27628 sampling fed via MQTT 5; daily TWA dashboard.
  • Result: Workplace exposure consistently < 10 % of NIOSH RNSH for 18 months; zero exposure incidents.

Certification process

  1. Application — system architecture, substance inventory, audit reports.
  2. Conformance suite — automated execution at conformance.wia-nano.org.
  3. Verifier interview — independent review of CI gates, traversal UX, safety-by-design checks.
  4. Penetration test — OWASP ASVS L2 (Bronze) or L3 (Silver/Gold).
  5. Issuance — WIA badge + W3C VC + verify URL.
  6. Maintenance — annual surveillance + 3-yearly recertification.

Closing

Nanotechnology sits at the boundary where claims about scale, safety, and effect intersect. WIA-NANO-016 is the wire format that lets a measurement made today be reproduced by a stranger tomorrow, that lets a regulator confirm a marketing claim within minutes instead of months, that lets a worker on a pilot line know their workplace meets exposure limits before they walk in. Build it carefully, build it generously, build it for the reader you will never meet.
