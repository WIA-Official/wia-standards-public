4.2 ISO/TS 80004 vocabulary

The ISO/TS 80004 series provides the canonical vocabulary. Parts most relevant to WIA-NANO-016:

  • Part 1 (core terms) — definitions of nano-object, nanoparticle, nanofibre, nanoplate, nanostructured material.
  • Part 2 (nano-objects) — geometry-based classification.
  • Part 3 (carbon nano-objects) — CNT, graphene, fullerenes.
  • Part 4 (nanostructured materials) — nanocomposites, nanoporous materials.
  • Part 6 (characterisation) — vocabulary used by the measurement series.
  • Part 7 (medical, health, personal care) — nanomedicine vocabulary.
  • Part 11 (nanolayers, nanocoatings) — surface engineering.
  • Part 13 (graphene-related materials) — aligned with IUPAC 2013.

4.6 IUPAC nomenclature for graphene-related materials

Graphene-related materials use IUPAC's 2013 recommendations as the controlled vocabulary. Key terms — pristine graphene, few-layer graphene (FLG, 2-10 layers), reduced graphene oxide (rGO), graphene oxide (GO) — are not interchangeable; the standard rejects ambiguous "graphene" labels at validation time.

4.8 Validation rules

  • Every physicalProperties.* field MUST cite the measurement method (ISO TG number).
  • Every measurement MUST report an uncertainty (k=1 or k=2) and the sample-count n.
  • Substance names MUST resolve to a CAS or InChIKey; trivial names alone are rejected.
  • Every nano-form intended for REACH submission MUST carry the EU 2018/1881 nano-form attributes (size distribution, shape, surface chemistry, functionalisation).
  • Signed payloads MUST canonicalise per RFC 8785 before signature verification.

Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  1. Substance identity = CAS + InChIKey + ChEBI + WIA URI; trivial names alone are rejected.
  2. ISO/TS 80004 is the controlled vocabulary; ISO measurement series provide the methods.
  3. The substance record is JSON-LD with pinned @context; primary evidence is Allotrope ADF.
  4. OECD TG 318/412/413 endpoints are first-class fields, not after-thoughts.
  5. Every measurement MUST cite method, instrument, uncertainty, and n.
