Chapter 7: Phase 4 — Integration

Chapter overview: What turns a substance record into an outcome — FDA / EMA / PMDA / MFDS / REACH dossier integration, EPCIS 2.0 supply-chain traceability, ESG reporting through WIA-CLIMATE-022, DNA data storage interoperability, and the public verification page that closes the loop with consumers.

7.1 FDA Nanotechnology Task Force pathway

FDA's NTF guidance asks whether a regulated product involves the application of nanotechnology. WIA-NANO-016 generates SPL XML modules with the four FDA NTF determination factors:

  1. Engineered material has at least one external dimension or internal structure 1–100 nm.
  2. Material exhibits dimensions outside the nano range up to 1 µm where properties are attributable to nanoscale.
  3. Material is engineered to exhibit specific properties.
  4. Material is a deliberate component of the regulated product.

7.2 EU REACH 2018/1881 nano-form registration

Annex VI of REACH (as amended by Regulation 2018/1881) requires nano-form-specific information for size distribution, shape, surface chemistry, surface treatment, specific surface area, and crystallinity. The standard generates an IUCLID-compatible JSON-LD twin signed with the Lead Registrant's key.

7.6 OECD WPMN dossier compilation

Voluntary OECD WPMN sponsorship-programme dossiers compile across institutions. The standard pulls a substance's TG-aligned endpoint records into a single WPMN dossier signed with the Lead Sponsor's key.

7.7 ESG reporting via WIA-CLIMATE-022

Nanomaterial production has a carbon and energy footprint reported under IFRS S2 / ESRS E1. The WIA-CLIMATE-022 binding routes Scope 1/2/3 emissions for each batch to the corporate disclosure twin. ISO 14083:2023 covers transport-stage emissions.

Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  1. FDA NTF determination is a first-class field in the substance record, not a downstream document.
  2. EU REACH 2018/1881 nano-form registration uses an IUCLID-compatible JSON-LD twin.
  3. Supply-chain transformations from precursor to product are EPCIS 2.0 events.
  4. DNA data storage uses a vendor-neutral API for archive, redundancy, and read-back.
  5. Public verification ties the product QR to a verifiable, blockchain-anchored substance summary.
