Chapter 2: Current Challenges in Nanotechnology Data

Chapter overview: Six unsolved frictions — characterisation irreproducibility, regulatory fragmentation, nanoEHS data silos, dossier-to-bench traceability gaps, IP claims that outrun safety evidence, and the verification gap that separates marketing claims from a verified TEM record — define the problem space WIA-NANO-016 addresses.

2.1 Characterisation irreproducibility

The 2019 NanoCommons round-robin study circulated identical TiO₂ samples to 14 European laboratories. Mean particle-size results spanned 18 nm to 47 nm depending on technique (TEM, DLS, BET). None of the labs was wrong; their methods measured different physical quantities. Without a wire format that captures method, instrument, sample-preparation protocol, and uncertainty model alongside the number, there is no way to align them.

2.2 Regulatory fragmentation

A nanomedicine sponsor pursuing a global launch reconciles at least seven dossier formats:

  • FDA — IND/NDA via eCTD with nanotechnology-specific addenda per the FDA NTF guidance.
  • EMA — centralised procedure with reflection paper on nanomedicines (CHMP/SWP).
  • PMDA — quality-by-design submission with QbD nano addenda.
  • MFDS (Korea) — supplementary application referencing 의약품 등의 안전에 관한 규칙.
  • NMPA (China) — Chemistry, Manufacturing and Controls Drug Master File supplements.
  • EU REACH — nano-form registration under Annex VI as amended by 2018/1881.
  • OECD WPMN — voluntary dossier for sponsorship programme materials.

2.3 nanoEHS data silos

Worker exposure data (NIOSH NMAM 7300/0500), environmental release studies (OECD TG 318), and consumer-product safety surveys (Nanosafety Cluster) live in three separate ecosystems. The 2024 ECHA Nanomaterial Observatory found that 58 % of registered nano-forms had at least one missing OECD endpoint and that cross-referencing between exposure data and toxicology was effectively manual.

2.4 The dossier-to-bench traceability gap

An FDA reviewer who needs to confirm "the 30 nm Au-PEG nanoshell distribution shown on page 47 of section 3.2.S.3.1" must traverse from the dossier PDF back to a primary TEM micrograph. Today, that traversal happens by emailing the principal investigator. WIA-NANO-016 binds every dossier figure to a content-addressed evidence URI — one click away.

2.5 IP claims outrun safety evidence

The 2025 USPTO Nano-Patents Watch Report highlighted that > 4 200 nanotechnology patents in 2024 cited "biocompatible" or "non-toxic" without a corresponding ISO 10993-aligned test record. Voluntary linking of patent claims to OECD-aligned safety data is a future regulator-priority area; the standard treats it as an MVP-day-one feature.

2.6 The verification gap

Marketing copy says "20 nm silver nanoparticles". The actual datasheet says "20 ± 12 nm by BET-equivalent diameter, n = 1 measurement, 2017". A consumer or auditor has no way to challenge the gap without a wire-format that carries instrument, method, uncertainty, and traceable evidence chain. WIA-NANO-016 makes the absence of those fields a contract violation.

Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  1. One sample, four techniques, four numbers — and all of them right. The wire format must carry the method, not just the number.
  2. Cross-jurisdiction nanomedicine dossiers cost time and money that machine-renderable JSON-LD can save.
  3. nanoEHS data is fragmented across exposure, environmental, and consumer-safety silos.
  4. Bench-to-dossier traversal must be one click; today it is one email.
  5. Marketing nano-claims must carry verifiable evidence, not adjectives.
