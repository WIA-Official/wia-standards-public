Chapter 1: Introduction to the WIA Nanotechnology Standard

Chapter overview: WIA-NANO-016 is an executable specification that binds ISO/TC 229 vocabulary, IUPAC nomenclature, OECD WPMN test guidelines, FDA / EMA regulatory paths, and Korean nano R&D ecosystem references into one wire format. This chapter explains why such a standard is needed, what it covers, and how to read the rest of the book.

1.1 Why a nano-scale standard?

A "nanomaterial" is most commonly defined as a material with at least one external dimension or internal structure on the scale 1–100 nm (ISO/TS 80004-1; EU Recommendation 2022/C 229/01). At that scale, properties — surface area, reactivity, electronic band structure, biological transport — change in ways that bulk-material specifications cannot describe. Without a standard wire form for who measured what at which scale with which instrument, two laboratories cannot reproduce each other's results, regulators cannot make findings, and clinicians cannot trust the safety dossiers.

WIA-NANO-016 is the engineering response to that gap. It does not invent characterisation methods or toxicology endpoints — those are owned by ISO, IUPAC, OECD, FDA, EMA, and PMDA. It is the interoperability layer that lets a TEM micrograph in Daejeon, an OECD TG 412 inhalation study in Cambridge, an FDA pre-IND nanomedicine submission in Silver Spring, and a REACH nano-form registration in Helsinki all reference the same identifiers, the same units, and the same traceable evidence chain.

1.2 Five things this standard makes possible

  1. Reproducible characterisation. A particle's size distribution, shape, surface chemistry, and zeta potential travel as a single signed JSON-LD record consumable by any analyst.
  2. Cross-jurisdiction regulatory submission. One dossier renders into FDA pre-IND, EMA centralised, PMDA QbD, and Korean MFDS supplementary applications.
  3. nanoEHS surveillance. Worker-exposure measurements (NIOSH NMAM 7300 / 0500) and environmental release data (OECD WPMN ENV/CHEM/NANO) are logged in a single time-series.
  4. DNA data storage tracking. Synthesis, sequencing, and decoding of DNA-encoded archives are recorded as deterministic, verifiable events.
  5. Supply-chain traceability. From precursor synthesis to product disposal, every transformation is signed with EdDSA-JCS-2022 over canonicalised JSON.

1.4 The four-phase implementation arc

Phase 1 — Data Format     ISO/TS 80004 vocab, IUPAC nomenclature, JSON-LD with units (UCUM), Allotrope ADF
Phase 2 — API Interface   OpenAPI 3.1 + AsyncAPI 3.0, OAuth 2.1 + DPoP, FAIR principles
Phase 3 — Protocol        Instrument integration (TEM, AFM, DLS, ICP-MS), MQTT 5 + OPC UA
Phase 4 — Integration     FDA / EMA / PMDA / MFDS / REACH dossier generation, supply-chain traceability

1.5 Reference authorities you will see throughout the book

  • ISO/TC 229 — the international technical committee for nanotechnologies; publishes the ISO/TS 80004 vocabulary parts and the ISO 19xxx measurement series.
  • IUPAC — nomenclature for nanostructured materials, including 2013 recommendations on graphene-related materials.
  • OECD WPMN — Working Party on Manufactured Nanomaterials; sponsors the nanosafety dossiers (TG 318 dispersion stability, TG 412 inhalation, TG 413 sub-chronic).
  • FDA Nanotechnology Task Force — guidance on whether an FDA-regulated product involves the application of nanotechnology (2014, updated 2022).
  • EU REACH 2018/1881 — annex amendments requiring registration of nano forms.
  • Korean ecosystem — KIMS (한국재료연구원), KIST 나노융합기술원, 한국나노기술원 NSP-Net, 나노안전성기술센터 (KCN-CC), and the 나노기술개발 촉진법 (Nanotechnology Development Promotion Act, 2002).

1.6 What this book is, and is not

This is a practitioner's reference. Read sequentially, it walks an engineering team from "what is the standard?" to "we just submitted our first FDA pre-IND that traces back to instrument-level characterisation evidence." It is not an introduction to nanoscience — for that, the IUPAC recommendations and the ISO/TS 80004 series remain authoritative. It is not a substitute for OECD test guidelines or FDA regulatory advice — those remain the sources of truth. WIA-NANO-016 makes those frameworks executable; their substantive content still applies.

Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  1. Nano-scale claims outpace the data systems that should make them verifiable; WIA-NANO-016 closes that gap.
  2. The standard binds ISO/TC 229 vocabulary, IUPAC nomenclature, OECD WPMN, FDA NTF, EMA, MFDS, REACH 2018/1881, and Korean KIMS/KIST sources into one wire format.
  3. Six domains — vocabulary, characterisation, toxicology, dossiers, worker safety, lifecycle — share one identifier and unit system.
  4. Four phases (Data, API, Protocol, Integration) sequence implementation from bytes to dossier.
  5. The standard is the interoperability layer; ISO measurement methods and regulator guidance remain the substantive authorities.
