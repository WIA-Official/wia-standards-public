Chapter 4: Special Economic Zones

In this chapter: We explore the design, implementation, and management of special economic zones (SEZs), including industrial complexes, free trade zones, technology parks, and cross-border economic cooperation areas that serve as engines of inter-Korean integration.

4.1 SEZ Framework and Types

Special Economic Zones are geographically delimited areas where economic laws differ from the rest of the country to encourage investment, trade, and economic activity. These zones serve as testing grounds for economic integration and provide controlled environments for cross-border cooperation.

Types of Economic Zones

  • Industrial Complexes: Manufacturing-focused zones with factories and production facilities
  • Free Trade Zones: Areas for warehousing, distribution, and re-export with minimal customs intervention
  • Technology Parks: Innovation hubs for R&D, high-tech manufacturing, and technology companies
  • Export Processing Zones: Specialized zones for export-oriented manufacturing
  • Tourism Development Zones: Areas designated for tourism infrastructure and services
  • Comprehensive SEZs: Multi-purpose zones combining industrial, commercial, and residential functions

4.2 Kaesong Industrial Complex: Case Study

The Kaesong Industrial Complex (KIC) stands as the most significant example of inter-Korean economic cooperation, providing valuable lessons for future SEZ development.

Kaesong Industrial Complex Overview

  • Location: 10km north of DMZ, 70km from Seoul
  • Size: 66 square kilometers (planned), 3.3 sq km developed
  • Operational Period: 2004-2016
  • Peak Performance: 125 South Korean companies, 54,000 North Korean workers
  • Total Production: Over $3.2 billion in cumulative output
  • Products: Textiles, electronics, machinery, watches, chemical products

Key Success Factors

  • Strategic location near Seoul with good access
  • Cost-effective North Korean labor force
  • South Korean management and technology
  • Preferential tariff treatment and market access
  • Stable operating environment within the zone
  • Government support and policy backing

Challenges and Lessons

  • Vulnerability to political tensions and geopolitical factors
  • Communication and logistics constraints
  • Limited expansion beyond initial development phase
  • Need for stronger legal frameworks and dispute resolution
  • Importance of international acceptance and sanctions compliance

4.3 SEZ Establishment Process

Creating a successful SEZ requires careful planning, substantial infrastructure investment, and comprehensive legal frameworks.

Development Phases

  1. Planning and Feasibility (6-12 months):
    • Site selection based on strategic criteria
    • Economic and technical feasibility studies
    • Environmental impact assessment
    • Investment requirements calculation
  2. Legal Framework (3-6 months):
    • Special legislation and regulations
    • Tax and incentive policies
    • Labor and employment rules
    • Customs and trade procedures
  3. Infrastructure Development (12-24 months):
    • Land preparation and site development
    • Roads, utilities, telecommunications
    • Administrative buildings and facilities
    • Housing and amenities for workers
  4. Marketing and Tenant Recruitment (Ongoing):
    • Investment promotion campaigns
    • Investor roadshows and presentations
    • One-stop-shop services for investors
    • Streamlined approval processes

4.4 SEZ Governance and Management

Effective governance structures ensure smooth operations, investor confidence, and sustainable development.

Joint Management Committee Structure

Recommended governance model for inter-Korean SEZs:

  • Co-Chairpersons: One from each side for balanced leadership
  • Management Board: Equal representation from North and South Korea
  • Administrative Office: Day-to-day operations and investor services
  • Technical Committees: Infrastructure, customs, labor, environment
  • Advisory Council: Industry experts and investor representatives

Key Management Functions

  • Investment Facilitation: One-stop services for licensing, permits, approvals
  • Infrastructure Maintenance: Utilities, roads, facilities management
  • Customs Administration: Import/export processing, duty collection
  • Labor Management: Worker recruitment, training, welfare services
  • Security and Safety: Access control, emergency response, safety protocols
  • Dispute Resolution: Mediation and arbitration services

4.5 Infrastructure and Utilities

World-class infrastructure is essential for SEZ competitiveness and investor attraction.

Essential Infrastructure Components

Core Infrastructure Requirements

  • Transportation:
    • All-weather roads connecting to major highways
    • Rail connections to ports and markets
    • Truck and cargo handling facilities
    • Proximity to international airport (< 2 hours)
  • Utilities:
    • Reliable 24/7 electricity supply (backup generators)
    • Clean water supply and wastewater treatment
    • Natural gas for industrial processes
    • District heating/cooling systems
  • Telecommunications:
    • High-speed fiber optic internet connectivity
    • International calling and data services
    • Mobile network coverage
    • Data centers and cloud services
  • Support Facilities:
    • Banks and financial services
    • Restaurants, shops, medical clinics
    • Housing for managers and workers
    • Recreation and cultural facilities

4.6 SEZ Incentives and Benefits

Competitive incentive packages are crucial for attracting investors to SEZs and ensuring their success.

Comprehensive Incentive Package for SEZ Investors:

• Tax Incentives: 5-10 year corporate income tax holidays, reduced rates thereafter

• Customs Benefits: Duty-free import of raw materials, equipment, and components

• Land Leases: Long-term land leases (50 years) at preferential rates

• Profit Repatriation: 100% profit repatriation rights without restrictions

• Labor Flexibility: Streamlined labor regulations and hiring procedures

• Regulatory Fast-Track: Expedited approvals and one-stop services

• Infrastructure Subsidies: Government co-funding for specialized infrastructure
