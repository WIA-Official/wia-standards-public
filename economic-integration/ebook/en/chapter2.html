Chapter 2: Trade Facilitation Framework

In this chapter: We examine the comprehensive framework for facilitating inter-Korean trade, including customs automation, logistics coordination, payment systems, and regulatory compliance mechanisms that enable smooth cross-border commerce.

2.1 Trade Documentation Standards

Standardized trade documentation is essential for efficient cross-border commerce. The WIA-UNI-004 framework defines clear formats for all trade-related documents, ensuring compatibility and reducing processing time.

Core Trade Documents

  • Commercial Invoice: Detailed description of goods, pricing, and terms of sale
  • Packing List: Comprehensive inventory of shipment contents
  • Certificate of Origin: Documentation proving goods' manufacturing location
  • Customs Declaration: Official statement of goods for customs clearance
  • Bill of Lading: Receipt and contract for goods transportation
  • Insurance Certificate: Proof of cargo insurance coverage

2.2 Customs Automation

Automated customs processing significantly reduces clearance time and errors. The framework includes digital systems for declaration submission, risk assessment, and clearance approval.

Customs Processing Workflow

  1. Pre-arrival Declaration: Submit customs documents electronically before goods arrive
  2. Risk Assessment: Automated system evaluates shipment risk level
  3. Green Channel Processing: Low-risk shipments cleared automatically within hours
  4. Inspection (if required): Selective physical inspection for flagged items
  5. Duty Calculation: Automatic calculation of applicable tariffs and taxes
  6. Payment Processing: Digital payment of customs duties
  7. Release Authorization: Electronic clearance and release notification

Special Economic Zone Benefits

Goods traded within designated SEZs enjoy preferential treatment:

  • Zero or reduced tariff rates
  • Simplified customs procedures
  • 24/7 customs clearance availability
  • Expedited processing (< 4 hours for most shipments)
  • Single window clearance system

2.3 Logistics Coordination

Efficient logistics are critical for trade success. The framework provides standards for transportation, warehousing, and supply chain visibility.

Transportation Modes

  • Road Transport: Trucks via designated border crossing points (Kaesong, Kumgang routes)
  • Rail Transport: Inter-Korean railway connections for bulk cargo
  • Maritime: Port-to-port shipping via designated harbors
  • Air Cargo: High-value, time-sensitive goods via approved airports

2.4 Payment and Settlement Systems

Secure and efficient payment mechanisms are essential for trade. The framework supports multiple payment methods while ensuring compliance with international regulations.

Supported Payment Methods

  • Letter of Credit (LC): Guaranteed payment through banking channels
  • Documentary Collection: Bank-mediated document exchange
  • Bank Transfer: Direct wire transfers for established relationships
  • Trade Finance: Short-term financing for working capital
  • Escrow Services: Third-party held funds for high-value transactions

Currency Handling

Multi-Currency Support

The framework supports transactions in multiple currencies:

  • Primary: USD, EUR, CNY (Chinese Yuan)
  • Secondary: KRW (South Korean Won), JPY (Japanese Yen)
  • Settlement: Designated settlement banks handle FX conversion
  • Rate Transparency: Daily published exchange rates

2.5 Product Categories and Standards

Clear product categorization ensures proper handling, inspection, and regulatory compliance.

Priority Trade Categories

  1. Raw Materials: Minerals, metals, timber, agricultural inputs
  2. Manufactured Goods: Electronics, textiles, machinery, consumer products
  3. Agricultural Products: Food items, crops, processed foods
  4. Technology & Equipment: Industrial machinery, IT equipment, tools
  5. Consumer Goods: Household items, clothing, personal care products

2.6 Compliance and Quality Control

All traded goods must meet quality and safety standards, with proper certification and testing protocols.

Quality Assurance Framework:

• Pre-shipment inspection by certified agencies

• Quality certificates for regulated products

• Sanitary and phytosanitary certifications for food/agriculture

• Safety compliance for electronics and machinery

• Environmental compliance certifications
