Chapter 6: Regulatory Reporting

Overview of Regulatory Reporting

Regulatory reporting is the process of submitting required information to regulatory authorities on specified schedules. RegTech automation transforms this from a manual, error-prone process to a streamlined, accurate, and timely operation.

Types of Regulatory Reports

AML/CFT Reports

Suspicious Activity Reports (SAR)

Filed when financial institutions detect transactions that may involve money laundering, terrorist financing, or other financial crimes.

Jurisdiction Report Name Filing Deadline
United States FinCEN SAR 30 days from detection
United Kingdom Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) As soon as possible
European Union Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) Varies by member state
Australia Suspicious Matter Report (SMR) 3 business days

Currency Transaction Reports (CTR)

Required for cash transactions exceeding specified thresholds (e.g., $10,000 in the US).

Large Cash Transaction Reports

Various jurisdictions require reporting of significant cash transactions, with different thresholds and timelines.

Prudential Reports

Capital Adequacy Reports

  • Basel III regulatory capital calculations
  • Risk-weighted assets (RWA) reporting
  • Leverage ratio calculations
  • Capital planning and stress testing results

Liquidity Reports

  • Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)
  • Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)
  • Cash flow projections and stress scenarios

Transaction Reports

MiFID II/MiFIR Transaction Reporting

All financial instruments trades must be reported to regulators within T+1 (one business day after trade).

Dodd-Frank Swap Reporting

Derivatives transactions must be reported to swap data repositories.

Consumer Protection Reports

  • Complaints handling and resolution
  • Product sales data and suitability assessments
  • Fair lending statistics (HMDA in US)

Operational Reports

  • Cyber incident reporting
  • System outages and business disruptions
  • Outsourcing and third-party arrangements

Automated Report Generation

Data Collection and Aggregation

RegTech platforms automatically gather data from multiple source systems, standardize formats, and aggregate for reporting.

Process Flow

  1. Identify data requirements for each report type
  2. Map source systems and data fields
  3. Extract data from systems (real-time or batch)
  4. Transform and standardize data formats
  5. Validate data quality and completeness
  6. Aggregate data according to reporting rules
  7. Generate report in required format

Report Templates and Formats

Regulatory reports must comply with specific formats and schemas. RegTech systems maintain libraries of report templates that are automatically updated when regulators change requirements.

Common Formats

  • XML: MiFID II ARM reports, EMIR derivatives reporting
  • CSV: Simple data exports, certain regulatory filings
  • PDF: Narrative reports, board certifications
  • XBRL: Financial statements, regulatory filings
  • JSON: Modern API-based reporting systems

Validation and Quality Checks

Before submission, reports undergo extensive validation to ensure accuracy and completeness.

Validation Layers

  1. Schema Validation: Report structure matches regulatory schema
  2. Business Rule Validation: Data passes logical consistency checks
  3. Cross-field Validation: Related fields are consistent
  4. Historical Validation: Comparison with previous reports for anomalies
  5. Regulatory Validation: Pre-submission check against regulator's validation rules

Electronic Filing Systems

Regulatory Portals

Most regulators provide electronic filing systems for report submission.

Major Filing Systems

  • FinCEN BSA E-Filing System: US AML/CTF reports
  • FCA RegData: UK regulatory data submissions
  • EBA EUCLID: European banking authority reporting
  • MAS MASNET: Singapore regulatory submissions

API-Based Submission

Modern regulators offer APIs for automated report submission, enabling real-time validation and confirmation.

Submission Workflows

  1. Generate report in required format
  2. Internal approval workflow (if required)
  3. Encrypt report for transmission
  4. Submit to regulatory portal/API
  5. Receive submission confirmation
  6. Process any validation errors or warnings
  7. Resubmit corrected report if necessary
  8. Store confirmation and audit trail

Report Reconciliation and Amendments

Reconciliation

Comparing submitted reports with source data to ensure accuracy and identify discrepancies.

Amendments

When errors are discovered in submitted reports, amendments must be filed promptly.

Amendment Process

  • Identify incorrect report and error details
  • Generate corrected report marking as amendment
  • Reference original report submission
  • Submit amendment through regulatory portal
  • Maintain documentation of correction and reason

Audit Trail and Documentation

Comprehensive Logging

All steps in report generation and submission must be logged for regulatory examination.

What to Log

  • Data extraction timestamps and sources
  • Transformations and calculations applied
  • Validation results and any warnings
  • User actions and approvals
  • Submission timestamps and confirmations
  • Any errors and remediation actions

Retention Requirements

Regulatory reports and supporting documentation must be retained for specified periods (typically 5-7 years).

Reporting Calendar Management

Automated Scheduling

RegTech systems maintain calendars of all reporting obligations and automatically trigger report generation processes.

Calendar Features

  • Multi-jurisdiction reporting deadline tracking
  • Automatic adjustment for holidays and weekends
  • Alerts and notifications for upcoming deadlines
  • Workflow kickoff based on lead times
  • Dashboard view of all pending reports

Challenges and Best Practices

Common Challenges

  • Regulatory Changes: Frequent updates to reporting requirements and formats
  • Data Quality: Incomplete or inaccurate source data
  • Tight Deadlines: Limited time between data cut-off and submission deadline
  • Complex Rules: Intricate calculation and validation rules
  • Multiple Jurisdictions: Different requirements across geographies

Best Practices

  • Maintain centralized reporting calendar across all jurisdictions
  • Implement robust data quality framework
  • Build buffer time into reporting workflows
  • Regular testing of report generation processes
  • Maintain relationships with regulatory authorities
  • Stay informed on regulatory changes through automated monitoring
  • Conduct regular reconciliation between systems and reports
  • Document all processes and maintain runbooks
Success Metric: Leading institutions achieve 99%+ on-time filing rates with zero critical errors through automated RegTech reporting systems, compared to 85-90% with manual processes.

Future of Regulatory Reporting

  • Real-Time Reporting: Moving from periodic to continuous reporting
  • Standardization: Global harmonization of reporting formats and requirements
  • API-First: All regulatory data exchange via APIs rather than file uploads
  • Machine-Readable Regulations: Regulations published in machine-executable format
  • Blockchain Integration: Immutable audit trails and shared regulatory ledgers
