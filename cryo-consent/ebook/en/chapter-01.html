1.1 WHY CONSENT STANDARDS MATTER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Cryopreservation is unlike any other medical decision.
You are making choices that may not be acted upon for decades or centuries.

[ID] UNIQUE CHALLENGES:

    TIME SCALE          -> Decisions made today affect revival 50-500 years later
    UNCERTAINTY         -> Technology may or may not succeed
    IRREVERSIBILITY     -> Once preserved, options become limited
    FAMILY IMPACT       -> Affects spouse, children, future generations
    LEGAL AMBIGUITY     -> Laws may change dramatically over time

[!] WITHOUT PROPER CONSENT:
    - Revival decisions become contested
    - Family disputes may prevent revival
    - Legal challenges delay or block procedures
    - Subject's wishes may be ignored


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.2 CORE PRINCIPLES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[ID] PRINCIPLE 1: INFORMED UNDERSTANDING

    The subject must genuinely understand:
    - What cryopreservation involves
    - All known risks and limitations
    - That revival is NOT guaranteed
    - Financial implications
    - Impact on family relationships

[ID] PRINCIPLE 2: VOLUNTARY DECISION

    The decision must be:
    - Made without coercion
    - Free from undue influence
    - Not made under duress
    - Subject to reconsideration

[ID] PRINCIPLE 3: DOCUMENTED WISHES

    Consent must clearly specify:
    - Conditions for revival
    - Who can make decisions
    - Alternative directives
    - Withdrawal conditions

[ID] PRINCIPLE 4: REVOCABILITY

    Subject retains the right to:
    - Withdraw consent before preservation
    - Modify conditions through amendments
    - Refuse revival after conditions are met
    - Delegate decisions to guardians


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.3 DOCUMENT LIFECYCLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[ID] CONSENT DOCUMENT STATES:

    DRAFT
      |
      v
    PENDING_SIGNATURES
      |
      v
    PENDING_NOTARIZATION
      |
      v
    ACTIVE ---------> SUSPENDED (if issues arise)
      |                   |
      |                   v
      |               ACTIVE (when resolved)
      |
      v
    EXECUTED (preservation begins)
      |
      +-------------> WITHDRAWN (if subject withdraws)
      |
      v
    COMPLETED (revival completed or terminated)

[ID] STATUS DEFINITIONS:

    DRAFT                -> Initial creation, not yet signed
    PENDING_SIGNATURES   -> Awaiting required signatures
    PENDING_NOTARIZATION -> Signed, awaiting notary
    ACTIVE               -> Fully executed, legally binding
    SUSPENDED            -> Temporarily on hold
    EXECUTED             -> Preservation has begun
    WITHDRAWN            -> Subject or guardians withdrew
    COMPLETED            -> Revival done or preservation ended


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.4 LEGAL FRAMEWORK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[ID] CONSENT REQUIREMENTS:

    +----------------------------------------------------------+
    | Requirement               | Status     | Notes           |
    +----------------------------------------------------------+
    | Subject mental capacity   | REQUIRED   | Assessment doc  |
    | Subject signature         | REQUIRED   | Digital or wet  |
    | Two witnesses             | REQUIRED   | Non-family      |
    | Notarization              | REQUIRED   | Before active   |
    | Spouse consent            | RECOMMENDED| If married      |
    | Legal counsel review      | RECOMMENDED| Complex cases   |
    +----------------------------------------------------------+

[ID] JURISDICTIONAL CONSIDERATIONS:

    - Consent validity varies by jurisdiction
    - Some countries may not recognize cryopreservation
    - Multi-jurisdictional protections recommended
    - Consider future legal changes

[i] LEGAL COUNSEL:
    We strongly recommend legal consultation before finalizing
    consent documents, especially for:
    - International subjects
    - Complex family situations
    - Significant assets
    - Research consent provisions
