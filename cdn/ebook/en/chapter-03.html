Standard Compliance Requirements

The WIA-CDN standard establishes comprehensive compliance requirements that ensure implementations meet minimum quality thresholds while enabling interoperability across diverse CDN solutions. Compliance encompasses technical specifications, operational procedures, security protocols, and documentation requirements. Organizations seeking certification must demonstrate conformance across all requirement categories through rigorous testing and evaluation procedures. The standard defines three compliance levels - Bronze, Silver, and Gold - each with progressively stricter requirements and corresponding benefits. Bronze certification requires 80% conformance to mandatory requirements, Silver demands 95% conformance plus implementation of recommended practices, while Gold certification requires 100% conformance across all requirements including advanced optional features.

Technical compliance testing evaluates CDN implementations against specific performance benchmarks, security requirements, and functional specifications. The WIA-CDN standard mandates testing across multiple dimensions including latency measurements from diverse geographic locations, throughput under various load conditions, cache hit ratio optimization, and correct implementation of security controls. Testing must be performed by accredited third-party laboratories using standardized test harnesses and methodologies defined by the WIA organization. Implementations must demonstrate consistent performance across a minimum of 50 distinct test scenarios covering normal operations, edge cases, and failure conditions. The standard requires annual recertification to ensure ongoing compliance as infrastructure evolves and new capabilities are deployed.

Operational compliance extends beyond technical specifications to encompass service level agreements, incident response procedures, and customer support capabilities. The WIA-CDN standard defines minimum SLA commitments for availability (99.9% for Bronze, 99.95% for Silver, 99.99% for Gold), performance (median latency under 100ms globally), and support response times (4 hours for critical issues, 24 hours for high-priority issues). Implementations must maintain comprehensive audit logs of all administrative actions, configuration changes, and security events with retention periods of at least 180 days. The standard mandates documented incident response procedures with defined escalation paths, mean-time-to-resolution targets, and post-incident review processes for continuous improvement.

Certification Process

Achieving WIA-CDN certification requires organizations to complete a multi-phase evaluation process that thoroughly assesses all aspects of their implementation. The process begins with self-assessment using comprehensive checklists and automated testing tools provided by the WIA organization. Organizations must document their architecture, configurations, and operational procedures in standardized formats that facilitate objective evaluation. Following successful self-assessment, applicants engage with accredited certification bodies that conduct independent verification through on-site inspections, technical testing, and documentation review. The certification process typically requires 3-6 months depending on implementation complexity and organizational readiness.
