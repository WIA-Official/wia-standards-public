7.1 Clean Room Fundamentals

Semiconductor manufacturing requires the cleanest manufacturing environment on Earth. A single particle smaller than the smallest feature can cause a catastrophic defect, making contamination control essential for yield. Modern fabs operate at cleanliness levels where a cubic meter of air contains fewer particles than a cubic meter of outer space.

Clean Room Classification

ISO Class US FED STD Particles/m3 (>=0.1um) Particles/m3 (>=0.5um) Application
ISO 1 Class 1 10 - Leading-edge lithography
ISO 2 Class 10 100 - Critical process bays
ISO 3 Class 100 1,000 8 Main fab floor

Contamination Sources

75% From People
15% From Equipment
5% From Materials
5% From Facility

Human-Generated Contamination

People are the largest source of particles in clean rooms. A typical person generates:

  • Standing Still: 100,000 particles/minute (>=0.3um)
  • Walking: 5,000,000 particles/minute
  • Running: 30,000,000 particles/minute
  • Sources: Skin flakes, hair, clothing fibers, cosmetics

7.2 Air Filtration Systems

Clean room air handling systems filter, temperature-control, and humidity-control the air while maintaining positive pressure to prevent contamination ingress.

HEPA and ULPA Filters

Filter Type Efficiency MPPS (um) Application
HEPA H13 99.95% 0.3 General clean room
HEPA H14 99.995% 0.3 Semiconductor fab
ULPA U15 99.9995% 0.12 Critical areas
ULPA U16 99.99995% 0.12 EUV lithography
ULPA U17 99.999995% 0.12 Special applications

Fan Filter Units (FFU)

FFU Specifications for Advanced Fabs

  • Filter Type: ULPA U15 or U16
  • Air Velocity: 0.3-0.5 m/s (unidirectional)
  • Coverage: 70-100% ceiling coverage
  • Energy: 0.5-1 W per CFM
  • Noise: Less than 55 dBA at 1m
  • Lifetime: 5-10 years typical

Airflow Patterns

  • Unidirectional (Laminar): Parallel streamlines from ceiling to floor, best for critical areas
  • Non-Unidirectional (Turbulent): Mixed airflow, adequate for lower-class areas
  • Mini-Environment: Local ISO 1-2 within tool enclosure
  • Air Changes: 300-600 per hour for ISO 3-4

Raised Floor and Return Air

Most fabs use raised access floors with perforated panels for return air:

  • Floor Height: 0.6-1.2m typical
  • Open Area: 15-25% perforation
  • Plenum Pressure: Slightly negative relative to clean room
  • Return Path: Through floor, walls, or dedicated chases

7.3 Chemical Contamination Control

Beyond particles, semiconductor manufacturing must control molecular contamination that can affect device performance or cause process excursions.

Airborne Molecular Contamination (AMC)

Category Examples Impact Limit (ppb)
Acids HF, HCl, HNO3, H2SO4 Corrosion, gate oxide degradation 0.1-1
Bases NH3, NMP, amines T-topping in lithography 0.1-1
Condensables Hydrocarbons, siloxanes Haze, organic residue 1-10
Dopants Boron, phosphorus compounds Cross-contamination 0.01

Chemical Filtration

AMC Control Strategies

  • Activated Carbon: Removes hydrocarbons and some acids/bases
  • Chemisorbent Media: Impregnated carbon for specific AMC
  • Ion Exchange: Removes ionic contaminants
  • Photocatalytic Oxidation: Destroys organic molecules
  • PTFE Membrane: Removes acids from process gases

AMC Monitoring

  • Real-Time Monitors: Cavity ring-down spectroscopy, ion mobility
  • Periodic Sampling: Impinger collection, GC-MS analysis
  • Witness Wafers: Surface contamination measurement
  • Detection Limits: ppt to ppb depending on species

7.4 Wafer Cleaning Equipment

Wafer cleaning removes particles, metals, organics, and native oxide from wafer surfaces. Modern fabs perform dozens of cleaning steps per wafer, making cleaning equipment among the most frequently used tools.

Cleaning Methods

Method Chemistry Target Contaminants Equipment Type
SC-1 NH4OH/H2O2/H2O Particles, organics Wet bench, single wafer
SC-2 HCl/H2O2/H2O Metal ions Wet bench, single wafer
DHF (Dilute HF) HF/H2O Native oxide, metals Single wafer spray
SPM (Piranha) H2SO4/H2O2 Heavy organics Wet bench
Ozone/DIW O3 dissolved in water Organics Single wafer

Single Wafer Cleaning

Single wafer processing provides better uniformity and process control than batch systems:

Single Wafer Cleaner Features

  • Spin Speed: Up to 3000 RPM for drying
  • Megasonic: 0.8-1.5 MHz for particle removal
  • Throughput: 100-200 wafers per hour
  • Chemical Consumption: 50-90% reduction vs. batch
  • Vendors: Screen (DNS), Lam (SEZ), TEL, SEMES

Advanced Cleaning Technologies

  • Cryogenic Cleaning: CO2 or Ar/N2 snow for damage-free particle removal
  • Dry Cleaning: Plasma or UV/ozone for organic removal
  • Supercritical CO2: For high aspect ratio cleaning
  • Brush Scrubbing: For backside cleaning

7.5 Ultra-Pure Water Systems

Semiconductor manufacturing consumes enormous quantities of ultra-pure water (UPW), with advanced fabs using 10-20 million liters per day. UPW quality directly impacts device yield.

UPW Specifications

Parameter Unit SEMI F63 (2019) Advanced Fab
Resistivity MΩ.cm >18.0 >18.2
TOC ppb <2 <1
Particles (>50nm) /mL <10 <1
Bacteria CFU/L <1 <0.1
Dissolved O2 ppb <2 <1
Silica ppb <0.5 <0.1

UPW Treatment Train

  • Pretreatment: Filtration, softening, activated carbon
  • Primary RO: Reverse osmosis membrane separation
  • Secondary RO/EDI: Electrodeionization for ionic purity
  • UV Oxidation: 185nm + 254nm for TOC destruction
  • Mixed Bed DI: Final ion exchange polishing
  • Point-of-Use Filter: 10-20nm membrane at tool

7.7 Automated Material Handling

Automated material handling systems (AMHS) transport wafers between process tools while maintaining contamination control.

FOUP (Front Opening Unified Pod)

  • Capacity: 25 wafers per FOUP
  • Material: Polycarbonate or PEEK
  • Purge: N2 purge capability for AMC control
  • Cleanliness: ISO 1 internal environment when sealed

AMHS Components

Component Function Throughput
OHT (Overhead Transport) Inter-bay transport 200-400 moves/hr/rail
Stocker FOUP storage 1000-10000 positions
Load Port Tool interface Variable
AGV/RGV Floor transport 20-50 moves/hr

N2 Purge Systems

Nitrogen purging prevents moisture and AMC accumulation during wafer storage:

  • In-FOUP Purge: During storage and transport
  • Stocker Purge: All FOUP positions purged
  • Flow Rate: 5-20 LPM per FOUP
  • Humidity Target: Less than 5% RH inside FOUP

7.8 WIA-SEMI-EQP-001 Facility Standards

The WIA-SEMI-EQP-001 standard defines integration requirements for facility equipment and environmental monitoring:

Data Interface Specification

// WIA-SEMI-EQP-001 Facility Equipment Interface
interface FacilityEquipment {
    // Equipment identification
    equipmentId: string;
    facilityType: 'HVAC' | 'UPW' | 'GAS' | 'AMHS' | 'MONITOR';
    
    // Environmental data
    getEnvironmentalData(): EnvironmentData;
    getAlarms(): AlarmStatus[];
    
    // Control interface
    setSetpoint(parameter: string, value: number): void;
    acknowledgeAlarm(alarmId: string): void;
}

interface EnvironmentData {
    temperature: number;
    humidity: number;
    pressure: number;
    particleCounts: ParticleData[];
    amcLevels: AMCData[];
    timestamp: Date;
}

interface CleaningEquipment {
    equipmentId: string;
    cleanType: 'WET' | 'DRY' | 'COMBINED';
    
    // Process monitoring
    getChemistryConcentration(): ChemistryData[];
    getParticleRemovalEfficiency(): number;
    getMegasonicPower(): number;
    
    // Maintenance
    getChemicalUsage(): ChemicalConsumption[];
    getFilterStatus(): FilterCondition[];
}

Integration Requirements

  • Environmental Monitoring: Real-time particle, temperature, humidity data
  • Alarm Integration: Common alarm codes and escalation
  • AMHS Interface: FOUP tracking and scheduling
  • Utility Monitoring: UPW quality, gas purity, power quality

Chapter Summary: Key Takeaways

  • Advanced fabs operate at ISO Class 1-3, with ULPA U15-U17 filtration
  • People are the primary contamination source (75%), requiring strict gowning protocols
  • AMC control is critical for lithography (base contamination) and gate oxide (acid contamination)
  • Single wafer cleaning provides better uniformity and lower chemical consumption than batch
  • UPW must achieve 18.2 MΩ.cm resistivity with less than 1 ppb TOC and sub-50nm particle control
  • AMHS with N2 purge maintains FOUP cleanliness during transport and storage
  • WIA-SEMI-EQP-001 standardizes facility equipment interfaces for fab-wide integration
