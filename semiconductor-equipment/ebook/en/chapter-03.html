3.1 Overview of Deposition Technologies

Thin film deposition is fundamental to semiconductor manufacturing, creating the layers of materials that form transistors, interconnects, and isolation structures. Modern chips contain hundreds of deposited layers, each with specific thickness, composition, and conformality requirements. The global deposition equipment market exceeds $24 billion annually, representing 22% of total semiconductor equipment spending.

Endura Platform Deep Dive

The Endura platform is Applied Materials' flagship PVD system, essential for creating the metal interconnects in advanced chips. Key specifications include:

  • Chamber Configuration: Up to 6 process chambers on single mainframe
  • Throughput: Greater than 60 wafers per hour
  • Film Uniformity: Better than 1% (1-sigma) across 300mm
  • Step Coverage: 90%+ in features with 10:1 aspect ratio
  • Metal Options: Cu, Ta, TaN, Ti, TiN, Co, Ru, W
  • Ionization: Greater than 70% for directional deposition

Ionized PVD for Advanced Nodes

At advanced nodes, traditional PVD cannot provide adequate step coverage in high aspect ratio features. Ionized PVD (iPVD) creates a plasma of metal ions that can be directed into trenches and vias using substrate bias, achieving conformal coverage essential for 7nm and below interconnects.

3.3 Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD)

CVD deposits thin films through chemical reactions of vapor-phase precursors on the substrate surface. The technology comes in several variants optimized for different applications.

PECVD - Plasma Enhanced CVD

PECVD uses plasma energy to enhance chemical reactions at lower temperatures than thermal CVD, enabling deposition on temperature-sensitive substrates.

PECVD Process Parameters

Parameter Typical Range Impact
Temperature 200-400 degrees C Film density, stress
Pressure 1-10 Torr Deposition rate, uniformity
RF Power 100-1000W Plasma density, film properties
Deposition Rate 100-1000 nm/min Throughput
Uniformity Less than 2% (1-sigma) Device performance

Low-Pressure CVD (LPCVD)

LPCVD operates at low pressures (0.1-1 Torr) and higher temperatures (500-800 degrees C), producing high-quality films with excellent uniformity and conformality.

  • Applications: Polysilicon gates, silicon nitride spacers, TEOS oxide
  • Advantages: Excellent step coverage, high film quality, batch processing
  • Limitations: High thermal budget, slower deposition rates

MOCVD for III-V Semiconductors

Metal-Organic CVD is essential for compound semiconductor devices used in power electronics, LEDs, and RF applications:

  • Materials: GaN, GaAs, InP, AlGaN
  • Precursors: TMGa, TMAl, TMIn, NH3, AsH3, PH3
  • Applications: Power devices, RF amplifiers, photonics
  • Key Vendors: Veeco, AIXTRON, Applied Materials

ALD for GAA Transistors

Gate-All-Around (GAA) nanosheet transistors, used in TSMC N2 and Samsung 2nm processes, require ALD for multiple critical applications:

GAA ALD Applications

  • Inner Spacer: SiO2 or SiN ALD between nanosheets (2-3nm thickness)
  • Gate Dielectric: HfO2 high-k with sub-2nm EOT
  • Work Function Metals: TiN, TiAl, TaN stacks for threshold voltage tuning
  • Metal Gate Fill: W ALD for void-free fill in confined spaces
  • Barrier Layers: TaN ALD preventing metal diffusion

3.5 Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD)

PVD, primarily sputtering, deposits metals and metal compounds for interconnect metallization. Despite competition from ALD and CVD, PVD remains essential for seed layers, barriers, and some contact metallization.

Magnetron Sputtering

Modern PVD systems use magnetron sputtering with several enhancements:

  • DC Magnetron: For conductive targets (Cu, Al, Ti, Ta)
  • RF Magnetron: For insulating targets (SiO2, Al2O3)
  • Pulsed DC: Reduced arcing, better film quality
  • HiPIMS: High Power Impulse Magnetron Sputtering for high ionization

Ionized PVD Technology

Ionized PVD is essential for advanced interconnects with high aspect ratio features:

iPVD Process Flow

  1. Sputtered atoms pass through high-density plasma
  2. Atoms are ionized (70%+ ionization fraction)
  3. Ions are accelerated toward substrate by RF bias
  4. Directional deposition provides bottom and sidewall coverage
  5. Results in conformal barrier/seed layers in high AR features

3.6 Epitaxial Deposition

Epitaxy grows crystalline films on crystalline substrates, maintaining the substrate's crystal structure. This technology is critical for creating the transistor channel and source/drain regions in advanced logic devices.

Silicon Epitaxy

  • Precursors: SiH4, SiH2Cl2, SiCl4
  • Temperature: 500-1100 degrees C
  • Growth Rate: 1-10 nm/min
  • Applications: Starting wafers, channel epitaxy

SiGe Epitaxy for Advanced Logic

SiGe epitaxy is essential for modern CMOS transistors:

SiGe Applications in GAA Transistors

  • Sacrificial Layer: SiGe removed to create nanosheet gaps
  • Source/Drain: Raised S/D for reduced resistance
  • Channel Material: High-mobility pFET channels
  • Strain Engineering: Mobility enhancement through lattice mismatch

Selective Epitaxy

Selective epitaxial growth (SEG) deposits material only on exposed silicon/SiGe while avoiding deposition on dielectric surfaces:

  • Chemistry: HCl addition for selectivity
  • Selectivity Ratio: Greater than 100:1 (Si vs. SiO2)
  • Applications: Raised S/D, embedded SiGe
  • Challenges: Faceting, loading effects, pattern dependency

3.7 Deposition Challenges at 2nm and Beyond

As transistor dimensions shrink to atomic scales, deposition technology faces unprecedented challenges:

Critical Challenges

Challenge Impact Solution Approach
Atomic-scale uniformity Device variability Advanced ALD, real-time control
Ultra-thin film continuity Barrier failure Nucleation engineering
Thermal budget Junction damage Low-temperature processes
Interface quality Carrier scattering In-situ cleans, plasma treatments
New materials integration Reliability concerns Extensive characterization

Emerging Deposition Technologies

  • Area-Selective Deposition (ASD): Inherent selectivity based on surface chemistry
  • Atomic Layer Etching (ALE): Complementary process for precision removal
  • Flowable CVD: Bottom-up fill for high AR structures
  • Supercritical Fluid Deposition: Novel precursor delivery

3.8 WIA-SEMI-EQP-001 Deposition Standards

The WIA-SEMI-EQP-001 standard defines comprehensive integration requirements for deposition equipment:

Data Exchange Format

// WIA-SEMI-EQP-001 Deposition Equipment Interface
interface DepositionEquipment {
    // Equipment identification
    equipmentId: string;
    processType: 'CVD' | 'ALD' | 'PVD' | 'EPI';
    vendor: string;
    model: string;
    
    // Process monitoring
    getFilmThickness(): FilmMeasurement;
    getProcessParameters(): ProcessData;
    getUniformityMap(): UniformityData;
    
    // Real-time control
    setRecipe(recipe: DepositionRecipe): void;
    adjustParameter(param: string, value: number): void;
    
    // Maintenance data
    getPrecursorLevels(): PrecursorStatus[];
    getChamberCondition(): ChamberStatus;
    predictMaintenance(): MaintenanceSchedule;
}

interface DepositionRecipe {
    name: string;
    material: string;
    targetThickness: number;
    steps: ProcessStep[];
    limits: ProcessLimits;
}

interface FilmMeasurement {
    thickness: number;
    uniformity: number;
    timestamp: Date;
    measurementPoints: MeasurementPoint[];
}

Process Integration Requirements

  • Recipe Management: Standard format for recipe exchange between systems
  • In-situ Metrology: Real-time thickness and composition monitoring
  • Chamber Matching: Data formats for tool-to-tool correlation
  • Predictive Maintenance: Equipment health monitoring and forecasting
