Chapter 7: Regulatory Compliance (FDA, ISO 22000)

FDA FSMA (Food Safety Modernization Act)

Signed into law in 2011, FSMA represents the most significant reform of U.S. food safety laws in 70 years. The focus shifts from responding to contamination to preventing it.

FSMA Key Requirements

1. Preventive Controls Rule

  • Written food safety plan with hazard analysis
  • Preventive controls (CCPs) implementation
  • Monitoring procedures
  • Corrective actions and verification
  • Supply-chain program

2. Traceability Rule (Final: January 2026)

Food companies must maintain Key Data Elements (KDEs) for traceability lot codes:

  • Traceability lot code (batch ID)
  • Product description
  • Quantity and unit of measure
  • Location (farm, facility address)
  • Date (harvest, shipping, receiving)
  • Reference document (invoice, BOL)

ISO 22000:2018 Food Safety Management

ISO 22000 is the international standard for food safety management systems. It combines HACCP principles with ISO 9001 management system requirements.

ISO 22000 Structure

Clause 4: Context of the Organization

  • Understanding organizational context
  • Food safety policy documentation
  • Stakeholder needs and expectations

Clause 5: Leadership

  • Top management commitment
  • Food safety team with defined roles
  • Management review process

Clause 6: Planning

  • Risk assessment
  • Food safety objectives
  • Change management

Clause 7: Support

  • Resources (people, infrastructure, equipment)
  • Competence and training
  • Communication and documentation

Clause 8: Operation

  • Prerequisite programs (PRPs)
  • HACCP plan execution
  • Traceability system
  • Emergency preparedness (recalls)

Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

  • Monitoring and measurement
  • Internal audits
  • Management review

Clause 10: Improvement

  • Nonconformity and corrective action
  • Continual improvement

ISO 22000 Certification Process

  1. Gap Analysis: Identify areas needing improvement
  2. Implementation: Build FSMS (6-12 months)
  3. Internal Audit: Verify system effectiveness
  4. Certification Audit Stage 1: Documentation review
  5. Certification Audit Stage 2: On-site audit
  6. Certification Issued: Valid for 3 years with annual surveillance audits

Other Key Standards

FSSC 22000 (Food Safety System Certification)

  • ISO 22000 + ISO/TS 22002-1 (prerequisite programs)
  • Recognized by Global Food Safety Initiative (GFSI)
  • Additional requirements: food defense, food fraud mitigation

BRC Global Standards (Issue 9)

  • Widely recognized by UK and European retailers
  • Senior management commitment
  • HACCP-based food safety plan
  • Product traceability and recall procedures

USDA Organic Certification

  • Organic production and handling standards
  • No synthetic pesticides or GMOs
  • Traceability from certified organic farm
  • Annual inspections
