Chapter 4: Cold Chain Management

What is the Cold Chain?

The cold chain is the temperature-controlled supply chain for perishable foods. Maintaining proper temperatures from harvest to consumption prevents bacterial growth and preserves food quality.

Critical Fact: Bacterial growth doubles every 20 minutes at room temperature. A 2-hour temperature abuse can increase bacterial count by 512x!

Temperature Requirements by Product Type

Product Storage Temperature Maximum Time at Room Temp
Fresh Meat ≤ 4°C (40°F) 2 hours
Poultry ≤ 4°C (40°F) 2 hours
Seafood 0-2°C (32-35°F) 1 hour
Dairy Products ≤ 4°C (40°F) 2 hours
Frozen Foods ≤ -18°C (0°F) N/A (must stay frozen)
Fresh Produce 0-4°C (32-40°F) 4 hours

IoT Sensor Network for Cold Chain Monitoring

WIA-FOOD_SAFETY Sensor Specifications:

  • Accuracy: ±0.1°C (Pt1000 RTD sensor)
  • Frequency: Readings every 60 seconds
  • Battery Life: 5 years (LoRaWAN low power)
  • Range: Up to 15 km in rural areas
  • Encryption: AES-128 for data security

Sensor Placement Strategy

  • Refrigerated Trucks: 3 sensors (front, middle, rear) to detect hot spots
  • Cold Storage Warehouses: 1 sensor per 100 m²
  • Retail Display Cases: 1 sensor per case
  • Transport Containers: 2 sensors per 40-foot container

Temperature Monitoring Algorithm

FDA Compliance Rule: Alert if temperature exceeds critical limit for more than 4 minutes.

out_of_spec_duration = 0

for each sensor_reading:
    if reading.temp > critical_limit:
        out_of_spec_duration += 60 seconds

        if out_of_spec_duration >= 240 seconds:  # 4 minutes
            trigger_alert(batch_id, location, temp)
            quarantine_batch(batch_id)
            notify_supervisor()
    else:
        out_of_spec_duration = 0  # Reset counter

Case Study: Temperature Abuse Detection

Scenario: Refrigerated truck transporting fresh chicken experiences compressor failure at 2:00 AM.

  • 2:00 AM: Temperature begins rising from 3°C
  • 2:05 AM: Temperature reaches 5°C (exceeds 4°C limit for > 4 minutes)
  • 2:05 AM: WIA-FOOD_SAFETY triggers SMS alert to driver and supervisor
  • 2:10 AM: Driver pulls over, calls for backup refrigerated truck
  • 2:30 AM: Product transferred to backup truck, temperature restored to 3°C
  • Outcome: Product saved. Without real-time monitoring, product would have reached unsafe temperatures (> 10°C) by morning, requiring disposal.
Cost Savings: $5,000 backup truck cost vs. $50,000 product disposal + liability risk

GPS Tracking Integration

WIA-FOOD_SAFETY combines temperature monitoring with GPS tracking to provide:

  • Real-time location of shipments
  • Route verification (detects unauthorized stops)
  • Geofencing alerts (notify when truck arrives/departs facility)
  • Environmental correlation (outdoor temperature vs. trailer temperature)

Humidity Monitoring

In addition to temperature, humidity affects food quality:

  • Fresh Produce: 85-95% RH to prevent wilting
  • Frozen Foods: < 80% RH to prevent freezer burn
  • Dry Goods: < 60% RH to prevent mold growth

Blockchain Integration

Temperature logs are batched and stored on blockchain every 10 minutes to balance cost and transparency:

  • Individual readings: Stored in database (fast queries)
  • Merkle root hash: Stored on blockchain (tamper-proof verification)
  • Anyone can verify temperature logs haven't been altered
