Chapter 2: HACCP Principles & Implementation

What is HACCP?

HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) is a systematic preventive approach to food safety. Developed in the 1960s for NASA's space program, HACCP is now the international standard for food safety management.

Core Philosophy: Prevention, not detection. Control hazards before they occur rather than testing finished products.

The 7 HACCP Principles

Principle 1: Conduct Hazard Analysis

Identify all potential biological, chemical, and physical hazards that could occur at each step of the food production process.

Principle 2: Determine Critical Control Points (CCPs)

Identify the points in the process where control can be applied to prevent, eliminate, or reduce hazards to acceptable levels.

CCP Hazard Control Measure
Receiving Bacterial growth (temperature abuse) Verify temperature ≤ 4°C (40°F)
Cooking Survival of pathogens Cook poultry to 74°C (165°F) for 15 seconds
Cold Storage Bacterial growth Maintain ≤ 4°C (40°F)
Metal Detection Metal fragments Scan all packaged products

Principle 3: Establish Critical Limits

Set maximum or minimum values for each CCP that must be met to control the hazard.

Examples:

  • Temperature: ≤ 4°C for refrigeration, ≥ 74°C for cooking poultry
  • Time: Maximum 4 hours at room temperature
  • pH: ≤ 4.6 for acidified foods
  • Water activity (aw): ≤ 0.85 to prevent microbial growth

Principle 4: Establish Monitoring Procedures

Implement procedures to ensure CCPs remain within critical limits.

WIA-FOOD_SAFETY Automation:
  • IoT sensors record temperature every 60 seconds (±0.1°C accuracy)
  • Automatic blockchain logging (tamper-proof records)
  • Real-time dashboard showing all CCP statuses
  • GPS tracking for transport vehicles

Principle 5: Establish Corrective Actions

Define what to do when monitoring indicates a CCP is out of control.

Example Corrective Action Workflow:

IF temperature > 4°C for > 4 minutes:
  1. Alert supervisor via SMS/push notification
  2. Log incident on blockchain (immutable)
  3. Quarantine affected batch in system
  4. Initiate product evaluation:
     - Visual inspection
     - Sensory evaluation (smell, appearance)
     - Laboratory testing if needed
  5. Document corrective action taken
  6. Retrain staff on proper procedures
  7. Review equipment (calibration check)

Principle 6: Establish Verification Procedures

Confirm that the HACCP system is working effectively.

  • Calibration: IoT sensors calibrated annually (±0.1°C accuracy verified)
  • Review: Monthly management review of CCP records
  • Testing: Environmental swab testing for pathogens
  • Audits: Third-party ISO 22000 certification audits
  • Blockchain Verification: Cryptographic proof of data integrity

Principle 7: Establish Record-Keeping

Maintain detailed records of the HACCP system, including CCP monitoring, corrective actions, and verification activities.
