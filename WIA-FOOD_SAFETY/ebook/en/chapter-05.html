Laboratory Testing Methods

1. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction)

  • How it works: Amplifies pathogen DNA for detection
  • Speed: 6-12 hours (rapid PCR)
  • Sensitivity: Detects < 10 CFU/g (colony-forming units)
  • Advantages: Fast, specific, no culturing required
  • Cost: $20-50 per test

2. Traditional Culture Methods

  • How it works: Grow bacteria on selective media
  • Speed: 24-72 hours
  • Advantages: Gold standard, confirms viable organisms
  • Cost: $10-20 per test

3. ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay)

  • How it works: Antibodies detect pathogen proteins
  • Speed: 2-4 hours
  • Use case: Rapid screening
  • Cost: $5-15 per test

4. ATP Bioluminescence

  • How it works: Detects ATP (energy molecule in living cells)
  • Speed: 1 minute
  • Use case: Surface cleanliness verification
  • Cost: $2-5 per test

Sampling Strategies

  • Random Sampling: Test 1-5% of batches depending on risk
  • Risk-Based Sampling: Test high-risk products (raw chicken, leafy greens) more frequently
  • Environmental Monitoring: Swab equipment, surfaces, drains monthly
  • Finished Product Testing: Test packaged products before release

Case Study: Chipotle E. coli Outbreak (2015)

  • What happened: E. coli contamination in multiple Chipotle locations
  • Impact: 55 people sick, 21 hospitalized
  • Root cause: Inadequate testing of fresh produce suppliers
  • Cost: $25 million in legal settlements + brand damage
  • Lesson: Supplier verification and regular testing are essential

Summary

  • Pathogen testing is essential for verifying food safety
  • PCR provides rapid results (6-12 hours) vs. traditional culture (24-72 hours)
  • WIA-FOOD_SAFETY integrates lab results via API for instant blockchain recording
  • Positive pathogen detection automatically triggers recall workflow
  • Regular environmental monitoring prevents cross-contamination
