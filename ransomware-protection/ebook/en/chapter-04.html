Immutable Backup Attestation with Merkle Roots

The backup_snapshot envelope is the standard's answer to a question that ransomware operators have made existential: when the encryption finishes, can the organisation prove that an unencrypted copy of its data still exists, and can it restore that copy without re-importing the breach?

4.1 The envelope schema

{
  "snapshot_id": "01HXRA9C2KQ3V7N4M8B6P0Z9F1",
  "tenant_id": "did:wia:tenant:acme-eu",
  "captured_at": "2026-04-27T02:00:00.000Z",
  "asset_class": "volume",
  "asset_ref": "vol-acme-prod-erp-01",
  "size_bytes": 1099511627776,
  "object_count": 3742891,
  "encryption": { "algorithm": "AES-256-GCM", "kms_key_ref": "..." },
  "immutability": {
    "retention_until": "2026-07-27T02:00:00.000Z",
    "lock_kind": "compliance",
    "broker": "did:wia:storage:acme-vault"
  },
  "merkle_root": "sha384-...",
  "signature": { "alg": "Ed25519", "value": "..." }
}

4.2 Why a Merkle root and not a single hash

A flat hash over a 1 TB snapshot proves the snapshot is intact but proves nothing about subsets of it. A Merkle root over the per-object hashes proves both: the root proves the whole, and the root plus a logarithmic proof path proves any individual object. During a partial restore — common in ransomware recovery, where only the encrypted prefix needs to be replaced — the Merkle proof lets the restoring system verify each restored object without re-reading the entire snapshot.

4.3 Immutability semantics

The immutability block names three things: when the snapshot becomes mutable again (retention_until), the kind of lock holding it (compliance means even the tenant administrator cannot remove it; governance means a tenant administrator can break the lock with audit; object-lock-s3 is the S3-compatible specialisation), and the broker that holds the lock (so an auditor can verify the lock independently).

4.4 Forensic preservation

A subset of snapshots are flagged forensic_preservation: true. These are exempt from normal retention expiry until the parent incident records an incident_closed_at timestamp. The standard does not mandate which jurisdiction's evidence-handling rules apply, but it does mandate the chain-of-custody fields: custodian, transfer_log, and tamper_seal.

4.5 Cross-region attestation

The standard recommends, but does not require, that critical assets carry attested snapshots in at least two geographically distinct regions. The recommendation is operational: a regional outage during an active ransomware incident is the worst possible time to discover that the off-site replica has not been refreshed for 96 hours.

4.6 Snapshot lifecycle envelopes

The lifecycle of a snapshot — created, verified, replicated, expired, deleted — is captured as a sequence of snapshot_lifecycle envelopes, each signed at the time of the transition. The lifecycle history is queryable so that a regulator can answer "show me every retention extension on this snapshot in the last 90 days" without having to reconstruct it from logs.

4.7 Air-gap declaration

Snapshots stored on truly air-gapped media (offline tape, sneakernet to a vaulting service) carry an air_gap_declaration sub-envelope identifying the custodian and the verification cadence. Air-gap is a control, not a marketing term; the declaration makes the control auditable.

Worked example

Consider a mid-size hospital network running 18 EHR servers, 240 imaging workstations, 42 IoT medical devices, and a separate biomedical engineering subnet. The SOC has one full-time analyst, two contracted analysts, and an MSSP relationship. The cyber-insurer requires annual posture evidence; the national health regulator requires 24-hour breach notification.

In a pre-standard world, the SOC re-keys every observation into the EDR portal, the SIEM, the MSSP ticketing system, the insurer questionnaire, and the regulator filing form. The total work per real incident exceeds the analyst headcount by a factor of three to five during the first 72 hours, and that is the work done after the technical containment is finished.

In a post-standard world, the same observation is signed once, propagated automatically to every consumer that has subscribed to the tenant under appropriate TLP and federation rules, and rendered into each consumer’s preferred form by a downstream adaptor. The SOC analyst spends the first 72 hours doing analysis and decision-making, not data entry. That is the entire return on signing the envelope.
