3.1 CPA COMPONENT CLASSIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Cryoprotectants are complex mixtures of chemicals that prevent ice damage
during freezing. Systematic classification enables comparison and optimization.

[CRYO] CPA COMPONENT CATEGORIES:

    PERMEATING CPAs (Cross cell membranes)
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Component      | MW    | Toxicity | Common Range | Mechanism     │
    ├────────────────┼───────┼──────────┼──────────────┼──────────────┤

    │ Glycerol       | 92    | Low      | 10-20%       | Colligative   │
    │ Ethylene       | 62    | High     | 5-10%        | Colligative   │
    │   Glycol       |       |          |              |               │

    NON-PERMEATING CPAs (Remain extracellular)

    ├────────────────┼───────┼────────────────────┼──────────────────┤

[i] COMPONENT INTERACTION MATRIX:

    Synergistic Combinations:
    ✓ DMSO + Trehalose: Enhanced membrane protection
    ✓ Glycerol + Sucrose: Balanced osmotic stress
    ✓ EG + AFP: Superior ice control

    Antagonistic Combinations:
    ✗ High DMSO + High EG: Excessive toxicity
    ✗ Methanol + weak cells: Severe membrane damage

3.3 CONCENTRATION UNITS AND STANDARDIZATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Concentration can be expressed in multiple units. Standardization prevents
confusion and enables automated conversion.

[!] CONCENTRATION UNITS:

    PERCENTAGE-BASED
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Unit    | Definition              | Example      | Notes         │
    ├─────────┼─────────────────────────┼──────────────┼──────────────┤
    │ % v/v   | Volume/Volume           | 10% DMSO     | For liquids   │
    │ % w/v   | Weight/Volume           | 5% Albumin   | For solids    │
    │ % w/w   | Weight/Weight           | 2% NaCl      | Rare in CPAs  │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    MOLAR CONCENTRATIONS
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Unit    | Definition              | Example      | Advantages    │
    ├─────────┼─────────────────────────┼──────────────┼──────────────┤
    │ M       | Moles/Liter             | 1.5 M DMSO   | Chemical      │
    │ mM      | Millimoles/Liter        | 200 mM       | accuracy      │
    │         |                         | Trehalose    |               │
    │ μM      | Micromoles/Liter        | 10 μM AFP    | Precise       │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    MASS-BASED
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Unit    | Definition              | Example      | Use Case      │
    ├─────────┼─────────────────────────┼──────────────┼──────────────┤
    │ mg/mL   | Milligrams/Milliliter   | 0.5 mg/mL AFP| Proteins      │
    │ μg/mL   | Micrograms/Milliliter   | 100 μg/mL    | Peptides      │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

[CRYO] CONVERSION FORMULAS:

    % v/v to Molarity:
    M = (% v/v × density × 10) / molecular_weight

    Example: 10% v/v DMSO to Molarity
    DMSO: MW = 78 g/mol, density = 1.10 g/mL
    M = (10 × 1.10 × 10) / 78 = 1.41 M

    mg/mL to Molarity:
    M = (mg/mL) / (molecular_weight)

    Example: 0.5 mg/mL AFP (MW ~7000)
    M = 0.5 / 7000 = 0.000071 M = 71 μM

[i] STANDARD UNIT RECOMMENDATIONS:

    Component Type          Preferred Unit    Alternative
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Small molecule CPAs     % v/v             Molarity
    Sugars                  mM                % w/v
    Proteins                mg/mL             μM
    Polymers                % w/v             mg/mL
    Salts                   mM                % w/v

3.4 MIXING PROTOCOLS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The order and method of mixing CPA components affects solution quality and
cell viability.

[!] MIXING ORDER GUIDELINES:

    RECOMMENDED SEQUENCE:
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Step | Component Type        | Rationale                         │
    ├──────┼───────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
    │  1   | Base medium           | Foundation                        │
    │  2   | Non-permeating CPAs   | Dissolve in aqueous phase         │
    │  3   | Salts and buffers     | Establish ionic strength, pH      │
    │  4   | Permeating CPAs       | Add slowly to avoid local highs   │
    │  5   | Proteins              | Add after pH adjustment           │
    │  6   | Ice blockers          | Add last, very expensive          │
    │  7   | pH adjustment         | Final fine-tuning                 │
    │  8   | Sterile filtration    | Remove particulates               │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

[CRYO] MIXING TECHNIQUE CONSIDERATIONS:

    AGITATION METHOD
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Method              | Pros                  | Cons               │
    ├─────────────────────┼───────────────────────┼───────────────────┤
    │ Magnetic stirring   | Gentle, controlled    | Slow for viscous   │
    │ Vortex mixing       | Fast                  | May denature       │
    │                     |                       | proteins           │
    │ Orbital shaking     | Good for large volume | Less control       │
    │ Hand inversion      | Gentle for proteins   | Inconsistent       │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    TEMPERATURE CONTROL
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Component      | Mixing Temp | Reason                            │
    ├────────────────┼─────────────┼──────────────────────────────────┤
    │ DMSO           | <25°C       | Exothermic mixing, avoid heat     │
    │ Glycerol       | 20-25°C     | Room temp OK                      │
    │ Trehalose      | 37°C        | Faster dissolution                │
    │ Proteins       | 4°C         | Prevent denaturation              │
    │ AFP            | 4°C         | Maintain activity                 │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

CHAPTER SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This chapter covered comprehensive cryoprotectant formulation documentation
and database management.

Key Takeaways:

  [OK] 1. CPA CLASSIFICATION
       CPAs are categorized as permeating (DMSO, glycerol), non-permeating
       (trehalose, sucrose), ice blockers (AFP), and additives (albumin).

  [OK] 2. FORMULATION SCHEMAS
