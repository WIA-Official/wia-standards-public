5.1 ASSESSMENT METHOD TAXONOMY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Viability assessment encompasses multiple assays that measure different aspects
of cell and tissue integrity. WIA-CRYO-010 standardizes methods, timing, and
reporting for comprehensive quality evaluation.

[CRYO] VIABILITY ASSAY CLASSIFICATION:

    MEMBRANE INTEGRITY ASSAYS
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Method            | Principle       | Time   | Pros/Cons           │
    ├───────────────────┼─────────────────┼────────┼────────────────────┤
    │ Trypan Blue       | Dye exclusion   | 5 min  | Simple/Subjective   │
    │ Propidium Iodide  | DNA binding     | 10 min | Quantitative/Cost   │
    │ 7-AAD             | DNA binding     | 15 min | Flow cyto/Expensive │
    │ Calcein AM        | Esterase active | 20 min | Fluorescent/Stable  │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    METABOLIC ACTIVITY ASSAYS
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Method            | Measures        | Time   | Application         │

    │ MTT               | Mitochondrial   | 2-4h   | Standard screening  │
    │                   | reductase       |        |                     │
    │ Alamar Blue       | Metabolic rate  | 4-24h  | Long-term tracking  │

    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    FUNCTIONAL ASSAYS (Cell-type specific)

    │ T-cells        | Cytokine production | ELISA, 48h                │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    APOPTOSIS/NECROSIS DETECTION
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Method            | Detects         | Time   | Information         │
    ├───────────────────┼─────────────────┼────────┼────────────────────┤
    │ Annexin V/PI      | Early apoptosis | 30 min | Distinguishes modes │
    │ TUNEL             | DNA breaks      | 2h     | Confirms apoptosis  │

    │ LDH Release       | Membrane damage | 30 min | Necrosis indicator  │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

[!] RECOMMENDED ASSESSMENT BATTERY:

    TIER 1 (Required - Immediate post-thaw)
    ☑ Trypan Blue or PI: Membrane integrity (target >80%)
    ☑ Cell count: Recovery rate (target >75% of input)
    ☑ Morphology: Visual inspection under microscope

    TIER 2 (Standard - 24 hours post-thaw)
    ☑ MTT or Alamar Blue: Metabolic activity (target >70% of fresh)
    ☑ Attachment efficiency: For adherent cells (target >75%)
    ☑ Annexin V/PI: Apoptosis rate (target <10%)

    TIER 3 (Comprehensive - 48-72 hours)
    ☑ Functional assay: Cell-type specific (target >70% of fresh)
    ☑ Proliferation: Growth rate comparison
    ☑ Gene expression: Stress markers (qPCR)

5.2 CELL VIABILITY METRICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Standardized metrics enable comparison across studies and institutions.

[CRYO] VIABILITY CALCULATION STANDARDS:

    BASIC VIABILITY PERCENTAGE
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                                  │
    │   Viability (%) = (Viable Cells / Total Cells) × 100            │
    │                                                                  │
    │   Where:                                                         │
    │   - Viable Cells = Cells excluding dye (Trypan Blue negative)   │
    │   - Total Cells = All counted cells                              │
    │                                                                  │
    │   Example:                                                       │
    │   Counted: 500 total cells, 437 viable, 63 dead                 │
    │   Viability = (437 / 500) × 100 = 87.4%                         │
    │                                                                  │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    RECOVERY RATE
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                                  │
    │   Recovery (%) = (Cells Post-Thaw / Cells Pre-Freeze) × 100     │
    │                                                                  │
    │   Accounts for cell loss during processing                       │
    │                                                                  │
    │   Example:                                                       │
    │   Frozen: 1.0 × 10⁶ cells                                       │
    │   Recovered: 8.2 × 10⁵ cells                                    │
    │   Recovery = (8.2 × 10⁵ / 1.0 × 10⁶) × 100 = 82%               │
    │                                                                  │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    EFFECTIVE VIABILITY
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                                  │
    │   Effective Viability = Viability × Recovery / 100              │
    │                                                                  │
    │   Example:                                                       │
    │   Viability = 87.4%                                             │
    │   Recovery = 82%                                                 │
    │   Effective = 87.4% × 82% / 100 = 71.7%                         │
    │                                                                  │
    │   This metric captures total usable cells                        │
    │                                                                  │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

5.4 FUNCTIONAL ASSESSMENT PROTOCOLS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ultimate validation requires demonstration of biological function.

[OK] FUNCTION-SPECIFIC PROTOCOLS:

    HEPATOCYTE FUNCTION (Example)
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Assay                  | Method         | Target (% fresh)      │
    ├────────────────────────┼────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
    │ Albumin Secretion      | ELISA, 24-48h  | >70%                  │
    │ Urea Synthesis         | Colorimetric   | >70%                  │
    │ CYP450 Activity        | P450-Glo       | >60%                  │
    │ Bile Acid Transport    | Fluorescence   | >65%                  │
    │ Glycogen Storage       | PAS staining   | Present/Normal        │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    Example Protocol: Albumin Secretion Assay
    1. Plate hepatocytes at 5×10⁴ cells/cm² in collagen-coated plates
    2. Culture in hepatocyte maintenance medium for 24 hours
    3. Replace with fresh medium, culture additional 24 hours
    4. Collect culture supernatant
    5. Measure albumin by ELISA (Human Albumin ELISA Kit)
    6. Normalize to cell number (count at end of assay)
    7. Compare to fresh hepatocyte control
    8. Calculate: (Frozen albumin / Fresh albumin) × 100%

5.5 TEMPORAL ANALYSIS STANDARDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Viability changes over time post-thaw. Standardized temporal assessment
captures recovery kinetics and delayed damage.

[CRYO] RECOMMENDED ASSESSMENT TIMELINE:

    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | Timepoint     | Assessments Required      | Rationale           |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | 0-2 hours     | - Membrane integrity      | Immediate damage    |
    |               | - Cell count/recovery     | assessment          |
    |               | - Morphology              |                     |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | 24 hours      | - Metabolic activity      | Early recovery      |
    |               | - Attachment (if adherent)| Delayed apoptosis   |
    |               | - Apoptosis markers       | detection           |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | 48-72 hours   | - Functional assays       | Confirmed recovery  |
    |               | - Proliferation initiation| Function validation |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | 7 days        | - Sustained proliferation | Long-term stability |
    |               | - Differentiation markers | Genomic integrity   |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | 14+ days      | - Full differentiation    | Complete validation |
    | (if applicable)| - Karyotype analysis     | Clinical readiness  |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+

5.6 STATISTICAL ANALYSIS REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Rigorous statistical analysis ensures conclusions are valid and reproducible.

[!] STATISTICAL STANDARDS:

    SAMPLE SIZE REQUIREMENTS
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Study Type         | Minimum n | Recommended | Rationale         │
    ├────────────────────┼───────────┼─────────────┼──────────────────┤
    │ Pilot/Feasibility  | 3         | 6           | Detect major      │
    │                    |           |             | issues            │
    │ Optimization       | 6         | 10-12       | Compare           │
    │                    |           |             | conditions        │
    │ Validation         | 20        | 30+         | Statistical power │
    │ Clinical           | 50        | 100+        | Regulatory        │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    STATISTICAL TESTS
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Comparison         | Test               | Requirements          │
    ├────────────────────┼────────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
    │ Two groups         | t-test             | Normal distribution   │
    │ Multiple groups    | ANOVA + post-hoc   | Normal, equal var     │
    │ Non-parametric     | Mann-Whitney       | Any distribution      │
    │ Paired data        | Paired t-test      | Before/after          │
    │ Proportions        | Chi-square         | Categorical data      │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
    ☑ Mean ± Standard Deviation (or SEM with justification)
    ☑ Sample size (n) for each group
    ☑ Statistical test used
    ☑ P-value (report exact value, not just p<0.05)
    ☑ Confidence intervals (95% CI recommended)
    ☑ Effect size (Cohen's d or similar)

[CRYO] EXAMPLE STATISTICAL REPORT:

    Viability Comparison: Optimized vs Standard CPA
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Optimized CPA (n=24): 87.3 ± 3.2%
    Standard CPA (n=24):  75.2 ± 4.8%
    
    Difference: 12.1% (95% CI: 9.8-14.4%)
    Statistical test: Independent t-test (two-tailed)
    t(46) = 10.23, p < 0.001
    Effect size: Cohen's d = 2.96 (large effect)
    
    Conclusion: Optimized CPA shows statistically significant
    and clinically meaningful improvement in viability.

5.8 INTER-LABORATORY VALIDATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Cross-lab validation ensures methods are reproducible beyond a single facility.

[OK] VALIDATION STUDY DESIGN:

    PHASE 1: METHOD TRANSFER
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Step 1: Detailed protocol documentation                          │
    │ Step 2: Training of receiving lab personnel                      │
    │ Step 3: Side-by-side assessment with same samples                │
    │ Step 4: Concordance analysis (target: r > 0.95)                  │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    PHASE 2: INDEPENDENT VALIDATION
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Each lab processes identical samples independently               │
    │ Minimum 3 labs, 20 samples each                                  │
    │ Blinded assessment                                               │
    │ Statistical comparison: ANOVA for inter-lab variability          │
    │ Success: CV between labs <20%                                    │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

[CRYO] INTER-LAB RESULTS EXAMPLE:

    Three-Lab Viability Validation Study
    ─────────────────────────────────────
    Method: Trypan Blue viability assessment
    Sample: Human hepatocytes (same batch, n=20 per lab)
    
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | Lab           | Mean Viability | SD    | CV     | Range          |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | Lab A (Lead)  | 87.3%          | 3.2%  | 3.7%   | 82.1-92.4%     |
    | Lab B         | 85.8%          | 4.1%  | 4.8%   | 79.2-91.3%     |
    | Lab C         | 86.5%          | 3.8%  | 4.4%   | 80.7-92.1%     |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | Overall       | 86.5%          | 3.8%  | 4.4%   | 79.2-92.4%     |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------+
    
    ANOVA Results:
    Between-lab variance: 0.92%
    Within-lab variance: 3.75%
    F(2,57) = 1.23, p = 0.30 (NS)
    
    Conclusion: No significant difference between labs.
    Method is reproducible across facilities.
    Inter-lab CV = 4.4% < 20% threshold → VALIDATED

CHAPTER SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This chapter covered comprehensive viability and functional assessment methods
for evaluating cryopreservation success.

Key Takeaways:

  [OK] 1. ASSESSMENT TAXONOMY
       Multiple assay types measure membrane integrity (Trypan Blue),
       metabolic activity (MTT), function (cell-specific), and
       apoptosis/necrosis (Annexin V).

  [OK] 2. STANDARDIZED METRICS
       Viability percentage, recovery rate, and effective viability
       provide comprehensive quality assessment with clear calculations.

  [OK] 3. TISSUE INTEGRITY
       Histology and imaging methods assess structural preservation
       using standardized 0-5 scoring systems.

  [OK] 4. FUNCTIONAL VALIDATION
       Cell-type specific assays (e.g., albumin secretion for hepatocytes)
       demonstrate biological function, the ultimate success criterion.

  [OK] 5. TEMPORAL ANALYSIS
       Assessment at multiple timepoints (0h, 24h, 48h, 7d, 14d) captures
       recovery kinetics and delayed effects.

  [OK] 6. STATISTICAL RIGOR
       Appropriate sample sizes (n≥6 for optimization, n≥20 for validation),
       proper statistical tests, and complete reporting (mean±SD, n, p-value,
       CI, effect size) ensure valid conclusions.

  [OK] 7. EQUIPMENT CALIBRATION
       Regular calibration (daily to annual depending on equipment) with
       documented standards ensures measurement accuracy.

  [OK] 8. INTER-LAB VALIDATION
       Cross-facility validation (≥3 labs, CV<20%) confirms method
       reproducibility and enables standardization.

longitudinal research tracking preservation success over months to years.
