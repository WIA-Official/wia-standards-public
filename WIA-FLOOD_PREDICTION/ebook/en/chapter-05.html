Chapter 5: Risk Mapping & Visualization

5.1 Flood Risk vs. Flood Hazard

Hazard: Physical phenomenon (water depth, velocity)
Risk: Probability × Consequence (people/property affected)

Risk = Hazard × Exposure × Vulnerability
A 2-meter flood in an empty field = Low risk
A 2-meter flood in downtown = High risk

5.2 Flood Depth Maps

Most critical parameter for evacuation planning and damage estimation.

Generation Method

  1. HEC-RAS 2D simulation: Solves shallow water equations on 10m×10m grid
  2. Input: River discharge forecast from SWAT model
  3. Output: Water surface elevation at each grid cell
  4. Depth calculation: Water elevation - Ground elevation (from LiDAR DEM)

Color Scheme (Universal Standard):

  • Blue (0-0.5m): Passable on foot, vehicle can cross
  • Yellow (0.5-1m): Dangerous for vehicles, adult can wade
  • Orange (1-2m): Life-threatening, immediate evacuation
  • Red (>2m): Catastrophic, multi-story buildings affected

5.3 Flood Velocity Maps

Why velocity matters: 15cm of water at 2 m/s exerts same force as 60cm of still water.

Safe Thresholds:

  • <0.5 m/s: Walking possible
  • 0.5-1 m/s: Difficult to walk, vehicle unstable
  • >1 m/s: Extremely dangerous, structural damage

5.4 Inundation Extent (GeoJSON Polygons)

Vector format for GIS integration, emergency response systems.

Example GeoJSON:

{
  "type": "Feature",
  "properties": {
    "prediction_id": "pred_20260118",
    "max_depth_m": 2.4,
    "affected_area_km2": 3.7,
    "population_at_risk": 12500
  },
  "geometry": {
    "type": "Polygon",
    "coordinates": [[[-77.12,38.88], [-77.01,38.88], ...]]
  }
}

5.5 Interactive Web Maps (Leaflet.js)

WIA-FLOOD_PREDICTION provides embeddable flood maps for emergency websites.

Features

  • Real-time prediction updates (via WebSocket)
  • Click on map → Get flood depth, arrival time, nearest shelter
  • Toggle layers: Satellite imagery, flood extent, evacuation routes
  • Mobile-responsive (60% of users access during emergencies via smartphone)

5.6 Damage Assessment

Convert flood depth to economic damage using depth-damage curves.

HAZUS Model (FEMA):

  • 0.5m depth: 20% damage to 1-story residential
  • 1.0m depth: 40% damage
  • 2.0m depth: 65% damage (structure may be condemned)
Example: A neighborhood with 500 homes ($300K average) flooded to 1m depth:
Damage = 500 homes × $300K × 40% = $60 million

5.7 Evacuation Planning

Key Outputs for Emergency Managers

  1. Time-to-Inundation Maps: "Your area will flood in 4 hours"
  2. Safe Routes: Roads remaining above water at peak flood
  3. Shelter Capacity: Match population-at-risk with available shelters
  4. Critical Infrastructure: Flag hospitals, power stations, water treatment plants at risk

5.8 Key Takeaways

  • Flood depth, velocity, and extent are the three critical hazard parameters
  • HEC-RAS 2D + LiDAR DEMs generate high-resolution (10m) inundation maps
  • GeoJSON format enables integration with emergency dispatch systems
  • Interactive web maps empower citizens to make informed evacuation decisions
  • Depth-damage curves (HAZUS) translate physical hazards into economic impact
