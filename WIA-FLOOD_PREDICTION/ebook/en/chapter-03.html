Chapter 3: Weather Forecasting Models

3.1 Numerical Weather Prediction (NWP)

Weather forecasts are generated by solving complex mathematical equations that simulate Earth's atmosphere. These equations (Navier-Stokes, thermodynamics, moisture conservation) are solved on supercomputers dividing the atmosphere into a 3D grid.

Key Insight: A 1% improvement in precipitation forecast accuracy can save $2 billion annually in flood damages (NOAA estimate).

3.2 Major Forecast Models

NOAA GFS (Global Forecast System)

ParameterValue
Resolution0.25° (~28km)

ECMWF IFS (Integrated Forecasting System)

Widely considered the world's most accurate weather model

Resolution9km globally
Forecast Horizon10 days (deterministic), 46 days (ensemble)
Accuracy8-9 day forecast as good as GFS 7-day forecast

HRRR (High-Resolution Rapid Refresh)

Best for short-term flash flood forecasting (USA only)

Resolution3km (can resolve thunderstorms)
Forecast Horizon18 hours
Update FrequencyEvery 1 hour
CoverageContinental USA + Alaska

3.3 Key Forecast Variables for Flood Prediction

  1. Total Precipitation (APCP): Accumulated rainfall over 3-6-12-24 hour periods
  2. Precipitation Rate: Intensity (mm/hour) - flash flood indicator
  3. Soil Moisture: Determines infiltration capacity
  4. Snowmelt: Major flood source in spring (mountain regions)
  5. Evapotranspiration: Water loss back to atmosphere
  6. Temperature: Affects snowmelt rate and evaporation

3.4 Ensemble Forecasting

Single forecasts are unreliable beyond 5-7 days due to chaos theory (butterfly effect). Solution: Run model 50+ times with slightly different initial conditions.

Ensemble Mean: Average of all members (more accurate than any single run)
Ensemble Spread: Disagreement between members = forecast uncertainty

Application: If 45 out of 50 ensemble members show >100mm rain, confidence is high. If 25/50 show rain, confidence is low.

3.6 Integrating Weather Forecasts into WIA-FLOOD_PREDICTION

Data Pipeline:

  1. Download NOAA GFS GRIB2 files every 6 hours
  2. Extract precipitation, temperature, soil moisture for watershed of interest
  3. Convert to NetCDF, store in time-series database (InfluxDB)
  4. Feed into SWAT hydrological model + LSTM neural network
  5. Generate 7-14 day flood probability forecast

3.7 Key Takeaways

  • Weather models solve physics equations on supercomputers
  • NOAA GFS (28km, 16-day) and ECMWF IFS (9km, 10-day) provide global forecasts
  • HRRR (3km, 18-hour) excels at short-term flash flood prediction (USA)
  • Ensemble forecasts quantify uncertainty via multiple simulations
  • Accuracy degrades beyond 7 days; 14-day forecasts useful for monitoring only
