Standard Overview

The WIA-REFUGEE-CREDENTIAL standard addresses one of humanity's most pressing challenges: providing secure, verifiable identity documentation to the over 100 million forcibly displaced people worldwide. Without proper identification, refugees face barriers to accessing healthcare, education, employment, financial services, and legal protection. This standard establishes a comprehensive framework for issuing, verifying, and recognizing digital credentials that restore dignity and enable opportunity for displaced populations.

Built on principles of privacy-by-design, interoperability, and humanitarian protection, the standard ensures that credential systems cannot be weaponized against vulnerable populations while still meeting legitimate security requirements of host countries. The specification covers biometric capture protocols, cryptographic security requirements, cross-border recognition frameworks, and ethical guidelines for implementation in humanitarian contexts.

Organizations implementing this standard join a global network committed to solving the identity gap that perpetuates refugee vulnerability. From UN agencies to national governments, NGOs to technology providers, the WIA-REFUGEE-CREDENTIAL certification demonstrates commitment to the highest standards of humanitarian technology.

Target Audiences

  • UN Agencies & NGOs: UNHCR, IOM, ICRC, and humanitarian organizations implementing refugee documentation programs
  • National Governments: Immigration authorities, border agencies, and social services ministries in host countries
  • Technology Providers: Identity management vendors, biometric solution providers, and blockchain developers
  • Financial Institutions: Banks and financial service providers enabling refugee banking and remittances
