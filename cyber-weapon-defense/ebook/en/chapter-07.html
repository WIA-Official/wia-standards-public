7.2 Incident Severity Classification

Severity Criteria Response Time Leadership
Critical (P1) Nation-state APT, data destruction, safety impact Immediate Executive, Government
High (P2) Active intrusion, data exfiltration, ransomware <1 hour CISO, Legal
Medium (P3) Suspected compromise, policy violation <4 hours Security Manager
Low (P4) Anomaly investigation, false positive triage <24 hours SOC Lead

7.3 IR Team Structure

Role Responsibilities Skills Required
Incident Commander Overall coordination, decisions, communications Leadership, crisis management
Technical Lead Technical investigation, analysis direction Deep technical expertise
Forensic Analyst Evidence collection, analysis, preservation Forensics certifications
Malware Analyst Reverse engineering, IOC extraction Reverse engineering
Communications Lead Stakeholder updates, external comms Crisis communications
Legal Counsel Legal guidance, regulatory compliance Cyber law expertise

7.4 Containment Strategies

7.4.1 Short-Term Containment

  • Network Isolation: Disconnect affected systems
  • Firewall Rules: Block C2 infrastructure
  • Account Disable: Lock compromised credentials
  • DNS Sinkhole: Redirect malicious domains

7.4.2 Long-Term Containment

  • Clean Systems: Rebuild from known-good state
  • Patch Vulnerabilities: Close exploited vectors
  • Enhanced Monitoring: Deploy additional detection
  • Credential Reset: Enterprise-wide password reset

7.6 Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  • The NIST framework provides a structured approach to incident response
  • Severity classification drives response urgency and leadership engagement
  • Clear roles and responsibilities enable effective coordination
  • Containment balances immediate action with long-term remediation
  • Communications must be planned before incidents occur
