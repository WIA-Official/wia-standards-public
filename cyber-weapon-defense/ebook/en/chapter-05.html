Learning Objectives

Critical Alert

Nation-state actors are actively pre-positioning in critical infrastructure networks worldwide. The ability to disrupt essential services during conflict represents a strategic capability that adversaries are actively developing.

5.3 IT/OT Architecture

5.3.1 Purdue Model for Industrial Security

Level Name Systems
Level 5 Enterprise Network Corporate IT, Internet
Level 4 Business Planning ERP, business systems
DMZ Demilitarized Zone Data historians, jump servers
Level 3 Operations SCADA, HMI, engineering workstations
Level 2 Control PLCs, RTUs, DCS
Level 1 Field Sensors, actuators, drives
Level 0 Process Physical process being controlled

5.4 ICS Security Controls

  • Network Segmentation: Strict separation between IT and OT networks
  • Unidirectional Gateways: Data diodes for one-way traffic
  • OT-Specific Detection: Protocol-aware monitoring (Modbus, DNP3)
  • Asset Inventory: Complete visibility of all OT assets
  • Secure Remote Access: Jump servers, MFA, session recording
  • Safety Instrumented Systems: Independent safety controls

5.5 Case Study: Ukraine Power Grid (2015)

In December 2015, Russian attackers (Sandworm) successfully disrupted the Ukrainian power grid, causing blackouts affecting 225,000 customers. The attack used spear-phishing, BlackEnergy malware, and destructive KillDisk wiper to maintain the outage.

Lessons: IT/OT segmentation, monitoring of SCADA protocols, manual override capabilities, and incident response procedures for OT environments are essential.

5.6 Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  • Critical infrastructure spans 16 sectors essential to society
  • OT security prioritizes safety and availability over confidentiality
  • The Purdue Model provides reference architecture for IT/OT separation
  • ICS-specific controls address unique operational requirements
  • Real-world attacks demonstrate the threat is active and serious
