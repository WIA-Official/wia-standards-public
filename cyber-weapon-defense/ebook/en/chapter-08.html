8.1 Defense Levels

DL-1 Basic DL-2 Enhanced DL-3 Advanced DL-4 Critical

Level Target Organization Threat Focus
DL-1: Basic Small organizations, limited exposure Opportunistic attacks, commodity malware
DL-2: Enhanced Medium enterprises, some critical data Targeted attacks, advanced malware
DL-3: Advanced Large enterprises, critical infrastructure Sophisticated APTs, targeted campaigns
DL-4: Critical National security, defense, CNI Nation-state cyber weapons

8.3 Certification Process

Stage Activities Duration
1. Application Submit organization details, target DL 1 week
2. Gap Assessment WIA assessor reviews current state 2-4 weeks
3. Implementation Address gaps, deploy controls 3-24 months
4. Documentation Prepare evidence package 2-4 weeks
5. Technical Audit Third-party verification, penetration testing 2-4 weeks
6. Certification Certificate issuance, listing 1-2 weeks

8.4 Continuous Improvement

  • Annual Assessment: Revalidate controls annually
  • Threat Updates: Adapt to evolving threat landscape
  • Exercise Program: Regular tabletop and technical exercises
  • Metrics Program: Track MTTD, MTTR, and coverage metrics
  • Intelligence Sharing: Participate in sharing communities

8.9 Conclusion

Cyber weapons represent one of the defining security challenges of the 21st century. As nation-states continue to develop and deploy sophisticated cyber capabilities, the need for robust defense has never been greater.

The WIA-SEC-025 Cyber Weapon Defense standard provides a comprehensive framework for meeting this challenge. Through defense in depth, continuous monitoring, threat intelligence, and coordinated response, organizations can build resilient defenses against even the most sophisticated adversaries.
