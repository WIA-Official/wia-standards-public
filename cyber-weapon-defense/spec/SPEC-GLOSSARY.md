# WIA-SEC-025: Cyber Weapon Defense - Glossary

**Standard ID:** WIA-SEC-025
**Version:** 1.0
**Document:** Glossary and Terminology
**Last Updated:** 2025-12-25

---

## A

**Active Defense**
Proactive cybersecurity measures that involve engaging with threats, rather than purely reactive defenses. May include deception technologies, threat hunting, and (where legally authorized) counter-operations.

**Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)**
A sophisticated, prolonged cyberattack by a well-resourced adversary (typically nation-state or state-sponsored) that gains unauthorized access to a network and remains undetected for extended periods.

**Anomaly Detection**
Security monitoring approach that identifies deviations from normal behavior patterns, using statistical analysis or machine learning to detect potential threats.

**APT-28 (Fancy Bear)**
Russian military intelligence cyber operations unit (GRU Unit 26165) responsible for numerous high-profile cyberattacks.

**APT-29 (Cozy Bear)**
Russian foreign intelligence cyber operations unit (SVR) known for sophisticated espionage campaigns.

**Attribution**
The process of identifying the source of a cyberattack, including the specific threat actor, organization, and nation-state responsible.

---

## B

**Behavioral Analytics**
Security monitoring technique that analyzes patterns of behavior (user, system, network) to identify suspicious or malicious activity.

**Business Continuity (BC)**
Organizational capability to continue essential functions during and after a disaster or disruption.

---

## C

**C2 (Command and Control)**
Infrastructure used by attackers to communicate with compromised systems and issue commands to malware.

**CEF (Common Event Format)**
Standardized log message format used for SIEM integration.

**CIRT (Computer Incident Response Team)**
Team responsible for responding to cybersecurity incidents.

**CISA (Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency)**
U.S. federal agency responsible for cybersecurity and infrastructure protection.

**Critical Infrastructure**
Systems and assets essential to national security, economic security, or public health and safety. Includes power grids, water systems, financial networks, telecommunications, and transportation.

**CrowdStrike**
Cybersecurity technology company providing endpoint protection and threat intelligence services.

**CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures)**
Standard for identifying publicly known cybersecurity vulnerabilities.

**CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System)**
Framework for rating the severity of security vulnerabilities (scale 0-10).

**Cyber Warfare**
Nation-state use of computer networks and digital technologies to attack adversary computer systems, networks, or infrastructure.

**Cyber Weapon**
Software, hardware, or techniques designed to cause damage, disruption, or unauthorized access to computer systems, typically developed by nation-states.

---

## D

**Dark Web**
Encrypted portion of the internet not indexed by search engines, often used for illicit activities.

**DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service)**
Cyberattack that overwhelms a target system with traffic from multiple sources, making it unavailable to legitimate users.

**DEFCON**
Defense Condition level (1-5) indicating military readiness; adapted for cybersecurity to indicate cyber defense posture.

**Defense in Depth**
Layered security approach using multiple defensive measures to protect systems.

**DLP (Data Loss Prevention)**
Technologies and policies to prevent unauthorized data exfiltration.

---

## E

**EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response)**
Security solution that monitors endpoints (workstations, servers) for suspicious activity and provides response capabilities.

**Exfiltration**
Unauthorized transfer of data from a computer or network.

**Exploit**
Code or technique that takes advantage of a vulnerability to gain unauthorized access or cause unintended behavior.

---

## F

**False Positive**
Security alert that incorrectly identifies benign activity as malicious.

**FIRST (Forum of Incident Response and Security Teams)**
Global organization of incident response teams promoting cooperation.

**Five Eyes**
Intelligence alliance between Australia, Canada, New Zealand, United Kingdom, and United States.

**Forensics (Digital)**
Scientific examination and analysis of digital evidence from computer systems.

---

## G

**GRU (Glavnoye Razvedyvatel'noye Upravleniye)**
Russian military intelligence agency, sponsor of APT-28 and other cyber operations.

---

## H

**Honeypot**
Decoy system designed to attract and detect attackers.

**HUMINT (Human Intelligence)**
Intelligence gathered from human sources, as opposed to technical means.

---

## I

**ICS (Industrial Control Systems)**
Computer systems that monitor and control industrial processes (manufacturing, energy, water treatment).

**IDS/IPS (Intrusion Detection/Prevention System)**
Security systems that monitor network traffic for suspicious activity and can block attacks.

**Indicator of Compromise (IOC)**
Artifact or observable that indicates a security breach has occurred (e.g., IP address, file hash, domain name).

**ISAC (Information Sharing and Analysis Center)**
Industry-specific organization for sharing cybersecurity threat information.

**ISP (Internet Service Provider)**
Organization that provides internet connectivity services.

---

## J

**JCOC (Joint Cyber Operations Center)**
Coordinated facility for managing cyber defense operations across military and civilian organizations.

---

## K

**Kill Chain (Cyber)**
Model describing stages of a cyberattack, from reconnaissance to objectives achieved. See also: Lockheed Martin Cyber Kill Chain.

---

## L

**Lateral Movement**
Technique used by attackers to move from one compromised system to others within a network.

**Lazarus Group**
North Korean state-sponsored APT group responsible for financial theft and espionage operations.

**Living off the Land (LOLBin)**
Attack technique using legitimate system tools and binaries for malicious purposes to evade detection.

**Log Aggregation**
Collection and centralization of log data from multiple sources for analysis.

---

## M

**Malware**
Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.

**Mean Time to Contain (MTTC)**
Average time from threat detection to containment.

**Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)**
Average time from attack initiation to detection.

**Mean Time to Recover (MTTR)**
Average time from incident to full recovery of systems and services.

**MISP (Malware Information Sharing Platform)**
Open-source threat intelligence platform for sharing indicators of compromise.

**MITRE ATT&CK**
Knowledge base of adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures based on real-world observations.

**Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**
Security process requiring two or more verification factors to authenticate users.

---

## N

**Nation-State Actor**
Cyber threat actor operating on behalf of a national government.

**NERC CIP (North American Electric Reliability Corporation Critical Infrastructure Protection)**
Cybersecurity standards for the North American power grid.

**NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology)**
U.S. federal agency that develops cybersecurity standards and frameworks.

**NVD (National Vulnerability Database)**
U.S. government repository of vulnerability data.

---

## O

**OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)**
Intelligence gathered from publicly available sources.

**OWASP (Open Web Application Security Project)**
Nonprofit foundation focused on improving software security.

---

## P

**Pass the Hash**
Attack technique that uses stolen password hashes to authenticate without knowing the actual password.

**Patch**
Software update that fixes vulnerabilities or bugs.

**Penetration Testing**
Authorized simulated cyberattack to evaluate security of systems.

**Persistence**
Attacker technique to maintain access to compromised systems across reboots and credential changes.

**Phishing**
Social engineering attack that tricks users into revealing credentials or installing malware, typically via email.

---

## Q

**QRadar**
IBM security information and event management (SIEM) platform.

---

## R

**Ransomware**
Malware that encrypts victim's data and demands payment for decryption key.

**Recorded Future**
Threat intelligence company providing real-time threat analysis.

**Resilience**
Ability of systems and organizations to withstand, recover from, and adapt to cyber incidents.

**Rootkit**
Malware that provides persistent, privileged access while hiding its presence.

---

## S

**SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition)**
Industrial control system used to monitor and control industrial processes.

**Script Kiddie**
Inexperienced attacker using pre-made tools without understanding underlying techniques (opposite of sophisticated APT).

**SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)**
Platform that collects, analyzes, and correlates security event data from multiple sources.

**SOAR (Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response)**
Platform that automates security operations and incident response.

**SOC (Security Operations Center)**
Centralized facility for monitoring, detecting, and responding to cybersecurity incidents.

**Social Engineering**
Manipulation techniques that exploit human psychology to gain unauthorized access or information.

**Spear Phishing**
Targeted phishing attack aimed at specific individuals or organizations.

**Splunk**
Data analytics platform commonly used for SIEM and security monitoring.

**STIX (Structured Threat Information Expression)**
Standardized language for describing cyber threat information.

**Supply Chain Attack**
Cyberattack that targets less-secure elements in an organization's supply chain (software vendors, contractors, etc.).

**SVR (Sluzhba Vneshney Razvedki)**
Russian Foreign Intelligence Service, sponsor of APT-29 and other cyber operations.

---

## T

**TAXII (Trusted Automated Exchange of Intelligence Information)**
Protocol for exchanging cyber threat intelligence.

**Threat Actor**
Individual, group, or organization conducting cyberattacks.

**Threat Hunting**
Proactive searching for threats that have evaded automated detection systems.

**Threat Intelligence**
Evidence-based knowledge about threats, including capabilities, intentions, and indicators.

**TLP (Traffic Light Protocol)**
Standard for sharing sensitive information (WHITE, GREEN, AMBER, RED).

**TTP (Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures)**
Patterns of activities or methods associated with specific threat actors.

---

## U

**User and Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA)**
Security technology that uses machine learning to detect abnormal user and entity behavior.

---

## V

**Virtual Patching**
Security policy that prevents exploitation of known vulnerabilities without modifying the vulnerable system.

**Vulnerability**
Weakness in a system that can be exploited to gain unauthorized access or cause harm.

---

## W

**WAF (Web Application Firewall)**
Security system that monitors and filters HTTP traffic to web applications.

**Watering Hole Attack**
Attack that compromises websites likely to be visited by target victims.

**WIA (World Certification Industry Association)**
International organization developing cybersecurity standards, including WIA-SEC-025.

**WMI (Windows Management Instrumentation)**
Windows framework for system management, sometimes abused by attackers for persistence.

---

## X

**X-Agent**
Modular backdoor malware used by APT-28.

---

## Z

**Zero Day**
Previously unknown vulnerability that has no patch available.

**Zero Trust**
Security model that assumes no user or system is trusted by default, requiring continuous verification.

**Zero-Day Exploit**
Attack that exploits a zero-day vulnerability.
