Industry Case Study

Success Story: Qi 2.2 deployment enabling 25W iPhone 16/17 charging and Samsung Galaxy S25 'Qi2 Ready' devices

Key Technologies: Qi 2.2 standard (25W-50W), Magnetic Power Profile, resonant inductive coupling

Regulatory Framework: WPC Qi certification, FCC Part 18, CE marking, SAR compliance for body-worn devices

Market Impact: Demonstrating commercial viability and establishing best practices for industry-wide adoption. The WIA standard played a crucial role in enabling rapid deployment through standardized testing protocols and certification pathways.

Key Takeaways

  • Integration phase (6-12 weeks) connects wireless charging subsystem with device power management, battery management, and user interface
  • API compliance ensures seamless communication between Qi transmitter and receiver using in-band signaling at 2 kbps data rate
  • System integration validates end-to-end charging scenarios including authentication, power negotiation, and graceful error handling
  • Cross-platform testing required across iOS, Android, and automotive systems to verify WPC Qi standard compliance
  • Interface validation includes testing USB-C fallback, simultaneous wired/wireless charging conflict resolution, and battery health monitoring
  • Automotive integration presents unique challenges: vibration tolerance, temperature extremes (-40°C to +85°C), and integration with vehicle CAN bus
