Chapter 4: Academic Records Management

4.1 Course Enrollment

WIA-EDU-010 tracks complete course enrollment history including registration, drops, adds, and withdrawals. Each enrollment event is timestamped and includes the reason for changes.

4.2 Grading Systems

The standard supports multiple grading systems:

  • Letter Grades: A, B, C, D, F with + and - modifiers
  • Pass/Fail: Binary outcomes
  • Numerical: 0-100 percentage scales
  • GPA Scale: 4.0, 5.0, or custom scales
  • International: ECTS, UK classifications, etc.

4.3 Transcript Generation

Official transcripts are generated with cryptographic signatures ensuring authenticity and tamper-evidence. Features include:

  • Digital signatures with institutional certificates
  • QR codes for instant verification
  • Blockchain anchoring for long-term integrity
  • Multiple format support (PDF, JSON, XML)
  • Automatic verification through WIA Registry

4.4 GPA Calculations

Standardized GPA calculation methods ensure consistency:

  • Semester GPA
  • Cumulative GPA
  • Major GPA
  • Weighted vs. unweighted
  • Transfer credit handling

4.5 Degree Progress Tracking

Students can track progress toward degree completion with automated audit tools showing:

  • Requirements completed
  • Requirements in progress
  • Remaining requirements
  • Substitutions and waivers
  • Projected graduation date

Best Practice

Maintain complete historical records. Even dropped courses should be tracked for audit purposes, though they may not appear on official transcripts. This transparency helps students and advisors make informed decisions.
