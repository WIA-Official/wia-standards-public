Chapter 5: Enrollment and Attendance Tracking

5.1 Enrollment Status Management

The WIA-EDU-010 standard defines precise enrollment statuses:

  • Active: Currently enrolled and attending
  • Leave of Absence: Temporarily not attending
  • Withdrawn: Voluntarily left
  • Graduated: Completed program
  • Suspended: Temporarily barred from attendance
  • Expelled: Permanently barred

5.2 Attendance Tracking Methods

Multiple attendance tracking approaches are supported:

  • Daily attendance (K-12 primary use)
  • Course-level attendance (higher education)
  • Time-based tracking with clock-in/out
  • Automated tracking via LMS logins
  • Biometric verification (where permitted)

5.3 Absence Categories

Clear categorization of absences helps with compliance and student support:

  • Present: Attended as scheduled
  • Excused Absence: Legitimate reason with documentation
  • Unexcused Absence: No valid reason provided
  • Tardy: Arrived late
  • Early Departure: Left before scheduled end

5.4 Attendance Analytics

Attendance data powers early intervention systems:

  • Attendance rate calculations
  • Trend analysis and predictions
  • At-risk student identification
  • Correlation with academic performance
  • Automated alerts to advisors

Privacy Consideration

Attendance tracking, especially with biometric data, requires careful privacy protection. Students must be informed of tracking methods, data retention, and can object to certain types of tracking under GDPR.

5.5 Integration with Financial Aid

Attendance verification is critical for financial aid compliance. The standard supports automated reporting for:

  • Last date of attendance for withdrawn students
  • Verification of enrollment for aid disbursement
  • Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) tracking
