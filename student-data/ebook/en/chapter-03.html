Chapter 3: Privacy and Compliance

3.1 FERPA Compliance

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) is the cornerstone of student privacy in the United States. WIA-EDU-010 implements FERPA requirements at the technical level.

Directory Information

FERPA allows institutions to disclose "directory information" without consent if students are notified and given the option to opt-out. WIA-EDU-010 makes this explicit:

  • Name, address, telephone number, email address
  • Date and place of birth
  • Dates of attendance, enrollment status
  • Degrees and awards received
  • Most recent previous educational institution attended

Access Control

3.2 GDPR Compliance

The General Data Protection Regulation provides comprehensive data protection rights. WIA-EDU-010 implements all GDPR requirements:

Right to Access (Article 15)

Students can request and receive a copy of all their personal data. The system must provide:

  • Complete data export in machine-readable format (JSON)
  • List of all data processing activities
  • Third parties who have received the data
  • Data retention periods
  • Source of data if not collected from student

Right to Rectification (Article 16)

Students can correct inaccurate data. The system tracks:

  • All correction requests
  • Approval workflow
  • Version history of changes
  • Notification to third parties who received incorrect data

Right to Erasure (Article 17)

Important Limitation

Educational institutions may retain records for legitimate purposes (e.g., degree verification, legal requirements). The right to erasure is balanced against institutional obligations to maintain academic records.

Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

The cornerstone of WIA-EDU-010! Students can:

  • Export all data in WIA-EDU-010 JSON format
  • Transfer directly to another institution
  • Receive data within 30 days
  • Free of charge

WIA-EDU-010 implements a granular consent framework:

Purpose Legal Basis Revocable?
Academic Records Contractual Necessity No
Directory Information Legitimate Interest Yes (opt-out)
Marketing Consent Yes
Research Consent Yes
Third-Party Sharing Consent Yes

3.4 Data Minimization

Collect only what's necessary. WIA-EDU-010 enforces data minimization by:

  • Required vs. optional field designation
  • Purpose-specific data collection
  • Automatic data expiration
  • Regular data audits

3.5 Audit Trails

Every access and modification is logged:

  • Who accessed the data
  • What data was accessed
  • When it was accessed
  • Purpose of access
  • Any modifications made

Privacy by Design

WIA-EDU-010 embodies "privacy by design" principles: privacy is built into the system architecture, not added as an afterthought. Default settings are privacy-protective, and students have clear, accessible controls.

3.6 International Considerations

Beyond FERPA and GDPR, the standard accommodates:

  • PIPEDA (Canada): Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act
  • POPIA (South Africa): Protection of Personal Information Act
  • LGPD (Brazil): Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados
  • APPI (Japan): Act on the Protection of Personal Information
