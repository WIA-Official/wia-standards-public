Chapter 5: Safety and Certification

Safety is paramount in battery technology. This chapter outlines the safety standards, testing requirements, and certification processes that must be documented in the battery passport. Comprehensive safety documentation protects users, enables proper handling, and ensures regulatory compliance across all markets.

Safety Certification Standards

Batteries intended for commercial use must comply with numerous safety standards depending on their application and target markets. The WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT standard requires documentation of all applicable certifications.

Standard Scope Application
UN 38.3 Transport safety testing All lithium batteries for transport
IEC 62619 Industrial applications safety Stationary storage, industrial use
IEC 62660 EV battery cell testing Electric vehicle batteries
UL 2580 EV battery safety North American market EVs
SAE J2464 EV battery abuse testing Automotive applications
GB/T 31485 Chinese EV battery standard Chinese market compliance

Battery Management System Safety

The Battery Management System (BMS) is critical to battery safety. The passport documents the BMS specifications and safety features implemented.

Required BMS Safety Features

  • Overvoltage Protection: Prevents cell voltage from exceeding safe limits
  • Undervoltage Protection: Prevents deep discharge damage
  • Overcurrent Protection: Limits current during charge and discharge
  • Temperature Monitoring: Cell-level temperature sensing and response
  • Thermal Runaway Detection: Early warning of thermal events
  • Cell Balancing: Active or passive balancing to prevent imbalance
  • Isolation Monitoring: Detection of ground faults in HV systems
  • Emergency Disconnect: Ability to disconnect pack in emergency

Safety Incident Reporting

Any safety incidents involving the battery must be documented in the passport. This creates a permanent record that can inform safety improvements and support incident investigations.

Incident Reporting Requirements

The following events must be recorded as safety incidents:

  • Thermal events (fire, smoke, excessive heat)
  • Mechanical damage affecting cell integrity
  • Electrical faults (internal short circuits, isolation failures)
  • BMS failures resulting in protection activation
  • User-reported safety concerns
  • Recall notifications affecting the battery model
