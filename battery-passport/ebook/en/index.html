WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT

Battery Passport Standards for Sustainable Energy Storage

WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT establishes comprehensive standards for digital battery passports, enabling complete lifecycle tracking, environmental compliance, and circular economy practices for batteries. This specification aligns with the European Union Battery Regulation (EU) 2023/1542 and global sustainability initiatives, providing a robust framework for battery transparency and accountability across the entire value chain.

Standard Overview

The Battery Passport is a digital identity document that accompanies every battery throughout its entire lifecycle—from raw material extraction to end-of-life recycling. This revolutionary approach to battery management addresses critical challenges in the energy transition: ensuring ethical sourcing, optimizing battery performance, enabling second-life applications, and achieving true circularity in battery materials.

As the world rapidly transitions to electric vehicles and renewable energy storage, batteries have become one of the most critical components of modern infrastructure. The WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT standard provides the technical framework necessary to track, verify, and optimize these essential energy storage devices throughout their operational life and beyond. This framework ensures transparency, promotes sustainability, and builds trust among all stakeholders in the battery value chain.

The standard addresses the growing need for accountability in battery production and usage. With increasing regulatory requirements worldwide, particularly the EU Battery Regulation, organizations need a comprehensive solution that provides both compliance and operational benefits. WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT delivers this through standardized data formats, interoperable systems, and blockchain-based verification.

Regulatory Framework

EU Battery Regulation (2023/1542)

The European Union's Battery Regulation mandates digital battery passports for all industrial and EV batteries by February 2027. WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT is designed to fully comply with these requirements while providing additional features for global interoperability and extended use cases.

  • Mandatory for batteries greater than 2 kWh capacity
  • QR code linked to digital passport on every battery
  • Carbon footprint declaration required with third-party verification
  • Recycled content tracking mandatory with increasing targets
  • Due diligence documentation for entire supply chain

Quick Start Example

// Initialize Battery Passport
const passport = new BatteryPassport({
    batteryId: "WIA-BP-2025-001234",
    manufacturer: "EcoCell Technologies",
    chemistry: "NMC811",
    nominalCapacity: 75000, // Wh
    productionDate: "2025-01-15",
    certifications: ["UN38.3", "IEC62619", "EU-2023/1542"]
});

// Register on blockchain
await passport.register({
    network: "WIA-CHAIN",
    visibility: "public",
    accessControl: ["manufacturer", "regulators", "consumers"]
});

// Add lifecycle event
await passport.addEvent({
    type: "INSTALLATION",
    timestamp: new Date(),
    location: "Munich, Germany",
    details: {
        vehicleId: "VIN123456789",
        stateOfHealth: 100
    }
});
