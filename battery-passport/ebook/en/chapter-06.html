Chapter 6: Recycling and Second-Life

The circular economy vision for batteries depends on effective recycling and second-life applications. This chapter details the standards and processes for battery end-of-life management, including repurposing assessments, recycling procedures, and material recovery tracking. The battery passport plays a crucial role in enabling these circular economy activities.

Second-Life Assessment

When a battery no longer meets the requirements for its original application, it may still have significant value for less demanding uses. Second-life applications extend the useful life of batteries and maximize the value extracted before recycling.

Second-Life Suitability Criteria

A battery is potentially suitable for second-life applications if it meets the following criteria:

  • State of Health: Between 60% and 80% of original capacity
  • Safety Status: No unresolved safety incidents or concerns
  • Cell Balance: Voltage variation between cells within acceptable limits
  • Degradation Pattern: Linear degradation without sudden capacity loss
  • Physical Condition: No visible damage or corrosion

Recycling Requirements

When a battery reaches true end-of-life, proper recycling ensures maximum material recovery and safe disposal of hazardous components. The EU Battery Regulation sets ambitious recycling efficiency targets that require sophisticated processes.

Extended Producer Responsibility

Battery manufacturers bear responsibility for the end-of-life management of their products under Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) schemes. The passport supports EPR compliance by tracking the chain of custody through to final recycling.

EPR Obligations

  • Collection Infrastructure: Ensure adequate collection points for end-of-life batteries
  • Take-Back Programs: Accept returns of batteries from consumers and operators
  • Recycling Contracts: Establish relationships with licensed recyclers
  • Reporting: Submit annual reports on collection and recycling rates
  • Financial Responsibility: Fund collection and recycling operations

Material Traceability in Recycling

The passport maintains material traceability even through recycling, enabling verification of recycled content claims in new batteries. Recovered materials are assigned new batch identifiers linked to the source passports.
