Chapter 4: Environmental Compliance

Environmental accountability is a core principle of WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT. This chapter details the methods and requirements for calculating, documenting, and verifying the environmental impact of batteries throughout their lifecycle. From carbon footprint calculations to recycled content tracking, these standards ensure that sustainability claims are credible and verifiable.

Carbon Footprint Calculation

The carbon footprint of a battery encompasses all greenhouse gas emissions associated with its production, use, and end-of-life processing. The WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT standard requires calculation according to the Product Environmental Footprint (PEF) methodology, with specific adaptations for batteries as defined in the EU Battery Regulation.

Calculation Boundaries

The carbon footprint calculation covers the following lifecycle stages:

  • Raw Material Acquisition: Mining, extraction, and initial processing of all battery materials
  • Material Processing: Refining, purification, and preparation of battery-grade materials
  • Cell Manufacturing: Electrode production, cell assembly, and formation
  • Pack Assembly: Module and pack construction, BMS integration
  • Distribution: Transportation from factory to point of installation

Recycled Content Requirements

The EU Battery Regulation mandates minimum levels of recycled content in new batteries, with requirements increasing over time. The passport must document recycled content percentages for key materials and provide evidence of sourcing.

Recycled Content Targets

  • Cobalt (2030): Minimum 12% recycled content, increasing to 20% by 2035
  • Lithium (2030): Minimum 4% recycled content, increasing to 10% by 2035
  • Nickel (2030): Minimum 4% recycled content, increasing to 12% by 2035
  • Lead (2030): Minimum 85% recycled content for lead-acid batteries

Due Diligence Requirements

Battery manufacturers must demonstrate responsible sourcing practices through documented due diligence processes. This is particularly important for materials associated with human rights risks or environmental concerns.

Due Diligence Framework

The WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT standard aligns with the OECD Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-Affected and High-Risk Areas. Key requirements include:

  • Establishment of strong management systems
  • Identification and assessment of supply chain risks
  • Design and implementation of risk mitigation strategies
  • Independent third-party audit of due diligence practices
  • Annual public reporting on due diligence efforts

Environmental Performance Monitoring

Beyond static declarations, the passport supports ongoing environmental performance monitoring during operation. This enables optimization of charging patterns and usage to minimize environmental impact.

// Environmental Performance Metrics
interface EnvironmentalMetrics {
    energyEfficiency: {
        roundTripEfficiency: number;  // Percentage
        selfDischargeRate: number;    // Percentage per month
        auxiliaryPowerConsumption: number;  // kWh per day
    };
    operationalFootprint: {
        totalEnergyThroughput: number;  // kWh
        estimatedCO2Avoided: number;    // kg CO2e
        gridMixUsed: string;            // Region identifier
    };
    sustainabilityScore: {
        score: number;  // 0-100
        factors: {
            efficiency: number;
            longevity: number;
            recycledContent: number;
            carbonFootprint: number;
        };
    };
}
