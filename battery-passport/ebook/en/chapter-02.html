Chapter 2: Battery Lifecycle Tracking

Lifecycle tracking is the foundation of the battery passport system. By capturing data at every stage of a battery's existence—from raw material extraction to final recycling—we create a complete digital twin that enables transparency, optimization, and circularity throughout the battery value chain.

The Complete Battery Lifecycle

Understanding the battery lifecycle is essential for implementing effective tracking systems. A battery passes through seven distinct stages, each with unique data requirements and tracking challenges. The WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT standard defines comprehensive data schemas for each stage, ensuring that no critical information is lost as the battery moves through its lifecycle.

1
Raw Material Extraction

The journey begins at mines and extraction facilities around the world. Key materials include lithium (primarily from Australia, Chile, and Argentina), cobalt (primarily from the Democratic Republic of Congo), nickel (from Indonesia, Philippines, and Russia), manganese (from South Africa and Australia), and graphite (from China and Mozambique).

At this stage, the passport captures mining facility identification, geographic coordinates of extraction sites, extraction date and batch identifiers, environmental permits and compliance records, labor practice certifications, and initial quality assessments of raw materials.

2
Material Processing and Refining

Raw materials undergo processing to achieve the purity required for battery production. Lithium is converted to battery-grade lithium hydroxide or carbonate. Cobalt and nickel are refined to remove impurities. Graphite is purified and sometimes coated with carbon.

Data captured includes processing facility details, input material traceability links, processing methods and parameters, output material specifications, energy consumption and emissions data, and quality certifications achieved.

3
Cell Manufacturing

Processed materials are assembled into battery cells through a complex manufacturing process. This includes electrode coating and calendering, cell assembly (winding or stacking), electrolyte filling, formation cycling, and quality testing and grading.

Manufacturing data captured includes production line identification, manufacturing date and time stamps, process parameters (temperature, humidity, pressures), formation cycle data, initial capacity and impedance measurements, and quality grade classification.

4
Pack Assembly and Integration

Individual cells are assembled into modules and packs, then integrated into their final application. This stage involves cell sorting and matching, thermal management system installation, battery management system (BMS) configuration, safety system integration, and final testing and certification.

Integration data includes pack configuration details, BMS firmware version and parameters, safety test results, calibration data, and final product certifications.

5
First Life Operation

The battery enters service in its primary application, whether in an electric vehicle, stationary storage system, or other device. Throughout this phase, operational data is continuously collected and linked to the passport.

Operational data includes charge and discharge cycles, state of health (SoH) evolution, usage patterns and duty cycles, maintenance events, firmware updates, and incident reports if any.

6
Second Life Application

When a battery no longer meets the requirements for its original application (typically below 80% SoH for EVs), it may be repurposed for less demanding applications such as stationary energy storage. The passport facilitates this transition by providing complete history.

Second-life data includes decommissioning assessment, repurposing facility information, reconfiguration details if applicable, new application specifications, and continued operational monitoring.

7
End-of-Life Processing

When the battery reaches end of life, proper recycling ensures that valuable materials are recovered and hazardous substances are safely handled. The passport provides critical information for safe and efficient processing.

End-of-life data includes collection and transport records, recycling facility certification, dismantling process documentation, material recovery quantities, and final disposal records for non-recoverable materials.

Event Tracking System

The WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT standard defines a comprehensive event tracking system that captures significant occurrences throughout the battery lifecycle. Each event is timestamped, geolocated, and linked to the responsible party.

// Lifecycle Event Types
enum LifecycleEventType {
    // Manufacturing Events
    RAW_MATERIAL_RECEIVED = "raw_material_received",
    CELL_MANUFACTURED = "cell_manufactured",
    PACK_ASSEMBLED = "pack_assembled",
    QUALITY_TEST_COMPLETED = "quality_test_completed",
    
    // Operational Events
    INSTALLATION = "installation",
    CHARGE_CYCLE = "charge_cycle",
    MAINTENANCE_PERFORMED = "maintenance_performed",
    FIRMWARE_UPDATED = "firmware_updated",
    INCIDENT_REPORTED = "incident_reported",
    
    // Transition Events
    OWNERSHIP_TRANSFERRED = "ownership_transferred",
    DECOMMISSIONED = "decommissioned",
    REPURPOSED = "repurposed",
    
    // End-of-Life Events
    COLLECTED_FOR_RECYCLING = "collected_for_recycling",
    DISMANTLED = "dismantled",
    MATERIALS_RECOVERED = "materials_recovered"
}

// Event Structure
interface LifecycleEvent {
    eventId: string;
    passportId: string;
    eventType: LifecycleEventType;
    timestamp: ISO8601DateTime;
    location: {
        facility: string;
        address: string;
        coordinates: {
            latitude: number;
            longitude: number;
        };
        country: ISO3166CountryCode;
    };
    actor: {
        organizationId: string;
        organizationName: string;
        role: string;
        certifications: string[];
    };
    data: Record<string, any>;
    verification: {
        method: "manual" | "automated" | "blockchain";
        verifierId: string;
        signature: string;
    };
}

State of Health Tracking

One of the most critical aspects of lifecycle tracking is monitoring the battery's State of Health (SoH). SoH represents the current condition of the battery compared to its original specifications and is a key factor in determining the battery's remaining useful life and value.

SoH Range Classification Typical Application Action Required
100-90% Excellent Primary EV use Normal operation
90-80% Good Primary EV use Monitor closely
80-70% Fair Second-life storage Repurposing assessment
70-50% Degraded Low-power applications Limited use cases
Below 50% End of Life Recycling Decommission and recycle

Data Collection Methods

Lifecycle data is collected through various methods depending on the stage and type of information:

Manufacturing Data Collection

During manufacturing, data is captured automatically through integration with manufacturing execution systems (MES), quality management systems (QMS), and laboratory information management systems (LIMS). Bar codes and RFID tags enable automatic identification and data association throughout the production process.

Operational Data Collection

During operation, the Battery Management System (BMS) continuously monitors key parameters and can transmit data to the passport system via telematics. Key metrics include voltage, current, and temperature of individual cells, calculated state of charge and state of health, cycle count and depth of discharge statistics, error codes and fault conditions, and charging behavior and patterns.

// BMS Data Integration Example
class BMSIntegration {
    private passport: BatteryPassport;
    private updateInterval: number = 3600000; // 1 hour

    async syncOperationalData(bmsData: BMSReading): Promise<void> {
        const event: LifecycleEvent = {
            eventId: generateUUID(),
            passportId: this.passport.id,
            eventType: LifecycleEventType.OPERATIONAL_UPDATE,
            timestamp: new Date().toISOString(),
            location: await this.getCurrentLocation(),
            actor: {
                organizationId: this.passport.currentOwner,
                organizationName: "Vehicle Owner",
                role: "operator",
                certifications: []
            },
            data: {
                stateOfCharge: bmsData.soc,
                stateOfHealth: bmsData.soh,
                cycleCount: bmsData.cycles,
                cellVoltages: bmsData.cellVoltages,
                temperatures: bmsData.temperatures,
                errorCodes: bmsData.errors
            },
            verification: {
                method: "automated",
                verifierId: bmsData.bmsId,
                signature: await this.signData(bmsData)
            }
        };

        await this.passport.addEvent(event);
    }
}

Traceability Chain

The WIA-BATTERY_PASSPORT standard ensures complete traceability by linking each entity in the value chain. Every material batch can be traced back to its origin, and every processing step is documented with input and output links. This creates an unbroken chain of custody from mine to recycling facility.

Data Retention Requirements

Different categories of lifecycle data have different retention requirements based on regulatory mandates and practical needs:

  • Identity data: Retained permanently (battery lifetime plus 10 years after recycling)
  • Manufacturing data: Retained for 15 years after production
  • Operational data (summary): Retained for battery lifetime plus 5 years
  • Operational data (detailed): Retained for 2 years, then summarized
  • Safety incident data: Retained permanently
  • End-of-life data: Retained for 10 years after recycling
