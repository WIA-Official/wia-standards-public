Chapter 1: Introduction to EU Battery Regulation

Understanding Regulation 2023/1542 and Digital Battery Passport Requirements

1.1 The EU Battery Regulation

Regulation (EU) 2023/1542

Concerning batteries and waste batteries, repealing Directive 2006/66/EC

The EU Battery Regulation represents the most comprehensive battery legislation in the world. It establishes sustainability requirements throughout the entire battery lifecycle - from sourcing of raw materials to end-of-life recycling.

[KEY] Key Requirements

  • Digital Battery Passport for all EV, industrial, and LMT batteries
  • Carbon footprint declaration and performance classes
  • Minimum recycled content requirements
  • Supply chain due diligence
  • QR code access to passport data

1.2 Implementation Timeline

Aug 2025
Industrial Batteries

Digital passport required for industrial batteries >2kWh

Aug 2026
LMT Batteries

E-bikes, e-scooters, and light means of transport batteries

Feb 2027
EV Batteries

Electric vehicle traction batteries

2031
Recycled Content

Mandatory recycled content: Co 16%, Li 6%, Ni 6%

1.3 Battery Categories

Category Description Passport Required
EV Battery Electric vehicle traction batteries Yes (Feb 2027)
Industrial Battery Batteries >2kWh for industrial use Yes (Aug 2025)
LMT Battery Light means of transport (e-bikes, etc.) Yes (Aug 2026)
SLI Battery Starting, lighting, ignition batteries No
Portable Battery Consumer electronics batteries No

1.4 WIA Standard Alignment

The WIA Battery Passport standard is designed to fully comply with EU Regulation 2023/1542 while providing additional interoperability features:

[CHECK] WIA Compliance Features

  • [OK] Unique battery identifier (DID-based)
  • [OK] Manufacturer information
  • [OK] Battery model and chemistry
  • [OK] Carbon footprint declaration
  • [OK] Recycled content information
  • [OK] State of Health (SoH) tracking
  • [OK] QR code with interoperable data access

1.5 The 4-Phase WIA Standard Structure

Like all WIA standards, the Battery Passport follows the 4-phase structure:

Phase 1: Data Format

Defines JSON-LD schemas for battery identification, carbon footprint, material composition, and lifecycle data.

Phase 2: Algorithms

Specifies algorithms for carbon footprint calculation, State of Health determination, and recycled content verification.

Phase 3: Protocol

Defines REST API endpoints, QR code encoding standards, and authentication mechanisms.

Phase 4: Integration

Provides integration guides for EU ESPR registry, supply chain systems, and recycling facilities.
