Chapter 8

📜 WIA-SATELLITE Standard

Certification Requirements and Implementation Guidelines

8.1 Standard Overview

The WIA-SATELLITE standard establishes comprehensive requirements for satellite systems certification, ensuring quality, reliability, safety, and interoperability across the global satellite industry. This standard applies to satellite manufacturers, operators, ground segment providers, and service integrators.

8.2 Certification Levels

WIA-SATELLITE offers four certification levels to accommodate organizations at different stages of capability maturity. Each level builds upon the previous, with increasing requirements for quality, reliability, and operational excellence.

🥉 Bronze

Entry-level certification for emerging satellite companies and educational institutions.

Requirements:

Basic documentation, safety compliance, spectrum coordination

🥈 Silver

Standard certification for commercial satellite operators and service providers.

Requirements:

Quality management, reliability analysis, ground segment verification

🥇 Gold

Advanced certification for critical infrastructure and high-reliability systems.

Requirements:

Redundancy verification, 99.9% availability, comprehensive testing

💎 Platinum

Elite certification for mission-critical and defense-grade satellite systems.

Requirements:

99.99% availability, cybersecurity audit, space sustainability compliance

8.3 Technical Requirements

8.3.1 Space Segment Requirements

  • SS-001: Satellite design shall comply with applicable space debris mitigation guidelines (IADC, FCC, ESA)
  • SS-002: End-of-life disposal plan required for all orbital altitudes (25-year rule for LEO, graveyard orbit for GEO)
  • SS-003: Trackable by Space Surveillance Network (SSN) or equivalent with TLE publication
  • SS-004: Collision avoidance capability required for LEO satellites above 400 km
  • SS-005: Power system shall maintain 90% of BOL capacity for planned mission life
  • SS-006: Attitude control accuracy shall meet payload requirements with documented margins
  • SS-007: Propulsion system (if equipped) shall have redundant isolation for safe passivation
  • SS-008: Thermal control shall maintain all components within qualified temperature ranges

8.3.2 Ground Segment Requirements

  • GS-001: Ground station availability shall meet mission requirements (typically ≥99.5%)
  • GS-002: Command authentication and encryption required for all TT&C links
  • GS-003: Orbit determination accuracy shall support collision avoidance requirements
  • GS-004: Backup command capability required within 24 hours of primary station outage
  • GS-005: Telemetry archival for minimum 5 years post-mission
  • GS-006: Mission control procedures documented and tested annually

8.3.3 Communications Requirements

  • COM-001: ITU frequency coordination completed before launch
  • COM-002: Interference mitigation procedures documented and tested
  • COM-003: Power flux density limits compliance verified
  • COM-004: Link budget analysis with ≥3 dB margin for all links
  • COM-005: Protocol compliance (DVB-S2/S2X, CCSDS, or equivalent) verified

8.4 Quality and Reliability Requirements

🔧 Quality Management

All certified organizations shall implement a quality management system compliant with ISO 9001 or equivalent. For space hardware, compliance with ECSS-Q-ST-20 (European) or MIL-HDBK-217 (US) reliability prediction standards is required.

8.5 Space Sustainability Requirements

WIA-SATELLITE places strong emphasis on space sustainability, recognizing that the orbital environment is a shared resource that must be protected for future generations.

🌍 Sustainability Commitments

Debris Mitigation: All satellites shall comply with 25-year post-mission disposal rule for LEO or graveyard orbit disposal for GEO.

Passivation: All stored energy (propellant, batteries, flywheels) shall be depleted or safed at end of life to prevent explosions.

Trackability: Satellites shall be trackable by space surveillance systems; radar reflectors recommended for small satellites.

Collision Avoidance: LEO operators shall participate in conjunction screening and execute avoidance maneuvers as needed.

Transparency: Operators shall share orbital data with space traffic coordination entities.

8.6 Cybersecurity Requirements

🔒 Security Framework

Satellite systems are critical infrastructure requiring robust cybersecurity. WIA-SATELLITE mandates security measures appropriate to mission criticality, following NIST Cybersecurity Framework principles.

  • SEC-001: Command link encryption (AES-256 or equivalent) required for all missions
  • SEC-002: Authentication for all ground-to-space commands (digital signatures)
  • SEC-003: Secure software development lifecycle (SSDLC) for flight software
  • SEC-004: Vulnerability assessment conducted prior to launch
  • SEC-005: Incident response plan documented and tested
  • SEC-006: Ground system network segmentation and access controls
  • SEC-007: Security updates capability for flight software (Gold/Platinum)

8.7 Certification Process

Application Submission

Organization submits application with system documentation, design descriptions, and self-assessment against applicable requirements.

Documentation Review

WIA assessors review submitted documentation for completeness and compliance. Gap analysis provided for any deficiencies.

Technical Assessment

On-site or virtual assessment of facilities, procedures, and technical capabilities. Includes interviews with key personnel.

Testing Verification

Review of test results, analysis reports, and operational demonstrations. May include witnessing of key tests for higher certification levels.

Certification Decision

WIA certification board reviews assessment results and issues certification decision. Valid for 3 years with annual surveillance.

Continuous Compliance

Certified organizations submit annual compliance reports and undergo surveillance audits. Major changes require notification and re-assessment.

8.8 Implementation Guidelines

📚 Getting Started with WIA-SATELLITE

Step 1: Assess current capabilities against WIA-SATELLITE requirements using the self-assessment checklist.

Step 2: Identify gaps and develop implementation roadmap with timeline and resources.

Step 3: Implement required quality management, documentation, and technical capabilities.

Step 4: Conduct internal audits to verify compliance before formal certification.

Step 5: Submit certification application with all required documentation.

Step 6: Support WIA assessment activities and address any findings.

Step 7: Maintain certification through continuous compliance and improvement.

🌐 Integration with Other Standards

WIA-SATELLITE is designed to complement existing industry standards:

  • ISO 9001: Quality Management Systems
  • ECSS: European Cooperation for Space Standardization
  • CCSDS: Consultative Committee for Space Data Systems
  • ITU: International Telecommunication Union regulations
  • NIST CSF: Cybersecurity Framework
  • IADC: Inter-Agency Space Debris Coordination Committee guidelines

8.9 Chapter Summary

📋 Key Takeaways

• WIA-SATELLITE provides comprehensive certification for satellite systems

• Four certification levels: Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum

• Requirements cover space segment, ground segment, and communications

• Strong emphasis on space sustainability and debris mitigation

• Cybersecurity requirements protect critical infrastructure

• Certification process includes documentation, assessment, and verification

• Integration with existing international standards (ISO, ECSS, CCSDS)

• 3-year certification validity with annual surveillance
