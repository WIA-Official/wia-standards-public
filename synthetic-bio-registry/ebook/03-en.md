# Chapter 3: iGEM Registry and Global Standards

## Overview

The International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) competition and its associated Registry of Standard Biological Parts represents the world's largest collection of synthetic biology components. This chapter explores iGEM's history, the registry's structure, contribution processes, global impact, and role in establishing international standards.

## 3.1 iGEM Competition Overview

### Origins at MIT (2003-2004)

**2003 - Independent Activities Period (IAP)**:
- Tom Knight's course at MIT
- Students built biological systems from standard parts
- ~10 students participated
- First proof-of-concept for BioBricks

**2004 - First iGEM Competition**:
- Summer program at MIT
- 5 teams from US institutions
- Each team designed and built genetic systems
- Presentations and judging in fall
- Demonstrated feasibility of undergraduate synthetic biology

**Founding Principles**:
- Education through hands-on research
- Open sharing of parts and designs
- Standardization enables rapid prototyping
- Undergraduate creativity drives innovation
- Fun and competitive environment

### Growth and Expansion

**Explosive Growth**:
- **2005**: 13 teams (international expansion begins)
- **2006**: 32 teams (Registry goes online)
- **2008**: 84 teams (spreading across continents)
- **2010**: 130 teams (Asia becomes major participant)
- **2012**: 190+ teams (Africa joins)
- **2016**: 300+ teams (iGEM Foundation incorporated)
- **2020**: 250+ teams (COVID-19 impacts but continues)
- **2025**: 400+ teams from 50+ countries

**Geographic Distribution** (2025):
- North America: 30%
- Asia: 35%
- Europe: 25%
- South America: 5%
- Africa: 3%
- Oceania: 2%

**Participation Levels**:
- Over 50,000 students participated (2004-2025)
- 7,000+ teams total
- 3,000+ institutions
- All seven continents (research stations in Antarctica!)

### Competition Structure

**Tracks** (specialized categories):
- **Diagnostics**: Disease detection, health monitoring
- **Energy**: Biofuels, energy generation/storage
- **Environment**: Pollution cleanup, sustainability
- **Food & Nutrition**: Food security, supplements
- **Foundational Advance**: New tools and techniques
- **Manufacturing**: Biomaterials, chemical production
- **New Application**: Novel applications
- **Therapeutics**: Drug development, disease treatment
- **Software**: Computational tools for synthetic biology
- **Hardware**: Physical devices and instruments

**Team Composition**:
- Typically 5-15 undergraduates
- 1-3 faculty advisors
- Sometimes graduate students as mentors
- Multidisciplinary (biology, engineering, design, business)

**Project Timeline**:
- **January-March**: Team formation, brainstorming
- **April-May**: Design and planning
- **June-August**: Laboratory work and construction
- **September**: Final push, wiki completion
- **October**: Regional Jamborees or presentations
- **November**: Grand Jamboree (championship event)

**Deliverables**:
- Functional genetic system or prototype
- BioBrick parts contributed to Registry
- Comprehensive wiki documenting project
- Presentation and poster
- Registry documentation
- (Optional) Hardware, software, models

**Judging Criteria**:
- Impact and significance
- Proof of concept
- Human practices (ethics, safety, outreach)
- Design quality
- Experimental execution
- Parts documentation
- Wiki quality
- Presentation effectiveness

**Awards**:
- Gold/Silver/Bronze medals (achievement-based)
- Track awards (best in each category)
- Special prizes (Best Wiki, Best Presentation, etc.)
- Grand Prize
- Regional winners

### Part Naming Convention

**Format**: BBa_XXXYYY (BioBrick according to iGEM format)

**Components**:
- **BBa_**: BioBrick assembly standard prefix
- **XXX**: Part type or source indicator
- **YYY**: Serial number within category

**Examples**:
- **BBa_E0040**: E = common part, 0040 = GFP
- **BBa_J23100**: J = constitutive promoter library
- **BBa_K000001**: K = iGEM year-specific, team specific
- **BBa_R0010**: R = regulatory (lac promoter)
- **BBa_B0034**: B = biological component (RBS)

**Year-Based Parts** (K-series):
- Format: BBa_K[YY][TTT][NNN]
- YY: Year (e.g., 23 for 2023)
- TTT: Team number
- NNN: Part number from that team

**Example**: BBa_K3780001
- K: iGEM part
- 37: Year 2023
- 800: Team 800
- 001: First part from that team

### Part Status Levels

**Status Categories**:

**Planning**:
- Conceptual stage
- Not yet constructed
- Placeholder in registry

**Existing**:
- Part has been constructed
- Sequence available
- May or may not have physical sample

**Available**:
- Physical sample in Registry distribution
- Can be ordered
- Sequence verified

**Missing**:
- Previously available but sample lost
- May be reconstructed on request

**Deleted**:
- Removed from active registry
- Kept for historical record

## 3.3 Part Submission Process

### Standard Submission Workflow

**Step 1: Design**
- Decide what part to contribute
- Check if part already exists
- Design sequence (remove illegal restriction sites)
- Plan characterization experiments

**Step 2: Construction**
- Clone into BioBricks vector (pSB1C3, etc.)
- Sequence verify
- Test functionality
- Characterize performance

**Step 3: Registry Submission**
- Create account on parts.igem.org
- Fill out online submission form
- Enter part information:
  - Name and short description
  - Part type
  - Full sequence
  - Expected behavior
  - Design notes

**Step 4: Physical Sample Submission**
- Send plasmid DNA to iGEM HQ
- Include completed submission form
- Registry staff:
  - Verify sequence
  - Archive stock
  - Make available for distribution

**Step 5: Documentation**
- Add experimental data
- Upload characterization results
- Include protocols used
- Add references

**Submission Form Fields**:
```
- Part Name: BBa_KXXXXXX
- Part Type: Promoter/RBS/CDS/etc.
- Short Description: (one line)
- Long Description: (detailed)
- Sequence: (paste DNA sequence)
- Length: (auto-calculated)
- Origin: (organism/synthetic)
- Design Considerations
- References
```

### DNA Distribution Kit

**Annual Part Kit**:
iGEM distributes standard parts to teams:

**Kit Contents** (2025):
- 4 96-well plates
- 384 most commonly used parts
- Lyophilized DNA in wells
- Includes:
  - Popular promoters
  - Standard RBS sequences
  - Fluorescent reporters
  - Regulatory elements
  - Standard vectors

**Usage**:
1. Add water to well
2. Transform into competent cells
3. Plate and select
4. Verify and use

**Benefits**:
- Immediate access to common parts
- No need to order or synthesize
- Standardized quality
- Enables rapid prototyping

**Popular Kit Parts**:
- BBa_J23100-119 (Anderson promoter library)
- BBa_E0040 (GFP)
- BBa_E1010 (RFP)
- BBa_B0034 (strong RBS)
- BBa_B0015 (double terminator)
- Standard plasmid backbones

### Quality Control Challenges

**Issues**:
- **Sequence Errors**: Mutations, incorrect sequences
- **Functional Failures**: Parts don't work as described
- **Lost Samples**: Physical stocks degraded or lost
- **Poor Documentation**: Incomplete information
- **No Characterization**: Parts never tested
- **Contamination**: Wrong plasmid in tube

**Mitigation Efforts**:

**Verification**:
- Sequence all submitted samples
- Flag discrepancies
- Request corrections from submitters

**Curation**:
- Registry staff review submissions
- Community curation programs
- Star system for quality rating

**Improvement Programs**:
- iGEM teams can improve existing parts
- Add characterization data
- Fix errors
- Better documentation

**Measurement Committee**:
- Establishes standards for characterization
- Inter-lab studies
- Reference protocols
- Standard units
