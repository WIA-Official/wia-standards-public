Limits of Sequential Processing: Why Process One at a Time?

[IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER_2: Single overloaded thread with other CPU cores sitting idle in inefficient state]

Modern CPUs feature 64+ cores, yet sequential programs use only one. This is like driving a Ferrari in first gear only.

Critical Inefficiencies of Sequential Processing

  • Resource Waste: 63 of 64 cores idle (98.4% resource waste)
  • Long Processing Times: Cannot meet real-time requirements
  • Lack of Scalability: Linear time increase with data growth
  • Lost Competitiveness: 1/100th performance vs parallelized competitors
  • Energy Inefficiency: 100x more power per task
  • Opportunity Cost: Real-time analysis impossible in AI era
