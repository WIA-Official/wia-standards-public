Executive Summary

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released WIA-LANG-002, an international standard for digital preservation and utilization of indigenous script systems, as free and open source under MIT License. This marks a historic moment as the Republic of Korea leads international standards in language preservation.

🚨 Current Situation: Why This Standard Is Needed

According to UNESCO, 40% of the world's 7,000 languages are at risk of extinction, with one language disappearing every two weeks. Indigenous script systems are vanishing even faster as they fail to adapt to the digital age. Existing Unicode systems are designed for major languages and cannot properly represent minority indigenous scripts, and digital archiving standards are absent. This threatens millennia of cultural heritage with permanent loss as elder generations pass away.

WIA's Solution: WIA-LANG-002 Indigenous Script Digitization

WIA-LANG-002 provides a complete framework for digitization, preservation, and utilization of indigenous scripts. It standardizes Unicode extension mapping, AI-based handwriting recognition, 3D scanning archiving, cultural context metadata, and educational digital font generation.

🏢 About WIA

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) is an international standardization organization with a mission to provide equal opportunities to all humanity through technology and certification.

WIA is developing over 400 international standards across various fields including accessibility, security, AI, environment, and healthcare, and releases all standards as free and open source.

🦀 Technology Stack: WIA's core API is developed in Rust. Rust is a systems programming language developed by Mozilla, the only language achieving both memory safety and zero-cost abstractions simultaneously.

👤 About the CEO
