Chapter 7

Departmental AI Implementation

Marketing, Sales, HR, Finance, IT - 5 Department Success Strategies

Introduction: Departmental Execution Determines AI Success

As of November 2025, corporate AI adoption is no longer a question of "whether" but "how." 55.7% of Korean companies are using generative AI either company-wide (22.4%) or in specific departments (33.2%), with 85% adoption expected by 2026. Globally, 78% of organizations have adopted AI in at least one function.

37% vs 80%
AI Success Rate: Without Strategy (37%) vs With Strategy (80%)

The critical insight: companies without AI strategy have only 37% success rate, while those with clear strategy achieve 80% success. 68% of executives report friction between IT and other departments, and 72% say AI applications are being developed in silos.

Three-Stage Framework for Departmental AI Success

Stage 1 (Surface): Tool Adoption - "Our department started using ChatGPT" → Minimal productivity gains, no clear use cases

Stage 2 (Structure): Process Integration - AI systematically integrated into workflows → 50% time reduction in specific tasks, but cross-department data sharing still manual

Stage 3 (Foundation): Organizational Redesign - Cross-functional AI platform sharing → Real-time data flow, 544% ROI (Nucleus Research)

1. Marketing Department: The ROI Winner

Marketing leads in AI success. 75% of companies use marketing automation tools, and 80% of marketers report AI tool ROI exceeded expectations. Average ROI achievement time is just 8.3 months — the fastest among all departments.

2. Sales Department: Expectations vs Reality

Sales AI adoption shows mixed results. 85% of Fortune 500 use Microsoft AI solutions, and Salesforce Einstein is widely adopted. Specific wins include Apollo.io users seeing 46% more meeting bookings and 35% higher reservation rates with AI messaging. Copy.ai achieved 480% revenue growth in 2024 through GTM workflow automation.

3. IT/Development: Coding AI Revolution

Software development leads AI adoption at 44.1%, followed by IT operations at 40.3%. Key use cases include document summarization (43.1%), data analysis (40.3%), and programming assistance (37.0%). The primary goal for 70.5% of companies is "improving work efficiency and productivity."

4. Korea's Gap: Adoption vs Impact

Korean companies show high adoption rates but limited real change. 73% of large companies (assets over 2 trillion won) have adopted AI, compared to only 30% of smaller companies. Meanwhile, 80.7% of SMEs say "AI isn't needed for our business." The AI adoption rate for overall Korean SMEs is just 5.3%.
