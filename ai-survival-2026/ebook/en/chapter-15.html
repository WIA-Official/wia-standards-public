Introduction: 2026, Where Three Crossroads Meet

November 2025, the world is full of remarkable predictions. Elon Musk claims AI surpassing humans will be born in 2026. Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei prophesies a "genius nation inside datacenters" will become reality that year. Meanwhile, Gartner predicts "death by AI" lawsuits will exceed 2,000 by end of 2026. With 2026 just weeks away, we must ask: Is 2026 truly special, or just another exaggerated prediction?

$312B
2026 Global AI Market (+27.7% vs 2025)

The answer lies in data. The global AI market grows from ~$244B (2025) to $312B (2026). Gartner predicts AI spending will exceed $2 trillion in 2026, growing 36.8% YoY. IDC forecasts enterprise AI investment doubling from $307B (2025) to $632B (2028). This isn't simple growth — it's the tipping point where AI becomes necessity, not choice.

The Triple Crossroads Theory of 2026

Crossroad 1: The AGI Truth — Optimistic: Musk/Amodei's 2026 AGI scenario. Realistic: Expert surveys point to 2040 median. Result: 2026 won't be AGI, but will be remembered as "AGI's eve."

Crossroad 2: Bubble vs Revolution — Bubble signals: "AI bubble" debate, overinvestment warnings. Revolution signals: $2T+ spending, 85% enterprise adoption. Result: 2026 = short-term correction + long-term revolution's start.

Crossroad 3: Fate of Jobs — Destruction: 50% middle management elimination, accelerated automation. Creation: Explosive AI expert demand, new job categories. Result: 2026 = "Year One of the Great Transition" with pain and opportunity coexisting for 10 years.

1. Global 2026 Technology Outlook

2026 marks when AI transitions from "tool" to "colleague." OpenAI CEO Sam Altman stated in October 2025 roadmap: "By September 2026, we'll implement AI research intern-level capability." Current ChatGPT completes tasks in minutes; 2026's AI will independently conduct complex research projects over days.

September 2026
OpenAI "AI Research Intern" Launch Target

GPT-6: Memory and Personalization Revolution

Acknowledging user complaints about GPT-5's "cold" feel, OpenAI is accelerating GPT-6 development. Sam Altman emphasized: "People want memory. They want product features that can understand them." GPT-6's innovations include long-term memory (cumulative learning from all conversations), personalization (user-specific responses), agentic capability (autonomous multi-step tasks), and continuity (context maintained across sessions).

Multimodal AI: Era of Unified Intelligence

2026 is the year multimodal AI enters daily life from laboratory. Gartner predicts 40% of generative AI solutions will be multimodal by 2027 (up from 1% in 2023). The multimodal AI market grows from $1.6B (2024) to $15.7B (2034) at 32.7% CAGR. Gemini 2.5's 1 million token context window is just the beginning.

Edge AI: Distributed Intelligence

1.6 billion edge AI chips expected to ship by 2026. Edge AI market grows from $20.8B (2024) to $66.5B (2030) at 21.7% CAGR. Key values: ultra-low latency (10ms vs 100ms), privacy (data stays on device), and 99% cloud traffic reduction.
