3.2 LoRaWAN: Long Range, Low Power

LoRaWAN (Long Range Wide Area Network) is ideal for livestock tracking because it offers 2-15km range on a single AA battery lasting 2-5 years.

3.2.1 LoRaWAN Advantages

  • Range: 2-5km (rural), 15km (open terrain)
  • Battery Life: 2-5 years on single battery
  • Cost: $5-15/year connectivity (vs $50-150/year for cellular)
  • Penetration: Works in barns, forests, hills
  • Private Network: Farmers can deploy their own gateways

3.4 Geofencing and Alerts

Geofencing creates virtual boundaries. When an animal crosses, the farmer receives an SMS or app notification.

Use Cases:

  • Theft Prevention: Alert if cattle leave property at night
  • Pasture Rotation: Ensure animals stay in designated zones
  • Safety: Warning if animals approach roads or cliffs
  • Labor Efficiency: No need for daily headcounts

3.5 Data Transmission Protocol

LoRaWAN Uplink (GPS Collar → Gateway):

Payload Structure (12 bytes):
Byte 0: Message Type (0x01 = GPS)
Byte 1-4: Latitude (signed 32-bit integer, multiply by 10^-7)
Byte 5-8: Longitude (signed 32-bit integer, multiply by 10^-7)
Byte 9: Battery % (0-100)
Byte 10: Temperature (signed, -128 to +127°C)
Byte 11: Activity (0=rest, 1=walk, 2=graze, 3=run)

Example: 01 16 5A 0E 4B 78 9A BC DE 64 15 02
Decoded: GPS data, Lat=37.566535°, Lng=126.977969°, Battery=100%, Temp=21°C, Activity=Grazing
