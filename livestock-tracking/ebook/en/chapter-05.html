Chapter 5: Farm-to-Table Traceability

5.1 The Supply Chain

Modern meat production involves 5-7 stages from farm to consumer:

StageDurationData Recorded
1. Birth/Calf Rearing0-6 monthsBirth date, dam/sire, vaccinations, growth
2. Backgrounding (weaning to feedlot)6-12 monthsPasture location, weight gain, health treatments
3. Feedlot Finishing12-24 monthsFeed ration, ADG, final weight
4. Transport to Slaughterhouse1-8 hoursTruck ID, departure/arrival time, animal welfare checks
5. Slaughter & Processing1-3 daysCarcass weight, meat grade, cut breakdown
6. Distribution3-7 daysCold chain temperature, warehouse locations
7. Retail1-14 daysStore location, sell-by date, pricing

5.2 Blockchain for Immutable Records

Blockchain ensures that once data is recorded, it cannot be altered or deleted—critical for food fraud prevention.

5.2.1 How It Works

Birth Event → Hash recorded on blockchain → Immutable record
Vaccination → Hash recorded → Linked to animal ID
Slaughter → Hash recorded → Proof of meat grade
Retail Sale → Hash recorded → Consumer can verify entire history

5.4 QR Codes for Consumer Verification

Shoppers scan QR code on meat package to view:

  • Farm name and location (with photo)
  • Animal breed and birth date
  • Certifications (Organic, Grass-fed, Animal Welfare Approved)
  • Slaughter date and meat grade (e.g., USDA Prime, Korean 1++)
  • Blockchain verification link

5.5 Fraud Prevention

Traceability prevents common meat fraud:

Fraud TypeExamplePrevention
Species SubstitutionHorse meat sold as beefDNA testing + blockchain proof
False Origin Claims"Australian Beef" actually from elsewhereVerifiable farm GPS location
Grade InflationLower grade sold as premiumOfficial inspector data on blockchain
Organic FraudConventional meat labeled organicCertified farm records + audits

Summary

Farm-to-table traceability, powered by blockchain and GS1 standards, creates transparent supply chains. Consumers can verify the origin, quality, and authenticity of their meat—building trust and commanding premium prices for farmers who invest in transparency.
