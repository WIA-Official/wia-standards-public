About This Book

This comprehensive guide explores the rapidly evolving world of carbon trading and climate finance. From fundamental concepts to advanced trading strategies, this book provides a complete understanding of how carbon markets work and their critical role in addressing climate change.

Whether you're a corporate sustainability officer, policy maker, environmental consultant, or finance professional, this book will equip you with the knowledge needed to navigate the $850+ billion global carbon market.

Based on the WIA-FIN-025 standard, this guide covers technical specifications, real-world implementations, regulatory frameworks, and future trends in carbon trading.

Table of Contents

  • CHAPTER 1
    Introduction to Carbon Trading
    Understanding carbon markets, their purpose, and the global climate crisis. Learn about the evolution from Kyoto Protocol to Paris Agreement and the role of carbon pricing in climate action.
  • CHAPTER 2
    Carbon Markets Fundamentals
    Deep dive into compliance vs. voluntary markets, types of carbon credits, pricing mechanisms, and the major carbon trading systems worldwide including EU ETS, California Cap-and-Trade, and China ETS.
  • CHAPTER 3
    Trading Mechanisms & Instruments
    Explore spot markets, futures, options, and derivatives. Learn trading strategies, risk management, portfolio optimization, and how financial institutions participate in carbon markets.
  • CHAPTER 4
    Verification & Standards
    Understanding ISO 14064/14065, Verra VCS, Gold Standard, and CDM methodologies. Learn about measurement, reporting, verification (MRV), and ensuring credit quality and integrity.
  • CHAPTER 5
    Implementation Guide
    Practical guide to developing carbon projects, from baseline assessment to credit issuance. Includes project types, financing, technology integration, and blockchain implementation.
  • CHAPTER 6
    Regulations & Compliance
    Navigate international frameworks, national regulations, and corporate compliance requirements. Covers Paris Agreement Article 6, CORSIA, SEC climate disclosure rules, and emerging regulations.
  • CHAPTER 7
    Case Studies
    Real-world examples of successful carbon projects: Microsoft's carbon negative strategy, Amazon rainforest REDD+ projects, renewable energy developments, and corporate net-zero initiatives.
  • CHAPTER 8
    Future of Carbon Trading
    Emerging trends, technological innovations, market evolution, and the path to net-zero 2050. Explores AI, blockchain, satellite monitoring, and the next generation of carbon markets.
