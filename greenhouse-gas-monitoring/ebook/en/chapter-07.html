7.1 The GHG Protocol - Corporate Accounting Standard

Three Scopes of Emissions

Scope 1 - Direct Emissions:

  • Emissions from sources owned/controlled by the company
  • Examples: Company vehicles, on-site combustion, industrial processes
  • Full control, relatively easy to measure

Scope 2 - Indirect Energy Emissions:

  • Emissions from purchased electricity, steam, heating, cooling
  • Occur at power plant, not company facility
  • Two calculation methods: Location-based, Market-based (with RECs)

Scope 3 - Value Chain Emissions:

  • All other indirect emissions (upstream and downstream)
  • 15 categories: Purchased goods, business travel, employee commuting, use of sold products, etc.
  • Typically 75-95% of total footprint
  • Most challenging to measure

Scope 3 Categories

Category Description Example Data Sources
1. Purchased Goods Extraction, production, transport of purchased goods Spend data, supplier-specific data, LCA databases
2. Capital Goods Purchased or leased capital equipment, buildings Purchase records, construction data
3. Fuel & Energy Upstream emissions from fuel/energy purchased Energy bills, fuel suppliers
4. Transportation (Upstream) Third-party transportation of purchased goods Shipping records, logistics data
5. Waste Treatment of waste generated in operations Waste hauler data, landfill records
6. Business Travel Employee travel for business purposes Travel booking systems, expense reports
7. Commuting Employee commuting to work Surveys, HR data, census data
11. Use of Sold Products Energy use of products during customer use Product specifications, usage patterns

7.2 Carbon Footprinting Methodologies

Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)

Cradle-to-Grave Carbon Accounting

Phases of LCA:

  1. Goal & Scope Definition: Define functional unit, system boundaries
  2. Inventory Analysis: Collect data on inputs/outputs for each process
  3. Impact Assessment: Convert inventory to environmental impacts (GWP, acidification, etc.)
  4. Interpretation: Identify hotspots, compare alternatives

LCA Databases:

  • ecoinvent: 18,000+ LCI datasets, global coverage
  • GaBi: Industry-specific data, esp. chemicals and materials
  • USEEIO: US-specific input-output model
  • Agri-footprint: Agricultural products

Product Carbon Footprints (PCF)

Standard Scope Key Features
ISO 14067 Product CFP Quantification, communication, full lifecycle
PAS 2050 Product CFP (UK) Widely used, clear methodology
GHG Protocol Product Standard Goods & services Aligns with corporate GHG Protocol

7.3 Carbon Markets and Credits

Compliance Markets

Market Instrument Price Range (2024) Key Features
EU ETS EUAs (EU Allowances) €60-90/tonne Cap-and-trade, declining cap
California CCA (CA Carbon Allowances) $30-40/tonne Auction + free allocation
RGGI (Northeast USA) RGGI Allowances $13-18/tonne Power sector only
China National CCER (CN Carbon Emission Allowances) ¥60-80/tonne (~$9-12) Power sector, intensity-based
Korea K-ETS KAU (Korea Allowance Units) ₩15,000-25,000/tonne (~$12-20) Multi-sector coverage

Voluntary Carbon Markets (VCM)

Carbon Credit Standards

Major Registries:

  • Verra (VCS): Largest registry, diverse project types
  • Gold Standard: Focus on sustainable development co-benefits
  • American Carbon Registry (ACR): Forest and agriculture projects
  • Climate Action Reserve (CAR): North American focus

Credit Quality Criteria:

  • Additionality: Would not have happened without carbon finance
  • Permanence: Emission reductions are long-lasting (esp. forestry)
  • Leakage: No displacement of emissions elsewhere
  • Verification: Third-party validated and verified
  • No Double Counting: Unique serial number, retired upon use

Project Types and Pricing

Project Type VCM Price (2024) Quality Perception Challenges
Renewable Energy $2-5/tonne Lower (additionality concerns) Many now grid-competitive
Cookstoves $5-15/tonne Variable (baseline challenges) Emission measurement uncertainty
Forestry (REDD+) $5-20/tonne Moderate to high Permanence, leakage, baselines
Direct Air Capture (DAC) $200-600/tonne Very high (removal) High cost, energy intensity
Biochar $100-300/tonne High (permanent removal) Scalability, MRV complexity
Enhanced Weathering $50-150/tonne High (geological permanence) Verification, scaling

7.4 Carbon Credit MRV

Project Development Cycle

  1. Project Design: Develop Project Design Document (PDD), select methodology
  2. Validation: Independent third-party validates project against standard
  3. Registration: Registry approves project, issues unique ID
  4. Implementation: Execute project activities
  5. Monitoring: Collect data per monitoring plan
  6. Verification: Independent audit of emission reductions claimed
  7. Issuance: Registry issues carbon credits (VCUs, CERs, etc.)
  8. Retirement: Buyer retires credits against their footprint

Blockchain-Based Carbon Credits

Digitizing Carbon Markets

Benefits:

  • Transparent registry: All transactions on-chain
  • No double-counting: Cryptographic uniqueness
  • Fractional ownership: Trade fractions of tonnes
  • Automated retirement: Smart contracts execute upon use
  • Programmable rules: Embed quality criteria in code

7.5 Internal Carbon Pricing

Corporate Carbon Pricing Strategies

Shadow Price:

  • Theoretical price used in investment decisions
  • Not actual cash flow
  • Example: $50/tonne hurdle for capital projects
  • Drives low-carbon technology adoption

Internal Carbon Fee:

  • Business units charged real fee for emissions
  • Revenue funds emission reduction projects
  • Creates financial accountability
  • Example: Microsoft charges $15/tonne CO2e

Implicit Price:

  • Derived from cost of abatement measures undertaken
  • Calculated post-hoc from spending on emission reductions
Company Internal Carbon Price Mechanism Purpose
Microsoft $15/tonne (increasing) Internal fee Fund Carbon Negative initiative
Shell $40/tonne (2025 planning) Shadow price Investment screening
Disney $10-60/tonne (scenario) Shadow price Risk assessment, planning
Unilever €40/tonne Shadow price Capital allocation decisions

7.6 Financial Accounting of Emissions

Carbon Assets and Liabilities

Item Accounting Treatment Standard
Purchased Allowances Intangible asset or inventory IFRIC 3 (withdrawn), local GAAP varies
Free Allocated Allowances Not recognized (or nominal value) Inconsistent treatment globally
Emission Liability Provision when emissions exceed allowances IAS 37 Provisions
Carbon Credits (VCM) Intangible asset, amortize when retired Local GAAP, no specific standard

Climate-Related Financial Disclosures

TCFD Framework

Four Pillars:

  • Governance: Board oversight of climate risks
  • Strategy: Climate risks/opportunities impact on business
  • Risk Management: Processes to identify and manage climate risks
  • Metrics & Targets: Scope 1,2,3 emissions, climate targets

Scenario Analysis:

  • 1.5°C scenario: Rapid transition, high carbon prices, stranded assets
  • 2°C scenario: Moderate transition
  • 3-4°C scenario: Physical risks dominate (floods, heat, storms)

7.8 Net Zero Target Setting

Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi)

Target Type Requirement Ambition
Near-term (2030) Scope 1+2: Min. 42% reduction
Scope 3: 25% reduction (if >40% of total)		 1.5°C aligned
Long-term (2050) 90% reduction across all scopes Net zero
Net Zero Standard Near-term + long-term + neutralization Net zero commitment

Emissions Reduction Hierarchy

  1. Avoid: Eliminate unnecessary emissions (e.g., reduce travel)
  2. Reduce: Decrease emissions intensity (e.g., energy efficiency)
  3. Replace: Switch to low-carbon alternatives (e.g., renewables)
  4. Compensate: Offset remaining emissions (carbon credits)

SBTi rule: Maximum 10% of reductions can come from offsets

7.9 Carbon Removal Accounting

Removals vs. Avoidance

Type Definition Examples Accounting
Avoidance Prevents new emissions Renewables, cookstoves, REDD+ Not removals, used for offsetting
Removal (nature) Extracts CO2 from atmosphere Afforestation, soil carbon, blue carbon Counted if additional to baseline
Removal (engineered) Technological extraction DAC, BECCS, biochar, mineralization Removals, typically permanent

7.10 Integration Example

End-to-End Carbon Accounting Workflow

// 1. Measure corporate footprint
const footprint = await wia.carbon.measureFootprint({
  organization: 'ACME Corporation',
  year: 2024,
  scopes: [1, 2, 3],
  boundaries: 'operational_control',
  data_sources: {
    scope1: 'facility_meters',
    scope2: 'utility_bills',
    scope3: ['spend_analysis', 'supplier_data']
  }
});

// 2. Set science-based target
const target = wia.carbon.setSBT({
  base_year: 2020,
  target_year: 2030,
  scope_1_2_reduction: 0.50, // 50%
  scope_3_reduction: 0.30,
  method: '1.5C_aligned'
});

// 3. Track progress
const progress = wia.carbon.trackProgress({
  current: footprint,
  target: target,
  trajectory: 'linear'
});

// 4. Purchase carbon credits for residual
const credits = await wia.carbon.purchaseCredits({
  amount: progress.gap_to_target,
  preferences: {
    project_type: ['DAC', 'afforestation'],
    vintage: 2024,
    co_benefits: ['biodiversity'],
    max_price: 100 // USD/tonne
  }
});

// 5. Generate disclosure report
const disclosure = wia.carbon.generateDisclosure({
  framework: ['TCFD', 'CSRD', 'CDP'],
  assurance_level: 'limited',
  include_scenario_analysis: true
});

console.log('2024 Emissions:', footprint.total_tCO2e);
console.log('Target progress:', progress.percent_to_target);
console.log('Credits purchased:', credits.total_tCO2e);
console.log('Disclosure ready:', disclosure.file_urls);

Key Takeaways

  • GHG Protocol defines three scopes: direct (1), energy indirect (2), value chain (3)
  • Scope 3 typically represents 75-95% of corporate footprints but is hardest to measure
  • LCA and PCF methodologies enable product-level carbon accounting
  • Carbon markets include compliance (ETS) and voluntary (VCM) with vastly different prices
  • Carbon credit quality depends on additionality, permanence, and verification
  • Internal carbon pricing drives corporate investment in emission reductions
  • Financial accounting of carbon assets/liabilities lacks global standardization
  • Supply chain traceability requires data sharing across tiers
  • Science Based Targets require 42% reduction by 2030 for 1.5°C alignment
  • WIA-ENE-051 integrates monitoring data with carbon accounting systems
