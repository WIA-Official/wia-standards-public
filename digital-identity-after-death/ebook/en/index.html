Digital Identity After Death

A Comprehensive Guide to Post-Mortem Digital Identity Management

WIA-LEG-010 Standard | Version 1.0

About This eBook

This comprehensive guide explores the critical and emerging field of digital identity management after death. As our lives become increasingly digital, the question of what happens to our digital identities, credentials, and online presence after we pass away has become one of the most pressing challenges in modern technology and law.

The WIA-LEG-010 standard provides a framework for managing the complete lifecycle of digital identity in the context of death—from the initial verification of death certificates to the ethical management of AI-generated personas and digital memorials. This standard addresses technical, legal, and ethical considerations while providing practical implementation guidelines.

Through eight comprehensive chapters, you'll explore death verification protocols, credential revocation mechanisms, posthumous authentication systems, digital legacy management, AI persona generation, legal frameworks, privacy considerations, and real-world implementation strategies.

Target Audience: Technology architects, legal professionals, digital estate planners, platform developers, security engineers, and policymakers working at the intersection of digital identity, death, and technology.
