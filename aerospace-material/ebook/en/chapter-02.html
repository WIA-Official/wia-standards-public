Chapter 2: Aluminum Alloys

Aluminum alloys have been the backbone of aircraft structures since the 1930s. Their excellent strength-to-weight ratio, corrosion resistance, and ease of fabrication make them ideal for aerospace applications. This chapter covers the primary aluminum alloy systems used in aviation, their heat treatment, and applications according to the WIA-AEROSPACE_MATERIAL standard.

2.1 Aluminum Alloy Classification

Wrought Alloy Designation System

Aluminum Alloy Series Classification
====================================

1xxx: Pure aluminum (>99% Al)
      - Excellent corrosion resistance
      - Low strength, high conductivity
      - Applications: electrical, chemical

2xxx: Al-Cu alloys (heat treatable)
      - High strength, good fatigue
      - Lower corrosion resistance
      - Applications: fuselage, wing skins

3xxx: Al-Mn alloys (non-heat treatable)
      - Moderate strength, good formability
      - Applications: general purpose

5xxx: Al-Mg alloys (non-heat treatable)
      - Good weldability, marine corrosion
      - Applications: pressure vessels

6xxx: Al-Mg-Si alloys (heat treatable)
      - Good extrudability
      - Applications: structural shapes

7xxx: Al-Zn-Mg-(Cu) alloys (heat treatable)
      - Highest strength aluminum alloys
      - Applications: structural members

2.4 Corrosion Considerations

Corrosion Types and Prevention

  • Pitting: Localized attack, coating protection
  • Intergranular: Grain boundary attack, proper heat treatment
  • Exfoliation: Layer delamination, T76 temper
  • Stress Corrosion (SCC): Cracking under stress, T73 temper
  • Galvanic: Dissimilar metal contact, insulation required

2.7 Cladding and Surface Protection

Alclad Products

Alclad materials consist of a high-strength core alloy metallurgically bonded to a thin layer of pure aluminum or corrosion-resistant alloy. The cladding provides cathodic protection to the core, sacrificially corroding to protect against pitting and corrosion.

  • Alclad 2024: 1230 cladding, fuselage skins
  • Alclad 7075: 7072 cladding, wing skins
  • Cladding thickness: 2.5-5% per side
