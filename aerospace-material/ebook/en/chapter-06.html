Chapter 6: High-Temperature Materials

High-temperature materials enable the extreme operating conditions in gas turbine engines and spacecraft thermal protection systems. Nickel-based superalloys, ceramic matrix composites, and thermal barrier coatings push the boundaries of material science to achieve higher efficiency and performance. This chapter covers these advanced materials according to the WIA-AEROSPACE_MATERIAL standard.

6.1 Nickel-Based Superalloys

Superalloy Strengthening Mechanisms

Nickel Superalloy Microstructure
================================

Gamma (γ) Matrix: FCC nickel solid solution
  - Strengthened by: Cr, Co, Mo, W, Re (solid solution)
  - Provides: ductility, oxidation resistance base

Gamma Prime (γ'): Ni3(Al,Ti) ordered L12 structure
  - Volume fraction: 40-70%
  - Size: 0.1-1.0 µm
  - Anomalous yield behavior (strengthens with temp)
  - Primary creep resistance mechanism

Gamma Double Prime (γ"): Ni3Nb, BCT structure
  - Present in: Inconel 718
  - Provides: high strength at lower temps
  - Metastable: transforms to δ above 650°C

Carbides: MC, M23C6, M6C
  - Grain boundary pinning
  - Can be beneficial or detrimental

TCP Phases: σ, µ, Laves
  - Undesirable brittle phases
  - Controlled by composition balance

6.4 Ceramic Matrix Composites

CMC Advantages and Challenges

  • Density: 2-3 g/cm³ vs 8+ for superalloys (60% reduction)
  • Temperature: Capable to 1300°C+ uncooled
  • Thermal shock: Excellent resistance due to fiber bridging
  • Challenges: EBC required, manufacturing cost, design database
  • Applications: Combustor liners, turbine shrouds, nozzles

6.6 Refractory Metals

Refractory Metal Applications

Refractory Metals in Aerospace:
===============================

Tungsten (W):
  - Melting point: 3410°C
  - Applications: rocket nozzle inserts, counterweights
  - Limitations: oxidizes above 500°C, very dense

Molybdenum (Mo):
  - Melting point: 2620°C
  - Applications: heat shields, furnace components
  - Advantage: lower density than W

Tantalum (Ta):
  - Melting point: 3017°C
  - Applications: rocket engines, chemical processing
  - Advantage: excellent corrosion resistance

Niobium (Nb):
  - Melting point: 2468°C
  - Applications: rocket nozzles (with silicide coating)
  - Advantage: lowest density of refractory metals

Rhenium (Re):
  - Melting point: 3180°C
  - Applications: rocket combustion chambers
  - Combined with Ir for oxidation resistance

6.8 Environmental Barrier Coatings

EBC for CMC Protection

Environmental barrier coatings protect SiC-based CMCs from water vapor attack in combustion environments. Steam reacts with the protective SiO2 scale forming volatile Si(OH)4, leading to recession.

  • Top coat: Rare earth silicates (Yb2Si2O7, Y2Si2O7)
  • Intermediate: Mullite or BSAS
  • Bond coat: Silicon
  • Requirements: CTE match, low permeability, CMAS resistance
