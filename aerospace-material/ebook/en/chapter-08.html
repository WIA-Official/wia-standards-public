Chapter 8: Qualification and Standards

Aerospace material qualification ensures that materials consistently meet the demanding requirements of flight applications. This process involves rigorous testing, documentation, and approval by regulatory authorities and OEMs. This chapter covers material specifications, qualification processes, and quality management according to the WIA-AEROSPACE_MATERIAL standard.

8.1 Specification Systems

Aerospace Material Specifications

Specification Organizations
===========================

AMS (Aerospace Material Specifications):
  - Published by SAE International
  - >2,500 specifications
  - Covers: metals, composites, processes
  - Example: AMS 4911 (Ti-6Al-4V sheet)

MIL/ASTM:
  - Military specifications (legacy)
  - Many converted to ASTM
  - Example: ASTM B209 (Al sheet)

OEM Specifications:
  - Boeing: BMS (Boeing Material Spec)
  - Airbus: AIMS (Airbus Material Spec)
  - Engine OEMs: proprietary specs

Industry Standards:
  - MMPDS: Design allowables database
  - CMH-17: Composite materials handbook
  - MSFC-SPEC-3479: NASA materials

8.7 Change Management

Material Change Categories

Change Classification and Response:
===================================

Minor Change:
  - Examples: supplier administrative, minor spec revision
  - Response: notification, documentation
  - Requalification: not typically required

Major Change:
  - Examples: composition adjustment, process parameter
  - Response: engineering evaluation, limited testing
  - Requalification: partial, equivalence demonstration

Source Change:
  - Examples: new supplier, new production site
  - Response: full supplier qualification
  - Requalification: significant testing program

Specification Change:
  - Examples: new revision, allowable update
  - Response: design review, impact assessment
  - Requalification: as required by change

The WIA-AEROSPACE_MATERIAL standard embodies the principle of benefiting humanity through safe, reliable aerospace materials. By establishing clear qualification and quality requirements, this standard ensures that materials perform as expected throughout the aircraft lifecycle, protecting passengers and enabling global transportation that connects people across the world.

Chapter Summary

This chapter covered aerospace material qualification including specification systems, qualification processes, special process control, regulatory requirements, and quality management. The WIA-AEROSPACE_MATERIAL standard provides comprehensive guidance for material qualification and production control to ensure flight safety.
