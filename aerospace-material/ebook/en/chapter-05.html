Chapter 5: Composite Materials

Composite materials have revolutionized aerospace design, enabling significant weight reductions and tailored structural properties. Carbon fiber reinforced polymers (CFRP) now comprise over 50% of modern commercial aircraft structures. This chapter covers composite constituents, manufacturing processes, and design principles according to the WIA-AEROSPACE_MATERIAL standard.

5.1 Composite Constituents

Fiber and Matrix Systems

Composite Material Components
=============================

Reinforcing Fibers:
├── Carbon (graphite)
│   ├── Standard modulus (230 GPa)
│   ├── Intermediate modulus (290 GPa)
│   └── High modulus (390+ GPa)
├── Glass
│   ├── E-glass (general purpose)
│   ├── S-glass (high strength)
│   └── R-glass (high performance)
├── Aramid (Kevlar)
│   ├── Impact resistance
│   └── Damage tolerance
└── Hybrid combinations

Matrix Resins:
├── Thermosets
│   ├── Epoxy (primary aerospace)
│   ├── BMI (higher temperature)
│   ├── Polyimide (very high temp)
│   └── Phenolic (fire resistance)
└── Thermoplastics
    ├── PEEK (high performance)
    ├── PPS (chemical resistant)
    └── PEKK (processing advantage)

5.4 Laminate Design

Laminate Stacking Rules

  • Balance: For each +θ ply, include a -θ ply
  • Symmetry: Symmetric about midplane to avoid warping
  • 10% Rule: Minimum 10% fibers in each direction (0/90/±45)
  • Grouping: Limit consecutive same-orientation plies to 4
  • Surface: ±45° on outer surfaces for damage resistance

5.5 Environmental Effects

Factor Effect Design Response
Moisture Matrix plasticization, Tg reduction Hot/wet design allowables

5.7 Hybrid Laminates

GLARE (Glass Laminate Aluminum Reinforced Epoxy)

GLARE is a fiber metal laminate consisting of alternating layers of aluminum and glass-fiber-reinforced epoxy. It combines the fatigue resistance of composites with the damage tolerance of aluminum.

GLARE Properties:
  Composition: 2024-T3 Al (0.3-0.5mm) + S2-glass/epoxy
  Density: 2.5 g/cm³ (vs 2.8 for Al)
  Fatigue: 10-100x improvement over Al
  Damage tolerance: Excellent, crack bridging
  Impact: Superior to monolithic Al
  Application: A380 fuselage upper shells
