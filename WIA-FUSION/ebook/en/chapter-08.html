The Road to Commercialization

From scientific demonstration to global deployment: the pathway to fusion-powered civilization and limitless clean energy for all humanity.

The Journey So Far

Fusion research has made remarkable progress over seven decades. From the first tokamak concepts in the 1950s to NIF's demonstration of scientific gain in 2022, each milestone has brought us closer to the ultimate goal: practical, commercial fusion power.

DecadeKey Achievements
1950s-60sTokamak concept, first plasma experiments
1970s-80sLarge tokamaks (TFTR, JET), 100M°C achieved
1990s-2000sD-T fusion demonstrated, 16 MW record
2010sITER construction, private fusion emerges
2020sNIF ignition (Q>1), KSTAR 48s record, HTS magnets

Roadmap to 2050

2025-2030: Demonstration Phase

  • SPARC achieves Q > 2 (Commonwealth Fusion Systems)
  • KSTAR reaches 300-second operation
  • ITER tokamak assembly completed
  • Private companies demonstrate net energy
  • Regulatory frameworks established

2030-2040: Pilot Plant Phase

  • ITER first plasma (2033) and D-T operation (2039)
  • DEMO designs finalized
  • First commercial pilot plants under construction
  • Supply chain scaled for mass production
  • Grid integration pilots successful

2040-2050: Commercial Deployment

  • Multiple GW-scale fusion plants operational
  • LCOE competitive with renewables ($30-50/MWh)
  • Global technology transfer underway
  • Fusion provides 5-10% of global electricity
  • Deep decarbonization accelerated

2050 and Beyond: Fusion Era

  • Fusion becomes backbone of global energy system
  • Energy abundance enables universal prosperity
  • Climate change mitigation achieved
  • Space propulsion and other applications emerge
  • 弘益人間 - Benefit All Humanity realized

Policy Recommendations

Achieving commercial fusion requires coordinated policy action:

  • Increase R&D Funding: Triple public fusion budgets to accelerate development
  • Streamline Regulation: Create fusion-specific licensing pathways
  • Support Private Innovation: Tax incentives and public-private partnerships
  • Invest in Supply Chain: Secure HTS manufacturing, tritium production
  • International Cooperation: Expand ITER model to commercial deployment
  • Workforce Development: Train next generation of fusion engineers

The WIA-FUSION Standard Role

The WIA-FUSION standard accelerates commercialization by:

  • Enabling data sharing across all devices and institutions
  • Providing common APIs for AI/ML development
  • Standardizing safety and operational protocols
  • Facilitating regulatory approval through consistent documentation
  • Reducing costs through interoperable components and tools
  • Accelerating learning curves through shared knowledge
