Phase 4: Integration

Connecting fusion power plants to electrical grids, international research networks, and regulatory frameworks for commercial deployment.

Grid Integration

A fusion power plant must integrate seamlessly with electrical grids, providing reliable baseload power while supporting grid stability services.

Grid Services

Fusion plants can provide multiple grid services:

  • Baseload Power: 85-90% capacity factor, >90% availability
  • Frequency Regulation: Response within 30 seconds, ±10% range
  • Voltage Support: ±200 MVAR reactive power
  • Black Start: Capability to restart grid from shutdown (optional)

Regulatory Framework

Fusion power plants require regulatory approval, though the framework differs significantly from fission due to fusion's inherent safety.

Licensing Phases

  1. Design Certification: Safety analysis, environmental assessment, security plan
  2. Construction License: Quality assurance, inspection protocols
  3. Operating License: Commissioning tests, operator training
  4. Decommissioning Plan: Waste management, site restoration

Fusion-Specific Advantages

NOT Required for Fusion (unlike fission):
  • Criticality analysis (no chain reaction possible)
  • Emergency planning zones (minimal offsite risk)
  • Proliferation safeguards (no fissile material)
