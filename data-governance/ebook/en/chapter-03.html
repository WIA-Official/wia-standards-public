CHAPTER 3

Data Stewardship

Establishing effective data stewardship programs and managing data domains across your organization.

Understanding Data Stewardship

Data stewardship is the management and oversight of an organization's data assets to ensure their quality, security, compliance, and proper use. Data stewards are the hands-on practitioners who execute governance policies and serve as the bridge between business and IT.

The Role of Data Stewards

Data stewards are essential to successful governance. They:

  • Monitor and maintain data quality
  • Enforce governance policies and standards
  • Manage metadata and documentation
  • Resolve data issues and conflicts
  • Facilitate data access and usage
  • Champion data literacy and best practices

Types of Data Stewards

Executive Stewards

Senior leaders who provide strategic oversight and support.

  • Level: VP or C-level executives
  • Focus: Strategic alignment, resource allocation, escalation resolution
  • Time Commitment: 5-10% of their time
  • Example: VP of Finance serving as executive steward for financial data

Business Stewards

Subject matter experts who understand data from a business perspective.

  • Level: Business analysts, domain experts, managers
  • Focus: Data quality, business rules, definitions, access decisions
  • Time Commitment: 20-40% of their time
  • Example: Marketing analyst serving as steward for customer data

Technical Stewards

IT professionals who handle technical aspects of data management.

  • Level: Data engineers, database administrators, architects
  • Focus: Data structures, integration, security implementation, technical metadata
  • Time Commitment: 30-50% of their time
  • Example: Data engineer managing data pipeline quality

Building a Stewardship Program

Phase 1: Planning

  1. Define Scope: Identify data domains requiring stewardship
  2. Set Objectives: Establish clear goals and success metrics
  3. Secure Resources: Allocate budget, tools, and time
  4. Develop Charter: Document program vision, roles, and responsibilities

Phase 2: Recruitment

Select the right people for stewardship roles:

  • Desired Qualities: Domain expertise, attention to detail, communication skills, collaborative mindset
  • Selection Criteria: Business knowledge, technical aptitude, credibility, availability
  • Volunteer vs. Assigned: Mix of volunteers (passionate advocates) and assigned resources (ensure coverage)

Phase 3: Training

Comprehensive training ensures steward success:

  • Data governance fundamentals and framework
  • Specific stewardship responsibilities and workflows
  • Data quality principles and assessment methods
  • Governance tools and technologies
  • Metadata management and documentation
  • Privacy and security requirements

Phase 4: Execution

Launch and operate the stewardship program:

  • Assign stewards to specific data domains
  • Establish regular workflows and meetings
  • Implement monitoring and reporting processes
  • Create collaboration channels and knowledge sharing

Data Domain Management

Organizing data into logical domains simplifies stewardship and accountability.

Common Data Domains

  • Customer Data: Customer profiles, interactions, preferences
  • Product Data: Product catalogs, specifications, pricing
  • Financial Data: Transactions, accounts, budgets, reporting
  • Employee Data: HR records, compensation, performance
  • Operational Data: Orders, inventory, logistics
  • Marketing Data: Campaigns, analytics, lead management
  • Reference Data: Codes, classifications, lookup tables

Domain Boundaries

Define clear boundaries for each domain:

  • What data assets fall within the domain
  • Who the domain steward is
  • What systems and applications are included
  • How the domain relates to other domains

Key Stewardship Activities

Data Quality Management

  • Assessment: Regularly evaluate data quality against standards
  • Monitoring: Track quality metrics and trends
  • Issue Resolution: Investigate and fix quality problems
  • Prevention: Implement controls to prevent future issues

Metadata Management

  • Business Glossary: Define and maintain business terms
  • Data Dictionary: Document technical data elements
  • Data Lineage: Map data flows and transformations
  • Documentation: Keep data documentation current

Access Management

  • Access Requests: Review and approve data access requests
  • Access Reviews: Periodically audit who has access
  • Deprovisioning: Remove access when no longer needed
  • Exception Handling: Manage special access cases

Issue Management

  • Intake: Receive and log data issues
  • Triage: Prioritize based on impact and urgency
  • Investigation: Determine root cause
  • Resolution: Fix the issue and verify
  • Communication: Update stakeholders

Data Steward Success Profile

Essential Skills:

  • Deep knowledge of their data domain
  • Understanding of data governance principles
  • Strong analytical and problem-solving abilities
  • Excellent communication and collaboration skills
  • Attention to detail and quality mindset

Key Behaviors:

  • Proactive monitoring and issue identification
  • Collaborative approach to problem-solving
  • Commitment to continuous improvement
  • Advocacy for data quality and governance

Measuring Stewardship Effectiveness

Performance Metrics

  • Data Quality Scores: Improvement in quality metrics
  • Issue Resolution Time: Speed of problem resolution
  • Policy Compliance: Adherence to governance policies
  • Metadata Completeness: Percentage of documented data assets
  • Stakeholder Satisfaction: Feedback from data consumers

Value Demonstration

Show the business impact of stewardship:

  • Cost savings from reduced errors and rework
  • Revenue improvements from better data insights
  • Risk reduction from compliance and security improvements
  • Efficiency gains from faster data access and better documentation

Common Challenges and Solutions

Challenge: Lack of Time

Problem: Stewardship is "extra work" on top of regular job.

Solutions:

  • Formally allocate time in job descriptions and performance goals
  • Automate routine stewardship tasks
  • Streamline workflows and eliminate unnecessary activities
  • Recognize and reward stewardship contributions

Challenge: Limited Authority

Problem: Stewards lack authority to enforce decisions.

Solutions:

  • Clearly define steward decision rights
  • Establish executive sponsorship and support
  • Create clear escalation paths
  • Document and communicate steward authority

Challenge: Tool Limitations

Problem: Inadequate tools make stewardship difficult.

Solutions:

  • Invest in proper data governance platforms
  • Integrate stewardship workflows with existing tools
  • Provide training on available tools
  • Start simple and expand tool capabilities over time

Building a Stewardship Community

Foster collaboration and knowledge sharing among stewards:

  • Regular Meetings: Monthly steward community meetings
  • Collaboration Platform: Shared workspace for communication
  • Training and Development: Ongoing learning opportunities
  • Recognition Program: Celebrate steward achievements
  • Best Practice Sharing: Learn from each other's successes

Stewardship Program Checklist

  • ✓ Define stewardship roles and responsibilities
  • ✓ Identify and map data domains
  • ✓ Recruit qualified stewards
  • ✓ Provide comprehensive training
  • ✓ Establish workflows and processes
  • ✓ Implement stewardship tools
  • ✓ Define success metrics
  • ✓ Create steward community
  • ✓ Secure executive support
  • ✓ Recognize and reward contributions
