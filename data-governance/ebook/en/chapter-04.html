CHAPTER 4

Data Policies and Standards

Creating effective policies, standards, and best practices for consistent data management and quality.

Policy Framework Overview

Data policies and standards provide the rules and guidelines that govern how data is managed, used, and protected across the organization. They translate governance principles into actionable requirements.

Policy Hierarchy

  • Policies: High-level principles and requirements (what must be done)
  • Standards: Specific, measurable requirements (how it must be done)
  • Procedures: Step-by-step instructions (detailed implementation)
  • Guidelines: Best practices and recommendations (suggested approaches)

Essential Data Policies

1. Data Quality Policy

Establishes expectations for data accuracy, completeness, consistency, and timeliness.

Policy Statement:
All enterprise data must meet defined quality standards appropriate to its intended use. Data owners are responsible for ensuring data quality within their domains.

Key Requirements:
• Define quality dimensions for each data domain
• Establish measurable quality metrics and thresholds
• Implement automated quality monitoring
• Document quality issues and resolution processes
• Regular quality reporting to stakeholders

2. Data Security Policy

Defines how data must be protected from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

  • Data classification requirements (public, internal, confidential, restricted)
  • Encryption requirements for data at rest and in transit
  • Access control requirements (authentication, authorization)
  • Security monitoring and incident response
  • Physical and logical security controls

3. Data Privacy Policy

Governs the collection, use, and protection of personal and sensitive information.

  • Privacy principles (purpose limitation, data minimization)
  • Consent management requirements
  • Individual rights (access, correction, deletion)
  • Privacy impact assessment requirements
  • Third-party data sharing controls

4. Data Retention Policy

Specifies how long different types of data must be retained and when they should be disposed.

Policy Statement:
Data must be retained for the minimum period required to meet business, legal, and regulatory requirements, then securely disposed of.

Retention Schedule Examples:
• Customer transaction records: 7 years
• Employee records: 7 years after termination
• Tax records: 7 years
• Email: 3 years
• Temporary files: 90 days

Disposal Requirements:
• Secure deletion methods based on data classification
• Documented disposal process
• Disposal verification and certification

5. Data Access Policy

Controls who can access what data and under what circumstances.

  • Principle of least privilege (minimum necessary access)
  • Access request and approval workflows
  • Regular access reviews and recertification
  • Privileged access management
  • Emergency access procedures

6. Data Sharing Policy

Governs internal and external data sharing.

  • Internal sharing requirements and approvals
  • Third-party data sharing agreements
  • Data protection requirements for shared data
  • Cross-border data transfer restrictions
  • Sharing audit and monitoring

Data Standards

Data Naming Standards

Consistent naming conventions for data elements, databases, and files:

  • Database Names: [business_domain]_[environment]_db (e.g., customer_prod_db)
  • Table Names: Descriptive, plural nouns (e.g., customers, orders)
  • Column Names: snake_case, descriptive (e.g., customer_email, order_date)
  • File Names: [type]_[description]_[date] (e.g., report_sales_2025-01-15)

Data Format Standards

  • Dates: ISO 8601 format (YYYY-MM-DD)
  • Times: 24-hour format with timezone (HH:MM:SS±HH:MM)
  • Phone Numbers: E.164 format (+[country][number])
  • Postal Codes: Country-specific standardized format
  • Currency: ISO 4217 currency codes (USD, EUR, GBP)

Data Validation Standards

Rules for validating data accuracy and completeness:

  • Required vs. optional fields
  • Data type validation (string, integer, decimal, etc.)
  • Format validation (regex patterns, length constraints)
  • Range validation (min/max values, allowable ranges)
  • Business rule validation (logical consistency checks)
  • Reference data validation (valid values, foreign keys)

Metadata Standards

Requirements for documenting data assets:

  • Business definitions and descriptions
  • Data lineage and transformation logic
  • Data ownership and stewardship
  • Classification and sensitivity levels
  • Quality metrics and thresholds
  • Usage guidelines and restrictions

Developing Effective Policies

Policy Development Process

  1. Identify Need: Business requirement, regulatory mandate, or risk
  2. Research: Review industry standards, regulations, best practices
  3. Draft Policy: Write clear, actionable policy statement
  4. Stakeholder Review: Gather feedback from affected parties
  5. Approval: Obtain governance council approval
  6. Communication: Announce and educate organization
  7. Implementation: Roll out enforcement mechanisms
  8. Monitoring: Track compliance and effectiveness
  9. Review: Periodic review and updates

Policy Writing Best Practices

  • Be Clear: Use simple, unambiguous language
  • Be Specific: Define measurable requirements
  • Be Practical: Ensure policies can be realistically implemented
  • Be Consistent: Align with other policies and standards
  • Be Complete: Cover scope, requirements, roles, exceptions
  • Be Accessible: Make policies easy to find and understand

Policy Template Structure

1. Purpose: Why this policy exists
2. Scope: What/who the policy applies to
3. Policy Statement: Core requirements
4. Roles and Responsibilities: Who does what
5. Standards and Procedures: Detailed requirements
6. Exceptions: When/how exceptions are granted
7. Compliance: How compliance is measured
8. Violations: Consequences of non-compliance
9. References: Related policies and regulations
10. Revision History: Change tracking

Policy Enforcement

Preventive Controls

  • System Controls: Technical enforcement (validation rules, access controls)
  • Training: Educate users on policy requirements
  • Templates and Tools: Make compliance easy with standard tools
  • Approval Workflows: Require approvals before risky actions

Detective Controls

  • Monitoring: Automated monitoring for policy violations
  • Auditing: Regular compliance audits
  • Reporting: Compliance dashboards and reports
  • Exception Tracking: Log and review exceptions

Corrective Controls

  • Issue Remediation: Fix violations when discovered
  • Process Improvement: Update processes to prevent recurrence
  • Disciplinary Action: Consequences for willful violations
  • Policy Updates: Revise policies based on lessons learned

Measuring Policy Effectiveness

Key Metrics

  • Compliance Rate: Percentage of policy compliance
  • Violation Frequency: Number of policy violations
  • Exception Rate: Percentage of approved exceptions
  • Awareness Level: Employee knowledge of policies
  • Time to Compliance: How quickly violations are remediated

Continuous Improvement

  • Regular policy review cycles (annually or bi-annually)
  • Gather feedback from policy users
  • Monitor changes in regulations and industry standards
  • Update policies based on incidents and lessons learned
  • Benchmark against industry best practices

Policy Success Factors

  • Executive sponsorship and visible support
  • Clear, understandable policy language
  • Practical and implementable requirements
  • Effective communication and training
  • Technical controls that enforce policies
  • Regular monitoring and compliance reporting
  • Consistent enforcement without favoritism
  • Responsive to feedback and change
