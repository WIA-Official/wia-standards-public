제 4: Phase 1 - Legal Data Format

4.1 Introduction to Legal Data Standardization

Phase 1 of WIA-UNI-014 establishes standardized data formats for representing legal documents, enabling systems from both North and South Korea to exchange legal information seamlessly. This foundational phase makes all subsequent integration possible by creating a common language for legal data.

Without standardized data formats, legal system integration would require custom translation for every interaction between systems—an approach that doesn't scale. Phase 1 solves this by defining canonical representations for all major legal document types.

4.6 Validation Rules

Data quality is ensured through comprehensive validation rules:

Structural Validation

  • JSON schema validation for all documents
  • Required field presence verification
  • Data type checking (dates, numbers, strings)
  • Enum value validation (status, type, etc.)
  • Cross-reference integrity (referenced documents exist)

Business Logic Validation

  • Effective date must be after issue date
  • Superseding documents must have later dates
  • Property transfers must have both seller and buyer
  • Contract signatures must be from parties to the contract
  • Court judgments must reference valid court identifiers

Legal Compliance Validation

  • Documents comply with jurisdiction requirements
  • Authority has jurisdiction to issue document
  • Required approvals and signatures present
  • No conflicts with higher-level legal provisions

제 Summary: Key Takeaways

  1. Standardized schema enables interoperability: Common data formats allow North and South Korean systems to exchange legal information seamlessly.
  2. Document types have specialized structures: Laws, judgments, contracts, and property records each have tailored schemas capturing their unique requirements.
  3. Identifiers provide global uniqueness: WIA-UNI-014 identifier standards ensure every legal document and entity can be uniquely referenced.
  4. Validation ensures data quality: Comprehensive validation rules catch errors and ensure legal compliance at the data level.
  5. Metadata enables management: Rich metadata supports discovery, classification, preservation, and access control of legal documents.
