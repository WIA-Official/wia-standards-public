1.1 THE CHALLENGE OF BIOLOGICAL PRESERVATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Cryopreservation represents one of humanity's most ambitious endeavors: the
suspension of biological time. At its core, this technology freezes biological
material at ultra-low temperatures to halt all metabolic processes, preserving
the subject for potential future revival.

[i] KEY TEMPERATURE ZONES:

    +------------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | Zone               | Temperature      | Characteristics               |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------------+
    | Ambient            | 20-37 C          | Normal biological activity    |
    | Hypothermic        | 0-4 C            | Reduced metabolism            |
    | Subzero            | -20 to -80 C     | Ice crystal formation risk    |
    | Cryogenic          | -130 to -196 C   | Glass transition zone         |
    | LN2 Storage        | -196 C           | Indefinite preservation       |
    +------------------------------------------------------------------------+

The Challenge:
  - Ice crystal formation causes cellular damage
  - Thermal stress can fracture tissues
  - Documentation must survive indefinitely
  - Chain of custody spans generations
  - Revival requires perfect records

Traditional paper records are inadequate for these timeframes. Digital systems
require cryptographic verification to ensure data integrity across decades
or centuries of storage.

1.2 WHY VERIFIABLE CREDENTIALS FOR CRYOPRESERVATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Verifiable Credentials (VCs) provide the foundation for trustworthy, long-term
documentation of cryopreservation subjects and procedures.

[CRYO] CREDENTIAL REQUIREMENTS:

    1. IMMUTABILITY
       Records must remain unchanged throughout storage period
       Cryptographic signatures prevent tampering
       Blockchain anchoring provides timestamp proof

    2. PORTABILITY
       Subject may be transferred between facilities
       Credentials travel with the subject
       Any compliant facility can verify authenticity

    3. PRIVACY
       Sensitive medical data requires protection
       Selective disclosure enables need-to-know access
       Zero-knowledge proofs for verification without exposure

    4. LONGEVITY
       Storage periods may exceed 100 years
       Cryptographic algorithms must be future-proof
       Format migration strategies required

1.3 SUBJECT TYPES AND CLASSIFICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The CRYO-PRESERVATION standard supports multiple subject categories, each
with specific documentation requirements.

[CRYO] SUBJECT TYPE TAXONOMY:

    WHOLE_BODY
      |-- Full organism preservation
      |-- Highest complexity protocols
      +-- Complete medical history required

    NEURO (Neuropreservation)
      |-- Brain/head preservation only
      |-- Focus on neural structure integrity
      +-- Reduced storage footprint

    ORGAN
      |-- Heart, liver, kidney, etc.
      |-- Transplant-focused preservation
      +-- HLA typing documentation

    TISSUE
      |-- Skin, bone, cartilage, vessels
      |-- Regenerative medicine applications
      +-- Batch processing common

    CELL_CULTURE
      |-- Stem cells, cell lines
      |-- Research and therapeutic use
      +-- Standardized vial formats

    EMBRYO
      |-- IVF preservation
      |-- Strict regulatory compliance
      +-- Genetic screening records

    GAMETE
      |-- Sperm and oocyte preservation
      |-- Fertility preservation
      +-- Donor/patient identification

[i] SPECIES CODES:

    - FELIS_CATUS (Cat)
    - EQUUS_CABALLUS (Horse)

1.4 CORE PRINCIPLES AND PHILOSOPHY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The CRYO-PRESERVATION standard is built on fundamental principles that guide
all technical decisions.

+----------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                                            |
|  "Benefit all humanity" - Hongik Ingan Philosophy                          |
|                                                                            |
|  Every preserved subject represents a potential future life. Our           |
|  standards honor this by demanding the highest levels of precision,        |
|  documentation, and care in preservation protocols.                        |
|                                                                            |
+----------------------------------------------------------------------------+

[OK] PRINCIPLE 1: SUBJECT DIGNITY
     Every subject, regardless of type, receives documentation that
     treats preservation as a sacred trust.

[OK] PRINCIPLE 2: SCIENTIFIC RIGOR
     All protocols must be based on peer-reviewed science and
     continuously updated as knowledge advances.

[OK] PRINCIPLE 3: TRANSPARENCY
     Procedures, parameters, and quality metrics must be fully
     documented and auditable.

[OK] PRINCIPLE 4: INTEROPERABILITY
     Credentials must be verifiable by any compliant facility
     worldwide, enabling global cooperation.

[OK] PRINCIPLE 5: FUTURE-PROOFING
     Standards anticipate technological advancement and include
     provisions for format migration and algorithm updates.
