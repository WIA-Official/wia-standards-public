Executive Summary

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released the international standard WIA-DEF-019 for underwater weapons, covering torpedoes, naval mines, unmanned underwater vehicles (UUVs), and sonar systems under MIT License at zero cost. This announcement positions the Republic of Korea as a leader in international maritime defense standardization.

🚨 Current Situation: Why This Standard Is Needed

Over 70% of global maritime disputes are related to territorial claims, and critical maritime infrastructure such as submarine cables and oil platforms face unprecedented threats. Existing underwater weapon systems use disparate specifications and protocols across nations, creating massive barriers to international cooperation and humanitarian mine clearance operations. The absence of standardized frameworks severely threatens the safety of civilian maritime activities.

WIA's Solution: WIA-DEF-019 Underwater Weapon

WIA-DEF-019 standardizes all aspects of underwater weapons including torpedo systems, naval mine warfare, UUV operations, sonar systems, underwater acoustics, guidance and control, and humanitarian mine clearance. It provides a comprehensive framework integrating heavy torpedoes (533mm), light torpedoes (324mm), supercavitating torpedoes, intelligent mines, and autonomous/remotely-operated UUVs.

🏢 About WIA

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) is an international standards organization whose mission is to provide equal opportunities to all humanity through technology and certification.

WIA is developing over 600 international standards in various fields including accessibility, security, AI, environment, and healthcare, and releases all standards as free open source.

🦀 Technology Stack: WIA's core APIs are developed in the Rust language. Rust is a systems programming language developed by Mozilla and is the only language that achieves both memory safety and zero-cost abstractions.

👤 About the CEO

Dr. Yeon Samheum is the world's only solo standardization architect who has built over 60 international standard infrastructures in collaboration with AI, and is recognized as a living legend in lifelong education.
