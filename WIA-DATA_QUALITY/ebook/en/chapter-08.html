8.1 Getting Started with WIA-DATA_QUALITY

Implementing the WIA-DATA_QUALITY Standard requires thoughtful planning and a phased approach. Organizations should begin by assessing their current data quality capabilities, identifying priority datasets, and building the foundational capabilities before expanding to comprehensive implementation. This chapter provides the roadmap for successful implementation and guidance on achieving WIA certification.

The journey typically begins with a current state assessment. Before implementing new capabilities, organizations must understand their existing data quality posture. What quality processes exist today? What tools are in use? What are the biggest quality pain points? What datasets are most critical to business operations? Answering these questions helps focus initial efforts where they will deliver the most value.

Successful implementations start small and expand incrementally. Rather than attempting to implement all four phases across all datasets simultaneously, begin with Phase 1 on a single critical dataset. Demonstrate value, learn from the experience, and then expand to additional datasets and phases. This approach reduces risk, builds organizational capability, and creates advocates for the program.

Executive sponsorship is essential for implementation success. Data quality affects every part of the organization, and addressing quality issues often requires changes to processes and behaviors. Without senior leadership commitment, data quality initiatives struggle to get the resources, attention, and authority needed to succeed. Identify an executive sponsor who understands data quality's business impact and can champion the initiative.

8.5 WIA Certification Process

WIA certification demonstrates that an organization has successfully implemented the WIA-DATA_QUALITY Standard and maintains quality practices that meet established benchmarks. Certification provides external validation, builds customer confidence, and differentiates certified organizations in the market.

Certification Level Requirements Validity
WIA-DQ Bronze Phase 1-2 implemented, 80%+ quality score 1 year
WIA-DQ Silver Phase 1-3 implemented, 90%+ quality score 2 years
WIA-DQ Gold All phases implemented, 95%+ quality score 3 years
WIA-DQ Platinum Gold + industry-specific requirements 3 years

Certification Steps

  1. Self-Assessment: Complete the WIA-DQ self-assessment questionnaire to evaluate readiness.
  2. Documentation: Prepare implementation documentation including architecture diagrams, quality profiles, and operational procedures.
  3. Application: Submit certification application with documentation to WIA Certification Authority.
  4. Audit: WIA-certified auditor conducts on-site or remote assessment of implementation.
  5. Findings: Receive audit findings and address any gaps identified.
  6. Certification: Upon successful audit, receive WIA-DQ certification.
  7. Maintenance: Submit annual compliance reports and undergo periodic re-certification.

8.7 Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Pitfall Impact Prevention Strategy
Boiling the ocean Overwhelmed team, delayed value Start small with pilot, expand incrementally
No executive sponsorship Lack of resources, blocked changes Secure sponsor before starting, maintain engagement
Technology-first approach Tools without process, no adoption Focus on process and people before technology
Unrealistic thresholds Constant failures, alert fatigue Start with achievable targets, improve over time
No stewardship Issues identified but not resolved Establish clear data stewardship from the start
Ignoring organizational change Resistance, workarounds Invest in change management, communicate benefits
