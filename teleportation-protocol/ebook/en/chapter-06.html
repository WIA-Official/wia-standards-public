Key Takeaways

  • Distributed Quantum Computing Enabler: Teleportation allows quantum processors to share quantum states without physical qubit transport. Example: factor 1024-bit number using 100-qubit processors across 3 locations (300 qubits total) via teleportation links. Requires 99.9% fidelity (Gold) to maintain quantum advantage over classical algorithms—core use case for $35B market segment.
  • Quantum Internet Architecture: Unlike classical internet (copy-and-forward), quantum internet uses entanglement distribution + teleportation (no-cloning compliant). Structure: entanglement generation at base stations → quantum repeaters for range extension → teleportation for state transfer → end-user quantum devices. No "quantum router" copies states; all operations preserve quantum information uniqueness.
  • Teleportation-Enhanced QKD: Traditional QKD limited to ~100km (fiber loss). Teleportation-based QKD uses quantum repeaters to extend range to 1000km+ while maintaining security—eavesdropping detection occurs at repeater nodes via Bell inequality tests. Enables metropolitan and regional quantum-secure networks, expanding addressable $12B market 10×.
  • Distributed Quantum Sensing: Entanglement between distributed sensors provides √N sensitivity enhancement over independent sensors (quantum advantage). Example: 100-station gravitational wave detector network with teleported entanglement achieves 10× better sensitivity than classical array. Applications: earth monitoring ($5B), medical imaging ($8B), fundamental physics ($5B) within $18B sensing market.
  • Standards-Driven Interoperability: WIA standard defines: (1) quantum state interface format (Bloch sphere parameters), (2) classical communication protocol (2-bit correction codes), (3) entanglement quality metrics (fidelity, concurrence), (4) API for vendor-neutral integration—enabling multi-vendor quantum internet analogous to TCP/IP for classical internet, critical for ecosystem development.
  • Standardization Economic Impact: Cost savings calculation: ($2M - $800K) × 5000 = $6B saved by 2030. Additionally: 6-12 month time-to-market reduction × $500K/month opportunity cost × 5000 deployments = $15-30B in accelerated revenue. Combined $20-35B economic impact represents 30-50% of $65B total market, demonstrating standardization as growth enabler, not just cost reducer.
  • Application Readiness Timeline: Bronze applications (QKD, basic quantum networks): deployable 2025-2026 based on Stuttgart 82% results. Silver applications (quantum sensor networks, small distributed quantum computers): 2027-2028 after coherence improvements. Gold applications (large-scale distributed quantum computing, global quantum internet backbone): 2029-2032 requiring full error correction.
