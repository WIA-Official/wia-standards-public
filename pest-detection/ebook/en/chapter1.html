1.1 The Global Pest Challenge

Pests and diseases destroy 20-40% of global crop production annually, causing economic losses exceeding $220 billion. Traditional reactive approaches—treating pests after visible damage—result in excessive pesticide use, environmental harm, and significant yield losses.

Global Impact:
  • 40% of global crops lost to pests and diseases annually
  • $220+ billion in economic losses worldwide
  • 2.5 million tons of pesticides used each year
  • Early detection can reduce losses by 40-60%

1.2 What is Modern Pest Detection?

Modern pest detection combines artificial intelligence, image recognition, environmental sensors, and predictive modeling to identify pest threats before significant damage occurs. This proactive approach enables targeted interventions, reducing pesticide use while protecting yields.

Key Technologies:

  1. AI Image Recognition: Automated pest identification from smartphone photos
  2. Smart Traps: IoT-enabled monitoring with real-time alerts
  3. Drones: Aerial surveillance of large agricultural areas
  4. Environmental Sensors: Weather and soil monitoring for risk prediction
  5. Predictive Models: Outbreak forecasting based on historical and real-time data

1.3 Benefits of AI-Powered Detection

Economic Benefits

  • 40-60% reduction in crop losses through early detection
  • 30-50% decrease in pesticide costs via targeted treatment
  • Labor savings from automated monitoring (50-70% reduction)

Environmental Benefits

  • Reduced pesticide use protects beneficial insects and pollinators

Food Security

1.4 Major Agricultural Pests

Insect Pests

Rice Planthopper (Nilaparvata lugens / 벼멸구): Sucks plant sap, transmits viral diseases, can devastate entire rice fields. Migration patterns influenced by monsoon winds. Economic threshold: 10-15 adults per 20 sweeps.

Rice Stem Borer (Chilo suppressalis / 이화명나방): Larvae bore into rice stems, causing "deadheart" and "whitehead" symptoms. Overwinters in crop stubble. Multiple generations per season.

Fungal Diseases

Powdery Mildew (흰가루병): White fungal growth on leaves, common in moderate temperatures (15-25°C) with moderate humidity. Reduces photosynthesis and weakens plants.

Anthracnose (탄저병): Causes dark lesions on fruits and leaves. Thrives in warm, wet conditions. Major problem for vegetables and tree crops.

1.5 The WIA-AGRI-013 Standard

The WIA-AGRI-013 standard provides a comprehensive framework for implementing modern pest detection systems. It covers:

  • Data formats for pest detection records
  • AI model specifications and accuracy requirements
  • Alert protocols for regional coordination
  • Integration with weather services and extension offices
  • Certification systems for pest-free exports
  • Privacy and data sharing guidelines
Standard Philosophy:

弘益人間 (홍익인간) - Benefit All Humanity. The WIA-AGRI-013 standard aims to protect global food security through accessible, standardized pest detection technology available to all farmers worldwide.
