WIA-ELECTRIC_V Standard Architecture

The WIA-ELECTRIC_V standard establishes a comprehensive framework for electric vehicle systems encompassing powertrain architecture, energy storage systems, charging infrastructure integration, vehicle-to-grid communications, and safety certification requirements. The standard is organized into twelve primary specification domains, each addressing critical aspects of electric vehicle technology with detailed technical requirements, testing protocols, and compliance criteria. This modular architecture enables organizations to implement the standard incrementally while maintaining interoperability across different vehicle platforms, charging networks, and grid integration systems. The standard mandates adherence to international safety standards including ISO 26262 for functional safety, UNECE R100 for battery safety, and ISO 15118 for bidirectional charging communication.

The architectural framework defines five hierarchical layers that correspond to different aspects of electric vehicle systems: the Physical Layer addressing hardware components and electrical interfaces, the Communication Layer specifying protocols for intra-vehicle networks and external connectivity, the Application Layer defining software functionality and control algorithms, the Security Layer establishing cryptographic requirements and access control mechanisms, and the Safety Layer encompassing fault detection, mitigation strategies, and emergency response protocols. Each layer includes specific conformance requirements with quantitative metrics that must be validated through standardized testing procedures administered by WIA-certified testing laboratories.

Compliance Testing Framework

The WIA-ELECTRIC_V certification process requires comprehensive testing across functional, safety, performance, and environmental domains using standardized test procedures developed in collaboration with international automotive testing organizations. Compliance testing is conducted at WIA-accredited laboratories equipped with specialized testing equipment including battery cyclers capable of 500A+ charge/discharge rates, electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) chambers meeting CISPR 25 requirements, high-voltage safety test equipment rated for 1500V DC systems, and thermal chambers simulating -40°C to +85°C operating conditions. The testing protocol encompasses over 200 individual test cases organized into mandatory and optional categories, with minimum pass rates of 100% for safety-critical tests and 95% for performance benchmarks.

Certification candidates must submit comprehensive technical documentation including complete electrical schematics, bill of materials with component specifications, software architecture documentation, hazard analysis and risk assessment (HARA) reports per ISO 26262, cybersecurity threat analysis per ISO 21434, and environmental impact assessments. The evaluation process includes design review, component-level testing, system integration testing, vehicle-level validation testing, and field trial verification over minimum 50,000 km under diverse operating conditions. Ongoing compliance is ensured through annual surveillance audits and mandatory recertification every three years or upon significant system modifications.

Safety Certification Requirements

Electric vehicle safety certification under WIA-ELECTRIC_V encompasses electrical safety, functional safety, crash safety, fire safety, and cybersecurity domains with specific requirements derived from international automotive safety standards. High-voltage electrical safety testing validates insulation resistance exceeding 500 Ω/V at working voltage, proper functioning of interlocks and manual service disconnects, automatic high-voltage disconnection within 100ms of airbag deployment, and protection against electric shock hazards meeting IP6K9K ingress protection ratings. Functional safety assessment per ISO 26262 requires achievement of ASIL-D rating for critical safety functions including high-voltage battery management, motor control systems, and brake-by-wire integration, with comprehensive fault injection testing demonstrating safe degradation or shutdown under single-point and latent fault conditions.

Environmental and Sustainability Standards

The WIA-ELECTRIC_V standard incorporates comprehensive environmental requirements addressing vehicle lifecycle impacts from raw material extraction through end-of-life recycling and disposal. Manufacturers must conduct complete lifecycle assessment (LCA) per ISO 14040/14044 methodologies, quantifying carbon footprint, water consumption, and environmental impacts across vehicle production, operation, and recycling phases. The standard mandates minimum 65% battery material recyclability, with specific targets for lithium (90%), cobalt (95%), nickel (95%), and other critical materials. Electromagnetic compatibility testing per CISPR 25 ensures radiated and conducted emissions remain below specified limits across 150kHz to 6GHz frequency ranges, preventing interference with vehicle electronics, charging infrastructure, and wireless communication systems. Supply chain transparency requirements mandate disclosure of conflict mineral sourcing and adherence to responsible mining practices for battery raw materials.
