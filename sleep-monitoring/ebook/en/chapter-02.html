Chapter 02: Sleep Stages and PSG Fundamentals

2.1 Sleep Stages per AASM

The AASM Manual defines five sleep stages: wake, n1 (light sleep), n2 (deeper light sleep with sleep spindles and K-complexes), n3 (slow wave sleep / deep sleep), and rem (rapid eye movement sleep). Stages are typically scored at 30-second epoch granularity from EEG, EOG, and chin EMG signals. WIA-MED-021 carries each epoch as a separate sleep_stage envelope so downstream consumers can render hypnograms and compute stage-percentage statistics without re-deriving them.

2.2 Polysomnography (PSG) signals

A full clinical PSG records many channels simultaneously: EEG (typically 6 channels covering frontal, central, and occipital regions), EOG (left and right eye), chin EMG, ECG, airflow (nasal pressure transducer + thermistor), chest and abdomen respiratory effort belts, pulse oximetry (SpO2), leg EMG (anterior tibialis), and snore microphone. The raw signals ride in a single EDF / EDF+ file referenced from the WIA session record.

2.3 Home sleep apnoea testing (HSAT)

HSAT devices are simpler: typically airflow + SpO2 + heart rate + respiratory effort, without EEG. They cannot score sleep stages directly but can detect respiratory events. WIA-MED-021 supports HSAT via session_kind: home_sleep_apnoea_test; the scoring metadata typically uses an algorithm that estimates total sleep time from movement / heart rate variability.

2.4 Consumer wearables

Consumer wearables (Apple Watch, Fitbit, Oura Ring, Whoop) estimate sleep stages from heart rate and accelerometer signals at 5-minute or 30-second epochs. WIA-MED-021 supports them via session_kind: consumer_wearable with aasm_compliance_level: consumer_estimate. The data is useful for population-level studies and for individual sleep tracking, but it should not be used for clinical apnoea diagnosis.

2.5 Respiratory events

The AASM defines respiratory events that the standard preserves: obstructive apnoea (10+ second airflow cessation with continued effort), central apnoea (10+ second cessation with no effort), mixed apnoea (combination), hypopnoea (30%+ airflow reduction with 3%+ desaturation or arousal), and RERA (Respiratory Effort Related Arousal). The Apnoea-Hypopnoea Index (AHI) summarises these per hour of sleep.

2.6 Arousals

Arousals are EEG-based or movement-based interruptions of sleep continuity. AASM scoring requires arousals to be at least 3 seconds in duration; the standard refuses sub-3-second arousal submissions to prevent vendors from generating spurious "fragmentation" metrics from sub-threshold events.

Patient and Clinician Considerations

Sleep data is biometric and high-stakes. Phase 1 carries a clinical_grade flag and per-channel signal quality metrics so consumer wearable estimates do not get conflated with clinical PSG measurements. Phase 3 enforces patient consent before any cross-host data flow, with explicit per-audience scopes (clinical_dashboard, ehr_export, research_de_identified). Phase 4 maps to the AASM compliance level so downstream consumers can filter by appropriateness for their clinical context.

The standard's default audience controls are intentionally restrictive: even the patient's own family / caregiver receives only stage histograms by default, not the raw event stream. Operators MAY relax these defaults per local policy, but the standard's baseline assumes minimum disclosure unless explicitly elevated by consent.
