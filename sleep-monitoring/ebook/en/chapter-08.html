Chapter 08: Conformance, AASM Compliance, Roadmap

8.1 Conformance test suite

A black-box conformance test suite is published at https://github.com/WIA-Official/wia-sleep-monitoring-conformance and walks through discovery document round-trip, session create / read / withdraw with clearance refusal path, stage bulk submit with idempotency, event submission with arousal-duration validation, scoring re-computation chain (supersedes), federation handshake against a mock peer, and rate-limit headers and exhaustion behaviour. Hosts publishing bridge_profile=Full SHOULD additionally pass the suite\x27s extension tests for at least one supported vendor (HealthKit / Google Fit / Samsung / Fitbit / Oura / Withings) and one AASM scoring algorithm beyond the default.

8.2 Reference container

The wia/sleep-monitoring-host:1.0.0 container image implements every Phase 2 endpoint with mock data. The container ships with a SQLite-backed store suitable for small-to-medium clinical-research deployments (up to 100 000 sessions) and a built-in AASM-2024-default scoring algorithm for the consumer-grade portion of the workload.

8.3 AASM compliance

The standard does not certify AASM compliance — that is the AASM\x27s prerogative. What the standard does is preserve the algorithm version and compliance level explicitly so downstream consumers can filter by appropriateness for their use case. A sleep clinic seeking AASM accreditation uses the standard\x27s audit log (Phase 3 §10) as evidence of conformant scoring practice; the scorer_id field ties each session\x27s scoring to a specific technician\x27s WIA-OMNI-API identity, satisfying the AASM requirement for traceable scoring provenance.

8.5 Backwards-compatibility promise

Within the 1.x line every Phase 2 endpoint MUST remain reachable and MUST continue to honour the documented status codes and content shapes. Hosts MAY add optional query parameters, response fields, new endpoints, or media types. Hosts MUST NOT remove or repurpose existing ones. Breaking changes ride a major version bump and MUST be preceded by a 12-month deprecation window per IETF RFC 8594 and RFC 9745.

8.6 Closing note

Sleep monitoring sits at the intersection of consumer wellness and clinical medicine. The dominant pattern today is per-vendor silos that prevent a unified view across a patient\x27s wearable history and clinical sleep studies. WIA-MED-021 exists to close that gap with an open, federation-aware, audit-logged standard that lets clinicians, researchers, and patients share sleep data on terms each can verify. The hope is that vendor lock-in stops being the default; that a patient\x27s clinical PSG and consumer wearable data can coexist on the same wire with appropriate compliance flagging; and that the field collectively benefits from a wire format that no single vendor controls.

Patient and Clinician Considerations

Sleep data is biometric and high-stakes. Phase 1 carries a clinical_grade flag and per-channel signal quality metrics so consumer wearable estimates do not get conflated with clinical PSG measurements. Phase 3 enforces patient consent before any cross-host data flow, with explicit per-audience scopes (clinical_dashboard, ehr_export, research_de_identified). Phase 4 maps to the AASM compliance level so downstream consumers can filter by appropriateness for their clinical context.

The standard's default audience controls are intentionally restrictive: even the patient's own family / caregiver receives only stage histograms by default, not the raw event stream. Operators MAY relax these defaults per local policy, but the standard's baseline assumes minimum disclosure unless explicitly elevated by consent.
