1.1 The Science of Pet Emotions

Animals experience a rich spectrum of emotions that influence their behavior, health, and quality of life. Modern neuroscience confirms that mammals share fundamental emotional circuitry with humans, enabling genuine emotional experiences.

Evolutionary Basis

Brain Structure Function Shared Across Species
Limbic System Emotional processing All mammals
Amygdala Fear and anxiety All vertebrates
Hypothalamus Emotional regulation All mammals
Prefrontal Cortex Emotional control Higher mammals

1.4 Core Emotion Categories

Primary Emotions

EMOTION SPECTRUM
================

Positive States:
  HAPPY        - Joy, pleasure, satisfaction
  CALM         - Relaxed, peaceful, content
  EXCITED      - Enthusiastic, energetic
  PLAYFUL      - Engaging, interactive

Social States:
  AFFECTIONATE - Bonding, loving, attached
  CURIOUS      - Interested, exploring
  ALERT        - Attentive, watchful

Negative States:
  SAD          - Dejected, withdrawn
  ANXIOUS      - Worried, nervous
  FEARFUL      - Scared, threatened
  FRUSTRATED   - Blocked, irritated
  AGGRESSIVE   - Hostile, defensive

Neutral States:
  BORED        - Understimulated
  TIRED        - Fatigued, sleepy
  CONTENT      - Satisfied, neutral

1.5 Expression Channels

Visual Signals

Signal Type Examples Emotions Indicated
Body Posture Relaxed, tense, crouched Overall state
Tail Position Wagging, tucked, raised Arousal, fear, excitement
Ear Position Forward, back, relaxed Attention, fear, comfort
Facial Expression Eye shape, mouth tension Specific emotions

Audio Signals

Vocalization Characteristics Typical Meaning
Bark (dog) Pitch, frequency, duration Alert, excitement, demand
Whine High pitch, continuous Anxiety, need, pain
Purr (cat) Low frequency vibration Content, self-soothing
Growl Low, rumbling Warning, discomfort

1.6 Physiological Indicators

PHYSIOLOGICAL MARKERS
=====================

Heart Rate:
  - Elevated: excitement, fear, anxiety
  - Lowered: calm, tired, content
  - Variable: playful, curious

Heart Rate Variability (HRV):
  - High HRV: relaxed, adaptable
  - Low HRV: stressed, fatigued

Activity Level:
  - Increased: excited, anxious, playful
  - Decreased: tired, sad, calm

Body Temperature:
  - Elevated: excitement, stress
  - Normal: calm, content

1.7 Species Considerations

Dogs (Canis familiaris)

  • [i] Highly expressive faces and body language
  • [i] Wide range of vocalizations
  • [i] Co-evolved with humans for 15,000+ years
  • [i] Strong social bonding emotions

Cats (Felis catus)

  • [i] Subtle facial expressions
  • [i] Tail and ear positions key indicators
  • [i] Vocalizations often directed at humans
  • [i] Independent but capable of deep attachment

Other Species

  • [i] Rabbits: Ear and body position focused
  • [i] Birds: Feather position, vocalizations
  • [i] Horses: Ear, eye, and nostril expressions
