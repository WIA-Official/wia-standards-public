Chapter 5: Safety and Validation

Safety is the paramount concern for autonomous vehicle deployment. This chapter details WIA-AUTO requirements for functional safety, validation methodologies, and comprehensive testing necessary to demonstrate autonomous vehicle safety.

5.1 Functional Safety Framework

WIA-AUTO builds upon ISO 26262 for functional safety of road vehicles, extending its concepts for autonomous driving systems.

5.2 Safety of the Intended Functionality (SOTIF)

SOTIF (ISO 21448) addresses safety risks arising from functional insufficiencies, even when the system is operating as designed.

SOTIF Scope

  • Sensor limitations (range, resolution, environmental conditions)
  • Algorithm limitations (edge cases, out-of-distribution inputs)
  • Triggering conditions not anticipated during design
  • User misuse within foreseeable boundaries
  • Interaction with unpredictable road user behavior

5.3 Validation Methodologies

Demonstrating autonomous vehicle safety requires extensive validation across multiple methodologies including simulation, track testing, and real-world driving.

Simulation Requirements
=======================
Environment Fidelity:
- Physics: Vehicle dynamics within 5% of real-world
- Sensors: Realistic noise, artifacts, failure modes
- Rendering: Photo-realistic for camera testing

Scenario Coverage:
- Functional scenarios: 1,000+ scenario categories
- Edge cases: Rare events with high consequence
- Adversarial scenarios: Designed to stress limits
- Monte Carlo: Statistical variation of parameters

Validation Metrics:
- No collisions in 10,000+ simulation hours
- Safe handling of 95% of edge case library
- Graceful degradation in out-of-ODD scenarios

5.6 Runtime Safety Monitoring

Safety Monitor Functions

  • Perception Plausibility: Cross-check sensor data for consistency
  • Planning Verification: Verify trajectories comply with safety constraints
  • ODD Monitoring: Detect when conditions exceed operational domain
  • System Health: Monitor computational resources, sensor status
  • Behavior Monitoring: Detect anomalous system behavior

5.7 Continuous Improvement

  • Field Data Collection: Systematic collection of operational data
  • Incident Investigation: Root cause analysis for safety events
  • OTA Update Process: Secure updates with validation requirements
  • Reporting Requirements: Mandatory reporting to authorities
