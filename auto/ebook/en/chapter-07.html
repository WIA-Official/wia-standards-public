Chapter 7: Legal and Ethical Framework

Autonomous vehicles operate within complex legal and ethical landscapes that vary across jurisdictions. This chapter addresses liability allocation, insurance requirements, ethical decision-making, and regulatory compliance for responsible AV deployment.

Data Recording Requirements

WIA-AUTO mandates Data Storage System for Automated Driving (DSSAD) recording:

  • ADS activation/deactivation status and mode
  • Driver availability and attention state
  • Takeover requests and driver responses
  • System failures and degradation events
  • Collision events with sensor data

7.4 Ethical Decision Making

WIA-AUTO Ethical Framework
==========================
Priority 1: Legal Compliance
    └── Obey traffic laws, yield to right-of-way

Priority 2: Human Life Protection
    └── Minimize risk of death to any road user

Priority 3: Injury Minimization
    └── Reduce severity of unavoidable impacts

Priority 4: Property Damage Minimization
    └── Prefer property damage over personal injury

Priority 5: Predictable Behavior
    └── Behave predictably to other road users

7.6 Accessibility Requirements

  • Physical Accessibility: Vehicle entry/exit accommodations for mobility devices
  • Sensory Accessibility: Audio, visual, and haptic interface options
  • Cognitive Accessibility: Clear, simple interfaces
  • Emergency Accessibility: Accessible emergency stop functions
  • Service Animal Support: Accommodation for guide dogs

7.8 International Cooperation

WIA-AUTO promotes international cooperation for cross-border AV operation:

  • Type approval and certification recognition
  • Incident data sharing for safety improvement
  • Coordinated regulatory development
  • Standard V2X protocols for cross-border communication
  • HD map data formats enabling international operation

7.9 Privacy and Data Protection

AV Data Privacy Framework

Autonomous vehicles generate vast amounts of data that require careful protection:

  • Location Data: Travel history, frequent destinations, real-time position
  • Biometric Data: Driver monitoring, voice commands, facial recognition
  • Behavioral Data: Driving patterns, preferences, usage habits
  • Environmental Data: Camera footage, sensor data capturing third parties
  • Vehicle Data: Diagnostic information, maintenance records
