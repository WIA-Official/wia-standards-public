## A

**Accountability**
The principle that data controllers must take responsibility for complying with GDPR and be able to demonstrate compliance through documentation and evidence.

**Adequacy Decision**
A decision by the European Commission that a third country, territory, or international organization provides an adequate level of data protection, allowing personal data transfers without additional safeguards.

**Anonymization**
The process of transforming personal data in such a way that it can no longer be attributed to a specific data subject, even with additional information. Anonymized data is not subject to GDPR.

**Appropriate Safeguards**
Measures that ensure an adequate level of protection for personal data transferred to third countries, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or Binding Corporate Rules.

**Automated Decision-Making**
Making decisions by technological means without human involvement, which can significantly affect individuals (Article 22).

---

## B

**BCRs (Binding Corporate Rules)**
Internal data protection policies adopted by multinational companies for transfers of personal data within the same corporate group to countries outside the EEA.

**Biometric Data**
Personal data resulting from specific technical processing relating to physical, physiological, or behavioral characteristics (e.g., facial images, fingerprints) that allow unique identification (Article 4(14)).

**Breach of Security**
See "Personal Data Breach"

---

## C

**CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)**
California state law providing privacy rights and consumer protection, similar to but distinct from GDPR.

**Consent**
A freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of the data subject's wishes by which they signify agreement to the processing of their personal data (Article 4(11)).

**Controller**
The natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which determines the purposes and means of processing personal data (Article 4(7)).

**Cross-Border Processing**
Processing of personal data that:
- Takes place in the context of establishments in more than one Member State, or
- Substantially affects or is likely to substantially affect data subjects in more than one Member State

---

## D

**Data Minimization**
The principle that personal data collected must be adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the processing purposes (Article 5(1)(c)).

**Data Portability**
The right of data subjects to receive personal data concerning them in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and transmit it to another controller (Article 20).

**Data Processing Agreement (DPA)**
A contract between a controller and processor setting out the processor's obligations under GDPR (Article 28).

**Data Protection by Design and by Default**
The requirement to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to integrate data protection principles into processing activities and ensure that by default, only necessary personal data is processed (Article 25).

**Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)**
A process to assess the impact of processing operations on the protection of personal data, required when processing is likely to result in high risk to individuals' rights and freedoms (Article 35).

**Data Protection Officer (DPO)**
An expert in data protection who monitors compliance with GDPR and acts as a contact point for data subjects and supervisory authorities (Article 37-39).

**Data Subject**
An identified or identifiable natural person whose personal data is processed (Article 4(1)).

**Data Subject Access Request (DSAR)**
A request by a data subject to exercise their right of access under Article 15, seeking confirmation of processing and a copy of their personal data.

**Derogation**
An exception to the general prohibition on transferring personal data to third countries, allowed in specific situations under Article 49 (e.g., explicit consent, contract necessity).

---

## E

**EDPB (European Data Protection Board)**
An independent EU body that ensures consistent application of GDPR across the EU and promotes cooperation between supervisory authorities.

**EEA (European Economic Area)**
The area comprising EU Member States plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway, where GDPR applies.

**Encryption**
The process of converting data into a code to prevent unauthorized access, considered an appropriate security measure under Article 32.

**Explicit Consent**
A heightened form of consent required for processing special category data, requiring a clear affirmative action (Article 9).

---

## F

**Filing System**
Any structured set of personal data accessible according to specific criteria, whether centralized, decentralized, or dispersed on a functional or geographical basis (Article 4(6)).

---

## G

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**
Regulation (EU) 2016/679, the European Union regulation governing data protection and privacy for individuals within the EU/EEA.

**Genetic Data**
Personal data relating to inherited or acquired genetic characteristics that provide unique information about physiology or health (Article 4(13)).

---

## H

**Health Data**
Personal data related to physical or mental health, including the provision of health care services (Article 4(15)).

---

## I

**ICO (Information Commissioner's Office)**
The UK's supervisory authority for data protection and information rights.

**Identifiable Natural Person**
A person who can be identified, directly or indirectly, by reference to identifiers such as name, ID number, location data, online identifiers, or factors specific to their physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity (Article 4(1)).

**Legitimate Interest**
A lawful basis for processing personal data where it is necessary for the controller's or third party's legitimate interests, unless outweighed by the data subject's interests or fundamental rights (Article 6(1)(f)).

**Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA)**
A balancing test to determine whether processing based on legitimate interest is justified.

---

## J

**Joint Controllers**
Two or more controllers that jointly determine the purposes and means of processing personal data (Article 26).

---

## K

**k-Anonymity**
A property of anonymized data whereby each individual is indistinguishable from at least k-1 other individuals in the dataset.

---

## L

**Lead Supervisory Authority**
The supervisory authority responsible for monitoring cross-border processing activities when a controller or processor has establishments in multiple Member States (Article 56).

**Legal Basis**
The lawful ground for processing personal data under Article 6(1): consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests.

**LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados)**
Brazil's General Data Protection Law, largely aligned with GDPR.

---

## M

**Main Establishment**
The place of central administration in the EU, or where decisions about processing purposes and means are made (Article 4(16)).

---

## O

**One-Stop-Shop**
The GDPR mechanism allowing organizations to deal with a single lead supervisory authority for cross-border processing (Article 56-58).

---

## P

**Personal Data**
Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (data subject) (Article 4(1)). Examples: name, email, IP address, location data, cookie ID.

**Personal Data Breach**
A breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to personal data (Article 4(12)).

**PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**
Another term for personal data, more commonly used in US privacy laws.

**PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)**
Canada's federal privacy law for commercial activities.

**Privacy by Design**
See "Data Protection by Design and by Default"

**Privacy Notice**
A document informing data subjects about the processing of their personal data (Articles 13-14).

**Processing**
Any operation performed on personal data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, restriction, erasure, or destruction (Article 4(2)).

**Processor**
A natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body that processes personal data on behalf of the controller (Article 4(8)).

**Profiling**
Automated processing of personal data to evaluate, analyze, or predict aspects concerning an individual's performance, economic situation, health, personal preferences, interests, reliability, behavior, location, or movements (Article 4(4)).

**Pseudonymization**
Processing personal data in such a way that it can no longer be attributed to a specific data subject without additional information, which is kept separately under security measures (Article 4(5)).

**Purpose Limitation**
The principle that personal data must be collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes (Article 5(1)(b)).

---

## R

**Recipient**
A natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body to which personal data is disclosed (Article 4(9)).

**Records of Processing Activities (RoPA)**
Documentation maintained by controllers and processors listing their processing activities (Article 30).

**Rectification**
The right of data subjects to have inaccurate personal data corrected and incomplete data completed (Article 16).

**Representative**
An entity designated by a controller or processor not established in the EU to act on its behalf regarding GDPR obligations (Article 27).

**Restriction of Processing**
Limiting the processing of personal data, typically by marking stored data to prevent further processing (Article 18).

**Right to be Forgotten**
See "Right to Erasure"

**Right to Erasure**
The right of data subjects to have their personal data erased under certain conditions (Article 17).

**Right to Object**
The right of data subjects to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes (Article 21).

**RTBF (Right to be Forgotten)**
Common term for the Right to Erasure under Article 17.

---

## S

**SCCs (Standard Contractual Clauses)**
Model clauses adopted by the European Commission for the transfer of personal data to third countries (Article 46(2)(c)). Updated version published in 2021.

**Special Category Data**
Personal data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data (for unique identification), health data, or data concerning sex life or sexual orientation (Article 9). Requires heightened protection.

**Storage Limitation**
The principle that personal data must not be kept in identifiable form longer than necessary for the processing purposes (Article 5(1)(e)).

**Subject Access Request**
See "Data Subject Access Request (DSAR)"

**Supervisory Authority**
An independent public authority established by an EU Member State to monitor and enforce GDPR (Article 51). Examples: CNIL (France), ICO (UK), DPC (Ireland).

---

## T

**Third Country**
A country outside the European Economic Area (EEA).

**Third Party**
A natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or body other than the data subject, controller, processor, and persons authorized to process personal data under the direct authority of controller or processor (Article 4(10)).

**Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA)**
An assessment of the legal framework in the third country receiving personal data to ensure adequate protection, required following the Schrems II decision.

**Transparency**
The principle that information about processing must be easily accessible, clear, and provided to data subjects in plain language (Article 5(1)(a)).

---

## V

**Verifiable Parental Consent**
Consent obtained from parents or legal guardians for processing children's personal data, requiring reasonable efforts to verify (Article 8).

---

## W

**Withdrawal of Consent**
The right of data subjects to withdraw consent at any time, which must be as easy as giving consent (Article 7(3)).

| Contract | 6(1)(b) | Necessary for service provision | Limited | No |

| Vital Interests | 6(1)(d) | Life-threatening situations | No | No |

## Data Subject Rights Overview

| Right | Article | Timeframe | Exceptions |
|-------|---------|-----------|------------|
| Access | 15 | 1 month (extendable to 3) | Manifestly unfounded/excessive requests |
| Rectification | 16 | Without undue delay | Must not affect rights of others |
| Erasure | 17 | Without undue delay | Legal obligation, public interest, legal claims |
| Restriction | 18 | Immediately | Limited exceptions |
| Portability | 20 | 1 month | Only for automated processing based on consent/contract |
| Object | 21 | Immediately (marketing) / Assessment required (legitimate interest) | Compelling legitimate grounds may override |
| Not Subject to Automated Decisions | 22 | N/A | Consent, contract necessity, legal authorization |
