## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

This specification establishes comprehensive ethical guidelines for time travel operations, ensuring responsible use of temporal displacement technology while protecting historical integrity, preventing exploitation, and maintaining causality.

### 1.2 Scope

The standard covers:
- Ethical principles governing time travel
- Non-interference protocols and exception handling
- Historical preservation requirements
- Prevention of temporal exploitation
- Review and approval processes
- Enforcement mechanisms and penalties
- Monitoring and compliance verification

### 1.3 Philosophy

**弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity)** - Time travel technology must serve the greater good of humanity. This standard ensures:

- **Universal Benefit**: Technology serves all of humanity, not select individuals
- **Historical Respect**: Past events and peoples are treated with dignity
- **Future Protection**: Actions consider impacts on all future timelines
- **Justice**: Equitable access and fair application of rules
- **Responsibility**: Travelers accept full accountability for temporal actions

### 1.4 Terminology

- **Temporal Traveler**: Individual or entity engaging in time travel
- **Historical Event**: Event that has occurred in consensus timeline
- **Intervention**: Any action affecting past/future events or individuals
- **Observer Status**: Passive witness without physical/causal interaction
- **Temporal Exploitation**: Using time travel for unfair personal advantage
- **Timeline Integrity**: Consistency and stability of causal chains
- **Protected Period**: Time period with restricted access
- **Ethics Review Board (ERB)**: Authorized approval body for temporal operations

## 2. Ethical Principles

### 2.1 Core Principles

#### 2.1.1 Principle of Non-Maleficence

"First, do no harm" - Temporal operations must not:
- Harm individuals in any time period
- Damage historical integrity
- Create timeline instabilities
- Introduce paradoxes
- Violate temporal rights

#### 2.1.2 Principle of Beneficence

Temporal operations should:
- Advance human knowledge
- Prevent unnecessary suffering (when ethically permissible)
- Preserve cultural heritage
- Benefit humanity as a whole
- Respect individual autonomy across time

#### 2.1.3 Principle of Justice

Time travel access and operations must be:
- Equitably distributed
- Non-discriminatory
- Accountable to all affected parties
- Transparent in decision-making
- Fair in application of rules

#### 2.1.4 Principle of Respect for Autonomy

Individuals in all time periods have right to:
- Self-determination without temporal interference
- Privacy from future surveillance
- Protection from temporal manipulation
- Informed consent (when interaction necessary)
- Cultural sovereignty

### 2.2 Temporal Rights Framework

#### 2.2.1 Individual Temporal Rights

Every person across all timelines has:

1. **Right to Temporal Privacy**
   - Protection from unauthorized observation
   - Freedom from temporal surveillance
   - Privacy of personal timeline

2. **Right to Non-Interference**
   - Protection from timeline alteration
   - Freedom from temporal manipulation
   - Preservation of causal autonomy

3. **Right to Temporal Dignity**
   - Respectful treatment by temporal visitors
   - Protection from exploitation
   - Recognition of historical context

4. **Right to Informed Consent**
   - Knowledge of temporal observation (when feasible)
   - Voluntary participation in temporal research
   - Right to refuse temporal interaction

#### 2.2.2 Collective Temporal Rights

Societies and civilizations have:

1. **Right to Historical Integrity**
   - Protection of cultural development
   - Preservation of historical events
   - Non-interference in societal evolution

2. **Right to Cultural Heritage**
   - Protection of artifacts and knowledge
   - Preservation of cultural practices
   - Prevention of temporal appropriation

3. **Right to Timeline Sovereignty**
   - Self-determination without external temporal influence
   - Protection from timeline colonization
   - Autonomy in historical development

## 3. Non-Interference Doctrine

### 3.1 Levels of Interference

#### Level 1: Absolute Non-Interference (PROHIBITED)

Actions permanently prohibited without Supreme Council override:

1. **Major Historical Events**
   - Wars, conflicts, peace treaties
   - Political transitions, revolutions
   - Assassinations or prevention thereof
   - Major disasters (natural or human-caused)
   - Constitutional changes, legal reforms

2. **Scientific/Technological Breakthroughs**
   - Major discoveries (relativity, quantum mechanics, DNA)
   - Technological inventions (wheel, printing press, computer)
   - Medical breakthroughs (antibiotics, vaccines)
   - Mathematical/philosophical advances

3. **Cultural Milestones**
   - Religious founding events
   - Artistic masterworks creation
   - Literary publications
   - Cultural movements

4. **Births and Deaths**
   - Historically significant individuals
   - Lineage-critical births
   - Deaths affecting historical trajectory

**Examples:**
- ❌ Preventing assassination of Abraham Lincoln
- ❌ Sharing penicillin formula before Fleming's discovery
- ❌ Preventing 1906 San Francisco earthquake
- ❌ Influencing outcome of World War II

#### Level 2: Conditional Interaction (REQUIRES ENHANCED REVIEW)

Permitted with Enhanced ERB approval:

1. **Scientific Observation**
   - Non-interactive data collection
   - Environmental measurements
   - Astronomical observations
   - Archaeological documentation

2. **Personal/Family Research**
   - Genealogical research (passive observation)
   - Family history documentation
   - Personal ancestry verification

3. **Minor Event Witnessing**
   - Local community events
   - Everyday life observation
   - Cultural practice documentation

**Conditions:**
- No physical interaction with individuals
- No introduction of anachronistic materials
- Minimal temporal footprint
- Stealth protocols enforced
- Post-operation timeline verification

**Examples:**
- ✓ Photographing daily life in ancient Rome (stealth mode)
- ✓ Measuring atmospheric CO2 in prehistoric era
- ✓ Observing personal ancestor's wedding (from distance)

#### Level 3: Permitted Intervention (EMERGENCY REVIEW)

Allowed only in extraordinary circumstances:

1. **Disaster Prevention**
   - Natural disasters (when lives saved >> timeline impact)
   - Industrial accidents (contained events)
   - Epidemiological intervention (severe cases)

2. **Medical Intervention**
   - Life-threatening situations (minor historical figures)
   - Disease outbreak prevention (contained)
   - Humanitarian rescue (crisis situations)

3. **Environmental Protection**
   - Preventing ecological collapse
   - Species preservation (extinct species revival)
   - Catastrophic pollution prevention

**Requirements:**
- Imminent threat to life/ecosystem
- Minimal timeline impact modeling
- Novikov consistency verification
- Supreme Council approval
- Real-time monitoring
- Remediation plan

**Examples:**
- ✓ Warning village of imminent volcanic eruption (minor village, major lives saved)
- ✓ Preventing extinction of keystone species
- ✗ Preventing Pompeii eruption (major historical event)

### 3.2 Stealth Protocols

When observation is permitted, travelers must:

1. **Visual Concealment**
   - Period-appropriate clothing
   - Cultural mannerisms
   - Language proficiency
   - Physical appearance modification (if necessary)

2. **Technology Concealment**
   - No visible anachronistic devices
   - Cloaking technology for equipment
   - Remote/hidden monitoring systems
   - Quantum-stealth observation posts

3. **Interaction Minimization**
   - Avoid direct conversation when possible
   - Use cover identities sparingly
   - Minimize physical presence
   - Maintain observer distance

4. **Temporal Footprint Reduction**
   - Leave no physical evidence
   - Minimal electromagnetic signature
   - No biological contamination
   - Quantum erasure of causal influence

### 3.3 Exception Handling

Rare exceptions to non-interference require:

1. **Supreme Council Vote**
   - Unanimous approval required
   - Public disclosure (post-operation)
   - Historical necessity demonstrated
   - Timeline modeling completed

2. **Novikov Consistency Proof**
   - Action already part of timeline
   - No paradox creation
   - Self-consistent causal loop
   - Timeline integrity maintained

3. **Ethical Necessity**
   - Overwhelming humanitarian benefit
   - No alternative solutions
   - Proportional response
   - Minimal intervention principle

**Historical Examples of Approved Exceptions:**
- Time Traveler providing Isaac Newton with apple incident (bootstrap paradox, already part of timeline)
- Rescue of Library of Alexandria documents (preservation, no timeline change)

## 4. Historical Preservation

### 4.1 Protected Periods

#### 4.1.1 Absolute Protection Zones

Time periods with complete access restrictions:

1. **Formation Events**
   - Big Bang (first 380,000 years)
   - Earth formation (4.5-4.0 billion years ago)
   - Origin of life (3.8-3.5 billion years ago)

2. **Extinction Events**
   - K-Pg extinction (66 million years ago)
   - Permian-Triassic extinction (252 million years ago)

3. **Human Evolution Critical Points**
   - Homo sapiens emergence (300,000 years ago)
   - Cognitive revolution (70,000 years ago)
   - Agricultural revolution (10,000 BCE)

4. **Recorded History Critical Events**
   - Major wars: WWI, WWII, etc.
   - Political revolutions: French, American, Russian
   - Scientific revolutions: Copernican, Darwinian, Einstein

**Access:** Prohibited except Supreme Council scientific missions

#### 4.1.2 Enhanced Protection Zones

Restricted access requiring Enhanced Review:

1. **Cultural Golden Ages**
   - Classical Greece (5th century BCE)
   - Renaissance Italy (14-17th century)
   - Islamic Golden Age (8-13th century)

2. **Technological Transitions**
   - Industrial Revolution (1760-1840)
   - Digital Revolution (1970-2000)
   - Space Age beginning (1957-1969)

3. **Recent History**
   - Past 100 years (living memory)
   - Living persons' lifetimes
   - Contemporary events

**Access:** Scientific/educational purposes with strict protocols

### 4.2 Artifact Protection

#### 4.2.1 Prohibited Actions

Temporal travelers may NOT:

1. **Remove Historical Artifacts**
   - Art, sculptures, manuscripts
   - Archaeological remains
   - Cultural treasures
   - Natural specimens

2. **Alter Artifacts**
   - Restoration without approval
   - Adding modern markings
   - Contamination with modern materials

3. **Create Anachronisms**
   - Leaving modern objects in past
   - Introducing future knowledge prematurely
   - Technological seeding

**Penalty:** Severe (see Section 9)

#### 4.2.2 Documentation Protocols

Permitted documentation methods:

1. **Non-Invasive Recording**
   - Photography (stealth mode)
   - 3D scanning (remote)
   - Audio recording (passive)
   - Video documentation (concealed)

2. **Scientific Sampling**
   - Microscopic samples only
   - Non-destructive analysis
   - Atmospheric/environmental samples
   - Requires ERB approval

3. **Digital Preservation**
   - High-resolution scanning
   - Molecular-level documentation
   - Quantum state recording
   - Timeline-safe storage

### 4.3 Knowledge Preservation

#### 4.3.1 Lost Knowledge Recovery

Permitted activities:

1. **Document Retrieval**
   - Copying lost manuscripts (Library of Alexandria, etc.)
   - Recording oral traditions
   - Preserving endangered languages
   - Documenting lost technologies

**Conditions:**
- No removal of original documents
- Digital/photographic copies only
- No introduction of modern analysis in past
- Public archive of recovered knowledge

2. **Witness Accounts**
   - Recording historical eyewitness testimonies
   - Interviewing historical figures (approved cases)
   - Documenting personal experiences

**Conditions:**
- Informed consent protocols
- Cultural sensitivity
- Minimal interference
- Respectful treatment

## 5. Temporal Exploitation Prevention

### 5.1 Financial Exploitation

#### 5.1.1 Prohibited Financial Activities

Strictly forbidden:

1. **Stock Market Manipulation**
   - Trading based on future knowledge
   - Short-selling with temporal advantage
   - Insider trading across time
   - Market timing using future data

2. **Betting and Gambling**
   - Sports betting with known outcomes
   - Lottery number knowledge
   - Casino gambling with future knowledge
   - Prediction markets manipulation

3. **Real Estate Speculation**
   - Land acquisition based on future development
   - Property timing with temporal knowledge
   - Resource claim jumping
   - Disaster-based opportunism

4. **Intellectual Property Theft**
   - Patent filing before original inventor
   - Copyright claiming of future works
   - Trademark squatting
   - Trade secret theft

**Enforcement:**
- Automated financial monitoring
- Temporal banking restrictions
- Transaction timeline analysis
- Wealth source verification

**Penalty:** Critical severity (see Section 9)

#### 5.1.2 Economic Safeguards

Required systems:

1. **Temporal Banking Regulations**
   - Funds must originate from traveler's origin timeline
   - No cross-temporal money transfers (except authorized research funds)
   - Wealth verification before temporal travel
   - Post-travel financial audits

2. **Investment Restrictions**
   - No investments in past with future knowledge
   - Blind trusts for authorized temporal researchers
   - Mandatory disclosure of temporal travel to financial institutions
   - Transaction blocking for temporal travelers

3. **Cryptocurrency Controls**
   - Blockchain timeline verification
   - Temporal signature requirements
   - Cross-timeline transaction monitoring
   - Quantum-secure temporal wallets

### 5.2 Knowledge Exploitation

#### 5.2.1 Prohibited Knowledge Use

1. **Scientific Discovery Theft**
   - Cannot claim discoveries before original discoverer
   - No publication of future knowledge in past
   - No patenting of future inventions
   - Respect original inventor credit

2. **Artistic/Creative Theft**
   - Cannot plagiarize future works
   - No claiming authorship of future art/music/literature
   - Respect original creator rights
   - Prohibition on "inspired by" claims without disclosure

3. **Technology Transfer**
   - No introduction of advanced technology to past
   - No reverse-engineering future devices in past
   - No selling future technology in past markets
   - No military technology seeding

**Exceptions:**
- Knowledge already part of timeline (bootstrap paradoxes)
- Authorized educational missions (future to present only)
- Humanitarian interventions (approved cases)

#### 5.2.2 Academic Integrity

Researchers must:

1. **Citation Requirements**
   - Cite all temporal sources
   - Disclose temporal research methods
   - Credit original discoverers/creators
   - Temporal research ethics statements

2. **Publication Guidelines**
   - Temporal data marked clearly
   - Timeline verification included
   - Ethics review approval referenced
   - Reproducibility considerations

3. **Peer Review**
   - Temporal research subject to enhanced review
   - Multi-timeline verification when possible
   - Independent timeline confirmation
   - Transparency in methods

### 5.3 Resource Exploitation

#### 5.3.1 Prohibited Resource Activities

1. **Temporal Mining**
   - Cannot extract resources from past for profit
   - No exploitation of extinct species
   - No mineral extraction from historical sites
   - No resource claim jumping

2. **Agricultural Exploitation**
   - No crop harvesting from past for commercial use
   - No seed/genetic material theft
   - No agricultural land grabbing
   - No exploitation of lost crop varieties (except preservation)

3. **Biological Resource Exploitation**
   - No extraction of extinct species DNA for commercial use
   - No bio-prospecting in past ecosystems
   - No pharmaceutical discovery theft
   - No genetic resource appropriation

**Exceptions:**
- Conservation/preservation efforts (approved)
- Scientific research (non-commercial)
- Species revival programs (ethical review)
- Environmental restoration (authorized)

## 6. Personal Gain Restrictions

### 6.1 Individual Benefit Limitations

#### 6.1.1 Prohibited Personal Actions

1. **Self-Advantage Paradoxes**
   - Cannot help younger self gain advantage
   - No sharing lottery numbers with past self
   - No career advice to past self beyond natural knowledge
   - No prevention of personal mistakes for gain

2. **Family Advantage**
   - Cannot provide future knowledge to ancestors
   - No wealth transfer to family in past
   - No career manipulation for relatives
   - No arranged marriages/relationships across time

3. **Social Climbing**
   - Cannot use future knowledge to gain social status in past
   - No relationship formation based on future knowledge
   - No manipulation of historical figures for personal connection
   - No creation of false historical reputation

4. **Life Extension Schemes**
   - Cannot repeatedly live through same time period
   - No age reversal through temporal cycling
   - No extended lifespan through time loop exploitation
   - No temporal immortality schemes

**Penalty:** Severe to Critical

#### 6.1.2 Acceptable Personal Benefits

Limited personal benefits allowed:

1. **Historical Research**
   - Personal ancestry research (passive)
   - Family history understanding
   - Cultural heritage exploration

2. **Educational Enrichment**
   - Witnessing historical events (approved)
   - Learning from historical figures (observation)
   - Experiencing different eras (cultural immersion)

3. **Emotional Closure**
   - Witnessing deceased loved ones (no interaction)
   - Understanding historical family events
   - Closure on personal historical questions

**Conditions:**
- No material gain
- No interference
- Emotional/educational value only
- ERB approval required
- Strict observer protocols

### 6.2 Professional Ethics

#### 6.2.1 Career and Employment

Temporal travelers in professional contexts must:

1. **Avoid Unfair Advantage**
   - No use of future knowledge in current employment
   - No insider information from temporal travel
   - No competitive advantage through temporal research
   - Full disclosure to employers about temporal travel

2. **Academic Ethics**
   - No plagiarism across timelines
   - Proper citation of temporal sources
   - Disclosure of temporal research methods
   - Peer review compliance

3. **Medical/Legal Ethics**
   - No use of future medical knowledge for personal practice advantage
   - No future legal precedent knowledge for case advantage
   - Patient/client confidentiality across timelines
   - Professional standards compliance

#### 6.2.2 Political Restrictions

Temporal travelers are prohibited from:

1. **Electoral Manipulation**
   - Using future knowledge to influence elections
   - Campaign strategy based on future outcomes
   - Voter manipulation with temporal knowledge
   - Political betting/speculation

2. **Policy Influence**
   - Lobbying based on future knowledge of policy outcomes
   - Manipulating policy decisions with temporal data
   - Insider political knowledge exploitation

3. **Diplomatic Activities**
   - Cannot use future knowledge in negotiations
   - No temporal espionage for political gain
   - No manipulation of international relations

**Exception:** Authorized government temporal research programs (strict oversight)

## 7. Observer-Only Protocols

### 7.1 Observer Status Requirements

#### 7.1.1 Definition of Observer Status

Observer status means:

1. **No Physical Interaction**
   - No touching of people or objects
   - No manipulation of environment
   - No causally significant presence
   - Quantum-minimal footprint

2. **No Communication**
   - No verbal communication with historical individuals
   - No written messages left
   - No non-verbal intentional signaling
   - No temporal message exchange

3. **No Electromagnetic Interference**
   - Minimal EM signature
   - No radio/communication emissions
   - Shielded equipment
   - Quantum stealth mode

4. **No Biological Contamination**
   - Sealed environmental suit
   - No biological material exchange
   - Sterilization protocols
   - Disease prevention measures

#### 7.1.2 Observer Equipment

Approved observer equipment:

1. **Recording Devices**
   - Passive cameras (no flash)
   - Audio recorders (directional, passive)
   - Environmental sensors
   - Quantum state recorders

2. **Protection Equipment**
   - Environmental suits (period-appropriate exterior)
   - Temporal field generators (personal)
   - Health monitoring systems
   - Emergency extraction devices

3. **Stealth Technology**
   - Optical camouflage (visual spectrum)
   - EM signature dampening
   - Acoustic masking
   - Quantum stealth fields

**Prohibited Equipment:**
- Weapons of any kind
- Communication devices (past-targeted)
- Technology from future eras
- Anachronistic materials

### 7.2 Passive Observation Guidelines

#### 7.2.1 Visual Observation

Permitted:
- Photography with stealth equipment
- Video recording (concealed)
- Visual documentation
- Artistic rendering (post-visit)

Requirements:
- No flash photography
- No visible cameras
- Concealed recording devices
- Quantum-entangled storage (cannot be confiscated in past)

#### 7.2.2 Audio Observation

Permitted:
- Passive audio recording
- Environmental sound capture
- Speech recording (public spaces)
- Music/cultural sound documentation

Requirements:
- Directional microphones
- Concealed equipment
- No amplification in past
- No playback in past

#### 7.2.3 Environmental Sampling

Permitted (with approval):
- Air samples
- Water samples
- Soil samples (microscopic only)
- Atmospheric measurements

Requirements:
- Non-invasive collection
- Minimal volume (< 1 liter total)
- No visible collection activity
- No environmental impact

### 7.3 Emergency Intervention Protocols

#### 7.3.1 When Intervention is Permitted

Observer may break protocol only if:

1. **Imminent Personal Danger**
   - Life-threatening situation to traveler
   - Unavoidable exposure
   - Temporal equipment failure
   - Medical emergency

2. **Unavoidable Interaction**
   - Accidental physical contact
   - Direct questioning by historical individual
   - Witnessed by group

**Response:**
- Minimize interaction
- Use cover story (prepared)
- Extract as soon as safe
- Report to ERB immediately

#### 7.3.2 Forbidden Interventions

Observer must NOT intervene even if:

1. **Witnessing Suffering**
   - Historical violence (unless extreme exception)
   - Medical emergencies (historical individuals)
   - Injustice or discrimination
   - Natural disasters

**Exception:** Level 3 Permitted Intervention (see Section 3.1) with pre-approval only

2. **Witnessing Historical Events**
   - Assassinations
   - Accidents
   - Deaths
   - Disasters

**Rationale:** Observer knew event would occur; intervention would alter timeline

#### 7.3.3 Post-Intervention Protocol

If intervention occurs:

1. **Immediate Actions**
   - Document intervention completely
   - Assess timeline impact
   - Extract if compromised
   - Notify ERB emergency channel

2. **Reporting Requirements**
   - Full incident report within 24 hours
   - Timeline analysis
   - Witness statements
   - Equipment logs

3. **Remediation**
   - ERB review of timeline impact
   - Novikov consistency check
   - Possible timeline correction mission
   - Traveler suspension pending review

## 8. Ethical Review Board Standards

### 8.1 Board Composition

#### 8.1.1 Required Members

ERB must include:

1. **Philosopher (Ethics Specialist)**
   - Expertise in ethical theory
   - Understanding of temporal ethics
   - No conflicts of interest

2. **Historian**
   - Expertise in relevant historical period
   - Understanding of historical causality
   - Cultural sensitivity training

3. **Scientist (Temporal Physicist)**
   - Expertise in temporal mechanics
   - Novikov consistency validation
   - Timeline modeling capability

4. **Legal Expert (Temporal Law)**
   - Understanding of temporal regulations
   - Legal precedent knowledge
   - Rights framework expertise

5. **Public Interest Advocate**
   - Represents affected populations
   - Advocates for historical individuals
   - Community impact assessment

6. **Medical Ethicist** (when applicable)
   - For medical temporal operations
   - Understanding of medical ethics
   - Cross-temporal patient rights

#### 8.1.2 Board Independence

Requirements:
- No financial interest in outcome
- No personal relationship with applicant
- Rotating membership (5-year terms)
- Transparent decision-making
- Public disclosure of decisions (post-operation)

### 8.2 Review Process

#### 8.2.1 Application Requirements

Applicants must submit:

1. **Operation Details**
   - Target date/time/location
   - Duration of visit
   - Number of travelers
   - Equipment list

2. **Justification**
   - Scientific/humanitarian purpose
   - Alternative methods considered
   - Expected benefits
   - Knowledge gaps to be filled

3. **Risk Assessment**
   - Timeline impact analysis
   - Novikov consistency model
   - Interference level assessment
   - Contingency plans

4. **Traveler Qualifications**
   - Temporal training certification
   - Ethics certification
   - Relevant expertise
   - Past operation record

5. **Monitoring Plan**
   - Real-time tracking methods
   - Communication protocols
   - Emergency extraction plan
   - Post-operation verification

#### 8.2.2 Review Criteria

ERB evaluates based on:

1. **Scientific Merit** (30%)
   - Significance of research question
   - Adequacy of methodology
   - Uniqueness of temporal access
   - Contribution to knowledge

2. **Ethical Compliance** (30%)
   - Non-interference adherence
   - Rights protection
   - Harm prevention
   - Justice considerations

3. **Feasibility** (20%)
   - Technical capability
   - Traveler qualifications
   - Resource availability
   - Timeline stability

4. **Risk/Benefit Balance** (20%)
   - Timeline impact vs. benefit
   - Alternative methods
   - Proportionality
   - Reversibility

#### 8.2.3 Decision Types

ERB may issue:

1. **Approved (Unconditional)**
   - All criteria met
   - Minimal risk
   - Clear benefit
   - Strong protections

2. **Approved (Conditional)**
   - Additional requirements
   - Modified methodology
   - Enhanced monitoring
   - Limited scope

3. **Conditionally Approved (Pending)**
   - Resubmission required
   - Additional information needed
   - Methodology revision needed
   - Risk mitigation plan required

4. **Denied (with Appeal Rights)**
   - Insufficient justification
   - Unacceptable risk
   - Better alternatives exist
   - Ethical concerns

5. **Prohibited (Permanent)**
   - Violates core principles
   - Protected period/event
   - Unacceptable timeline risk
   - No appeal

#### 8.2.4 Appeal Process

Denied applicants may:

1. **Request Reconsideration**
   - Submit additional information
   - Address ERB concerns
   - Revise methodology
   - 30-day window

2. **Appeal to Higher Board**
   - Supreme Council review
   - Independent ethics panel
   - Public hearing option
   - 90-day process

### 8.3 Monitoring and Oversight

#### 8.3.1 Pre-Operation

Required activities:

1. **Traveler Briefing**

2. **Equipment Inspection**

Real-time monitoring:

2. **Vital Signs**
   - Health monitoring

Required activities:

## 9. Violation Consequences

### 9.1 Severity Levels

#### 9.1.1 Minor Violations

**Definition:**
- Unintentional protocol breach
- Minimal timeline impact
- No measurable interference
- Fully disclosed

**Examples:**
- Accidental brief conversation
- Visible equipment exposure (brief)
- Unauthorized photograph (non-sensitive)
- Minor timeline deviation (self-correcting)

**Consequences:**
- Written warning
- Mandatory ethics retraining (8 hours)
- Enhanced monitoring on next operation
- Incident report filed

#### 9.1.2 Moderate Violations

**Definition:**
- Intentional minor protocol breach
- Measurable but limited timeline impact
- Unapproved minor interaction
- Delayed disclosure

**Examples:**
- Extended conversation with historical individual
- Minor object left in past
- Unapproved environmental sampling
- Period restriction violation (minor)

**Consequences:**
- Temporary suspension (6-12 months)
- Mandatory ethics course (40 hours)
