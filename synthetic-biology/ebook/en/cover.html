WIA-SYNTHETIC-BIOLOGY v1.0
Standardizing Synthetic Biology Research, Education, and Applications with Ethical Responsibility

WIA-SYNTHETIC-BIOLOGY establishes a comprehensive framework for synthetic biology research, education, and commercial applications, encompassing CRISPR gene editing, BioBricks standardization, iGEM competition frameworks, and rigorous biosafety protocols. As synthetic biology advances from laboratory research to real-world applications—with CRISPR therapies approved by the FDA, Duke iGEM's 2025-2026 program addressing human health challenges, and the ATLAS microbiome project transforming sustainable mining—responsible governance has never been more critical.

This standard integrates cutting-edge 2025-2026 developments: MIT's 60x more precise CRISPR system, Stanford's CRISPR-GPT AI integration, the Cornell Microbe-Mineral Atlas Project for sustainable critical metal extraction, and Italy's 2025 biosafety framework. It emphasizes ethical considerations including biosafety levels (BSL-1 through BSL-4), biosecurity protocols, environmental release assessment, and equitable benefit-sharing.
