Executive Summary

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released the international standard WIA-AUTO-027 for universal fluid systems in modern mobility applications under the MIT License for free. This announcement positions South Korea as a leader in international standards for sustainable vehicle management.

🚨 Current Situation: Why This Standard Is Needed

Modern vehicles require 5-10 different fluids for cooling, lubrication, hydraulics, and more. This increases inventory complexity, causes environmental pollution, and results in annual maintenance costs of thousands of dollars. Additionally, different replacement cycles for each fluid create inefficiency and environmental burden.

[IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER_2: Problem Visualization Image]

Dr. Yeon Sam-heum, CEO:
"Vehicle fluid management should no longer be a complex and environmentally harmful process. We need a system where one universal fluid performs multiple functions, offers biodegradable options, and provides intelligent monitoring to indicate optimal replacement times. This is about sustainability and efficiency."

WIA's Solution: WIA-AUTO-027 Universal Fluid

WIA-AUTO-027 standardizes multi-purpose fluid systems for cooling, lubrication, and hydraulics, providing real-time fluid condition analysis and predictive maintenance. From biodegradable bio-based fluids to high-performance synthetic fluids, it offers optimized solutions for all vehicle types.

🏢 About WIA

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) is an international standards organization with a mission to provide equal opportunities to all humanity through technology and certification.

WIA is developing over 600 international standards in accessibility, security, AI, environment, healthcare, and more, releasing all standards as free open source.

🦀 Tech Stack: WIA's core APIs are developed in Rust. Rust is a systems programming language developed by Mozilla that uniquely achieves both memory safety and zero-cost abstraction.

👤 About the CEO
