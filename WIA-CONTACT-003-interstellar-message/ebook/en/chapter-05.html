Chapter 5: Visual and Symbolic Systems

Beyond Text: Encoding Spatial Information

While mathematics provides abstract foundations, conveying concrete information about our world, our biology, and our astronomical context requires visual and spatial representations. Yet we cannot assume that extraterrestrial recipients process visual information the way humans do—or at all.

This chapter explores how WIA-CONTACT-003 encodes images, diagrams, and spatial data in ways that transcend assumptions about sensory modalities, allowing any intelligence to extract meaning through pattern analysis, even without "seeing" in the human sense.

The Bitmap Message Structure

Prime Factorization Reveals Dimensions

The elegant solution pioneered by the Arecibo Message: encode a two-dimensional image as a one-dimensional bitstream whose total length is the product of two primes.

BITMAP ENCODING PRINCIPLE: ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ Total bits: N = p × q (where p, q are prime) │ │ │ │ Example: 1,679 bits = 23 × 73 │ │ │ │ Receiver attempts factorizations: │ │ 1,679 = 1 × 1,679? (invalid: produces line) │ │ 1,679 = 23 × 73? (valid: produces grid) │ │ │ │ Arrange bits in 23 rows, 73 columns: │ │ │ │ 0101010101... (row 1) │ │ 1010101010... (row 2) │ │ ... │ │ 0110011001... (row 23) │ │ │ │ Pattern emerges from spatial arrangement! │ └────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Multi-Level Gray scale Encoding

Beyond binary (0/1), multi-bit encoding allows grayscale or even color images:

Bits per Pixel Levels Usage Example
1 2 (binary) Simple diagrams 0=black, 1=white

Avoiding Visual Bias

Not Assuming "Vision"

Critical consideration: recipients may not perceive electromagnetic radiation in the visible spectrum, or at all. Visual representations must be interpretable through mathematical pattern analysis alone.

Design Principles for Non-Visual Interpretation:

  • Spatial correlation: Adjacent pixels should show correlation (objects have boundaries)
  • Symmetry: Many natural forms exhibit bilateral or radial symmetry
  • Repetition: Periodic structures signal artificial design
  • Hierarchical organization: Nested structures from atom → cell → organism
  • Contrast gradients: Smooth transitions indicate surfaces and shapes

Summary

Visual and symbolic systems extend the mathematical foundation of interstellar messages to convey concrete, specific information about our world, our biology, and our place in the cosmos. The WIA-CONTACT-003 standard specifies methods for encoding spatial data that:

  • Do not assume recipients have vision as humans experience it
  • Can be interpreted through pattern analysis and mathematics alone
  • Provide redundant encoding through multiple representation methods
  • Show hierarchical organization from atomic to astronomical scales
  • Include error detection mechanisms for integrity verification
  • Convey both static structures and dynamic processes

By combining mathematical foundations with visual representations, we create messages that are both abstract and concrete, universal and specific—messages that describe not just the principles of mathematics and physics, but the particular instantiation of those principles in our corner of the universe.

Essential Principle: The true power of visual encoding lies not in creating pretty pictures for alien eyes, but in embedding spatial and hierarchical relationships as patterns that can be discovered through mathematical analysis. A civilization that has never "seen" in the human sense can still extract every bit of information from our images by recognizing correlation, symmetry, and structure in the data.
