# WIA-PET-009 Pet Cloning Standard v1.0

**标准编号**: WIA-PET-009
**版本**: 1.0
**发布日期**: 2022-01-15
**状态**: Superseded by v2.0
**类别**: Biotechnology / Reproductive Technology
**哲学**: 弘益人間 (Benefit All Humanity)

## Summary

This is the initial release of the WIA-PET-009 Pet Cloning Standard, establishing foundational requirements for somatic cell nuclear transfer (SCNT) procedures in companion animals.

## Major Sections

1. **Scope**: Covers dogs and cats only (equines added in v1.2)
2. **Basic SCNT Protocol**: Core technical procedures
3. **Animal Welfare**: Minimum requirements for animal care
4. **Quality Standards**: Basic quality control measures
5. **Ethical Guidelines**: Fundamental ethical principles

## Key Features of v1.0

- Established basic SCNT methodology
- Defined minimum cell viability standards (>90%)
- Set animal welfare baseline requirements
- Required licensed veterinary oversight
- Established informed consent framework

## Limitations Addressed in Later Versions

- No cryopreservation protocols (added in v1.1)
- Limited quality control measures (enhanced in v1.2)
- No species-specific equine protocols (added in v1.2)
- Basic data security provisions (expanded in v2.0)
- Limited success rate tracking (improved in v1.2 and v2.0)

## Minimum Requirements (v1.0)

### Cell Quality
- Cell viability: >90% (increased to >95% in v2.0)
- DNA integrity: >0.85 (increased to >0.90 in v2.0)
- Contamination: <5% (tightened to <0.1% in v2.0)

### Success Targets
- Fusion rate: >70% (increased to >75% in v2.0)
- Blastocyst rate: Species-dependent, no specific targets
- Live birth rate: Not specified (added in v1.2)

### Animal Welfare
- Basic housing and care requirements
- Veterinary supervision required
- Pain management for procedures
- No specific limits on oocyte collections per year (added in v1.1)

### Documentation
- Basic record keeping required
- 5-year retention period (increased to 10 years in v2.0)

## Superseded By

**Version 1.1** (2023-06-01): Added cryopreservation protocols and enhanced quality measures
**Version 1.2** (2024-03-10): Added equine protocols and embryo grading system
**Version 2.0** (2025-01-15): Current version with comprehensive updates
