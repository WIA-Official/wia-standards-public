Critical Situation

Coral reefs are experiencing unprecedented decline. Scientists estimate that 70-90% of all coral reefs could disappear within the next 20 years if current trends continue. The time to act is now.

2.1 Climate Change Impacts

Climate change poses the greatest threat to coral reef ecosystems worldwide. Rising ocean temperatures, changing weather patterns, and sea level rise all contribute to reef degradation at an alarming rate.

Climate Factor Impact on Reefs Severity
Ocean Warming Coral bleaching, metabolic stress Critical
Ocean Acidification Reduced calcification, skeleton erosion Severe
Sea Level Rise Light reduction, sedimentation Moderate
Extreme Weather Physical destruction, storm damage High
Altered Currents Disrupted larval dispersal Moderate

2.2 Coral Bleaching

Coral bleaching occurs when stressed corals expel their symbiotic algae (zooxanthellae), which provide up to 90% of the coral's energy through photosynthesis. Without these algae, corals appear white or "bleached" and begin to starve.

2.3 Ocean Acidification

As oceans absorb atmospheric CO2, seawater becomes more acidic. This reduces the availability of carbonate ions that corals need to build their calcium carbonate skeletons.

The Chemistry of Acidification

Ocean pH has dropped from 8.2 to 8.1 since the Industrial Revolution—a 30% increase in acidity. By 2100, pH could fall to 7.8, making it extremely difficult for corals to calcify.

2.3.1 Acidification Effects

  • Reduced Growth: Corals grow 40-50% slower in acidified water
  • Weaker Structures: Skeletons become porous and fragile
  • Dissolution: Existing structures may begin to dissolve
  • Energy Drain: More energy spent on calcification, less on reproduction
  • Ecosystem Effects: Shell-forming organisms affected throughout food web

2.6 Compounding Effects

These stressors rarely act in isolation. A reef weakened by bleaching is more susceptible to disease. Nutrient pollution can accelerate algae growth that smothers recovering corals. Overfishing removes herbivores that control algae. This creates downward spirals that are difficult to reverse.

2.7 Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  • Climate change is the greatest threat to coral reefs
  • Bleaching occurs when corals expel symbiotic algae due to stress
  • Ocean acidification reduces coral calcification ability
  • Human activities add local stressors to global threats
  • Multiple stressors compound to create accelerating decline
