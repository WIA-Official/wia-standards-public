WIA Interplanetary Travel Standard

행성 간 여행 표준

WIA-SPACE-013 v1.0.0

弘益人間 · Benefit All Humanity

Interplanetary travel represents humanity's greatest ambition - to explore the planets, moons, asteroids, and beyond within our solar system. This standard covers everything from orbital mechanics and deep space propulsion to long-duration spaceflight, outer planet exploration, and the foundations of interstellar travel. From Mars to Saturn's moon Titan, and beyond to the edge of the solar system and the stars.

Chapter 1
Overview of Interplanetary Travel
Solar system structure, planetary characteristics, history of interplanetary exploration, and current and future missions.
Chapter 2
Interplanetary Orbital Mechanics
Hohmann transfer orbits, gravity assists, launch windows, orbital insertion, and the core principles of interplanetary navigation.
Chapter 3
Deep Space Propulsion Systems
Chemical rockets, ion propulsion, nuclear propulsion, solar sails, and revolutionary future propulsion technologies.
Chapter 4
Long-Duration Spaceflight
Space radiation, effects of microgravity on the human body, hibernation technology, and psychological challenges.
Chapter 5
Deep Space Communications
Deep Space Network, signal delays, relay communications, laser communications, and the necessity of autonomous systems.
Chapter 6
Outer Planet Exploration
Exploration of Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and their moons (Europa, Titan, Enceladus, etc.).
Chapter 7
Asteroid Belt and Kuiper Belt
Asteroid belt navigation, asteroid mining, Kuiper Belt objects, and exploration beyond Pluto.
Chapter 8
Interstellar Travel Concepts
Breakthrough Starshot, generation ships, relativistic travel, and envisioning humanity's distant future leaving the solar system.
