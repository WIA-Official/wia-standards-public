Chapter 07: Phase 4 — Integration with Existing Hydrology Stacks

7.1 Phase 4 in one paragraph

Phase 4 specifies how WIA Water Scarcity Response integrates with existing hydrology data systems: WMO drought indices, USGS National Water Information System (NWIS), EU WISE-WFD reporting, FAO AquaStat global water resources statistics, and the WIA family standards.

7.2 WMO drought index alignment

The standard\x27s index_kind tokens align with the WMO standardised method definitions for SPI, SPEI, PDSI, and their windowed variants. The alignment table is at spec/profiles/wmo-drought-indices.md.

7.3 USGS NWIS bridge

The bridge ingests USGS National Water Information System gauge data (streamflow, groundwater levels, reservoir storage) and emits WIA drought_index_observation envelopes plus supply_augmentation envelopes for storage releases. National jurisdictions outside the US use their own equivalent (Environment Canada Wateroffice, EU Copernicus Hydrology, Australia Bureau of Meteorology) via parallel bridges.

7.4 EU WISE-WFD bridge

The European Water Framework Directive (WFD) mandates standardised water-quality and water-quantity reporting from EU member states via the WISE platform. The bridge maps WIA envelopes to WISE-WFD reporting templates so EU jurisdictions can submit through the same pipe they already use for federation.

7.5 FAO AquaStat bridge

FAO AquaStat is the global water resources statistics dataset. The bridge contributes WIA-derived basin-level statistics to AquaStat\x27s annual update cycle, providing higher-resolution data than current AquaStat sources where WIA-conformant infrastructure exists.

7.6 WIA family integration

WIA-OMNI-API holds jurisdiction agency credentials, basin coordinator credentials, supplier accounts. WIA-AIR-SHIELD provides transport hardening for cross-jurisdiction federation. WIA-ACCESSIBILITY drives public-facing dashboard accommodations including low-bandwidth modes for rural community access.

7.7 Compliance mappings

UNECE Water Convention provides international cooperation framework that the standard\x27s federation primitives directly support. UN Sustainable Development Goal 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation) reporting can read from WIA aggregate data via the public audience.

Watershed-First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through: does this make the basin\x27s aggregate water-scarcity picture more visible to the people in the basin, or just more accessible to the bureaucratic centre? When the two conflict, basin-population visibility wins. That is why the standard mandates public read for aggregate basin data, refuses to centralise the data into a single coordinator, and requires every cross-jurisdiction read to be audit-logged so basin populations can verify what their water data is doing.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in water-scarcity infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, jurisdiction authority verification: writes to allocation/restriction/emergency endpoints require the writer to demonstrate authority for the jurisdiction (verified via WIA-OMNI-API jurisdictional_authority claims). Second, replay defence for emergency endpoints: a duplicated emergency declaration triggers duplicate response activations, so the seen-nonce cache MUST be persistent across host restarts. Third, public dashboard rate limits: drought-index streams attract dashboard traffic during dry seasons; hosts SHOULD provision read replicas to keep ingest paths responsive.
