Chapter 04: Phase 1 — Data Format

4.1 Phase 1 in one paragraph

Phase 1 specifies JSON envelopes for water scarcity coordination. Every envelope is signed with Ed25519 (IETF RFC 8032) and round-trips byte-identically through encode/decode.

4.3 Water allocation

Required fields: basin_id, jurisdiction_id, allocation_year, allocated_volume_m3, purpose, priority_class. Reserved purpose tokens: agricultural_irrigation, municipal_supply, industrial_use, environmental_flow, hydropower. Reserved priority_class tokens: senior_rights, junior_rights, supplemental, emergency_only.

4.4 Restriction declaration

Voluntary or mandatory water-use restriction. Reserved stage tokens: stage_1_advisory, stage_2_voluntary_conservation, stage_3_mandatory_restrictions, stage_4_emergency_rationing. The covered_uses array enumerates which uses are restricted (lawn_irrigation, vehicle_washing, decorative_fountains, etc.).

4.5 Emergency response

Formal emergency declaration with associated actions. Severity ladder: stage_1_warning, stage_2_alert, stage_3_critical, stage_4_catastrophic. Actions array enumerates concrete response measures (mandatory_rationing, supply_augmentation_review, interstate_compact_invocation, federal_disaster_declaration).

4.6 Supply augmentation

Desalination, water reuse, interbasin transfer, storage release records. Each carries volume, source, destination, energy cost (for desalination), and environmental impact assessment reference.

4.7 Public consultation

Record of public comment period and disposition for major allocation changes. Required fields: subject (allocation_id or restriction_id), comment_period_start, comment_period_end, total_comments, summary_disposition, full_record_url. The standard does not mandate a consultation requirement; provides the data shape for jurisdictions that do.

Watershed-First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through: does this make the basin\x27s aggregate water-scarcity picture more visible to the people in the basin, or just more accessible to the bureaucratic centre? When the two conflict, basin-population visibility wins. That is why the standard mandates public read for aggregate basin data, refuses to centralise the data into a single coordinator, and requires every cross-jurisdiction read to be audit-logged so basin populations can verify what their water data is doing.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in water-scarcity infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, jurisdiction authority verification: writes to allocation/restriction/emergency endpoints require the writer to demonstrate authority for the jurisdiction (verified via WIA-OMNI-API jurisdictional_authority claims). Second, replay defence for emergency endpoints: a duplicated emergency declaration triggers duplicate response activations, so the seen-nonce cache MUST be persistent across host restarts. Third, public dashboard rate limits: drought-index streams attract dashboard traffic during dry seasons; hosts SHOULD provision read replicas to keep ingest paths responsive.
