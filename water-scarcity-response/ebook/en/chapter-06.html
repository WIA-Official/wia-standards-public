Chapter 06: Phase 3 — Federation Protocol

6.1 Phase 3 in one paragraph

Phase 3 specifies how independent jurisdictions federate their water-scarcity data without merging databases, defend against replay, and enforce the audience-control boundary.

6.2 Roles

Jurisdiction agency, basin coordinator, supplier, regulator, research consortium, public. Trust is established by federation handshake and recorded in signed receipts.

6.3 Federation handshake

Reuses the WIA-SOCIAL Phase 3 §5 receipt shape. State machine: IDLE → PENDING (origin verifies signature) → ACCEPTED (origin issues receipt) → optional REVOKED. Receipts persist for at least 25 years per typical international water data retention norms.

6.4 Replay defence

Each signed envelope carries a 96-bit nonce + RFC 3339 timestamp. Receivers MUST reject envelopes with skew greater than ±300 seconds, reject envelopes whose (signer, nonce) tuple has been seen within the last 600 seconds, and maintain the seen-nonce cache for at least 600 seconds.

6.5 Audience controls

AudienceVisibility
publicAggregate basin statistics, drought index observations, restriction declarations
jurisdiction_partnerPer-allocation detail (individual rights holders), emergency response detail
basin_coordinatorFull basin-wide allocation + restriction + emergency data
regulatorFull data within regulatory jurisdiction
researchDe-identified data per local privacy rules (rights-holder identities anonymised)

6.6 Public consultation envelope

For jurisdictions that require public consultation on major allocation changes, the standard provides a public_consultation envelope (Phase 1 §4.7) that records the comment period and disposition. Federation peers can verify that consultation was conducted before honouring the resulting allocation change.

Watershed-First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through: does this make the basin\x27s aggregate water-scarcity picture more visible to the people in the basin, or just more accessible to the bureaucratic centre? When the two conflict, basin-population visibility wins. That is why the standard mandates public read for aggregate basin data, refuses to centralise the data into a single coordinator, and requires every cross-jurisdiction read to be audit-logged so basin populations can verify what their water data is doing.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in water-scarcity infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, jurisdiction authority verification: writes to allocation/restriction/emergency endpoints require the writer to demonstrate authority for the jurisdiction (verified via WIA-OMNI-API jurisdictional_authority claims). Second, replay defence for emergency endpoints: a duplicated emergency declaration triggers duplicate response activations, so the seen-nonce cache MUST be persistent across host restarts. Third, public dashboard rate limits: drought-index streams attract dashboard traffic during dry seasons; hosts SHOULD provision read replicas to keep ingest paths responsive.
