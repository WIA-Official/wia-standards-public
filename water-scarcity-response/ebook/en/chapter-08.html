Chapter 08: Conformance, Roadmap, Closing Notes

8.1 Conformance test suite

A black-box conformance test suite is published at https://github.com/WIA-Official/wia-water-scarcity-response-conformance and walks discovery, drought index publish/read/stream, allocation publish with jurisdictional authority verification, restriction with covered_uses validation, emergency declaration with severity ladder enforcement, federation handshake, audience-control matrix, rate-limit headers and exhaustion behaviour. Hosts publishing bridge_profile=Full SHOULD additionally pass the suite extension tests for at least one of USGS NWIS or EU WISE-WFD or FAO AquaStat bridges.

8.2 Reference container

The wia/water-scarcity-response-host:1.0.0 container image implements every Phase 2 endpoint with mock data. The container ships with built-in SPI computation per the WMO standardised method and a minimal jurisdiction-authority verification mechanism suitable for development and testing.

8.4 Backwards-compatibility promise

Within the 1.x line every Phase 2 endpoint MUST remain reachable and MUST continue to honour documented status codes and content shapes. Hosts MAY add optional query parameters, response fields, new endpoints, or media types. Hosts MUST NOT remove or repurpose existing ones. Breaking changes ride a major version bump and MUST be preceded by a 12-month deprecation window per IETF RFC 8594 and RFC 9745.

8.5 Closing note

Water scarcity is a watershed-level problem treated by jurisdictional silos. WIA Water Scarcity Response exists to close the gap with an open, federation-aware, audit-logged standard that lets jurisdictions, basin coordinators, regulators, and researchers share scarcity data on terms each can verify. The hope is that multi-year droughts stop catching basin populations off guard for lack of cross-border data flow.

Watershed-First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through: does this make the basin\x27s aggregate water-scarcity picture more visible to the people in the basin, or just more accessible to the bureaucratic centre? When the two conflict, basin-population visibility wins. That is why the standard mandates public read for aggregate basin data, refuses to centralise the data into a single coordinator, and requires every cross-jurisdiction read to be audit-logged so basin populations can verify what their water data is doing.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in water-scarcity infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, jurisdiction authority verification: writes to allocation/restriction/emergency endpoints require the writer to demonstrate authority for the jurisdiction (verified via WIA-OMNI-API jurisdictional_authority claims). Second, replay defence for emergency endpoints: a duplicated emergency declaration triggers duplicate response activations, so the seen-nonce cache MUST be persistent across host restarts. Third, public dashboard rate limits: drought-index streams attract dashboard traffic during dry seasons; hosts SHOULD provision read replicas to keep ingest paths responsive.
