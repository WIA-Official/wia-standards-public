Chapter 04: Phase 1 — Data Format

4.1 Phase 1 in one paragraph

Phase 1 specifies JSON envelopes for desalination plant operations. Every envelope is signed with Ed25519 (IETF RFC 8032) and round-trips byte-identically.

4.2 Plant operations envelope

{
  "wia_ene_052_version": "1.0.0",
  "type": "plant_operations",
  "plant_id": "did:wia:plant:carlsbad-swro-1",
  "captured_at": "2026-04-27T10:00:00Z",
  "production_m3_per_hour": 4500,
  "recovery_rate_pct": 45,
  "specific_energy_consumption_kwh_per_m3": 3.2,
  "membrane_status": "nominal",
  "pre_treatment_dp_kpa": 25,
  "signature": { "alg": "Ed25519", "value": "..." }
}

4.3 Energy consumption envelope

Required fields: plant_id, captured_at, kwh_consumed, source_mix (grid / solar / wind / cogeneration percentages), carbon_intensity_g_co2_per_kwh. The source_mix tracks what powered the desalination during the reporting interval.

4.4 Brine discharge envelope

Required fields: plant_id, captured_at, discharge_volume_m3, brine_salinity_ppt, brine_temperature_c, ambient_salinity_ppt, ambient_temperature_c, dispersion_zone_id, permit_limit_id. The permit_limit_id references the regulatory permit terms; downstream audit consumers can verify the discharge against those terms in real time.

4.5 Water quality envelope

Required fields: plant_id, captured_at, tds_mg_per_l, conductivity_us_per_cm, chloride_mg_per_l, hardness_mg_per_l_caco3, boron_mg_per_l, free_chlorine_mg_per_l, ph. Boron is particularly important for agricultural reuse — boron above 1.0 mg/L damages many crop species.

4.6 Maintenance record envelope

Membrane cleaning, replacement, pump overhaul, instrument calibration. Each carries date, performed_by, cost, downstream impact (production loss in m³ during downtime).

4.7 Environmental impact assessment envelope

Periodic compliance report tied to permit terms. Includes brine discharge cumulative summary, energy consumption summary, ecological impact monitoring data (if any), and the regulator\x27s acceptance receipt.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in desalination plant infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, brine discharge audit latency: environmental regulators historically receive monthly compliance reports; the standard's real-time brine_discharge stream collapses this latency to minutes, but plants MUST provision the regulator-feed endpoint with sufficient throughput to handle aggregated jurisdiction-wide subscriptions. Second, energy consumption volatility: SWRO plants modulate production within minutes for grid demand response; the energy_consumption envelope must be published frequently enough (typically 1-minute cadence) for grid operators to use the data for balancing. Third, membrane fouling cascade: pre-treatment failures cascade quickly into membrane fouling; the standard's plant_operations envelope MUST include pre_treatment_dp_kpa so downstream consumers can detect early warning of cascade failures.
