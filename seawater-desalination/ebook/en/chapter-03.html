Chapter 03: WIA-ENE-052 Standard Overview

3.1 Standard at a glance

WIA-ENE-052 is a four-phase open standard for seawater desalination plant operations. Phase 1 fixes JSON envelopes. Phase 2 fixes the HTTP surface. Phase 3 fixes federation and audience controls. Phase 4 bridges to ISO 50001/14001, IDA standards, IEC 60743, and the WIA family.

3.2 Object families

  • Plant operations — production volume, recovery rate, specific energy consumption, membrane status
  • Energy consumption — kWh per m³, source mix (grid / solar / wind / cogeneration), carbon intensity
  • Brine discharge — discharge volume, salinity, temperature, dispersion zone reference, permit limit reference
  • Water quality — produced water TDS, conductivity, chloride, hardness, boron, free chlorine residual
  • Maintenance record — membrane cleaning, replacement, pump overhaul, instrument calibration
  • Environmental impact assessment — periodic compliance report tied to permit terms

3.3 Roles in the federation

Phase 3 defines six roles: plant operator (signs operations envelopes), water utility (consumes operations + water quality streams), energy supplier (consumes energy consumption envelopes for grid balancing), environmental regulator (audits brine discharge against permit limits), EHS auditor (independent third-party safety auditor), research consortium (consumes de-identified operational data for membrane research).

3.5 Where it sits in the WIA family

WIA-ENE-052 composes with WIA-OMNI-API for credentials (operator identity, regulator credentials), WIA-AIR-SHIELD for transport hardening, and WIA Water Scarcity Response for basin-level supply augmentation tracking.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in desalination plant infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, brine discharge audit latency: environmental regulators historically receive monthly compliance reports; the standard's real-time brine_discharge stream collapses this latency to minutes, but plants MUST provision the regulator-feed endpoint with sufficient throughput to handle aggregated jurisdiction-wide subscriptions. Second, energy consumption volatility: SWRO plants modulate production within minutes for grid demand response; the energy_consumption envelope must be published frequently enough (typically 1-minute cadence) for grid operators to use the data for balancing. Third, membrane fouling cascade: pre-treatment failures cascade quickly into membrane fouling; the standard's plant_operations envelope MUST include pre_treatment_dp_kpa so downstream consumers can detect early warning of cascade failures.
