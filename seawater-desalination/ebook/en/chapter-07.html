Chapter 07: Phase 4 — Integration with Energy + Water Systems

7.1 Phase 4 in one paragraph

Phase 4 specifies how WIA-ENE-052 integrates with existing energy and water industry standards: ISO 50001 (energy management), ISO 14001 (environmental management), IDA (International Desalination Association) operational standards, IEC 60743 (electrical safety for water industry), and the WIA family.

7.2 ISO 50001 energy management bridge

ISO 50001 provides the energy management system framework. The bridge maps WIA energy_consumption envelopes to ISO 50001 energy performance indicators (EnPIs) so plant operators can satisfy ISO 50001 reporting requirements directly from WIA wire data.

7.3 ISO 14001 environmental management bridge

ISO 14001 provides the environmental management system framework. The bridge maps WIA brine_discharge and environmental_impact_assessment envelopes to ISO 14001 environmental aspects and impacts registers.

7.4 IDA standards integration

The International Desalination Association publishes operational standards for plant performance reporting. The bridge translates WIA plant_operations envelopes into IDA-format reports suitable for industry benchmarking.

7.5 IEC 60743 electrical safety bridge

IEC 60743 covers electrical safety for water industry equipment. The bridge surfaces WIA maintenance_record envelopes that include electrical safety verification (earth bonding tests, insulation resistance measurements) for IEC 60743 compliance audits.

7.6 WIA family integration

WIA-OMNI-API holds plant operator credentials, regulator credentials, EHS auditor credentials. WIA-AIR-SHIELD provides transport hardening. WIA Water Scarcity Response composes with WIA-ENE-052 — desalination plants are a supply augmentation source feeding into basin-level water scarcity response.

7.7 Energy grid integration

Modern grid operators want flexible loads to balance renewable energy variability. SWRO desalination is one of the most flexible large industrial loads (production can be modulated within minutes). The energy_consumption envelope\x27s source_mix and real-time demand fields support grid-operator integration for demand response participation.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in desalination plant infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, brine discharge audit latency: environmental regulators historically receive monthly compliance reports; the standard's real-time brine_discharge stream collapses this latency to minutes, but plants MUST provision the regulator-feed endpoint with sufficient throughput to handle aggregated jurisdiction-wide subscriptions. Second, energy consumption volatility: SWRO plants modulate production within minutes for grid demand response; the energy_consumption envelope must be published frequently enough (typically 1-minute cadence) for grid operators to use the data for balancing. Third, membrane fouling cascade: pre-treatment failures cascade quickly into membrane fouling; the standard's plant_operations envelope MUST include pre_treatment_dp_kpa so downstream consumers can detect early warning of cascade failures.
