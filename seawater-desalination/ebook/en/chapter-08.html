Chapter 08: Conformance, Brine Discharge, Roadmap

8.1 Conformance test suite

A black-box conformance test suite is published at https://github.com/WIA-Official/wia-ene-052-conformance and walks discovery, plant operations publish/stream, energy consumption query, brine discharge stream with regulator feed aggregation, water quality publish, maintenance records, federation handshake, audience-control matrix, rate-limit headers and exhaustion behaviour. Hosts publishing bridge_profile=Full SHOULD additionally pass the suite extension tests for ISO 50001 EnPI mapping, ISO 14001 environmental aspects mapping, and IDA report generation.

8.2 Reference container

The wia/seawater-desalination-host:1.0.0 container image implements every Phase 2 endpoint with mock data. The container ships with built-in SWRO operational simulation suitable for development and testing.

8.3 Brine discharge audit considerations

The standard\x27s brine_discharge envelope is designed to satisfy environmental-regulator audit requirements directly. The permit_limit_id field references the operational permit issued by the regulator; the envelope carries the parameters needed to verify discharge against those limits. Plant operators benefit from continuous regulatory transparency rather than periodic compliance audits; regulators benefit from real-time visibility rather than reactive enforcement.

8.5 Backwards-compatibility promise

Within the 1.x line every Phase 2 endpoint MUST remain reachable and MUST continue to honour documented status codes and content shapes. Hosts MAY add optional query parameters, response fields, new endpoints, or media types. Hosts MUST NOT remove or repurpose existing ones. Breaking changes ride a major version bump and MUST be preceded by a 12-month deprecation window per IETF RFC 8594 and RFC 9745.

8.6 Closing note

Seawater desalination has moved from luxury to necessity for many coastal regions. WIA-ENE-052 exists to make plant operations transparent to water utilities, energy suppliers, environmental regulators, and the public — without forcing any single party to be the central database. The hope is that desalination plants stop being opaque vendor systems and start being verifiable infrastructure that coastal populations can trust.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in desalination plant infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, brine discharge audit latency: environmental regulators historically receive monthly compliance reports; the standard's real-time brine_discharge stream collapses this latency to minutes, but plants MUST provision the regulator-feed endpoint with sufficient throughput to handle aggregated jurisdiction-wide subscriptions. Second, energy consumption volatility: SWRO plants modulate production within minutes for grid demand response; the energy_consumption envelope must be published frequently enough (typically 1-minute cadence) for grid operators to use the data for balancing. Third, membrane fouling cascade: pre-treatment failures cascade quickly into membrane fouling; the standard's plant_operations envelope MUST include pre_treatment_dp_kpa so downstream consumers can detect early warning of cascade failures.
