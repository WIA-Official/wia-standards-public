Chapter 06: Phase 3 — Federation Protocol

6.1 Phase 3 in one paragraph

Phase 3 specifies how desalination plant operators federate with water utilities, energy suppliers, environmental regulators, and research consortia.

6.2 Roles

Plant operator, water utility, energy supplier, environmental regulator, EHS auditor, research consortium. Trust established by federation handshake, recorded in signed receipts.

6.3 Federation handshake

Reuses WIA-SOCIAL Phase 3 §5 receipt shape. State machine: IDLE → PENDING (origin verifies signature) → ACCEPTED (origin issues receipt) → optional REVOKED. Receipts persist for at least 25 years per typical environmental compliance retention norms.

6.4 Replay defence

Each signed envelope carries 96-bit nonce + RFC 3339 timestamp. Receivers MUST reject envelopes with skew greater than ±300 seconds, reject envelopes whose (signer, nonce) tuple has been seen within the last 600 seconds, maintain seen-nonce cache for at least 600 seconds.

6.5 Audience controls

6.6 Real-time regulatory streaming

The brine_discharge stream is the primary mechanism for real-time regulatory compliance monitoring. Environmental regulators subscribe via the regulator-feed endpoint and receive a continuous flow of brine discharge envelopes from all plants in their jurisdiction. The regulator\x27s monitoring system can flag deviations from permit limits within minutes of occurrence rather than waiting for monthly compliance reports.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in desalination plant infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, brine discharge audit latency: environmental regulators historically receive monthly compliance reports; the standard's real-time brine_discharge stream collapses this latency to minutes, but plants MUST provision the regulator-feed endpoint with sufficient throughput to handle aggregated jurisdiction-wide subscriptions. Second, energy consumption volatility: SWRO plants modulate production within minutes for grid demand response; the energy_consumption envelope must be published frequently enough (typically 1-minute cadence) for grid operators to use the data for balancing. Third, membrane fouling cascade: pre-treatment failures cascade quickly into membrane fouling; the standard's plant_operations envelope MUST include pre_treatment_dp_kpa so downstream consumers can detect early warning of cascade failures.
