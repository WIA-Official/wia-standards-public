Chapter 05: Phase 2 — API Interface

5.1 Phase 2 in one paragraph

Phase 2 specifies the HTTPS surface for desalination plant data: operations publish/stream, energy consumption query, brine discharge regulatory stream, water quality publish, maintenance records.

5.2 Discovery

Every host publishes a discovery document at /.well-known/wia-ene-052 listing endpoints, supported plant technologies (SWRO/MED/MSF), regulatory jurisdictions, and rate limits.

5.3 Plant operations endpoints

POST /sd/operations              (signed; plant operator authority)
GET  /sd/operations?plant_id={id}&from={ts}&to={ts}
GET  /sd/operations/stream?plant_id={id}  (Server-Sent Events, typical 1-minute cadence)

5.4 Energy consumption endpoints

POST /sd/energy                  (signed)
GET  /sd/energy?plant_id={id}&from={ts}&to={ts}

5.5 Brine discharge endpoints

POST /sd/brine                   (signed)
GET  /sd/brine/stream?plant_id={id}     (Server-Sent Events, regulatory subscriber)
GET  /sd/brine/regulator-feed?jurisdiction={id}  (cross-plant aggregation for regulator)

5.6 Water quality endpoints

POST /sd/water-quality           (signed)
GET  /sd/water-quality?plant_id={id}

5.7 Maintenance endpoints

POST /sd/maintenance             (signed)
GET  /sd/maintenance?plant_id={id}&kind={k}

5.8 Authentication

Writes require HTTP Message Signatures per IETF RFC 9421. Plant operator authority, regulator authority, EHS auditor authority verified via WIA-OMNI-API role claims.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in desalination plant infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, brine discharge audit latency: environmental regulators historically receive monthly compliance reports; the standard's real-time brine_discharge stream collapses this latency to minutes, but plants MUST provision the regulator-feed endpoint with sufficient throughput to handle aggregated jurisdiction-wide subscriptions. Second, energy consumption volatility: SWRO plants modulate production within minutes for grid demand response; the energy_consumption envelope must be published frequently enough (typically 1-minute cadence) for grid operators to use the data for balancing. Third, membrane fouling cascade: pre-treatment failures cascade quickly into membrane fouling; the standard's plant_operations envelope MUST include pre_treatment_dp_kpa so downstream consumers can detect early warning of cascade failures.
