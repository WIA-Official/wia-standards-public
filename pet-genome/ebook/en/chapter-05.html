Chapter 5: Genetic Traits

"Genetics shapes not just health, but the unique characteristics that make each pet special."

5.1 Overview

Genetic trait analysis predicts physical characteristics, behavioral tendencies, and performance capabilities based on genetic markers. While health predictions focus on disease, trait analysis reveals the broader genetic blueprint that shapes each animal.

Trait Categories

  • Physical Traits - Coat, size, body structure
  • Behavioral Traits - Temperament, trainability, drives
  • Performance Traits - Athletic ability, working capacity
  • Sensory Traits - Vision, hearing, scent ability
  • Longevity Traits - Aging markers, lifespan predictors

Important Note on Behavioral Predictions

[i] BEHAVIORAL TRAIT DISCLAIMER

Behavioral traits are influenced by both genetics AND environment.
Genetic predictions indicate tendencies, not certainties.

Factors affecting actual behavior:
- Early socialization experiences
- Training quality and consistency
- Living environment
- Health status
- Individual variation

Genetic component typically: 30-70% depending on trait
Environmental component: 30-70% depending on trait

Use behavioral predictions as guidance for training approach,
not as absolute predictions of behavior.

Summary

Genetic trait analysis reveals the unique characteristics encoded in each pet's DNA. From coat color to behavioral tendencies, these insights help owners understand their pets better and provide appropriate care, training, and enrichment. While genetics provides the blueprint, environment and experience shape the final expression of these traits.
