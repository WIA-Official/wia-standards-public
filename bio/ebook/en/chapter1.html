Chapter 1: Introduction & Overview

1.1 The Biotechnology Data Challenge

Modern biotechnology generates massive amounts of heterogeneous data—from DNA sequences to CRISPR editing results, from protein structure predictions to synthetic biology part definitions. Yet, the lack of unified data standards hampers research reproducibility, cross-lab collaboration, and industrial-scale automation.

Core Principle: The WIA Biotechnology Data Standard enables seamless interoperability across gene editing, synthetic biology, and protein engineering workflows.

The Data Interoperability Crisis

Challenge Impact WIA Solution
Format Fragmentation Each lab uses custom data formats Unified JSON schemas with FASTA/SBOL compatibility
CRISPR Data Inconsistency gRNA designs not reusable across platforms Standardized CRISPR experiment schema
Protein Structure Silos AlphaFold data incompatible with lab systems PDB-compatible structure format
BioBrick Incompatibility Synthetic biology parts not shareable SBOL-compatible part definitions

1.2 Standard Scope and Goals

In Scope

  • Gene Sequences (유전자 서열): DNA, RNA, mRNA, protein sequences with annotations
  • CRISPR Experiments: gRNA (가이드 RNA) design, editing protocols, results tracking
  • Protein Structures (단백질 구조): AlphaFold predictions, PDB integration, confidence metrics
  • Synthetic Biology Parts: BioBrick definitions, Golden Gate assembly, MoClo systems
  • Experiment Metadata: Complete provenance and reproducibility tracking

Out of Scope (Phase 1)

  • DNA computing and data storage (Phase 2+)
  • Real-time biosensor streaming (Phase 3)
  • Biofoundry automation integration (Phase 4)

1.3 Design Principles

Five Pillars of Biotech Data Standards

  • Interoperability: Compatible with FASTA, FASTQ, SBOL, PDB, GenBank
  • Extensibility: Easy addition of new technologies and modalities
  • Self-Describing: JSON-based human-readable metadata
  • Traceability: Full experimental reproducibility tracking
  • Accessibility: Open standard with free reference implementations

1.5 WIA Philosophy: Hongik Ingan (弘益人間)

The WIA Biotechnology Data Standard embodies the Korean principle of Hongik Ingan — "Benefit All Humanity." By providing free, open standards for critical life sciences data, we enable:

  • Accelerated Research: Eliminate data conversion overhead
  • Global Collaboration: Seamless sharing across borders and institutions
  • Reproducibility: Complete experimental provenance tracking
  • Democratization: Small labs access the same standards as large pharma
  • Innovation: Build applications on standardized data layers
Open Standard Commitment: All WIA Biotech schemas, APIs, and SDKs are free forever under MIT license. Certification is optional for commercial products.
