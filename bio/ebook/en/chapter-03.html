Chapter 03

Genetic Engineering Protocols

Standardized procedures for genetic modification, gene editing technologies, and genomic research practices.

3.1 Overview of Genetic Engineering

Genetic engineering encompasses techniques for modifying the genetic material of organisms. The WIA-BIO standard provides comprehensive protocols for conducting genetic engineering work safely and effectively while ensuring scientific reproducibility and regulatory compliance.

Scope of Genetic Engineering Protocols

These protocols cover all aspects of genetic modification including recombinant DNA technology, gene editing using CRISPR-Cas systems, synthetic biology, and related techniques. Organizations must implement these protocols based on their specific activities and risk assessments.

Key Technology Categories

  • Recombinant DNA (rDNA): Traditional genetic engineering using restriction enzymes and ligation
  • Gene Editing: CRISPR-Cas9, TALENs, ZFNs, and base editing technologies
  • Synthetic Biology: Design and construction of new biological systems and pathways
  • Gene Therapy: Genetic modification of human cells for therapeutic purposes
  • Transgenic Technology: Creation of genetically modified organisms (GMOs)

3.2 Recombinant DNA Protocols

Recombinant DNA technology involves combining genetic material from different sources to create new genetic combinations. This foundational technology requires standardized protocols to ensure safety and reproducibility.

Standard rDNA Cloning Protocol
==============================

1. Vector Preparation
   ├── Select appropriate vector based on application
   ├── Digest with restriction enzyme(s)
   ├── Dephosphorylate if using single enzyme
   └── Purify linearized vector

2. Insert Preparation
   ├── Amplify target sequence by PCR
   ├── Add appropriate restriction sites
   ├── Digest with compatible enzymes
   └── Purify digested insert

3. Ligation
   ├── Calculate vector:insert molar ratio (1:3 to 1:5)
   ├── Set up ligation reaction
   │   - Vector: 50-100 ng
   │   - Insert: calculated amount
   │   - Ligase buffer: 1x
   │   - T4 DNA Ligase: 1 unit
   ├── Incubate 16°C overnight or 25°C for 1 hour
   └── Heat inactivate if necessary

4. Transformation
   ├── Thaw competent cells on ice
   ├── Add ligation mixture (1-5 μL)
   ├── Incubate on ice 30 minutes
   ├── Heat shock 42°C for 30-45 seconds
   ├── Add recovery medium
   ├── Incubate 37°C for 1 hour
   └── Plate on selective medium

Quality Control Checkpoints

Stage QC Method Acceptance Criteria
Vector Digestion Gel electrophoresis Single band at expected size
Insert Amplification Gel electrophoresis Correct size, no non-specific bands
Colony Screening Colony PCR Positive amplification of insert
Clone Verification Sequencing 100% match to expected sequence

3.3 CRISPR-Cas9 Gene Editing

CRISPR-Cas9 technology has revolutionized genetic engineering by enabling precise, efficient genome editing. The WIA-BIO standard establishes rigorous protocols for CRISPR applications to ensure safety and effectiveness.

CRISPR Applications

CRISPR technology can be applied for gene knockout, gene knockin, gene correction, epigenetic modification, and transcriptional regulation. Each application requires specific protocol adaptations and safety considerations.

Guide RNA Design Standards

1 Target Sequence Selection

Identify target sequences with the form 5'-N20-NGG-3' (for SpCas9). The 20-nucleotide guide sequence immediately upstream of the PAM site determines specificity.

2 Off-Target Analysis

Screen all potential guide sequences for off-target sites using validated algorithms. Reject guides with predicted off-targets in coding regions or regulatory elements.

3 Efficiency Prediction

Use validated prediction tools to estimate on-target efficiency. Select guides with predicted efficiency scores in the top quartile.

4 Synthesis and Validation

Synthesize selected guide RNAs and validate activity in vitro before cellular application. Use positive controls with known activity.

CRISPR-Cas9 Delivery Methods
============================

Method              | Efficiency | Duration | Applications
--------------------|------------|----------|---------------
Plasmid             | Moderate   | Extended | Stable expression
RNP Complex         | High       | Transient| Reduced off-target
mRNA + sgRNA        | High       | Transient| In vivo delivery
Lentiviral          | High       | Stable   | Pooled screens
AAV                 | Moderate   | Long-term| Gene therapy

RNP Complex Protocol:
1. Combine Cas9 protein (10 μg) with sgRNA (3 μg)
2. Incubate at room temperature for 10 minutes
3. Deliver to cells via electroporation or lipofection
4. Analyze editing 48-72 hours post-delivery
5. Perform off-target analysis on edited cells

3.4 Synthetic Biology Standards

Synthetic biology involves the design and construction of new biological parts, devices, and systems. The WIA-BIO standard provides guidelines for synthetic biology practices to ensure safety, reproducibility, and responsible innovation.

Design Principles

  • Modularity: Biological parts should be designed as modular components that can be combined in different configurations
  • Standardization: Parts should conform to standard assembly formats (BioBrick, Golden Gate, Gibson)
  • Characterization: All parts must be thoroughly characterized and documented
  • Orthogonality: Synthetic systems should minimize cross-talk with host systems
  • Predictability: System behavior should be predictable from component characteristics

3.5 Gene Therapy Protocols

Gene therapy involves the modification of human genetic material for therapeutic purposes. These applications require the highest standards of safety and quality due to their clinical implications.

Critical Safety Requirements

Gene therapy product development must comply with all applicable regulations including FDA CBER guidance, EMA guidelines, and ICH requirements. Products must undergo extensive preclinical safety testing before clinical application.

Gene Therapy Vector Production

Vector Type Capacity Integration Common Applications
Lentivirus ~8 kb Yes Ex vivo gene therapy, CAR-T
AAV ~4.7 kb Rare In vivo gene delivery
Adenovirus ~36 kb No Vaccines, cancer therapy
Non-viral Variable No mRNA delivery, transient expression

Vector Quality Requirements

  • Titer: Minimum specifications based on application requirements
  • Purity: Specified limits for host cell DNA, protein, and endotoxin
  • Identity: Confirmed by sequencing and restriction analysis
  • Potency: Functional assay demonstrating biological activity
  • Safety: Replication competence testing, adventitious agent testing

3.6 Transgenic Organism Development

Development of transgenic organisms requires careful planning, execution, and monitoring. The WIA-BIO standard provides comprehensive guidelines for transgenic work in plants, animals, and microorganisms.

Transgenic Organism Development Workflow
========================================

Phase 1: Design and Planning
├── Define phenotype objectives
├── Select host organism
├── Design genetic construct
├── Conduct risk assessment
├── Obtain regulatory approvals
└── Establish containment measures

Phase 2: Construct Development
├── Clone transgene cassette
├── Include selection markers
├── Add regulatory elements
├── Verify construct by sequencing
└── Prepare for transformation/transfection

Phase 3: Generation of Transgenic Lines
├── Transform/transfect host organism
├── Select transformants
├── Screen for integration
├── Analyze copy number
├── Confirm transgene expression
└── Establish stable lines

Phase 4: Characterization
├── Phenotype analysis
├── Molecular characterization
├── Integration site analysis
├── Expression stability over generations
├── Off-target effect assessment
└── Environmental impact evaluation

Phase 5: Regulatory and Safety
├── Compile regulatory dossier
├── Conduct contained field trials (if applicable)
├── Monitor for unintended effects
├── Maintain transgenic inventory
└── Implement containment protocols

3.7 Documentation and Traceability

All genetic engineering activities must be thoroughly documented to ensure traceability, reproducibility, and regulatory compliance.

Documentation Requirements

Every genetic engineering project must maintain complete documentation including construct maps, sequence files, laboratory notebooks, analytical results, and safety assessments. Electronic records must comply with data integrity requirements.

Required Records

  • Detailed construct maps with annotated features
  • Complete sequence files in standard formats (GenBank, FASTA)
  • Laboratory notebooks documenting all procedures
  • Analytical results (gel images, sequencing data, qPCR results)
  • Risk assessments and safety approvals
  • Inventory records for all modified organisms and materials
