Chapter 4

Power & Thermal Systems

Electrical and Thermal Control

⚡ ISS Power System

ISS consumes 84-120 kW continuously - equivalent to 40-50 homes. Solar power is the only practical energy source in orbit.

Power Statistics

  • Peak Generation: 120 kW
  • Average Usage: 84 kW
  • Solar Array Area: 2,500 m²
  • Number of Arrays: 8 arrays (4 pairs)

☀️ Solar Arrays

Solar Array Wings (SAW)

  • Size: 34m length × 12m width per wing
  • Total cells: 262,400 silicon cells across 8 wings
  • Cell efficiency: ~14-15%
  • Output voltage: 160V DC
  • Power per wing: up to 32.8 kW (when perpendicular to sun)

Sun Tracking

Arrays continuously rotate to track the sun using Beta Gimbal Assemblies:

  • Alpha Gimbal: Continuous rotation along truss axis
  • Beta Gimbal: ±180° rotation perpendicular to truss
  • Tracking accuracy: ±0.5°
  • Rotation rate: 4° per minute

ISS completes 16 orbits daily, experiencing 16 sunrises and sunsets. Arrays constantly adjust to maximize sunlight exposure.

iROSA Upgrade Arrays

New ISS Roll-Out Solar Arrays installed starting 2021:

  • Size: 19m × 6m (smaller than original)
  • Efficiency: ~30% (double original)
  • Installation: Partially cover existing arrays
  • Additional power: +20 kW per channel
  • Design life: 15 years
  • Roll-out design enables compact transport

⚡ Power Distribution

Distribution Architecture

  1. Primary Power (160V DC): Solar arrays → Sequential Shunt Unit (SSU)
    • SSU dissipates excess power as heat
    • Manages battery charging current
  2. Secondary Power (160V DC): Main Bus Switching Unit (MBSU)
    • Manages 8 power channels
    • Fault detection and automatic switching
  3. Tertiary Power (120V DC): DC-to-DC Converter Unit (DDCU)
    • Converts 160V to 120V
    • US segment standard voltage
  4. User Power: Power Distribution Units in each module
    • Converts to 28V, 120V as needed
    • Supplies individual equipment

Power Management

  • Total wiring: ~13 km
  • Power channels: 8 independent channels
  • Redundancy: Dual power sources for critical systems
  • Protection: Overcurrent, short circuit, overvoltage protection
  • Monitoring: Real-time power usage tracking

❄️ Thermal Control System

Space temperature extremes: +121°C (sunlit) to -157°C (shadow). ISS maintains internal 18-27°C.

Heat Sources

  • Electrical equipment: ~70 kW (largest source)
  • Solar radiation: Absorbed on sunlit surfaces
  • Human bodies: ~100W per person
  • Experimental equipment: Variable but significant

Active Thermal Control System (ATCS)

External ATCS (Ammonia-based)

  • Coolant: Anhydrous ammonia (NH₃)
  • Loops: 2 independent loops
  • Total ammonia: ~1,000 kg
  • Operating temp: -20°C to +20°C
  • Flow rate: ~258 L/min
  • Radiator area: ~1,200 m²

Heat Exchange Process

  1. Internal Loop (water-based): Absorbs heat from equipment, non-toxic water coolant, temp 4-60°C
  2. Heat Exchanger: Transfers heat between water and ammonia loops (physically separated for safety)
  3. External Loop (ammonia): Ammonia carries heat to radiators, radiates heat to space, cooled ammonia returns

Radiators

  • Total area: ~1,200 m² (three tennis courts)
  • 14 panels on P1, S1, P3, P4, S3, S4 truss segments
  • Panel size: ~1.7m × 10.7m each
  • Cooling capacity: up to 70 kW
  • Surface: Reflective silver Teflon coating
  • Rotatable to control sun exposure

Passive Thermal Control

  • Insulation: Multi-Layer Insulation (MLI) blankets
  • Surface coatings: Specialized paints (reflective or absorptive)
  • Heat pipes: Passive heat transfer devices
  • Insulated windows: Quadruple-pane glass minimizes heat loss
