Chapter 3

Life Support Systems

ECLSS and Environmental Control

💨 Sustaining Life in Space

Space is utterly inhospitable - no oxygen, extreme temperatures, dangerous radiation. The Environmental Control and Life Support System (ECLSS) creates and maintains a habitable environment inside the station.

ECLSS Core Functions

  • Atmosphere Management: Oxygen supply and CO₂ removal
  • Temperature Control: Maintain comfortable 18-27°C
  • Humidity Control: Regulate 30-70% humidity
  • Contaminant Removal: Filter harmful substances
  • Water Recovery: Recycle water from all sources

💧 Oxygen Generation

Electrolysis Oxygen Generators

ISS primary oxygen source splits water into oxygen and hydrogen using electricity:

2H₂O + electricity → 2H₂ + O₂

US Oxygen Generation System (OGS)

  • Production: 5-9 kg/day (enough for 6 crew)
  • Power: 1,000-1,500W
  • Location: Destiny module
  • Byproduct hydrogen sent to Sabatier Reactor

Russian Elektron System

  • Production: ~6 kg/day
  • Heritage from Mir station
  • Over 30 years operational experience
  • Location: Zvezda service module

Solid Fuel Oxygen Generators (SFOG)

  • Backup/emergency oxygen source
  • Burns sodium chlorate candles
  • Generates 600L oxygen per candle
  • Cannot be stopped once started
  • Reaction temperature: 450-500°C

🌬️ Carbon Dioxide Removal

Carbon Dioxide Removal Assembly (CDRA)

US system using zeolite molecular sieves to absorb CO₂.

  • Two beds alternate: one absorbing, one regenerating
  • Adsorption: 30 min | Desorption: 30 min | Cooling: 90 min
  • Removes CO₂ from crew of 6
  • Regenerates by vacuum and heating

Russian Vozdukh System

  • Uses wet amine scrubber
  • Chemically absorbs CO₂ into amine solution
  • Regenerates by heating to release CO₂
  • Over 30 years heritage

Sabatier Reactor

Recycles CO₂ by reacting with hydrogen to produce water and methane:

CO₂ + 4H₂ → CH₄ + 2H₂O + heat

  • Water recycled back to electrolysis
  • Methane vented to space
  • Recovers ~50% of water
  • Operating temperature: 400°C
  • Reduces resupply needs significantly

💦 Water Recovery System

Water costs ~$20,000/kg to launch, making recycling essential.

Water Sources (per crew member per day)

SourceAmount
Urine~1.5 L
Humidity condensate~2.0 L
Hygiene water~0.5 L
Sabatier byproduct~0.5 L

Urine Processor Assembly (UPA)

  • Vacuum distillation process
  • Recovery rate: 85-90%
  • Capacity: ~9 L/day
  • Pretreatment prevents crystallization
  • Concentrated brine disposed periodically

Water Processor Assembly (WPA)

Purifies recovered water to potable standards through:

  1. Multi-stage filtration (particles, biologicals)
  2. Catalytic reactor (oxidizes organics at high temp)
  3. Ion exchange (removes inorganic ions)
  4. Iodine addition (disinfection)
  5. Quality testing (conductivity, pH)

Overall water recovery: ~93%

Annual savings: ~2,700 kg water (~$54 million)

🌡️ Temperature and Humidity Control

  • Target temperature: 18.3-26.7°C (65-80°F)
  • Target humidity: 30-70% (ideally 40-60%)
  • Air circulation: 8-10 times per hour
  • HEPA filtration removes particulates

Humidity Removal

Condensation on cold surfaces captures moisture:

  • ~2L per crew member per day from respiration and perspiration
  • Condensate sent to WPA for recycling
  • Prevents equipment corrosion and mold growth

🧪 Trace Contaminant Control

Trace Contaminant Control System (TCCS)

  • Activated charcoal bed absorbs volatile organics
  • Catalytic oxidizer breaks down organics to CO₂ and H₂O
  • Operating temperature: ~400°C
  • Removes 200+ contaminants (methanol, benzene, toluene, freon, etc.)

🔄 Integrated Closed-Loop System

All systems work together in harmony:

  1. Electrolyze water → oxygen + hydrogen
  2. Crew breathes oxygen → exhales CO₂
  3. Remove CO₂ → react with hydrogen (Sabatier)
  4. Sabatier produces water → back to electrolysis
  5. Cycle repeats continuously

This closed-loop approach is essential for future Moon and Mars missions where resupply is impossible.
