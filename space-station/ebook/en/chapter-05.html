Chapter 5

Astronaut Life

Daily Routines in Space

🌅 A Day in Orbit

Astronauts experience 16 sunrises and sunsets daily as ISS orbits Earth every 90 minutes. To maintain circadian rhythms, crew follows GMT or Houston time with strict schedules.

Typical Daily Schedule

TimeActivity
06:00Wake up, morning routine
07:30Breakfast
08:00Daily conference (ground control)
08:30Work begins (experiments, maintenance)
13:00Lunch
14:00Work resumes
16:00Exercise (2 hours required)
19:30Dinner
20:00Free time (family calls, hobbies)
21:30Pre-sleep routine
22:00Sleep

Schedules vary with emergencies, spacecraft arrivals, spacewalks, and experiments.

😴 Sleep in Microgravity

Crew Quarters

  • Size: Phone booth (~2m × 1m × 1m)
  • Sleeping bag attached to wall
  • Arms float naturally at chest level
  • Continuous noise from fans and pumps
  • Window covers block sunlight
  • Personal items: laptop, photos, books
  • Individual air vent provides fresh air

Adaptation challenges: floating sensation, noise, confined space. Some crew use sleep aids initially.

🍽️ Food and Nutrition

ISS offers 200+ menu items - far beyond 1960s toothpaste-tube food.

Food Types

  • Rehydratable: Add water (soups, cereals, beverages)
  • Thermostabilized: Just heat (steak, chicken)
  • Intermediate moisture: Ready to eat (dried fruit)
  • Natural form: Nuts, cookies, energy bars
  • Fresh food: Occasional delivery (fruits, vegetables)

🌶️ Taste in Space

Microgravity shifts body fluids toward the head, creating nasal congestion. This dulls taste, making astronauts prefer spicy foods. Tabasco sauce and wasabi are popular condiments.

Mealtime

Meals are important social time. Crew gathers together, food packages attached to tray with Velcro. Astronauts anchor feet to wall or strap into chairs.

💪 Exercise - Critical for Health

Without exercise, astronauts lose 20% muscle mass and 1-2% bone density monthly. Daily exercise is mandatory.

Exercise Equipment

TVIS (Treadmill with Vibration Isolation)

  • Harness system applies 60-80% body weight load
  • Special vibration isolation prevents station shaking
  • Running and walking possible

CEVIS (Cycle Ergometer)

  • Stationary bicycle
  • Adjustable magnetic resistance
  • Monitors heart rate, power output, calories
  • Excellent cardiovascular exercise

ARED (Advanced Resistive Exercise Device)

  • Resistance training equipment
  • Simulates weights up to 270 kg
  • Enables squats, deadlifts, bench press
  • Maintains muscle strength and bone density

Required exercise: 2-2.5 hours daily, strictly enforced.

🚿 Hygiene

Washing

No showers - water droplets would float and damage equipment.

  • No-rinse shampoo: Special formulation
  • Wet towels: For body cleaning
  • Toothpaste: Edible type (no spitting possible)
  • Shaving: Electric razor (cream vacuumed away)

Space Toilet

Sophisticated system using airflow suction (no gravity).

  • Urine: Funnel and hose, vacuum suction
  • Feces: Small opening (~10cm), airflow suction
  • Restraints: Foot restraints and thigh straps
  • Processing: Urine recycled, feces sealed and stored
  • Odor: Activated carbon filters

Universal Waste Management System (UWMS) installed 2020 is 65% smaller and lighter, with improved design for female astronauts.

👕 Clothing

No laundry facilities - clothes worn then discarded.

  • Underwear: Changed every 2 days
  • Shirts/pants: Changed every 10 days
  • Socks: Weekly change
  • Exercise clothes: Weekly change

Dirty clothes loaded into cargo ship and burned during atmospheric reentry.

📞 Communication and Leisure

Staying Connected

Regular family contact via internet, email, video calls, social media (slow connection speed).

Leisure Activities

  • Earth viewing: Most popular - watching Earth from Cupola
  • Photography/video: Many astronauts photograph Earth
  • Movies: Watching films and TV series on laptops
  • Reading: E-books or physical books
  • Music: Playing instruments (guitar, keyboard)
  • Games: Chess, cards, video games

🧠 Mental Health

Long-term confinement challenges mental health. Space agencies prioritize psychological wellbeing.

💚 Psychological Support

  • Regular counseling: Private sessions with psychologists
  • Team bonding: Shared meals, movie nights
  • Personal space: Private crew quarters
  • Special events: Birthday and holiday celebrations
  • Surprise packages: Messages and gifts from family

🌍 Missing Earth

Astronauts miss simple things: rain sounds, wind, natural smells, unrestricted movement, fresh food. Most say the experience makes hardships worthwhile.
