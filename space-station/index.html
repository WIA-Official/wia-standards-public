🌌 About Space Stations

Space stations are orbiting laboratories where astronauts live and work for extended periods. They serve as platforms for scientific research, technology development, and international cooperation. From the early Salyut and Skylab to today's International Space Station, these orbital outposts have advanced our understanding of space and paved the way for future exploration of the Moon, Mars, and beyond.
