Chapter 7: Regulation and Approval

[Table of Contents]

Regulatory approval ensures safety and effectiveness. Pathways vary by
jurisdiction and device risk classification.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 FDA Approval Process (United States)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Device Classification:
  Class I: Low risk, general controls (mostly 510(k) exempt)
  Class II: Moderate risk, general + special controls → 510(k) pathway
  Class III: High risk, premarket approval (PMA) → most rigorous

510(k) Premarket Notification:
  - Requirement: Demonstrate substantial equivalence to predicate device
  - Contents: Device description, predicate comparison, performance testing,
    biocompatibility (ISO 10993), software documentation, labeling
  - Clinical: Generally not required if equivalence established
  - Timeline: 90 days FDA goal (actual 4-12 months)
  - Smart pill example: New pH sensor capsule vs. SmartPill predicate

De Novo Pathway:
  - Scenario: No predicate exists but Class II appropriate
  - Process: 510(k) → NSE (Not Substantially Equivalent) → De Novo request →
    FDA reclassifies to Class II → approval (becomes future predicate)
  - Example: Proteus sensor (first ingestible adherence sensor)
  - Timeline: 150 days FDA goal

PMA (Premarket Approval):
  - Standard: "Reasonable assurance" of safety and effectiveness
  - Evidence: Scientific data (primarily clinical trials)
  - Contents: Comprehensive summary, device description, manufacturing/QC,
    nonclinical testing, clinical trials (pivotal), benefit-risk analysis, labeling
  - Clinical: Usually mandatory (prospective, controlled, adequate sample size)
  - Review: 180 days goal (actual 1-3 years), FDA multidisciplinary team,
    advisory panel (for novel/controversial)
  - Example: PillCam capsule endoscopy (2001 EU, 2007 US FDA)

Post-Approval:
  - Conditions: Approved labeling only, approved manufacturing
  - Surveillance: MDR (Medical Device Reporting) for adverse events
  - Changes: PMA supplement or 30-day notice
  - Inspections: Periodic manufacturing facility audits

7.2 Clinical Trial Requirements
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDE (Investigational Device Exemption):
  - Purpose: Permit unapproved device use in clinical investigation
  - NSR (Non-Significant Risk): IRB only (FDA exempted)
  - SR (Significant Risk): IRB + FDA IDE approval
  - Application: Protocol, risk analysis, device description, preclinical data,
    investigator qualifications, informed consent, monitoring plan
  - Review: 30 days (silence = approval), clinical hold, or disapproval

Trial Design:
  Phase I analog: Initial safety/feasibility (10-30 subjects, open-label)
  Phase II analog: Pilot efficacy (50-100 subjects, single-arm or small randomized)
  Phase III analog: Pivotal efficacy (100-500+ subjects, randomized, controlled,
    multicenter) - basis for PMA approval
  Phase IV analog: Post-market (hundreds-thousands, real-world performance)

Endpoints:
  - Primary: Pre-specified main efficacy measure (single, objective, clinically meaningful)
  - Secondary: Additional benefits, safety
  - Safety: AEs (adverse events), SAEs (serious: death, life-threatening, hospitalization,
    permanent impairment), device-specific (obstruction, perforation, allergy)

Statistical Considerations:
  - Sample size: Based on effect size, α = 0.05, power = 0.80-0.90, dropout
  - Populations: ITT (intention-to-treat), PP (per-protocol), safety
  - Hypothesis: Superiority, non-inferiority, or equivalence
  - Interim analysis: DMB (Data Monitoring Board), alpha-spending methods

Patient Population:
  - Inclusion: Diagnosis confirmed, appropriate age, informed consent
  - Exclusion: Contraindications (strictures, pregnancy), severe comorbidities
  - Informed consent: Purpose, procedures, risks/benefits, alternatives, confidentiality

7.3 International Regulatory Standards
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

European Union (MDR 2017/745):
  - Classification: Annex VIII rules → typically Class IIa or IIb
  - Conformity assessment: Notified Body (NB) review, technical documentation,
    clinical evaluation (MEDDEV 2.7/1), quality system audit
  - CE marking: Indicates EU compliance, valid 5 years
  - Clinical investigation: ISO 14155, Competent Authority + Ethics Committee (60 days)
  - PMCF (Post-Market Clinical Follow-up): Mandatory for all devices, updates CER
  - UDI: Unique Device Identification in EUDAMED database

Japan (PMDA):
  - Classification: Class I-IV (most smart pills Class II-III)
  - Approval: PMDA review (6-12 months) → MHLW (Ministry) final approval
  - Clinical: Generally required (Class III/IV), Japanese data preferred (bridging possible)
  - Sakigake: Fast-track designation for breakthrough devices (6-month review)
  - Post-approval: PMS (post-market surveillance), re-examination (3-7 years)

China (NMPA):
  - Classification: Class I-III (most smart pills Class III)
  - Approval pathway: CIR registration → clinical trial approval → trial (China) →
    technical review (CMDE) → administrative review → NMPA approval (2-5 years)
  - Clinical: Class III mandatory, Chinese subjects required
  - Innovation: Special review for breakthrough devices (priority/expedited)

Other Markets:
  - Canada (Health Canada): Class I-IV, Medical Device License (MDL)
  - Australia (TGA): ARTG registration, EU CE recognition (mutual)
  - Korea (MFDS): Grade 1-4, clinical data (Korean) for 3-4
  - Brazil (ANVISA), India (CDSCO): Various national pathways

Harmonization:
  - IMDRF (International Medical Device Regulators Forum): Risk classification,
    clinical evaluation, UDI, cybersecurity guidance
  - ISO 13485: Quality management system (global recognition)
  - MDSAP (Medical Device Single Audit Program): Single audit for multiple countries

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.4 Post-Market Surveillance
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Adverse Event Reporting:
  US MDR (21 CFR 803):
    - Manufacturers: Death (30d + 5d), serious injury (30d), malfunction (30d), baseline annual
    - User facilities: Death (FDA + manufacturer, 10d), serious (manufacturer, 10d)
    - Submission: eSTAR system

  EU Vigilance (MDR Article 87-92):
    - Serious incidents: Report to Competent Authority (2-10 days based on severity)
    - FSCA (Field Safety Corrective Action): Recalls, field corrections, warnings
    - EUDAMED database (future public access)

Post-Market Studies:
  US Section 522: FDA-mandated PMA condition (long-term safety/efficacy, rare AEs,
    specific subgroups, real-world performance)
  EU PMCF: Plan (rationale, methods, data sources), active (studies) or passive
    (literature, vigilance), updates CER

Registries:
  - Purpose: Real-world evidence, long-term outcomes, comparative effectiveness
  - Examples: Device-specific (e.g., PillCam registry), disease-specific (IBD registry),
    national (MAUDE-US, EUDAMED-EU, J-MDRS-Japan)

Manufacturing Quality:
  QSR (21 CFR 820 - US): Design controls, purchasing, production validation, inspection,
    CAPA (corrective/preventive action), records
  ISO 13485: International QMS standard, third-party certification
  GMP: EU, China, other regions (similar to QSR/ISO 13485)
  Inspections: Routine (2-4 years), for-cause (complaints, recalls), FDA 483 observations

Key Principle: Regulatory requirements evolve continuously. Stay updated with
latest guidance and collaborate with regulatory experts.
