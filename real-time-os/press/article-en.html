Executive Summary

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) has released WIA-COMP-019, a comprehensive real-time operating system framework covering task scheduling, inter-process communication, resource management, timing analysis, and deterministic behavior guarantees, as free and open source under MIT License. This announcement positions the Republic of Korea as a leader in safety-critical systems international standards.

🚨 Current Situation: Why This Standard Is Needed

Real-time systems in safety-critical domains like aerospace, medical, and automotive require microsecond-level precision. Missing deadlines isn't just performance degradation—it can lead to life-threatening accidents. Existing RTOS solutions lack standardization, with vendors using different APIs and scheduling policies, and issues like priority inversion, race conditions, and inadequate timing analysis leave system reliability unguaranteed.

WIA's Solution: WIA-COMP-019 Real-Time OS

WIA-COMP-019 standardizes every aspect of real-time OS from scheduling algorithms like Rate Monotonic (RM) and Earliest Deadline First (EDF) to IPC mechanisms including semaphores, mutexes, queues, and events, priority inheritance protocols, WCET analysis, SMP/AMP multi-core support, and safety certifications including MISRA-C, DO-178C, and IEC 61508.

🏢 About WIA

World Certification Industry Association (WIA) is an international standards organization dedicated to providing equal opportunities for all humanity through technology and certification.

WIA develops over 600 international standards across accessibility, security, AI, environment, healthcare, and more—all released as free and open source.

🦀 Tech Stack: WIA's core APIs are built in Rust—the systems programming language that achieves both memory safety and zero-cost abstractions.
