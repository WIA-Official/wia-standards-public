# WIA-OCEAN_CONSERVATION: Phase 3 - Protocol Specification
## Version 1.0 | 弘익人間 (Benefit All Humanity)

---

## 1. Overview

This specification defines the operational protocols, monitoring procedures, and intervention guidelines for ocean conservation activities under the WIA-OCEAN_CONSERVATION standard. These protocols ensure consistent, effective, and scientifically-sound conservation practices across organizations and jurisdictions.

**Philosophy**: 弘益人間 - Systematic protection of marine ecosystems benefits all humanity.

## 2. Species Monitoring Protocols

### 2.1 Marine Species Survey Protocol

#### 2.1.1 Pre-Survey Requirements
- **Permits**: Obtain required research permits from relevant authorities
- **Equipment**: Calibrate all monitoring equipment within 30 days of survey
- **Team**: Minimum 2 trained observers for visual surveys
- **Safety**: Complete marine safety briefing and emergency procedures
- **Weather**: Survey only in conditions with visibility >10m and sea state <3

#### 2.1.2 Survey Execution
1. **Transect Planning**
   - Define survey area using stratified random sampling
   - Establish transect lines with minimum 500m spacing
   - Record GPS waypoints for all transect endpoints
   - Document environmental conditions at start

2. **Data Collection**
   - Record all species observations using standardized forms
   - Photograph or video each encounter when possible
   - Note behavior, group size, age classes
   - Log environmental parameters every 15 minutes
   - Use double-observer protocol for quality assurance

3. **Quality Control**
   - Cross-verify species identification with field guides
   - Validate GPS coordinates against known landmarks
   - Check data entry for completeness within 24 hours
   - Flag uncertain identifications for expert review

#### 2.1.3 Post-Survey Procedures
- Submit data to central repository within 7 days
- Archive raw data (photos, videos, field notes) for 10 years
- Generate preliminary analysis report within 30 days
- Share findings with relevant conservation authorities

### 2.2 Acoustic Monitoring Protocol

#### 2.2.1 Deployment
- Deploy acoustic recorders at predetermined locations
- Depth range: 10-100m depending on target species
- Recording schedule: Continuous or duty-cycled (10 min/hour)
- Battery life: Minimum 90 days
- Data storage: Minimum 256GB capacity

#### 2.2.2 Data Processing
- Download data every 90-120 days
- Apply automated detection algorithms (min 85% accuracy)
- Manual validation of 10% of detections
- Generate species presence/absence matrices
- Calculate acoustic diversity indices

### 2.3 Satellite Tracking Protocol

#### 2.3.1 Tag Deployment
- Use appropriate tag type for species (ARGOS, GPS, acoustic)
- Minimum tracking duration: 180 days
- Attachment method: Species-specific protocols
- Post-deployment monitoring: 24 hours observation
- Data transmission: Hourly to daily intervals

#### 2.3.2 Movement Analysis
- Filter location data for accuracy (Argos LC 2+ or GPS)
- Calculate home range, migration routes, critical habitats
- Identify marine corridors and connectivity
- Assess MPA effectiveness for target species

## 3. Ecosystem Health Monitoring Protocols

### 3.1 Coral Reef Assessment Protocol

#### 3.1.1 Site Selection
- Stratified random sampling within reef zones
- Minimum 3 sites per reef area
- Depth range: 5-15m (shallow) and 15-30m (deep)
- Site marking: Permanent markers or GPS coordinates

#### 3.1.2 Benthic Survey Method
- **Line Intercept Transect (LIT)**
  - 3 x 50m transects per site
  - Record substrate type every 50cm
  - Categories: Live coral, dead coral, algae, other
  - Calculate percentage cover for each category

- **Photo Quadrat Method**
  - 50 photo quadrats (0.5m x 0.5m) per transect
  - Randomly spaced along transect
  - Analyze using CoralNet or CPCe software
  - Minimum 100 random points per image

#### 3.1.3 Coral Health Indicators
- **Bleaching Assessment**
  - Classify corals: 0 (no bleaching) to 6 (dead)
  - Record bleaching intensity and extent
  - Photograph representative colonies
  - Resurvey every 2 weeks during bleaching events

- **Disease Monitoring**
  - Identify disease types (white syndrome, black band, etc.)
  - Measure affected area on each colony
  - Tag diseased colonies for monitoring
  - Report outbreaks to regional networks

- **Recruitment Assessment**
  - Count juvenile corals (<5cm diameter)
  - Identify to genus when possible
  - Calculate recruitment rate per m²
  - Annual monitoring recommended

#### 3.1.4 Water Quality Sampling
- **Temperature**: Continuous logging (hourly intervals)
- **pH and Alkalinity**: Weekly sampling
- **Nutrients**: Monthly sampling (nitrate, phosphate, ammonia)
- **Turbidity**: In-situ measurement during surveys
- **Dissolved Oxygen**: Profile measurements (surface to 30m)

### 3.2 Ocean Acidification Monitoring Protocol

#### 3.2.1 Sampling Strategy
- Fixed monitoring stations in key regions
- Depth profiles: Surface, 10m, 25m, 50m, 100m
- Sampling frequency: Monthly minimum
- Storm event sampling: Within 48 hours of major events

#### 3.2.2 Parameters and Methods
- **pH**: Spectrophotometric method (±0.003 precision)
- **Total Alkalinity**: Potentiometric titration
- **Dissolved Inorganic Carbon**: Coulometric method
- **pCO2**: Calculated from pH and alkalinity
- **Saturation States**: Calculate for aragonite and calcite

#### 3.2.3 Quality Assurance
- Use certified reference materials (CRMs) every 20 samples
- Duplicate samples: 10% of total
- Instrument calibration: Daily before use
- Participate in international proficiency testing

## 4. Marine Protected Area Management Protocols

### 4.1 MPA Surveillance Protocol

#### 4.1.1 Patrol Planning
- Risk-based patrol scheduling
- Coverage: Minimum 70% of MPA area monthly
- Patrol types: Vessel, aerial, shore-based
- Timing: Variable to avoid predictability
- Joint patrols: Coordinate with enforcement agencies

#### 4.1.2 Patrol Execution
- Record patrol route with GPS tracking
- Document all vessels encountered (name, flag, activity)
- Photograph violations and collect evidence
- Report violations within 2 hours
- Complete patrol log within 24 hours

#### 4.1.3 Technology Integration
- AIS monitoring: Real-time vessel tracking
- Radar surveillance: 24/7 coverage
- Satellite imagery: Weekly review
- Drone surveillance: High-risk areas
- Acoustic sensors: Detect vessel engines

### 4.2 MPA Effectiveness Evaluation

#### 4.2.1 Biological Indicators
- **Fish Biomass**: Underwater visual census, quarterly
- **Species Richness**: Complete species inventory, annually
- **Trophic Structure**: Assess predator/prey ratios
- **Size Structure**: Measure size distribution of key species
- **Recovery Rate**: Compare to baseline and control sites

#### 4.2.2 Socioeconomic Indicators
- **Compliance Rate**: Violations per patrol hour
- **Community Support**: Annual surveys
- **Alternative Livelihoods**: Participation rates
- **Tourism Revenue**: Economic impact assessment
- **Fishing Effort**: Reduction in adjacent areas

#### 4.2.3 Reporting
- Quarterly status reports to management authority
- Annual comprehensive assessment
- 5-year management plan review
- Public reporting through online dashboard

## 5. Illegal Fishing Detection Protocol

### 5.1 AIS Monitoring Protocol

#### 5.1.1 Suspicious Pattern Detection
- **AIS Gaps**: >4 hours within EEZ or MPA
- **Speed Anomalies**: Sudden changes >5 knots
- **Location Spoofing**: Position inconsistent with other data
- **Rendezvous**: Proximity to known transshipment vessels
- **Night Operations**: Unexplained nighttime activity in MPAs

#### 5.1.2 Alert Triggers
- MPA boundary crossing by unauthorized vessel
- Loitering within 5km of MPA boundary
- AIS turned off within monitored area
- Vessel type inconsistent with activity
- Blacklisted vessel detection

#### 5.1.3 Response Procedure
1. Verify alert using secondary data sources
2. Assess threat level (Low/Medium/High/Critical)
3. Notify relevant enforcement authority within 1 hour
4. Track vessel until enforcement response
5. Document outcome and update database

### 5.2 Satellite Monitoring Protocol

#### 5.2.1 Image Analysis
- Daily satellite imagery review of priority areas
- Automated vessel detection using machine learning
- Manual verification of high-confidence detections
- Cross-reference with AIS data
- Identify dark vessels (no AIS signal)

#### 5.2.2 Vessel Identification
- Estimate vessel length from imagery
- Classify vessel type (fishing, cargo, tanker)
- Detect fishing gear (longlines, purse seines)
- Track vessel course and speed
- Generate alert for enforcement

## 6. Pollution Response Protocols

### 6.1 Oil Spill Response Protocol

#### 6.1.1 Initial Response (0-24 hours)
- Activate response team within 2 hours
- Assess spill extent and drift trajectory
- Deploy containment booms if feasible
- Notify wildlife rescue teams
- Establish command center

#### 6.1.2 Active Response (1-14 days)
- Deploy skimmers and recovery vessels
- Apply dispersants if conditions appropriate
- Conduct wildlife rescue operations
- Monitor shoreline impact
- Daily situation reports

#### 6.1.3 Post-Response (14+ days)
- Shoreline cleanup operations
- Wildlife rehabilitation
- Environmental damage assessment
- Long-term monitoring plan
- After-action review

### 6.2 Marine Debris Cleanup Protocol

#### 6.2.1 Survey and Planning
- Identify debris accumulation zones
- Assess debris type and quantity
- Plan cleanup logistics (vessels, equipment, disposal)
- Recruit volunteers if applicable
- Obtain necessary permits

#### 6.2.2 Cleanup Operations
- Collect debris using appropriate methods
- Segregate recyclable materials
- Document debris types and sources
- Monitor wildlife interactions
- Proper disposal or recycling

#### 6.2.3 Data Collection
- Record debris composition (plastic types, metals, etc.)
- Estimate quantities by weight and volume
- Photograph representative items
- Track GPS locations of major accumulations
- Submit data to global databases (e.g., Ocean Conservancy)

## 7. Conservation Intervention Protocols

### 7.1 Coral Restoration Protocol

#### 7.1.1 Coral Nursery Establishment
- Site selection: Protected area with good water quality
- Structure type: Floating, mid-water, or substrate-based
- Source colonies: Healthy, disease-free, multiple genotypes
- Fragmentation: 5-10cm fragments, 6-8 fragments per colony
- Attachment: Epoxy or cable ties
- Monitoring: Weekly growth measurements

#### 7.1.2 Outplanting
- Site preparation: Remove algae and debris
- Optimal timing: Low wave energy season
- Spacing: Minimum 20cm between colonies
- Attachment: Epoxy to substrate
- Survival monitoring: Monthly for first year
- Success criteria: >70% survival at 12 months

### 7.2 Species Rescue and Rehabilitation

#### 7.2.1 Marine Mammal Stranding Response
- Assess animal condition: Alive or deceased
- Contact authorized response network immediately
- Secure area and control public access
- Keep animal wet and cool if alive
- Do not attempt to return to water without expert guidance
- Document with photos and GPS coordinates

#### 7.2.2 Sea Turtle Rehabilitation
- Initial assessment: Injuries, body condition, hydration
- Stabilization: Fluids, antibiotics if needed
- Diagnostic testing: Bloodwork, radiographs
- Treatment plan: Surgery, medications, supportive care
- Recovery period: Weeks to months
- Release criteria: Healthy weight, active, normal blood values

## 8. Data Management and Reporting

### 8.1 Data Quality Protocols

#### 8.1.1 Quality Control Checks
- Completeness: All required fields populated
- Accuracy: Values within expected ranges
- Consistency: Cross-field validation
- Timeliness: Data entered within required timeframes
- Traceability: Clear audit trail of all changes

#### 8.1.2 Data Verification
- 10% random sample reviewed by supervisor
- Expert validation for uncertain species IDs
- Statistical outlier detection and investigation
- Peer review for critical datasets
- Version control for all data files

### 8.2 Reporting Requirements

#### 8.2.1 Routine Reports
- **Daily**: Patrol logs, incident reports
- **Weekly**: Surveillance summaries, alert statistics
- **Monthly**: Species observations, water quality data
- **Quarterly**: MPA compliance, conservation metrics
- **Annually**: Comprehensive conservation assessment

#### 8.2.2 Incident Reports
- **Critical Incidents**: Within 2 hours
- **Major Incidents**: Within 24 hours
- **Minor Incidents**: Within 7 days
- **Format**: Standardized incident report form
- **Distribution**: Relevant authorities and stakeholders

## 9. Safety and Ethics

### 9.1 Safety Protocols
- All field personnel must complete marine safety training
- Personal flotation devices required for all boat operations
- Buddy system mandatory for diving operations
- Emergency communication equipment required
- Weather monitoring and trip cancellation criteria
- First aid kits and emergency oxygen available

### 9.2 Research Ethics
- Minimize disturbance to wildlife
- Obtain ethical approval for invasive procedures
- Follow animal handling guidelines (IACUC or equivalent)
- Respect traditional fishing rights and local communities
- Share data and findings with local stakeholders
- Acknowledge indigenous knowledge and participation
