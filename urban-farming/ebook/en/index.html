This comprehensive guide covers the WIA-AGRI-031 Urban Farming Standard, providing detailed insights into city-based agriculture, rooftop gardens, community farming, container growing, and urban food forests. 📚 Table of Contents

  • Chapter 1: Introduction to Urban Farming
    History, evolution, and principles of urban agriculture. Benefits, challenges, and global impact on food security.
  • Chapter 2: Rooftop Gardens & Building-Based Agriculture
    Design, construction, and management of rooftop gardens. Structural considerations, water management, and crop selection.
  • Chapter 3: Community Gardens & Social Impact
    Creating and managing community gardens. Plot allocation, member engagement, conflict resolution, and community building.
  • Chapter 4: Container Farming & Small-Space Growing
    Container selection, soil mixes, balcony gardens, and maximizing production in limited urban spaces.
  • Chapter 5: Urban Food Forests & Edible Landscapes
    Designing permaculture food forests in cities. Multi-layered planting, perennial crops, and ecosystem services.
  • Chapter 6: Water Management & Urban Composting
    Rainwater harvesting, greywater systems, drip irrigation, and city-scale composting operations.
  • Chapter 7: Community Engagement & Education
    Building community participation, educational programs, volunteer management, and social outcomes.
  • Chapter 8: Urban Integration & Future Trends
    City planning integration, policy frameworks, market opportunities, and the future of urban agriculture.
