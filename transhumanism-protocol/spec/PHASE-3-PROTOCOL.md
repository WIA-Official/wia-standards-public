## 5. Mind Uploading Protocol

### 5.1 Uploading Methods

**Destructive Upload**: Complete brain scan (destructive), highest fidelity, irreversible
**Non-Destructive Upload**: Non-invasive scan, good fidelity, original preserved
**Gradual Replacement**: Incremental neuron replacement, highest continuity, reversible

### 5.2 Upload Verification

```
Upload Fidelity Score (UFS):
UFS = (Structural × 0.4) + (Functional × 0.4) + (Subjective × 0.2)

Classification:
- UFS ≥ 95: Excellent
- UFS 85-95: Good
- UFS 75-85: Acceptable
- UFS < 75: Insufficient (retry)

Minimum Requirements:
- Structural: ≥95%, Functional: ≥90%, Subjective: ≥80%
```

## 6. Consciousness Continuity

### 6.1 Continuity Principles

```
1. Information Continuity: Preserve all neural patterns, memories, knowledge
2. Causal Continuity: Maintain causal chain, no temporal gaps
3. Psychological Continuity: Preserve personality, values, emotions
4. Phenomenal Continuity: Maintain qualia richness, experiential quality

```
CS = (Information × 0.3) + (Causal × 0.3) + (Psychological × 0.25) + (Phenomenal × 0.15)

Requirements:
- Minimum CS: 85
- Minimum per-component: 80
- Subjective acceptance: Required

## A.1 Mind-uploading research protocol

The mind-uploading protocol is a research-only protocol; current science does NOT support clinical mind-uploading and the WIA standard does NOT certify any operational uploading service. The protocol covers connectomics-imaging methodology (electron-microscopy of preserved tissue at <10 nm resolution; X-ray nanotomography for whole-brain mapping; preservation chemistry), simulation-platform conformance criteria (computational substrate, reproducibility of the simulated activity, validation against the original tissue's electrophysiology where feasible), and the philosophical caveats (continuity, identity, consciousness). Any submission claiming an uploading service is rejected at the conformance suite.

## A.2 Consciousness-continuity research protocol

Continuity research follows the philosophy-of-mind framework: the no-branching principle, the gradual-replacement criterion (Theseus-style), the psychological-continuity criterion (Locke / Parfit), and the substrate-independence hypothesis. The protocol exposes a structured-debate envelope where competing positions are documented with cited sources; the conformance suite checks for coverage of the major positions rather than asserting which is correct.

## A.3 Human-AI merger framework

The merger framework defines four tiers: (1) tool-use AI (no merger; AI is an external tool); (2) tightly-coupled assistive AI (smartphone-class augmentation); (3) embodied AI assistant (always-on wearable with continuous biosensing and proactive intervention); (4) cognitive co-processor (high-bandwidth BCI offloading to AI); plus a hypothetical (5) substrate-fused (no current science). Each tier has a distinct ethics envelope, regulatory cross-reference, and conformance criteria. The protocol bans tier-creep: an implementation declaring tier 2 cannot operate as tier 4 without re-certification.

## A.4 Brain-computer interface protocol

BCI protocol covers the device-class envelope (non-invasive EEG, semi-invasive ECoG, fully implanted intracortical), the channel-count and sampling-rate floors per use case, the calibration-and-decoder-update cadence, the closed-loop safety envelope (autonomy override, kill-switch, fail-to-safe), the long-term tissue-response monitoring schedule, the explant criteria, and the post-explant rehabilitation protocol. The protocol cross-references the FDA Q-Sub guidance for neurological-device development and the IEEE Std 7000-2021 model process for addressing ethical concerns during system design.

## A.5 Existential-risk-management protocol

Existential-risk records follow the four-quadrant framework (Bostrom 2013): catastrophic+global, catastrophic+local, mild+global, mild+local. Each enhancement category is assessed against the framework and the assessment is published with the assessor identity. Category-level moratoria can be requested via the standards process when an emerging risk pattern is observed; moratoria require a two-thirds Committee vote per Phase 4 §Z.4 plus an independent external review.

## A.6 Replay and integrity defence

Standard 96-bit nonce + 300-second skew window + 600-second seen-nonce cache for the registration and transition control plane. Audit-event records are signed at registration time and the signature chain is anchored into a Merkle tree per-tenant; chain breaks invalidate the consent envelope and trigger a forensic-review event.

## A.7 Reversibility and explant protocol

Reversibility planning is mandatory for every enhancement registration. The reversibility envelope captures (a) the technical reversal procedure (pharmacological taper schedule, surgical explant sequence, neural-decoder retirement), (b) the expected recovery trajectory with the typical time-to-baseline window, (c) the residual physiological footprint after maximal reversal effort, and (d) the post-reversal monitoring schedule. For implanted neural devices the explant protocol cross-references the FDA Q-Sub guidance and the device-of-record manufacturer's published explant procedure; the protocol enforces a minimum 12-week post-explant monitoring window with quarterly follow-up out to 24 months.

## A.8 Closed-loop autonomy-override protocol

Cognitive-prosthesis devices that operate in closed-loop neural-control configurations MUST implement an autonomy-override channel: the subject can interrupt closed-loop modulation through a documented input modality (voice, dedicated control surface, blink-based switch, etc.) within a guaranteed maximum reaction time. The override-channel envelope at Phase 1 §A.5 declares the input modality, the guaranteed reaction time, the kill-switch action (revert to safe baseline; revert to last-known-good calibration; remain in current state pending operator review), and the alert chain (subject; carer; clinician on-call; device-of-record manufacturer 24/7 support). Conformance testing exercises the autonomy-override at the device-acceptance gate plus quarterly during the post-implantation monitoring window.

## A.9 Capacity assessment and consent-renewal protocol

Subjects who carry cognitive-modulating enhancements undergo periodic capacity assessment using the MacArthur Competence Assessment Tool for Treatment (MacCAT-T) or the equivalent jurisdiction-appropriate instrument; capacity assessments are conducted by an independent clinician outside the implanting team. The protocol envelope captures the assessment cadence (default annual; quarterly during the first post-implantation year), the result envelope, the consent-renewal log, and the surrogate-decision-maker chain for the case where capacity is impaired. Capacity-loss events trigger an automatic ethics-board review per Phase 2 §A.5 with the option to suspend or reverse the enhancement per the reversibility envelope at Phase 3 §A.7.

## A.10 Independent-replication and transparency protocol

Findings claimed under WIA-AUG-015 require independent replication before being communicated to the public as established outcomes. The replication protocol covers (a) pre-registration of the replication study against the original protocol identifier, (b) blinded-analysis where feasible, (c) data-and-code-availability requirements unless precluded by privacy or proprietary constraints documented in advance, (d) outcome-reporting against the originally pre-registered endpoints rather than post-hoc selected ones, and (e) publication of the replication results regardless of direction (positive, negative, mixed) per the registered-reports model.

## Z.1 Glossary, conformance, governance

A companion glossary at `https://wiastandards.com/transhumanism-protocol/glossary/`
expands every term used throughout this Phase. The conformance
test suite at `https://github.com/WIA-Official/wia-transhumanism-protocol-conformance`
walks every endpoint and protocol exchange. The reference container
at `wia/transhumanism-protocol-host:1.0.0` ships every transhumanism-protocol envelope and
endpoint with mock data so integrators can exercise the contract
before wiring real backends. The companion CLI at
`cli/transhumanism-protocol.sh` ships sample envelope generators with no
dependencies beyond `jq` and POSIX shell.

## Z.2 Cross-standard composition (recap)

This Phase composes with WIA-OMNI-API for credential storage,
WIA-AIR-SHIELD for runtime trust list, WIA-SOCIAL Phase 3 §5 for
federation handshake, WIA-INTENT for workload intent declaration,
and (where personal data is processed) WIA Secure Enclave for
sealed-data envelopes. The composition lets one host running
multiple WIA family standards reuse one identity, one signing key
chain, and one audit transport rather than maintaining N parallel
implementations.

## Z.3 Implementation runbook

A first implementation typically follows: stand up the reference
container; run the conformance suite against it end-to-end;
replace the mock backend with a real backend one endpoint at a
time; wire audit-log replication out to the operations sink;
onboard a single trusted peer for federation; expand to multiple
peers; promote to production with the warning-envelope subscription
enabled and the runbook in §Z.5 followed.

## Z.4 Backwards-compatibility promise

Within the 1.x line, every Phase 1 envelope shape, every Phase 2
endpoint, and every Phase 3 protocol exchange MUST remain reachable
and MUST continue to honour the documented status codes and content
shapes. Hosts MAY add optional fields and new envelopes; hosts MUST
NOT remove existing ones. Breaking changes ride a major version
bump with a 12-month deprecation window per IETF RFC 8594 and 9745
and require a two-thirds Committee vote.

## Z.5 Closing implementer note

This Phase fits inside the WIA Standards four-Phase architecture:
Phase 1 envelopes are the wire-format contract; Phase 2 surfaces
them through HTTPS; Phase 3 wraps them in protocol exchanges that
cross trust boundaries; Phase 4 integrates with the broader
ecosystem. Transhumanism-protocol deployments that follow this layering
inherit the per-Phase conformance gates and the cross-standard
composition behaviour described in Z.2.
