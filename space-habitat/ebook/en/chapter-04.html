Need for Closed Ecosystems

If space habitats depend on Earth resupply, long-term habitation is impossible due to cost and logistics. Launching 1kg to ISS costs ~$10,000. Closed ecosystems recycle resources internally, achieving self-sufficiency. A closed ecological system recycles air, water, and nutrients without external input. Complete closure is extremely difficult - ISS currently recycles 93% of water.

Bioregenerative Life Support Systems

Biological life support systems use plants, microorganisms, and potentially animals to regenerate air, water, and food. Core cycles: - Oxygen Cycle: CO₂ (human breath) → Plant photosynthesis → O₂ - Water Cycle: Wastewater → Purification → Clean water - Nutrient Cycle: Waste → Microbial decomposition → Plant nutrients - Food Cycle: Nutrients + water + CO₂ → Plant growth → Edible crops Plants provide multiple benefits: oxygen production (20-30m² per person), food production, water cycling through transpiration, and psychological benefits.

Space Agriculture Technology Details

1. Hydroponics

Growing plants without soil by dissolving nutrients in water. Most widely used cultivation method in space.

Advantages:

  • 90% less water than soil cultivation
  • 30-50% faster growth
  • Easier pest management
  • Less weight and volume

Disadvantages:

  • Power outages can quickly kill plants
  • Complex nutrient solution management
  • Difficult recovery from system failures

2. Aeroponics

Plants suspended in air with nutrient solution sprayed on roots. NASA is actively researching this technology.

Advantages:

  • Even less water than hydroponics (98% reduction)
  • Optimal oxygen supply for fastest growth
  • Easy root observation and management
  • Most suitable for microgravity

Disadvantages:

  • Risk of spray nozzle clogging
  • Complex system prone to failures
  • Plant damage within minutes of power loss

Cultivable Crops

Space-suitable crops must meet these criteria:

  • Fast growth cycle
  • High nutritional value
  • Low space requirements
  • Hardiness and disease resistance

Priority Space Agriculture Crops

Currently grown successfully on ISS:

  • Lettuce, kale, mustard greens (25-30 days)
  • Radishes (30-40 days)
  • Tomatoes (60-80 days)
  • Peppers (90 days)

Next-generation target crops:

  • Wheat (staple grain, 90-100 days)
  • Potatoes (high calorie, 90-120 days)
  • Soybeans (protein, 100-120 days)
  • Strawberries (vitamins, psychological benefits)

Water Recycling System Details

Water is one of space's most precious resources. ISS currently achieves ~93% water recycling rate, with goal of 98%+.

Water Cycling Stages

  1. Collection:
    • Toilet water, handwashing, shower water
    • Atmospheric humidity (dehumidifier collection)
    • Plant transpiration vapor
    • Human sweat and breath vapor
  2. Pre-treatment:
    • Remove large particles (filtering)
    • Remove chemicals (activated carbon)
    • Break down organics (microbes or chemical reaction)
  3. Purification:
    • Distillation (heat to vapor then condense)
    • Reverse osmosis (RO membrane removes impurities)
    • UV sterilization (removes microorganisms)
    • Ion exchange (mineral adjustment)
  4. Quality Testing:
    • Chemical sensors detect contaminants
    • Microbial testing
    • Taste and odor evaluation
  5. Storage and Distribution:
    • Drinking water, cooking water
    • Hygiene water
    • Plant irrigation
    • Oxygen generation (electrolysis)

ISS Water Recovery System

ISS water recovery system consists of two main components:

System Processes Recovery Technology
Urine Processor Urine ~85% Distillation + chemical treatment
Water Processor Greywater, humidity ~99% Multi-stage filtration + catalyst

These systems save ISS approximately 2,700 kg of water launches annually, equivalent to ~$27 million in cost savings.
