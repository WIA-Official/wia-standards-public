Solar Power Systems

Solar power is the most abundant and reliable energy source in space. In Earth orbit, 1,366 W/m² solar radiation is received (5-10x Earth surface). ISS has eight 240m² solar arrays producing 84kW. Latest 3rd generation solar cells achieve 40% efficiency. Using Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) further improves performance. Challenges include day/night cycles (every 90min in LEO), panel orientation, micrometeorite damage, and radiation degradation. On Mars, solar radiation is 43% of Earth and reduced further by dust storms requiring backup power.

Nuclear Energy

Nuclear power is essential where solar is insufficient (outer planets, polar regions, dust storms). Kilopower project develops 1-10kWe small reactors operating via thermoelectric conversion without cooling fans. Advantages: 24-hour stable power, high energy density (1kg nuclear fuel = millions of tons coal), 10+ year lifespan, compact and lightweight. Safety ensured by passive cooling, double containment, remote deployment (100m+ from habitat), automatic shutdown. Radioactive waste is minimized and stored in shielded containers.

Water Mining Technology

Water is a critical resource for life support, oxygen production, and rocket fuel. The Moon's permanently shadowed regions (PSR) craters contain millions of tons of water ice. NASA's VIPER rover is scheduled to map lunar south pole water distribution in 2024.

Lunar Water Mining Process

  1. Exploration and Mapping: VIPER rover explores south pole PSRs to map ice concentration and depth.
  2. Excavation: Robot excavators like RASSOR dig surface regolith. Ice typically 0.5-2m below surface.
  3. Heating and Extraction:
    • Heat mined regolith to 100-200°C
    • Ice sublimates to water vapor
    • Condense vapor on cold surface to collect liquid water
    • Efficiency: 5-20 kg water per 100 kg regolith expected
  4. Purification and Storage:
    • Filter to remove dust
    • Electrolyze to separate into oxygen and hydrogen (if needed)
    • Store in insulated tanks (maintain liquid even at -200°C surface)
