# WIA-CRYO-009: Cryo Transport Standard v1.0

**Status:** Superseded  
**Published:** 2023-01-15  
**Superseded By:** v1.1  

## 1. Scope

This specification defines requirements for the safe transport of cryopreserved human bodies between facilities. It covers temperature maintenance, physical protection, tracking, and documentation.

## 2. Normative References

- ISO 21973:2020 - Cryogenic vessels
- IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations (DGR)
- UN1977 - Nitrogen, refrigerated liquid

## 3. Terms and Definitions

### 3.1 Cryopreservation
The process of preserving cells, tissues, or organisms by cooling to sub-zero temperatures, typically -196°C (liquid nitrogen temperature).

### 3.2 Dewar
A specialized vacuum-insulated container designed to store and transport cryogenic liquids.

### 3.3 Chain of Custody
Documented transfer of responsibility for the cryopreserved patient from one authorized party to another.

## 4. Temperature Requirements

### 4.1 Operating Range
- **Optimal:** -196°C to -185°C
- **Acceptable:** -185°C to -140°C  
- **Warning:** -140°C to -130°C
- **Critical:** Above -130°C

### 4.2 Monitoring
- Temperature must be monitored continuously
- Sampling rate: minimum 1 reading per minute
- Accuracy: ±0.5°C
- Data retention: minimum 30 days

## 5. Container Requirements

### 5.1 Insulation
- Multi-layer vacuum insulation (MLI)
- Vacuum level: <10⁻⁵ torr
- Static hold time: minimum 30 days for 100L container

### 5.2 Structural
- Drop test: must withstand 1.2m drop onto concrete
- Pressure relief valve: set at 2.5 bar
- Material: stainless steel 304 or 316

## 6. Mechanical Protection

### 6.1 G-Force Limits
- Continuous vibration: <0.5g
- Periodic bumps: <2g for <1 second
- Sudden shock: <5g for <0.1 second  
- Maximum impact: <10g

### 6.2 Monitoring
- 6-axis accelerometer required
- Sampling rate: minimum 100 Hz
- Alert on events >2g

## 7. Tracking and Location

### 7.1 GPS Requirements
- Position accuracy: ±10 meters
- Update interval: maximum 5 minutes
- Coverage: global (GPS + GLONASS)

### 7.2 Communication
- Cellular or satellite data transmission
- Status updates every 15 minutes minimum

## 8. Chain of Custody

### 8.1 Transfer Documentation
Each custody transfer must include:
- Date and time
- Location (GPS coordinates)
- Transferor and receiver identification
- Signatures (digital or physical)
- Container seal numbers
- Temperature and LN2 level at transfer

### 8.2 Seals
- Numbered tamper-evident seals required
- Minimum 3 seal points: lid, valve, body

## 9. Emergency Procedures

### 9.1 Temperature Excursion
If temperature rises above -140°C:
1. Immediate notification to all stakeholders
2. Assess LN2 level and add if possible
3. Determine cause (vacuum failure, depletion, external heat)
4. Activate emergency response team

### 9.2 Mechanical Damage
If g-force exceeds 10g:
1. Stop transport immediately
2. Visual inspection for cracks/damage
3. Check vacuum integrity
4. Document with photos
5. Assess if transport can continue

## 10. Documentation

### 10.1 Pre-Transport
- Transport plan and route
- Equipment certifications
- Personnel qualifications
- Insurance certificates
- Regulatory permits

### 10.2 During Transport
- Continuous sensor logs
- Event log (transfers, inspections, alerts)
- Communication log

### 10.3 Post-Transport
- Condition report
- Sensor data analysis
- Chain of custody certificate
- Incident reports (if any)

## 11. Compliance Levels

### Level 1 - Basic
- Temperature monitoring
- Basic shock protection  
- Custody documentation
- Suitable for local ground transport <100km

### Level 2 - Standard  
- All Level 1 requirements
- GPS tracking
- Real-time alerts
- Enhanced documentation
- Suitable for most transports

### Level 3 - Premium
- All Level 2 requirements
- Redundant systems
- 24/7 monitoring center
- Dedicated transport team
- Suitable for high-value/high-risk transports
