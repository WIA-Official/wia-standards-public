8.1 PRE-ARRIVAL COORDINATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Successful facility handoff begins well before physical arrival.

[CRYO] PRE-ARRIVAL PROTOCOL:

T-24 hours:
  ☐ Confirm arrival time estimate
  ☐ Verify receiving facility readiness
  ☐ Confirm storage space availability
  ☐ Brief receiving team on any incidents

T-4 hours:
  ☐ Update ETA based on current progress
  ☐ Transmit final temperature/LN2 data
  ☐ Confirm receiving personnel on-site
  ☐ Alert to any special handling requirements

T-30 minutes:
  ☐ Final arrival notification
  ☐ Current location and ETA
  ☐ Container status summary
  ☐ Receiving area preparation confirmation

Upon Arrival:
  ☐ Immediate check-in with receiving officer
  ☐ Present transport documentation
  ☐ Begin handoff procedure

8.2 RECEIVING PROTOCOLS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Physical handoff follows structured verification process.

[CRYO] RECEIVING CHECKLIST:

Identity Verification:
  ☐ Transport team credentials confirmed
  ☐ Container ID matches manifest
  ☐ Subject ID verified
  ☐ Authorization documents reviewed

Container Inspection:
  ☐ External visual inspection (no damage)
  ☐ Seal integrity verified
  ☐ Instrumentation functioning
  ☐ No signs of LN2 leak or vacuum failure

Status Verification:
  ☐ Current temperature recorded
  ☐ LN2 level measured
  ☐ Shock log reviewed
  ☐ GPS track reviewed
  ☐ Any incidents documented

Documentation Transfer:
  ☐ Original transport manifest
  ☐ Complete monitoring logs
  ☐ Incident reports (if any)
  ☐ Chain of custody signatures
  ☐ Digital signature from receiving officer

Physical Transfer:
  ☐ Subject transferred to facility storage
  ☐ Temperature continuously monitored during transfer
  ☐ Final position documented
  ☐ Handoff completion timestamp recorded


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8.3 QUALITY VERIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Post-arrival quality assessment ensures transport success.

[CRYO] QUALITY ASSESSMENT:

Temperature Review:
  - Calculate Transport Quality Index (TQI)
  - Verify all data points within acceptable ranges
  - Identify any excursions and assess impact
  - Compare to quality standards

Documentation Completeness:
  - All required forms present
  - Signatures valid and timestamped
  - Data logs complete with no gaps
  - Incident reports filed if applicable

Container Condition:
  - No damage observed
  - Sensors functioning correctly
  - Vacuum integrity maintained
  - LN2 consumption within expected range

Subject Assessment:
  - Temperature stable at -196°C to -190°C
  - No signs of thermal events
  - Proper positioning maintained
  - Ready for long-term storage

Quality Grading:
  - Grade A: Perfect transport (TQI > 0.95)
  - Grade B: Excellent (TQI 0.85-0.94)
  - Grade C: Acceptable (TQI 0.70-0.84)
  - Grade D: Marginal (TQI 0.50-0.69)
  - Grade F: Failed (TQI < 0.50)

8.4 FACILITY CERTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Facilities must meet certification standards to send/receive transports.

[!] WIA TRANSPORT FACILITY CERTIFICATION:

Level Requirements:
  - L1: Basic (research/training only)
  - L2: Standard (tissue/organ transport)
  - L3: Advanced (whole body/neuro transport)

Certification Criteria:
  ☐ Facility licensing current
  ☐ Staff certifications up-to-date
  ☐ LN2 storage capacity adequate
  ☐ Transfer equipment available
  ☐ Emergency protocols documented
  ☐ Quality management system operational
  ☐ Insurance coverage verified
  ☐ Annual audit completed

Certification Process:
  1. Submit application
  2. Documentation review
  3. On-site inspection
  4. Audit findings addressed
  5. Certification issued (12 months validity)
  6. Annual recertification

8.5 PERSONNEL TRAINING STANDARDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

All personnel involved in transport handoffs require specific training.

[CRYO] TRAINING REQUIREMENTS:

Receiving Officers:
  - Cryogenic safety fundamentals (8 hours)
  - Container inspection procedures (4 hours)
  - Documentation requirements (4 hours)
  - Emergency response (4 hours)
  - Practical exercises (8 hours)
  - Annual refresher (4 hours)

Transport Coordinators:
  - Complete receiving officer training
  - Route planning and optimization (8 hours)
  - Regulatory compliance (8 hours)
  - Incident management (8 hours)
  - Quality assurance (4 hours)
  - Annual refresher (8 hours)

Facility Directors:
  - Executive overview (4 hours)
  - Legal and liability (4 hours)
  - Quality management (4 hours)
  - Continuous improvement (4 hours)
