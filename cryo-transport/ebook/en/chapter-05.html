================================================================================
CHAPTER 5: CHAIN OF CUSTODY DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

[BACK TO TABLE OF CONTENTS]

Chain of custody documentation provides legal, regulatory, and operational
evidence of proper handling throughout transport. Complete documentation
protects all parties and ensures accountability.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[CRYO] REQUIRED DOCUMENTS:

Transport Manifest:
  - Transport ID and date
  - Origin and destination facilities
  - Subject identification
  - Container specifications
  - Route plan
  - Personnel assignments

Transfer Records:
  - Release authorization (origin)
  - Acceptance confirmation (destination)
  - Intermediate handoffs (if any)
  - Digital signatures with timestamps

Monitoring Logs:
  - Temperature data (continuous)
  - GPS tracking records
  - LN2 level measurements
  - Shock/vibration events

Incident Reports (if applicable):
  - Alert events
  - Temperature excursions
  - Route deviations
  - Emergency responses


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.2 DIGITAL SIGNATURE SYSTEMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Digital signatures provide cryptographic proof of document authenticity
and non-repudiation.

[i] SIGNATURE TECHNOLOGY:

Algorithm: RSA-2048 or ECDSA P-256
Hash: SHA-256
Standard: PKCS#7 or XMLDSig
Timestamp: RFC 3161 trusted timestamp
Verification: Public key infrastructure (PKI)

Signature Events:
  1. Origin Release: Facility officer signs manifest
  2. Transport Acceptance: Driver signs custody transfer
  3. Destination Delivery: Receiving officer signs acceptance
  4. Incident Acknowledgment: All parties sign incident reports


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.3 HANDOFF PROTOCOLS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Physical custody transfers are critical control points requiring detailed
verification.

[CRYO] HANDOFF PROCEDURE:

Pre-Handoff:
  ☐ Verify identity of receiving party (photo ID + credentials)
  ☐ Confirm transport authorization
  ☐ Review current status (temperature, LN2, location)

During Handoff:
  ☐ Physical inspection of container
  ☐ Temperature verification
  ☐ LN2 level check
  ☐ Seal integrity verification
  ☐ Documentation review

Post-Handoff:
  ☐ Digital signatures captured
  ☐ Biometric verification (optional but recommended)
  ☐ Timestamp recorded
  ☐ GPS location logged
  ☐ Notification sent to all parties


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.4 LEGAL AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Transport documentation must satisfy multiple regulatory requirements.

[!] COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST:

DOT Hazardous Materials:
  ☐ Shipping papers (DOT format)
  ☐ Hazmat labels and placards
  ☐ Driver training certification
  ☐ Emergency response information

FDA (if applicable):
  ☐ Human cells/tissues/cellular-based products documentation
  ☐ Establishment registration
  ☐ Chain of custody records

International (for cross-border):
  ☐ Customs documentation
  ☐ Import/export permits
  ☐ Biological material certificates
  ☐ Country-specific requirements


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CHAPTER SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Takeaways:

  [1] Complete documentation includes transport manifest, transfer records,
      continuous monitoring logs, and incident reports (if applicable).

  [2] Digital signatures using cryptographic algorithms provide non-repudiation
      and legal validity for chain of custody transfers.

  [3] Physical handoff protocols require identity verification, container
      inspection, and multi-party digital signatures with timestamps.

  [4] Regulatory compliance encompasses DOT hazmat, FDA tissue regulations,
      and international customs requirements for cross-border transports.

  [5] Blockchain anchoring (optional) provides tamper-proof audit trail
      for long-term archival and legal proceedings.
