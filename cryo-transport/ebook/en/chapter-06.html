6.1 EMERGENCY CLASSIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Emergency events during cryogenic transport require rapid, coordinated
response to protect subject integrity.

[CRYO] EMERGENCY CLASSIFICATIONS:

LEVEL 1: Minor Incident
  - Temperature -190°C to -180°C for <2 hours
  - LN2 level 30-50%
  - GPS signal temporarily lost (<30 minutes)
  - Minor route deviation
  Response: Increased monitoring, documentation

LEVEL 2: Moderate Emergency
  - Temperature -180°C to -150°C
  - LN2 level <30%
  - Vehicle breakdown (repairable on-site)
  - Unplanned stop >2 hours
  Response: Emergency team activation, prepare backup

LEVEL 3: Critical Emergency
  - Temperature >-150°C
  - LN2 depleted
  - Vehicle accident with container damage
  - Natural disaster impact
  Response: Immediate emergency transfer, facility coordination


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.2 RESPONSE PROCEDURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[CRYO] EMERGENCY RESPONSE WORKFLOW:

Detection:
  1. Automated alert triggers (sensors, GPS)
  2. Driver reports issue
  3. Monitoring team identifies anomaly

Initial Assessment (0-5 minutes):
  1. Classify emergency level
  2. Verify subject status (temperature)
  3. Assess immediate risks
  4. Activate appropriate response tier

Immediate Actions (5-30 minutes):
  1. Notify all parties (origin, destination, coordinator)
  2. Identify nearest backup facility
  3. Dispatch emergency support if needed
  4. Implement containment measures

Short-term Response (30 minutes - 4 hours):
  1. Execute emergency transfer if required
  2. Coordinate with backup facility
  3. Maintain continuous communication
  4. Document all actions

Resolution (4+ hours):
  1. Complete emergency transfer or resume transport
  2. Verify subject stability
  3. Update all parties
  4. Begin incident investigation

6.3 BACKUP FACILITY NETWORK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Pre-identified backup facilities enable rapid emergency response.

[i] BACKUP FACILITY CRITERIA:

Essential Requirements:
  ☐ LN2 storage capability
  ☐ 24/7 staffing
  ☐ Emergency receiving capability
  ☐ Certified for cryogenic operations
  ☐ <2 hour drive from major transport routes

Preferred Features:
  ☐ Subject container compatibility
  ☐ Transfer equipment available
  ☐ Medical/cryonics expertise
  ☐ Prior agreement with transport network

Backup Facility Spacing:
  - Ground transport: Every 200-300 km
  - Air transport: Major airports with LN2 capability
  - International: At least 2 per country on route


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.4 INCIDENT DOCUMENTATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Complete incident documentation supports analysis, improvement, and
legal/regulatory compliance.

[CRYO] INCIDENT REPORT STRUCTURE:

Section 1: Event Details
  - Incident ID and timestamp
  - Location (GPS coordinates)
  - Classification level
  - Detection method

Section 2: Subject Status
  - Temperature at detection
  - Temperature throughout incident
  - LN2 level
  - Container condition

Section 3: Response Actions
  - Initial assessment findings
  - Actions taken (chronological)
  - Personnel involved
  - Resources utilized

Section 4: Resolution
  - Final status
  - Transfer completion (if applicable)
  - Subject condition assessment
  - Transport continuation or termination

Section 5: Root Cause Analysis
  - Primary cause
  - Contributing factors
  - Preventability assessment
  - Corrective actions

CHAPTER SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Takeaways:

  [1] Emergency classification (Level 1-3) determines response intensity,
      with Level 3 critical emergencies requiring immediate transfer.

  [2] Response workflow proceeds through detection, assessment, immediate
      actions, short-term response, and resolution with defined timeframes.

  [3] Backup facility network must provide coverage every 200-300 km for
      ground transport with 24/7 capability and LN2 storage.

  [4] Incident documentation captures complete event timeline, response
      actions, and root cause analysis for improvement.

  [5] Post-incident analysis identifies systemic improvements to prevent
      recurrence and enhance overall transport safety.
