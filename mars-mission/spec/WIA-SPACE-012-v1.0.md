# WIA-SPACE-012: Mars Mission Standard v1.0.0

**Standard ID:** WIA-SPACE-012
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Status:** Final
**Date:** 2025-01-01
**Category:** Space Exploration
**Organization:** World Certification Industry Association (WIA)

## Abstract

This standard defines comprehensive guidelines, protocols, and best practices for Mars exploration missions including robotic probes, orbital dynamics, landing technologies, surface operations, human missions, and long-term colonization strategies.

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

WIA-SPACE-012 provides a unified framework for:
- Mars mission planning and execution
- Orbital mechanics and trajectory optimization
- Entry, descent, and landing (EDL) procedures
- Surface exploration and scientific research
- Human mission preparation and safety
- Long-term colonization and terraforming

### 1.2 Scope

This standard covers all aspects of Mars exploration from initial mission design through potential terraforming and permanent settlement.

### 1.3 Philosophy

弘益人間 (Hongik Ingan) - Benefit All Humanity

Mars exploration serves humanity's collective future as a multi-planetary species.

## 2. Mars Mission Categories

### 2.1 Flyby Missions
- Trajectory: Hyperbolic orbit past Mars
- Distance: < 10,000 km from surface
- Data: Photography, spectroscopy, magnetometry

### 2.2 Orbital Missions
- Orbit types: Polar, equatorial, elliptical
- Altitude: 250-50,000 km
- Duration: 1-20+ years
- Functions: Mapping, climate monitoring, communication relay

### 2.3 Lander Missions
- Landing systems: Retropropulsion, airbag, sky crane
- Mobility: Static or short-range
- Power: RTG or solar
- Duration: 90 days to 10+ years

### 2.4 Rover Missions
- Mobility: Wheels, tracks
- Range: 0.1-50+ km
- Instruments: Cameras, spectrometers, drills
- Autonomy: Supervised autonomous navigation

### 2.5 Sample Return Missions
- Collection: Surface and subsurface samples
- Storage: Sealed containers
- Return: Mars Ascent Vehicle + Earth Return Orbiter
- Analysis: Biosafety Level 4 facilities

### 2.6 Human Missions
- Crew: 4-12 astronauts
- Duration: 2-3 years total (including transit)
- Surface stay: 500-800 days
- Infrastructure: Habitats, ISRU, power systems

## 3. Orbital Mechanics

### 3.1 Transfer Orbits

#### 3.1.1 Hohmann Transfer
- Type I: < 180° transfer angle
- Flight time: 150-250 days
- Delta-V: 5.5-6.5 km/s total

#### 3.1.2 Launch Windows
- Frequency: Every 26 months (synodic period)
- Duration: 30-45 days
- C3 Energy: 10-20 km²/s²

### 3.2 Mars Orbit Insertion (MOI)
- Burn duration: 20-40 minutes
- Delta-V: 1.4-2.5 km/s
- Orbit: Initial elliptical, final circular or near-circular

### 3.3 Aerobraking
- Periapsis altitude: 100-130 km
- Duration: 3-6 months
- Fuel savings: 90%

## 4. Landing Technologies

### 4.1 Entry, Descent, Landing (EDL)

#### 4.1.1 Entry Phase
- Velocity: 5.5-7 km/s
- Altitude: 125 km
- Duration: ~4 minutes
- Deceleration: Heat shield with ablative material

#### 4.1.2 Descent Phase
- Parachute deployment: Mach 2, altitude 10-11 km
- Parachute type: Supersonic disk-gap-band
- Diameter: 16-21 m
- Duration: ~2 minutes

#### 4.1.3 Landing Phase
- Systems: Retropropulsion, airbag, or sky crane
- Final velocity: < 2 m/s
- Duration: ~1 minute

### 4.2 Landing Site Selection Criteria
- Latitude: ±30° (solar energy)
- Elevation: < 0 km MOLA (atmosphere density)
- Slope: < 15°
- Rock abundance: < 10%
- Scientific value: High

## 5. Surface Operations

### 5.1 Power Systems
- RTG: 110W initial, 2.5% degradation/year
- Solar: 100-1000W depending on array size
- Batteries: Li-ion for energy storage

### 5.2 Communication
- Direct to Earth: X-band, < 500 bps
- Relay via orbiter: UHF, up to 2 Mbps
- Data volume: 100-500 MB/day

### 5.3 Mobility
- Speed: 0.01-0.15 m/s
- Range: 50m-50km per mission
- Autonomy: Hazard avoidance, path planning

### 5.4 Scientific Instruments
- Cameras: Navigation, science, hazard
- Spectrometers: Visible, IR, Raman, LIBS
- Drills: Surface to 5cm depth, future 100m+
- Weather station: Temperature, pressure, wind, humidity

## 6. Human Mission Requirements

### 6.1 Crew Health and Safety
- Radiation exposure: < 500 mSv per mission
- Atmosphere: 70 kPa, 21% O₂, 78% N₂
- Temperature: 18-24°C
- Humidity: 40-70%
- Exercise: 2 hours/day minimum

### 6.2 Life Support (ECLSS)
- Water recycling: > 95%
- O₂ generation: Electrolysis or MOXIE-based
- CO₂ removal: Sabatier reaction
- Waste management: Recycling and composting

### 6.3 In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU)
- Water extraction: Subsurface ice mining
- O₂ production: 10-20 kg/day per MOXIE unit
- Fuel production: CH₄ + O₂ via Sabatier reaction
- Building materials: Regolith 3D printing

### 6.4 Habitat Requirements
- Volume: 25-40 m³ per person
- Radiation shielding: 2-3m regolith or water
- Redundancy: N+2 for critical systems
- Emergency shelter: 72-hour autonomous

## 7. Colonization Phases

### 7.1 Phase 1: Outpost (2033-2050)
- Population: 4-1,000
- Duration: Short stays (26 months)
- Dependence: 80% Earth supplies

### 7.2 Phase 2: Settlement (2050-2080)
- Population: 1,000-25,000
- Duration: Permanent residents
- Self-sufficiency: 50%

### 7.3 Phase 3: Colony (2080-2150)
- Population: 25,000-1,000,000
- Infrastructure: Multiple cities
- Self-sufficiency: 90%

### 7.4 Phase 4: Terraforming (2150+)
- Warming: +60°C increase
- Atmosphere: 60+ kPa pressure
- O₂: 15-20% concentration
- Timeframe: 500-10,000 years

## 8. Safety and Redundancy

### 8.1 Mission Assurance
- Redundancy: Triple for critical systems
- Fault tolerance: Graceful degradation
- Backup plans: Abort scenarios for all phases

### 8.2 Planetary Protection
- Forward contamination: Sterilization protocols
- Backward contamination: Biosafety containment
- Sample handling: BSL-4 facilities
