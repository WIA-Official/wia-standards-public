## Overview

The WIA-SPACE-008 standard defines protocols, best practices, and technical specifications for:

- **Lunar Mining**: Helium-3, water ice, rare earth elements, titanium, oxygen extraction
- **Asteroid Mining**: C-type, S-type, M-type asteroid resource extraction and processing
- **Mars Resources**: ISRU (In-Situ Resource Utilization), water, CO₂, regolith utilization
- **Outer Planets**: Europa ocean, Titan hydrocarbons, Enceladus water plumes
- **Transportation**: Orbital mechanics, space elevators, electromagnetic catapults
- **Legal Compliance**: Outer Space Treaty, Artemis Accords, national space laws
- **Economic Models**: Cost analysis, market projections, investment frameworks

## Use Cases

### 🌙 Lunar Base Construction

**Scenario**: NASA Artemis program builds permanent lunar base at South Pole.

**Resources Used**:
- Water ice from Shackleton Crater → Electrolysis → H₂ + O₂ for life support and propellant
- Lunar regolith → 3D printing → Habitat structures and landing pads
- Titanium extraction → Lightweight structural components
- Oxygen from regolith (40% by mass) → Breathing air

**Economic Impact**: Reduces Earth-to-Moon cargo by 80%, saving $10B+ in launch costs.

### ☄️ Asteroid Mining Mission

**Scenario**: AstroForge targets 100m diameter M-type NEA.

**Process**:
1. Survey mission: Spectroscopy confirms 90% Fe-Ni, 100 ppm PGM
2. Mining robot: Anchored drilling extracts 1 million tons over 5 years
3. Space refining: Magnetic separation, solar furnace smelting
4. Earth return: 100 tons PGM (Pt, Pd, Rh) → $3-5 billion revenue

**ROI**: $2.5B investment → $3-5B revenue = 20-100% return.

### 🔴 Mars ISRU for Propellant

**Scenario**: SpaceX Starship uses Mars-produced methane for return journey.

**Process**:
1. Atmospheric CO₂ capture (95% of Mars atmosphere)
2. Underground ice mining → Water extraction
3. Sabatier reaction: CO₂ + 4H₂ → CH₄ + 2H₂O (350°C, Ni catalyst)
4. Water electrolysis: H₂O → H₂ + O₂
5. Storage: 240 tons CH₄ + 860 tons O₂ for Starship return

**Benefit**: Eliminates need to carry return fuel from Earth, enabling Mars missions.

## Legal Framework

### International Treaties

- **Outer Space Treaty (1967)**: 111 ratifications
  - Article II: No national appropriation of celestial bodies
  - Interpretation: Territorial sovereignty prohibited, but resource extraction allowed

- **Moon Agreement (1979)**: 18 ratifications
  - "Common Heritage of Mankind" concept
  - Major spacefaring nations NOT party (US, Russia, China)

- **Artemis Accords (2020)**: 30+ signatories
  - Resource extraction explicitly allowed
  - Safety zones to prevent interference
  - Transparency and interoperability requirements

### National Laws

- **United States (2015 SPACE Act)**: Citizens can own, transport, and sell space resources
- **Luxembourg (2017)**: First European nation to recognize resource ownership
- **Japan, UAE, India**: Developing national frameworks

### Key Legal Questions

1. **Ownership vs. Appropriation**: Can companies own extracted resources? ✅ Yes (US, Luxembourg)
2. **Safety Zones**: How large can exclusion zones be? ⚠️ "Reasonable" per Artemis Accords (not specified)
3. **Environmental Protection**: Are impact assessments required? ⚠️ Recommended but not mandatory
4. **Conflict Resolution**: How to resolve competing claims? ⚠️ No international mechanism yet
