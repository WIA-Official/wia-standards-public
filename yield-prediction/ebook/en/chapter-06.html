Chapter 06

System Integration and Applications

6.1 Where yield-prediction outputs land

Yield-prediction envelopes are produced by models and consumed by four downstream system classes: farm management software (input ordering, harvest scheduling), commodity-market participants (futures trading desks, grain elevator capacity planning), insurers (parametric crop insurance trigger calculation), and government statistical agencies (national-level production forecasting). Each consumer extracts a different field from the same envelope, so the standard's wire format must serve all four without favouring any one.

6.2 Integration with farm management software

Farm management software typically integrates by polling a forecast endpoint per-field, weekly during the growing season and daily during the final 30 days before harvest. The standard specifies an HTTP cache directive that allows a producer to return a cached prediction without re-running the model when no new feature data has arrived. This drops model-server load by an order of magnitude on busy days.

6.3 Commodity-market integration

Aggregated regional forecasts feed commodity-market participants. The standard's regional-aggregation envelope sums field-level forecasts weighted by planted area and carries the underlying field count plus the prediction-interval band aggregated under independence assumptions. Consumers needing tighter aggregation (joint-distribution-aware) can request the per-field envelopes directly under explicit consent.

6.4 Insurance triggers

Parametric crop insurance pays out when an objective measurement crosses a contractual threshold, without claims adjustment. Yield-prediction envelopes are increasingly used as the measurement layer for these contracts. The standard requires extra rigor for insurance-grade predictions: the model must be independently audited annually, the model's training data must be archived for the contract duration, and the prediction interval must be reported alongside the point prediction.

6.5 Government statistical reporting

National statistical agencies (USDA NASS, Eurostat, KOSTAT) publish official production forecasts that move markets. These agencies increasingly augment field-survey-based estimates with model-derived predictions. The standard's regional-aggregation envelope carries metadata distinguishing model-derived from survey-derived inputs so that the agency's published methodology statement remains honest about the source mix.
