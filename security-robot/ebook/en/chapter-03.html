Surveillance and Monitoring Capabilities

📖 18 min read 🏷️ Sensors 📅 Updated Jan 2025

The Sensor-Driven Security Paradigm

Security robots are fundamentally sensor platforms. While their mobility enables them to position sensors throughout an environment, it is the sensors themselves—and the intelligence applied to their data—that deliver security value. This chapter explores the comprehensive sensor capabilities that transform security robots from simple patrol vehicles into sophisticated surveillance systems.

Modern security robots leverage multiple sensor modalities simultaneously, creating a rich, multi-dimensional view of the environment that exceeds human sensory capabilities. This sensor fusion approach enables detection of threats that would be invisible to any single sensing technology.

Beyond Human Perception

Security robots can perceive electromagnetic radiation across spectrums invisible to humans, detect sounds outside the range of human hearing, measure environmental parameters like air quality and radiation, and maintain continuous alertness without fatigue. This superhuman sensing capability is what makes robotic surveillance transformative rather than merely supplementary.

Thermal Imaging

Thermal cameras detect infrared radiation emitted by objects based on their temperature, enabling vision in complete darkness and detection of heat signatures through obstructions that block visible light.

Thermal Technology Types

🌡️
Uncooled Microbolometer

Most common in security robots. Lower cost, no cooling required, good sensitivity for typical applications.

❄️
Cooled Infrared

Higher sensitivity and resolution. Requires cryogenic cooling. Used in high-end military/critical infrastructure.

📊
Radiometric

Measures actual temperatures, not just relative heat. Enables temperature-based alerts for fire detection.

Security Applications of Thermal

  • Perimeter Detection: Detect intruders through darkness, fog, and foliage
  • Concealed Object Detection: Identify objects hidden under clothing
  • Fire Detection: Early warning of hot spots before visible flames
  • Equipment Monitoring: Identify overheating machinery or electrical systems
  • Occupancy Verification: Confirm presence of people in areas that should be vacant

Thermal + Visible Fusion

Advanced security robots overlay thermal data onto visible imagery, combining the identification capability of optical cameras with the detection capability of thermal sensors. This fusion enables operators to quickly locate and identify thermal anomalies.

LiDAR for Surveillance

While primarily used for navigation (see Chapter 2), LiDAR also serves surveillance functions. Its ability to create precise 3D maps enables detection of environmental changes that may indicate security events.

Change Detection

By comparing current LiDAR scans against baseline maps, security robots can detect:

  • New Objects: Items placed in the environment since last patrol
  • Missing Objects: Assets removed from expected locations
  • Structural Changes: Doors opened/closed, gates moved, barriers repositioned
  • Intrusion Detection: Presence of people or vehicles in restricted zones

3D Volumetric Surveillance

3D LiDAR enables volumetric monitoring—tracking objects through three-dimensional space rather than 2D image planes. This capability is particularly valuable for:

  • Drone detection and tracking
  • Accurate size/dimension estimation of objects
  • Motion detection in cluttered environments
  • Distinguishing humans from animals based on size/shape

LiDAR Limitations for Surveillance

LiDAR does not capture color or texture information useful for identification. It cannot read text, recognize faces, or determine object types from appearance alone. LiDAR is best used in combination with cameras for comprehensive surveillance.

Weather Monitoring

For outdoor deployments, security robots may include weather sensors:

  • Temperature/Humidity: Environmental awareness and equipment protection
  • Barometric Pressure: Weather prediction and altitude awareness
  • Wind Speed/Direction: Operational safety in high winds
  • Rain Detection: Triggering protective behaviors or shelter seeking

Privacy Considerations

ALPR systems collect sensitive data tracking vehicle movements. Deployments must comply with privacy regulations including data retention limits, access controls, and in some jurisdictions, notification requirements. Chapter 6 addresses privacy compliance in detail.

Sensor Fusion Architecture

The true power of security robot surveillance emerges from combining multiple sensors through intelligent fusion algorithms. Rather than treating each sensor independently, fusion creates a unified situational awareness picture.

Fusion Levels

SENSOR FUSION HIERARCHY ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ DECISION FUSION │ │ Combine high-level interpretations │ │ "Intruder detected with high conf." │ └─────────────────┬───────────────────────┘ │ ┌─────────────────┴───────────────────────┐ │ FEATURE FUSION │ │ Combine extracted features │ │ "Moving object + heat signature" │ └─────────────────┬───────────────────────┘ │ ┌─────────────────┴───────────────────────┐ │ DATA FUSION │ │ Combine raw sensor measurements │ │ "LiDAR points + camera pixels" │ └─────────────────────────────────────────┘

Fusion Benefits

  • Increased Accuracy: Multiple sensors confirming same event reduces false alarms
  • Extended Coverage: Each sensor covers others' blind spots
  • Graceful Degradation: System continues operating if one sensor fails
  • Rich Context: Combined data provides more complete threat picture

Key Takeaways from Chapter 3

  • Security robots combine multiple sensor modalities for comprehensive surveillance
  • Optical cameras provide identification capability; thermal enables night/obscured vision
  • LiDAR adds 3D awareness and change detection capabilities
  • Audio sensors detect threats invisible to visual systems
  • Environmental sensors extend security to safety monitoring
  • Sensor fusion multiplies effectiveness beyond individual sensor capabilities
  • Real-time alerting ensures surveillance data drives appropriate response
