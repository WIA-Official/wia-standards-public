Chapter 3: WIA-MED-003 Standard Overview

3.1 Mission and Goals

Mission: Enable seamless communication and data sharing between all vital sign monitoring devices to improve patient health management and increase medical accessibility.

3.3 Supported Vital Signs

  • ECG/EKG - Electrical heart activity
  • SpO2 - Oxygen saturation
  • Blood Pressure - Systolic/Diastolic
  • HRV - Heart rate variability
  • Temperature - Body temperature
  • Respiratory Rate - Breathing rate

3.4 Design Principles

  1. Openness: Free public standard, no licensing fees
  2. Interoperability: All implementations compatible
  3. Security First: End-to-end encryption required
  4. Scalability: Future-proof architecture
  5. User-Centric: Patients control their data
  6. Performance: <100ms latency target

3.6 Conformance Levels (Bridge Profile)

Beyond the certification programme above, hosts publish a bridge_profile in their discovery document indicating which integrations they implement. This is orthogonal to certification: certification proves clinical safety and interoperability; bridge profile communicates which third-party systems the host federates with.

3.7 The Self-Describing Frame Principle

A core design choice of the standard is that every frame is self-describing: a receiver presented with one envelope can reconstruct calibrated values without consulting out-of-band metadata. This makes the wire format robust against partial connectivity, late-joining subscribers, and replay-from-storage scenarios that are common in clinical settings.

The trade-off is per-frame overhead. The standard mitigates this with the optional channel descriptor caching mechanism documented in Phase 1 §Channel Descriptor: a high-rate stream publishes a one-shot descriptor that the receiver caches, and subsequent frames omit the descriptor's fields. Receivers MUST honour the descriptor only for the (stream_id, channel) pair it covers and MUST refuse frames received outside the descriptor's valid_until window.

3.8 Patient-First Audience Controls

Audience-based access (clinician, nurse station, EHR bridge, research, family, emergency service) is enforced at the host's read endpoint, not on a per-record marking. The patient's signed consent envelope (Phase 3 §Patient Consent Envelope) is the source of truth; hosts MUST refuse subscription requests whose patient identifier lacks an active consent envelope from the custodian.
