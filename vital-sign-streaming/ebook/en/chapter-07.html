7.6 WIA Family Integration

WIA Vital Sign Streaming composes with neighbouring WIA standards to keep clinical workflows coherent across the rest of the platform.

  • WIA-OMNI-API — clinician identity and credentials (medical licence, role assignments, training records) live in WIA-OMNI-API; hosts fetch them by DID rather than holding raw documents.
  • WIA-ACCESSIBILITY — clinician and patient accessibility profiles drive UI choices: large-text mode, high-contrast waveform rendering, audio-cued alerts for visually impaired clinicians, simplified summary view for cognitively impaired patients.
  • WIA-AIR-SHIELD — transport hardening; hosts MAY refuse subscription requests from peers whose AIR-SHIELD score is below a host-set threshold.
  • WIA-INTENT — a clinician's "show me last hour for patient 01HXY" intent is lowered to fetch consent → resolve stream → issue replay → render under WIA-ACCESSIBILITY profile.
  • WIA-SOCIAL — clinical milestones (de-identified) MAY be published via WIA-SOCIAL bridges with audience=internal for departmental announcements without leaving the EHR perimeter.

7.7 Migration Paths

7.7.1 From a vendor-locked bedside monitor

A hospital migrating from a single-vendor bedside monitor follows: deploy a WIA host alongside the existing monitor; run a vendor-bridge adapter that reads the monitor's serial / network protocol and emits WIA frames; run a 14-day shadow period with both surfaces active; switch alerting to WIA only after three consecutive zero-divergence days.

7.7.2 Between hospitals

Patient transfer A → B: A signs a move envelope; B replays the prior 60 minutes from A and opens its own subscriber stream; A continues to source frames for 30 minutes after handover for clinical continuity, then closes the source. A returns 301 Moved Permanently for the prior stream URL for at least 12 months.

7.9 Security Considerations

  • Vendor adapters hold device-side credentials; operators MUST use WIA-OMNI-API for credential storage and HSM-backed refresh tokens.
  • Streaming endpoints carry PHI; hosts MUST treat them as the highest audit priority and MUST page on-call when authentication failures spike beyond a baseline threshold.
  • AI/ML anomaly bridges receive de-identified or pseudo-identified data per the patient's consent envelope; bridges MUST refuse data whose audience does not permit the planned downstream use.
