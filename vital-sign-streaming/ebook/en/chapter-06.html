6.7 Federation Across Hosts

Multiple WIA Vital Sign Streaming hosts (a hospital ICU, an ambulance, a remote monitoring service) federate so a patient's signal can follow the patient across care boundaries without re-instrumentation. The roles and handshake state machine are defined in the Phase 3 specification; this chapter walks through how those mechanics surface to integrators.

6.7.1 Roles

  • Producer — holds the device-side stream and signs frames.
  • Consumer — subscribes to one or more streams (clinician dashboard, EHR bridge, alert routing service).
  • Custodian — holds the patient's consent envelope and authorises which consumers may subscribe.
  • Auditor — holds the audit log of every subscription and replay request.

6.7.2 Patient Consent Envelope

{
  "wia_vital_sign_streaming_version": "1.0.0",
  "type": "patient_consent",
  "patient_id": "did:wia:patient:01HXY",
  "custodian_id": "did:wia:custodian:hospital-A",
  "scope": ["icu", "remote_monitoring"],
  "valid_from": "2026-04-27T00:00:00Z",
  "valid_until": "2026-05-27T00:00:00Z",
  "consent_artefact_url": "https://omni.example/consent/01HXY/2026-04-27.pdf",
  "signature": { "alg": "Ed25519", "value": "AY3o..." }
}

Producers and consumers MUST refuse subscription requests whose patient identifier lacks an active consent envelope from the custodian, except in declared emergency scope (scope = ["emergency"]) which auto-expires after 24 hours and triggers a mandatory custodian review.

6.8 Replay Defence

Each signed envelope (frame, descriptor, alert, subscribe, replay request) carries a 96-bit nonce and an RFC 3339 timestamp. Receivers MUST reject envelopes with skew > ±300 s, reject envelopes whose (signer, nonce) tuple has been seen within the last 600 s, and maintain the seen-nonce cache for at least 600 s. For high-rate frame streams the cache MAY be a Bloom filter; false positives MUST trigger a replay-segment fetch rather than a silent drop.
