"Benefit All Humanity"

Mangrove forests are disappearing at an alarming rate, threatening coastal communities, marine biodiversity, and critical carbon sinks. The WIA Mangrove Restoration Standard provides a comprehensive framework for ecosystem restoration, blue carbon accounting, coastal protection, and community-based conservation. Together, we can restore these vital coastal guardians.

Technical Specifications

Data Format

The standard uses JSON for data exchange with comprehensive schemas for:

  • Site Information: GPS coordinates, ecosystem type, coverage area
  • Species Data: Scientific names, density, height, age distribution
  • Water Quality: Salinity, temperature, pH, tidal range
  • Carbon Stocks: Above/belowground biomass, soil carbon
  • Coastal Protection: Wave attenuation, erosion rates
  • Biodiversity: Marine species counts, habitat quality scores

API Endpoints

RESTful API with OAuth 2.0 authentication:

POST   /api/v1/mangrove/sites
GET    /api/v1/mangrove/sites/{siteId}
POST   /api/v1/mangrove/carbon/calculate
POST   /api/v1/mangrove/coastal/assess
GET    /api/v1/mangrove/reports/site

Communication Protocols

Support for IoT and remote monitoring:

  • MQTT: Real-time sensor data streaming
  • LoRaWAN: Long-range, low-power coastal sensors
  • WebSocket: Live monitoring dashboards
  • CoAP: Constrained device communication

Use Cases

1. Community-Based Restoration Projects

Local communities in the Philippines use the WIA standard to:

  • Monitor 25 hectares of restored mangroves
  • Generate 125 carbon credits annually
  • Earn $50,000 in blue carbon revenue
  • Support 45 fisher families through improved fish stocks
  • Protect 2.5 km of coastline from erosion

2. National Conservation Programs

Government agencies use the standard to:

  • Track mangrove coverage at national scale
  • Report to UNFCCC on blue carbon sequestration
  • Assess coastal vulnerability and protection needs
  • Coordinate with marine protected area networks
  • Support sustainable fisheries management

3. Corporate Sustainability Initiatives

Companies leverage the standard for:

  • Carbon offset portfolio diversification
  • ESG reporting and transparency
  • Supply chain resilience (coastal protection)
  • Biodiversity conservation commitments
  • Community partnership programs

Getting Started

Quick Start Guide

  1. Site Registration: Create a mangrove restoration site record
  2. Baseline Survey: Conduct initial coverage and species assessment
  3. Monitoring Setup: Deploy sensors for water quality and growth tracking
  4. Data Collection: Regular field surveys and automated sensor readings
  5. Carbon Calculation: Estimate blue carbon stocks and sequestration
  6. Reporting: Generate reports for carbon markets and stakeholders

Standards Compliance

The WIA Mangrove Restoration Standard is compatible with:

  • Verra VCS VM0033: Methodology for Tidal Wetland and Seagrass Restoration
  • Gold Standard: Land Use & Forests requirements
  • IPCC Guidelines: 2013 Wetlands Supplement
  • OBIS: Ocean Biodiversity Information System
  • W3C VC: Verifiable Credentials for certification
  • ISO 14064: Greenhouse gas quantification
