Chapter 05: Phase 2 — API Interface

5.1 Phase 2 in one paragraph

Phase 2 specifies the HTTPS surface for UHI data. Transport is TLS 1.3 (IETF RFC 8446) only.

5.2 Discovery

Every host publishes a discovery document at /.well-known/wia-urban-heat-island.

5.3 Surface temperature endpoints

POST /uhi/temp                   (signed)
GET  /uhi/temp?city_id={id}&from={ts}&to={ts}
GET  /uhi/temp/stream?city_id={id}    (Server-Sent Events for live LST + air temp)

5.4 Canopy endpoints

POST /uhi/canopy                 (signed; city authority)
GET  /uhi/canopy/{assessment_id}
GET  /uhi/canopy?city_id={id}

5.5 Mitigation endpoints

POST /uhi/mitigation             (signed; city authority)
GET  /uhi/mitigation/{intervention_id}
GET  /uhi/mitigation?city_id={id}&kind={k}

5.6 Heat health endpoints

POST /uhi/health-event           (signed; emergency-management authority)
GET  /uhi/health-event/{event_id}
GET  /uhi/health-event/stream?city_id={id}    (Server-Sent Events for advisory updates)

5.7 Vulnerable population endpoints

POST /uhi/vulnerable-area        (signed; city + public health joint authority)
GET  /uhi/vulnerable-area?city_id={id}    (audience-gated; aggregate only)

5.8 Authentication

Writes require HTTP Message Signatures per IETF RFC 9421. City authority verified via WIA-OMNI-API municipal_authority claims; emergency-management authority verified via emergency_response_authority claims.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in UHI infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, satellite revisit cadence: Landsat revisits any spot every 16 days; Sentinel-3 daily but 1-km resolution; ECOSTRESS sub-daily but ISS orbital coverage variable. Hosts MUST document data-source revisit characteristics in the discovery document. Second, vulnerable population data: aggregate-only by Phase 1 §4.6 design; downstream consumers requesting individual identification MUST be refused. Third, heat advisory latency: the gap between NWS issuance and public dissemination is critical; the standard\x27s heat_health_event stream targets < 5 minute latency from issuance to subscriber receipt.

Korea-Specific Application Note

Korea's 2018 and 2024 heat waves drove formal heat-emergency response across municipal and national agencies. KMA (Korea Meteorological Administration) issues heat advisories aligned with NOAA NWS's structure but adapted for Korean climate; the Ministry of the Interior and Safety coordinates muders-shelter (무더위 쉼터) activation across cities. The standard's heat_health_event envelope maps cleanly onto KMA advisories via the data_source field, and the Korean municipal climate offices in Seoul, Daejeon, Daegu, and Gwangju have published municipal climate adaptation plans that align with the standard's mitigation_intervention vocabulary.

Vulnerable-Population-First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through: does this protect the people most at risk during heat events while supporting effective outreach, or does it build identification infrastructure that outlasts the emergency? When the two conflict, vulnerable-population data sovereignty wins. That is why the standard mandates aggregate-only vulnerable_population_area envelopes, refuses to allow individual identification even in emergency mode, and requires emergency-management workflows to operate on neighbourhood counts rather than personal lists.
