Chapter 07: Phase 4 — Integration with Climate Data Stacks

7.1 Phase 4 in one paragraph

Phase 4 specifies how WIA Urban Heat Island integrates with existing climate data systems: Landsat / Sentinel-3 thermal infrared products, NOAA NWS heat advisories, EU Copernicus climate change service, USGS National Land Cover Database, and the WIA family.

7.2 Landsat / Sentinel-3 thermal IR bridge

Landsat 8/9 Band 10 + Band 11 thermal imagery and Sentinel-3 SLSTR are the dominant satellite TIR sources. The bridge ingests Level-2 Surface Temperature products (USGS Landsat Collection 2) or Sentinel-3 SLSTR Level-2 LST and emits WIA surface_temperature_observation envelopes with scene_id preservation for downstream re-fetching.

7.3 NOAA NWS heat advisory bridge

NOAA National Weather Service issues heat advisories per local Weather Forecast Office criteria. The bridge ingests NWS XML alerts and emits WIA heat_health_event envelopes. EU Copernicus C3S and KMA equivalents follow analogous patterns via parallel bridges.

7.4 USGS NLCD canopy bridge

USGS National Land Cover Database provides US 30-meter resolution land cover including impervious surface and tree canopy. The bridge ingests NLCD raster products and emits WIA canopy_assessment envelopes per administrative boundary.

7.5 EU Copernicus climate bridge

EU Copernicus C3S provides climate reanalysis (ERA5) and seasonal forecasts. The bridge ingests Copernicus products for climate-context envelopes that downstream consumers can use to put current observations in historical perspective.

7.6 WIA family integration

WIA-OMNI-API holds city agency credentials. WIA Water Scarcity Response composes — drought + heat compound stress shows up in both standards; cross-standard analytics need the joint view. WIA Time-Series Data composes — weather station continuous streams ride as time-series envelopes. WIA-AIR-SHIELD provides cross-jurisdiction transport hardening.

7.7 Compliance mappings

WMO heat-wave standards inform the heat_health_event advisory levels. WHO Public Health Surveillance for Heat Hazards provides the vulnerable population categorisation framework. Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction underlies the cross-jurisdiction sharing pattern.

Implementer Notes

Three operational concerns recur in UHI infrastructure and are baked into the standard. First, satellite revisit cadence: Landsat revisits any spot every 16 days; Sentinel-3 daily but 1-km resolution; ECOSTRESS sub-daily but ISS orbital coverage variable. Hosts MUST document data-source revisit characteristics in the discovery document. Second, vulnerable population data: aggregate-only by Phase 1 §4.6 design; downstream consumers requesting individual identification MUST be refused. Third, heat advisory latency: the gap between NWS issuance and public dissemination is critical; the standard\x27s heat_health_event stream targets < 5 minute latency from issuance to subscriber receipt.

Korea-Specific Application Note

Korea's 2018 and 2024 heat waves drove formal heat-emergency response across municipal and national agencies. KMA (Korea Meteorological Administration) issues heat advisories aligned with NOAA NWS's structure but adapted for Korean climate; the Ministry of the Interior and Safety coordinates muders-shelter (무더위 쉼터) activation across cities. The standard's heat_health_event envelope maps cleanly onto KMA advisories via the data_source field, and the Korean municipal climate offices in Seoul, Daejeon, Daegu, and Gwangju have published municipal climate adaptation plans that align with the standard's mitigation_intervention vocabulary.

Vulnerable-Population-First Design

Every choice in this standard is filtered through: does this protect the people most at risk during heat events while supporting effective outreach, or does it build identification infrastructure that outlasts the emergency? When the two conflict, vulnerable-population data sovereignty wins. That is why the standard mandates aggregate-only vulnerable_population_area envelopes, refuses to allow individual identification even in emergency mode, and requires emergency-management workflows to operate on neighbourhood counts rather than personal lists.
