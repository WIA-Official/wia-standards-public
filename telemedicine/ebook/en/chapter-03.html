Clinical Encounter Envelopes and FHIR R5

The clinical_encounter envelope is the post-session record of the interaction's clinical content. It maps directly to FHIR R5 Encounter and carries the diagnostic codes, findings, and follow-up plan that downstream care depends on.

3.1 Envelope schema

{
  "wia_telemedicine_version": "1.0.0",
  "type": "clinical_encounter",
  "encounter_id": "01HXR...",
  "session_id":   "01HXR...",
  "patient_id":   "did:wia:patient:...",
  "started_at": "2026-04-27T14:00:00Z",
  "ended_at":   "2026-04-27T14:50:00Z",
  "icd11_codes": [
    { "code": "6A70.0", "display": "Single episode depressive disorder, mild" }
  ],
  "snomed_findings": [
    { "code": "35489007", "display": "Depressive disorder" }
  ],
  "vitals_observations": [
    "fhir:Observation/123-bp"
  ],
  "imaging_refs": [],
  "follow_up_required": true,
  "signature_by_provider": "Ed25519"
}

3.2 Coding system discipline

Diagnostic codes use WHO ICD-11; clinical findings use SNOMED CT International. The standard does not redistribute either — implementations license SNOMED separately under the SNOMED International Affiliate Licence and obtain ICD-11 under WHO terms. The licensing is not a barrier; both are widely available to qualified healthcare implementers.

3.3 FHIR mapping

Each clinical_encounter envelope corresponds to a FHIR R5 Encounter resource with status "finished". The envelope's icd11_codes become Condition resources linked to the Encounter; SNOMED findings become Observation resources. Vital signs referenced by URI live as separate FHIR Observation resources with LOINC codes (e.g., 85354-9 for blood pressure panel) and UCUM units.

3.4 Provider signature is mandatory

Unlike the session envelope (which may be signed by either party), the encounter envelope is always signed by the provider. The provider's signature is the legal attestation of the clinical content. Patient counter-signatures are optional and used to acknowledge receipt or to dispute content.

3.5 Imaging refs

Imaging studies are referenced as DICOM Web URLs (WADO-RS endpoints at the originating PACS). The standard does not redistribute imaging payloads; access control is enforced at the PACS, not at the envelope.

3.6 Follow-up commitments

The follow_up_required field is mandatory and feeds the appointment-scheduling workflow. A provider that records "follow_up_required: true" without ever publishing a follow-up appointment slot is generating a measurable continuity-of-care gap that downstream auditors can detect.

Worked example

A psychiatry encounter publishes ICD-11 6A70.0 (mild depression, single episode), SNOMED 35489007 (depressive disorder), with a vital-sign Observation resource recording resting heart rate captured by the patient's wearable during the session. The follow-up flag is set; an appointment slot for 4 weeks later is published in parallel. The patient's wallet now contains the full encounter record signed by the provider, plus the booked follow-up.
