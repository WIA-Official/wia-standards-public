Worked Examples — Psychiatry, Telestroke, Chronic-Care Diabetes

The previous seven chapters introduced the envelope set and the operational discipline. This closing chapter walks through three end-to-end worked examples spanning the most consequential telemedicine modalities in current clinical practice.

8.1 Psychiatry consultation

A patient in Seoul with depressive symptoms books a video session with a Korean psychiatrist. The flow:

  • Patient signs patient_consent (telemedicine_consultation + recording_session)
  • Patient books an appointment_slot matching their language and jurisdiction
  • consultation_session opens at the scheduled time; WebRTC media established within 800 ms
  • 50-minute session; provider publishes clinical_encounter with ICD-11 6A70.0
  • Provider publishes e_prescription for sertraline 50 mg daily, 30 days, 2 refills, with drug-interaction check and allergy review attested
  • Patient submits weekly patient_reported_outcome (PHQ-9) for 12 weeks
  • Follow-up appointment_slot + consultation_session 4 weeks later; tapering decision references the PRO sequence

The patient's wallet at the end contains a complete signed audit trail of the entire treatment course — consent, session, encounter, prescription, PRO sequence, follow-up — owned by the patient and verifiable by any future clinician.

8.2 Telestroke

A rural emergency department in California without 24/7 neurology coverage federates with a tele-stroke service. A patient presents with acute stroke symptoms:

  • Local clinician initiates federation_handshake under emergency exception (patient cannot consent)
  • Remote neurologist receives cross_border_bundle containing CT imaging refs and clinical signs
  • Remote neurologist publishes a referral envelope recommending thrombolysis
  • Local clinician administers thrombolysis under their own licence within the 4.5-hour window
  • Patient counter-signs the consent retroactively during recovery
  • Both encounters are linked to the patient's master record by DID; the patient retains both signed records

Without federation, the local clinician would have administered thrombolysis without specialist consultation or transferred the patient to a stroke centre at the cost of a 2-hour ground or 45-minute air ambulance — both of which would consume most of the treatment window.

8.3 Chronic-care diabetes management

A patient with type-2 diabetes uses a CGM at home and consults monthly with their endocrinologist:

  • WIA Vital-Sign Streaming carries CGM readings continuously under signed envelopes
  • Monthly consultation_session + clinical_encounter records the consultation
  • e_prescription for insulin or oral agents, adjusted based on the CGM data and the encounter
  • Weekly patient_reported_outcome (custom diabetes-specific instrument) captures patient-reported burden
  • HIRA receives quarterly claim envelopes derived from the encounter stream
  • 의료마이데이터 portal exposes the full envelope history to the patient

Three regulators (의료법, HIRA, 개인정보보호위원회), one envelope stream, one patient-owned record.

8.4 What this standard does not promise

The standard does not file regulatory reports on the clinic's behalf, does not negotiate insurance coverage, and does not replace clinical judgement. It promises one specific thing: when remote care is delivered, the chain of evidence from the first consent click to the final follow-up will be cryptographically continuous, machine-comparable across providers and vendors, and small enough that even a single-clinician practice can produce it.

8.5 弘益人間 — Benefit All Humanity

The philosophy that drives this standard is simple: the patient who needs care most is rarely the patient with the easiest access to care. Telemedicine collapses geographic distance; the standard's envelope discipline collapses the schema-drift distance. Together they put a sub-specialist in reach of a rural patient, a continuous record in the hands of a patient who travels, a verifiable consent at the centre of every interaction. The standard is open, the envelopes are public, and the implementation is small enough that a five-person clinic can deliver it.
