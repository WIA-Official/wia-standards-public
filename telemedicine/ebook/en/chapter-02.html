Consultation Session Envelopes and WebRTC Signalling

The consultation_session envelope is the smallest unit of remote care. It binds a patient and a provider to a modality (video, audio, chat, async messaging) and a scheduled time, and references the patient's consent.

2.1 Envelope schema

{
  "wia_telemedicine_version": "1.0.0",
  "type": "consultation_session",
  "session_id": "01HXR...",
  "patient_id": "did:wia:patient:...",
  "provider_id": "did:wia:provider:...",
  "modality": "video",
  "specialty": "psychiatry",
  "scheduled_start": "2026-04-27T14:00:00Z",
  "scheduled_end":   "2026-04-27T14:50:00Z",
  "patient_consent_ref": "did:wia:consent:...",
  "jurisdiction": "KR",
  "language_pref": "ko",
  "signature": { "alg": "Ed25519", "value": "..." }
}

2.2 WebRTC signalling

The session establishes media via the WebRTC offer/answer + ICE candidate exchange over the /sessions/{id}/signalling endpoint. Signalling is TLS 1.3; media is SRTP with AES-128-GCM. The endpoint is bidirectional — either side may POST — and every signalling message is signed.

2.3 Consent first, session second

The standard rejects sessions that lack a referenced, signed patient_consent envelope. This is structural, not procedural. A clinician cannot bypass consent by clicking through a modal; the platform refuses to open the session at the protocol layer.

2.4 Recording

Recording requires a separate patient_consent envelope with scope: recording_session. Patient-facing UI MUST display a clear visual indicator while recording is active. Recordings are stored under the patient's tenant with retention determined by jurisdiction (HIPAA: 6 years, KR 의료법: 10 years, EU: patient-controlled).

2.5 Latency budget

End-to-end signalling latency SHOULD target p95 under 250 ms. Re-keying SRTP streams on the hour is standard; mid-call re-keys can introduce audio glitches and SHOULD be reserved for credential rotation events.

2.6 Modality selection

Video is the default for synchronous consultations. Audio is appropriate for follow-ups where visual examination is unnecessary and avoids the bandwidth burden on patients with poor connectivity. Chat is appropriate for brief clinical questions. Async messaging covers the long-tail of clarifications that do not justify a synchronous slot. Store-and-forward (e.g., a dermatology image submitted now, reviewed within 24 hours) is the appropriate modality for image-based subspecialties where real-time interaction adds little value.

Worked example

A patient in Seoul, language preference Korean, books a video consultation with a psychiatrist licensed in Korea. The booking flow signs the consent envelope (telemedicine_consultation + recording_session), publishes the session envelope, and at session start time the WebRTC signalling completes within 800 ms. The session runs 50 minutes; the encounter envelope is published 30 minutes later with ICD-11 6A70.0; an e-prescription for sertraline is published 5 minutes after the encounter. The patient receives all four envelopes in their wallet within the hour.
