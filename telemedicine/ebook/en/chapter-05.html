Appointment Slots and Cross-Jurisdiction Licensing

The appointment_slot envelope is the public-facing offer of provider availability. It carries duration, modality, specialty, and — critically — the jurisdictions in which the provider is licensed to practice.

5.1 Envelope schema

{
  "wia_telemedicine_version": "1.0.0",
  "type": "appointment_slot",
  "slot_id": "01HXR...",
  "provider_id": "did:wia:provider:...",
  "available_from": "2026-05-15T09:00:00Z",
  "duration_min": 30,
  "modality": "video",
  "specialty": "psychiatry",
  "languages_supported": ["en", "ko"],
  "jurisdictions_licensed": ["KR", "US-CA"],
  "signature": { "alg": "Ed25519", "value": "..." }
}

5.2 Why jurisdictions are mandatory

Cross-jurisdiction prescribing has different licensing requirements than cross-jurisdiction consultation; a patient discovering at booking time that the provider is not licensed in the patient's jurisdiction is the worst possible time for that discovery. The standard moves the discovery to slot publication, where the patient's booking client can filter the slots before showing them.

5.3 Booking flow

The patient's client posts to /tm/v1/slots/{id}/book with the patient's jurisdiction and a fresh consent envelope. The booking server verifies that the patient's jurisdiction is in jurisdictions_licensed before accepting; mismatches return HTTP 403 with a Problem Details payload naming the missing licence.

5.4 Multi-language support

The languages_supported field uses BCP 47 language tags. The patient's client filters slots against the patient's preferred language, with optional fallback to interpreter-supported sessions. The standard does not redefine interpreter sourcing; it provides the binding so that interpreter-supported sessions are visible to the patient.

5.5 Interstate licensing compacts

For US providers, interstate licensing compacts (IMLC for medical, PSY-PACT for psychology, NLC for nursing) expand the effective jurisdictions_licensed set. The standard does not adjudicate compact membership; it accepts the compact-derived list as part of the provider's license inventory.

5.6 Korean medical licensing

Korean providers operate under the 의료법 licence framework. The standard records the licence number in the provider's tenant identity attestation, separate from the slot envelope. The slot envelope carries only the jurisdictions, not the licence number, to keep slot publication free of personally identifying provider data.

Worked example

A psychiatrist licensed in Korea and California publishes weekly evening slots in both KST and PST. A patient in Korea sees only the KST slots; a patient in California sees only the PST slots. A Korean expatriate temporarily in California can see both, with the patient's jurisdiction determining which licence is exercised at booking time.
