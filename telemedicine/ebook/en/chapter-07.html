Patient-Reported Outcomes and Chronic-Care Continuity

Outcome measurement under remote care is otherwise sparse; instrument-based patient-reported outcomes (PROs) are the most reliable signal of treatment efficacy in telemedicine modalities. The standard treats PROs as first-class signed envelopes.

7.1 Envelope schema

{
  "wia_telemedicine_version": "1.0.0",
  "type": "patient_reported_outcome",
  "pro_id": "01HXR...",
  "encounter_id": "01HXR...",
  "instrument": "PHQ-9",
  "captured_at": "2026-04-28T08:00:00Z",
  "responses": [
    { "item": "PHQ-9-1", "value": 2 },
    { "item": "PHQ-9-2", "value": 2 },
    { "item": "PHQ-9-3", "value": 1 }
  ],
  "score_summary": 12,
  "signed_by_patient": "Ed25519"
}

7.2 Standard instruments

The standard recognises commonly used PRO instruments: PHQ-9 (depression), GAD-7 (anxiety), PROMIS-29 (general health), EQ-5D-5L (quality of life). Vendors may publish custom instruments with their own identifiers; the standard does not adjudicate clinical validity.

7.3 Patient signature

PRO envelopes are signed by the patient, not the provider. The patient's signature is the legal attestation that the responses are the patient's own. Provider-administered instruments are recorded as Observation resources within the encounter envelope, not as PROs.

7.4 Chronic-care continuity

For chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension, depression, COPD), PRO cadence is the difference between effective and ineffective remote care. The standard recommends weekly to monthly PRO cadence depending on instrument; missed PROs feed the next provider encounter as a continuity signal.

7.5 Wearable and home-monitoring binding

Wearable-derived data (continuous glucose monitor, blood pressure cuff, heart rate variability, sleep tracker) flows under the WIA Vital-Sign Streaming companion standard and is referenced from the encounter envelope. The combination of clinician-administered encounter, patient-administered PRO, and wearable-derived continuous data forms the most complete remote-care record currently feasible.

7.6 Caregiver delegation

For patients with cognitive impairment or other barriers to self-administration of PROs, a caregiver may submit on the patient's behalf with the patient's prior signed delegation. The PRO envelope records both the patient identity and the caregiver identity; downstream consumers know the response source is delegated rather than direct.

Worked example

A patient with major depressive disorder receives weekly PHQ-9 instruments via their patient app. The cadence captures the shift from a baseline PHQ-9 of 18 to a 12-week sustained PHQ-9 of 6, providing measured evidence of treatment response that supports the prescriber's decision to taper the medication. The PRO sequence is signed by the patient throughout; the prescriber's tapering decision references the PRO envelopes by identifier.
