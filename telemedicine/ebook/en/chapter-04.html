e-Prescription Envelopes and Drug-Interaction Discipline

The e_prescription envelope is the canonical digital prescription. It carries the medication identity, dose, frequency, duration, refills, and — critically — the drug-interaction and allergy review evidence that protects the patient from the most common preventable harm in remote care.

4.1 Envelope schema

{
  "wia_telemedicine_version": "1.0.0",
  "type": "e_prescription",
  "rx_id": "01HXR...",
  "encounter_id": "01HXR...",
  "patient_id":   "did:wia:patient:...",
  "prescriber_id": "did:wia:provider:...",
  "rxnorm_code": "312036",
  "atc_code":    "N06AB06",
  "dose":        "50 mg",
  "route":       "oral",
  "frequency":   "QD",
  "duration_days": 30,
  "refills_authorised": 2,
  "controlled_substance_schedule": null,
  "drug_interaction_checks": [
    { "kind": "ddi", "severity": "minor",
      "summary": "Potential interaction with metoprolol; clinically not significant at this dose" }
  ],
  "patient_allergy_review_completed": true,
  "signature_by_prescriber": "Ed25519"
}

4.2 Mandatory interaction checks

The drug_interaction_checks field is mandatory. The most common preventable harm from teleprescribing is a drug-drug or drug-allergy interaction that an in-person prescriber would catch by looking at a paper chart. By making the field mandatory, the standard forces every prescriber to record what was checked, even when no significant interaction was found.

4.3 Allergy review

Similarly, patient_allergy_review_completed: true is the prescriber's attestation that they reviewed the patient's allergy history before prescribing. The field accepts only "true"; if no allergy review occurred, the prescription cannot be published. The standard does not allow false attestations as a workaround.

4.4 Controlled substances

The controlled_substance_schedule field is set per jurisdiction (US DEA Schedule II–V, KR 마약류 분류, EU national scheduling). Controlled-substance teleprescribing is governed by jurisdiction-specific rules (US Ryan Haight Act, etc.) that the standard does not attempt to subsume. The envelope records the schedule so that downstream pharmacy systems can apply the correct dispensing logic.

4.5 RxNorm and ATC binding

Medications use RxNorm identifiers for US prescribers and the WHO ATC/DDD classification for cross-border consistency. Both fields are populated when both apply, allowing the same prescription to be dispensed at a US pharmacy or a Korean pharmacy without re-coding.

4.6 Pharmacy routing

Pharmacies consume the envelope via their existing gateway (SureScripts in the US, KFTC 처방전 전송 in Korea, national e-prescription gateways in EU member states). The WIA envelope is the canonical signed prescription; the gateway protocol is the transport.

Worked example

An e-prescription for sertraline 50 mg daily for 30 days, 2 refills, in an encounter that recorded SNOMED 35489007 (depressive disorder). The prescriber attests that allergies were reviewed and a minor interaction with the patient's metoprolol was identified but determined non-significant. The patient's pharmacy receives the envelope through KFTC and dispenses; the dispensing event is recorded as a separate envelope referencing the prescription.
