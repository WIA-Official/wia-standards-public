Chapter 6: Third-Party Providers (TPPs)

WIA-FIN-022 Open Banking Standard

6.1 Types of TPPs

Account Information Service Providers (AISPs)

AISPs access customer account information to provide value-added services:

  • Account Aggregation: View multiple bank accounts in one app (Plaid, Yolt, Emma)
  • Personal Finance Management: Budgeting, expense tracking, financial planning
  • Creditworthiness Assessment: Alternative credit scoring using bank data
  • Tax Preparation: Automated transaction categorization for tax filing
  • Accounting Software: Business account integration (QuickBooks, Xero)

Payment Initiation Service Providers (PISPs)

PISPs initiate payments from customer accounts on their behalf:

  • E-commerce Checkout: Pay-by-bank alternatives to cards
  • Bill Payment Services: Automated utility and subscription payments
  • Request-to-Pay: Invoice payments and payment requests
  • P2P Payments: Person-to-person money transfers
  • Merchant Services: Point-of-sale payment acceptance

Card-Based Payment Instrument Issuers (CBPIIs)

CBPIIs check funds availability before authorizing card transactions, reducing declined payments and overdraft fees.

6.2 Regulatory Requirements

Authorization Process

  • Register with national competent authority (FCA in UK, BaFin in Germany, etc.)
  • Demonstrate technical and financial resources
  • Provide business plan and risk assessment
  • Appoint responsible persons with required qualifications
  • Establish governance and compliance frameworks
  • Maintain professional indemnity insurance (€1-5M depending on services)

Ongoing Obligations

  • Annual reporting to regulators
  • Maintain capital requirements
  • Incident reporting within 24-72 hours
  • Customer complaint handling procedures
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Staff training and awareness programs

6.3 Building AISP Applications

Use Case: Personal Finance Manager

Key Features:
  • Multi-bank account aggregation
  • Automatic transaction categorization
  • Spending analytics and trends
  • Budget creation and tracking
  • Bill reminders and recurring payment detection
  • Financial goal setting and progress tracking

Technical Implementation

Data Synchronization Strategy:

  • Initial sync: Fetch historical transactions (90-180 days)
  • Regular sync: Poll for new transactions every 4-24 hours
  • Webhook updates: Real-time notifications when available
  • Delta sync: Only fetch transactions since last sync

Transaction Categorization:

  • Rule-based: Merchant name matching
  • ML-based: Train models on user corrections
  • MCC codes: Use merchant category codes
  • User feedback: Learn from manual categorization

6.4 Building PISP Applications

Use Case: E-Commerce Payment Gateway

Value Proposition:
  • Lower transaction fees (0.3-0.5% vs 2-3% for cards)
  • Instant payment confirmation
  • Reduced cart abandonment
  • No chargebacks (payments are irrevocable)
  • Better conversion rates on high-value items

Payment Flow Design

  • Bank Selection: Let customer choose their bank
  • Payment Consent: Create consent with amount and payee
  • Authorization: Redirect to bank for SCA
  • Confirmation: Poll payment status or use webhook
  • Settlement: Funds transferred to merchant account

6.5 Developer Resources

Essential Tools and Libraries

  • OAuth Libraries: passport-oauth2, simple-oauth2
  • HTTP Clients: axios, node-fetch with retry logic
  • Certificate Management: OpenSSL, Let's Encrypt
  • Signing/Verification: node-jose, jsonwebtoken
  • Testing: Postman, Insomnia, Newman for automation

Community and Support

  • Open Banking Directory: Central registry of TPPs and ASPSPs
  • Developer Forums: Stack Overflow, dedicated Slack channels
  • API Aggregators: Services that normalize multi-bank integration
  • Standards Bodies: Open Banking UK, Berlin Group, STET

6.6 Monetization Strategies

For AISPs

  • Freemium Model: Basic features free, premium features paid
  • Subscription: Monthly/annual fees for advanced analytics
  • Affiliate Revenue: Recommend financial products
  • Data Insights: Anonymized aggregate insights (with consent)
  • B2B Licensing: White-label solutions for other companies

For PISPs

  • Transaction Fees: Fixed fee or percentage per payment
  • Merchant Services: Monthly subscriptions for businesses
  • Float Income: Interest on funds in transit
  • Value-Added Services: Reconciliation, reporting, analytics
  • Platform Fees: Marketplace or multi-sided platform models

Compliance Checklist:
  • ✓ Valid regulatory authorization maintained
