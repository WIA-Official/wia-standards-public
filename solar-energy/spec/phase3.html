☀️ WIA-ENE-005: Phase 3 Specification

Optimization - Performance Tuning and Efficiency Improvements

1. Overview

Phase 3 focuses on system-wide optimization, advanced analytics, and predictive capabilities. This phase enables maximum energy harvest, extended equipment life, and optimal economic performance through intelligent algorithms and machine learning.

2. Advanced MPPT Algorithms

2.1 Multi-Peak Tracking

Systems must detect and track multiple local maxima under partial shading:

  • Global MPPT scan interval: Every 5 minutes or on 20% power change
  • Local MPPT tracking: Continuous
  • Scan resolution: ≤5V steps across voltage range
  • Scan duration: ≤30 seconds
  • Power loss during scan: ≤5% over scan duration

2.2 Adaptive MPPT

MPPT behavior adapts to conditions:

  • Fast tracking (100ms update) during variable conditions
  • Slow tracking (10s update) during stable conditions
  • Automatic transition based on power variance
  • Machine learning prediction of MPP movement

4. Performance Analytics

4.1 Automated Performance Diagnostics

Daily analysis identifies performance issues:

  • String-to-string comparison (>10% variance flagged)
  • Actual vs. expected production (accounting for weather)
  • Efficiency trending (>2% decline/year flagged)
  • Soiling loss estimation (>5% triggers cleaning recommendation)
  • Shading analysis and quantification

4.2 Benchmarking and Reporting

Automated performance reports generated weekly/monthly:

  • Energy production vs. prediction
  • Performance ratio trending
  • Availability and uptime statistics
  • Economic performance ($/kWh, ROI tracking)
  • Comparison to similar systems (anonymized)
  • Carbon offset and environmental impact

5. Grid Services Optimization

5.1 Revenue Stacking

Simultaneous participation in multiple value streams:

  • Energy arbitrage (time-of-use optimization)
  • Frequency regulation (fast response)
  • Demand response (scheduled curtailment)
  • Capacity market participation
  • Renewable energy certificate (REC) generation

6. Advanced Energy Management

6.1 Load Forecasting

Machine learning models predict consumption:

  • 15-minute resolution forecasts
  • 24-hour ahead prediction
  • Consideration of: day of week, season, weather, historical patterns
  • Accuracy target: MAPE <10% for next-day forecast

6.2 Multi-Objective Optimization

Balancing competing objectives:

  • Minimize electricity costs
  • Maximize self-consumption
  • Ensure backup energy availability
  • Limit grid export (if constrained)
  • Maximize battery life (limit cycles)
  • Provide contracted grid services

7. Advanced Diagnostics

7.1 I-V Curve Analysis

Periodic I-V curve sweeps characterize panel health:

  • Frequency: Quarterly or on performance degradation
  • Automated comparison to manufacturer specifications
  • Detection of: series resistance increase, shunt resistance decrease, fill factor degradation
  • Quantification of power loss mechanisms

7.2 Thermal Imaging Integration

Support for automated thermal camera systems:

  • Drone or ground-based thermal survey data import
  • Automated hot spot detection (>15°C above average)
  • Correlation with electrical production data
  • Prioritized maintenance task generation
